    I'm not sure if this peacoat fits right even though my mother and sister say it does...

    Those pics are definitely better. The coat can be distinguished from the background; the side-lighting shows some depth and allows us to see any folds and wrinkles. It's also good that you have a photo where you wear the coat buttoned up.

    I'm interested to hear how you feel about the fit. If no one else was telling you it's fine, or it's too big, or it's too small, what would be your sense of how it feels and looks on you? I'm guessing that you have a sense that it's too big, since you went to the trouble of posting an inquiry here on MFA. Those instincts would be correct.

    The sleeves are definitely too long. Since there are no features on the cuff, this is something that could be corrected (shortened) by a tailor.

    The body is too wide -- it's clear that the coat is standing away from your body; there is no good body-shaped (coming in at the waist) line apparent; and there are folds of extra fabric apparent around the upper chest and under the arms. The width of a coat can usually be adjusted up to a couple of inches by a tailor; but this is too much material for it all to be taken in.

    The coat is also too long. It's approaching 3/4 coat length, and while some pea coats (notably ones based on a classic naval design, more appropriately called 'naval jackets') may be made as 3/4 length, it's not as common these days. The proper length is just covering the seat or just below the zipper. Modern cuts will be even shorter yet.

    The most significant point is that the shoulder seams drop past the curve of your shoulder. The shoulders are too wide; and that's something that can't be corrected by a tailor. In addition, it means that taking in material at the waist will still leave awkward billows beneath the shoulders (upper chest and back, and around the armpits), and may end up looking worse.

    There are different ways that clothing 'doesn't fit'. Some coats and jackets may be 'off' according to the form of your body -- they may fit well except for a certain dimension (say, if everything else is fine, but the hem is too long). Others may just not be the kind of cut you're looking for: you may want a fit tailored close to your waist, whereas the cut may be intentionally more flowing or boxy. This coat, however, is simply not your size. It's too big in all dimensions.

    One of the great things about a pea coat is the sharpness of the cut. Sharp shoulders and a shape to the waist, along with the lapels and rows of buttons, create a trim, respectable military form. While some coats may work or even look better with a loose or baggy fit, a pea coat does not; it loses much of the appeal of the design.

    Note the fit and cut in these examples of pea coats. The shoulders are sharp, the waists are fitted, and the hems come to just below the seat, if not higher. Even a somewhat long pea coat will not come down the leg as far as the one you're wearing.
