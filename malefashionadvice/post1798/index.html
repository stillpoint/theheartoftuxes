    Bought my first suit with your help. What do I need to do to it to make it look better? And what should I wear with it for a somewhat casual fall wedding?

    It does look like there's some extra material in the back; so you may want to have the jacket taken in at the sides or/and at the middle seam. Consult your tailor; but before you do, consider whether you want a classic look or a modern/euro look that's tailored more closely to your body and shows more of a curve at the waist. Neither one's right or wrong; it's a matter of preference. Since this is a Hilfiger, with a higher button set, it may look good in a classic tailoring, without too much of an indent at the sides.

    To critique the pants, I'd want to see them with the jacket off, to see how they fit at the waist and seat. From the looks of the legs, I'd say they're a tad short rather than long. Once you have shoes on, you'll want enough length for a single break at the front of the pant leg. Since they are not slim fit, they're not so appropriate at a shorter, break-free length. The fabric should drape and move, with a bend just above the shoe (you've got the start of one on the left leg there).

    Yes, I agree with you that the shirt and tie pictured are not so interesting. White shirt and navy sharkskin tie, or a solid tie of any number of deep or bright colors, should look great. I like (love!) the pocket square on its own; but you should be careful not to come too close to the color of the tie, or the effect will be too matchy-matchy. Either go for a white pocket square, or bring in a completely new color and pattern, or ballpark-match it with a less obvious item, such as socks or cufflinks or a minor accent color in a tie.

    What I like about your new choice of shirt and tie is the contrast. That navy will look great with grey. Note, though, that the texture of the new tie is very close to the texture of the suit; and together with the narrowness of the tie, that is a slick look. In that case, shiny black oxford shoes or cap toes would go better.

    My preference is the brown shoe, especially the wingtips, with a grey suit. Very elegant! But it's a warmer, more approachable look. So if you choose the brown wingtips, I'd suggest more texture in the tie, such as in a grenadine; or more pattern, like a stripe or floral foulard.

    Your lapels are wide enough to warrant a wider tie as well; and if you still want a plain silk, you could go brighter blue. That's if you're looking to stick with blue. Like I said, there are many other colors that would look great with your grey suit.

    Just an idea: I'm picking up a bit of adventuresome nature with the choice of pink polka-dot pocket square. A light pink shirt would look amazing under the grey jacket. Then you could go same color family with a deep pink/fuschia or burgundy tie, or contrast with with a purple or mid-blue tie. (Love this french blue knit with pink or white shirt.) Then the pocket square would really snap. Add the brown wing tips, and... let's just say you shouldn't stand too close to the groom or you may steal the spotlight.
