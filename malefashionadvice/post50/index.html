    HOW to wear a "Polo"/"Golf shirt" under a v-neck sweater? Collar in, or out on top?

    For the most part, we try to keep our collars in and under whatever layer we're wearing, be it v-neck or crew neck sweater, or sportjacket or windbreaker. Even with this intention, under v-neck sweaters collars will arrange themselves and sometimes slip over the edge or pop out completely; and none more than polo collars. If the occasion and outfit are very casual, keep the best intent and then let the collar do what it will. Don't concern yourself.

    If the occasion and look are a step above very casual -- if any degree of sharpness is required -- opt for a button-down collar. Up one more level to dressy casual or business casual, and you'll be choosing a point or spread collar that stands up by itself (points in, under the sweater).

    The strength of the v-neck is as a layering piece for dressy casual, or as a clean and classic piece in itself, worn without an under-layer or with a simple v-neck t-shirt. More compatible and along the lines of the polo is the casual cardigan, which has a similar ease and rumpling to it.

    Here's what may be a polo collar under a chunky cardigan. Though the collar points are within the sweater, one side of the collar is upright and the other is flat against the collarbone. No problem.

    If you are going to wear a v-neck, the advantages of the polo are the short sleeves (cooler), and the cleaner button-free front under the sweater.

    In this day and age, maybe we are not so comfortable and confident with our casual clothing. Things are slimmer, closer to the body, more controlled, and certainly more preened. I think this dynamic extends to the collar in/collar out question. From the '60s: Here's Sean Connery as James Bond. Are you going to tell him his collar should be in and under?
