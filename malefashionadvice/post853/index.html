    What can I change?

    When posting for advice, please show the entire outfit so we can see all the pieces and how they look on you as a whole.

    This outfit is a write-off; the pieces can be used in other outfits, because they fit fairly well, but you should start over. You're going to have trouble finding any tie that goes with a black shirt. A light tie on a black shirt is almost always a bad look, reminiscent of a gangster costume, which is about as cheesy as it gets. This goes for most dark shirt light tie combinations.

    Your second problem here is that the shirt, pants, and belt are very close in shade, but differ in their hue. This is disturbing. It often happens with black-on-black outfits that the blacks don't match. People think "it's all black" and don't use their eyes to see what's in front of them. The truth is that there are many, many different hues of black — warm or cool, brown-black, blue-black, shiny or dull, and so on — and if you don't match the blacks exactly it will look 'off'. That is why it is best to vary the shades (the darkness) and textures of your pieces. Pair a single black piece with different shades of grey, and let them have contrasting textures, or include a pattern.

    If you're going to use black in an outfit, you should understand its qualities. You should understand what it is saying about you and what energies it brings out in you. (Actually, this is true of any color or element of style: you should understand what it is doing there.) Black is very serious, dour. It can come off as sullen and brooding, and/or it can come off as mysterious and secretive. These are strong effects, and if they don't match your body and your personal qualities it can make you look like you're trying to be something you're not.

    Black also by nature creates the highest contrast possible with other colors, and introduces a very sharp, stark quality to any outfit. Again, that starkness should match your own energy and your intended look; otherwise, tone down the contrast.

    In your case, I think you can look quite good in black if you do it right. That means using the qualities of black, and of that particular shirt, in a conscious manner. The depth and luxury implied by a satiny black shirt is enough on its own. (Unfortunately, that shirt looks more synthetic than silky, but it will do.) Wear it without the tie, undo the top button or two, and wear it with light-neutral dress pant or nice chino (tan/khaki/beige/taupe/light grey/stone/cream), and possible a light-neutral sportjacket. The contrast will be much more interesting than an entire dark, colorless outfit, and will allow the viewer's eye to move back and forth between the different fields of shade. Just don't go too light with the other pieces, or the contrast will be too stark.

    It is possible to pair black with other colors, but it happens at a very sophisticated level. Deep, rich colors can play on the depth of black to create something very lush, if it's done right. Purple, aubergine, and some browns can be used in this way.

    • Here, the dark purple tie adds depth and elegance where a black tie would only be sharp (and predictable). The white shirt helps a lot by providing the sharp contrast, and avoids overdoing the darkness, which would be a cheap move.

    • The example of an aubergine tie with black shirt and suit shows the kind of richness that is possible; but it fails here because it is not an appropriate look for the wearer's age and appearance — he's not that deep and mysterious, and so the outfit looks awkward on him.
