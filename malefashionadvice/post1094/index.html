    Can cargo pants (not shorts!) ever look nice?

    They can look good, but for what they are, which is military-inspired, rugged, very casual pants. Attempts to bring the cargo pant up into even slightly more formal or fashion-forward looks usually fail. The focus of invective against the cargos is, of course, the pockets. Cargo pants are not chinos, though many try to use them in the same role. If instead we embrace the pockets (and possibly extra loops and tags), we find a pant that brings the attention down and out, sinking and spreading in casual ease.

    Earthy, grounded, and focused on the stance of the posture, as opposed to the carriage -- these are the energies through which the pant emerged in the '90s. Although cargo pants found their popularity at raves, where all-night dancers sought options for keeping wallet, keys, smokes, and whatnot close at hand while dancing, the pants really found their full expression in grunge culture, as those same relaxed, earthy energies manifested in style of dress and activity.

    So if you're going to wear cargo pants, you should be thinking "down" and "relax" in your outfit. Also, "simple". It's when trying to fit them into the "up" and "in" of pressed button-up shirts, snazzy fabrics and patterns, slim fits, and sleek footwear and accessories, or when trying to be too fancy with the outfit that the cargo pant is then found lacking.

    It's hard to find pics of cargo pants done right. You'll have to extrapolate. Like this is the way it's done, with olive drab pants and belt, muted half-out t-shirt, and sneakers. Even better if the sneakers are somewhat dingy and beat up; and even better if they're replaced with chunky, well-worn leather boots or desert boots.

    Another example, with simple plain disheveled t-shirt and sneakers. If you were wearing a button-up, it should be a slightly mussed plain oxford, untucked or half-tucked. A jean jacket would be another possible element. The upper half of the outfit can be somewhat more fitted, but should in some way participate as well in the grounded downward and relaxed feel of the pants.

    And as for whether cargo pants can look good on women: a resounding Shyeah!!.
