    Is there such thing as an acceptable wide brimmed hat?

    Acceptability depends on the milieu, on the situation, and on your own style, age, and swag. For example, cowboy hats can be worn in some situations in some places, but elsewhere look out of place on all but the most dedicated 'westerners'. Some hats are better for young folk; others can only be worn by someone older and possessed of a degree of gravitas. Gender is also a factor: girls usually look cute in all kinds of hats; boys tend to look dorky.

    I do wear a straw hat (panama/fedora) sometimes in the summer; but there's a limited range of outfits it will go with; i.e., a bit dressy or at least dapper in a classic way without becoming a retro caricature. Luckily, I'm fond of dusty old-world styles, which go well with a straw hat (say, linen sportcoat, light chinos, canvas and leather accessories, classic tortoiseshell eyewear).

    When dressing down in the hot sun, I'll sometimes wear a boonie hat, which is an army version of a bucket hat. In summer, the boonie may occupy a niche at least a bit similar to the slouch beanie in winter: casual, comfortable, droopy, and a bit hip. You'll find the boonie used a bit more frequently in hip-hop wear. I use both desert camo and plain olive drab versions. As long as you avoid matching it with other military gear and making it into a costume, the boonie offers a nice character and an alternative to preppie and Aussie bucket hats.

    For years I've been on the lookout for a big ol' Italian farmer's hat. Wide, wide brim with a tall crown and made in a rustic loose straw weave, it's an exceptional meeting of function and unpolished, earthy style. As with other hats, the whole outfit should match in style and level of formality; so in this case modern flash and bling would be mostly abandoned, and I might go for espadrilles, plain plimsolls, or work shoes, relaxed fit pants with an impromptu cuff, a plain relaxed linen shirt or maybe nautical-stripe t-shirt, and if the situation requires, a textured or even threadbare old cardigan. Not something I'd wear in the heart of the city, but fantastically comfortable in the country or by the sea. For an even bigger brim (sombrero territory), check out Korean farmer's hats.
