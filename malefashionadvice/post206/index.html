    Why do my pants do this?

    Sometimes the 'advice' offered by people on MFA is decidedly speculative. The type of fabric used in the pants has little to do with pocket flare. The waist size is also not the determining issue. Within reason, you shouldn't be expected to change the shape of your body to fit into your clothes. And if the cut is right, rise has nothing to do with the pockets popping out. Different pockets may not flare, but that doesn't fix the underlying problem.

    A couple of redditors have identified the actual issue, which is tightness "across the crotch". I prefer to call it tightness in the seat; but it's the same difference. If you picture a band of tight fabric across the middle of your seat you can pretty well visualize what's going on: the material that is sewn together around your hips is under tension; and the pockets, which are not sewn down, lever outward (their pivot being the front of the pant near the zipper).

    Yes, it is best to find pants that fit properly from the get-go; but some dress pants that otherwise fit well have enough fabric in the seat to be let out sufficiently. Clothing manufacturers for the most part aren't putting in as much extra allowance as they did in the past; but it's worth it to have a look or check with your tailor.

    I'm telling you... it can sometimes be like you have a whole new pair of pants; and when a tailor's alteration makes them comfortable and good-looking, it can make your day.

    If you have difficulty finding pants that fit you in the seat, it can be easier to buy a size larger and have the waist taken in than to find pants with sufficient extra fabric to let out. (Having a skillful tailor or seamstress is also an issue with letting out the seat, in that a proper line has to be sewn that will fit you, not just mimic the previous line of the pants.) When the waist is taken in, usually just a small wedge is taken in from the upper seat as well, if that; so overall it's a higher percentage strategy.
