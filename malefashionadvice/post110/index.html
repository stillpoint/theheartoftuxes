    Just found out I need glasses, getting them this week. Does wearing glasses change how you have to go about putting an outfit together? If yes, how so?

    Yes. Just the fact that there will be eyewear on your face will already suggest something to others. Whatever type of frame is involved, the glasses themselves call up a certain -- I don't know -- studious quality, if you know what I mean. It hilights your engagement with life through the eyes (and by extension, through the thinking).

    Besides that, the style of the glasses can not possibly be universal, so they either limit the range of styles you can wear them with, or they will clash with certain outfits. It may not be a big deal, or it may be a deal-breaker, depending on how put-together you like to appear.

    Just considering the hats that you might don in fall and winter, or even in warmer weather, it is clear that some hat-and-glasses combos work and some don't. A retro hat like, say, a poor boy cap may go with some modern frames like metal or brightly colored plastic, but not as well as with retro frames like tortoise-shell. Casual hats like ballcaps won't go well with some very precise urban designs like angular frames or thin shiny wire. A cowboy hat and thick horn-rimmed glasses only work in the most quirky, nerdy way.

    Extrapolate this to the outfit as a whole and you can get a sense that some frames will look awkward with some outfits. So you need to consider what style or styles you plan to wear, and 'who' you want to be when you're wearing the glasses.

    Facing this issue, many people will will go for the most average and least obtrusive frames possible -- designs that are completely lacking in character, or frameless lenses that, the person hopes, will disappear on the face. This is a mistake in my opinion. It doesn't amount to neutrality and silence; rather, it sends a message of boredom, retreat, and fear of making a statement. It says "I choose to be average".

    That's fine if that's really your choice; but finding out you have to wear glasses can also be a defining moment where you can discover more about who you are, and bring out (and literally stand behind) aspects that were previously hidden. I encourage you to take a risk and say something boldly with your eyewear. You may find that you receive a lot of positive response to it. Decorating or augmenting the face and especially the eyes is one of the most direct assertions possible with your attire.

    If you want to discuss possibilities, feel free to post again, with examples. We can talk about the qualities and the potential benefits and drawbacks of certain designs.
