    I can't put the pieces together...

    Make a thousand mistakes, and learn from your mistakes. That's how you will develop mastery.

    If you want more specific advice, check my recent post in another thread.

    I'd venture that there's a gap for you between visualization and outcome, which would indicate both that you need to develop your eye and ability to envision, and that you need to try more â€” try on more pieces in the store, bring home more pieces to try with your wardrobe (and return what you don't use), try more combinations with what you already own (you can do this quickly by laying them out on the bed first), and be more adventurous in your day-to-day outfits. Start crossing those thousand mistakes off your list.

    As to developing your eye for style, that's a very broad and gradual process that requires you to be interested and involved with your life of the senses. Become aware of colors, patterns, textures, rhythms, energies in all things, especially in art and culture. Be more involved in how things make you feel. Take interest in trends, not in order to be trendy, but to understand better how the human world moves and how style unfolds in the world.

    As to developing your ability to envision, start playing around with outfits. Create a scrapbook or lookbook and consider re-combining the pieces in the images you collect. Use your bed to lay out pieces together and quickly see the potential of new combinations (then check the mirror when you think you have something; always try it on and check the mirror or a photo). You can even use one of the style or fashion apps available for smartphones, where you photograph your wardrobe and then can mix and match pieces and save records of outfits that work.
