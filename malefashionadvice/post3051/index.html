    Accidentally shrunk a wool sweater in the wash. Any tips on stretching it back to normal? Or should I just give it to my girlfriend...

    You may be able to get it back to near its original shape; but the quality of the wool will likely be different because the fibers will have separated more.

    First of all; don't throw woolen items in the washer or dryer. Wash them gently by hand, soak up excess water by pressing them inside a towel, then lay them out on a dry towel to dry (don't hang-dry).

    To stretch the sweater back, first soak it in cool water. Some sources say to use soap to help the fibers open; but if you do, make sure that it is wool-friendly soap in a very dilute solution, and certainly not detergent or a strong soapy mix.

    After soaking well, press excess water out of the sweater and lay it onto a dry towel. A cheap wool blanket is also okay for this.

    It will help if you have an outline of a sweater that fits or some way of judging the proper dimensions for length and width of sleeves and body. Begin to pull the sweater out to those dimensions. Coax it little by little to avoid stretching it into a weird shape or pulling it too far in a certain dimension.

    Once it is stretched out to correct form, leave it to dry. You might check in a couple of hours to see if it is remaining in the proper form or if you need to pull it out a bit more.

    You might find that you will sacrifice some width in order to achieve length, though it is possible to pull sleeves and body out somewhat too. You may also find that the sweater is not as smooth as before, as some of the fibers will likely pull loose or stretch out of the twist of the yarn.

    Good luck.
