    What to wear over a shirt?

    Jumpers' -- crewneck or v-neck sweaters -- aren't necessarily preppy. If by that you mean that you want a more easy, casual, or rugged image, you can go with more layering, both for the style of it and for warmth.

    A white t-shirt or solid henley (or both) peeking out from under a casual button-up (or fully exposed by an unbuttoned casual shirt) can be further layered with a hoodie on top. A jean jacket can also still be worn under many winter coats; so that could replace the hoodie or used as an optional layer on top of it.

    Here's a henley, chambray button-up, and field jacket in a confident, casual look. Henleys can be layered over other henleys.

    Be aware that hoodies don't have to be slouchy and sloppy. Thin and slim hoodies are available that will bump up your style while avoiding 'prep'; check Vince and J Crew.

    Polos can be layered under knits and dress shirts; and dress shirts can be layered under long-sleeve polo sweaters. When using polos and long-sleeved knits, stay away from broad horizontal rugby stripes and collegiate striping on arms and borders -- they are all at times part of the prep look vocabulary.

    Zip cardigans and track suit jackets (disregard the Justin Bieber 'do) or jackets inspired by that look can also avoid the 'buttoned-down', conservative look of jumpers and cardigans, which may be the 'preppy' quality you mention. High-end designers often have a track suit line. Just don't wear the track pants unless you're going for a hip-hop look, in which case you can add wrap-around sunglasses and a driver hat.

    In this example, the mustache, bottle hooked on belt, and picnic basket (!) are either weird or in-your-face daring, depending on your outlook; but the basic outfit is excellent.

    Even shiny nylon windbreakers, which alone might seem cheap, can do well in the right outfit because their sheen provides a nice contrast to soft cottons and other textures.

    You may want to try some sweaters, though, as they're pretty much a staple of the wardrobe. A basic crew or vee can be very versatile, not to mention generously warm. Just pair them with the right supporting cast of clothing: jeans; wider belts with heavier buckles; sneakers or chunkier shoes or boots; and so on. Without overdoing accessories, something along the lines of the following can also shift the look from preppy/conservative to more earthy, masculine, or relaxed: large casual watch; thick leather wristband; leather string necklace; workboots, construction boots, hiking boots, etc.

    Counterintuitively, you can check the website of that bastion of preppiness, Ralph Lauren, for ideas on how to layer and not look preppy. For example, here you can back off from the rad cargo pants and tucking into the boots, and you'll have a fairly masculine look. To remove the least hint of prep, also tone down the plaid dress shirt or substitute with henley or hoodie.

    The type and construction of sweater can also change the look. Rather than smooth fine-knit sweaters (what they call 'layering sweaters'), you can look for chunkier and more textured knits, contrast borders, natural wood or bone buttons on cardigans and half-necks, and patterns like marled/tweed and stripe.
