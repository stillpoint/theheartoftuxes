    Is buttoned really better?

    If the jacket actually fit, yes, buttoned would be better. In this case, it's trying to pick between two bad examples.

    The two main issues with unbuttoning a suit jacket or sport jacket are that (1) the line of the jacket is ruined and (2) the shape and amount of exposed shirt changes.

    A sport jacket has a very intricate geometry that is designed to make you look your best. It gives you the appearance of straight, wide shoulders and a fit, narrowed waist. It cleans up the profile with crisp lines and unified texture. It aims the attention upward and gives a sense of regal posture with the energy moving up and out. It frames and brings focus to the face, and gives it a vertical foundation of color or pattern.

    (1) Line of the jacket: When the jacket is undone, the line of the waist is usually lost, and the athletic proportions that come from the difference in width between shoulders and waist disappear. Rather than a smooth and crisp body profile, the shape becomes square and amorphous -- not something we see as an ideal body shape.

    (2) Shape of the exposed shirt: What also happens when the jacket is undone is that the crisply angled vee under the face is lost. The lapels lose their influence as guides for the eye; the up-and-out quality of posture and energy is lost somewhat. And the shape of exposed shirt goes from something clean and organized, opening to the hilight of the whole outfit -- the face -- to a shape that is lax and boring and indistinct. When you think of energy moving upward and outward from a point at the midsection, as in the classic vee gorge of a suit, it's like fireworks. When you think of a vague column, as with the outline when the jacket's open, the effect is slumpy and lacks energy. It also can expose too much of a strong color or pattern; check your pictures: the buttoned jacket shows lines of pattern organized by increasing width; the unbuttoned jacket shows many more lines, but they're all more or less the same length -- it's both monotonous and too eye-catching.

    How to do it: The way to do an unbuttoned jacket is with one that is either very well structured and tailored, so that the waist shape is maintained, or with an unstructured jacket that has some flow to it. Both should be in the context of a very casual outfit; and in either case, you need to be more careful about the shape, color, and pattern of exposed (or over-exposed?) shirt underneath.

    For sharp, dressy outfits, always button up unless sitting.
