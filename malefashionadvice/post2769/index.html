    Sock-less desert boots?

    I prefer to distinguish between Chukka Boots, which many people also call 'Desert Boots', and these kinds of Desert Boots.

    You have to try the boot to see if it rubs at all on the achilles tendon. Multiply by 10,000 steps, and you'll know if -- comfort-wise -- it's an option for you.

    In terms of style, you can definitely wear desert boots without socks. When worn with shorts, it may even look better than the neck of a crew sock sticking out of the boot. (Another option would be to flip or roll the crew sock down to the top of the boot.)

    Please, though, wear a more stylish short. I checked out the Half Moon Shorts; and if ever there was a 'dad short' they's it. Not good. Find shorts with a slimmer fit (at least not baggy) that end just above the knee, have no elastic at the waist, and also have no cargo pockets. In addition, get them in a fabric that drapes better than canvas -- you'll be cooler as well.

    Consider sockless desert boots with light pants as well. Also here.

    Slim shorts rolled just above the knee; with desert boots and khaki shirt. This is a good look for desert boots; olive, brown, or other earth-tone shorts would be even better.

    Edit: I stand corrected. According to Wikipedia, boots known as Desert Boots can be very similar to Chukkas: "The name chukka comes from the game of polo.Chukka boots are similar to desert boots, worn by British forces in the Western Desert Campaign of World War II. Desert boots are looser at the ankle than chukka boots and have a crepe sole."
