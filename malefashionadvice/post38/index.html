    What is a good substitue for a hoody?

    I agree with others that even a casual blazer is going to be quite far from a hoodie in its level of formality — maybe not an everyday item at your age.

    The closest item to a hoodie will probably be a track jacket. It's just a fraction of a step up in sharpness, though, which may or may not be good enough for you. If you do go for a track jacket, seek one in a plain design with minimal branding (no big logos or company names).

    Hoodies are more of a mid-layer that sometimes become outer layers, and in this they're similar to cardigans. Depending on how preppy you want to go, a varsity cardigan can bring some youthfulness to a piece that is sometimes regarded as a 'grandad sweater' but is now very commonly worn by young people (and is an essential piece in hipster style).

    A shawl-neck cardigan is especially attractive, and is almost an essential item because of its versatility and cozy look. If you were looking at that blazer in order to upgrade your look, a shawl-neck sweater can do that but stay comfortable and laid-back. That combination of qualities — sharp but laid-back — can be alluring. Shawl-neck sweaters come in a wide variety of thicknesses (from summer-weight jersey to chunky cable knit) and a wide variety of patterns.

    Back to outer layers: If you really want a blazer as an everyday item — that is, if you're determined to dress at a higher, more formal level than almost all of your peers — then a less stuffy option may be a knit blazer, in jersey or sweater knit material. Dedication to items like this as everyday wear may mean an overhaul of your wardrobe. Sportier versions are available, like this one, similar to the varsity cardigan linked above.

    But it doesn't sound like that is reflective of your lifestyle. Your clothes should support your actual life, not a fantasy. You can dress for what you want to become; but it should still make sense according to where you're coming from. This is very important. Your clothes should help you to become more of who you are and to express that more completely.

    So you might want to look at pieces like a field jacket, a waxed cotton moto (motorcycle) jacket, a cotton bomber, or a Harrington jacket. Heck, even a jean jacket might do as an everyday piece. But you must not key on a single item of clothing and try to build an image around it. It works the other way: first understand your own style — your own personal qualities and your actual lifestyle — and let your wardrobe grow according to that understanding.

    The items I mentioned all have quite different qualities. Elegant vs. rough, refined vs. rugged, sporty or earthy or urban, laid-back or put-together. The more you understand your own qualities, the better you will be able to choose between possible purchases, and the more your clothes will suit you and project your image clearly into the world.

    Whatever you buy, make sure it fits very well. This will set you apart, whereas buying a fancy piece to show off will make you stick out. Also determine ahead of time your best colors, and the color and shade of jacket that will go best with the other clothing you're going to be wearing. You'll want to include some contrast (dark pants: light jacket; light pants: dark jacket) and some color without limiting the combinations you can create.
