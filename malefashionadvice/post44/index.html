    Thoughts on materialism?

    Yes! Why buy this clothing and not that? Why do anything? Why bother to eat and breathe?

    It's not the clothes themselves that are the problem, it's the clinging, manipulating mind. This mind doesn't understand what is of true value.

    Alan Watts said that in fact the modern world is generally not materialistic. If we were materialistic, we would honor and respect our material things much more than we do. We would take loving care of our shirts, and enjoy every aspect of them — their fineness and their imperfection. If we were materialists, he says, we would become intimate with material possessions and be fulfilled by them.

    But most of us treat our possessions as we treat our days: with dullness, attachment, and an almost arrogant disdain. We clutch at what we like, push away what we don't like, and ignore what's neutral to us. I think this comes from our ignorance about life itself. Although we know theoretically that we will die, that we must die, we go about our days as if we will live forever. We treat our days and everything in them as disposable and valueless.

    This all comes from our ignorance about life. We don't know who we really are. We are not our possessions or relationships or thoughts or body. We are not the idea "spirit" or "soul". But we have not grasped who we really are.

    In a moment when you see who you really are, then everything becomes equal. There is no high and low, no good and bad. Everything is just like this. Then you can have real engagement with your days and the objects and beings and events that populate them. One by one, each thing has its place and its value. One by one, each thing is complete.

    I think "style" starts within you. Who you are is already radiant. It's just a question of whether you reveal that or whether you cover it up with masks and cloud it with opinions. Who you are unfolds through everything you contact. It ripples out like circles from a pebble dropped in a pond.

    The forms will pass away. How much we pay for clothing doesn't matter. Sometimes our thinking makes it seem to matter, but in essence it doesn't matter. What matters is the central point that makes all of those ripples — the you behind the clothes and body and thinking.

    If you don't understand your true self, then all clothing is wasteful. If you understand yourself and your life, then all clothing is equal, and you can use it for your true purpose.

    I like what Jesus said in the sermon on the mount: >And why worry about your clothing? Look at the lilies of the field and how they grow. They don't work or make their clothing, and yet Solomon in all his glory was not dressed as beautifully as they are.

    When your outer form lets your true self shine through, that is real beauty. I think there are countless ways to celebrate this.
