    Looking for advice regarding a tentative look for my buddy's birthday at a swanky hotel.

    No, my friend, that sounds bad. I think if you wear that outfit it will mark you as never having been to a swanky event.

    Black suit and black shirt is overdoing it; and the whole all-black thing is quite old by now. Adding a black vest is yet again too much. The hot pink tie is just an act of desperation by itself, much less against a sea of black. It brings to mind the Famous People Players Theater.

    A light tie on a dark shirt is rarely classy; a very bright tie on a black shirt is worse â€” it is a cheap look. White or partially white shoes with a black suit will also be far too contrasty and eye-catching.

    It seems that you are looking for gimmicks because you may not really have an idea of how to put together an outfit that will suit you, and be comfortable, and look great. The most important point here is that you are attending the party, and the focus should be on you rather than on your clothes. Your outfit should help to show you, the person, in the best light. If you go for eye-catching gimmicks, not only will it look try-hard and cheap, it will focus the attention on the clothing and take it away from you.

    So you should first realize that you are a complex being worthy of respect in your own right, and that you have every right to attend and enjoy a swanky rooftop party. You don't need to drum up a manufactured image with your outfit.

    A much more elegant style will come from the integrity of the outfit than from any one showpiece item. When all the pieces fit your body well, and when they fit in with each other, and with the occasion, and with your character, then there is a sense of completion and unity that is very attractive.

    A suit is possible for a chic party, but you must be comfortable in it and it must fit perfectly and be of a youthful, contemporary style. If it doesn't fit or if it's in an old style it will look more conservative or childish than badass.

    "Cocktail wear" and dress for chic evening parties does tend to be dark; but that doesn't have to mean black. The focus is on deep, rich color, lush texture (like satin and velvet), sleek line, and fit or drape that emphasizes physicality and even sexuality. (That's not the same as being overtly sexual, which is another kind of cheap gimmick.)

    Is your suit perfectly fitted and in a contemporary style? What other shirt options do you have? What other sharp clothing options do you have in general?

    Bringing in color doesn't have to be (and shouldn't be) as blatant and in-your-face as hot pink on black. Much more elegant would be midnight blue, burgundy, aubergine, plum, and other deep evening colors. The shirt can be in one of these deep colors, or in a light buff color, or even white. Lose the tie, open the shirt a couple of buttons, add a dark burgundy pocket square, and now we're starting to cook.

    A non-black suit is also viable. Shoes in any case should be elegant, not showy. Remember, the more sophisticated impact is made when the whole outfit works well together and suits your body and personality.
