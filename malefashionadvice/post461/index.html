    I never liked wearing watches and have never really considered one. Do you guys have any links that might change my mind?

    Riding the subway the other day, I witnessed how vivid an effect a watch can have on one's look. I saw a guy in a very basic, even plain, outfit -- chinos and a nondescript solid short sleeve button-up -- and he looked great... because he was wearing a watch. To tell the truth, it didn't matter all that much what kind of watch. I didn't notice the brand of watch, but it had a steel bracelet, and looked like a sport/diver/driver kind of watch. The point is that without the watch, the outfit was plain and very boring. Clearly, the guy was very comfortable in his clothing, not putting on a show for anyone, not dressing to impress; but the simple addition of a watch added a flavor of elegance and flair. From boring and drab to comfortably elegant: a small effort for a big difference.

    In fact, I think it may be the simple outfits that benefit the most from the addition of a watch. In an outfit that employs several other pieces of flair, the watch's glint of glass, mechanics, and leather or steel on the wrist is just one more point of attention among many, although it may provide a supporting statement for the entire look. But in a simple outfit, that focal point on the wrist can really stand out and make a defining statement about the wearer and the look they're projecting. For instance: precision (chronograph); clarity (clean watch face & steel bracelet); outdoorsy confidence (field watch & leather band); toughness (military or chunky design); verve and athletic activity (sport watch); artistry and elegance (slim casing and vintage design); wealth (gold or other eye-catching features); and so on.
