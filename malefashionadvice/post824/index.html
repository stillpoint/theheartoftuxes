    Thinking of buying this, what do you guys think?

    This jersey knit blazer is more on the casual side, but will still dress up a casual outfit. I'd wear it with bottom button open, or both bottom and top open.

    I was thinking that, although the OP's linked jacket is fine, something more midway between a blazer and cardigan could be more versatile. In fact, a shawl-collar cardigan is a classic foundation piece that will approximate the jersey blazer, but do more work in other looks. It can be worn with jeans and a t-shirt, or up the next level to khakis and casual button-up, or up another level to dress chinos, dress shirt, and knit tie. It's all in how you wear it, of course; keep some buttons open unless going for hipster nerd.

    This is a look like the OP was describing: casual pants and a t-shirt, with the jacket thrown on over top. Looks good. Simple. And simplicity can attest to confidence.

    Another way to go is to take it to a slightly more dressy level. Here the knit has more of a blazer look, though the jersey knit will always keep it within the casual realm. (Also, it doesn't fit the model.)

    The unstructured blazer, in woven (non-padded) fabric rather than knit, is another viable piece on the dressy end of comfortable/casual.

    What is one hoping to achieve; what qualities is one hoping to project? Any of the options is possible; some more comfortable-looking, some visibly making the effort to dress up. Cardigans will in general, I think, cover the bases fairly well at both ends of the casual scale.

    Now that I think about it, DrJWilson is right; the more contemporary, trendy cut of the OP's linked jacket is good for a young guy wanting to jazz it up. And the color and style are simple enough that it won't push anyone's buttons or become outré in a year or two.
