    Me in Russia. Am I doing it right?

    If you merely want validation, say so in the title of your post. If you are actually looking for Male Fashion Advice, consider letting go of defensive reactions and taking in what jdbee and others are saying.

    "I like" and "I don't like" are important considerations, but they don't necessarily have anything to do with what others see when they look at your attire. For the most part, people here are offering advice in a helpful, not spiteful, spirit.

    In the first pic:

    • Running shoe style is the lowest form of footwear, though those are better than some monstrosities. Still, if you are at all concerned with bringing your outfits up a notch you'll consider switching to a more classy shoe.

    • Exposed socks with shorts frequently appear juvenile, and this is especially true with white athletic socks.

    • The shorts are too baggy, too long, and cargo style (and bulky at that) â€” three strikes against them. Stretch your comfort zone and actually try some shorter, better fitting alternatives; unless you're determined to stagnate in your own opinions, don't be complacent about seeking out change. Your shorts or pants don't have to be skinny, but you definitely have a lot of room to work with in bringing the fit closer in. The best fit for you may be something that is tight to the upper thigh and falls straight from there, without the extra folds of fabric and definitely without the extra length and pockets that stick out, which both make the shorts appear more massive than necessary.

    • All the brown is a bit matchy-matchy. Not terrible, and better than clashing, but one would look forward to more sophisticated matching with the use of different hues, shades, and patterns.

    • The ivy cap / flat cap is not bad on its own, but it is out of place in the outfit. For one, it is small and flat, making it and your head appear smaller in relation to the larger solid masses of fabric below. (This problem with proportion is another reason why more trim shorts would help.) It is also of a very different style than the rest of the pieces. A flat cap has a cheeky, sporty, and slightly ethnic feel to it, whereas the rest of the outfit is boyish, or one could say immature.

    In the second pic:

    • We can't see your shoes.

    • We can't see the full length of your jeans, so we don't have a good take on their cut and fit. They aren't boot cut are they? From what I see, they seem a bit loose but not far off from a good fit. The fade is low-class and, again, a bit juvenile â€” even if you do see similar jeans worn by many people. The fit and fade are okay only for a very casual knock-about jean; not so much for out in public. If you want to look like you care about your appearance, consider a darker wash and closer fit.

    • The light shirt provides a contrast with the jeans, which is good. Actually, in both outfits you have included contrast; and that bodes well for the development of your sense of style. The light color of the shirt is just not so interesting, and in combination with the humdrum faded jeans and with your light skin tone it is washed out. The shirt color makes you recede and look less yourself. A different color and possibly pattern could bring more vibrancy, interplay, and finesse to your appearance.

    • Jdbee caught the fact that your collars are spreading out almost as far as they possibly can, which contributes to the sloppiness of the look. Button-down collars would help that. Your shirt sleeves are rolled in a very rough way, adding to the mess. If you are going to commit to such a laid-back, ultra-casual way then don't think that the presence of a hat or sunglasses will somehow make you look sharp or stylish. Tucking and untucking the shirt should also usually follow the level of formality present in the outfit â€” sharp: tuck; very casual: untuck.

    • The trilby hat comes off as a bit dorky, both in its own right and in combination with the rest of the outfit.
