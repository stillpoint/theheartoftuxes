    Can pale guys wear white?

    The main thing is how the colors worn near your face look. With pants, you can do whatever works for your stylistically.

    Style is all cause-and-effect. Cut, color, pattern, texture, features, and symbolism or cultural reference all have particular effects on the viewer. Some colors will make you look healthier, some will make you look pale, or very pink, or even ill. And certain other hues that don't match a skin tone will tend to take center stage, so it becomes all about the item of clothing, and the person recedes into the background.

    All of these effects are neither good nor bad. What do you want? Some guys consciously choose to look pale and drawn, as part of an aloof goth or hipster style. Others want so much to draw attention that they will showcase almost clownlike color combinations even if it means they disappear behind them.

    Back to your question, white tends to be problematic for many guys; and if you are very pale you may do better with light blues as your central shirt colors. But wearing white pants still allows you to choose flattering colors to wear next to your face.

    If the white jeans suit you in other ways, no reason — color-wise — not to buy them.
