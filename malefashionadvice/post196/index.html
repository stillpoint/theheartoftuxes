    What goes with white shirt + black pants + black shoes?

    All black with a white shirt is very serious. It has no 'in between', only the polar opposites of shade, without any color. Because of its serious and formal appearance, the combination has been used for waiters' uniforms and funeral outfits. Because of its intensity, it has been used in very slick evening outfits. None of those suit your situation.

    A third, in-between shade of grey brings another element into the mix; so there's some interplay, some interest, some possibility. The lighter color also brightens the mood somewhat. It's unfortunate that your school uniform uses black pants, since they are going to be very contrasty with most of what you wear above; and that's the case with the lighter grey. It's just a little too light to be a perfect match, but much better than the black cardigan.

    Now, if you could introduce some color!! Even just the slightest hint of brown in a grey cardigan -- a taupe, anywhere from dark-mid to light -- would be so much more interesting. A deep burgundy would still be very classy with the black, but would have a lot more passion and warmth to it. A light blue would be lighthearted. A mid green would express a lot of life (as in natural landscapes).

    If you're stuck with no color, grey is the much better choice.

    As to fit, the cardigans look like they're around the proper length, but look wide in the shoulders and maybe long in the arms. The grey looks better in fit; the black emphasizes slouchiness.

    And, yes, please let your chest float up and out. No need to push, but also no need to hide. Physical activity will help put some movement and tone into your posture.
