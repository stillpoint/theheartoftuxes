    Cardigans. Which colors to pick, which to stay away from. Help me out MFA!

    What kind of style do you like; what do you look like; and what clothing would you like the cardigans to go with?

    You'll generally want to avoid having all of one color or all of one level of darkness in your outfit. Some contrast between layers is usually advised. One exception is if you want to go for monochromatic outfits. These are strong options if your coloration is very muted, in which case you shouldn't wear strong colors that will overpower your skin and hair tone. Monochromatic outfits are not all one color, but varied shades and closely related hues of one color (usually a neutral).

    If you are truly buying your first cardigan, follow the advice of previous posters and go with basic solids. The most adaptable neutral colors are brown and grey, followed by navy blue, muted earth tones like olive, and beige/taupe (light grey-brown). Not all browns, greens, or beiges will look good on all people. Medium to light blues tend to suit many skin tones.

    Depending on your wardrobe and your own coloration, black can also be an option. It will offer more sharp, formal options than other colors (because of black's depth and also the contrast achievable with white); but it will be harder to combine in casual outfits because it won't match as well with many colors.

    If you already have many basic solids in your shirts and pants, you may want a more colorful or patterned cardigan, to provide some oomph in your outfits. Choose a color that makes you look great, not one that just looks great by itself. Just know that a non-neutral color is more eye-catching; and so re-wearing will register more easily among the people you hang out with -- they'll start to think "he's worn that already" sooner than if you wore a sweater that blends in more. And if buying a patterned sweater, consider carefully the patterns that you may want to wear with it, in your shirts, ties, coats, scarves, etc. (Or which patterns may not be possible to wear with it.)

    If you want something basic and versatile, yet hope for more interest than a mere plain solid, your best bet is to buy a very textured but neutral sweater like a fisherman knit or cable knit cardigan. The neutral color will go well in many combinations, but the texture will add interest and a 'cozy' factor (which women tend to like on a man, by the way).

    Without seeing you and without knowing your needs, it's hard to give definitive advice. If it fits with the situations you'll be entering most often, a thick shawl-neck cardigan in a neutral color can be your absolute no-brainer, go-to sweater.
