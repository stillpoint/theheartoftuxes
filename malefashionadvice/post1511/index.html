    Are my jeans looking a bit ridiculous?

    You're welcome.

    I hear that you mainly want feedback about the jeans. The question in your thread title, though, always has to be taken in context of the rest of the outfit and in context of your own character and activity and the situation in which you will wear the piece. Jeans that look ridiculous in one situation may look great in another, or with different clothing, or worn by a different person.

    Back to the jeans, though. Almost all jeans will stretch as you wear them. It's almost essential to buy jeans that feel too tight, as they will usually loosen up as you wear them and launder them. A knowledgeable salesperson should know how much a particular brand is expected to stretch.

    On occasion, a hot washing followed by drying in a hot dryer will shrink the jeans again; but then they will likely stretch back out again with wear. In addition, hot washing and drying will degrade the cotton much faster than the occasional cold wash or rinse and hang-to-dry. In any case, they are too long; and that can be corrected.

    Yeah, I'm not sure about the jacket myself now that I see the close-ups. At a distance it looks like a suit jacket; but the close-ups look like it's a knit (stretchy sweater-like or track-jacket-like material). Is that correct? If it is a knit, it needs to be more tailored so it will hug the body a bit; and in fact it may be too big to be well tailored to your frame.

    Since we don't see the whole jacket we can't see the degree of structure involved (more structure is more formal); but it does look like it lies very smoothly, which contributes to a sharper, more formal range. Knit blazers and 'French Jersey' cardigans were popular in the past year or so. They can be worn in a fairly sharp style. If with jeans, probably something more fitted and less faded. Maybe not with a second knit underneath; but if so, then probably only with a thinner, finer v-neck.
