    title: Wing Collars: Tie or Bow Tie? summary: 1.0 authors: TheHeartofTuxes date: 2012-01-24 00:00:24 some_url:

    The main thing in dressing to match a date's dress is to avoid clashing and to avoid matching too closely (what they call "matchy-matchy"). Full-on matching is juvenile, as is identically matching any combination of tie/vest/cummerbund/pocket square. It's cheap, classless, and amateurish. But since it's a widespread tradition among young guys who don't know any better (and may not have dressed up for an event in their lives), it sometimes creates its own pocket of acceptability -- the dress code becomes the local custom rather than what is considered proper in the rest of the world.

    You have to determine of you're going by proper, traditional dress code or by local rules. But if you want the higher road, the correct outfit is black dinner jacket, pants, shoes, tie, and waistcoat or cummerbund, and white shirt. In the modern black tie outfit, sometimes neither waistcoat nor cummerbund are worn. The only pocket square that a dinner jacket would take, if any, would be white or ivory. I can't imagine the traditional black tie outfit ever clashing with anything your date might wear.

    If you discover that the local tradition is for tuxedos to match the date's dress (gag), try to do it subtly, matching the color with cuff links or bow tie (gag). She can also pick up the black of your tuxedo in an accessory like a purse or scarf.

    In 'informal' (non-black-tie, but still dressy) outfits, if you're wearing a pocket square it should not be the same color or pattern as the tie; it should be completely different. Perhaps you can pick up a minor color from the tie or socks or cufflinks; or perhaps the pocket square will be an independent flair. Just as within your own outfit, being too matchy-matchy between your outfit and your date's is also immature and unsophisticated. It makes you look like a team in costume rather than elegant individuals accompanying each other. And yet, again, there are so many who consider the 'team costume' the pinnacle of romance that the practice wins out in some circles. Check as to what the local custom is.

    The correct way is to ask your date what she's planning to wear, so that you can dress to present her in her best light. It will also give you the necessary information to pass on to the florist so you can have a corsage made. (Order several days to a couple of weeks ahead of time, and pick it up on the day of the event; don't wait to the last minute.) Unless she expresses a specific request, just try not to clash or match too much: if she's wearing mostly black, don't wear mostly brown; if she's wearing pink, don't wear burgundy; and so on. (And note that you don't have to bow to every request a girl makes. You can negotiate some things and decide some things for yourself. Politeness, graciousness, and chivalry don't mean you only ever say 'yes'.)
