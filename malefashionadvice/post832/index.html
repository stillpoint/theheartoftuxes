    Any advice for a dude just looking for a good haircut?

    Finding a good stylist is a bit like finding a good tailor. You have to check them out over time to see how well they listen, how you connect on the vision you have, whether they give good alternative advice or merely respond robotically, and of course their actual skill in cutting and the quality of the styling you receive. Some things will be deal-breakers after the first visit: the stylist does something drastic without asking, or goes against your instructions, etc. Other things may take some time, such as conveying the idea that you have in mind.

    Bring pictures, give immediate feedback (call or visit the next day to say "great job" or "I actually wanted it more textured over here" or whatever), and be open to suggestions from the stylist and from others (especially suggestions from stylish girlfriends). I've brought multiple photos showing the style I'm aiming at, as well as photos of myself wearing previous styles, so the stylist can have an idea of how my hair behaves and possible alternatives. Even if you can't find an exact picture, bring something in so that you have a jumping-off point for discussion -- "I like the sides here, but the top is too shaggy in the photo", etc.

    If you find a stylist who is skilled, is knowledgeable about your type of hair, and listens well, that's a treasure.

    Do make sure that you sit down to have a consultation before they touch your hair. It's no good to talk about how your hair falls or where the cowlick is after they've already given you a shampoo. Make sure you're on the same page before you even commit to letting them do any work. I've found it best to state clearly what I want, but also to actually ask for other suggestions. Sometimes you won't like the style sense of the person doing your hair; but often they will give very good suggestions, and may see something that you don't. After all, they see so many examples of good and bad haircuts, it could pay off to make use of their expertise.

    Be ready to pay. The fact that a salon or stylist is expensive doesn't necessarily mean they're good; but the fact that a salon or stylist is very inexpensive almost always means they're not so good. As with buying shoes, this is one area where more often than not you get what you pay for.

    Also ask for instructions on how to maintain the hairstyle. Unless you know how to prepare your hair and use product, the result will often look quite different from when you first left the salon. Over time you'll learn the cuts, techniques, and products that suit your hair and look the best.

    As to Nate's style in Uncharted, it'll probably require fairly thick hair as well as the use of product (and even possibly two products at once, like mousse and pomade).
