    Which boot is the most versatile?

    With jeans and beige or grey cords, the most versatile color of boot will be a neutral that contrasts, like a dark brown, chestnut brown, or a tan. While the Timberland you linked to is a bit of a construction boot hybrid, the color is not bad for many combinations.

    Dark brown or chestnut brown (slightly lighter than chocolate, and with some red in it) will give you very nice combinations with mid-to-light shades of pants, and will look fine with any shade of blue jeans. Tan will combine nicely with darker shades of pants, and will also look good with jeans. One might think that mid-grey is a safe choice because it's very neutral (the most neutral possible); but the problem is that it lacks the liveliness of a color, and in addition will just blend in with mid-shades of pants. Grey shoes may contribute to a blah look if they are too close to the color of the pants you'll be wearing. In the context of a monotone or close-monotone outfit, though, grey can be extremely elegant. Those Supra Boots, however, are not elegant.

    The Supras don't fit with the style of clothing you say you usually wear. Something in leather would be far better looking; and if it's also something well-made you will be spending your money well, not having to buy new boots every year.

    The reason certain footwear becomes classic or even clich√© is that its design and construction work so well for many people, and stand the test of time. The suggestions that are repeated over and over on MFA are indeed well-tested standards that will serve you well. If you're looking for something more 'out there', that's your prerogative; but if you're looking for something to form a foundation of numerous outfits, look to the classics -- the Katahdins and Red Wings and Clarks Desert Boots already mentioned here.

    For sheer versatility, don't buy winter boots. If you can fit a thick wool sock in standard boots, it will do for most weather; but winter boots are limited to (parts of) a single season.
