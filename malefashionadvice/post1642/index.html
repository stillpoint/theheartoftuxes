    Does this unbuttoned shirt work?

    > This is one of those cases where the simple answer is "never wear a shirt unbuttoned as a jacket" and the real answer is "only if the rest of your outfit is perfect and you have the right attitude."

    I'm inclined to agree with ehsu here. I'd like to see the whole outfit and body, to get a better sense of the proportions and relationships between the pieces, and of how you wear them. I'd also like to see pictures of the shirt buttoned closed and fully opened in a couple of different postures. Usually we ask for full-body, hands by sides poses; but since opening a shirt makes it into a very drapey, flowing piece, I'd want to get a sense of how the drape and flow behave. As it stands, you don't look bad in that picture.

    The major reason why you keep a shirt buttoned is the same reason you keep a sportjacket or blazer buttoned: it allows the piece to do the work of creating a good body line, and avoids misshapen and messy profiles.

    In a very casual outfit, the open shirt is much more in order than an open jacket; the relaxed drape can contribute to a laid-back devil-may-care look. The very real risk, though, is that it will just look sloppy and immature. The risk is mitigated if you (A) have an athletic body shape that is so well-defined it comes through the amorphous shapes created by the loose shirt, or (B) are wearing a shirt that is very well fitted, and maintains a good body shape even as it flows. This is something you'll have to check yourself in real time (or, perhaps an MFA first, post a video to show the movement of the piece).

    David Beckham plays around with untucking and unbuttoning in very casual situations; but he has the body for it, and I've never seen the shirts fully opened. Thing is, I don't know many guys who can pull off the quarter-tuck.
