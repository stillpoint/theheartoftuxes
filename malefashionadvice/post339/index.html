    Am I the only one that would not advise wearing colored chinos?

    White and black only? Pardon my gut response, but yuck. How do you make that work?

    What about wearing color elsewhere in your outfits? Do you object to colored shirts, sweaters, jackets, footwear, accessories; or is it just the chinos that rankle? Not being argumentative, just wondering.

    Brightly or deeply colored chinos (there's an important difference!) may look odd to me, depending on how they're worn; but they may on the other hand look brilliant and celebratory, speaking of a more sophisticated style and a more confident and engaged relationship with life.

    In general, with color and style as with many other fields, I'm more happy to see someone try and fail than not to see them try at all. I'm not saying that this is the case with the OP, but part of the widespread discomfort with color is a much broader and deeper issue with confidence in style: as a society, we have not valued and transmitted true confidence and expressivity, but rather have substituted shortcuts and fakery. As a result, many guys don't know how to express the full breadth of their personalities, and get stuck in a narrow range of style or even react negatively to the slightest acts of expression. Because we haven't been instilled with (and haven't ourselves earned) a full and stable sense of self, arrogance and confidence are often confused.

    Back to colored chinos:

    • There are many colors that are much more easy-going and versatile than black or white

    • Expanding that palette just a bit, we get colors with more character that can still be worn fairly conservatively

    • A burgundy chino and denim combo is not too far out there for most

    • 'Merlot' is just a bit bolder, but works for some

    • Pale yellow, in the context of this summer outfit, shouldn't be too objectionable. Swap out the belt and the outfit is toned down even more.

    • Nantucket Reds are decidedly preppie, but classic; and if you mingle in the circles where they're prominent they may become as natural to your eye as jeans are in other circles

    • Bright colors: bold, fun, celebratory... or cheesy and garish? Context makes all the difference: who are you, where are you coming from, and where are you headed?
