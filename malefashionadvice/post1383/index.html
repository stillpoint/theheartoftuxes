    Jacket fit... am I crazy?

    Whether or not you think you're "cut out for men's fashion", there will likely be times when you need to wear a suit, for the sake of decorum or for the sake of your personal advancement. It's usually part of being a mature male. So if you are going to be wearing a suit anyway, you may want to be as physically and psychically comfortable as possible; and you may want to increase your chances of looking great rather than looking like you're out of your element. If that's so, it means trying â€” looking into it more deeply.

    If, on the other hand, you avoid all situations where a suit is necessary, that would free you from having to deal with the issue; but it would also be an avoidance and a limit to your greater freedom. Quite often, doing the thing that is uncomfortable on the outside gives you much more satisfaction and comfort on the inside. Men in particular need to constantly challenge their previous limits, venture out of their comfort zones, and avoid falling into set patterns of behavior.

    I am not saying that wearing a suit is either good or bad. I'm not the cheerleader for wearing a suit. I'm just offering the reality of the situation, which is that there will be some restriction of mobility due to the way suits are constructed. The structure that creates sharp lines, smooth unified surfaces, athletic form, and attractive proportions is a structure that places some limitation on movement.

    We don't know yet whether your experience trying on suits indicates an average level of constriction, or whether those suits didn't fit your body well. As I said, depending on your physique it may very well be that you need a different cut of jacket or to have the jacket tailored to provide more room in the back (which is something that can easily be done).

    The right, properly fitted suit or sportjacket is in fact meant to be lived in. It should help you look at ease, confident, and dynamic. Although more constrictive than wearing no jacket, it should still allow for expressive movement and relative comfort in everyday activity.
