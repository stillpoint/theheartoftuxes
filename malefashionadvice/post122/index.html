    Jeans/casual day at work tomorrow - which is best? Criticism welcome

    The first outfit has a nice color up top, and good contrast between top and bottom. It is somewhat plain, though, in that it involves all solids. The shoes are too close in shade to the pants for this look. If you were going for a muted or monotone look, they'd be okay; but as it stands they should live up to the contrast established up top. In addition, the contrast and color established by the belt should be matched by the shoes. Dark brown shoes would be better.

    In the following outfits, I must say that I really like that use of nautical stripe in a polo (and long-sleeve at that) rather than crew neck, and in an interesting departure from the ubiquitous blue stripes.

    The second outfit is a good example of what I was saying about the shoes above. In a muted, monochrome look the lower contrast between shoes and pants works. In addition, the lower contrast and style of belt works with the shoes. This is a nice, unified outfit in terms of color; and the pattern up top adds good rhythm and hence interest. The corduroy is not bad with it, but it's not completely in the genre of boat shoes and nautical stripes; those pieces are summery and outdoorsy, while the cords seem like a nod to autumn. I think that difference in style is more easily overlooked because of the color matching. The only other issue is that because of the monochrome palette this is a very intentional looking outfit; it works, but people will see that you put thought into the outfit, and depending on your character that's not always desirable.

    The change of shoes in the third outfit cures the possibly over-matched monochrome color scheme of the previous outfit. The wine purple is a good combination with the sage green. Here the monochrome of the rest of the outfit is a setup for the pop of color in the shoes, so the belt doesn't have to match: the shoes are the highlight. The sneakers also somewhat ameliorate the previous boating theme; unfortunately, they are too casual.

    The untucked shirt in the fourth outfit is too casual for a work environment; and I don't care how casual others say the dress code could be â€” dress code or not, that kind of look is for lounging around on your off time. Even for casual day, you should present an image that shows you're ready to work.

    The last outfit also has the problem with the shirt being untucked. The jeans are pre-faded and whiskered, which is also too casual for a work environment. If they were a solid dark wash, you could be on to something. There is enough unity between the shoe color and shirt stripe for the outfit to make sense, without looking too matchy-matchy. The (right) jeans also tone down the nautical feel of the shirt, making the stripes less iconic and more about the rhythm of the pattern. Again, there is not a lot of difference in shade between shoes and pants; but it works better here because the hue is different (and with dark wash jeans, there would be even better definition). These shoes are most appropriate for a casual work day; fill in the other details.

    Sneakers and stripes are somewhat boyish; boat shoes and stripes are linked with boating: depending on how casual the dress code is (or in any case), you may not want to appear that young or leisurely, or seem focused on activities outside of work.

    Good fits on all the pieces, good colors, and good quality. Just focus on making the outfit appropriate for the occasion and you're there.
