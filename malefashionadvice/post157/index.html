    Looking for a personal uniform

    If you're going to commit to a single outfit for the foreseeable future, it might as well...

    • reflect who you are, or at least suit you well enough to allow you to feel free and relaxed when you wear it

    • be physically utilitarian and comfortable

    • look fantastic

    You will, then, have to do much of the same work in learning how to put together an outfit that suits you and your situation. You will have to look into your own character and lifestyle, determine the colors and fabrics and styles that look best on you, and consider what works for you both practically and emotionally. If you really refuse to do any of that and simply want someone to tell you what to wear, you can either throw darts at a magazine or ask a mother figure to choose for you.

    To my thinking, a personal uniform should have pieces that are themselves quite versatile, serving a wide range of activities and levels of formality. Unless you are fixed on loving a particular look forever, a balance should be struck between pieces that are so bland you disappear and pieces that are so unique they become grating after a few wears.

    Your idea of blue and white OCBDs and tan duck pants sounds like a good start, but could use some enhancements:

    A good-looking, basic outfit has harmony of color, contrast, and pattern. Light shirts, for instance, call for contrast by using mid-to-dark shakes in the pants. And if we assume the occasional addition of a neutral cardigan or other sweater, at least one or two shirts should be patterned. Dark neutral, light neutral, and pattern create a triad with interesting internal relationships; and the asymmetry of a triad is itself interesting since it includes movement rather than a static two-sided relationship. So some possible combinations:

    • dark colored pants, light patterned shirts, and mid-neutral cardigan and/or jacket
    • light pants, mid-colored and patterned shirts, and dark cardigan or jacket
    • medium neutral pants, light colored shirts, and mid-to-dark patterned pullover and/or jacket

    Let the shoes be a rich, charactered leather that contrasts well with the pants.

    Seek out neutrals that are slightly deeper or more nuanced than usual — instead of grey, look for taupe; in place of tan, try cognac; instead of muted brown, check out chestnut; and so on. These colors will be neutral enough to do daily duty, but will add needed character to the look.

    Seek interesting texture as well. Chinos can be a herringbone twill; jeans can be distressed, or a raw denim that will develop its own fades; knits in particular can add fantastic textures through ribbing, cabling, and other knit patterns; sportjackets can offer tweed or seersucker or moleskin textures. On the backdrop of a very basic, classic outfit, a single textured piece can look absolutely fantastic.

    Let your choice of colors be predicated on the color that looks best next to your face. If it's going to be there 200 days of the year or more, it should be something that makes you look fantastic; so let your shirt color be your very best color. Not just "light blue", but the particular hue and shade that is best (or the particular hue and shade of whatever color is best).

    Make sure the fit is perfect. Buy clothes that fit properly off the rack and have them tailored to the ideal body-conforming shape, or have the clothes made-to-measure or made bespoke. Since you will be saving thousands of dollars otherwise spent on other clothes purchases, you can well afford to put some of that to use in getting the best constructed and best fitting clothes.

    All of this is written on the assumption that you are not just using MFA to facilitate your own giving up. It seems that you have a lazy streak and think that taking the easy road will help you, and it may very well help in interesting ways; but since you are posting for advice it also seems that part of you is seeking encouragement to present yourself well — and to try, and to improve. You actually want to be called to a higher standard, not for any reason other than the life in you wishes to grow.
