    MFA! Just graduated - Got a suit for interviews! omg

    I'd like to see the jacket an inch longer, possibly two. I'm not sure that means switching to a 44L. But you could try it. Right now, the jacket's at a contemporary length; but it's a classic cut, not a modern cut. Classic length would allow your fingers to curl around the bottom of the hem with your arm hanging extended. From the back, the jacket would also cover the seat; so that crease wouldn't show.

    The sleeves on the jacket are not bad, maybe verging on the short side. I think the issue with the cuff occurs because that shirt is a bit long in the sleeve, so it hangs down over the back of your hand and thus shows too much cuff. It should show about 1/4", with latitude up to 1/2"; but that's quite a bit more than 1/2" of cuff showing. Old-school style sometimes showed much more cuff; but that was with a short jacket sleeve, not a too-long shirt.

    The fit around the body seems good.

    You give the measurements of the pants, but you don't say how they relate to your body measurements. Also, you don't show a view from behind with the jacket off; so it's difficult to say what the issues are with fit at the waist and at the seat. I do see what looks like a bit of pulling across the front (the pocket seems to flare just a little). It's possible that there is a bit of tightness around the hips and seat. A tailor will be able to guide you. It's usually a very simple alteration to have the seat let out.

    I prefer brown oxfords to black, for the relative liveliness and character of the color; but black is acceptable. Please do add a pattern in the tie and/or shirt. Since there is no pattern in the suit (and especially with black shoes), using only solids in shirt and tie is conservative and uninspired. You need more rhythm and movement somewhere in the outfit.

    All in all, good for off the rack.
