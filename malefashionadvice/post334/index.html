    MFA I think I may of made a terrible mistake.

    The thing that limits black is its depth and lack of color. Black is deep, brooding, serious, and mysterious. It provides the sharpest contrast for any light shade worn with it — nothing else will be as dark.

    You can turn these 'limiting' qualities into strengths, and end up wearing the sharpest suit in the room because of it. You can use the sharpness and contrast-creating qualities to your advantage.

    With white shirt and pocket square, a the focus goes to the tie and then vertically to the face. With this combination, a boldly colored tie will really jump out. Or wear a slick, dark solid tie paired with a pastel shirt: very sharp. White, light pink, powder blue, and lavender will all work well with black. (Or, these days, bold shirts are in; and almost any color will work for this bold look.) The contemporary design of that jacket will allow you to wear narrow(2.5") ties, contributing to a more youthful, slick look.

    Even colors that are usually muted gain new life framed in black. (There's a reason black picture frames are so prevalent.) This set, though it is very matchy-matchy, shows how the intensifying power of black can bump up a normally muted tone.

    With similarly classy black belt and shoes, you'll always know that your outfit will have a suave feel to it. And if you're adventurous you can play with different shades and hues of shoes and belt.

    With a mostly-black outfit, using rich and distinctive materials in your accessories is important. Since color won't be setting the pieces apart, something else should — a high shine in leather or a [soft texture in suede](http://i.ebayimg.com/t/Stacy-Adams-Telford-Mens-Dress-Shoes-24723-Black-Suede-All-Sizes-/00/s/Mzc1WDM3NQ==/$(KGrHqN,!jEE7blB4n0(BO+dZ3(VL!~~60_35.JPG); cool metallic glints; detailed flashes of color or pattern in watch face, cuff links, socks, pocket square, shoelaces.... Use materials, textures, and/or patterns to add interest value.

    Here's a masterful use of patterns, with sophisticated, understated color (navy) together with the black suit.

    While the black suit will, if it fits well, almost always be slick, you can downplay the formality of it by eliminating the tie. Using a pocket square as well could add expressive interest without losing the casual quality.
