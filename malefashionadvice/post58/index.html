    My mom laughed at me

    There's a strong tradition of bright pants (chinos in particular) in menswear, with Nantucket reds, 'go to hell' pants, and so on. So, it's done. A good guideline when uncertain is to keep the eye-catching boldness down to a single item in any given outfit. If either color or pattern are loud in one piece, use solids and neutrals in the rest of the outfit. Once you have command of color and pattern combining, you can go to town.

    Aside from the fact that the pattern of the shirt competes with the pants for attention, from the pictures it seems like the shirt is using warm colors while the pants are a cool pink. Even if the pattern weren't an issue, the differences in color temperatures would be a problem. Previous suggestions of white shirt mostly covered by the grey cardigan should work. Also, add a belt.

    So there is precedent for those kinds of pants and that kind of outfit, but it is indeed a young, preppy, and summery look. Outside of that range (and even within it sometimes) it can look somewhat ridiculous and laughable.

    I'd like to encourage continued variety, exploration, and risk-taking with your wardrobe. If you're looking for more color in your outfits without the extreme flamboyance of the pink, seek out more sophisticated colors like burgundy, berry or 'terra cotta' or gold; or other bright colors like legion blue or french blue.

    Oh, and your mom may have some good things to teach about putting together an outfit, but don't give her words too much weight unless you want to live to her standard and perspective. (Anyway, no woman should tell you wear.) The advice you get from a chic college girl may have a more youthful and contemporary focus better suited to where you are in life.
