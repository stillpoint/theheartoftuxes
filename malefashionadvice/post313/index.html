    Looking for advice on this outfit for a summer date

    The shirt can be seen as a bit loud and tacky, or alternatively as a bold and playful statement. (It seems a bit retro, like it's hearkening back to 1980s Italian-American interior design. Can I ask where you picked it up?) From your stance it seems that you can carry it off, and from your description of the climate where you live it is likely that bold-patterned short sleeve shirts are likely more acceptable than in other locales. You pair it with a basic solid neutral pant, which is the right way to do it.

    Covering part of a bold pattern is another strategy you can use. A light unstructured blazer or other cover-up like a fitted windbreaker or light cardigan can tone down the pattern while not completely doing away with its movement and energy.

    There's too much cuffing going on. Bookends. And along with the pattern of the shirt and the tightness of both top and bottom, it's all too much â€” too try-hard. Let go of one set of cuffs, at least. Since the shirt cuff contrasts with the shirt and stands out, and since the shirt is already quite tight (and the cuffs over-emphasize the bound up feeling), I'd say let go of the cuff in the short sleeves; but you can choose. If the pants are short enough you can let go of the cuff there instead (or as well). The sleeve should hit between mid-bicep and the bottom of the deltoid; if it goes lower after uncuffing you need to have it altered.

    I see that you like close-fitting clothes. That's fine, and for someone young and fit like you it works, especially for a date. But take care: the shirt is splaying out at the hips, and you're right on the verge of smarmy with the over-all look. Any tighter and it's too obvious you're making an overt point of your physicality.

    I agree with Komania that the shoe color doesn't work with the outfit. I also personally don't like the design, which to my eye looks too pointy, plasticky, and fey; but even if we just say it's a Euro-inspired design that is less familiar where I'm from (North America), the shoe is too slick to be worn with that casual summery outfit â€” especially since you're cuffing the pants, which signals laid-back ease rather than slickness.

    Along with the close fits, the shoes add to the smarmy quality. One fears that slicked-back hair and an overdose of cologne will complete the picture.

    You might actually do better with the wingtips, if those are truly your only other option. The previously mentioned sneakers, bucks, boat shoes, and espadrilles would all work as well. You should be able to pick up some sneaks or espadrilles pretty cheaply as stores rotate their inventory.

    As far as accessories go, a bit more weight and contrast on the wrist would work better than the thin light band you have on. (Is that an elastic band?) A metallic watch or even a nice sport watch would work; or if you want to use a band, let it be a thicker leather. Your accessory at the wrist should play well with the shoes you're going to be wearing: no need to match exactly, but the material, color, and style should fit in with the whole outfit and take the opportunity to create an interesting or unifying interplay with the footwear.
