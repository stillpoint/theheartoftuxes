    Tuck untuck, and which shoes to go with?

    First; it's hard to see detail in your photos, so that makes it more difficult to give a definitive recommendation. Take it with a grain of salt.

    The reason you might go with an untuck is to achieve a more casual look and feel. The instinct may then be to go with sneakers, but in this case they don't work because they don't match the plaid shirt and jeans (and brown leather watchstrap) in style: the shoes are sporty in style and color; the shirt and jeans are 'workwear' â€” more country/outdoorsy/Americana. If you were to tuck the shirt, the sneakers would be even further away in style since the look would be less casual, and the leather belt would add to the rugged workwear feel.

    The untucked shirt with brown leather boots is not bad. It depends on what you like. But all things being equal, I'd recommend making the extra bit of effort to look put-together when you're going out. Why not call ourselves to a higher standard when we can?

    What image do you hope to project? Clean vs. laid-back (or uptight vs. slovenly)? The tucked shirt does provide a more clean look, and the story there seems to include a bit more self-respect or attention to detail. The fact that the belt is then visible, and that it matches the boots, contributes to the put-together look.

    It's good that there is a pattern in the shirt, because the main color is very close to the color of the jeans, and would be monotonous if not for the movement there. (For the sake of self-education, think about how the outfit would look with a plaid shirt in a different color.)

    As others have mentioned, please do away with the visible undershirt. If you're going for a messy 'lumberjack' sort of deal, with wide-open shirt and defiant exposure of underwear (which an undershirt is), that's a different story; but you have to back that look up with your personal style and (rugged/aloof) energy, and everything in your outfit and demeanor have to align, or it will look clichÃ© and try-hard.
