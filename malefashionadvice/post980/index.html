    What does MFA think about my thrift store haul today? (Sorry for the poor picture quality)

    What piece/pieces were included in the "haul"?

    Sorry to say, the vest is very bad. It is too big; but more importantly, it's awkward and out of place. I can't see enough of the shirt and pants to give complete feedback there. The sleeves are indeed rolled up too high.

    Consider this a rite of passage: your foray into style via misguided waistcoat (vest) purchase. It seems to be an exceedingly common event. I tried it myself, way back when. I'll pass what I tell most young guys who try it: you don't 'get style' by buying a piece that you hope will set you apart or bestow character upon you. Style appears when your own being shines through what you wear and how you wear it, and what you do and how you do it.

    This vest is not you; it's a piece of someone else's old life. When you have looked into your own lifestyle — as-is — and your own innate qualities, then you can start to express yourself authentically through your clothing. And the pieces can start to make sense in terms of what they say about who you really are and who you intend to be. What this waistcoat says about you is that you don't know yet what to express, but you're trying. So like I said, it's a rite of passage; get rid of the vest and move on to learning the basics of style and practicing them. (Check the sidebar material for a start.)

    An outfit should make sense for you and your lifestyle. The fit, cut, style, level of formality, colors, and patterns should all be in harmony. Your enjoyment and sense of adventure must work with those basic elements. That is style, not something you can get from a store.

    It's not object-related. It moves from the inside, out.
