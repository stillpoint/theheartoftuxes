    I always hear people saying "don't wear a visible undershirt"...

    Ugh! He looks like a security guard trying out his first accessory. Might as well have a mullet.

    Just to get this out of the way, let's all agree that the show-through undershirt is nasty bad.

    The t-shirt visible under an unbuttoned shirt has its place in a very casual, even rugged setting. In any less casual context (and this still includes much of the 'casual' range) and where the t-shirt is not a focal point, it usually looks like underwear is being exposed -- a slightly less egregious version of the exposed boxers. Or, shall we say, that is what offends many people about it: the elements that are supposed to be working for you in the upper layers -- the frame provided by a collar, the sharpness of its crease and the unwrinkled smoothness of the fabric, the meticulous care displayed by a row of buttons -- are defeated by the appearance of an undergarment. It can even be felt as somewhat insulting in a subconscious way.

    It is this exposure of a 'private' garment that can be employed as an intentional act of revealing that can be regarded as sexy.

    Another issue may be that the elegant vee created by opening the neck of a button-down is negated by a crew neck, in a way similar to a crew neck t-shirt worn with a v-neck sweater or sport jacket. It's done; but it's not a sophisticated use of the shapes created by the clothes you wear.

    Finally, if the undershirt is in a contrasting hue, the little spot of color right under the face may look like an aberration; the exposed color may be neither the right size nor shape to integrate well with the rest of the outfit, and it almost invariably wrenches focus away from the face and from whatever magnificent pieces or unity may be present in the rest of the outfit.

    Check out Don Johnson in Miami Vice. He never closed his blazer when wearing a t-shirt, possibly to avoid the awkward shapes created by the vee of the gorge and the crew neck.
