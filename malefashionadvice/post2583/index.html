    How far should should shirt cuffs stick out/ constructive criticism?

    The accepted range is 1/4" to 1/2". More exposure than that is old-school, and should only be performed by those in control of their look.

    I seem to recall that Sammy Davis Jr. (remember the Rat Pack?) liked a full couple of inches of cuff — if not the whole thing — to show. Maybe the thinking was that a french cuff and cufflink were worth exposing. It seems like a forerunner of the Thom Browne scheme of shortened hems and sleeves.

    But I recommend against following a rule slavishly and without awareness. It is much better to understand why you would want to expose a cuff, and what it changes about the overall look. Step back and look at the outfit as a whole. Blur or soften your vision so that instead of flitting from point to point you're taking in the entire scene. You can see the interplay between the cuffs and the parts of the shirt exposed in the jacket's gorge. Notice the difference between showing and hiding the cuffs.

    Exposing more cuff brings more interplay: instead of one piece of shirt color showing under the face, there are now three bits of color showing. Three is an interesting grouping; there is a natural movement within the grouping (much better than even numbers two or four). There's a give and take between adding more flash to the outfit via contrast at the wrist, and drawing the eye too much — competing with the primary elements of shirt collar and tie or bringing too much complication into the look.

    Every part of the outfit speaks. It's your job to harmonize all of those voices. The metallic buttons on your blazer are also sharply eye-catching; so that will affect how much cuff is required to (A) balance that sharpness and (B) keep the overall complexity reasonable. A tie with contrasting strips and metallics is another element with repeated flashes; so you need to strategize about how many little flashes and how many bold and solid elements will create the look you want to project, and indeed how those elements should relate.

    You may very well want more cuff to show, to avoid a too-detailed feel. Or you may want to keep it as it is to limit the competition for attention within the outfit. I'm not sure there's a single right answer here, just areas where you can develop awareness and create your own strategy.

    Edit: This is from GQ: >One man’s normal is another man’s abomination. That brilliant flash of shirt linen that was once so much a part of masculine sartorial allure is today almost extinct. Sinatra showed three quarters to a full inch, Dino an inch to an inch and a half, and I seem to recall Sammy showing the whole damn French cuff. I think a half inch of shirt cuff revealed, with the arms at one’s sides, is the minimum. From there it’s what you dare—though the cuff should never pop all the way out. Ultimately, the shirt cuff attracts the eye, making one’s arms appear long and limber. This has proved important for dancers, like Astaire, and conductors, like Bernstein. His crisp white cuffs made a baton almost unnecessary.
