    My fiancee is set on this tux for our wedding this summer. What are your thoughts MFA?

    Matching doesn't have to mean the same. I would much prefer to see the groom's party each expressing themselves individually within a certain style and level of formality, and perhaps a certain color scheme. That is the more elegant way, as it embraces the humanity and uniqueness of the groomsmen instead of requiring them all to fit the same mold.

    The practice is ubiquitous, but I find the requirement for exact matching to be a bit crass and juvenile, with the sole exception of black tie formal wear.

    On another note: personally, I would never think of requiring or even suggesting that my friends pay their own money to fit in with my vision of things. If I'm asking for conformity to something I've dreamed up, I figure it's my responsibility to pay for it. The heart of weddings often gets trampled in pursuit of the (unattainable) perfect form and format. But the important thing, after all, is the love and commitment being expressed, and the joy and support shared with friends and family. I'd shudder at the thought of obscuring that by inconveniencing my friends over clothing and money. A dress code and request for stylistic unity: yes. Making decisions with someone else's money: no.

    Edit: And if I may continue sharing my opinion, there's something I'd like to say in support of all men on their wedding days: Contrary to the pop culture version of a wedding, the event is not supposed to be a day when the bride gets her every wish and fantasy fulfilled; it is a day when two people approach each other from a place of full empowerment and freedom, to share a commitment that will form the basis of their life to come. If that basis is weak or unbalanced or built upon shallow-minded motives, then it is a poor start to married life. It is important for the man to be fully the man, and not give in to pleas or demands for a costume that offends his sensibility (or anything that requires him to sell out on his sense of integrity). A fiancée's clamoring mind may hate it, but the part of her that fell in love with the man will love and respect him that much more for his continued integrity. ...just my two cents worth; I recognize that others may operate with a different set of considerations. Best wishes to all, regardless.
