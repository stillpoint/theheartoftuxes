    Need advice for t-shirts

    A more mature look will come from an overall integrity to your outfit, not from the cachet derived from a single piece. You might go so far as to say that attaching one's hopes for one's image to a single piece of clothing is inherently immature, with its origins in little boys seeking power rings or little girls wanting to wear tiaras. Later in life, it translates to adherence to team hats and lucky shirts. Maturity moves away from attachment to the item itself, and toward its function in conveying what is moving through you -- using apparel as an extension of and support for one's inner image.

    I'd recommend doing some comparisons, and trying some alternatives. This would be an experiment; you don't have to start wearing stuff you're not into.

    First, establish a neutral, classic basis with some well-fitting chinos or jeans, and matching belt and shoes. Then try on some well-fitting examples of alternatives to t-shirts (and try on at least one graphic and one plain t-shirt to compare). Try at least one or two henley shirts, solid color piqu√© polo shirts, a plain white or blue collared shirt, and a subtly patterned collared shirt.

    A further step would be layering. Try each of these shirts with a hoodie, a henley (yes, you can layer a henley over almost any shirt except for dressy ones), a crew-neck sweater, a v-neck sweater, and maybe various casual or dress jackets.

    If your 'over' layers are neutrals (beige, brown, khaki, grey, olive), they will be easy to match with a wide variety of 'under' layers and pants. If you have the hang of that, you can introduce some colors that are also versatile, like light, mid, and dark blues, or branch off into the whimsy of bright hues.

    You could undertake this experiment during a shopping trip, trying all the clothing on in the fitting rooms; or you could buy the garments at a store that has a good return policy, try it all at home (also with your existing wardrobe), and return whatever you don't want to keep.

    Another helpful hint is to lay out prospective outfits on your bed or on a table, to see how items might go together without the hassle of actually donning and doffing them. Once you think you might be on to something, put on the outfit and check it out in a full length mirror. (You do have a full-length mirror, don't you? If not, buy one and put it where you have good light and room to turn around. This is your number one style tool.)
