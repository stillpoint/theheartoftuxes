    Need help with understanding fashion.

    Fashion, but more specifically Style, at its peak is a shamanic skill. (And at its lowest, is shallow, frivolous, and egocentric.) Like music, dance, art, design of all kinds, fragrance and incense, and cuisine, one's clothing is a movement and communication, a refinement of the materials of life to bring more life forth. Style can have a personal side, though, a movement from within. It is a way of altering energy, shifting mood and inclination -- your own and those of others.

    With your clothing you can bring energy up or down, with practically infinite subtle increments and variations. You can support, downplay, or even conjure personal qualities like ease, confidence, sharpness, depth, joy, grace, sensitivity. You can nourish who you really are; and you can choose to be something new.

    Most human archetypes have a particular outfit associated with them: worker, rebel, tramp, scholar, athlete, superhero. And there are countless more subtle and refined archetypes available, which reach deeply into the consciousness of others. With your attire, you can move another's mind and mood deeply and instantly, before even the first word of greeting. And likewise, you can move your own mind and mood, growing or leaping out of previous molds.

    Style is energy-bending, no less than playing with the elemental powers of solidity, fluidity, radiance, dispersion, and space.

    But this may not be why most people take style to be either of importance or of little importance. Both ends of that spectrum are rooted in ego: people like or don't like fashion and style largely because of who they think they are. It is, maybe, either a vain attraction that keeps one thinking about one's own appearance, or the threat of exposure that keeps one avoiding it.

    ~

    To me, the central question of style and of 'dressing well' is "who am I?" Knowing yourself, or at least seeking yourself, is at the heart of every expression that springs from you. Who are you; what is your character? And what is the character waiting to be unfolded? What do you refrain from; and what do you dare? What quality do you want to share, with your wife, with your colleagues, with your community?
