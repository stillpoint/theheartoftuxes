    Need some quick help,

    A humble suggestion for future postings: add more info to your subject line than simply "help needed". Redditors shouldn't have to open the thread to know what the topic is.

    Your shirt/tie combo: It doesn't grab me. It's merely okay since the jacket and pants are so neutral, and thus forgiving of loudness. Complementary colors are to be used when you want to bring out their intensity -- each color intensifies its complement. If that's what you're hoping for, more power to you. What the combo has going for it is youthful daring; but it would be more youthful and expressive if either the pink or the green were brighter.

    Said more simply, the tie and shirt colors don't have anything to do with each other. If that's what you want, fine.

    My post here might speak to your situation with the black&white plaid tie. It's a very contrasty tie; so you'll probably want darker, muted, and solid-colored jacket and pants. That may make the tie the star of the show, as the only stand-out piece.

    With the jersey (the material, not the state) jacket, you have to take into account the messages it sends. It has some reference to formality, as it is cut like a sport jacket; but the jersey knit (and to a lesser degree the patch pockets) makes it quite a bit more casual than a regular sport jacket. Conversely, the material is like that of a hoodie or sweatshirt; but the design is considerably more formal. So that's the in-between range that you're working with; and you'll look best if the rest of your outfit conforms to that range, for the most part: the comfort of a hoodie, with a dressier slant.

    Said more simply, this is not a blazer. Don't treat it as one.

    I like the v-neck idea, either a t-shirt or sweater under the jacket. An open-collar button-up would be fine. If you're sprucing it up with a tie, then have some of your other items support that; you might go chino rather than jean, loafer rather than sneaker. And if you wear a tie, keep it casual with a knit; a silk dress tie doesn't conform to the nature of the jacket.

    But there's another issue that is standing out: the armholes on that jacket look huge! I hope that it's not as noticeable as in the photo, or that you can return the jacket. The shoulder/arm scye area looks out of proportion; and you'll likely get a lot of tugging and stretching when you raise your arms.

    You can tie the boots the standard way, or wrap the laces around the tops before tying. The latter option can be done at the top eyelet or down a couple, leaving the top slightly open. They're in quite a dark navy, it seems, so black may actually be a better option for both lace and belt. If swapping out the lace, you might want to go a bit lighter, to a mid-brown or just darker than tan, in which case your belt would also likely be either tan or navy. And I take it you won't be wearing the pants pictured with your planned footwear -- don't do navy boots, brown laces, black pants, and brown belt; no, no no!
