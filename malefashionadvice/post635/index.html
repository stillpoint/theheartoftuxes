    I suck at tucking!

    A well-fitting real polo shirt does not usually need to be tucked in, although it may be done that way in some settings to maintain a sharp or professional look.

    The reasons a shirt comes untucked or poofs out of the waistband may be as follows:

    • The fit is poor, so there is too much fabric billowing out. The fix: buy the proper size, and have it tailored to fit.

    • The body's geometry (especially convex curves) forces the shirt out of the waistband of the pants upon movement. The fix: slim down.

    • The armholes of the shirt are too low, so that the tails are pulled on when the arms are raised. The fix: buy shirts with a high armhole.

    Golf polos tend to be cut poorly, resulting in a baggy shape.     The sleeves on a polo should end no lower than halfway down the bicep, and no higher than the top of the bicep/bottom of the deltoid. The fabric should skim the torso without clinging and without billowing. Some guys opt for a more form-fitting look, which is also okay as long as it doesn't stretch the shirt tight over the body.
