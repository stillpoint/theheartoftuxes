    About to pull the trigger on this jacket, could khakis be pulled off with this in the Tar Striped color?

    The first point is: is that a good color for your skin tone? Check in a mirror with a similar swatch of fabric; and ask a friend. It could wash out some complexions. If it is indeed a good color for you, the suggestions below should hold true.

    If the khakis are a distinct color, I think the jacket would look great. A bit of a military/safari vibe, without being too literal about it. With the khakis, you'll just want to have a dusty, muted-earth-tone palette without absolutely matching colors. Avoid the unitard or jumpsuit look that comes when the top and bottom halves of the outfit are in colors that are too similar.

    Since the "Tar Stripe" is a taupe, there will be many warm khakis available in lighter tones. Add moss, olive green, and warm or cool greys in your shirt and scarf; and light tan, chestnut brown, or chocolate brown in your footwear and leather accessories -- great!

    By 'khakis', some people mean chinos in general, not just dust-colored ones. If that is the case here, you can also wear the jacket as shown, with darker indigo or black. Taupe and black is a very elegant combination. (In that case, you'd focus on cooler shades of color, and your accessories would be black, silver, and gunmetal.)

    You can keep the whole look earthy and muted for a very calm, solid energy; but either of those two palettes would also rock with a single flash of color, like a sky-blue or orange scarf, french blue shirt, burgundy belt, etc. Try the flashes of color with similarly dusty or faded tones.

    One caveat: the jacket is a straight cut; so fit will be critical, or it may look too boxy. Sometimes jackets like that will have internal or external drawstrings, a belt, or a waistband across the back, to draw in the waist and provide some shape.
