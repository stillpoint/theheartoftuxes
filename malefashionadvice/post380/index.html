    These are called Breton stripe or nautical stripe shirts. They have their roots in the French navy and, later, in common use by other French mariners and fishermen. The iconic design found its way into French high style (the navy and white colors and simple graphic are essential in the Chanel lineage, for example).

    Besides its historical and cultural references, the pattern visually has a nautical feel; the juxtaposition of colors is a very fresh and vibrant one, evocative of sunny seasides. Though the pattern was originally adopted for its high visibility on the ocean (in case a sailor fell into the drink), it continues to be a classic because it is both intense and simple — a rare combination that can be used in sophisticated ways.

    The very high contrast and vibrational rhythm of the stripes is both an attraction and a risk. You have to tune the boldness of the stripes to your style and your skin tone, because on some people such a shirt will stand out as too loud. The contrast intensity of the pattern can be downplayed with stripes in colors of lower contrast (like white with medium or even light grey) or with smaller stripes on a wider, calmer ground (like navy background and thin white pinstripes). The vibrational intensity can be slowed down by spacing the same sized stripes wider apart or by using wider stripes (which leads ultimately to color blocking — two or three wide fields of color). At some point, these changes depart from 'Breton' or 'nautical', but it's not clear where that point is exactly.

    I believe the one pictured is a Fred Perry; I actually tried one on but didn't buy it.

    Most summer collections will offer one or more nautical stripe shirts. From Old Navy to Club Monaco to Ralph Lauren to Gant to Ben Sherman, almost every outlet will offer something in this design, and especially this year, when the pattern has a strong popularity. Search terms "Breton", "nautical", "long sleeve", and "henley" in various combinations. But hurry; the collections will be switching out to the fall lineup soon.
