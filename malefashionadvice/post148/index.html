    Went on a shopping spree. Opinions on the new clothes, fit, etc? Are the Jeans too Skinny? (Almost 15)

    Not bad. You'll grow into your style, both in terms of knowledge and in terms of physical mass; but just being willing to ask and learn puts you ahead of the game at your age.

    Probably the biggest change you can make is to buy clothes that fit you better, and if necessary have clothes altered to fit. Read the sidebar guide on How Clothes Should Fit, and try some online research for examples of good fit and poor fit. As is the case with most 15-year-old guys, your shirts are too big. You don't want tight, body-hugging shirts, but the ones you are wearing billow away from the body too much; and in particular, the button-up shirts you show are too wide and too long in the torso.

    Next, start to look at your outfits and others' outfits with a discerning eye, keying on contrast. You can check your own look in a mirror or in photos, and also check magazine pictures, and research images on the internet. And an easier way to experiment with combinations is to lay the pieces down on a bed. This allows you to make quick changes and see many different combinations, and saves you from donning and doffing clothing all the time. But when you do come up with an outfit to try, just be sure to always check in a full-length mirror how it fits and how it goes together.

    • In your first outfit, the dark shirt, dark pants, and dark shoes are close in shade. How would it look if you had dark and light pieces next to each other; say, by switching the jeans for khakis or lighter jeans?

    • In the second outfit, the shade (darkness) of pieces is similar; but the hue (color) of the green shirt helps it to stand distinct from the jeans.

    • Contrast also works between solids and patterns. The combination of a patterned shirt with a solid pant, like in your third and fourth outfits, is best. The 'louder' the pattern is on a piece, the more the rest of your outfit should be plain and neutral.

    So there are your starting points: fit and contrast. At your age, you don't need to worry much about style. Wear what you like, but learn how to combine it well. After you know more about correct fit and how to use contrast in an outfit, then you can go on to the next steps, finding your best colors (the colors that make you look healthy and full of life), and learning how to combine colors. After that, you might move on to learning about the use of line, texture, style, and accessories.
