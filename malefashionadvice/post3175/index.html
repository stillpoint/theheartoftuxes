    Dress shirt and tie with denim pants. Tuck or un-tuck?

    You shouldn't be wearing a shirt and tie without a jacket, or at the very minimum a sweater. Jacket is much better.

    If you must, in your youthful verve and rebellious nonchalance, untuck the shirt, go without the tie and open a button or two at the top. As noted by others, the level of formality expressed with the tie does not match the level of casualness expressed by untucking. The untucked shirt should (as babingbongbang says) have shorter tails, usually squared off, but sometimes in a very shallow curve.

    Also consider carefully how you actually look with the shirt untucked, versus how you wish or imagine you look. Self-photos can add some objectivity more difficult with just a mirror; but a mirror check is still a thousand times better than going based on your mental image. The opinion of a couple of honest, well-dressed girls trumps both.

    The concern, of course, is that instead of looking cool or at ease, you may look disheveled, clueless, like you don't care about your appearance, or like you're trying to look cool. (That said, I do like to untuck from time to time.)

    ~

    Next, let's get clear on terminology. Just because a person uses the term 'rock' or 'rocking' doesn't mean that what they're wearing looks good. "Rocking the unzipped fly." See? In fact, I'd be quite happy to never again hear someone say "I'm rocking the..." whatever. Very rarely, in my experience, is the 'whatever' actually 'rocked'. More often, there are just hopes attached to a certain piece or outfit, which is a bit the opposite of 'rocking'.
