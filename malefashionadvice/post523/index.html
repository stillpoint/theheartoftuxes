    Fashion Emergency! Does this work as "Cocktail Attire" ?

    You're not wearing white socks with a dressy outfit.

    Now that we've taken care of that, let's look at the rest of the outfit. Cocktail attire usually means dressy, like a suit or less commonly an outfit with a sportjacket, but with more flair and elegance than we see with business attire. Some of the strictness of the more formal echelons of dress has been relaxed, and more emphasis is placed on luxury and sensuality.

    These pants, shirt, and tie shown might do for daytime, warm-weather events; but "cocktail attire" would usually have more to do with an evening look, which is darker. If that's the case in your situation we might stay away from gingham patterns and light-colored pants.

    In any case, the contrast within your outfit is a bit much; the dark shoes and jacket bracket the light pants an a way that looks disconnected. With such a light and sunny inner layer, mid-shades in jacket and shoes would be more in keeping with the feel. But in classic cocktail wear we would rather switch the pants for something darker, and also something with more drape and less tendency to wrinkle. I get the sense that the pants and the jacket in your outfit are from two different levels of formality.

    Think lush and luxurious in terms of materials, deep in terms of pattern and color, sharp in terms of cut and design, and relaxed in terms of how it's put together. Fine wool blends, silks, velvet, and satin are possible choices for fabric. Raw silk and grenadine are possible tie choices. You might opt for a pocket square instead of a tie, or in addition. If you're not skilled in matching accessories, just make sure your socks are a darker version of the pants' color.

    Next, is that a suit jacket? In the close-up the fabric looks like it belongs to a matched suit, in which case you should only wear the jacket with the matching pants. With the addition of some depth and intensity in the accompanying colors and materials a well-fitting suit is a good choice for cocktail wear. The shoes might more appropriately be shiny leather rather than suede (which is more casual), although if you keyed on a sporty sportjacket look suede shoes might follow. A tie in a deep solid color would do better than gingham. And you might consider a colored shirt or some other way to bring forth a more elegant and interesting quality — pocket square, cuff-links, tie bar, metal watch, fine silk socks (in moderate doses — choose one or two catchy accessories, no more).
