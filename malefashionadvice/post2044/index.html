    Are my N&F Weird guys too big?

    Certainly. To tell the truth, it's not that bad; and if you can live with the shoulders, the side seam can be taken in and/or darts can be installed in the back to make it fit much better.

    The first thing that hits the eye when seeing the whole outfit is the lack of body shape — the pants are rumpled and misshapen all the way up the leg (but you already know that); and the shirt falls straight down at the sides, without any attractive, sleek, athletic curve at the waist. You can also see long wrinkles in the fabric at front, not just in the lap but diagonally from the arms, indicating the presence of excess fabric.

    Next, it's difficult to see the shoulder seam in the photos; but if you look closely and follow the attachment of the epaulets you can see that the shoulder seam passes the point of the shoulder by quite a bit, and that there is a fold of fabric where the yoke overhangs the shoulder. In addition, you can see the outer edge of the epaulets curving down around the shoulder rather than standing out straight.

    Finally, the hem comes down too far for someone your size, creating a proportion where the top of the outfit (and of you) is too long in relation to the bottom. This effect is increased by the lightness of the shirt in comparison to the pants and shoes, creating a field of light blue that floats and spreads upward above an irregular dark stump.

    A shirt that is untucked already starts from a disheveled place; you need to keep that under control so that it looks casual rather than messy. That means the shirt should be well fitted and well tailored — a good shape, just skimming over the body, with no lumps or folds of extra fabric. The hem should hit around mid-zipper.

    The hip arch in the hem is not too, too high in your shirt, but any arch adds some element of dishevelment that has to be tempered with good fitting.

    You should also be aware that untucking will always lengthen the torso and shorten the legs. What you gain in a casual feel you may lose in good, elegant, masculine proportion.

    Contrast helps provide interest in an outfit; but if there is high contrast between top and bottom, it emphasizes the separation between the halves, whereas you may want to emphasize the unity so that the long continuous line will contribute to a sense of height and elegant presence.

    Having a good smooth line in the pants, a proper transition from pants to shoes, and a good line and tonal relationship between upper and lower halves will all contribute to that height and elegance. It will show your body in its best light, and put the focus where it belongs: on you as a dynamic individual, not on how special your clothes are.
