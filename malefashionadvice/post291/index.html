    What is a good, warm alternative to wool coats?

    Sorry to hear that. I thought wool was hypoallergenic itself. I guess layering is your friend. For a New York winter, you need inches of clothing. Any number of layers (from one to five) will do, as long as you have that certain total thickness of insulation. (After five layers, you have diminishing returns.)

    What comes to mind:

    • I'm sure some leather coats can be found with insulation or zip-out linings. Though insulation may be thinner than you want for deep cold, the leather itself is an excellent shield against wind and snow, and it breathes.

    • If you find something that looks good but doesn't have quite enough insulative power, you can add a jacket liner.

    • Based on the jacket liner, quilted nylon and polyester coats are being made in good cuts these days by Barbour and others. They also tend not to be of winter thickness, but might be a good lead to investigate.

    • Other searches you may want to perform would include cotton polyester pea coat (scroll down) and the like. They also won't be as warm as wool, but may do the trick in combination with a liner.

    • Higher-end menswear stores should have some examples of convertible coats, 3-in-1 coats, or whatever they call a nylon shell with removable insulation. Many of these are designed to go over a suit, and have great features like removable chest-and-neck insulation that zips up high to the chin.

    • Among synthetic-shell insulated coats: Wellenstyn makes a whole line of great-looking winter coats; and from the UK, this Aigle Blackmore coat also looks great.

    • Down coats will be your warmest options, as long as you can avoid getting them soaked in rain or wet snow. Also from across the pond, Alpha has been making down coats for city dwellers for quite a while, and Montcler always seems to have good designs in down.

    • If you have the $$, you can visit a furrier and ask about something similar to a shearling coat, but done in fur, with the fur facing inward. (The standard men's fur coats are an abomination.) Apparently Burberry also made a duffle coat that appears to be a cotton canvas with rabbit fur lining

    • A search for other options might use the term "vintage corduroy" or something similar, as the old (non-wool) coats tended to be more hefty and better designed for winter wear.

    • The only other option I can think of is to buy the wool coat and protect yourself from contact by wearing scarfs, high collars, turtlenecks, liners, etc.

    Good luck!
