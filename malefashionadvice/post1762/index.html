    What can I wear a white tie with?

    It's tough, and may take more effort than it's worth. The first question I want to ask, as usual, is what is the context? What kind of situation would you want to pull out the white tie for? Until that is answered, responses can only be general or hit-and-miss; but I'll try to cover a couple of different levels of formality:

    If you're wearing a tie, you should also be wearing a jacket (sportcoat, that is). So the sportcoat should be a light color, and perhaps a light summer fabric. I think your instincts are right -- go for a light solid color in your shirt, so as not to give too much contrast. Light grey, light blue, light purple could work (check the mirror). Pink with white tie could come off as too feminine, like a 'pink lady' drink, unless other elements contributed some darkness, boldness, or intensity. Avoid dark shirts, which will almost certainly create a gangster look. With a solid white tie, you could also try a shirt with a pattern that comes off as a light solid from a distance.

    Speaking of what patterns look like from a distance, depending on the plaid in your tie, it could possibly be used in a way similar to a grey tie, in which case many mid-grey, mid-blue, and mid-brown suits and jackets would come into play.

    Having the plaid in your tie as your only pattern could come off as contrived, so you may want a subtle pattern elsewhere -- jacket, pocket square....

    I could imagine a light, crisp, summery outfit may be topped off with a white tie; for example, a light tan or off-white linen or seersucker suit, with one of the shirts suggested above. This kind of look, just swap out the tie, which would then provide a similar high-key contrast with the off white suit as that provided by the bright white shoes.

    This outfit is clean and simple. To add a white tie, add some contrast with the shirt -- very light buff blue or purple or grey, or even light beige or cream.

    If we're not attached to that particular plaid tie, another look that could end up being quite sophisticated is to match whites in shirt and tie, but have very different and visible textures. This could go with an off-white jacket, or with a wider range of summer or nautical colors like royal blue blazer, for example.

    And at yet a higher level of formality, something approaching or hinting at 'white tie' rules, such as a black or charcoal suit or black velvet blazer, with white shirt, tie, and pocket square in different sheens and textures. (Pocket square colors could be played with.) This is a starting point, but it's too matchy-matchy.

    As I've said in other posts, a good deal is only a good deal if you'll use the item. Consider cost per wear, and a $100 navy grenadine could end up being a better deal than a $5 white-with-plaid. If you find yourself trying hard to wedge a piece in with the rest of your wardrobe, consider getting rid of it. You'll gain in both physical and psychic space.
