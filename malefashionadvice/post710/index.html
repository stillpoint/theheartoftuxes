    Comfortable 'dressy' shoes at an affordable price?

    Are there any parameters -- shoes that either you or your clients won't tolerate? If not, there's a very wide range of possibilities; although your $100-120 budget may make it more difficult to find something well-made.

    If I gather correctly, your major requirements will be comfort, durability and/or disposability (which translates to cheap price), and a dressy look.

    Desert boots are very easy to match with a lot of different outfits. They tend to be pretty comfortable, and can be found at the low end of what other casual shoes go for. You'll have to feel out whether the ankle-high leather will be a problem, or whether it could actually help by keeping the boot on your foot as you squirm around under desks.

    You might be a good candidate for leather sneakers, especially if your look is a bit youthful. Don't pile up on me, MFA!! I'm not talking about Skechers and sneaker-dress hybrids. There are some fantastic, sleek, sporty leather sneakers out there. Some of them are made by high-end designers like Prada and Gucci and Lacoste; but even then you may find them on sale or clearance.

    Here's an example that's out of your budget; but I have, for sure, seen similar shoes on sale in the low hundreds $$. Here's an example of a designer suede sneaker/high-top at a sale price. Fossil, Diesel, and Guess may have some alternatives for you, also at the clearance shelves.

    At $160, we're getting closer to your budget with these. John Varvatos and Converse are apparently collaborating; and these leather sneakers are available at Mr. Porter for $145.

    There's too much running shoe referencing for my liking in these designs by Adidas and Puma; but you may have luck checking out the leather lineups of traditional sneaker brands.

    If you stay away from trainer- and technical-looking hybrids, the leather should automatically make the shoe a bit more dressy. You'll also have a bit of a style advantage by having a shoe that not many others are wearing. And here's a great tip: Swap out the cotton laces for thin leather lacing. The shades of brown leather will look great together, and will take another step upward from the scuffy, knockabout sneakers one wears mowing the lawn.

    The Tretorn Krona is a great-looking leather high-top that clearly borrows from the desert boot look. I think leather lacing would look great on these.

    Bucks should also be available on sale at lower prices, as well as buck-inspired shoes like the Adidas Samba. But I'd be sceptical about the durability of suede being dragged on carpets dozens of times a day.

    Skewing more toward comfort, and depending on the type of casual clothing you're wearing, moccasin drivers or moccasin boots may do. But this is wandering pretty far from a professional image, if that's of any concern.

    If you feel fine going more expressive with your footwear, the sportiness of Onitsuka Tigers is super. The shoes conform to the feet very well, and are very flexible, which will allow you to curl your toes under comfortably when you kneel. They come in a vast variety of color combinations. I purchased a couple of pairs at $100 each; and they're my go-to sneaker.
