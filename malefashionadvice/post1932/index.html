    I've worn a livestrong bracelet for years. Does MFA find it tacky or douchey?

    I'm not a fan of clothing as marketing, whether it's sporting a shoe company's oversized logo on a t-shirt or a charity's doo-dad on your wrist. I prefer to let my actions do the talking; and if anyone still needs to know where I stand on an issue, they can ask me.

    The positive messages intended by LiveStrong bracelets, pink ribbon lapel pins, and so on, do not balance the fact that the simple grace and dignity of human life is being encumbered and drowned out by the noise of a million different advertisements, some lofty and some petty, but each clamoring for attention. It's not that the cause is not worthy or that it shouldn't inspire others; but I would much prefer to see a person whose attire, while it may display a personal style, is more of an open book, allowing room for all kinds of other expressions. We are multi-dimensional beings with unknown potential, and I find it crass to start limiting ourselves to a single cause or lifestyle statement with our attire.

    Your mileage may vary, though; and if it is actually something that is moving through you strongly every day, I would not hold it against you if you were to wear a LiveStrong bracelet or one of many other 'cause accessories'. (No swastikas, please.) It may be tacky; but your sincerity keeps it from being douchey. I would be interested to know whether it really is something that you want to keep stating, over and over and over, all day, every day; or if there are times when you want to state that you are other things as well, or leave room for the unstated to be there as well. Could it be that a self-image has developed in relationship to the 'cause', with attachments both physical (such as certain clothing that has become a habit) and mental (such as rigid opinions and habits of relating)?

    On a more MFA tack, style-wise the bracelet isn't so great. The plastic and the bright color will only suit a narrow range of looks: Urban, synthetic, and sporty would all likely have to be part of the mix for the bracelet to have a place in the outfit that makes sense. I find little character or patina to the item; its cheap mass-production quality and material don't speak of Nature and the vitality of living things. Its physical character is somewhat shallow and lifeless -- quite contrary to the message it is supposed to send.

    So how about taking the message to another level and actually integrate Live Strong into the whole outfit, by wearing bolder, more lush, more alive materials and patterns and colors? Whatever the bracelet was supposed to remind you of or say to others, let the clothing you wear (and the way that you wear it) express that message. Then you will be transcending the narrowness of an advertising gimmick. You will not only be wearing a loud object on your wrist, you will be embodying your values in your personal style and the way you go about your day -- walking the talk.
