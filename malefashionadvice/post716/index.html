    How often do you re-wear outfits?

    I'm very changeable, and that goes for my changeability as well â€” sometimes I'll change outfits a couple of times in a day, and sometimes I'll wear pretty much the same thing for days in a row, or keep one outfit in a rotation and come back to it every few days.

    It depends partly on my mood and partly on opportunity. I tend to be very permissive with my moods, especially in terms of style; and even if the situation calls for a particular dress code, unless it's black tie I usually find there's a lot of leeway within that code.

    Sometimes I don't want to be so self-reflexive; that is, there are days or weeks or months when I want to avoid as much self-centered thought and effort as possible. Stop preening and get to living, you know? That's often when the personal uniform comes into play. It doesn't have to be the exact same white t-shirt with those exact same chinos, but I may rotate between two white t-shirts and a white OCBD, say, worn with the same one or two pairs of chinos over the course of a week.

    At other times, it's all about self-expression, and I feel almost uncomfortable in my skin if my clothing doesn't reflect my changing moods. And it works both ways: I'll also use my attire to change my moods, dressing more comfortably and simply when life is chaotic, dressing up when I'm down, or dressing more brightly or flamboyantly when everyday activities are getting stale.

    Having some knowledge about style and dressing well can make it easier to keep the dressing routine both simple and fresh. Having a solid wardrobe of basics that all go together well, and that are tailored to you, both literally (in terms of fit) and figuratively (in terms of your personal style), can make it almost effortless to throw together an outfit that looks good, works for you practically, and is comfortable to your body and mind.

    Another good tip is that the same core outfit, if it comprises classic elements, can change quite dramatically with a quick change of outerwear and footwear. The same chinos and OCBD become a whole new thing when either a faded denim jacket or a fitted casual blazer are worn on top; and likewise with shoes: the same outfit can change dramatically when based on sneakers, versus leather oxfords.

    One thing I almost never do is wear clothing that is dirty or smelly. (The exception is during extended wilderness activities like tracking and stalking where it's necessary â€” and fun â€” to get down in the dirt and mud.) Wearing clothing that has old layers of me or the city or the day on it is like descending into the animal realm; I feel the dullness come down like a veil. So even when wearing "the same" outfit, there's still a sense of movement and renewal for me as I swap fresh pieces into the outfit. This is based on the reality of the situation rather than on any principle or personal rule; if a shirt needs changing I'll change it, even if the day isn't over yet.
