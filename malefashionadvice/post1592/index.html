    Not sure if this belongs here, but I need help with hair styling products!

    The answer depends a lot on the kind of hairstyle you have, the properties of your hair (thick, thin, straight, curly, dense, fine, etc.).

    I have some experience in this area. Unless you're sure you want a gel, I'd suggest a paste or mud that is designated as "remoldable".

    Schwarzkopf Got2Be Paste is thick, with high hold. It maintains hold pretty well through high activity & high humidity, and has a fair degree of re-shape-ability. If necessary, a few drops of water spread on the palms will re-activate it. The downside is that it is sticky, and remains so on hair that's damp with sweat and humidity.

    Sebastian Molding Mud is much lighter, on the way to mousse rather than a hard paste. So it has less hold, but allows some types of hair to move more naturally (when dancing, for example), and is quite remoldable. It will not hold one particular shape as well, but by the same token it will also not keep your hair plastered to your head once you start sweating, like some gels. Depending on your hair, this could be your best bet.

    Lots of guys have luck with American Crew products. Their Fiber paste is between the above two products in its density, but my sense is that you'd be better served by choosing either higher hold or better shapeability.

    If you don't care about hold, only require some separation, and can stand the wilting that comes with a long night at one of the events that you're talking about, any of a number of thick waxes/pomades may do.

    If you are insistent on using a gel, I have found no better gel for sweaty, head-banging activity than AG Rubberizer Molding Resin. It holds very well through sweaty activity, with a bit of flexibility. The fragrance is... different, to the extent that it may make choosing between products easier.

    It is quite possible that none of these products will achieve what you're looking for. The solution may be to find the hairstyle and cut that allows your hair to look good no matter how it moves or lies. Of course, you'll have to pay for it when you find someone capable of recommending and achieving the right cut. In any case, product or not, it's not a bad plan.
