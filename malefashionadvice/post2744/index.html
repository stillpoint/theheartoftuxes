    How do I prevent dress shirt from clumping up around the waist of pants?

    There are a few reasons why shirts clump around the waist. Some of these may be anatomical, if for instance the relationship between belly and waistband causes the shirt to be pushed out with movement. The major issues, though, usually have to do with the cut and fit of the shirt.

    The worst culprit is perhaps the low armhole. Off-the-rack shirts are often made to accommodate as many body shapes as possible, so profit is made by selling more shirts and having to make fewer different cuts. The problem is that this doesn't even address an imagined 'average' body shape, but rather a body shape that matches the average plus a lot of margin for error.

    One very common feature of this strategy is the low armhole. The manufacturer creates a lot of space for differences in the arm and upper chest, so the shirt sleeve joins the body lower down at the side of the ribs rather than tucked up underneath the armpit. One might think this would be more comfortable as it gives you more room, but if you look at the geometry of it, it means that every time you raise your arm it creates a lever at the side of your ribcage which constricts arm movement and pulls the shirt up and out of the pants.

    The next issue is the extra fabric that billows around the waist and balloons at the small of the back in shirts that are not a slim, modern cut and well fitted or tailored to your form. Whether it's the extra fabric of a classic cut or a shirt that is slim in some places but loose around the waist, even 'properly sized' shirts most often require some tailoring to better follow the shape of body. Any extra fabric will sag and form a clump at the waist of the pants, and in addition may drag more material out in increments.

    Remedies such as rubberized waistbands that grip the shirt tails, shirt garters that stretch under the pants and connect to the lower legs, or methods of folding and tucking extra fabric don't fix the problem of poor fit. The best prevention for clumping is to have all of: high armhole, well-tailored fit, and flat belly. Note that most off-the-rack shirts can only be tailored so much; seldom perfectly. Better fit is achieved with made-to-measure or bespoke shirts sewn to your exact measurements.

    TL; DNR: The best prevention for clumping is to have all of: high armhole and well-tailored fit on the shirt, and a flat belly on your body.
