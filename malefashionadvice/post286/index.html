    Combining black and navy

    It is true that sometimes black and certain dark blues when paired look either 'off' or lifeless. It's also quite possible that people have seen mismatches (between, say, blue jeans and black dress shoes) and have assumed that the colors were the only culprit. I suppose it has happened often enough that it eventually became crystallized in a rule that, in my opinion, is far too broad.

    Some navy, and some dark browns, will appear to some people as an awkward match. In fact, a few people actually have a violent reaction to the combinations, responding as if someone is drawing their fingernails across a blackboard. Others don't see the problem.

    Two things are necessary: (1) check the mirror (or a photo); and (2) evolve your color sense. The latter includes studying color matching in a formal or informal way, trying new combinations, scanning images online or in magazines with an eye toward the color matching, and also requesting feedback from people who dress well. As with art, on the one hand you can see for yourself what you like; and on the other hand, as you educate yourself your tastes will develop. It's not that there's one overarching rule; but over time, knowledge and skill and subtlety will point more often to what looks good (and that view may then differ from those with less developed tastes).

The clashy quality is definitely there, and it can be played up, for better or worse; and with awareness of intent the combo can be seen as an in your face style statement. Or it can just look damn ugly (IMHO).
