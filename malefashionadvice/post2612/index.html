    To tuck or not to tuck?

    There are two guides for you to follow: (1) the situation, and (2) the expression that moves from within you. Both are important, but in the end it comes down to what you want to be and what you want to express.

    Also, you're probably in the best position to determine both of those points â€” the situation and your inner movement.

    As to the situation, we can probably safely say at least that untucking is not seen as too casual in most high school settings. Tucking in is more sharp and respectful. It could be seen as more mature and self-confident; or it could be seen as uptight, boring, and nerdy.

    Untucking is less structured and less rule-bound. It could also be seen as confident in a different way, as well as easy-going or rebellious; and on the other hand it could be seen as immature, lazy, and sloppy.

    I'd bet that there would be a mixture of views on the issue, as well as a large portion of the school population that doesn't notice or doesn't care.

    Whether it's too casual or too formal really depends on the culture of your particular high school, and on how a particular look suits who you are and what you're on about. If there's one thing high school kids are good at, it's sensing bulls%#; and although everyone* is experimenting with styles and trying out new things in their own ways, if kids feel that you're wearing a look that doesn't match who you are inside (or who they think you are), they can be pretty brutal.

    So I'd like to ask you about what is happening in your own mind about it. Are you interested in trying a more mature, put-together look because you might be ready, say, to step into new levels of responsibility? Or are you interested in letting it 'hang out' more, because maybe you feel like letting loose and living more freely? It could very well be both!

    If you really want to establish your freedom of expression, why not try both ways? Try tucked and put-together one day, and untucked and laid back the next. Neither way is good or bad in itself; they just say different things and affect people in different ways, like words or music.

    Maybe what MFAers prefer is not the important point for you.
