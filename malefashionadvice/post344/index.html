    What in the hell am I supposed to wear with these?!

    Just wear neutrals in the rest of your pieces, perhaps one subdued pattern in a shirt, sweater, or jacket, and you're good to go. The great thing about coloured pants is they make the rest of the outfit easy -- you already have the unique, eye-catching bit, so just fill the rest in with neutrals (browns, greys, olive green, indigo or navy blue).

    False neutrals like black and white will also work with these pants if they make sense within the rest of the outfit; that is, if the situation calls for something that contrasty and bright (warm weather/daytime) or dark (evening).

    The basic rules of style apply: make sure all pieces fit well, and make sue that whatever neutral you wear near your face is a great color for your skin tone.

    If you're into color combining, blues would go well. Burgundy and other deep reds would also combine well, but would be very vibrant. Dark green is a classic varsity combination with purple, but perhaps better for autumn. And the complementary color range for purple, to intensify the energy between colors, would be yellow and orange.

    So if you're going for a sharp look with the one colorful piece, use neutrals (maybe a grey henley with a denim trucker jacket; or a tan poplin shirt with a brown Harrington or leather jacket). If you want to combine colors but keep it cool, try mixed blues (like a royal blue collared shirt with a subdued pattern such as pindots, and a dark navy or dark indigo jacket or sweater; a simple dark chambray would also do well). If you want to go for vibrant colors, try ochre or rust orange up top.

    The way to combine colors with a vivid piece is to keep the colors muted or dark. So rather than going with a bright lemon or mustard yellow, which in combination with purple would knock the eyes out of people's heads, you use a more subdued, earthy yellow like ochre, or even a deep tan -- it verges on neutral, but since it has some of the complementary (opposite) color it still makes the colors pop. This you remain dignified, but very much in command of people's attention.

    The pants are a warm-weather pastel, so avoid pastels or muted colors for two reasons: without contrast, the outfit will look foggy and receding, if not feminine; and most pastels will come off as easter-egg colors with the purple. If you want to go for a muted look intentionally, maybe try a light grey sweater that will provide the least contrast, along with charcoal socks and grey bucks.

    Edit: bonus tip: Check the place where you bought the piece, in this case J Crew, for ideas on combining. Mannequins and sales associates in-store, model photos and shopping assistants online, and catalog images will all equip you with some good ideas. Note that the J Crew corporate stylists are unapologetic about combining patterns and colors that others might find questionable together.
