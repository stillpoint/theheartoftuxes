    Apologies for the Abrams lens flares, but what does MFA think of my outfit?

    As Oprah says, when you know better, you do better. I also used to wear the white tee exposed at the collar; but now that I've had a look at myself in photos and my eye has developed somewhat, I know that it's not going to look good.

    One way you can wear the t-shirt exposed is within a laid-back outfit, with the intent made obvious: open 3 or 4 (not just 1 or 2) buttons; and let other parts of the outfit be a bit slouchy, worn-in, untucked, or otherwise slightly disheveled. Shirt pulled out slightly on one side, long-hemmed jeans that drag a bit, a belt end not fastened in the loop -- these set the stage for the t-shirt reveal.

    You have a concern that just shirt, jeans, and shoes will be boring. This is understandable; but with more knowledge and skill, you'll be able to make those three simple elements work for you. In fact, if you start there and gain expertise in combining only shirt, pants, and shoes, you will always be able to build an outfit from its basic foundation.

    Trying to glean interest by adding a special article of clothing is a less mature stylistic approach. Interest should come primarily from the unity of the outfit and the interplay of the elements.

    These considerations are all significant, of themselves as well as in their interplay with each other (the first ones are perhaps most important):

    • Fit and cut

    • Line and proportion

    • Color

    • Pattern

    • Fabric and texture

    Your instinct about the all-dark outfit being drab was correct; so if you keep developing your eye you should do well. Check the mirror (or photos) often to analyze what's working and what's not.

    In particular, consider the range of contrast available between pieces. High contrast can be either exciting or annoying. Low contrast can be either boring or calming or intense. Medium contrast tends to offer many interesting options.

    So whenever putting together an outfit for the next while, you might want to consider how you can achieve an interesting amount of contrast between the upper and lower halves. If you start with a color that is ideal next to your face (the color that, next to your skin and hair and eye color, makes you look healthy and happy and alive), then you will consider what pants will provide some measure of contrast, some lighter or darker shade that will inject an interesting but not overwhelming difference into the look.
