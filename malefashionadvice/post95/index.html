    Does anyone else hate the shopping experience as much as me?

    I have some advice; take it or leave it, as useful:

    At some point I realized that if I stay centered I will have a much more pleasant shopping experience. Shopping can be exhausting in the same way that visiting museums and art galleries can be exhausting: too much energy is spent through the eyes; so much looking, seeking, choosing. Add to this the anxiety that can arise around throngs of other people, and it's clear why the patience of many shoppers can quickly wear thin.

    I think a big part of this is that the mindset of seeking an object (looking for your sought-after item of clothing, or your ideal purchase) takes you outside of yourself. Your mind is way ahead in the future, attached to the holy grail you seek, instead of present and dealing with the moment. When you have a seeking attitude like this, then other people become obstacles — they're 'in the way' of what you seek. This is effectively demonstrated in your example of the people in the way of what you're trying to look at.

    But there's a different way of moving through your day that doesn't have as much seeking; or at least it's more patient and open-handed. This is when you are just where you are, dealing with what's facing you. Then other people aren't 'in your way', they're just where they are, and you are just where you are. You can ask them to move, or you can move around them, or you can wait, and all of these are okay because the most important thing is no longer that you have to get what you're seeking as soon as possible. The most important thing is that you are present in your life, moment to moment.

    This is the same as saying "it's not the destination, but the journey that matters". If you want your shopping journey to be better, focus more on the way you go about it, not so much on the result.

    Another point is that introverted and extroverted people relate to crowds in completely different ways. Introverts get their energy from solitude. They may sometimes enjoy being in large groups, but it does require them to expend their energy. Extroverts, on the other hand, get energy from others. Big crowds energize them. It's being alone that drains extroverts.

    So how can you avoid losing your energy in a crowd if you're an introvert? You can at least help yourself by what I suggested earlier — letting go of the seeking mind, and being present and centered in yourself. When I go shopping or do other activities in a big crowd, I usually stay mindful of my feet. You can try this; maybe it will help. Focus on the actual sensation of your feet standing or walking. At all times, keep this awareness. Your energy will not fly off as easily, and you will be more responsive to the situations that appear around you. When you are centered like that, you can also have a lot more focus in your tasks (shopping included) because you will not be distracted as easily.
