    What Shoes with Brown Jeans

    With dark brown jeans, a mid-to-light neutral would go very well.

    The contrast is the main thing. The black shoes you pictured are too close in shade, and come off looking mismatched. The same would be true of dark navy. Dark brown would not necessarily be mismatched, but would be too matchy-matchy.

    The white pair of Onitsukas (right?) you posted are better than the black; but then you have too much contrast: given the color of the pants, the look is very deep and grounded above, but light and sporty below -- heavy above and light below, a reverse of the usual and natural balance of things (heavy base, light top). It could work if you had other light and sporty accents -- playful, sporty shirt and accessories in light colors (e.g., white belt); but then you'd be tailoring the rest of the outfit to match the shoes rather than having a versatile shoe-pant combination.

    So if the brown jeans will be major players in your rotation, check out mid-to-light grey, tan, sand, buff, khaki, or taupe, lighter browns (more interesting with a hint of tint like red-brown), and deep red-browns.

    Sleek sneakers like the Onitsukas are versatile; but with a heavy-fabric jean in a heavy color, you may want a more substantial shoe or boot. Something more grounded and possibly more masculine. Chukkas, desert boots (check out Palladium and similar, which still have a bit of the sneaker vibe but in a chunkier, more grounded format); or, if you want something that can be dressed up and still be fun and casual, check out bucks. Light grey or tan bucks would do very well for you. Dressier still would be wingtips and more plain oxfords.

    If you really want a combo that will pop, see how a deep red will use the depth of the jeans color rather than float out on its own like the white does.

    In terms of colors for shirt and jacket, the aforementioned deep red is an intense but classy choice. Burgundy, etc., also good. If your image is not so intense, light blues from pale to sky blue go very well with dark brown. A light blue shirt under a tan jacket (3 contrasting, yet unified, shades) would be a superb palette to showcase any of the previously recommended shoes. Add a couple of light-brown leather accessories, and Bob's yer uncle.
