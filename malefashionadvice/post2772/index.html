    Stretching a wardrobe

    If the pieces you own work together in terms of colour and style, then you have quite a huge number of possible combinations there.

    Furthermore, if the outfits fit well and make you look great, it doesn't matter if people notice you wearing the same ones a lot -- you'll be looking great, comfortable in what you're wearing, and they will hopefully make sense with who you are.

    Your clothes should reflect your personal style. Do these pieces speak of who you are or the image you want to project? Or are they sort of default purchases -- because they were on sale, or the first items you happened to see, or because you don't yet know what kind of style options are available to you? When clothes look like they belong on you, it all makes sense to the viewer, and comes off as 'looking good'.

    The items should mostly go together well in terms of colour, achieving interesting contrast, and being of appropriate genre and level of formality. Choosing a particular colour palette helps to stretch your wardrobe. It should be a palette that makes you look great.

    If you have the basics down (and it seems that you may), with enough neutral and other easily combined colors, your future purchases can be interesting statement pieces that combine well (starting with nice and perhaps more daring outerwear), or accessories that add a spark of style (like belt, watch, glasses/sunglasses, shoes, etc.). Shoes and outerwear add the most value in terms of sprucing up an outfit with a single piece, and both can be used day after day together with many different outfits.

    ~

    There's a best-selling book out on the magic of tidying, which offers a way of relating to possessions that could be very meaningful to you: the author asks you to hold each item in your hands and ask if it sparks joy for you -- do you love it?

    You might try that with your clothing. It's one thing to stretch a wardrobe, but to do it arbitrarily and for the sake of others drains your life of joy. Are these pieces of clothing things that you want to wear? Do you feel right in them? If not, I would encourage you to let go of the ones that aren't helping you feel satisfied and happy. If you're happy with what you're wearing, and if it makes sense with who you are and who you want to be, it won't be such an issue what others think.
