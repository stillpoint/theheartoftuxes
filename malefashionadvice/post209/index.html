    Starting to revamp my wardrobe, how'd I do on my first time out?

    I need to see the whole outfit, including the shoes. This is necessary, for us to see how the shoes fit into the scheme, to see the proportions correctly, and to get a sense of the look as a whole â€” the immediate impact of it all.

    I like the differing shades of blue; the pants and shirt relate to each other in hue, and have enough contrast to be interesting. Because of the simple color scheme, the white buttons stand out much more as a design feature.

    I like the contrasting color of belt. It's a color that goes well with the blues, it's bright enough to bring some pep to the outfit, and it doesn't go overboard. Any brighter, and the belt would be too much of a cutting point, separating top from bottom. If the shoes match well, it should look fine.

    The pieces fit very well (the jeans are maybe too tight; but I'd like to see the bottom of the leg and the shoes to be able to judge definitively). It's a simple, clean look. Well done.

    Possible upgrades:

    • Glasses with more character (which may mean thicker, with more presence on your face), and that match the saddle-tan belt. Brown, a warm color, or tortoiseshell are possibilities.

    • A hairstyle that's frames/exposes the face better, and shows more movement. This may be achieved with a cut and product, or possibly with product alone.

    • An accessory or piece of flair, a matching watch being the first choice.
