    title: Polos: tucked or untucked? summary: 3.0 authors: TheHeartofTuxes date: 2012-05-19 17:35:44 some_url:

    Polos without the extra-long back tail can be, and usually are, worn untucked. Sometimes you'll find polo shirts with a slightly longer (by an inch) back tail that can still be worn untucked.

    Those with long tails are intended to be tucked. They're essentially tennis shirts, and the extra fabric keeps them in the pants even during vigorous physical activity.

    Part of the feared 'Best Buy employee' look may be the tucking; but other factors play into it. In your linked image you're wearing extremely casual jeans that are loose, faded and worn in, and have a high waist in back. Together with the chunky belt, that creates a look that is quite far from a tucked-polo level of formality. It's a bit of a mullet â€” the difference between buttoned-down and somewhat sharp look on top and the laid-back, sloppy look below.

    If you take care of the fit, style, and level of formality of the rest of your outfit, the tucked polo will be more appropriate.

    Color matching is also an issue that makes the polo look out of place. Faded indigo together with a navy top is a boyish, unsophisticated combination. (The fit exaggerates that.) Lack of integrity in the outfit can make the shirt look like a required uniform, like something that you have to wear regardless of what pants you have on.

    Here the fit, the colors, and the style and level of formality all support the tucked look. The belt is slimmer, more refined; the color of the shirt is also a bit more sophisticated, and the chinos are a sharper match.

    I'm not a fan of the black accessories here; but because they are sleek and sharp, tucked-in looks like it belongs.

    With faded blue jeans, something from the complementary side of the color wheel looks good: burgundy, deep red, patterned red such as gingham or plaid, and pink. (Since complementaries intensify each other, bright red would become too vivid.) Many browns are also a good match, as well as some whites.

    Untucked can also look good, but in a more casual range. One of the risks with all untucked shirts is that the top half of the body appears elongated; so the length of the tails is critical, and you should check the mirror for how it looks. This is much too long. But this is a better length.
