    Need tie advice

    You have some great pattern with the glen plaid (what they call a grid) in the jacket; it has some movement but at a distance reads as solid grey. With the plain shirt you can go for a solid tie or a tie with a different pattern; i.e., not plaid, check, or thin stripe. That leaves a lot of leeway for expression.

    Google "color wheel" to help understand color relationships. Color-wise, you can go for a different, contrasting blue; and another safe but interesting option is to use color families on the color wheel which are on either side of the color you're trying to match. This rule of thumb will serve you well in the future. In this case, you're matching aqua blue; so aqua greens and purple blues are sharp choices for ties.

    I actually like the tie option shown by UpTheDownEscalator. The blue contrasts the shirt; it's a color that will show up very nicely with the tan & grey patterned jacket; the pattern picks up the blue in the shirt, the pattern type differs enough from the jacket, and it doesn't impinge on the large fields of blue in the tie.

    With the 'either side' color families, you could do a grenadine tie in teal or in indigo. The colors would provide an elegant, interesting harmony; and the grenadine weave would provide a texture to contrast the shirt and jacket (adding another interesting feature without having competing patterns; adding a new texture to the outfit is a good substitute for a pattern when you're in doubt as to how busy it may look). Since with this option you have some movement in the jacket, and some interest between the shirt-tie colors, you could also do a straight solid silk tie without it being boring. Foulard patterns will also go well; and you can get as festive as you like with them.

    A third option, for the flashy personality, is to do a complementary color with the tie. That means orange in this case, which would really pop!

    The pocket square is done in an unrelated color, and a different texture if you can swing it. You could do the other 'either side' color from the tie color; or either side of the complement if you want flash (so, orange-red or peach); but you might have more luck seeking out patterns like multicolor stripes, paisley, or cream linen weaves and choosing what looks best in a mirror.
