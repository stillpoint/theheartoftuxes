    Does this outfit work at all?

    I wouldn't know if the interplay between shirt and jacket check patterns would be too energetic unless I saw them together. It was just a heads-up to a possible issue.

    The other shirt you're linking to is a pretty big scale for a gingham, but may also work. It is a different feel, though. The first one is more reserved; this one is more 'out there'.

    But do notice how good the shirt looks with the model's rust pants. A plain monochromatic pattern looks good next to a field of strong color. The solid color brings interest and groundedness, and the monochrome pattern adds motion or rhythm without interfering with the color. Without the color to anchor it, the plain pattern has more of a lost feeling, almost like the outfit is starting to disperse into thin air. Imagine a whole-body outfit of neutral-colored camouflage; see how it would break up your profile and make your form recede into the background and become invisible. Is that what you are trying to do?

    Indigo (in the jeans) is a strong grounding element; but in your case it doesn't help as much because it is monotonous along with the blue-and-white check (that from a distance looks like a dispersing grey) and the grey-and-white check â€” all these plain, dim influences.

    A pocket square may help; but it has to be in the right context. Out of context, it looks contrived. What situations will you be entering? What image do you want to project? A single very colorful pocket square within a very plain outfit can look sharp, or it can look like a lame attempt; it's a fine line between the two.

    Those two pocket squares are also pretty conservative. Lack of vibrancy in what could be the only flourish of color in your outfit could end up being a sad disappointment to the viewer, like wearing a wilted flower.

    I'm getting the sense that you're afraid of or avoiding color. Is there an intent behind that, or is it because you haven't ventured into that territory yet?

    • It has a bit of a country vibe, but see how happy a red gingham looks with blue jeans.

    • If you don't want it quite that summery, and keep some blue in the mix with a multicolor gingham

    • Purple gingham will also be a great choice for adding color to the outfit in question.

    • Subtle striped colors like lavender stripe, as well as pastel solids like solid lavender are every bit as good, as long as the particular hue is good with your skin tone.
