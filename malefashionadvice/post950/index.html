    Critique my shoe collection

    Any critique we provide can't be complete, because we have little idea of the context. What roles are the shoes expected to fill? What activities will be important to you? What outfits will they be worn with; what wardrobe will you have to draw on; what colors and styles will you be working with? Any piece of clothing is neither good nor bad; it just works or doesn't work within a particular context.

    Anway, doing my best; from left to right:

    • The square and bicycle-stitched toe shoes aren't going to win you any friends here. They look like they'd be worn by a 50-year-old man stuck in a 2-decade-old wardrobe. The shoes may find some play if you run with a foreign crowd; but for contemporary North America you should be much more discriminating in buying dress shoes, especially at your age. Check the sidebar guide to shoes.

    • I can't imagine any purpose, ever, for black square-toed monkstrap shoes. This is a bad mish-mosh of styles. The black color is too conservative, the monkstrap too decorative, the bicycle stitching too quaint, and the square toe too amateurish for any integrity to be possible between your footwear and your outfit. If you want to jump into monkstrap territory (which is perhaps a more advanced style move), take to heart the nature of the shoe as a very elegant yet declarative statement. The foot shape should be elegant; the color should be rich and full of character; the strap and buckle themselves should be of a higher quality; and it should be worn with confidence in a similarly meticulous and elegant outfit. But save this for after you have your basics down pat.

    • The chukkas are a good, versatile shoe. The color is good; the full grain leather is a nice change from the more common desert boot suede. Their profile is a bit chunky, both from the welt that extends from the upper and from their height; but that's okay. It just leaves you in a casual, gregarious American genre instead of a more sleek style. The soles of the Clarks are synthetic rather than real rubber; but that's not a deal-breaker, especially for an otherwise well-made casual shoe. These shoes will take you from quite casual to sharp-casual and business casual with ease.

    • The Topsiders boat shoes are fairly classic, and the dark brown will be versatile with a lot of summer wear. The color may not be perfect with dark jeans, and may stand out as too contrasty with light colored shorts, but then you have your chukkas for the former and your classic sneakers or plimsolls (which you will buy in place of the Nikes) for the latter. The boat shoes look so squat it's hard to believe they're the same size as the other shoes pictured. Also firmly in the American classic style. The white contrast soles are trendy this year and may not be so trendy in the near future; but they're not a big problem, and once they gather some dirt the contrast won't be as apparent.
