    Going formal, attempt number two.

    I'm sorry; that jacket is too small for you. First, and most critically, the shoulders are too narrow. The shoulder seam should be at the corner of your own shoulder; but the curve of your shoulder can clearly be seen in the picture, pushing out against the sleeve material. So rather than sitting atop your shoulders comfortably and providing a sleek, straight, wide line, the padding bunches and twists. Your shoulders are even pulling the fabric out at the chest, evidenced by the wrinkle next to the lapel.

    Next, the arms are much too narrow. Your shirt sleeve should ride smoothly and independently within the jacket sleeve. One shouldn't see bunching at the forearm or wrinkling at the upper arm, and the shape of the arm certainly shouldn't be visible. But these are apparent in your photo because the fabric is wrapping tightly against your arms.

    Although it is less drastic than the previous two issues, there is some tugging at the button, which means that the girth of the jacket is too small -- at the waist, or possibly at the chest and hips.

    There also appears to be some taper in the legs. Though we can't see the pants completely, I would wonder if the entire suit is too small, and thus if the pants are also too tight around the legs.

    A suit is a highly refined enhancement of the body. It makes the frame appear more athletic and well-proportioned, makes the posture appear upright and noble, and brings attention upward to the face. It achieves its design when its components -- padding, canvas, outer fabric, lining, and stitching -- sit, drape, and move in correct relationship to the body wearing it. Correct fit is required for the smooth lines and lively movement of the suit to come into play. Shoulders should appear as an extension of your own; arms should hang smoothly; torso should have a smooth and integrated appearance.

    Unfortunately, the problems with this jacket can't be corrected for you. There's no way to widen the shoulders without completely resewing the jacket.

    You can't always find an honest and knowledgeable salesperson these days; but if you do, have him or her fit you correctly. Or at least make sure you can consult with a tailor before the sale is final. Also have your tailor measure you; and write down the measurements for future reference when shopping.

    Good luck.
