    Anyone know of something definitively Swiss and fashion related? Budget is probably no higher than $200.

    The first company that leaps to mind is Victorinox. They offer designs that are decidedly Swiss, but as such focus heavily on the use of black, and offer cuts that will definitely be fairly unique in most places in North America.

    The hallmark look from Victorinox may be their 3/4 length quilted jackets, overshirts, and blazers for men. Outerwear often comes replete with patch pockets, or at least uncommon pocket treatments. But these will be out of your price range.

    Given the budget as well as the risks involved in buying for someone else without the opportunity for fitting, you may be better off seeking accessories rather than basic attire. This also allows you to take more risk with the design, as accessories are meant to be accents whereas a very contemporary Euro 'statement' coat, for example, may easily overwhelm the rest of any outfit you wear. Focusing on accessories can also open up more traditional options, while you'd avoid them like the plague when seeking shirts or pants.

    A Victorinox scarf could be a nice acquisition, if you're into the Fairisle-type patterns.

    Besides watches and chocolate, another thing the Swiss do very well that's more pertinent to the discussion is activewear, and in particular alpine activewear. If you're into skiing, mountaineering, hiking, and the like, maybe you'd like a zip-up mock-neck sweater or something along those lines.

    If your hope is more along the lines of current fashion, then I'd suggest hopping on a Swiss fashion forum or /r/Switzerland and asking about the best men's fashion stores to visit on or near Bahnhof Strasse. You may even luck out by being able to browse an online catalog before your mom's visit. Ask about boutiques rather than large chains, as they'll likely have the more unique offerings.
