    How to look confident?

    This is outside of the scope of MFA, and might be better posted in other subreddits (malelifestyle, lifeprotips, seduction, and others). But since you ask....

    ~~~~~

    Know yourself; and be sincere.

    What are your actual qualities? What are your actual values? What is and isn't important to you, despite what others are doing? Where are your strengths and weaknesses? How has your life story shaped you, in positive and negative ways?

    When you rely on the truth, you have a rock-solid basis. But if you try to don an image that doesn't suit who you are, there will always be an inner conflict, a self-doubt.

    Some of the 'things you are' are not yet expressed. The suggestions of others to "fake it 'til you make it" and "act confident" are not phony, but rather focus on something you already have that you may not know that you have. Your true self, because it is true, is already confident; so you can express it, bringing out the previously hidden quality, and exercise it, making it stronger.

    A big part of that is posture. The body and mind and environment condition each other. The posture of your mind affects your body and environment. The posture of your body and environment affect your mind. An upright but relaxed body posture, and an organized and functional living space, will affect your mind in positive ways. This, of course, includes dressing well and in a way that matches who you are.

    Doing vigorous physical activity can help open the posture to a more natural stance. You want to get out of rigid tension and into a flowing physicality; so frequent full-body movement and expression is very helpful. Not only building muscles, but using your body to express yourself artistically or competitively will help the body-mind posture be more natural and confident.

    Another related point is that you tend to be confident when you do what you are good at or what you love to do. What's you area of expertise? What do you get so engaged in that you forget yourself?

    The other side is that achievement will lead to confidence. Go and do a lot of things!

    Men need constant challenge. The aim is not to seek out comfort, but to seek out challenges that can test your heart and will. Occasional failure teaches you that you have an inner value beyond success and failure; but if you only avoid failure you will have a hard time discovering that. So always try expanding your comfort zone — do a lot of things that are a bit new, difficult, or uncomfortable; and once in a while do a few things that are way out of your comfort zone.

    A frequent 'defining challenge' may be to change one habit for a week, just to know you can do it; or just to exercise the part of you that's willing to try. A big 'defining challenge' may be to do something once a year that terrifies you or that you could never imagine yourself doing.

    Finally, one of the best ways to be confident — and a way that is immediately available to you — is to change the focus from yourself to others. Instead of worrying how you look and feel, look to help others or put others at ease. Rather than being the 'guest' in every situation, hoping for the right things to come your way, play the 'host' and take care of others. Being the guest is coming from a place of poverty ("I don't yet have what I need"); being the host is coming from a place of ability ("whatever the situation, I have something to offer").
