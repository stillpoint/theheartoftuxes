    Are these pants reasonable or ridiculous?

    Fantastic if you know how to use them, and if they fit in with the rest of your outfit(s) in a cohesive style; maybe a bit cheesy if you don't know how to use them. My sense is that guys don't really realize how definite, non-neutral colors can (A) really free up wardrobes otherwise based on neutrals and (B) bring a lot more vitality to their look.

    Regarding point (A): for example, a strong color like the clear sky blue in the jeans can make it a no-brainer for putting together the rest of the outfit — you already have your statement piece, the piece that is eye-catching and expresses quite a bit of verve — so just fill in the rest of the outfit with neutrals and you're good to go. Knowing that all solid colors are being used, you can toss in a single patterned piece (for greater interest through movement within the patterned piece and interplay between it and the other pieces), and assuming it all fits well you have an instant high-class outfit.

    If on the other hand you're only working with safe neutral colors in your wardrobe's pallette, you have to figure out how to inject interest with contrast, tonal variation, pattern, texture, and design features. While this can also produce great outfits, it takes more skill and knowledge; and it still lacks the vivacity and style of a single bold-colored piece on a stalwart supportive background of neutrals.

    'Neutrals' include greys, browns, navy, dark indigo as in jeans, olive, taupe, and white (though because of its starkness white will perhaps be used in fewer situations). Black is commonly considered a neutral, but not by me; it brings light and bright colors into very sharp contrast, which depending on the look you're aiming at may make for awkward matching, and it is sometimes also a disturbing match with other dark colors. Consider the qualities expressed by the piece you're considering buying, and that will give you more of an indicator of whether you should or shouldn't. "I like it, but don't know why" is not good enough. Investigate! What keywords or phrases come up for you when you see them? Sunny, bright but soothing, sky-like, fresh, light, casual, outdoorsy.... (And you will have your own associations.) This tells you the kinds of situations in which they will be most appropriate. For instance, they would work well in fresh, outdoors, warm-weather, casual situations; whereas on cold, dark winter days they could only be seen as a very bold (and depending on your swag, likely too effortful) move to add a brightness that doesn't belong naturally to the situation.

    If you have a core wardrobe of neutrals and you like the pants, and if they fit in with a style and level of formality that makes sense for your lifestyle (your common activities and locales), go for it. If your wardrobe is a random mix of colors and patterns, think twice about how you're going to match the jeans.

    In fact, so many of the questions posted on MFA would be rendered immaterial if guys took the time to consider the style that suits their life and the possible matches achievable with their current wardrobe. You probably already have the ability to take your shirts and shoes out, lay them on your bed, and picture the combinations that could work and the ones that would be bad. And if RD has a good return policy there is always the option of buying the pants, trying them with your whole wardrobe, and returning them if they don't work for you. (It's faster to work out combinations on the bed than actually wearing them all on the body, but once you have some good outfits you can confirm by trying on in front of a full-length mirror).
