    Questions about matching colors

    That really depends on the type of jeans and the color and darkness of their wash, as well as the image you're trying to project.

    A rule of thumb that is usually, but not necessarily always, followed is to provide some contrast between top and bottom halves of the outfit, without going all the way to opposite ends. The contrast can be in terms of shade (lightness/darkness) or in terms of hue (different color).

    As to the waffle-knit crew necks you linked to, both the black and the white are very stark, and will look awkward. Black can actually be difficult to match; but it would go better with medium grey or tan than with blue. Harsh white rarely looks good in a pullover, unless it's in the context of a bright summery outfit meant for wearing outdoors.

    The light blue is a great color. The issue there is the possible similarity to the blue jeans. If it is similar to the jeans color, you can either get a unitard look or a very annoying slightly-unmatching color combination. If the jeans are a darker wash, the light blue should look great. You will want to mitigate the amount of blue in the outfit with the addition of other elements -- brown shoes and belt, for instance, or white shirt (collared or henley) under the pullover. Other colors/patterns in combination could work to break up the sea of blue as well.

    The orange-red is a complementary (opposite) color to blue, which means that they bring out the intensity in each other. A red pullover with blue jeans will be much too jarring a difference. It would be more comfortable with tan to medium brown khakis (camel, caf√© au lait, sienna). If you want the intensity that complementary colors provide, for instance to achieve a very fresh quality, then tone down or fade the complementary color. So, instead of a bright red, a deep brick red would look great with blue jeans. Or, in the other, brighter, direction: a pink shirt or a red-and-white check that reads as pink from a distance would both also look striking with blue jeans.

    The eggplant provides a nice difference to blue jeans, since it is a near neighbor to indigo. (On the other side of blue on the color wheel is green, some hues of which could also do well with jeans.) The issues with the eggplant color are twofold. First; if the darkness of the top (eggplant) is the same as the darkness of the bottom (dark-wash jeans), the overall look of the outfit will be deep and dark -- not terrible, but also maybe not using the full potential of contrast and movement. It could look drab or like an unskilled matching. Second; eggplant is verging on a novelty color. You can wear it occasionally; but too often or in the wrong way and you can end up being associated with Mr. Grape or "Hey, Koolaid!"

    Greys and browns tend to go well with all kinds of blue jeans. If you follow the principle of some but not too much contrast, and add movement or interest with layers and accessories, those color combos should be easy to match.
