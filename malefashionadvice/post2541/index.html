    I like to wear slim pants, but I wear a size 11.5 shoe and am only 5'9", should I just stick with boot cut?

    Pictures would allow for more definitive advice.

    The fact that you are aware of the issue of proportion is a good sign. I think you probably have a good sense of things just by looking in the mirror or at photos. I tend to think that a subtle bootcut is a good solution in some cases (such as yours), but many others on and off MFA dislike the bootcut. Be aware that bootcut is not dressy, and that it requires substantial visual mass in the footwear; so I wouldn't recommend it in most cases where you'd be wearing dress shoes.

    5'9" is not so very short. My guess is that you can split the difference: you don't have to go full-on skinny or full-on bootcut; a slim pant with a leg opening that allows for some drape over the shoe may do well. I'd look at having the tailor put a half break in the pant â€” enough to drape over the shoe, but not so much that it causes a big disruption in the long line of the leg. (Although Robert Downey Jr., also 5'9" tall, employs full break in all his pants, no matter how wide or slim the leg. Maybe because it provides a good frame for his daring shoe choices.)

    You are also aware that the shoes you wear are part of the equation. At some time in the future, you may do well to replace your pointed dress shoes with ones that are a bit more rounded, or that otherwise de-emphasize the length of the foot. (Square toe is usually frowned upon, though.) Again, the mirror check and feedback on photos will be more decisive that these speculations.
