    A bit of a shoe challenge for you guys... (first MFA post)

    Professional; comfortable; reasonably breathable; low maintenance — got it.

    I'd advise against Skechers, not only for their poor and unprofessional aesthetic design, but because they are not likely to be very breathable due to the cheaper materials they're made of, and primarily because they have terrible cushioning. Skechers are not made with a midsole; there's just the outer sole and a foamy insole, which itself will compress over time.

    One would think well-made desert boots would be a good option, fitting all your criteria (they were originally designed for desert travel, after all); but then there are the motor oils and fluids to contend with. The crepe soles that most desert boots have will disintegrate quickly in contact with oil and gasoline. Ditto most sneaker soles: though they may hold out slightly better than crepe-soled boots, they will still break down in contact with oil. Beware of tightly woven or treated canvas as well, since it can at times be even less breathable than leather.

    The first shoe that came to mind to suit your needs is a Dr. Martens. The Doc Martens shoes/boots are famous for their comfort, and most of them use a very durable oil- and slip-resistant sole. The brand features typically chunky shoes with thick soles — something that may be hard to avoid given your requirements — but lately they offer a few designs that tone down the 'Frankenstein' look:

    • A suede or buck should be comparably breathable.

    • There's a two-eyelet blucher that's not half bad, available in a nice chestnut brown as well as black.

    • The sole of their desert boot differs from the original. You'd have to check with Doc Martens customer service to see if it's oil-resistant.

    If you want a standard oil-resistant lace-up shoe for long hours on your feet, you'll find many very similar options at military outlets and uniform stores that supply police, security guards, and EMT workers. Because of the need for a proper sole, you may not be able to avoid a somewhat chunky look; but some choices will be, let's say, "less ugly" than others.

    Wolverine makes a great boot that could work for you: the Beck Desert Suede Wedge. The sole is oil resistant. This boot looks better than most other options I've seen. My concern is that the leather seems like it may be thicker than in some desert boots, so may not breathe as well. If I were you I'd try to find a store or site with a good return policy so I could wear the boots at home and determine their comfort level before committing to buy.

    Finally, if you are set on sneakers you should research footwear made for motor sports. Puma Speed Cat SD are made with oil-resistant soles, as are several other Puma lines (including their Ferrari shoes). Other companies may cater to motor sports as well.

    Edit: You may find, upon doing some research, that boat shoes will have soles resistant to oils, since they are made for working around boats and along docksides. Their look, however, is decidedly not professional, and they offer very little support and very little cushioning for long-term standing and walking. But you might talk to the boat shoe companies (Sperry, Sebago) to see if they offer a shoe with a good sole, but with more substantial construction.
