    I'll reboot the gist of my previous comment: the impact of a formal outfit should come from your true and good character shining through, not from gimmicky style moves. The formal outfit does not obscure or stand in for you; it provides a unified structure that presents you at your best, and it is up to you (not articles of clothing) to fill it out with your own personality. That's why, to my mind anyway, attempts to shake it up with quirky casual pieces seem to be a fearful retreat from the situation.

    A dinner jacket says "here I am" in a stark and open way. The black and white palette, the sheen of the fabric and shoes, the structure of the pieces, the defined frame for the face â€” it's all a set-up, a platform for the person behind the suit. It's then up to the wearer to step up to the plate with his own social graces, charisma, confidence, and sincerity. I think many (most?) guys don't understand this but sense it, and fear that without some other flash or flair they will fall short. It's a gut-check. It's where the rubber meets the road in men's style. "Am I enough?"

    This is partly why the dinner jacket is appropriate for some weddings. The groom is presenting himself at his best, but without embellishment. No gimmick necessary, just the man. It is a sincere and open moment: "here I am, as-is". And the groom is also stepping into his own self-possession: "here I am, and I am enough".
