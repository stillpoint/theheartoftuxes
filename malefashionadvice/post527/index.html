    Frangrance/cologne advice for REAL people, not people who really give a shit about the intricacy of perfume.

    I like your emphasis on context; the setting and purpose are important points in choosing your style. I also agree that there can be a lot of pretentiousness around fragrances, as with many of the cultivated pursuits of humans. The very fact of cultivation means that some will be more attuned to the subtleties, others will be relatively clumsy in their approach, and others will seem to go overboard in their refinement, becoming more arcane and unapproachable. But you also seem to encourage an attitude of refusing to refine both one's senses and one's views. Is that what you intend to convey?

    "If you like it, go for it" is a valid encouragement, but only in combination with a growing awareness. If that's all that you have, then you can easily get stuck in pig heaven: "I like crap! That's all I need!"

    And to insist that cologne is primarily for the purpose of hooking up with someone may be realistic for many, but also boorish and beside the point for others. Like food, wine, art, and indeed fashion, fragrances can (and maybe should) be enjoyed for their own sake, because they can be sublime examples of the human ability to extract essences from nature and make something completely new and uplifting. This is in fact the pivotal difference between culture and pretension, between maturity and immaturity; if you're only wearing something to attract a reaction, and not because you actually understand and like it yourself, how pretentious is that?

    I often see a complete misunderstanding of Old-World European refinement among Americans. They think that it's snobbish, but they miss the fact that (many) Europeans merely enjoy a hands-on, sense-filled engagement with life. It's not that they think they are better because of it (although some may, and it is fear of this that drives the stereotypical American reaction). It's part of their tradition — something we are sadly lacking in our object-obsessed, consumer-driven society.

    The reason for the wine collection, the flowers on the table, the exquisite cuisine, the high-style clothes, and the complex fragrances is not to pretentiously lord it over others; it's because we can actually be energized and illuminated by these things. We can experience depth, heart. Maybe it doesn't matter if you can't relate to the depth of a masterpiece by Beethoven or Van Gogh (or Banksy); but maybe it's also a shame.

    Some cultures encourage training the perception to be able to enjoy both the simple and the sophisticated things in life. Some families and societies nurture the development of refined senses and sensibilities, from childhood onward. It's seen as part of day-to-day life, nothing special. On the contrary, those who judge those talents and choices are often quite pretentious in their rejection of depth and refinement.

    If someone equates culture with bullshit, that may indicate the extent of their culture.
