    Help with finding a jacket/blazer

    Learning a few of the basics of matching can open a whole world. Rather than giving you a fish, I'll point you to where you can learn to fish for yourself:

    • This Reddit's sidebar guide to Matching Colors.

    • AskAndyAboutClothes color guide.

    • Another online color scheme guide., where the most useful tool is the color wheel. Don't go too deep into the schemes presented; the first 2 or 3 are what we work with most of the time.

    To tell the truth, the smallest amount of color theory will go a long way. Once you learn a few basic tips, as in epicviking's sidebar guide, you'll have hundreds of outfit options at hand.

    To get you started: The lighter shoes you linked to will go better with the dark chinos you're looking at (as well as with dark jeans); the others are too close to the color of the pant, and will look either boring or just bad. But if you go for the light ones, certain light colored pants will not do well. The alternative, for a more versatile shoe, is to get something in a different hue of brown, like chestnut, which will go with the chinos pictured as well as lighter pants and jeans. Take note that the shoes you linked to will also keep your outfits at a casual level, and can't be dressed up all that much (i.e., just to the casual end of a sporty blazer look).

    When you have a foundation of solid neutral colors in your outfit, you can insert pretty much any color or pattern and it will match. The neutral colors are earth tones (khaki, tan, browns, olive), greys and taupes (brown-greys), and navy. White is also a neutral, but in some cases is more difficult to match. Black is sometimes considered a neutral, but not in my book; and it's more difficult to match.

    'Stone' (taupe) chinos, tan or brown shoes, and a navy blazer is an all-neutral outfit that will allow you to wear almost any color shirt. Just be careful not to approach the blazer color too closely.

    Besides color matching, be aware of contrast in your outfit. If everything is the same shade, it can be boring. A loose rule of thumb is "light bottom, dark top; or vice versa".
