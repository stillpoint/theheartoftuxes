    Undershirts?

    You described the general outfit but not the setting. Context is the key, and will also guide how you wear the clothes you choose. It would also be helpful to understand the purpose: is it a style statement or a matter of practicality?

    Many people will object to a visible show of underwear, and that's what an undershirt showing beneath a polo is — underwear. Some put it on par with exposing your gotchies above your waistband.

    With a closed polo collar, the look is excessively restrained. With buttons open, a contrasty cone of white at the bottom of your neck is still not the best look, and it accentuates the crew neck (I call it the ice cream cone look, with your head as the ice cream). An open vee is more flattering, less stuffy and boyish; it allows for at least a bit of physicality, whereas the exposed crew neck suggests that no-one is ever going to get underneath that shirt.

    With a black shirt on top it will only be worse, because of the extreme contrast between the shades of black and white. In most public situations we'd recommend against it; but in very casual situations where a carefree, rumpled, or even tough and rebellious angle is in order — say, drinks with the boys at a cheap bar — the exposed undershirt/tee is not wrong. But in that case, does a polo shirt fit the situation, or are you sending conflicting messages: carefree and tough, but well-dressed and preppy? More classic, and more in line with the rugged intent, would be a denim or plaid flannel shirt and tee. You may also want to actually show more of the undershirt, if not the whole front, in which case a button-up is desirable.

    I wouldn't discount that there's a niche for tennis shoes, jeans, polo, and exposed shirt or tee; but you're going to put some people off with that, and if you run into a girl you want to hang out with, is she going to think you can't manage your look?

    Again, understand that this must suit the situation; don't just take my words as permission across the board. If in doubt about the polo-undershirt combo, wear a v-neck underneath, with a deep enough vee for the undershirt to remain invisible.

    Bad example: Glenn Frey is a disaster here, with an ill-fitting polo in a bad color for his skin tone and a very contrasty crew-neck underneath. The message it sends is that he's old and sexless.
