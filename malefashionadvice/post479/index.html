    I recommend starting with a solid navy/dark blue tie. It's sharp, it adds some color without dominating, and blue is almost universally flattering. So it will be versatile: good for both celebrations and for times when you need to look sharp and distinguished.

    A black tie is commonly appropriate for funerals, although anything not too vivid is usually respectful enough.

    The solid navy will do well with both plain white shirt and some patterned shirts. If you wear your suit enough to want or need a change, a patterned tie will add interest when paired with a plain white shirt. Plaid or gingham are usually fresh and young and not too serious; foulard and pindot are more classically sophisticated; stripe usually feels either collegiate or professional; and paisley is lush and bohemian — I'd recommend choosing one and becoming familiar with it and its pairings before complicating the issue with more choices. And do let your choice reflect who you are — your true situation and personal qualities.

    Another part of "who you are" is your physique and coloration. As soon as possible, you should learn your best and worst colors — the colors that make you come to the forefront and look great, versus the colors that take center stage themselves or make you look 'less' or not as vibrant. You should also learn what cuts and fits are best for your physical proportions; i.e., whether long and slim lines are best for you, or whether substantial and weighty forms are best.

    Let the tie width be somewhat proportional to the suit's lapel width; but if you are young or if a youthful energy suits you, then lean toward narrow (2.5") ties — not skinny (2"). Up to 3" wide is okay if the suit supports something that wide, and if you prefer a more classic style. No wider than that, please.

    Just a couple of basics should do well for you, especially if you're not wearing your suit very often. Instead of 'leaving money in your closet' with ties that you never or almost never wear, put your money toward clothes that will support (A) activities that are very significant to you (like possibly job interviews or dates) or (B) activities that you enjoy most often (school-day or chilling or out-on-the-town).
