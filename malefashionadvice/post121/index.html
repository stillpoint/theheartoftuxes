    Found this jacket it my dad's closet. Too retro or a good find?

    Yes, much too retro, and not a poor fit. The color is also not good.

    Coats generally look better when buttoned, as it shows their proper structure. This is crucial when it comes to double-breasted coats and jackets; they should almost never be seen unbuttoned, as the resulting shape around the body is most... unfortunate.

    This one, however, seems too big and boxy in any case, and the wide peak lapels are from another age. Although there is a trend toward wide lapels this year, they still are to go with a very slim and trim cut. The color of the coat really doesn't do you any favors.

    Your coat should help you to look vibrant and in the pink. Contemporary length is above the knee on most coats, and contemporary fit is slimmer and more shaped to the body's contour. The color should echo the dynamism of the double-breasted (DB) design.

    Finally, if you're thinking of wearing a double-breasted coat, make sure you want to be making that assertive a statement. The boldness of a DB's design can backfire; instead of an intrepid style statement it can come off as stuffy or out of place if not backed up with the rest of the design features and other parts of the outfit. In this case, the color of the coat is "old", and defeats the assertiveness that could come with a DB coat.
