    title: Question for MFA: If you are only to choose one, would you choose a money clip or a wallet, and why? summary: 0.0 authors: TheHeartofTuxes date: 2012-06-11 07:32:22 some_url:

    I keep bills folded in my back pocket, smaller bills to the outside, without any wallet or clip. I keep cards and ID in a small, very slim 'card case' wallet, either in a jacket pocket or in the inner pocket of a shoulder bag. I use the Hartmann Belting Leather Card Case. Bank and credit card slip in one side, ID in the window side, with a couple of business cards slipped in behind it; there's also an outer pocket that is good for receipts or a credit card sized freznel magnifier.

    I minimize the cards I use. I use a single credit card, and steer clear of club cards in general. If it's an issue for you, you can consolidate most or all of your club and discount cards on a single card (here's another option) or on an electronic keychain device.

    If I know I'm going to be accessing my cards more frequently, or if I need to do away with the shoulder bag, or if security is a concern, I can easily transfer the wallet to my front pocket. But I prefer not to have any wallet in any pants pocket, for the sake of both comfort and appearance.

    As to money clips, I don't like the inflexible metal in my pockets either. If there were something more minimalistic than most clips, about the size of a bobby pin, then I'd consider using it â€” mostly because a clip acts as a bookmark to find the center of the folded bills.
