    Fall is coming. Anybody have any advice on buying a trench coat?

    That trench coat is too long and overworked. The double-breasted design plus large contrast collar, epaulettes, and cape are too much. And the cut is far from slim and modern. The color is also very yellow and will not look good on many complexions. For all the reasons I listed in my earlier post, I recommend avoiding this coat.

    ~

    Your skin and hair coloration is not the only issue in considering color. You'll hear it commonly said that black will go with anything, but don't believe it; black is notoriously hard to match for several reasons. It is the most formal color, often too formal for one's outfit; it is the most dull and dour non-color; it lacks character (such as the calm stability of earth tones, the cool calmness of blues, the warm vibrancy of reds, etc.); it intensifies other colors and brings the greatest contrast into outfits that have light shades or colors in them, and because of that is often too stark.

    Many guys default to black because (A) they don't yet have the skill, imagination, or courage to use colors and shades and (B) they envision black as the sign of badass strength. The upshot is usually bland, dull, somewhat inept.

    Black should really be used consciously to bring intensity into an outfit. In that sense, black will go with many bright colors in extremely bold looks (often more suited for women). If this is the intent, fine; but if you're going to be wearing dark pants, then a black trench coat will bring that much more dreariness into the world. And with jeans, a black trench coat is... blech!

    ~
