    How to wear these designer bucketfeet sneakers?

    The rule of thumb for wearing loud 'statement pieces', AKA 'go to heck/hell/fuck off pieces' (the loudest ones), is to tone it down with the rest of the outfit. Wear mostly solid neutrals in the rest of the outfit, keep it plain so there's not too much going on, as you're doing in the photos.

    If you want to introduce more color elsewhere besides the sneakers, one strategy is to let it be an analogous color, close to one of the colors in the shoes, but in a darker or muted (softer) shade. Colors that are too close will appear too matchy-matchy.

    If you want to throw caution to the wind and get peacocking, you can start by adding smaller pops of color in accessories like belt, watch band, sunglasses rims, hat. Even the smallest dabs of color will pay off of the exuberant, brash sneaker design. The aim of the overall look is playful and creative without vomiting color and pattern all over the place. Even without much added color, you can bolster the playful joie de vivre agenda with one calm pattern elsewhere, and one or two casual moves (maybe a trilby hat in warm sunny weather, a fabric or woven belt strap hanging out a bit, a shirt half- or quarter-tuck, layering colored t-shirts and/or rolling the sleeve cuffs, etc.)

    These are all very laid-back, casual moves, so the occasion must fit. For sharper occasions, keep the rest of the outfit sharp and subdued and let the shoes be the lone statement piece.

    Whatever you try, check it in the mirror or in a photo. You can more easily try combinations by laying them out on the bed before selecting one to try on. Research blogs, magazines, even movies to see how similar pieces are combined well (or poorly).

    To go all out, you can start combining colors and patterns. The sky's the limit. For instance, I think a camo blazer or jacket (a less angular woodland camo, in a different colorway) would look great, with pants in a deep or intense solid color. Maintain balance by separating the patterned pieces and interspersing solids. In loud outfits, maintain groundedness with deep, dark colors in the bottom half.

    But before all of that, you need to learn proper fit and care of your clothes. The baggy, wrinkled jeans and t-shirt in your photos are just nasty. (Baggy crotch: Dude, no!) Read the sidebar, learn proper fit, and show self-respect by cleaning up the look (I don't mean that you necessarily don't respect yourself, just that it doesn't show well to others).

    Awesome, high-couture shoes with a sloppy outfit will look immature, as if you're trying to buy your way to style. Intentionally loose is different, a whole style move in itself, which also has to be learned. Start by learning proper fit.

    Notes: Only cuff that high in the summer at the beach or similar situations. The watch isn't terrible, but isn't the same kind of style as the rest of the outfit. It betrays a conservatism. A more funky watch (fabric or colored plastic or leather band) or, say, woven or leather bracelet would better express the leisure and fun implied by the shoes and other pieces.
