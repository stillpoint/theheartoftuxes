    MFA, I need your help real quick. Thanks.

    I don't think a bowtie will go so well with a black suit. There could be precedent, but I've never seen either a positive or negative example. However, a black suit is formal and resides at the 'sharp' end of the style spectrum, whereas a bowtie (other than "black tie") is flashy, cheeky, and quirky. The two don't go together. I suggest you stand out not because you wear a loud or gaudy piece, but because your outfit is extremely well-conceived and cohesive.

    You have to use what you have for your benefit; so you need to understand the qualities of your all-black suit and shoes, and make use of those qualities. What a black suit has that other suits don't is intensity. It puts light shades and bright colors into deeper contrast than any other background. In addition, as mentioned above, there is a sharp, slick quality to a black suit. Those can be your outfit's strengths.

    To keep it sharp but respectful, simple black-and-white will be a good high-contrast background for a tie that is notable for its elegance rather than its loudness. Black suit, white shirt, and a dark solid or high-contrast patterned tie should be good. A rich dark royal or navy blue would be classy; and grey or silver tie, even more so (but would rely heavily on the sheen or texture of the tie). Silver or white dot on black is also elegant.

    If you require more pizzazz, and if you think it appropriate, I would think in terms of a shirt with a vivid solid color that will be intensified by the black. Neons are big this year, if you want to go that far; but any bright color of shirt that suits your skin tone will do. Bright pinks and purples will do well. Just be aware that you may channel a '60s doo-wop group. The slickness of the tie can derive from narrowness, or from a luxurious sheen. Deep, rich, shiny silk is in order.

    To go all the way with an explosion of color, do a power clash with solids — hot pink shirt and orange tie; bright violet shirt and emerald green tie; etc.

    Other options for expressivity:

    • Lush pattern. A tone-on-tone paisley can play off of the rigidity of the black suit. Red paisley and purple paisley have oomph; but any jewel tone will do well against the black. Also, deep colors like burgundy and aubergine are very elegant with black, but not "in your face".

    • Do it with the accessories. If you forego the bright colors and stay refined and low-key with the patterns, let high-quality accessories provide the sparkle. Tie clip, watch, sunglasses, cuff links, pocket square, socks — but not all at once! Choose a one or two to bring sparkle to the combination.

    A graduation has some ceremonial formality to it; it's no night club. Also remember that it is your friend's day, not yours. You should consider your desire to "stand out" in this light. If it turns out that there is opportunity for more extravagant style, you might think about doing away with the tie and wearing a bright, luxurious pocket square that spills out of your breast pocket. Add a high-shine accessory (i.e., watch), and you're good.
