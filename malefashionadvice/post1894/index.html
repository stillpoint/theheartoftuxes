    title: Hey MFA: What's the point? summary: 2.0 authors: TheHeartofTuxes date: 2011-11-15 17:32:50 some_url:

    For me, at least part of the answer is that it's a creative expression that merges with and colors my persona -- how I appear to others and how I appear to myself. It could be very ego-oriented, making up self-images and playing out fantasies, with or without the participation of others (those who might or might not buy into the image I present). But I find that it can also be quite the opposite of ego-serving; it can be a reminder and ratification that I and my self-image are always changing, shifting, re-forming.

    So in that sense, the point of style for me is to realize the flexibility and ineffability of my self.

    In another sense, there is no point; it's just an expression that comes from me. But in terms of functionality, I often find myself feeling out whether an outfit 'sits right' with me, whether it's what I want to say and reflects what I feel like. The same would be true of how I arrange and decorate my living space, how I use words and posture and tone of voice, which people I choose to associate with and which objects I choose to possess. And the more of a vocabulary I develop with clothing and style, the more subtle and powerful an influence I am able have on myself and others.

    A Traditional Chinese Doctor once told a lady friend of mine to brighten up her makeup. She was wearing dark, smoky eye shadow and blush, and dark lipstick; and he said that it reproduced the look of someone who doesn't have good circulation, so that every time she looked in the mirror she would see a sick person. I have definitely found that I can influence my own mood and energy with clothing. When I'm particularly down I might compensate by wearing a tie and fine shoes, or brighter colors. Or I might feel like just fully expressing the quality of that darkness with an intense outfit, or a muted one. An untucked flannel shirt, chunky sweater, and boots can help me approach the day in a more laid-back manner. When I want to boost my sense of directness, a slick business suit or all-black casual clothes offer different flavors of that clear, forward-looking energy. The variety of expression possible is, I think, infinite.

    At a certain level, it can approach -- no, it can be -- art. It can take mundane experience and transform it into something new, something greater, something more lively or connected. Walking to work, or going on a date, or having a barbeque with friends becomes an event that is fully participated with your action and feeling and what you wear, and that full participation affects not only you but those around you.

    I also see, quite obviously, the difference in how others relate to me depending on how I'm dressed. I can even step into roles that are out of my comfort zone, in part by dressing the part.

    So with all this adaptation, the question arises: "Without the costuming, without the masks, what am I?" And the movement from day to day, situation to situation provides the answer by itself. There is just constant change; and it's up to me to do what I want with it.
