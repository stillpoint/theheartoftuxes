    I am going for a modelling test shoot on Sunday. I'm not very fashionable and they want 6 different "looks". Help!?

    I guess part of the point could be to see how knowledgeable and aware you are of style genres and range of formality, and how interested and invested you are in fashion; so if you aren't able to do this yourself you should consider (A) picking up the study of style and fashion, or (B) whether this is really something you want to get into.

    Since you're asking for help...

    Consider what the prospective employers are looking for, what will be most helpful and useful for them. Think about the kinds of products they are looking to sell, and about what kinds of qualities or abilities they're looking for — what aspect will put their minds at ease when they when they find it.

    You already have mentioned an Active or Sports look. Here are a few more ideas:

    • Sporty will be different than Active/Sports. Check my recent post.
    • Sharp-Casual: the common nice jeans, casual button-up shirt, sweater, and/or sportcoat; as seen in Banana Republic and J Crew ads.
    • Beach/Summer Recreation: shorts and t-shirt/polo plus throw-on layer, in light, sunny colors, crisp patterns.
    • Old-school Casual: classic and perhaps weathered sneakers, chinos, t-shirt, and maybe a denim jacket or Harrington. (Take care with vintage, used, and weathered pieces so that it's clear that you intend to use them that way, rather than thinking a used piece is 'as good as' a new, contemporary one. These guys are in the sales business, after all.)
    • Workwear: somewhat rugged jeans or twill pants, boots and chunky leather belt, chambray shirt, field jacket.
    • Hipster: skinny jeans, deep-vee t-shirt, cardigan, wristband or multiple bracelets, emphatic eyeglasses, slouchy fits (possibly including slouchy beanie), brown-and-bold color combo, simple but loud patterns.
    • Dressy: the best formal outfit you can muster (and if you can't muster a good one take a pass, because this style should be spot-on).
    • College Prep: what a spiffy college student might wear, including slim-fit jeans or chinos, polo, canvas belt, varsity sweater, loafers or boat shoes (check ivy-style.com and others).
    • In-your-face Streetwear: think outside the box and use interesting pieces and provocative combinations (there'll be lots of online reference material available. like streetetiquette.com/, superfuture streetstyle forum, and hypebeast streetsnaps.
    • Rocker; Country; Ethnic; Military; Period (from another era); High Style... and playing with themes such as monochrome, mixed pattern, texture, drape, etc..

    With any of these, avoid making it into a costume. Don't be too clichéd about it. The employers will want to know that you can make their clothing look wearable (i.e., buyable), like a perfectly reasonable thing to wear rather than part of an unapproachable concept or stereotype. By surfing the web you should get all the ideas you'll ever need.
