    MFA, how do you organize your clothing?

    Twice a year, at the end of winter and at the end of summer, I sift through my wardrobe to determine what I didn't wear or won't wear again next year, and get rid of it. Next, I pack away the past season's (seasonal) clothes to go into storage and retrieve the next season's.

    In addition to closet rails, I have those plastic-coated custom shelves in my closet. T-shirts, sweaters, and other knits get folded and piled on the shelves. I use plastic sheets on top of the shelving so my clothes don't receive snags or ridge impressions. (The plastic sheets are marketed as a way to keep piles of folded clothes from sliding around when place in their midst, but I find that they have the opposite effect, and only use them on the bottom of the pile.)

    I used to separate graphic tees from solids, and arrange them according to color; but during a busy time I found the system was too inconvenient, so now I just place new-folded items on top of the pile. There's not much practical difference, only difference in visual effect.

    I keep casual button-downs (plaids, flannels, denim) and short-sleeve button-downs on hangers, but in a different section from my dress shirts, which are also on hangers, and color-coded. Organizing by color is a lot easier with hung items than folded ones: instead of trying to lift a pile and fit a shirt in the middle, all you have to do is slide the shirts aside.

    Athletic/synthetic t-shirts go in a drawer. Dress socks are folded and go in a drawer, inside clear plastic organizers. I really like being able to leaf through my socks like going through a rolodex. Athletic socks are flipped into their own elastic neck and thrown in a bin in the closet.

    Casual pants and jeans and shorts are folded in a drawer. Dress pants hang in the closet on no-slip pants hangers. I don't like using clips to hang pants, for fear of leaving lasting dents. Jackets and suits hang next to the dress pants.

    I haven't used an app for wardrobe organization; but I've used spreadsheets to organize backpacking equipment and travel gear, so I may in the future return to a simple spreadsheet for my wardrobe too.

    It's not just about what you own, but about what you use and what helps you be who you want to be. Making a survey of your activities and the places/people you associate with the most will also help determine what will be the best use of your money when purchasing clothing in the future. So that spreadsheet will likely break down into sections: Formal; About Town; Casual Social; Casual At Home; Work; Sports (add whatever categories are essential to your lifestyle). This will enhance awareness of what you really need, as opposed to what you imagine or picture.

    The most critical point for me is, at those twice-a-year purges, to be diligent about paring down and simplifying. If I'm doubtful about an item, I'll lay it on the bed and see how many outfits I can make with it that I'll be happy to wear. If the result is less than thrilling, I'll sell the piece or send it to charity.
