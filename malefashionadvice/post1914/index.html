    What's the consensus on these style of shoes?

    Visually, some Skechers may do for a sporty and very casual outfit for sporty and casual activities. This does not include teaching.

    Furthermore, check out the construction of the shoe and you'll find there's no midsole. When you're in the store looking at these shoes, pull out the insole and you'll see bare hard outsole. So the notion that these shoes will be more comfortable than other, more appropriate, shoes may be a fallacy linked to their apparently sporty design. You may not want to run or stand much in them.

    I second the recommendation of, among others, Rockports. Other brands that have quite comfortable dressier shoes:

    • Ecco (known for their comfortable dressier shoes)
    • Cole Haan (which are using Nike Air technology in their own 'Air' line)
    • Johnston & Murphy (see their Comfort System line with gel cushioned insoles)
    • Clarks and Bostonian (a branch of Clarks dedicated to greater comfort)
    • Allen Edmonds (more traditional construction that conforms to the foot)

    Note that I have used the term "dressy shoes" rather than "dress shoes". With most of the brands named above, you'll have to wade through many square toes, thick crépe soles or ridiculous treads, boorish stitching, and lame hybrid designs to get to actual appropriate and good-looking shoes. Even the most formal offerings will almost invariably have rubber soles, and so can't correctly be called dress shoes although they will do fine in a classroom setting and for smart-casual wear.

    Of the brands listed, Allen Edmonds has the most traditional approach. The soles of their shoes tend to be thick, and the shoe designs tend toward the chunky end of the spectrum. AE's claim to comfort doesn't hinge on new cushioning technology, but on the fact that the materials and the way they're put together will conform to the foot over time. Their cork midsole, for instance, offers both cushioning and conforming properties. This approach may also mean that their shoes will last much longer than any that involve synthetic or inflated cushioning.
