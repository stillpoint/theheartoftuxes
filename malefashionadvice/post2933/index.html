    Feedback Request - Casual & Cocktail Attire

    This tie is much better, both because it's narrower and because it has a solid field of color, and so is not quite as loud.

    Of the dress pants you've shown, the pants you posted in your reply to icy are indeed the slimmest and best of the bunch. The v-neck sweater is good with the tie. I suspect that at a party the sweater will be hot and will come off at some point (by which time you'll probably be in the midst of revelry and most other guys will also be happily disheveled).

    If you can't come up with a slimmer shirt, try a military tuck. It's a way to fold in the extra material to bring the shirt closer to the body. Basically, you draw your hands back around your waistband to pull the front and side of the shirt tight. Then the slack material at the small of the back is folded at side/back of the waist in two wings (folded back). It's not nearly as good as a properly fitted shirt, but it provides you with a flat front and takes up some of the billow.

    A variation to this tucking method has the wings more to the small of the back, bringing the waist to a better shape from the front view.

    What I want to know is whether you have any dark wash jeans, especially slim-fitting ones. With clean, dark jeans (not light, faded, or distressed) you could wear the shirt, tie, and sweater, and look both youthful and sharp. I didn't get a clear look at the brown shoes you wore with your casual outfit, but they may be a good option with jeans.

    Enjoy your time and treat the girls with respect. Those are better than any clothes.
