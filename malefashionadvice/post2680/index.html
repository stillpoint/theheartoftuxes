    MFA, what can I wear for a top / shoes with this color of dark grey pants? They're a sort of casual pant with a small pattern.

    I want to see the pant itself. Just seeing the color is only a small part of the story. The cut and fit and pattern and texture are all involved in the look. It's true that grey is versatile. But to say that a pant goes with "nearly anything" doesn't really say much at all. Nothing comes to mind that will go equally well with runners and beachwear, dinner jacket and black tie, winter plaids and a toque, and cashmere argyle prepster outfits.

    Show the whole pant; and better yet, show how it looks when worn.

    I think you will be able to work out a lot on your own by thinking through a few points. Ask yourself what level or range of formality is met. Consider whether it fits into a classic or modern look. Look into the qualities associated with the piece (think keywords -- comfortable; professional; slick; rugged; dull; in-your-face; fun; elegant... whatever leaps to mind for you).

    You can also analyze the various aspects of the item as to their quality -- the message they send. What is being communicated with the cut (the form or line) of the piece, with its design features and embellishments or lack thereof, with its color, pattern, texture, and movement (its drape and flow)? What is being communicated with the way it fits and the way it sits on your body?

    When you answer these in detail, you'll have a mastery over the message you're sending with your clothing.

    ~

    While you say that the pants are "sort of casual", at first glance they look like dress pants. Dress pants in charcoal grey would be quite conservative. They would indeed match well with a lot of standard 'smart' outfits; but would not in themselves be very expressive. From the picture, they certainly appear to fit within the realm of business wear and not so much in a rugged or masculine casual look, or in a stylish streetwear look, for example. But that impression could change if we saw the whole design on you or on a model.
