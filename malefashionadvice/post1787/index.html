    I need to buy clothes for a waiter job - how can I take this opportunity to improve my wardrobe?

    The smells, spills, and mileage you encounter as a waiter might preclude the clothes you wear for the job from being part of your everyday wardrobe. If you buy an expensive shirt, expecting to wear it on other occasions for the next several years, you might be very disappointed to spill tomato soup on it. I might go the other way: buy the least expensive shirt and pants that fit well and look good (enough).

    But if you do want to follow your strategy of buying for the future, you'll want to have something basic and classic that will combine well with many pieces, and you will want it to fit extremely well. Check the sidebar on How Clothes Should Fit. For pants, find something in a straight cut that is roomy enough to be comfortable for the walking and bending that you'll be doing but without billowing. Although the party will be 'elegant', pants for waitering can be a bit slimmer and more modern in their cut than the pants that guests will be wearing. If the cut is slimmer, it's also okay to go without a full break in the pants -- the hem can just touch the top of the shoes rather than (more traditionally) the top of the heel. Avoid pleats.

    The shirt should be opaque, so that neither features of the body nor any undershirt worn will show through. Armholes that are high and close to the armpit will save you from having to re-tuck your shirt a hundred times during the evening. Buy a shirt that fits you well in the shoulders and arms, and at least fairly well in the body; and you can get it tailored to hug the waist and remove excess material at the small of the back. You might also want to check the button distances, so that you can undo the top button without exposing too much chest.
