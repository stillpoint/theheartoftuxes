    Which is better for pairing with a dress shirt (sometimes tie) and jeans? Blazer or Sports Coat?

    To my eye, a sharp blazer is too far from jeans in formality and style. In this example and this one, there's too much of a disconnect between top and bottom.

    A more casual textured or chino blazer is more in line with the jeans aesthetic. The tweed sportcoat goes further in that direction. Its cozy, palpable texture and softer edges suit the rougher connotation of jeans.

    Color will make a big difference in the way you can combine the piece. Earthy, casual browns seem to welcome the blue denim. A textured jacket will also play better with jeans and with an overall relaxed look — better than a sleek flat blazer.

    But I'd encourage you to think beyond blue jeans as the only casual or sharp-casual option. Chinos, casual slacks, tweeds, corduroys, and even colored jeans will open up a world of interest in color and texture combining, and will help you avoid painting yourself into a corner with the workwear connotations of blue denim. You can inject tremendous interest and novelty into your outfits without using wild colors. An earthy autumn palette can have great impact without looking over-the-top, if the colors suit you.

    If you're set on denim, another great and less run-of-the-mill option is to wear a denim shirt rather than jeans. This provides more interplay between the denim and the jacket, and the pants can bring a greater unity to the look. That is, if the pants are more in the genre, formality, and color family of the jacket, the overall look will have more unity — the denim will provide color and style contrast in a measured dose, framed by the other unified pieces, as opposed to creating the disconnect seen with the first two blazer pics.
