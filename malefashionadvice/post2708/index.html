    High school formal - criticise everything please.

    I think you mean "critique" rather than "criticize"; but I have to agree that the suit is too big. Since shoulders on a suit jacket can't be altered, even tailoring won't get it to fit, but you may want to consult a tailor anyway to see if things can be improved.

    A plain white shirt and narrow, not skinny, tie (2.5" to 3" wide) would be appropriate. Going to an actual skinny tie narrower than 2.5" is a retro look, but I fear it would exacerbate the bad fit of the suit. With a skinny tie, everything else should be skinny and fit very close to the body. Nothing else in what you're wearing supports that slick, young look.

    A black tie would be very formal, but would also make the whole outfit dark and serious. If your partner is open to an accent of color, you might want to use a colored tie to match. Depending on how much color can be introduced, you could go for a festive color in the shirt â€” pink (not necessarily this dark) and lavender both would look great with black. Other bright colors are viable too. (Though some guys in high school, in their paranoia about effeminacy, may condemn the color pink on principle, pink also does seem to be prevalent as a 'go to hell' color in prom tuxedos.)

    If you stick with a black and white theme, you might want a pattern in your tie to add some interest. Black with white dots or other pattern; grey with a pattern; and solid silver are all classy options.

    A shirt collar should have just enough room to allow two fingers to slip in snugly, no more.

    The shoes are not the best â€” they don't look like the most fashionable item out there â€” but they're leather lace-ups, they're black, and they're in good condition; so you're good to go.

    Take care not to be more colorful than your date. The focus of the evening should be on showing her a good time and helping her feel comfortable and happy; how you look is a lesser concern by far. The good news is that most of the other guys at the formal are in the same boat, and you likely won't stand out as looking any worse.
