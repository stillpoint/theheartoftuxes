    Help with wardrobe overhaul

    One of the best things you can do for yourself regarding regulation of body temperature and moisture (clamminess) is to wear merino wool underwear. A thin merino short-sleeved (or, if warranted, long-sleeved) undershirt should immediately make your outer layers work better in a wider range of temperatures. Some technical synthetics designed for wicking also work; but it's difficult to match the natural materials that have evolved (in this instance, to insulate sheep) over millions of years.

    Layers that are very convenient to adjust would also be a good idea. Rather than having to don and doff whole layers, opening and closing a sweater or jacket is far more convenient, and offers immediate relief. The ideal piece in terms of temperature regulation would be a full-zip, mock-neck, thin wool sweater. Each of these features is significant. The full zip gives you complete flexibility for venting heat. The mock-neck allows you to quickly cover even your neck without depending on accessories. A thick sweater, even when unzipped, may become too hot during strenuous activity; and if you start to sweat it will undo some of the insulative benefit of your layers. A thin sweater will also allow you to roll up the sleeves more easily, adding that as a venting option. Wool provides comfortable, breathable insulation across a wider range of conditions than cotton, and will transport body moisture rather than soak it up and form a damp layer next to your torso.

    On top of these two layers, you can add a stylish track-style jacket, and you're all set. A sportcoat would also do, but it sounds like that may get in the way of your physical activity.

    For the very best outfit, a leather bomber would also be a fantastic outer layer -- breathable and insulating. It would be difficult to top this combination. Two thin wool layers (one of them a full-zip) plus a leather zip-up jacket can stand you in good stead from almost summer temperatures to fairly deep, near-freezing, cold. Add a button-up shirt if it suits you.
