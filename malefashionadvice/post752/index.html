    Which Color Jacket is the Most Versatile?

    It depends on the rest of your wardrobe, and on your best colors (the colors that make you look great when worn near your face).

    Contrast is the first, most basic element of style that establishes interest in an outfit. In order to achieve contrast between top and bottom, you'll need to be wearing medium to light pants with the navy jacket, and dark or light (not medium) pants with the olive green jacket. The navy jacket could be worn with dark blue pants â€” it can be a nice sort of strong monolithic look, but it gets old fast because of the monotony. The other issue that arises is that if lighter shade pants are worn with the navy jacket, although contrast is achieved it's harder to establish a strong base, so the outfit can look top-heavy; therefore you'll need darker, perhaps chunky shoes or boots, or some other footwear that creates visual weight (like a loud bright color).

    Both navy and olive are neutral colors, and can be worn with almost any other color. But if you wear blue jeans often, the olive may work better for you.

    I haven't mentioned the black yet, because black is not a true neutral, is more difficult to combine with other colors, can tend to look too dour or conservative, and is too intense for some skin colors. For that matter, olive may make some complexions look more sallow, but it tends to be more wearable than black. Navy works well for many complexions, but may be too intense for some (though not as much as black). The navy seems to work best with the skin tone of the Everlane model.

    If you have the basic pieces for blue, brown, or grey-based outfits, the olive will be versatile. Darker olive pants can also work.

    Black pants will work best with the olive jacket, may be okay with the black (if all-black is your thing), and may clash with the navy.

    The olive will also accommodate bright colors better.

    Without knowing the other parameters involved, the olive looks more versatile, with the navy second, and the black... not so much.
