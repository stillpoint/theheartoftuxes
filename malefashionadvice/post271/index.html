    $250 to start a wardrobe from scratch, what is the best way to approach this?

    With a small budget, it's even more important to be efficient; and that means having a plan and knowing where you'll get the most bang for your buck.

    • What do you need, and how will a purchased piece help you?

    • Will the pieces combine well with each other and with what you already own?

    • Do the purchases fit your lifestyle, your body, and the image you're hoping to project?

    The more clarity you can get on context, the better chance you have of buying clothing that works well for you. You should also know the colors that are best for you and that work well together; and you should be clear on whether the pieces will match in terms of style, function, and level of formality.

    If you really want to be efficient with money, then, you'll do an overview of your wardrobe from the perspective of your lifestyle. There are two areas that are given more weight: the activities that you engage in most often, and the activities that are critical to the unfolding of your life (which may mean job interviews, or may mean dating, and so on). Don't waste money on fantasies, dressing for events or activities that don't have much to do with your real life. One exception is when you are trying to shake things up by changing your style; but don't do this at the expense of the basics that you need for everyday living.

    In terms of actual items, the single most influential piece is usually considered to be either outerwear or shoes. Outerwear (jacket or coat), because that is what most people will see first when you are out in public; and shoes, because they form the basis of the outfit are an immediate signal of both style and level of formality.

    Lots of good advice can be found with a forum search, and by reading the sidebar material.

    Basic upgrades:

    • Polo shirts and collared button-ups rather than t-shirts

    • Dark wash jeans rather than light wash, faded, or distressed

    • Chinos rather than jeans

    • Cardigans and v-necks rather than sweatshirts and hoodies

    • Leather lace-up shoes rather than sneakers and slip-ons

    • Higher quality accessories (a nice watch can bump up most outfits)

    With $250 you can make some headway; but you won't be revamping your wardrobe. The single most thing you can do to "dress nicely", as you say, is to buy clothes that fit closer to the body. This instantly brings a more vibrant, modern, and stylish quality to the clothes you wear. And often the best use of your money is not to make a purchase at all, but to bring the pieces that you already own — the good-looking and useful ones — to the tailor and have them altered to fit you better.

    You can extend the principle of improving the clothing you already own to eliminating everything that is merely average or worse. If it doesn't make you look great, it's out. It may mean a drastic reduction in your wardrobe; but it will also mean that your clothes will always support your image.
