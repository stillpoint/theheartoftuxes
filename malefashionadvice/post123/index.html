    Sprucing up the wardrobe a bit to start off the new year on a good note

    Oh, sorry, those are not flattering shoes. The first and worst issue is the square toes -- that's not going to be well received here. The bicycle toe stitching and thick rubber sole are also not doing you any favors. It's an awkward, cheap, and synthetic look. This image says it all.

    Besides that, black dress shoes are not going to look good with a lot of jeans. You'll need much darker, dressier jeans than pictured, for instance, to go with black dress shoes. That shade of denim is quite bad with black.

    You need to learn how to match color, style, and level of formality. All three are vital if you want to avoid big mistakes in your attire. (There are other variables -- line, proportion, material, pattern, texture, design features, build quality, and so on -- that are also important.)

    In terms of color; black shoes could go with black or faded-black jeans, or with the darkest indigo, but that would limit your color possibilities with the rest of your outfit. Brown shoes will go better with most jeans, and will allow you to use a wider variety of color in the rest of your outfits. (Brown is also a no-brainer as a great match with blues.) Guys not used to wearing color may have a gut reaction against brown; but trust me, over time as your eye develops you will see it as the more versatile and often more stylish choice.

    In terms of style; you need to be aware of what your outfit and the pieces in it are saying, and be specific about it. Are you going for dressy casual or relaxed casual? Are you using very urban pieces, or items that have an outdoorsy feel? Are you looking to express sleek elegance or rough-and-tumble masculinity? The shoes should match the rest of the outfit in terms of style; or actually, vice versa, since the shoes are the foundation of the entire look. There's a reason women check out a man's shoes first: they are a defining element.

    In terms of level of formality; you need to harmonize the pieces as to how dressy or casual they are. This goes for the whole outfit, but especially pertains to pieces that are next to each other. Shiny leather dress shoes and faded jeans have two completely different levels of formality, and if worn together will look very immature and awkward. Some guys, advanced in their abilities, will stretch the limits; but even then it sometimes comes off as poorly conceived. As a rule, you shouldn't stray more than a step away in formality between any pieces.

    • Although there are more casual and more dressy versions of jeans, they all reside in the 'casual' category of attire. It's the opposite with dress shoes: they are all dressy, but some are on the more casual side of dressy. So if you're going to wear dress shoes with jeans, you need dressy jeans in dark washes and smooth denims, and for the most part casual dress shoes with design features like wingtip brogue designs, slip-on wear, or buckles. In any case, smooth, slick, sleek, and sharp go together with each other.

    • If you want to avoid being always committed to either something very dressy or something very throw-on casual, look to versatile footwear like desert boots, chukkas, and workboots. These are extremely popular for a reason: they work very well with a wide range of outfits. These boots can go with casual outfits like faded jeans and a t-shirt, or with sharp outfits like slim chinos and a button-down shirt, or even dressy jeans, sweater, and tie. Clarks are the popular favorite.

    • You can save money on many pieces of clothing; but for the most part with shoes you get what you pay for, so you should count on spending some money on a shoe that will look great and last a long time.
