    Club collars - when?

    A club collar is most appropriate at an English public school or at a country club. By extension, it has made its way into preppy wardrobes and casual upscale venues.

    The first thing to note about the club collar is that for such a subtle detail, the difference is radical -- the difference between a corner and a curve. So the first thing to consider, for example in regard to business settings, is how different one wants to be. Will that kind of difference be received well by peers and superiors?

    The curve carries an inherent cheekiness, going against the stream of the vast majority of shirt collars. It is this cheekiness that is employed as a style statement. In addition, the club collar is usually more casual than points or spreads.

    I wouldn't say that club collars are almost always white-on-blue contrast collars. I've mostly seen them in the same fabric as the body of the shirt. I'd wager that vendors desperate to provide businessmen with 'something different' are figuring that the club collar is both different enough and safe enough to fit the bill.

    The contrast collar is clearly and definitively a reference to powerful executives, bankers, and financiers. And 'reference' is a kind term. 'Stereotype' is perhaps more accurate. The contrast collar is by now inextricably linked with the character who seeks an aggressive, even Machiavellian, statement of power with his attire. Underlings who use the move run the risk of being seen as either self-deluding wannabees or, less often, threatening challengers.

    Whether in business or casual settings, it's my opinion that the individualistic event set in motion by the club collar will almost certainly go overboard with the addition of the contrast collar.

    Although it is usually either the uniform of regimented schoolchildren or a fun thumbing of the nose, I have seen the club collar in the context of extreme elegance. In an outfit where everything else is of fine design and in impeccable balance, and with no cheap or loud statement pieces, the single difference of a crisp (non-contrast) club collar can vault an outfit to sartorial heights. Somehow, when everything else in an outfit is saying "high class", the club collar sheds its immature and cheeky nature, and adopts a simple and slightly arcane richness.
