    Been lurking for a couple weeks, how did I do?

    Hey, chill; the fit on the chinos is worse than so-so. The taper is quite bad: baggy on the top, clingy on the bottom, and much too long. The sweater is also too big. There's a lot of extra fabric around the chest and waist, the hem is slouchy, and the sleeves are bunched up.

    The gingham shirt is not a bad idea. It can be versatile, and adds movement and interest to an outfit while being fairly easy to match. But in this outfit, the large, deep vee of the sweater reveals quite a swath of the gingham pattern, which comes off as a bit too contrasty and loud. If the sweater were not as dark and/or didn't have as big a vee, the contrast would probably be okay.

    The untucked shirt tails, sorry to say, look terrible. I get that you want the outfit to be casual and not too serious; but the little wedge of contrasty shirt is too eye-catching, and especially in combination with other items that don't fit well the untucked shirt appears very messy and unkempt -- the opposite of stylish. With very fitted pants and shirt, an untucked look might be okay because it might then not be seen as slobbish; but a wedge of bright fabric peeking out from a sweater is usually not a good shape to wear in any case.

    If you can, trade the chinos for slim or straight fit, not tapered. If you can't return the cardigan for something in the right size and more fitted, you might consult a seamstress or tailor to see if the body can be taken in and sleeves shortened. A solid color or not so bright pattern would work better with the dark sweater.

    For future reference, when posting a photo to ask about fit, keep hands out of pockets.
