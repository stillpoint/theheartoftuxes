    How's my suit?

    I'd take comments that the pants are too long with a grain of salt. They appear to be at almost a classic length; and a fraction of an inch shorter would be perfect if the pants (and possibly the jacket) were of a looser and more classic cut. The slimness of the pants is what makes them more modern, and so you may want to take them up more so they'll hang straight without a break. Just know that some may see that as an error, and that it may at some point become dated when 'modern' cuts again go for more fabric and drape. It's more up to you and your sense of it than a hard and fast rule.

    The jacket likewise has a bit of a modern American cut: slim, high button stance (central button near the sternum rather than at the waist), and a bit short by classic standards. A classic jacket would reach a couple of inches lower; but these days jackets do go this short and shorter, though they look progressively more boyish as they do.

    The modern look also embraces wearing pants without a belt; but you could, if you wish, add one. In any case, keep your jacket closed when standing/walking. It's not only proper etiquette, it shows off the lines of your suit the best.

    The fit next to your body of both jacket and pants are excellent. Nothing wrong with your shirt collar as well.

    You can go a bit more narrow with the tie, both because the width of the jacket lapels will accommodate that and because it's a younger, more modern look that will suit you and the outfit. You can also choose a tie with more color or pattern to announce your boisterous youthfulness and verve. The suit fits so well that for many occasions it would look great not to wear a tie; and if you want to get out there more with your style, you could wear your suit with just a shirt, opened a button or two, and pocket square.

    Well done.
