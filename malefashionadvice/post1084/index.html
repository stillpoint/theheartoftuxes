    You tell 'em! Keep them on point, and never mind about the hair. (IMHO, might as well try it long while you're young.)

    From what we can see, the suit looks great. A navy suit will be very versatile. Fits well at the shoulders, seems okay around the body, though we'd know better if it was buttoned.

    Arm length looks serviceable; but if you want to be classically correct the arms could be a bit shorter to show some of the shirt cuff. The general rule of thumb is just that: show a little bit of cuff. This could mean anything from a sliver to a chunk. The generally accepted median is to allow 1/2" of shirt cuff to show. Some like to minimize this, down to 1/8"; others (check out some old-school photos of the Rat Pack) show quite a bit more. If you'll be wearing french cuffs and cufflinks, you may want to have the links just peeking out from under the jacket. Also consider that the shirt-jacket relationship will move as you move, and if the jacket sleeves are too short the shirt sleeves can puff out beyond the ends, which is not a good look.

    Width of the legs seems good. It's hard to tell, but at first sight I don't see a break in the pant legs. It could be a matter of preference; but the classic style is for the pants to be long enough to break (i.e., bend or wrinkle) once, a few inches above the shoes. If it matters to you, you could have a tailor see if there's enough material to lengthen the legs.

    Black shoes and belt work fine with the suit. They also make sense given your dark hair. At some point, I'd also encourage you to wear brown shoes and belt, as the look is warmer, more interesting, and more elegant. It may take some time to see it that way, though; no rush. If you do eventually investigate brown shoes to go with the navy suit (or grey, or many standard outfit colors), you'll find a wide range of hues and shades to choose from.

    I like that you're wearing a bright, colorful tie. Keep that up, and enjoy the exuberance of your full artistic expression when wearing your suit, or really at any time. I hear that one of the top beefs women have with the way men dress is how lazy they can be about expressing themselves, and how often men show a lack of vision and daring when using color.

    Edit: On viewing your supplemental photos, it does seem that the arms are too long and the legs too short. The fit around the body/waist is a matter of preference. It is fine as it is; and it could also be taken in a little if you like more shape shown. As it is, it looks comfortable. If you want to intentionally project a more artistic or stylish look, you can take in the waist (and not that you need to do that — only do it if it suits who you are).
