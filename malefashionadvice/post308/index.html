    How does one dress posh?

    Following the basics of style will be essential. Proper fit and combining (color, pattern, texture, level of formality, and genre) will make or break the outfit.

    Basic building blocks of a 'posh' outfit:

    • Lush, textured fabrics like velvet for blazers or pants, silk for ties, royal oxford or broadcloth with a sheen for shirts
    • Satin accents are possible
    • Deep and rich colors like black, burgundy, plum, aubergine, midnight blue
    • Luxurious patterns like paisley, intricate foulards, and some (dense) pindots or checks to be used in accessories
    • Sleek, well-shined leather dress shoes, or leather shoes with texture
    • Predominantly dark outfit, or high contrast with dark jacket and tie on white shirt
    • Perfect tailored fit
    • Accessories and design elements with flair: silky pocket square, buttons made of expensive or unique material (mother of pearl, expensive metals), tie clip or cuff links made with expensive metals and jewels

    These elements must be combined in sophisticated ways. If your taste and ability are not sophisticated, then do a basic color scheme. There is a danger in being too matchy-matchy; for example, don't try to match tie and pocket square. Pattern-on-pattern combinations can add to the lush look, but might be difficult for the uninitiated to get right on the first go. Basically, you want a good portion of dash without overdoing it.

    There's also an implication of indulgence, decadence, sensuality, and hedonism in the term "posh". That is one reason why items should fit closer or else have a flowing drape (sometimes both in one outfit): in order to emphasize one's physicality and thus one's sensuality and sexuality.

    "Formal" is not the same thing, although a posh look may include formality. But a rigid formality somewhat opposes a posh look. You want to achieve a high-class vibe, but with some elements that push the envelope and demonstrate that you are not tied to a rigid code. A velvet blazer, white dress shirt with a couple of buttons open, and a bright or richly patterned, flowing pocket square may be a good start. Add a flash of metal at the wrist with cuff links and/or high quality metal watch, well fitted pants, and patent leather shoes or maybe black or colored suede loafers.

    Take some risks with accessories, but only one or two. Overdo pattern and color only if your personality can support it.

    An outfit that is rich in content but provides a sense of leisure — as if you do nothing with your life but lounge around looking magnificent and drinking the best liquor — might be called "posh".
