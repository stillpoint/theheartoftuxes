    Help with getting started

    BFE?

    I'd suggest seeking out a good tailor. This might take a few tries. A good tailor may seem more expensive at first; but will be much cheaper in the long run because you won't be wasting clothing -- you'll get good advice and good craftsmanship. Bring one shirt to the tailor, and see what kind of job he does and what kind of help he is willing to give. Note that tailors, while they may be expert on cut and form, may have little interest or knowledge about style; but still, that's a place to start for your correct measurements and advice on the structure of clothing.

    If you can find a high-end menswear store where they are open to questions, that's going to be a benefit. You don't have to buy anything there at first; you can ask questions while trying things on and experimenting. After your first visit or two, you may have to pull the trigger on a purchase lest they kick you to the curb.

    See the sidebar on the right of this page for the How Clothes Should Fit guide; and research fit online, both in instructional articles and in photos of actors, models, and other well-dressed men. See for yourself what 'good fit' means.

    Buy and use a full-length mirror, hung where there is good light and space to turn and move. Also take pictures of your proposed outfits and critique them with an objective eye. Fashionable girlfriends are good candidates for consultation. Finally, if you really want to learn, start putting together a lookbook of outfits and pieces that you like or respond to, and outfits or pieces that you have questions about ("How would this look on me?" "Why does this look bad?" "Why is this being worn in this way?")

    Perhaps the most critical point is context. Your wardrobe should serve you and your life; you should not be slave to your clothing or someone else's idea of what you should wear. Everything comes from the central point of knowing yourself. What are you engaged in; what is moving through you; what are your essential strengths and offerings to the world; where is your area of growth in the near future? These points will set the stage for the tools and supports you acquire, whether they are clothes or furnishings or professional tools. Whatever you purchase should support who you really are, and what your potential could be.

    So don't waste time chasing ideals posted on the internet, but be real about the kind of wardrobe that will help you be the best you.
