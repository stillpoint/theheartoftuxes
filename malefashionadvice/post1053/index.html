    Suit help

    OP, please don't put your hand in your pocket when posting a pic for advice. We need to see how the jacket fits and sits on you straight-up.

    The muted windowpane pattern gives some movement, which is helpful; but in combination with the mid-blue color it's a conservative, middle-management kind of look. Good, close tailoring can help that, along with assertive color combining.

    First impression from the width of the pants is that the suit is too big or a loose cut. If other crucial dimensions like pants waist, jacket waist, shoulders, and armholes fit properly (we can't tell from the pic), pants could be narrowed by a tailor to make the suit a better form all around.

    It seems that you like blue! Too much mid-blue in the outfit makes it monotonous, conservative, boring. More contrast (and hence impact) could be gained with a solid, dark blue tie like a navy grenadine. More contrast yet could be gained with a white shirt as well. A step further would be to bring in another color -- something in a solid or subtle pattern with the field close to blue (green or purple), or something opposite to blue (gold, orange, or brown; or even deep red). Take to heart the advice about contrast even with the additional color; let the shade be deep to provide variation against the mid-blue suit. With a colored tie, the blue shirt can come back in if preferred.

    The tie clip is not strictly necessary, since you are wearing a jacket that keeps the tie from flapping around; so it is an aesthetic move, which is fine. Placement is fine.

    In situations where more stylish expression is in order, you could wear a pocket square in a different color and pattern (and a color different from the tie color as well). This can be done with or without a tie clip -- you'll have to check a mirror to see if 'with' is too much.

    If the pocket square is subtle, or just passed on, a bold color statement can come in with the socks as well (same colors as the tie options).

    With brown shoes, a white shirt, deep brown or navy grenadine tie, and patterned pocket square (say, cream or beige with a contrasting pattern) should be nice.
