    Call for moderator nominations & applications

    I was surprised and grateful to hear that I've been nominated for a moderator position. I'm quite touched, actually, by the votes of confidence, and very happy that some people are finding my posts useful.

    I want to help MFA if I can; but feel I should decline, for the following reasons:

    • My schedule is not consistent, and there may be weeks or months when I'll be away from the subreddit.
    • I would be new and inexperienced at the role. (I actually am not clear on what would be expected of a moderator.)
    • There are others who are likely more qualified, based on greater experience in the world of clothes/fashion and a broader knowledge of style.

    I think that jdbee, zzzaz, and AlGoreVidalSassoon are all worthy nominees, among contributors I'm familiar with; I'm sure there are other good choices too. Maybe my best role for the time being is as a contributor. When I feel more capable of devoting the care and time that would do the role of moderator justice, I'll be happy to help out.

    ~

    Growth can turn out to be both a blessing and a curse for MFA. I believe that 'getting the message out' to more and more people should never come ahead of the qualities and values that are most central to the forum. As more people take part, entropy increases. Along with the brilliance of new opinions comes the risk of confusion, dilution of the original intent, and a swing toward lowest-common-denominator thinking. So it becomes even more important for the aim of the forum to be understood and voiced very clearly, and for everyone to make extra efforts to communicate clearly and respectfully, and to do their very best in posting helpful material.

    Growth always requires a balancing or limiting effect, to keep it in check. For example, if either the diversity or repetitive nature of topics becomes too much to handle on MFA, it may be that the creation of new niche subreddits or the revival of old ones should be encouraged. And an improved search function (e.g., with possible access via a cloud of tags) could mean less duplicated effort.

    If I were to make a single suggestion, it's this, to all MFA readers: Don't complain; instead, consider how you can help.
