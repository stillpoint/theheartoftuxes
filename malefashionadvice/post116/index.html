    My fiancee is set on this tux for our wedding this summer. What are your thoughts MFA?

    To me it looks very gimmicky. As Siegfried_Furst says, it's not really a tuxedo/dinner jacket, but rather a sort of hybrid grey suit. It is clearly meant to be taken as a tux: the peak lapels, the satin edge on lapels, collar, and pockets, the absence of buttons on the sleeves, and the addition of satin waistcoat or cummerbund all point to the design template of a tuxedo. But while a tuxedo with its dark shade and high contrast is the pinnacle of formality in contemporary society the light grey sharkskin fabric here is more suited to a daytime business or leisure suit milieu — the fabric and design are at odds.

    I don't support the mitigation of a dinner jacket to a lower level of formality. Let's keep some things sacred; let's keep class classy. If a lower level of formality is preferred, then check out a proper suit or even sportcoat.

    As is often the case, this mashup fails at a compromise and makes the piece shoddy at both the higher and lower level of formality. This is a slightly higher quality take on those cheesy colored or patterned prom rental tuxes. It is born of a desperation for difference or newness — something to market, something to fill the design portfolio — which misses the point of a dinner jacket entirely. The dinner jacket or tuxedo has a strict form that puts the emphasis on the wearer rather than the clothing. It is the epitome of style and confidence because it lets the dignity of the man shine forth rather than upstaging it with flash or contrivance. Though there is room for creativity and flair, it is achieved in a more classy way by skillfully working within the form. Diversion too far from the form may often be taken for a lack of confidence in one's own character: in a word, desperate.

    I don't think you want a tux. You are leaning toward something more casual, and you are leaning to something more daytime and summery. A well-fitting light grey wool mix or linen mix suit will be much, much better. The situation, your own character and sensibilities, and the design of the clothing should all align. That integrity provides the radiance you would hope for on your wedding day, every part of your being sending the same message.
