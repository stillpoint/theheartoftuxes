    Hello MFA. Looking for some thoughts on this outfit. Does it work?

    Not only is the jacket too big, it is an orphaned suit jacket, and should only be worn with the rest of the suit. For pairing with jeans you should have a patterned sportjacket or possibly a blazer. But if you are going to wear a sportjacket with jeans, the jeans need to come closer to the level of formality of the jacket, so they should be in a dark wash and sleek fit. Your belt should also harmonize with your shoes; it doesn't have to be the same color blue canvas, but it should go along with the casual and light feel and make sense with the color.

    For a party, at your age, I see you in something more natural. Wearing a (peak lapel!) jacket in a casual outfit looks a bit like reaching right now. I think you'd look great in a thin, well-fitting v-neck sweater or wool polo shirt (and if you can swing it, something in a brighter color or with a pattern) â€” a very clean look, comfortable, and maybe a bit more sleek than the usual party outfits. A well-fitting collared shirt with a couple of buttons open at top would also work (a chambray would be a very versatile shirt to own).

    In cooler weather, a shawl-neck pullover or a shawl-neck cardigan, layered with a t-shirt and/or a collared shirt (here's where the chambray comes in) would look fantastic on you. These are pieces that will be much more versatile for you, and will probably make a lot more sense for your lifestyle. Whatever you buy, though, make sure it fits well.

    You're a good looking guy; you don't need special pieces of clothing to look good, you just need for them to fit and combine well. You will ooze style when your outfit makes sense together and fits well. I think many people, and especially young guys, try to 'buy style' by pinning their hopes on one or more fancy pieces of clothing; but that's not it at all. Integrity within the outfit, and integrity between you â€” your character and lifestyle â€” and your clothes is what will come off as stylish. You will be utterly at ease in your clothes because they will be an extension of you rather than an image you try to force yourself into.
