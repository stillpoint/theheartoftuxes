    MFA, going out tonight, is this too 'boring'?

    Yeah, not bad. Good for bar-hopping or hanging out. I like the use of some color in the sweater (it's a good color for you); I like the contrast in the shoes; and I like and the fact that for the most part things fit well. The coat is nice. You're well groomed. And the watch is a good accessory for you -- it adds a bit of verve to the whole outfit.

    Some people find zip-necks (aka 'quarter zips') unpalatable. And some will object to deep browns with black.

    The sweater could be a bit more fitted. It's not bad, but could improve with a bit more shape to the body. And a v-neck or cardigan would be seen by most as an upgrade. The zip-neck sweater has a bit of an active-wear association, reminiscent of the ski lodge. It has also translated to a kind of retro/euro look with very simple pieces and clean lines; like slim, creased slacks with a turtleneck or zip-neck (no layers; perhaps just a sports watch). So I'd say it's not bad for chatting at the bar or playing darts; but for higher profile 'going out' you might want to up your game.

    I like lighter, contrasting shoes with black jeans. In that sense your instinct is good. The sneakers are a bit lightweight and maybe a bit summery. Again, fine enough for hanging out with the guys; but something in leather and something more substantial would add elegance and masculinity. Tan or caramel wingtips, workboots, or desert boots would have a more classy and weighty presence in an outfit for a night on the town. Light canvas sneakers will be strong with lighter chinos and brighter colors, in daytime outfits.

    Once the coat is on, the entire outfit is a single dark shade. The darkness works fine for evening wear; but the lack of contrast is not interesting, and it combines with the withdrawn, serious energy of black color to pull you out of view, so to speak. Some black adds depth; all black can make you disappear. The addition of a bright or deeply colored or starkly patterned scarf can provide a needed pop. Or else, consider bringing in more of a difference between shades in your coat and pants.

    The jeans have a faded black wash; and that's fine for casual situations. Again, it goes with the scenario mentioned above; and so your outfit actually does work together, with all the pieces on a similar level. But if you are going to a nicer place, spending a special night out, going on a date, or just want to bring the level of your outfit up a notch or two, a more solid dark wash of jeans will be better.

    Going up another notch with a similar idea, you'd swap jeans for dark or rich colored chinos, add a crisp and patterned dress shirt, leather dress shoes, and either a well-fitting sweater with a cultured texture (such as a cashmere v-neck or a fisherman's knit cardigan) or a textured/patterned sportcoat.
