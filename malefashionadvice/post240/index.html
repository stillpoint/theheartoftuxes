    How's the fit/color combo?

    Mustard yellow (orange-yellow) is the complementary (opposite) color to indigo. As such, it is the most intense and antagonistic combination â€” the colors create tension with each other and intensify each other's presence. So there's no sort of precedent or basis for the mustard color elsewhere in your outfit; there's nothing that brings it in. Instead, the blues are combining with it as water does with oil. (Not terrible, but off.) This is okay if your intention is to provoke; but in that case, I would expect a less somber background, and a pattern with contrast (like, say, broad stripes).

    If you had a shirt that was more harmonious with the cardigan, then the color contrast between sweater and jeans would be fantastic. The depth of color and definition between top and bottom would be a classy look. You could use some white, grey, or buff brown (or a simple pattern based on one of those) to tone down the contrast up top and bring the mustard into the outfit.

    In addition, the comfortable, homey feel of a thin, colored cardigan contradicts somewhat the workwear feel of denim and chambray. A chunky knit, or even better a leather jacket, in a more grounded brown would better suit the style.

    Finally, I'd like to see how the sweater fits with your arms down. Right now I'm wondering if it's a bit short. (And tight.)
