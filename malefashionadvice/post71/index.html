    The current state of hats in men's fashion?

    I think one of the biggest issues here is not necessarily the hat itself, but how it is used. Too many guys attach to the articles of clothing themselves, as if the clothing is what gives them style. It does not. Style comes from you and your self-knowledge and self-expression.

    Whether it's a fedora hat or a studded belt or a vest worn without a jacket, trying to establish a look based on a single article of clothing rarely works — it's the most amateurish approach. No statement piece will look good if it doesn't belong, if it doesn't dovetail with your true personal style and make sense with the rest of the outfit.

    A lot of us have not grown up with an education in style: we have not been encouraged to express ourselves; and if anything refinement, culture, sensitivity, sensuality — the building-blocks of style — may be discouraged in our upbringing, as they don't fit the common (but vulgar and immature) conceptions of what is masculine. So just as someone who has not been steeped in a certain language lacks the vocabulary for self-expression, many of us don't have the skills and style vocabulary to put together integral outfits that grow organically from our personalities and lifestyles. Similar to Tarzan's poor command of the English language, in our style language we are left grasping at straws, clumsily pinning our hopes on one iconic piece of clothing to 'give us style'.

    For a hat to look good on a guy, or even just okay, it must suit his style and the rest of his outfit. But that is not to say that his style or outfit wouldn't look better without the hat.

    Hats are so deeply iconic and reminiscent of past style eras and archetypes that it's exceedingly difficult to avoid looking try-hard when wearing one. It very easily looks like you're buying into the iconic status of the hat — it looks like you're trying to be the tough detective, or the smart-ass punk, or the gangsta, or the old-timey sophisticate, or whatever image the hat evokes. If it happens to suit your actual style, it may work better than most cases; but it may also be that a certain proportion of the viewing public will still see it as too contrived, and hence immature, fake, self-involved, cheesy, lame.
