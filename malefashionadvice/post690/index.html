    Wife thinks I look good in a fedora. It seems counter to MFA advice.

    No, man, no.

    A properly fitted had may have looked okay in the context of an outfit with a lot of classic character; but that hat doesn't look like it belongs with your glasses, face, and sweater. In fact, the black hat and scarf together look a bit like a costume set.

    Classic cardigan, upscale hornrimmed or browline glasses, wool dress coat, and a patterned scarf may be the kind of outfit that could accommodate a (well-made, well-fitted) brimmed hat; but is that you?

    In the context of the present outfit, it does come off as very young -- younger than a married person. To leave that image behind, find eyewear that is bolder and has more character, avoid the wide horizontal stripe or rugby stripes in sweaters and shirts (they're generally a school-age reference), and steer clear of cheap stingy-brim hats. Also, full-on solid black is not a great color for accessories unless you're going to be wearing some intense outfits. Black (e.g., in a sweater or coat) does hilight your blond hair; but it's too strong for your look as-is.

    Loathe as I am for you to cede decision-making power to your wife, what does she think of you in a poor-boy cap?
