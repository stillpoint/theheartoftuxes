    How do you guys feel about brown shoes with a black suit?

    In common use, it's very seldom good. Lighter tones in the brown family can work with black — light tan, beige, and taupe — because of the contrast and neutrality. Chocolate or walnut brown can be nauseating. But in a very conscious, high-style usage, brown and black together can play on the very dynamic that is often problematic between the two colors.

    The darkness, density, and dullness (as an energetic quality, not as a judgment) of the two colors can make them an uncomfortable match, like they're too close but also too different, like cousins being too intimate. (OK, forget I used that analogy.) But if there is enough support in a mostly-black outfit for the presence of brown, the quality of it can really jump out. That means that the brown can't appear just in isolation; the geography of the pieces has to be thought out and positioned so that they relate to each other. It is very clearly an intentional, conceptual move; it doesn't happen in an "oh, I just threw these together" way.

    I saw a women's black and brown outfit recently that was fantastic. Stunning. I wish I could remember where I saw it, to post the example. But in a general sense, I'd suggest staying away from brown and black, especially if the shoes will be the only instance of brown in the outfit.
