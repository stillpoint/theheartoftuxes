    My insurance is letting me buy shoes at almost any cost. Any recommendations?

    Yup, you found some good ones.

    The Hush Puppies Timeworn look like a cross between a chelsea and a cowboy boot, but it's done tastefully. I like the low toe profile, the minimal expanse of elastic, and the color of suede. The dipped toe is not terrible, but may be a dated look in the future.

    The Earthkeepers City Chukka is also nice (and also with the influence of a heavier full boot and the mere hint of cowboy in the toe shape). It's a good option if you want the ruggedness of that sole on a chukka; not so good if you sometimes need the more refined quality. As with the Timeworns, the dipped toe could eventually seem pass√©.

    I find the Timberland Tackhead Patch boot clunky. The overall shape is inelegant, too tied to right angles; the toe profile is awkward; and the sole has too much of a synthetic feel, with the dark black clashing somewhat with that color of brown. It comes off as the kind of boot Frankenstein's monster wears.

    The Timberland Wodehouse Oxford is a very nice design. The design features are interesting but not obtrusive. The minimal quarter piece (with the eyelets) is a unique take on a blucher. The emphasis on the asymmetrical curve of the shoe (see the view from above) is interesting; but I wonder if in person it would cause a pigeon-toed look.

    I notice that your choice of colors runs to a very neutral, greyed dark brown -- in the taupe range. Is that intentional? It could be quite versatile, but perhaps lacking the warmth and presence of a more intense brown. Depending on the pants worn, a good color though, and one that I'd like to have in my collection.

    The Florsheim Tatum shoe you linked in your other post is a very nice shoe, head and shoulders above any so-called dress shoes offered by the other companies. Florsheims often show a very conservative American (read: clunky) profile; but those are comparatively sleek.
