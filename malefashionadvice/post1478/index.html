    Looking to buy some quality, versatile shoes! What does MFA think of these?

    It sounds like you're doing well in learning and building your wardrobe. How do you think it's going? I mainly want to encourage you that it's important to have balance. You may derive a lot of benefit from a new and better wardrobe; but it's not the central point in life, and it doesn't change your essence. I wouldn't want you to overextend yourself mentally or financially.

    Others, like jenkinsorn, may suggest other brands for your dress shoes; and I would also agree that taking a look around is worthwhile so you can be sure the shoe you will live with for the next decade is the best choice.

    As to the casual shoes, if the majority of your activities comprise business casual and evening/weekend club and lounge scene, this may be one case where the desert boots or chukkas can wait until the dress shoe and dressy casual shoe are taken care of.

    As to the AE Townleys (brogues), I don't think that's too much flair by any stretch. But it does depend on your preference and aesthetic. They, or something similar, may be a good choice for going out if you want to stay sharp; and they may also do double duty in some business situations. But they are a bit spiffy, and they diverge from the more casual and adventurous styles you're looking at with the boots. This is where you benefit from (A) knowing how you want to fit into the scenes you'll be frequenting and (B) understanding who you are and what you want to say with your personal style. At heart, and in your most natural activity, does the sharpness or the laid-back ease make the most sense? Or, some other qualities not yet named?

    I'd advise against black boots unless and until you know that it's a style you are into. My casual black boots wait at the sidelines much of the time because, try as I may to get them in the rotation, they just don't match with as many outfits. In cost-per-use terms, they weren't a good purchase. If you find yourself turning toward more black in your outfits and seeking some edge to your footwear, then maybe that's a good time to start thinking about them; but not until then.

    You don't have to have a lot of knowledge to discover your best colors. Get a mirror or camera in some good outdoor light coming through a window, and try on every conceivable color that might ever end up near your face. If you don't have a lot of clothing in different colors, try holding other people's clothing or just swatches of fabric or sheets of color near your face. Also try on every color option when shopping for clothes, even if it's not something you think would look good or not something you think you'd buy. On your own, with a well-dressed friend or girlfriend, or via MFA, just see which colors make you look healthy and energetic and which ones make you look too pale or flushed or sick, or make you fade into the background. For some people, intense colors make them fade; for others, muted colors have that effect; and for yet others, it's some of each. You may be surprised at some of the results, so don't be prejudiced by things you might have heard or assume rules (like matching your eye color). The key is not just that the color should look good by itself or within an outfit, but that it should make you look good. We are not bringing focus to the clothing you're wearing, we're bringing focus to you.
