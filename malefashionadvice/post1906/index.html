    How do I protect my pockets from my keys?

    First, if the sharp edge of a particular key is the main problem, you can get it buffed on a wire wheel wherever keys are made or copied (like hardware stores and shoe repair shops).

    Some folks like to use a lanyard like the one you linked to, to suspend the keys in the pocket. A second advantage is that the keys are easily retrievable with a tug on the lanyard. The Maxpedition Keyper wraps around your belt. Other lanyards can be made with paracord and looped in a lark's head/girth hitch around a belt loop. Chains and retractable cables add to security and ease of withdrawal, but are bulky and nerdish. The problem with all of these solutions is the fact that some portion of the lanyard will remain visible. (And if the keys are not secured well, it may even be an invitation to pickpockets.)

    If you prefer to stick with the basic concept of keeping keys in your pocket, a keyport may be your favorite solution. This also provides you with options to carry an LED light, USB key, and various tools together with your keys in a package that won't abrade pockets. A variety of lanyard connections are also available, including retractable cable.

    For a lighter keyring option that may help keep keys from spreading apart in the pocket, try a more flexible cable ring. Also here. I have no personal experience with this alternative.
