    Is there the tiniest possibility in pairing boots with shorts?

    I think it is definitely possible — very possible — to pair boots with shorts. In my opinion, it is not boots themselves that are the issue, but matching the style of the boot to the rest of the outfit. You just have to be aware of what you're working with, aware of how chunky, heavy, rugged... or upscale, dressy, etc., the boots are.

    In my recent post on Palladium boots, I included a couple of examples showing how the right boots can create a more substantial foundation for summer outfits that would otherwise be quite airy and light. Boots can bring a bit more mature masculinity to short-sleeved and short-legged outfits that inherently reference what little boys wear on the playground. That same 'masculinizing', rugged effect can also work in styles that lean toward the outdoorsy; but the boldness can also easily be overdone, dropping into the 'metalhead' and 'redneck' territory, as destroytheheart mentioned.

    As jdbee noted, proportion is an issue; but with the right body shapes, the boots can work to your advantage. Low-profile plimsolls and sneakers can look too small and dainty on a wide-legged guy, so having more substance at the base of the outfit could help. And for long legs or slim legs, boots can not only provide more of a base, but also define a wider (unseen) line between the shorts hem and the footwear. Muscular, or otherwise large legs, and short legs will be more difficult to balance; the boots will often overemphasize the mass. Even those up to mesomorphic proportions can run into this problem (hence the cartoons in this thread lampooning massive guys with massive footwear).

    The material, style, color, and proportions of the boots have to all harmonize with the outfit. For example, some leather boots that look great with jeans will be "too much" when fully exposed, and with a lot of leg showing — they will be too far from the casual nature of the outfit. Boots that are meant to be worn with shorts, like jungle boots and, originally, desert boots are good options based on their styles, the materials with which they're constructed, and their proportions. Hiking boots may also do, though they take you into a more stereotyped outdoorsman look.

    Since women tend to have more graceful proportions and less mass, the grounding aspect of boots can figure prominently in their summer fashions. They will also have much more leeway with the style, material, and size of the boots worn.
