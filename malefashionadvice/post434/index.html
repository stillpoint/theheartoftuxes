    Help my date complement my attire at a wedding! I appreciate all suggestions!

    > I wanted us to not really match, but complement each other!

    Bless you! The more elegant choice.

    Metcarfre gave a very good suggestion. A gray suit would not only go well with what you'll be wearing, it will serve him well in a wide variety of situations in the future. To avoid being too matchy-matchy, let there be a difference in shades of gray. Mid- to charcoal-gray would be very versatile for him; light gray would be appropriate for daytime and warmer-weather events.

    A completely different color of suit is another option. Navy blue or warm neutral, with a gray patterned tie, would also go well.

    There is no reason why a gray suit has to be stodgy. If it is in a contemporary cut, high quality fabric, and if it fits well, a gray suit can look fantastically sharp and youthful. Metcarfre again hits the nail on the head by suggesting a plaid pattern if solid gray is somehow distasteful. To go even more expressive (but less versatile), check out windowpane patterns.

    Okjustshutup's suggestion of a blazer and chinos also works. Notice that both suggestions feature blue (or the more adventuresome purple) as the complement to your yellow, and any echos of yellow in the man's outfit are kept to minor, secondary instances (a second color of a tie patter, for instance). Even that can easily become too matchy, though. The main point is that you look good together if you're both in a photo or if you see yourselves together in a mirror. Matching directly is not important.

    The less structured, more casual blazer is a possible compromise for your date. Although sharper structured jackets are the norm at weddings, if the rest of the outfit is very sharp (slick narrow — not skinny — and well-tied tie, sharp well-fitting pants, simple and high-quality accessory, good dress shoes) then the unstructured blazer can come off as respectful enough for the situation while maybe comfortable and nonchalant and young enough to suit your date's taste.

    A departure from the standard navy blue is also possible; the muted indigo in okjustshutup's link is nice, and others would also work, depending on the guy's skin color (cobalt blue, cadet blue, french blue...).

    Another strategy to steer clear of "old and boring" suit and tie is to wear a suit without a tie — just a white or colored shirt with the top two buttons open. The flair of a pocket square in a lush pattern or color can bump the look up a notch if he is so inclined.

    Caramel-brown skin tone suggests to me that any neutrals worn could be warmer earth tones. Even the gray of the suit can be a warm gray or taupe. The trio of grey-blue-brown will be a useful combo, with infinite possible variations in the hues chosen, and with the predominant color up to the whim of the wearer.

    Please keep in mind that while you can state clearly what you hope for and expect from a date/boyfriend, you can't change someone who is not open to change. A cold reality. You may have to decide what you yourself are willing to go along with, or not. Good luck.
