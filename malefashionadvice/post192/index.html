    Help settle an argument?

    I must caution you that my perspective is very personal here, though strongly held. In my opinion, 'formal' doesn't mean matchy-matchy. Requiring the whole wedding party to wear matching colors and designs is a gauche, juvenile approach that unfortunately has become so deeply entrenched in cultural consciousness that it's taken for the benchmark of style. This is not a teen prom; it's an adult rite of passage that deserves a more cultivated attire.

    I wouldn't accept brown for the groomsmen's suits; it's too casual. Whether the design of the suits matches or varies, black is the rule for a formal event. Matching boutonnieres would, in my opinion, be more classy than matching solid-colored ties. I wouldn't even consider matching waistcoats. All-black suits or, if you're all rich enough, all-black dinner jackets (tuxedos) would be ideal.

    Black ties or bowties would be ideal. In other circumstances that allow or require individual variation, I would usually suggest matching only secondary colors in a tie pattern upon a neutral background, but since the bridesmaids' dresses are in single, solid jewel tones the level of detail in patterned ties would not match visually. In this case, yes, solid matching ties would present a more unified panorama of the wedding party. Without actually seeing how it looks, though, my guess is that I would prefer black suit/dinner jacket and tie with only boutonnieres matching colors. But what do you prefer?

    Also consider that this is not only a case of following guidelines of attire, but also a negotiation of opinion and power between you and your bride-to-be. On the one hand, you may want to set aside certain 'rules of dress' or opinions (your own and others') in order to accommodate your fianc√©e's vision and hopes. On the other hand, you may want to set a precedent of holding to the vision and boundaries that are important to you: though at first you run the risk of upsetting her, holding to your own vision is instrumental in maintaining her ongoing, deep respect of you.

    Good luck and congratulations.
