    are light jeans in?

    I'm not up on next year's trends; but as far as I know for this year and the coming spring/summer, I've seen no mention of light-wash jeans being 'in'. They are, however, more accepted in certain circles; or, shall we say, they turn up more in certain demographics: West coast, and especially California; rural rather than urban; sunny, warm-weather locations; and in ages 45 and older. Light wash is not on trend; but it has never been fully purged from our collective style vocabulary either, and remains a go-to jean for many, including style mavens and celebrities.

    No color is bad in itself. Where a color succeeds or fails lies in the intention and awareness behind how it is used, how it relates to other colors in an outfit, how it relates to cut, fit, and other elements of design, and how it relates to the wearer's form and personal style. Wielding clothing includes awareness of cultural reverberations: how a piece of clothing relates to the history of style, and what people tend to remember or the opinion that they tend to have about it now.

    We tend to vastly underestimate the influence of cultural sentiment in what we consider proper dress and good style. (For example, can you bring yourself to consider a 14th Century cothardie on equal par with jeans and a t-shirt when dressing for the day?) Nostalgia, trend cycles, and adoption or avoidance of retro themes play into themes like the light wash jean as much as the simple fact of its appearance.

    In the first link, the Bay Island Sportswear, there's a mountain of bad clothing and bad style. Cheesy, ill-fitting, poorly matched clothing seems to be what they're advertising. So I don't think that's any support for the notion that light jeans are in style.

    In the second link, the fellow clearly has the prepster-hipster thing going on that we see in many online blogs and lookbooks. The light wash, pre-distressing, and loose fit make the bottom of his outfit (we don't see the shoes) very relaxed casual and unconstrained, in contrast to the planned, structured, and color-matched top. The greater contrast, in style, formality, and color, achieved with the light wash is part of the whole concept. We might even gather that there's a bit of irony at work, actually using the widespread disdain for light jeans and making them into a version of "go to hell pants".

    In a middle-of-the-road outfit, light jeans can make sense in a more rural (less metrosexual) wardrobe that might include construction boots, t-shirts, henleys, flannel button-ups, and canvas or leather jackets. (But not only.) The palette will include lighter hues and muted earth tones. (Also not only.) The appropriate situations will be casual, possibly outdoorsy, daytime activities like having a bbq or watching the ball game. But not only.
