    What color undershirt should I wear with a dark blue chambray shirt?

    If the aim is, as you write, to â€œstep up your fashion game" you should absolutely avoid showing an undershirt beneath a button-up. It's very tacky.

    That's a very interesting shirt -- the weave and color are pretty unique. It gives off a slick, chic, sexy vibe, which is likely the reason you think it looks great with more buttons opened. That look will be destroyed by showing an undershirt. It's a goofy, too-casual move that not only offends (you're displaying your underwear to the public, after all) but goes against the style of the button-up shirt.

    In very casual situations (like backyard barbeques) you can wear a very casual button-up layered with a t-shirt, not undershirt. It's still a bit of a boyish look, but it's done. The circumstances and the shirt are completely different, though, from the shirt pictured.

    So your options are to open only the top button or to open one more button and do something about the exposed chest.

    As to the first option, it's perfectly valid to open only the top button. If the shirt fits well and is tailored to your form, the shape, sheen, and color will make it quite elegant enough to be written that way. If you're concerned that it's too conservative a look you can bump up the sexiness with the other pieces you wear. But honestly, if you keep it simple and well-fitting, and maybe add a single flash of metal at the wrist with a watch or bracelet, you should look great. Even with only the top button open it will look far, far more elegant than with a crude undershirt showing.

    Your second option is to get a tan and do some manscaping, taming but not necessarily eliminating the chest hair. Honestly, that next button shouldn't be opened unless certain conditions are met: (A) the occasion warrants the exposure, which pretty much is limited to going to a dance club at night -- other occasions will either be too laid-back or too public or formal for such an exposure of skin and hair; and (B) you must be fit and clean and have something worthy of showing off with the extra open button.

    The self-respect implicit in being well dressed generally means you let the entire look -- the integrity of the whole -- do the talking rather than stooping to ham-handed moves or garish items that signal your desperation to be noticed. Get it all fitting well and working together, and you'll be leaps and bounds ahead of the average man on the street. No need to show off with a single daring move, unless that is truly your personal and well-developed style.

    Edit: If you absolutely require an undershirt, get an one with a very deep vee, so the undershirt will not be seen at all with the buttons open. You can search online for deep-vee undershirts, or check the selection at American Apparel; but make sure none of the under-layer is visible. The same conditions apply as above: only for nightclub scenarios, and only if what you're showing is worth the view.
