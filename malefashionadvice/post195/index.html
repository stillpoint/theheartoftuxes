    Earth tones for young men ?

    First, understand the qualities of earth tones; then you will know when to use them and when not to use them. So what are their qualities? Some of the words we can use to describe them: earthy, natural, grounded, heavy, plain, stable, quiet, unassuming, and (except for a few greens) dry. Earth tones bring to mind to autumn, land and trees, camouflage, safaris, cowboys, old-time old-world travel.

    There is absolutely no reason why a young person would need to avoid earth tones; but if you're only wearing earth tones, be aware of those qualities (above) that you will be associating yourself with exclusively. The instinctive awareness that earth tones may be "boring" or "old" is not far from the truth.

    In their lives, young guys should be making use of many different energies, not only stability; and so it is appropriate for this to be reflected in their clothing as well.

    When "stability" is overdone, it becomes stagnation. So then you may want to bring in energy and movement â€” energy with clear, vibrant colors like red or burgundy or violet; movement with patterns like stripes, dots, checks, plaids, and so on.

    If the dry feel of all-earth-tones outfits chokes the life out of you, you might use clear and deep blues (French blue, cadet blue, navy, midnight blue) to add some refreshment to the look.

    Earth tones can be heavy or they can be light and muted, you can lighten up further with light and bright colors like pastels; and if the mood is too quiet, swap those muted pastels for neons. Every quality, every energy can be used if you understand it; and if it is overdone, there is a balance or cure.

    A great example of balance is dark brown shoes and belt, khaki or mid-brown chinos, light grey or tan shirt, and deep blue cardigan. Throw a nice blue in with those earth tones and neutrals, and you can rotate the colors anywhere while maintaining balance (e.g., tan shoes, dark blue jeans, white or khaki shirt, and brown jacket).

    Another example would be an earth-tones outfit where bright colors are used as accents or focal points upon the stable backdrop of the earthy neutrals. Pops of color in socks, shoes, belt, watch, scarf, or layering pieces can be effective, and can lift a dusty, receding look into something quite sophisticated. (Notice I wrote "or", not "and". It is usually best to create just one unusual focal point, or three if you don't mind looking quite conceptual. Two is too symmetrical and empty; more than three is too busy.)

    The second thing I recommend, after understanding the qualities of the colors you are thinking of using, is to check from experience which colors are best for you. Guidelines don't always apply. Just because someone says that earth tones go with blue eyes doesn't mean it's true in your case; they may be terrible with your skin tone, or some may highlight your eyes and others may not.

    So try many different colors next to your face. Check in a mirror with good outside light, take photos, and/or ask a friend for feedback. The colors that are good for you should make you look great. The colors that are bad for you will make you disappear, make you look less yourself, or ill or pale, or will bring out too much of your skin color (too green or too pink, etc.). Use your eyes to see rather than assuming anything or trying to apply ideas you've heard. There are many different browns, many different greens; you need to see for yourself which, if any, make you look fantastic.

    Final word: wearing black seems to be the default for those who are afraid of or don't understand color. Black should be reserved for those situations (and parts of an outfit) where it will help you look spectacular. It's too dark, dull, and serious to be used often or in large fields (like a solid black shirt). Against a mixed-shade earth-tone outfit, black shoes, belt, and scarf for instance could look very dashing. But once you get familiar with the richness, character, and versatility of brown you may want to purge black from your wardrobe almost completely.
