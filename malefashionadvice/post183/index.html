    Help picking out outfit for Museum Date

    Four main points for putting together a date outfit, off the top of my head:

    • Know your best colors.
    • Focus on fit.
    • Wear nice shoes/boots.
    • Be comfortable and true to your nature.

    Your best colors

    Are you confident that both burgundy and rust are good colors for you (not to mention dark green)? Usually the skin coloration that works best with one is not so good with the other. The main issue is that the clothes should put the focus on you and show you in your best light. A sweater might be a good color in itself, but if it's all about the sweater to the detriment of you and your appearance, it's no good. Try the colors right next to your face and see which ones make you come to the forefront and look healthy and vibrant (and conversely, which colors make you look more sick or make you seem to recede).

    Fit

    Although matching colors and patterns is somewhat important, and helps bump outfits up to the next level of sophistication, none of that will matter if the outfit is schlumpy. The better your tops and bottoms fit you, the more stylish and together and better-looking you will appear. Correct fit should, in general, skim the body without clinging and without billowing. (There are exceptions when you get into drape and flow of garments, and contrast thereof; but you don't need to concern yourself about that for a date outfit.)

    Shoes

    Women look at shoes. And shoes have a disproportionally great influence on the overall look of an outfit. They are also always visible regardless of what layers you don or doff. For example, a t-shirt and jeans can suddenly become a stylish outfit with the addition of rocking sneakers or nice leather oxfords, wingtips, workboots. Since you are going on a date, you will not be looking at jeans and t-shirts; but the oxfords, wingtips, and workboots will be in play. Do you have a nice pair of leather boots or shoes?

    Comfortable and natural

    This is another facet of the tenet that your clothes should bring you to the forefront. Whatever outfit you buy should enhance the real you, and you should not feel burdened wearing it. This is distinguished from the case where an outfit calls you to a higher level of self-care or expression. The main point is that the whole package has an integrity: what you say and what you do and how you look all express the same things. What arises from this is to your benefit: you send a clear message that people (in this case, your date) can respond to; a feeling of trust can arise since there are no mixed messages or self-sabotage; and you can be comfortable in yourself, which can be a very attractive quality.

    Bright sweaters on the opposite side of the color wheel from blue — the burgundy, orange, or rust reds — will appear more intensely in combination with jeans. If it is your intention to present that bold an image, you should probably let that be the main statement in your outfit. The shirt should then be basic and neutral in both color and pattern. White is fine if the sweater is a good color for your face (since white tends not to be the best color on its own for most complexions). Even when buying a white shirt, check that the tint of it is good for your complexion — do you take a stark white, or yellowish cream, or pinkish buff? A grey muted plaid or stripe is about as bold as you should go with pattern when combined with a bright sweater. A medium or faded blue chambray would unify with blue jeans while furthering the contrast that allows the red sweaters to pop (just make sure that the blue is sufficiently different from the blue of the jeans, to avoid the Canadian Tuxedo denim-on-denim look). Some solid browns could also go with burn orange or rust.

    Dark green comprises a very wide range of hues. Forest, aqua, teal, olive, moss.... You really have to check next to your face to see which works best for you. The white or blue chambray shirts should also be fine with some greens. Grey is almost universally safe to combine. If the green is not intense but rather neutral, then (since the rest of your outfit — the jeans and sweater — is fairly neutral) you can add more color and pattern in the shirt. A neutral plaid with orange stripe is a possibility. Deep aqua tones (with more blue in the green) tend to go well with plum color in the pattern. To be honest, the best way is to go to a store with well-trained staff (J Crew, Club Monaco), get some suggestions, and try them on. The mirror or a photo will provide your best feedback.
