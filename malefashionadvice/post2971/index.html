    What type of clothing do you think would look best on me?

    That is such a big, wide-open question. A million things could look great on you; and those same things could look dumb if they didn't fit your situation and character. So context is vital.

    The first response that leaps to mind when viewing your picture is "clothing that fits". This makes an enormous difference in how you look, and it's a defining line among guys your age: the same clothes can look awesome or dorky depending on fit. Read the sidebar guides to the right side of this page, starting with How Clothes Should Fit. In addition, start looking at clothes with a critical eye; from the standpoint of best fit, assess the outfits that you see friends, the general public, and celebrities wearing.

    The next change that comes to mind is a more groomed hairstyle. Check out online photos, ask for opinions from those who will give you a straight answer, and especially consult with a good hairstylist as to what would be the best cut for you. When you get to the stage where you're ready to try out a new cut, you can ask for opinions on r/malehairadvice.

    You say that you tend to prefer darker clothes. That's valid, and fine; but it also tends to be a teenage perspective. Just as the young person's palate has often not developed to the point where he would appreciate the unusual flavors and subtle combinations of haute cuisine, the young person's eye for style has in most cases not developed an appreciation for varieties and combinations of color, pattern, line, texture, and so on. All-dark outfits are kind of no-brainers for teenage guys -- either because there is a (reasonable, in high school) fear of standing out or looking flamboyant, or because there just isn't any knowledge of how to use color. Perhaps the all-dark uniform is to style what the freezie-pop is to cuisine.

    One of the key tools in building an outfit is contrast. Knowing how to use contrast -- or even just being aware that it is an issue -- can set you head and shoulders above others in your attire. It's not that you have to give up wearing dark pieces; but their darkness can be used to effect when different shades (levels of darkness) and/or hues (types of color) are used together with the darks. In fact, all-dark can sometimes come off as boring, depressing, and withdrawn -- not the moodiness or intensity that may be the intended message. Worse yet, all-dark can make you look clueless about putting together an outfit, or like a self-centered poser. Check out dark outfits that work and ones that don't work, and consider why. I always like this example of David Beckham with denim, a white t-shirt, and black jeans and beanie: the black is used so well in that outfit; its intensity is brought out dramatically because of the contrast, there is unity to the look, and there is a lot of interest with the different colors and shades. But this guy uses black poorly, without any contrast or creative color; and it comes off looking dumb and try-hard.

    As to purchasing clothing, the two biggest differences you can make may be with footwear and outerwear. While shirts and pants will be rotated in and out of your outfits, the same shoes and jackets/coats can sometimes be worn day after day. In addition, shoes provide the foundation for your outfit, and a jacket or coat will be the first piece of clothing people will see when you're out and about or when you're first entering a place. Well-designed shoes and coat can make you look well-dressed, probably even if the rest of your outfit is just jeans and a t-shirt.
