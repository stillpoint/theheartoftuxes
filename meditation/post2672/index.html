    Went running, then meditated, feeling directionless since. Has anyone else experienced this? and if so how did you over come this?

    That's all wonderful.

    It's all part of the process. If it is true that you don't have a clear direction to your life, then experiencing that, seeing it, and feeling it deeply are very fortunate events. Then you have some awareness and motivation toward clarifying your direction.

    If it is just the bubbling-up of some darkness from within, it is very fortunate that it has bubbled up and moved out.

    The only way you can go wrong is if you fixate on this and get stuck. That would be generating new causes, new drama, new problems. So don't check your situation, only respond to the moment facing you.

    If you need to look into your life direction, then look into it. Question radically, feel deeply, pray and yearn and mourn as necessary. When I hear a meditator say that he/she is experiencing pointlessness, I celebrate. This is a precious opportunity, to no longer be fooled by shiny facades like a baby distracted by jangling keys.

    Personally, I have indeed encountered something similar many times, and at times excruciatingly deeply. I wouldn't say I have finished my work with it. That dance isn't over yet. But I do so value being brought to the bottom of my life.

    It is important to continue. If you want to achieve unconditional awakening you have to do unconditional practice. You don't know if this is "something" or if this is "nothing". You don't know if it's just a product of your exhaustion, or if it signals the approach of something that could threaten to overthrow the ego (prompting a fear or boredom response to try to throw you off course), or if it's caused by a change in breathing and blood chemistry, or if it's a psychological issue from the past that is just now triggered in some way. You don't know; and to some extent it doesn't matter. Only continue.

    If you believe in meditation practice, then commit to it more strongly. Get the support of a good container — a clear practice environment, a schedule, a community, a method, a teacher, and a ritual commitment on your part. If you mistrust meditation and feel that it is putting too much pressure on your mind, then you don't have a proper relationship to meditation and should back off. Even so, you must continue and not get stuck. Go on to the next thing. Always go on to the next thing.
