    Consistently meditating for 2+ years. How to move to next level?

    >Not that it's rampant, not that it's all that common, but one should be aware of these pitfalls because a lot of people who come to spiritual stuff like this can be vulnerable...

    Awareness is key. And I think we are learning these days that awareness is not only for our own sake but for each other's as well: we need to take care with and for each other.

    >Then commit to a situation where you are required to surrender, where the program is not all based on your own whim (and hence your own ego).

    Conditional results can be gained by conditional behavior; unconditional results cannot be gained by conditional behavior. That is to say, we can't be free of our likes and dislikes by indulging them. As much as we wish it to be true, no amount of getting what we like will liberate us from the up and down of life. To the contrary, we are thrown about by the up and down of life precisely because of our attached, conditional mind â€” the mind for which happiness depends on getting what we want and avoiding what we don't want. (And we can add: ignoring what we don't care about.) The name for this conditional mind is ignorance; and the situation it produces is cyclical suffering.

    >Give to Caesar what is Caesar's, and give to God what is God's.

    > -Jesus, in The Gospels

    To get what lies beyond the conditional requires an unconditional effort. We have to surrender, without trying to hold anything. Just as in the list you posted of harmful, life-hating approaches, the liberating mind does not hold or force or avoid anything. It is not enough to just talk about it or criticize others who don't carry it through; we have to embody it ourselves.

    That is a radical departure from ego-based living. It does not mean that the healthy ego is abandoned; no, it should be encouraged. But it does mean that self-identity is not the alpha and omega of our life. To embody unconditional love or wisdom is surrender. To love the truth more than anything is surrender of contrivance and illusion and speculation and habit, and all those trappings of the conditional mind. There is no reconciling truth without surrender to truth; if you hold something extra, that is not truth.

    >The Great Way is not difficult for those who do not pick and choose.

    > -Zen Master Seng Chao, The Mind Of Faith

    The ways of surrendering are plentiful. There are many practices and many different kinds of relationships possible. The central point is surrender. If you don't like that word, you can say "openhandedness" or "unknowing" or "sincerity" or "no-mind". The word doesn't matter. Doing it matters.

    But our human habit is to cling to our whims and let the self-identity run the show. Left to our own devices, spiritual practices are soon diverted in favor of the ego rather than the truth. So unless there is already tremendous aspiration and clarity at work, we require an unconditional structure to help us get beyond our own ignorance and attachment. Since our ignorance is not well equipped to choose a liberative structure, our best aid is an awakened teacher, someone who can consistently speak for the awakened, liberated, unconditional perspective.

    It is completely up to us whether we choose to tread the path of awakening or not; but if we do we must expect that many things we now cling to will be stripped away. There is no way to hold on to our ignorance and awaken from it; the ignorance has to be surrendered.
