    Clear' means it's not vague. Aspects aren't left to one's guessing or vacillating. It also means the steps are well-defined, the relationship between aim and method is apparent, and the practice is taught in a beneficial order.

    Some (so-called) teachers only have their own idea, and little experience teaching to others or even attaining the fruits of the practice. They're mostly improvising rather than taking the student step by step; and the prospective meditator gets the short end of the stick. That's unclear teaching.

    A proper source is one that can virtually assure success in the (clear) aim if the teaching is followed.

    The source is ideally a living person who has...

    • learned the practice completely and

    • used it as a tool of transformation in their life (not merely dabbled or done studies on others or philosophized about practice)

    • has experienced the pitfalls and challenges of the path of practice and has overcome them

    • has attained the fruit of practice â€” the full beneficial results

    • has been trained to teach others (which is a quite different matter from merely being experienced in the practice itself)

    • has been acknowledged to have gained full attainment (which in some cases is awakening) and approved by other masters to teach the practice

    When the method is a traditional method taught by a lineage carrier, one can know that the method has (A) been passed down by clear, brilliant, wise, and compassionate masters, (B) been tested and accomplished and enhanced by many millions of practitioners and thousands of masters over (in most cases) centuries of refinement, and (C) transmitted by someone who has been observed and tested and confirmed by those who have previously accomplished fulfillment. It's not a foolproof arrangement, and one needs to maintain responsibility for decisions about one's own benefit and safety; but for the most part the traditional path is far deeper than book learning, apps, self-invented practice, and modern study-based methods in terms of wisdom transmitted and attained, and far broader in terms of wholistic application in real life, and far more certain and stable in terms of the students' safety and attainment.

    A clear teacher can:

    • transmit authentic teaching (rather than supposition and incomplete teaching)

    • assess the character and needs of the student and adapt the teaching accordingly

    • emphasize points that would otherwise be missed

    • quickly address misconceptions, before they grow into ingrained views and rigid habits

    • give encouragement and practical support

    • embody the practice, teach by example, and inspire by direct action
