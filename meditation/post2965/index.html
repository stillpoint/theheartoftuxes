    title: I have a beginner question: I have began meditating using some good instructions. But I am having trouble with mindfulness -it seems harder and a bit separate from meditation. How do you do it? write a journal? summary: 2.0 authors: TheHeartofTuxes date: 2015-02-23 23:20:27 some_url:

    You have the idea that dissociating is easier. Have you tried them both out to see if it's true? Remember, 'reality' isn't your idea of what's been going on lately or your label for what the general situation is. Reality is only momentary. Only what's happening just now, without adding your idea to it. That is very easy.

    Zen Master Shunryu Suzuki said if you have a problem you should limit its scope: instead of dealing with something over the course of years, months, or even days (as we do in thinking 'my life sucks'), limit its scope in time. A minute of problem is easier to deal with; a second yet easier. But some things are so intensely painful for us that even a second of involvement is too much. So we should limit that pain to the smallest duration, even eliminating duration altogether. In any case, we help ourselves by limiting the range of our activity to just a moment. We can always handle a moment.

    Dissociation tends to be a problem among meditators involved with drugs it other addictive or mind-altering behaviors. If this is the case for you, you may have to take some other steps to regulate your activity and environment, either along with or before trying mindfulness. I mean, mindfulness is always a good idea; but if it is so difficult that you only waste your time struggling with yourself, other life influences may have to be taken care of to clear the way.
