    I can't clear my mind...

    Your post suggests that you haven't learned a proper method. If you want to meditate, you should find a proven method and learn it well, then apply it completely.

    Meditation isn't pushing one part of your mind with another. That is just inner fighting. Uncomfortable!

    In meditation, you open your hands, so to speak. You let go of the demand for things to be one way or another, and sit in the center of things as they are. Openhanded awareness. Something comes up: no problem. Something continues: no problem. Something goes away: no problem. You watch it all.

    But many people are too agitated to do this by themselves, so there are methods that provide some clear object to return to, like feeling or counting breath, or repeating a mantra, or feeling the sensations of the feet when walking. When the mind wanders, you gently and instantly bring it back to the object of concentration. You may not notice for a while that you've gone away, but when you do, don't make a big deal of it; just return instantly to the object of concentration. The returning is the faculty that is practiced and strengthened. Never mind how things are otherwise; the state of your mind is none of your business. Your job in meditation is only to return to the object, to the method. Don't peek to see 'how it's going'.

    There are many, many possible objects and methods. The important thing is to find a traditional method that has been proven effective by many practitioners over time.

    I won't give more instruction until you find your good method and learn it. If I (or someone else) give instruction out of turn, it might not apply, or could get in the way of what you are learning. If you want a suggestion on method I can provide it.
