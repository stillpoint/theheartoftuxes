    I am trying to decide not to be stressed out about things in my life but am having trouble. Can meditation help with this - are there any specific mantras I should do?

    /u/Gojeezy makes a very good point: it is much easier to bring the mind down when you have already established practice at times when you're not so stressed out. It's like, being out on the ocean in a storm is not the best time to start learning how to sail. Still, it wouldn't hurt to learn; and likewise, if you try in the right way you can help yourself.

    The main thing is not to get into a desperation that grasps at a certain state or ideal image. The point is to settle down with this reality, not to avoid it, stuff it down, deny it, or try to make something different happen.

    The correct approach is openhanded. In fact, if you just open your hands and experience what that does in your bodymind, that can be the start of settling down.

    When you haven't already established groundedness in practice, facing an agitated mind can be difficult. Mantra is good practice. Physical activity, especially prostration, is good practice. Sitting in meditation may or may not be helpful for you; for some people, maintaining body stillness strongly helps the mind to calm down; but others never settle enough, and the stillness just contributes to the tension — something else has to happen before sitting meditation becomes possible.

    That is why I recommend physical practice. Check this recent post and all my related links.

    Mantra is very good practice, but it should be practiced continuously — or as continuously as you can manage. The name mantra means 'mind protection'. Making the effort to repeat the phrase time after time gives you an anchor in the present moment. When distracting, tormenting, attached thoughts appear, you have a chance to let them go. Because you are reciting the mantra, you can identify when those thoughts start to take you away. When they start to take you away, you lose the mantra. Either you stop reciting the mantra, or it starts to fade into the background as you engage with the thoughts. Some thoughts are very enticing! Especially thoughts that seem somehow important — thoughts about desires, wishes, worries, plans, realizations. These are all just thoughts, and are to be let go of, ungrasped. It doesn't mean they necessarily disappear, but you don't engage with them. That is how your mind is protected by mantra. You let go of the sneaky, enticing thoughts, and return to the simple reality of the repeated phrase.

    Almost any phrase is fine for this kind of practice; but there are some traditional mantras handed down. In cases of overthinking, a mantra that can easily be repeated in quick repetition is best. You can match the energy of the upset with fast, energetic mantra.

    "Kwanseum Bosal" (pronounced Kwan Say Oom Bo Sahl) is the name of the Bodhisattva of Compassion. This energy will take care of you, and will also help you take care of others. This mantra, or some variation of it, is used by millions of people in East Asia.

    Perhaps the most famous mantra is Om Mani Padme Hum (pronounced Ohm Mah Nee Pahd May Hoom in Sanskrit, or Ohm Mah Nee Ped May Hoong in Tibetan) is used by millions of people in Central Asia. It literally means "Om (the sound of all manifestation), the jewel in the lotus, Hum (indivisible)". One of many interpretations is that it talks about pure, clear mind within the unfolding of all things.

    Either of these mantras is very good and effective. You can choose, and then do it moment to moment to moment.

    But also check physical practice as a way to help yourself.
