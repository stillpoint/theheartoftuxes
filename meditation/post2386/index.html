    Today will be my first time meditating. Any advice?

    No one can say what it will be like for you. They can only say what it was like for them, or what they imagine.

    One of the very great things about the Vipassana tradition (if you are indeed entering a tradition) is the quality of what in Sanskrit is called "ehipassiko". This loosely translates as "come-see-ish". It means no one can tell you what you should experience or understand; you come and see for yourself. Then what you get from it is something that is truly yours and can't be taken away. Others with experience and wisdom may point in a direction, but you do it yourself and so you get your own experience and wisdom.

    .

    >I'm not sure what to expect, honestly.

    That is wonderful, and precious. When practitioners are just starting out, they don't realize how precious that is. But that's also okay. Not knowing what will happen and not knowing how to act and feeling lost or overwhelmed at times — all of these are appropriate for the beginning stages of any significant pursuit. So everything is well and good.

    But do keep that. We usually try to be experts too soon. During beginner time, just be a beginner. That is correct. Then this marvelous teaching will be able to work on you in ways you can't imagine and could never fashion by yourself even with all the expertise in the world.

    Just participate: that's the main thing. Follow the situation as it arises, and all will come out well.

    .

    In terms of specific practical tools, the one very most important and helpful tool is this: don't check your experience. Don't step outside of yourself to judge how it's going (or how it went); don't make decisions about whether you succeeded or failed; don't make any ideas about your experience — only do it.

    And if (or when, really) you do judge and check and make ideas, then quickly realize "oh, I'm just checking" and drop it. The biggest problem in meditation is checking. The more you can let it go, the better you will do.

    That means that when things are difficult, they are just difficult; and when things are easy, they are just easy. No need to add ideas to that. Only continue, continue, continue.

    .

    Very happy for you on your new journey. Best wishes. Don't check.
