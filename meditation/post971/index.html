    For one thing, you learn and train with someone who is beyond illusion. You yourself can't reliably judge your practice because, as you've noted, illusion arises.

    It's only when you've purified your perception -- which can be verified by a master, by the way -- that you can begin to judge your own practice on any more than the most cursory level.

    It also requires knowing what constitutes good practice, what constitutes happiness, and so on. There's a learning curve; and it takes time to weed out misconceptions and make good use of language. That's why training within a single (proven) path is crucial: jumping around doesn't allow you to learn deeply, and picking and choosing deepens the illusory, object-attached mind.

    ~

    Practice comes from the center and radiates outward. You don't check it, you just do it. When you know correct practice and apply it sincerely and meticulously, it has its right effect. But if you step outside of your experience and try to judge, already that's not mindfulness; that's checking, making ideas, and attaching to objects, all of which are illusory.

    So right predictor definitely has a faith aspect to it: faith in the practice and faith in your unadorned mind. You have to have confidence that your attention is enough -- that you are enough -- without adding anything fancy or avoiding what's real.

    How do you get that faith or confidence?

    • Right teaching.

    • Good example of those who are 'getting it' along the way and those who have fully 'got it' (teachers who have fulfilled the practice and as a result are consistently clear, compassionate, and happy).

    • Verifying the teaching through your own direct experience.

    • Doing complete letting go... and discovering that it's okay, and true.

    But you don't get to check. You don't necessarily know how your practice is going. You apply a proven method in proven way, and then the only question is whether you are sincere. From that center of sincerity, the entire practice unfolds by itself. If the center is right, what unfolds from it will be right.
