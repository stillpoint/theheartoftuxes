    Worth paying for a teacher?

    It may be worth it, but you won't know if it's worth it until later, when you see the results. I am just a bit suspicious of any meditation teacher that requires direct payment. Maybe suspicious is too strong a word; but I think twice about it. Sometimes a membership payment is required in order to support the temple or venue, and to allow the teacher to continue her or his work, but if it's a money-making venture I might question the priorities.

    Many centers offer at least some kind of free or nominal-charge meditation opportunities. Maybe try some of these and get a feel for the sincerity and capability of the teacher before committing to a big payment.

    Money is an interesting issue in spiritual training. In traditional Asian cultures there has been a symbiosis between monastics and laypeople, where an exchange is expected. In the West, the issue of payment is loaded with both suspicion and value: we may question the motives of a teacher that asks for money, but if we aren't required to pay anything we may not value the teaching! I've seen it many times. People who get to go on a course or retreat for free often tend to treat it as disposable. Less effort and respect are offered py the participant than if a sizeable payment were required. So sometimes money is asked for, for the sake of the participant.

    Ultimately, your true mind doesn't need anything else, and you already have everything you need in order to embark on the journey of meditation. Some teachers take a traditionally pure approach and only invite dana, or voluntary offerings. Monks in some lineages are not supposed to handle money at all. Others go along with the times and play the game that society plays.

    You will be better able to determine the appropriate course of action if your own direction is clear: What do you want and expect of meditation? What is the end goal? If your direction is clear and non-grasping, you won't fall for shiny packaging and fancy talk.
