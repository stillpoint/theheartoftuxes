    I think I had better results in the beginning than now after 3 months

    Two problems: checking and lack of training.

    "I think I had better results..." is checking. You leave your practice and step outside of the moment to have a look at how you're doing; then you judge it, label it, and react to it. All this is a mind sickness that you and all of us participate in. It is an epidemic mind sickness.

    Your practice must just be your practice. Only do it, don't check.

    The second problem is lack of training. That means both that you need the proper supportive structure and you need to apply it:

    • good teacher

    • good and frequent and consistent teaching

    • practice with a group

    • good schedule

    • good practice space

    • conducive lifestyle (without strongly negative actions or undue distractions)

    • proper proven method

    • proper learning of the method

    • proper application of the method

    When these factors are in place, you have a very strong structure that will support you. Once you have the supportive structure, then the way you save yourself is to do the practice. Very simple. In any case, you can continue and if you keep a determined attitude, then you don't need the outer structure to be perfect.

    Your mind is still monkey-mind, flying here and there. You are using your own structure ("everything perfect") to help control your mind; but this is not always sustainable. A different structure, as above — especially regular contact with a teacher, a teaching, and commitment to a practice group — will allow you to control your mind even when things are not perfect.

    That is known as 'hot training'.

    'Cool training' means you have things easy and relaxing: it feels good, but when something changes you can no longer find your stability. Therefore cool training can't 'cook' you; your practice remains raw, immature, depending on outside circumstances.

    'Hot training' keeps requiring you to go outside your comfort zone. But it is overseen by a wise teacher, who can lead the way because he or she has travelled the way. This is different than going outside of your comfort zone without a clear direction (like, say, extreme sports or other thrill-seeking).

    When you do hot training, you learn how to be stable regardless of changes in environment, relationship, situation, inner condition, and so on. Thus your life gets cooked; your practice matures, and you become able to offer compassion to anyone in any situation. Your peace of mind and compassion no longer depend on your like and dislike.

    If you practice on your own, you must learn a method well; the method must be a good, proven one; it must suit your aim; and you must entrust yourself to the method. No stepping outside to check and judge. No comparing 'me vs. him' or 'now vs. before'. Only do it!

    Your mind moves because at some level you do not give yourself over to the method. When you give yourself over to the method there will be no problem. Sometimes the thinking mind is more active, so you return to the method more often. Sometimes the mind is more calm, so you only need to return a few times. Both are correct. The main thing is that you return, and don't bargain with yourself. Don't harbor the notion that there is something more important or helpful than the method — some insight or adjustment that will work its magic, or the idea that it's necessary to think about some aspect of your life or figure out something about the method of meditation. If you haven't accepted the method — if you have doubt about it, then that needs to be resolved outside of meditation. You need to get clear on your direction or aim, and the method that will serve it; and then let go of doubt and dive in. Put down all restless checking and manipulation, and return completely to the method.

    Finally, you don't know what constitutes 'better' or 'worse' in terms of progress in meditation. There is a vast landscape with many levels and dimensions that you as yet have no perception of. So you are not really in a position to judge whether you really doing worse. If you are continuing, then maybe you are doing better. Of course, some occasional review is necessary, to see how your mind and life are being affected. If you're moving away from sanity, you should stop, for example. But within meditation there is no place for analysis; there is only the task at hand.
