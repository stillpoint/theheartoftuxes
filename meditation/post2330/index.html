    Do you think mindfulness meditation and visualization contradict itself?

    Don't try to invent your own practice. Beginners (and that includes most of us) are not equipped to judge the right way to practice or know the ramifications of various techniques. Remember that we are starting from a position of ignorance, already habitually mistaken about the very nature of our own selves and the world.

    Practitioners need to clarify their direction first. What is the correct and complete purpose of any practice you try? Why do any of them? You have to understand this, or your choices will be arbitrary. So that means understanding the correct purpose of your life. Knowing that in human life you have both an incredibly precious opportunity and a very fleeting time in which to achieve it, your motivation can be clarified.

    Trying to figure out combinations of techniques on your own, and going for advice you an online forum of various strangers from various paths, with various levels of attainment and various scattered motivations, is a very haphazard approach. You'd do much better to find a good teacher and practice group. Even practicing with a middling teacher in a form you're initially not so comfortable with will be a very valuable experience, much better than your own trial and error. If you happen to find a good teacher, that is better than winning a thousand lotteries.

    Whether you have immediate access to a teacher or not, you must clarify your direction -- your correct motivation. This will make your choices more clear. If access to a teacher or practice group is not possible (it usually is, if you try) you can choose one respected, tried-and-true path, study it, and follow it meticulously. Trying to invent your own practice is the ego's bad influence, and it will invade your meditation.

    If you want recommendations, feel free to send me a private message, or post in this forum to receive recommendations from others.

    Low motivation means seeking some temporary benefit for your life, to make your situation better. Medium level motivation means seeking awakening for yourself. High level motivation means seeking awakening in order to be of service to all others, leading them all out of suffering and to awakening. You can meditate with any of these motivations, no problem. Results will vary according to motivation.
