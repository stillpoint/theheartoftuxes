    title: Mantra meditation: Looking for guidance. summary: 3.0 authors: TheHeartofTuxes date: 2015-07-19 15:35:09 some_url:

    There are many approaches and methods of mantra practice. It varies according to the tradition of the practice; so it's good that you're looking for a center or teacher, so that you can get consistent, integrated teaching rather than a smattering of various (reasonable or unfounded) opinions on an indistinct collection of different practices.

    Is your goal to feel more focused, or to be more focused? They are not the same. Don't attach to feeling. This in itself will help your focus, as you will not be chasing after appearances. When you don't chase after appearances your mind can be still; and then you can apply it to your positive activity.

    You won't necessarily always know when you're more or less focused. The mind is like a mirror; it reflects what appears in it. So the task is to come closer to your mirror-like nature and to disengage with the things that come and go as images (i.e., thoughts, feelings, sense objects, events). But until you are able to perceive the difference between your basic reflecting mind and the images that appear in it, sometimes you might think that you are not focused when in fact your focused mind is just reflecting outside movement like a chaotic situation or an upheaval of thinking.

    The problem is not with thinking but with attachment to it. The problem is not with appearance but with the assumption that it has its own reality apart from the mind.

    All this by way of saying that you don't need to judge your practice. This is a kind of hindrance. Only do it; then you will get the fruit of it. But yes, you should learn a method properly: the why and the how of it.

    There are many good centers in New York. The question is which ones will teach mantra meditation. I know that the Kwan Um School center (Chogye International Zen Center) teaches mantra practice along with other methods, and the teachers can help you use it as your main practice if you choose. In case you need some evidence that you can get the results you're seeking, you can see for yourself that the teachers there are quite focused.

    Regarding the avoidance of anything too religious: (From the video on the Chogye IZC page linked to above, What is Zen?)

    >Zen is before words, and it points to a question that is there before concepts, philosophy, or religion.

    Aside from that, you can do a Google search for meditation in New York and make some calls inquiring about their style of practice.
