    Would somebody please help me find a new perspective?

    Thank you for bringing down these walls.

    You have already reached out, you have already tried something; so already you have gone beyond your previous situation.

    You write that your mind blocks you at every turn. So which part is you, and which part is your mind? How many minds are there?

    >"Master!"

    Of of the teaching questions from this kong-an (Korean for koan) is "Seong Am used to call himself and answer himself; so there are two minds. Which mind is the correct master?"

    All confusion comes from being too complicated in your thinking. If you are simple, then things are very clear: Chili pepper is spicy, honey is sweet. Water is wet, fire is hot. Shoes on the feet, hat on the head. The sun rises in the east and sets in the west.

    Everything is already very clear. There is not much that you need to do. But your mind blocks you because you say your mind blocks you. Something happens in your thinking, and you follow it and then say "Dang it! My mind blocked me." But that is just your story. In this moment, there is no blocking; there is just what is happening in the moment. Maybe that includes some thinking, maybe not. It doesn't matter. In the moment, everything is just truth.

    So to help yourself you need to let go of judgments and stories that happen over time, and keep making your moment-mind stronger and stronger. Put the emphasis on simplicity, and put the emphasis on physical activity rather than thinking. You can't figure your way out of your thinking.

    Take every opportunity to have a very simple, very plain relationship with whatever faces you in the moment. No need to make ideas. No need to make 'this mind' and 'that mind'. Return to very plain, physical engagement with the moment.

    The moment will always save you.

    You may find that when you return to the moment, soon you happen to follow your thinking again and go off into trains of thought that say "this is impossible" or "I'm no good" or whatever. No problem! Thinking will keep coming up. No problem! Only return. Let the moment save you.

    Never mind the past moment. Never mind the past situation. That's not happening any more. So if you're struggling with something from the past, you can know it's because you're making a story about it. Therefore, return to the plain moment, and use your physicality to help you.

    For example, right now feel your feet on the floor. If you are sitting, feel your butt and legs on the chair or sofa. Next, what is in front of your face. That will guide your activity. Just follow what is in front of your face... follow your nose to its end, and see what is beyond it.

    Right now, these words are in front of your face. So your job this moment is to be in your body and to read these words.

    When you leave reddit, something else will face you. Is it time for work? Is it time to eat? Is there nothing to do? Whatever situation faces you, follow it. Work time: just work. Eating time: eat. Doing nothing time: do nothing.

    The situation will keep changing. It may change slowly or quickly. Only follow it; and use your body to help you come into the present moment. When working, you can feel your feet on the floor. If you are using a tool, feel it in your hands and see it with your vision. When eating, feel and smell and taste the food. Be plain and momentary. When doing nothing, be plain and let everything speak to your body.

    If you still have trouble, use your body (and breath) more: move, exercise, dance, walk, run, cycle, swim. Every time, focus on simple and momentary and physical. This will help you.

    You don't know if it's okay to bring down the walls you have put up. I don't know. No one knows. You will only know that it's okay to bring down the walls by trying, and then seeing. If you try and get a good result, then you may have basis for trusting further. If you try and get a negative result, then maybe the walls should stay up a while longer... or maybe you need to try in a different way.

    There is no guarantee ahead of time; there is only your courage expressed in the simple moment. Just now, is there a wall between you and me?
