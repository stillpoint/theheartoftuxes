    Combining various forms of meditation in one sitting?

    On paper, this is a very good practice, and a skillful way of using your bodymind lucidly for 20 minutes. But I wonder what you mean by 'mindfulness', as the term can refer to many different ways of using the mind, the object of mindfulness can vary widely, and people can misname various activities as 'mindfulness'. If you are practicing a clear, traditional method, and if you are following all the directions, then yes this is a very good practice.

    Later, as your relationship with meditation deepens, you will naturally have more aspiration to develop the factors of enlightenment, including effort and concentration. At that point you can lengthen your sittings.

    Lots of beginners, as well as much of the general public, think that if something is difficult it must be wrong. That is simply untrue. The reason you have trouble is the reason for the meditation practice; if you didn't have trouble, you wouldn't need to practice. But because your mind is not completely stable and free of attachment it wanders here and there, puts out various uncomfortable thoughts, causes the body to react with tension and unrest, and generally creates the meditation situation you name "trouble'. More practice is necessary, that's all.

    So it would also not be wrong to just keep with the practice and see it through. It's just that the motivation to meditate at all, young and sprout-like as it is, may be crushed by putting yourself under unrealistic strain. This is something that differs for each person: some need to be challenged, and can quickly throw off old habits; others need to be nurtured, and grow best (and fastest) at a gradual pace.

    Since consistency is key, and since your consistency has in the past wavered, this arrangement could be very good for you if it helps you stay with daily meditation practice. Soon, though, please find a teacher and join a group so the sprout of practice can grow strong and independent. A teacher and practice group can provide knowledge, reflection, support, and rigor that are impossible to get all on your own.

    Finally, 20 minutes is not the end; and once or twice or thrice-daily sittings are not enough. The practice has to reach into your life; it can't remain separate. If it is separate it is suppressed, kept partly unreal. The correct expression of meditation is ~~your~~ whole life.
