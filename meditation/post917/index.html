    Does anyone else get tingling in the forehead, right above the section between your eyes during deep meditation?

    Lots of meditators (and others) experience it.

    Some will say that it has to do with the third eye opening or activation of the upper dantien. It's true that these events can occasionally happen among advanced meditators or very rarely as spontaneous events in people who have the right confluence of circumstances; but the tingling/tickling/throbbing and the surface location of the sensation are not related to these.

    In cases where these kinds of sensations, or pressure, feelings of fullness, or aches appear it is far, far more likely a case of what is called 'qi effect'. This is a blockage or local stagnation of energy getting highlighted by a new flow of energy. Sometimes particular kinds of relaxation or flow bring about the tingling or make it feel more intense. It is really nothing special at all, but rather a sign that more practice is necessary. In energetic terms, more flow, more letting go, and more dissolving are necessary.

    Purified spiritual energy is cool and smooth. It doesn't stir up these kinds of sensations. It feels empty or absent rather than full or stimulated. So these kinds of things are just local blocks or densities. It's like when electric current runs through a thin wire and heats it up. If the wire is thick and conducting well, there's little build-up of heat.

    But this brings us to a more overarching issue, that of checking. One of the most common kinds of question we hear on this forum or out in meditation circles is "does anyone else experience this?" Another version is "can anyone tell me what this is?" These are both forms of checking. One steps outside of one's experience and questions it, tries to label it, judge it, compare it, conceptualize it, pin it down, and use it as a springboard to further story-making. It's all ego-based activity.

    Meditation means just being in the experience. You and the object of meditation become one.

    All kinds of events and experiences will come — some common, some boring, some unusual. The self-clinging habit doesn't want to dissolve into an experience of unity; it doesn't want real meditation. It wants to keep making a story. So the self-clinging habit will keep looking for the next fascinating distraction. Anything different, anything arcane will be used as fodder for a big drama — the drama of you, the meditator. Either it's something potentially disastrous or it's something potentially spectacular; either you're threatened with harm or you're possibly a superior being. The stories take these qualities because those are the kinds of stories that grab you, that fascinate you enough to distract you from the real purpose of meditation, which is just to do it.

    So never mind the things that appear. Appearance and disappearance are not your area of concern. Your job is to understand or become the witness, the awareness behind it all.

    Finally, you say that this happens during deep meditation; but that's not deep. At some point, if you are working with an object you will unify with the object and everything will disappear. There will be no sense of an object and no sense of a self experiencing it. That is a taste of emptiness, your true nature. You can get a flash of insight into it as you enter or come out of the emptiness. But even that is not yet "deep".

    Deep means you just do what you are doing. This moment, as-is, is deep meditation.
