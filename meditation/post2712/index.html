    title: Help: Exercises to prevent choking summary: 2.0 authors: TheHeartofTuxes date: 2013-05-29 23:22:28 some_url:

    A couple of things have been very useful for me as a performer:

    First and foremost, look into and change your relationship with "performance". The fear comes because the situation is conceived as a succeed-or-fail test. "How will I deal with this?" is already starting from a position of weakness, as if something bad or dangerous is out there, and as if you are vulnerable or your ability questionable. The energy is all wrong in that relationship.

    Turn it around by focusing on what you'd like to share or what you'd like to participate in. If it's about self-aggrandizement, well then you could fail. If it's about participating in the challenge of it, then there's only the thrill of participation. ...And if it's about sharing yourself, your heart, your love of music, your best effort with others, then there's only the joy of sharing. Far from an ominous challenge, the situation becomes a happy opportunity to offer yourself, wholeheartedly and with no apologies.

    Second, expose yourself to similar situations, more and more. Seek out audiences and enter more situations where you're called on to perform. Make it commonplace for yourself, the rule rather than the exception, to be witnessed or under scrutiny.

    Meditation will help in setting the stage, in that it trains you in letting go of distracting and disruptive thoughts. You can let go of what's irrelevant and keep what is central. As a technique on the day of competition, it might just be a distraction. I don't know; that's something you'd have to feel out for yourself. But you do still need to contemplate that point: what is central here? What is the point of competing? When your direction is clear in that respect, then the dark fear will be transformed into its light side: intensity of presence.

    These are points that helped me; others may have different recommendations or more specific techniques to use.

    For me, this is all about sincerity. If you are sincere in the moment everything will be right, because your thoughts and your energy will be moving in the right direction. It is a wonderful, transcendent moment when your skill and effort of practice connect with a clear direction in your heart. This direction is not "for me", but for everyone. The clear, sincere heart can't be defeated.

    (Also, check the Bhagavad Gita, the seminal story of spiritual fulfillment through competition.)
