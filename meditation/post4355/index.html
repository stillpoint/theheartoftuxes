    Concentration Meditation....

    It depends on the course of training, and it depends on you -- your aims, and the skill set you bring to the table.

    Some basic level of concentration is necessary -- as far as I can see -- for all authentic practices. Insofar as concentration means relinquishing distraction and returning to reality, it is essential. Just how much concentration is necessary is a matter that varies between schools and methods.

    Concentration practice is given as either a preliminary or companion practice when starting out in some schools. In other schools, it may be assigned according to need or bent of nature. In yet other schools, the assigned method includes concentration in the qualities being trained, so no separate method is given.

    One may also move on to concentration work later in one's practice, once one has established a particular understanding or attainment. It is very important to have correct direction established before developing concentrative power, as power itself can tend to distract and corrupt. In some schools, strict form and the watchful eye of the teacher suffice to instill correct direction over time; but especially in the modern and western society it becomes more and more imperative that the practitioner herself/himself understand correct direction before venturing into practices that stir up unusual experiences and special powers, as concentrative methods tend to do.

    This means what is the correct purpose of concentration? What will you do with Samadhi and enlightenment when you get them?

    The short answer is that the course of training varies between schools and teachers.
