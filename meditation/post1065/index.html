    It's a chain of cause and effect.

    If you want to go to Las Vegas, you need transportation, a route, and certain resources (money, gas, maps or gps, and so on). There are several ways to get to Las Vegas, but you have to take one of them in order to get there — you have to follow the steps that bring you to Las Vegas. If you don't know where it is, or if you don't know where you are right now, or if you don't know how to drive or what bus to take, you will go around and around and around. Maybe you get stuck at the Atlantic Ocean or wander around in some desert you don't know.

    Furthermore, you may by accident find Miami and think "Ah, Las Vegas, I'm here!" Maybe you walk around for a while not realizing where you are until someone helps you.

    It's the same with method. If you have a goal, you take the steps that will attain that goal. You can wander around and around; or you can rely on accidents to take you places you don't recognize. Without proper teaching and method it is difficult to find your direction.

    You have been asking a lot of questions on Reddit that show you don't understand where you're going and how to get there. You don't know Las Vegas, and you don't know where you are in relation to it. Various people tell you to try New York or San Francisco or Denver. Others tell you things about Las Vegas, but they actually don't know how to get there themselves. You also receive different recommendations about how to get to Las Vegas — bus, plane, walk, hitch-hike... various routes. Maybe someone from the West coast 'knows' you have to go East; but you're up North so you actually need to go South. And some may point out step by step what you need to get to Las Vegas — "Well, here's a map; I've circled Las Vegas. You can take Highway 15 West from here, or you can get the bus at the downtown station this evening."

    Too much information! Some wrong; some right but not in your situation; some right but not complete; and some pretty accurate if you only follow up with your own action. What do you choose?

    You need a proper, proven method. Step by step you get there. Do step 1, then step 2, then step 3, and so on.

    You are already confused, so you need proper steps and a proper guide. It is not more confusing; it is less confusing. But you resist and resist. Every new post here you second-guess good advice and delay your progress. If you already know what to do, just do it and stop asking. If you don't know what to do, get proper instruction. It is very simple cause and effect.
