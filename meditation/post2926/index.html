    Nirvana as a temporary state

    Nirvana is not a state, so not subject to change. In a conventional sense it is attained, because we need to gather the conditions that allow us to overcome the habits, attachments, blind spots, negative tendencies, and other obstacles that cover up this basic and essential non-thing. In an absolute sense it is not attained because there is not really a self to attain anything, and Nirvana has never not been our essential nature.

    But in its essence Nirvana is not created or conditioned or changing in any way; and after we 'attain' it, it is not lost.

    Along the way you may have glimpses of your original (Nirvanic) nature, but the glimpses are conditional and temporary as long as there is some vestige of clinging to selfhood and appearance. If you think of Nirvana as a 'thing' at all — as an object or state or dimension — you will be completely off the mark.

    Next, as a point of practice: if you want to progress in spiritual practice you should embrace humility. Clinging to opinion regenerates the illusion of self which is the very obstacle we are trying to overcome. These kinds of personal beliefs are just speculation. If you don't know, it is much better to admit that you don't know than to dredge up one more in an endless litany of opinions to cover the fact. Focus on practice. Be curious, do your inquiry, but free yourself from speculation and opinion. It will reap benefits in your spiritual career.

    We have been deeply conditioned to think that we have to know. Many times in our lives, we have been called to task or shamed for not having the answer or being in on the joke. We carry this habit with us into spiritual practice, where it doesn't belong. It is a tremendous hindrance to seek a sort of prideful comfort by making opinions, rather than the plain honest truth. Don't settle for anything other than complete freedom from doubt, which is only attainable through experience — through the practice of letting go of concept — and never attainable through thinking-understanding. Nirvana is boundless and uncreated; how can thought touch it?
