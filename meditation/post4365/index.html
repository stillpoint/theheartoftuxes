    Struggling to meditate due to sinus issues

    Thousands of people who have come down with cold or flu during meditation retreat can maybe relate to what you're going through. The difficulty breathing, the dry mouth, the swallowing (and for those who are ill, the sore throat)....

    That's all okay. The only thing it means is: difficult breathing, dry mouth, swallowing (and sore throat). It's not good or bad. It is one type of difficulty among many that will arise during the meditator's career. It only becomes a problem because of your thinking. Only you have the power to say whether this keeps you from meditating or whether it becomes your meditation.

    I do commiserate. We all have our own set of pains and weaknesses and difficulties. But there's nothing that makes a sinus issue essentially a game-stopper. I've practiced with cancer patients, people who suffer from non-stop migraines, people who have been maimed in wars and disasters. And that's not to mention all the different styles of mental and emotional suffering that arise. The meditative mind can embrace all of it, and can make medicine of all of it.

    In practical terms specific to your situation, how does meditation differ from the rest of your days? Do you also have to breathe through the mouth, swallow frequently, and get a dry mouth in your daily life, or is there something unique about the situation when you sit down to meditate? Maybe something can be adjusted to make it easier on you.

    Also, as others have mentioned, there are many forms of meditation that don't key on the breath. Sticking with it can benefit you in ways you may not imagine; but it is also possible to change the method and sidestep the tendency to fixate on the breath. Then when your practice is stronger and more stable you can either choose to try breathing-style again or continue with another practice if it is one that will garner the results you seek.

    Most experienced meditators will tell you: Get used to "this doesn't feel right". Meditation is not about getting some ideal state, it's about training the ability to be with things as they are. The mind is trained to be clear and present, whether one likes the situation or not. Therefore, like and dislike no longer have a hold, and one can see clearly and act clearly.

    The main point is to keep returning to presence. Whether you use the breath to do that, or some other object, or whether you do it without any object, relinquishing distraction and returning to the here and now is the main point. You write that you will see a doctor about your issue but that "I don't want to not meditate until then." So just decide, and do it. If you want to keep going and not be swayed by like and dislike, just continue and find your way through. If you want to choose another method, just choose and do it.

    If you need help choosing a method, you can contact me and we can talk about your intent and what might best serve it.
