    Fellow Reditators need your help!

    You've apparently heard that ego or the idea of selfhood is a problem; but that's just part of the picture. You shouldn't then jump to the conclusion that you have to shut down or destroy it. When its unreality is recognized it disappears.

    When I was a child I woke up one night to see a strange man sitting across from my bed and looking at me. I was terrified! The strange man didn't move, he just sat there looking at me. As dawn's light started to rise I could see better what was there, and realized that the clothing I had thrown on my chair just looked man-like in the dark. At that, my fear went away and I was relieved. With the dawning of light also came dawning of realization.

    Similarly, when you see what's really there the ego/self-idea is no longer an issue. No need to fight with phantoms.

    You can let go of the notions of ego death and what you're 'supposed to' believe. The aim is to be with what is. No special arrangements need to be made.

    When you sleep deeply, you are very relaxed and at peace even though there is no thought of self. When you lie on the ground watching clouds, there is nothing in particular to do about them. Similarly in practice, you don't need to make yourself believe something or experience something. Trust that you are safe, as you are when you are sleeping deeply. Only watch the comings and goings of inner and outer things, like watching clouds.

    You say that you can't seem to let go; but that's not true. You already know how to let go. If you didn't, you couldn't ever move or do anything new. But you have already come here and posted a request, so you know how to let go of the thing that came before that. It's just that you think that letting go has some particular result.

    Letting go doesn't necessarily mean the thing you let go of will disappear. It may stay for some moments; it may stay for longer. It may go away and then something very similar may appear again. So then, you just keep practicing letting go.

    If you stop practicing letting-go as soon as something distasteful appears, that is weak practice. Weak practice gets weak result. But if you keep opening your hands and just watching — for ten thousand years non-stop — then you will get a very clear and strong result. Cause and result are infallably connected; cause and result are clear.

    Learn a good, proven method, and use it to help you practice letting go of the things that take your mind away (like thinking about ego death, for example), and use it to help you practice open-handed watching, like watching clouds move through the sky. You don't need our help; you know how to do it already.

    (But to help overcome patterns of self-diversion, make it a priority to find a teacher and practice group. The teacher and Sangha will help you even at times when you can't help yourself.)
