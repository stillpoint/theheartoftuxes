    Essential Oils

    I'm so glad you see the danger of intellectualizing things. Do you really see the whole danger?

    I can only offer my opinion here; I can't say that it is authoritative. From what I see, systems of knowledge about oils, herbs, crystals, the energetic use of metals, and so on, are not cut-and-dried. We can talk about the character of a plant the way we talk about the character of a person: we can describe tendencies, patterns, energies (for example, the feeling you get when the person walks in the room, or the aspects of your being that are catalyzed by their presence); but if we think that the description is the final word, that's nonsense. Our society as a whole has a tendency not only to over-intellectualization but to over-simplification. We have become addicted not only to words and concepts but to infographics, tag lines, and short sound bites. It feeds the ego's desire to have everything all wrapped up and under control.

    But I see layer upon layer of interrelationship, subtlety, and complexity. So while we may associate the (general) character of a substance with the (general) character of a chakra, so that we can make good use of the association, we can't say this belongs here and only here.

    Next, here's a vital question: do you know what a chakra is? Beyond the words and concepts you've been fed, do you know?

    Does the person who has been talking about it to you know? Do the people who agree together that it's a big deal know?

    Using the word chakra has fooled generations of people into thinking they know what that is, and moreover has fooled them into thinking that there is such a thing at all. But just as there is no solid, unchanging thing such as a hand, there is no object such as a chakra. A hand is just the name we use for an ever-changing association of skin, bone, blood, nerves, actions, perceptions, memories (of what we've done or felt through "hands"), cultural expressions (of what others describe as "hands"), and so on. A chakra is just a word for movement.

    "Chakra" means "wheel". But it is a wheel like the one that appears in your bathtub when it drains. There is a swirl, a whirlpool, but the whirlpool is not a thing. Without the water and air and gravity there is no such thing as the whirlpool. Likewise, the chakra is only an association, a tracing of certain movements. We name them so that we can come into a more aware and intentional relationship with the bodymind; but we shouldn't become a slave of the name.

    As I said before, if you let it all be then all of nature knows how to take care of itself. If you let it be, the bathtub swirl is already clear. The same is true of the chakras. While specific chakra work may be beneficial for many people, there is a primary point behind it that is the most important part. This primary point doesn't depend on chakras or bodies or thoughts. If you have a propensity to attach to concepts, you should take great care not to get caught up chasing phantoms, lest you lose sight of the primary point.

    The tendency to waver from the primary point is so strong in humans that — if we are serious about spiritual practice — we should be very cautious of losing our way down side roads. Use your own mind to find itself; use your own effort to support yourself. You don't need gimmicks. Ask yourself "Why is my mind looking for more guidebooks and contraptions? Why is my mind not avid for practice, for God?"

    The thinking goes out to all of these objects in the illusion that they are special, that they will proffer special powers upon us. The thinking goes out to all of these concepts and knowledges and strategies in the illusion that they are significant, that they will help us progress. Why does the thinking mind go out? The mind goes out because it can't stand to be at rest. It seeks agitation, it seeks drama, it seeks relationship so that it doesn't have to be with its boundless, voiceless nature.

    So essential oils are neither good nor bad. Just be aware that when the thinking starts to make a big deal of using them it is likely that your mind is trying to avoid the main point.
