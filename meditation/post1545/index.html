    Sounds like time to sit on your own, without being led through it by someone else. Learn a proven method that suits your aim, and follow it meticulously.

    But this raises the question of aim and motivation. If your practice is not clear, you have to look to the underlying motivation, and clarify that. It's like if you lose connection with the internet, first you check if the modern is still plugged in and powered on.

    Check the basic foundation first: why are you practicing meditation? If that's unclear or incorrect, then of course it will manifest as vagueness, sloppiness, and laxity during meditation: there will be no connection to the power driving the whole enterprise -- the vital interest and values in your life.

    If your purpose in meditation is just sort of vaguely to get some calmness or other ability for yourself, or to go the scenic route and kill time or get some entertainment out of it, then the result will match that input. â€œFor me" will always, always wander and peter out, as the grasping habit moves on to what next thing seems desirable or easy. For high-level clarity in meditation, you need higher-level motivation: What is your life for? What noble path are you willing to -- aching to -- offer yourself to?

    Clarifying motivation (sometimes called your 'direction') will also clarify the type of method you should be working with. There are many different styles with many different effects. So why you meditate is an important question if you want to improve.

    If you are acutely aware of the preciousness and brevity of life, holding in your heart the knowledge that it will soon end, there will be no question of wasting time with a wandering mind; when the mind wanders in meditation you will bring it back quickly and clearly.

    When will you wake up from life's dream? Next year? Ten or twenty years? Not at all? Cause and effect are clear: you are in charge of your own waking-up. Do whatever it takes: clarify your direction, clarify your life, find a teacher and method, do the work with interest and energy. Then in ten or twenty years... maybe waking up is possible.
