    Is trying to meditate during shavasana a good idea?... (x-post r/yoga)

    Follow the prescribed method. Study with a teacher and offer yourself to the form.

    You can do a meditation 'method' lying down, to better or worse effect (lying down and closing the eyes make your practice dull and dreamy, and unless the aspiration is very exceptional you will develop bad habits of slacking off, misunderstanding dream for clarity, and attaching to sleep). Sometimes due to injury or incapacity it is necessary to practice lying down. But that is not shavasana.

    Shavasana means undoing. Put it all down, let go of all busy-work, grasping, and making. Banish thoughts of gaining something. Don't try to be productive. The effort is in a wholly different direction.

    Shavasana, as the death pose, requires you to willfully and consciously step into your death -- the death of the grasping, holding, making self. You give up your body, perhaps piece by piece, with the attitude that you are already dead. The body is born, so death is inevitable; therefore it is like we are already dead, living on borrowed time. So you let go of any hold, any tension directed to keep or make with the body, and give it back to the universe. â€œIf it happens that living continues, or if it happens that death comes, I give myself over to the Great One."

    Likewise, the mind and heart and everything you are are given over. In the conscious dying you settle in the eternal deathless reality.

    This practice allows you to merge with reality, or rather to unveil the integrity that has always been there. Prana / life energy flows naturally, without being blocked outer manipulated by you. The tension and blockage of resident bodymind patterns dissipate. The mind settles into deep stillness. It is a very healing practice, and one that can bring profound depth of relaxation -- based on radical self-trust and giving over -- to more awake or active meditation practices.

    You can do what you want lying down, but it won't necessarily be shavasana.
