    Can not meditate anymore. My brain tells me that I am a loser if I do so etc.

    First of all, let's not blame your brain. Your brain helps you a great deal.

    A. Your mind is not yet stable. You have not practiced enough in regular days to handle times of upheaval with even-mindedness.

    B. You are going through emotional pain based on clinging. It's inherently disturbing. Because you strongly attach to a fantasy about a person, when the person leaves or the fantasy is pierced by reality your result is painful to you. Everyone experiences this kind of pain. You can use it to help yourself wake up: know that no person or situation can fulfill you; know that grasping leads to pain; know that your satisfaction comes from within.

    C. At some point in your life you received the (untrue) message that you are a loser. Someone gave it to you. It was not yours originally. Your original mind is pure and clear; it has no winning or losing quality of its own, it is just pure awareness.

    ~

    Rather than fighting with your mind to force it to calm down, you can use your painful situation to grow in understanding and compassion.

    Understanding

    All suffering comes from three movements of mind, called (in Buddhism) the Three Poisons: attachment, aversion, and ignorance. Attachment means we hope to hold on to what we like. But everything is changing, so we can't hold on to anything. Aversion means we hope to avoid what we don't like. But things are as they are, so we can't avoid what arises. Ignorance means we don't pay attention to things that are neutral for us. But if we don't pay attention we lose our life, and unconscious habit then controls our results.

    Compassion

    Whatever pain you are going through, based on your own actions, is a drop in the ocean of pain that all beings go through, based on their actions. Billions upon billions of painful relationship situations are going on every day. Countless people fight. Countless beings go hungry or suffer from heat or cold. Countless beings endure disability of body or mind. Countless beings go through illness, aging, and death.

    Your pain is not only your own; it belongs to the pain of the world.

    If you realize this, you can be free of feeling sorry for yourself. You can experience the tender sadness of the world, and you can vow to help others be happy. You can vow to wake up from the dream of self that causes suffering through the Three Poisons, and bring yourself and all others to complete fulfillment.

    >All the joy the world contains Has come through wishing happiness for others.

    >All the misery the world contains Has come from wanting pleasure for oneself.

    Shantideva (685-763)

    ~

    If you want to do a meditation practice to help yourself, but your mind keeps racing, there are a some methods that are prescribed for situations like this:

    First, you can use your body to help you settle. Prostration practice is exceptional for processing all kinds of disturbance and crisis, and bringing the mind down quickly. You can do prostrations slowly or quickly, depending on your energy. Fast prostrations settle the mind quickly. Work up to 30, 108, 300, or even 500 in a day.

    Chinese Style | Tibetan Style | Korean Style

    Other forms of rhythmic, full-body-and-breath activities may help (but perhaps to a lesser degree); these include running, swimming, rowing, yogic sun salutations, hindu squats, and so on. Movement practices like Taiji or Kung Fu may also help. Once the mind comes down, you may be ready to sit in meditation again.

    Second, you can use mantra or chanting. Mantra is the continuous repetition of a sacred phrase. Even if the mind is racing, you can still use your outer (out loud) or inner (silent) voice to help you. You can keep repeating the phrase through any emotion or situation. Your condition then joins with the mantra and you can experience the continuously stable mind that is always there behind your experience. The mantra Om Mani Padme Hum is very famous, and has the effect of stabilizing the mind and curing suffering. Many millions of people practice this mantra, so your practice can join with a strong current of practice worldwide.

    Chanting can be done with a mantra, a long mantra (dharani), or a scripture. You read and recite the text while using your voice. You can pour yourself into the chanting, letting any feeling feed it. So the chanting is naturally healing for the heart; it can accept any feeling that arises, and transform it into the energy of the voice and the sacred words spreading out from you. You can use any kind of sing-song voice, or just recite in a regular reading voice, at any pace. Slower is more devotional; faster helps deal with racing thoughts.

    For chanting: The Heart Sutra | Thich Nhat Hanh's commentary | Other translations

    ~

    You can choose any of these methods to help yourself. You don't have to do all of them. Choose one, or perhaps two, and do many hundreds or thousands of repetitions.
