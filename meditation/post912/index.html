    Am I doing this right?

    Correct meditation doesn't depend on feeling, and doesn't attach to results. That is why people here are encouraging you to just keep practicing without bothering about the things you experience.

    What you experience — whether it's mundane or spectacular, and whether it's a sound, a sensation, an image, an energetic manifestation, or a good idea or realization — will all come down to projections of mind. It's all your own mind material. We are not to become hooked by any projections of mind, and we are not to attach to any good, bad, or neutral experience. It's the equanimity — the stability in the face of changing experience — that we seek. This stability and the stillness it cultivates allow us to see what's at the root of it all. If we trade it in for the comings and goings of formations, we will never get to the root.

    Actually, you're supposed to feel just whatever is real just now, without adding any of your own assumption and story to it. Sometimes you like it, sometimes you don't. It is very plain.

    You may sometimes have a moment of emptiness, and you may sometimes have a moment of losing awareness. They are not the same thing. But once you come out of your experience you tend to make the idea "I just experienced emptiness", or whatever label you apply to the experience. The label is not the experience; it is nothing like the experience. So the experience, whatever it really was, has passed and all you have left is a label that doesn't help you.

    If you then try to justify the label you go further and further from the reality. Some people waste years or lifetimes justifying the labels they have made. Therefore it is better to do away with the checking, grasping, judging, labeling habit altogether. Thus the instruction: "never mind, just continue."

    ~

    As to eyes open or closed, it depends on the type of meditation you are practicing. And the type of meditation you are practicing depends on the aim and situation. What are you trying to achieve? Many people don't even know or can't yet digest the highest aims of meditation. That's okay, it's just the way it is. But the form of method should follow the aim.

    Eyes open is better except in cases where closed eyes are required for a particular reason. Closed eyes very frequently leads to sleepiness and dreaminess, and the unconscious habit mind tends to take over the process. Those who close their eyes tend to be defensive of the comfort and seclusion they experience, but also tend not to be aware of their lack of clarity. So a teacher and practice within a tradition are important, to give you guidance that will work within the context of a long-range plan. Beginning meditators are woefully incapable of guiding their own practice, since their habit, ignorance, and blind spots all play into their decisions and condition their experience.

    Meditation is for waking up to reality, so eyes open is, in general, the better option. The aim is not to hold on to one's own situation, but to merge inner and outer; so for that reason as well eyes open is better. The motivation is compassion for all beings, not a better situation for oneself; and that is a third reason why open eyes is better.

    But in some methods such as visualisation or energetic or touch-sense work, closed eyes may be necessary; and in some forms of relaxation practice, closed eyes may be more effective. One has to understand the point of the practice, and its advantages and drawbacks.
