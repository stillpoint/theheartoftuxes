    How long should it take to notice improvement?

    The improvement happens the instant you let go. You just don't understand that it is an improvement. That means that you have a very strong habit of holding on: making ideas, imagining other situations, wanting certain results, and so on.

    You already know that you have a lot of chatter playing in your mind. It is this same chatter that judges your progress and makes pronouncements about your situation. So you believe that some other situation or result (perhaps based on astral projection) will be better than the present-moment situation. Furthermore, you add to the chatter by checking to see how well you're doing and how long it has taken.

    So you have to let everything go and practice meditation correctly, just being present without trying to get something. This can be done without an object of meditation, but because there is so much chatter in your mind it would probably be best if you did have a proper object of meditation (or rather concentration) to work with. Is your mantra neutral? That is, your mantra shouldn't stir up thinking in you.

    It would serve you well to also get much more in touch with your body and your concrete surroundings. Physical exercise, simple and physically engaging work, and physical meditations will in all likelihood help you by getting you out of your head and into your body. It is also important to develop a good relationship to the reality facing you instead of seeking special powers or states. If you can be where you are, then you are free; if you always need to go somewhere else, you are in prison.

    The amount of time you spend in meditation can be significant when you are performing the meditation correctly. But if you are only reinforcing your patterns when you sit, then greater duration may not help much and in fact may make you fight within yourself. The most important thing is not 15 minutes versus 45 minutes; the most important thing is making a complete effort to be open-handed with this moment. Then you can extend that, and make it long.
