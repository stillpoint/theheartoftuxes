    TheHeartofTuxes

    If you commit to a form closely without wavering, it will provide you with the mirror. Since you don't compromise or make deals with yourself, it will become quite clear when you depart. Over time you will see the patterns of your mind: everything that takes you away from presence, all the attachments and avoidances.

    It happens by dint of consistent practice and complete commitment to the form. If you compromise the form it becomes less clear what is distraction and what is within the range of what you 'allow' yourself, so you have less opportunity to see your karma.

    That is the ongoing, gradual knowledge you gain. But the other aspect is sudden realization. There is no schedule or agenda for this. You only have to let go of doubt and do the practice, merge with it. When your mind and the object of meditation become one, you get a glimpse of your original non-dual nature, and the non-dual nature of all reality. That is, you experience directly that your mind and the universe are not different. This overturns all the dramas of your past life (and countless previous lives). The completion and intensity of overturning depends on the depth of revelatory experience.

    A few points on this second aspect:

    • This indeed can't be planned for or rushed. You need to settle into practice and let it deepen. Although unnaturally forceful methods can promote certain revelations, they can't help you integrate that experience; and if your character is not deepened in balance with the insight experience it can lead to denial, mental illness, and other issues of balance. It is much better, happier, and more helpful for character and insight to develop together gradually.

    • In order to proceed without doubt you need to have confidence in the method. In part this comes from your continued practice and the experiences you go through over time; but first it depends on (A) having a proper traditional method, (B) learning it from a completely reliable source (ideally an awakened master), (C) learning it completely, (D) applying it meticulously, with the correct non-grasping mindset, and applying it consistently. Those who learn a grab-bag of methods, invent their own recipe, learn from suspect sources, or have wrong habits that go unchecked will almost certainly have underlying doubt that will prevent commitment at a deep level. One needs to 'jump off the cliff' at some point (or as one great Faqir used to say, to 'hang on the gallows', offering up everything that has been clung to previously).

    These are the two aspects of practice: gradual and sudden. They are not separate, but not the same.

    As the mind settles, distraction from reality diminishes. Perception becomes clear. Instead of seeing your thoughts you will begin to see plain reality: soap smells soapy; warm water is warm and wet; feet touch the ground just like this.... This is actually getting to know yourself. But without good teaching you may not realize this point. You may think there is something else to get, some kind of thinking-understanding or external power. This is how we lead ourselves astray, based on our resident ignorance.

    Instead of basing your judgment of practice on your current understanding, begin to gather the causes and conditions for progress: the important supports of teacher, consistent teaching, sincere practice companions, truth-based relationships, environments and life situations that contribute to clarity and non-distraction, and behaviors and habits that aim at insight rather than mere thinking or other diversions.
