    title: Acceptance versus Motivation: How to Find Drive? summary: 6.0 authors: TheHeartofTuxes date: 2015-07-31 13:33:10 some_url:

    I hear you, and I am sorry you are going through this difficulty.

    In your situation, I don't think it's about finding drive but about movement. When you get moving, your drive will appear. Actually, flow will appear, in which your inner drive will move naturally from situation to situation.

    Many people going through grief shut down. They don't want the flow; they don't want to feel any more grief. And sometimes, they feel a certain comfort being the victim, thinking of themselves a certain way, having others relate to them a certain way, and maybe trying to be closer to what they have lost by resisting the movement of life. There are many reasons why people shut down. It is more rare for people to be fully in movement even through grief.

    One aspect of depression is its lack of movement. One could (and sometimes should) seek out cures related to the source, but it is often very effective simply to start moving. This can in some cases mean initiating life activity, but very often it's as simple as moving the body. This can be surprisingly helpful. And I get the sense that it would be helpful for you in your situation.

    As Gabrielle Roth says,

    >When we free the body, the heart begins to open. When the body and the heart taste freedom, the mind wonâ€™t be far behind. And when we put the psyche into motion, it will start to heal itself.

    Let me be clear: this is not saying that you have to force yourself to do something in particular. Especially having encountered trauma, it is important to be kind to yourself. Do you give yourself that permission? Do you fully take care of yourself and fully give yourself space to be whatever you are and feel whatever you feel?

    Giving yourself space and permission can be the trigger for new movement to appear spontaneously. Because it is often when we still have self-criticism or limitation that the resistant mind appears. "You have to get on with things!" "No, leave me alone!" "Look, it's getting late, and you have to accomplish this and this and this...." "Get off me! I want to be free!" This kind of inner dynamic. When space is offered, natural movement appears, because your being wants to move; your being is movement already.

    But in an overwhelmingly large number of cases, the encouragement to move is very helpful. Put on some music and dance. Swim. Run. Bike. Do 108 prostrations.

    The physical movement itself, the deeper breath, the flow of blood and energy will help feelings and thoughts dislodge from their stuck positions. It will help you to find your natural flow where you're not trying to figure everything out in your head, but moving confidently from your center.

    If the movement can be free-flowing and creative, so much the better. For this reason, free dance is at the pinnacle of activities that can help the psyche to move and heal. This kind of movement can actually embrace and integrate anything that you are going through, whether it's grief or rage or exhaustion or joy or mystery or plainness.... You can take the shape of what is happening for you, express it, and move on to the next thing. Your suffering becomes art, it becomes conscious, and it becomes liberated from you and you from it. There are many movement modalities that welcome your sincere, spontaneous movement (5Rhythms, Soul Motion, Movement Medicine, Open Floor, Biodanza, Continuum, and others), but a relatively safe 'barefoot boogie' might do. If you have access to a pool, lake, or ocean, playful movement in water can also be very healing.

    Even if you feel very inert, try moving your limbs around or changing your posture or view. This can be the impetus for your sincere inner movement to appear. Then follow it. Crying time: cry. Doing time: do.

    Keep it moving. The movement reveals the motivation. If you fixate on finding cures and resources you may find it more difficult to actually move on.
