    Forms of meditation focusing on things that make you uncomfortable.

    >I don't want to unknowingly act on fear so I want to get down to the root of the problems before I try to solve the problems because at the moment I have no idea where to start or even where to end.

    Sometimes you just have to take a step. Movement itself will work things out. Sometimes you literally have to get up and make a move.

    We usually can't know all the forces that motivate us; not until we are Buddhas, awakened to every last territory of mind.

    You want to be sure that you are not acting out of fear when you make important decisions. You want important decisions to be based on a good and clear foundation so good and clear results can follow. But your approach is turned around. The correct relationship with the mind isn't to try and control its every move, in the belief that you can eradicate mistakes this way. The correct relationship is open-handed and trusting.

    The movement of your energy should not turn back around to check and obsess on what is happening in your situation; the movement of your energy should radiate outward. The effort moves from the inside, out.

    The key, then, isn't to do quality control checks. The key is to be sincere. You must practice sincerity, and you must practice trust in yourself and in life. The sincerity appears at the center and radiates to all aspects of your life. All parts of the mandala (the unfolding configuration of your situation) are formed by the center. Clear center: clear mandala.

    I suspect that part of the fear involved is in trying to get a perfect situation, or trying to avoid discomfort or trouble in the future. So I tell you: you can't avoid trouble, and you can't avoid discomfort. Forget that, and act with trust. Your courage will go further than you can go by trying to manipulate the way the situation unfolds. The former has much more energy, and much clearer energy than turning the attention back to check and fiddle with things. Checking is the mind disease shared by meditators everywhere (and humans in general).

    Imagine walking while you check every aspect of your walking: "Is my stride too long or too short? Is my arm swinging the right way? Is my back at the right angle? Where should my head be looking? Oh, now have I lost the right arm-swinging?...." The walking will have no chance of appearing in its correct flow and vitality. It will be awkward and prone to errors and aberrations.

    Although our habitual inclination is to try and control outcomes, that is not the way. You must trust your walking, and you must trust your being's movement. Imagine now walking while your energy moves out in front of you: you are prepared to enjoy and engage in anything that may appear along the way, and your walking takes care of itself; in fact your walking has a vibrant quality to it, attentive and in the flow of life.

    You can't avoid mistakes. Mistakes are necessary. If you refrain from checking your action, you can flow with the moment, then when mistakes appear you can quickly turn them into learning, new opportunity, character development, and unimagined possibility. Upon being asked "What is enlightenment?" one Zen Master replied, "One endless mistake."

    Lightning finds its own path of last resistance. Water flows according to the terrain. Wind moves in harmony with the landscape. If you let go of the checking, manipulating habit, your mind and body will also find their own path; energies will be allowed to move appropriately in their own ways. Your being has this intelligence, if you step out of the way of yourself.

    Originally, your mind is clear. Originally, your mind is trustworthy. Only be sincere in facing life.

    ~

    If you want to talk about specifics, feel free to message me privately. It may help to consciously set an intention, or even make a sincere vow. This is not the same as checking; it projects your energy clearly and helps bring you back when you wander off the path.
