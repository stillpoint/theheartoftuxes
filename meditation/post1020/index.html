    Cold showers for meditation?

    If you are very sensitive to your own physical and energetic responses you can generally feel if your constitution is appropriate for cold showers. Some cold and neutral constitutions are injured by sudden or intense cold; and certainly weak constitutions shouldn't be exposed. So if you are small-framed, thin, exhibit signs of qi deficiency or adrenal exhaustion, or if you react negatively to extremes, this may not be the best way to engender clarity.

    Also be aware that extreme contrasts tend to produce extreme effects. So while brightness may increase, so may jumpiness, anxiety, reactivity, inflammation, blood pressure. These are things to consider. It is not a matter of "is this good or bad?" These matters of practice and matters of health are very rarely that cut and dried. They should be taken within the broad context and seen for their cause-and-effect relationship.

    Another aspect is that some people, especially but not exclusively young men, tend toward a sort of heroic and spectacular ideal. They think if it's special or unusual or arcane, it must be good. If it is strenuous, it must be deep. If it prompts a dramatic change in energy or mood, it must be effective. This kind of thinking is why young men make good soldiers; they are ripe for the kind of gung-ho, self-glorified approach. But that doesn't necessarily have anything to do with depth or effectiveness.

    If you are interested, you can look into traditional waterfall meditation, and you can also consult with qigong teachers, some of whom may be familiar with the effects of cold splashes.     You might also get checked by a Traditional Chinese Medicine doctor, to know whether your constitution is suitable for the practice. And by a western medical doctor, for that matter, to know whether your heart is in good condition to sustain the repeated shock.

    If you want to try cold water but mitigate the negative effects, try splashing cold water on your face and washing your feet with cool (not cold) water the moment you wake up. Then go straight to your sitting meditation. Walking on cool, dewy earth can also be very refreshing and clarifying for your energy body. The caution here would be that cold can be 'absorbed' into the joints of the foot and into the channels leading to your deeper stores of energy, causing joint problems or illness.

    A couple of ways to brighten your energy without the risks associated with extremes are (A) to eat a good, clean diet with less meat and sugar, and only to eat to 70% of your capacity; and (B) to do lots of prostration practice.
