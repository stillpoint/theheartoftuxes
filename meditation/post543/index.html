    In the morning.....

    When you wake up, you are often still partly in the drifting, dreaming state of sleep. So the habitual level of your mind is more prominent than your bright awareness and intelligence. In these cases it is helpful to establish morning routines that take you straight to beneficial, clarifying activity rather than depending on your sketchy decision-making process. You can leave decision-making and concern about mind-states until such a time as you are more awake, present, and self-possessed.

    The clarity of your mind going into sleep, and the quality of your sleep also have a lot to do with how you wake. Some people wake up very clear and alert. You can imagine the kinds of lifestyle supports that are necessary for that to happen in your life — proper diet, healthy physical and mental activities, good sleep schedule, winding down to calm and clarity before sleep (perhaps night-time meditation), and so on….

    According to both ancient and modern health authorities, you should go to sleep by 10:30, and ideally wake up before dawn. Don't linger in bed; don't decide whether to get up or not; just get up. Lingering in the half-dream state allows negative emotions to take over, and in fact they tend to color your experience of the whole day. If you do take some moments in bed after you wake, they should be predetermined: consciously come back into your body, consciously embrace the day — the new chance at life — thankfully, consciously renew vows that are important to you like living clearly and compassionately. Making these structures automatic helps to overcome the shroud of negativity that can appear around waking time.

    If you find it more useful to just jump out of bed, do it decisively and with clear energy. Let your first act of the day be your act, not something half-assed or imposed upon you. Jump out of bed, splash cold water on your face, brush your teeth, and then go straight to your meditation mat or altar.

    Connect with the big picture of life at the start of the day. Many influences will draw you into mundane details throughout the day. So at least at the start of the day, re-establish the high view — what is this day for; what is your life for? You could do worse than vow to awaken and use your day and life to benefit others. This could be the first thing you do with your voice and heart. The big view and big vow will leave no room for negativity.

    Then you can do whatever practice you find useful in the morning. Vigorous, whole-body-and-breath exercise is very healthful and grounding. Simply sitting in open awareness or watching your breath is also a wonderful way to start the day. Chanting or mantra or any of a thousand beneficial practices are possible. A few minutes of consciously offering yourself to your best, highest mind can steer the beginning of your day. Performed every morning, it can steer the beginnings of your days for your whole life.

    Negativity comes from self-absorption. It's the self-clinging habit that brings up all negative emotions and makes them bigger and bigger. One worries, frets, grasps, indulges, and experiences fear, guilt, and shame all based on self-clinging habit. Replace that habit with a positive morning routine.
