    Just made a how to meditate video (4 mins) mantra repetition! what do you think? any tips?

    Since you ask for feedback: In the video, you offer the opinion that mantra is the easiest method for beginners; but you don't know what will be easiest for all beginners. No one method is easiest or best; the different methods exist because people have different minds and constitutions and situations. A different method may be much better for a different person.

    Also, you don't state your authority to teach or the lineage or tradition you're connected to, though you mention 'beginners', implying that you're not a beginner.

    This is not to question your sincerity in sharing the video, nor the helpfulness of the instruction; but more care and transparency is necessary in the presentation. When we consciously or unconsciously make sweeping statements or omit information to pump up the importance of what we're presenting, or to place ourselves in the role of an authority figure, it suggests that we don't really trust the subject to stand on its own merits. There's no need to say this particular practice done in this particular way is best for everyone. It's enough to say that you have found it to be useful.

    You may want to examine your motives for posting an instructional video, and for tweaking the message to make it seem more of a big deal. These efforts are almost inevitably a mix of experience and inexperience, altruism and ego. If we insist that they are completely pure, It's like saying we're speaking from an enlightened place, and that is a huge self-deception.

    This is an issue that comes up a lot among practitioners who have gained some experience and have begun to see some fruit appear in their practice: a confidence appears that is very easily co-opted by ego. It's pretty much a clich√© at meditation centers: the intermediate student who is bold enough to start talking about practice, instructing others, saying what's what. ‚ÄúThey get a bit of Dharma energy and start throwing their weight around."     I'm not saying that you're necessarily in this category. I'm just saying it's a very common issue, and getting beyond it is one of the steps toward mature practice. Along with greater insight and greater Dharma energy, the mature practitioner also develops an impeccable, transcendent humility.

    Once you put yourself out there in a video or other public expression, it can be quite bracing to realize how much is revealed. Thank you for receiving my feedback, and good luck with your practice and helping others in theirs.
