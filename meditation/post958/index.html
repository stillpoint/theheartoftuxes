    I serious obstacle to meditation. Is it also happening to others? Is there an effective practical approach against it?

    The vast majority of people on these forums who express difficulty with depersonalization and feelings of losing touch with reality have been involved in drug use. In fact, I don't remember a case that didn't involve drug use. This is significant, and can tell you a lot about what is required to promote peaceful, satisfactory states of mind.

    You need to normalize your mind. Not in the sense that you're not normal, but like a DJ uses normalization to keep the levels of his mix from getting too loud or too soft. Grounded, balanced activity is required rather than chasing after ecstatic experiences or particular states of mind.

    Simple physical work is good medicine in this case. Yard work, cleaning, gardening -- the classic â€œchop wood, carry water" are the kinds of activities that will help you.

    Also, all kinds of simple mindful activities can help, from washing dishes to stitching clothes.

    During meditation, keep a non-grasping attitude. Don't try to get or make anything, only follow the method. Focus on a simple mind, free of complication.

    You might want to check in with a teacher or experienced practitioner about your method. Is the method a good one, and are you clear about what you are supposed to be doing with your body and mind?

    I suppose that the most direct, complete, and concrete response to your question is that you should place yourself within a structure that will keep you grounded in simple reality. Spend much time with a good teacher, submit to a good practice situation (even residential), and associate with regular, sincere, grounded people. A guided working retreat may help you a lot.

    Do you have opportunities for simple physical work in your life?
