    Why would you want to try it? Why would you not want to try it?

    If you get people telling you about their experience, it may get in the way of you having your own. No need to compare or imagine.

    This kind of visualized exercise engages your power of imagination to harness attention. Some people connect very well physically; some connect through sound; some are analytical in nature; some live through their heart; and some find that imagination is adaptable and helpful. Even if it doesn't happen to be your bent of nature, getting at an issue from different angles can be helpful in itself, and can open you to new pathways of learning and practicing.

    Here, the visualization helps you bring your attention downward and pay attention to your connection with the ground. Most of us live almost completely in our heads. We are not very much in the real world, but in the world of our own thoughts. And as our global society evolves it is becoming more and more 'up in the head' â€” more about data, more about virtual reality, more 'meta'.

    Add to this the sedentary nature of our lives, the rampant disconnection from emotional life, from a sense of place, from ancestry and lineage, and you get a world of humans who are very ungrounded. As a species, we are not in reality; we have difficulty returning to the plain, real moment.

    The downward-focused attention in this exercise helps bring energy out of its stuck place in the head, and helps draw it into the body. It also provides an anchor to a sense of place that is both real (as it's the place where you're standing) and comforting (as you realize you are not drifting about without roots).

    The symbolic meanings may also have an effect on you as you establish 'roots' in the real world, a sense of stability, a channel for nourishment to pass to you, and so on.

    What is the proposed aim of the practice; and who teaches it? The aim is important. Your intent is important. And the context in which a practice is done is important.
