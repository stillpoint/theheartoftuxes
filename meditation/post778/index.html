    Ending an intimate relationship and sangha

    You already know. It's just difficult. You just have to do the hard thing.

    What is most important?

    Your practice and your Sangha are a deeper and more significant relationship than romance. Your relationship to your path of awakening is most important of all. Your ex is also a member of the Sangha, and so even if it didn't work out you still need to offer each other the space to practice, and give kind support when it's needed.

    Don't make stories in your head about her. You don't know her situation, you only know your own. No need to guess at what she needs. Give her space; and if in the course of things you find that you sincerely have no clue, you can ask her "what do you need from me?" Otherwise, just be regular; and that includes feeling whatever you're feeling, without going into stories.

    If you avoid the difficulty of practicing together, what does that get you in terms of your future relationships? What does that get you in terms of your practice? Not only is this a step toward maturity and self-mastery, but your Sangha and place of practice are the ideal situation for digesting this experience. Is there a better place for you to overcome suffering patterns, or to practice appropriate love?

    Don't let emotional discomfort be the boss of you, whatever form it takes. There may be sorrow, anger, resentment, pride, shame, exhaustion, grief. There may also be seemingly baseless moments of relief, joy, openness, unconditional love. Fine! Never mind! Only continue.

    So I already recommended not to make a story about your ex, and to let her have and take care of her own reality. Also, don't make a story about yourself. Being fixated on finding 'the compassionate action' may be a way of avoiding your feelings or awareness of your responsibility (seeing clearly how you got into this mess). Don't make yourself into the victim, the martyr, the rescuer, the good student, the expert, the loser, the winner, the weak one, the strong one; don't make these ego stories. And of course you (we) will make these stories; so when they arise, drop them quickly. Practice doesn't mean you don't have difficulty; it means you soon drop your grasping.

    For a time, you may need to sit in this sort of incapable, unknowable space. That's fine; that's perfect. Only keep going. Shunryu Suzuki Roshi said "Enlightenment is one continuous mistake." Only keep going.

    As to compassion: Compassion is openhandedness. There is no agenda, no advance strategy. If there is a strategy or if there is an image you're trying to live up to, that's not real compassion; it's play-acting at compassion. Compassion means you stand in Unknowing — present, open, ready, and without anything to grasp. From there comes appropriate action; but you won't know what it will be ahead of time, your bodymind will know it in the moment.

    Look at images of Kuan Yin in the posture of Royal Ease: the posture is so calm and relaxed, but one foot is on the ground, indicating a readiness to move forward toward anyone in need. If a need appears, one responds. Before a need appears, one is just relaxed, open, ready. That is the embodiment of your great compassionate nature.

    If you keep practicing, sit in Unknowing, and offer openhandedness to your ex, that is compassionate action. Thank you for your compassionate practice.
