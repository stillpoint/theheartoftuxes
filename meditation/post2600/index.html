    Anxiety caused by meditation?

    >...I was hoping to use meditation as a tool to curb those feelings.

    This is the crux of it. If you set up an internal fight, you will get violent results. So meditation is not used to hammer down some part of ourselves. It is more like letting the steering wheel of the car slide through your hands as the car finds its own straight line: you let go and let all things return to themselves without your interference. This includes all of your thoughts and moods and patterns. They may linger for a time or they may disappear or they may come on strong; your only job is to maintain your disengagement.

    So your relationship to your thoughts and feelings should be relaxed. No drama, just open hands. "Everything is no problem." Then repeat that and eventually make that continuous.

    >...a few positive experiences prompted me to do it semi regularly.

    This is also problematic. Although it is natural to seek positive experiences, no type of experience should determine whether or not you return to balance through meditation. If you only meditate when things are positive you will lose determination very easily. And furthermore you will not be meditating (and returning to balance) when you need it most â€” when you're dealing with negativity.

    I would suggest having an experienced student or teacher check your method of meditation. Some methods are not good for people who tend to have rapid or very active thinking; and there are also some tools to help your mind settle so that you can sit more comfortably.

    Sometimes, underlying anxiety will be revealed (not caused) by meditation. But in your case, I think it is a matter of being too influenced by thinking and then trying to drive away the thinking. A different approach will help you. (Let me know if you would like some suggestions from me. Private conversation is possible.)
