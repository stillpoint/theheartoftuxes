    Garbage In: Garbage Out. Stop putting creepy stuff into your mind, and eventually after you've done your mind-cleaning through meditation it will stop coming out.

    All thoughts are just thoughts, the same way all dreams are just dreams. It's all mental formation, made of the same substance. The reason it is so bad for you is because of your attachment to it. You give certain thoughts more power over you, more credence and apparent reality and importance. But they are just thoughts made of dream-material.

    To diminish the negative effect you need to diminish the attachment. That means you need to practice. Each time, let go and return to your method of meditation. Let go and return. Let go and return.

    The more you fret and react, the more mental garbage you make.

    If it persists and disturbs you, see someone who can help you look into the reason for the pattern â€” likely an ongoing wound from some loss or abandonment early in your life.

    If it persists in meditation, it means either that you have not learned meditation properly, or that you are not applying it meticulously, or that you ought to stop meditating and find a healthier environment for your mind.

    The ego will 'troll' you. The ego doesn't want you to meditate, because when you meditate you let go of self-obsession and start to get clear. The ego doesn't want clarity; it just wants to exist and have its existence definite. So whenever you start to relax and let go and just be, the ego will tend to send you stuff to engage with. Rather than just being, you become a doer; you regain an identity as the 'good meditator' or 'bad meditator' or 'hero' or 'victim' or 'tormented one', and so on.

    Some people love philosophical concepts, so the ego will send conceptual thoughts to get them to disengage from meditation. It is very clever in the way it hooks you in. But maybe you are able to let go of philosophies and just return to meditation; so the ego will 'troll' like a fisherman trolls the waters, putting out new bait. For you, it's a particular kind of fear that gets you reacting and engaging and doing. For someone else, maybe it will be holy visions and the pride at having them; or just random and trivial thoughts that eventually annoy by their unimportance. Whatever gets your mind to move.

    So your protection is in immediately and gently returning to your method. You may be taken away for some seconds or minutes; but whenever you notice, just lightly and immediately return to the method. Make nothing of it. Don't give power to any distraction; only keep returning.

    That means it is very important to know your method and have faith in it, so that it is very clear what you are returning to. If you are not clear on the why and how of the method, returning will be vague and messy, and then distractions will catch you and possess you.

    Finally, learn the truth of impermanence and let it become very clear and real for you. All things that are born will die. When the causes for their formation end, the formation dissipates. So in the life of all beings there is sickness, old age, and death. We are not meant to be overwhelmed by fear of loss. We are meant to live completely. To have such a wide and strong and loving heart that we can embrace all of it â€” the having and the losing â€” without turning away.

    It is because we all go away that meditation is important; so that you can be clear and fearless and alive with your loved ones and for your loved ones; and so you can help them happily embrace the whole cycle of life. Thich Nhat Hanh asks us how we will be a force of peace for others if we don't have peace ourselves. So your meditation isn't an avoidance of the possibility of loss; it is a peaceful embrace of all gain and loss.

    In Buddhism there is the teaching of the Worldly Winds. These are the categories of things that control us the most. If we have stable mind, we can be content and we can act freely; but we are not happy and we are not free, because the Worldly Winds move our minds.

    The Eight Worldly Winds are:
    Loss and Gain
    Pleasure and Pain
    Fame and Shame
    Praise and Blame

    If you do correct meditation and correct practice of mindfulness and equanimity in life, you can become more stable and less moved by the Worldly Winds (in this case, by the Worldly Wind of loss).
