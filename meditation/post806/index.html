    People sometimes fidget and check and tinker and dabble with practice as if they're five-year-olds making 'magic potions' in the bathroom sink. Don't try to invent and manipulate your own practice. If you do, ego is always in the driver's seat.

    The manipulating habit emerges strongly in various stages of practice. In the beginning stage, it has to do with the dream of gaining power over life. In intermediate stages of practice, it often arises because methods have become familiar, and the monkey-mind looks for something new to fiddle with, some new buttons to press. In more advanced stages it may re-emerge as arrogance over mastery, or as resistance to final letting-go.

    The hallmark of practice is surrender — letting go of the manipulating mindset. That is why we commit to a form. And that is why the form must be correct — it must address the right aim and motivation in life.

    Therefore, find a correct traditional course of training, taught by a legitimate and confirmed teacher. Learn it well. Apply it meticulously. Don't waver and fidget and manipulate.

    The discomfort we feel in surrendering to a set form is the resistance of ego, the resistance of the controlling mind habit. That is why uncomfortable or difficult is not necessarily wrong, and in any case necessary for one's spiritual unfolding. That which serves and enamors the spirit is the same thing that pisses off the ego.

    Don't come here for practice instruction. Who are we? We're not your teacher. We didn't give you your training path. We haven't been with you as you unfold it. We don't know your attainment or capacity. Go to a proper teacher, and get face-to-face, living teaching.

    The bottom line here in what you write is this: why do it? What is your practice for? What is the aim and direction?

    When life direction is clear, your practice choices are clear. When direction is unclear, your practice choices are confused and for the most part ego-driven, playing out as speculation, grasping, avoiding, dithering, and self-confusing.

    So, what is the overall aim? That is the first point to clarify. Adjusting the method may or may not be necessary, but it comes later, after this first point is made clear.
