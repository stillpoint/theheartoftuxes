    TheHeartofTuxes

    I list other options besides the most common sitting meditations in this recent post and the ones following it.

    Besides "what practice to do" there's a quite different but also influential issue of finding your supportive conditions. These are things that help to stabilize the heart and settle the mind, regardless of the chosen meditation method. In a very general sense, one brings the quality of ground to one's life: consistency, regularity, balance, eveneness. In Ayurveda it is the energy of sattva. In the five elements it is the energy of earth.

    A menu of grounding influences:

    • Focus on momentary enjoyment rather than getting to an end goal.

    • Eat in a regular schedule.

    • Eat frequently.

    • Eat a balanced diet, with grounding foods (whole foods rather than processed; limit sugar and refined carbohydrates; limit strong spice).

    • Regulate sleep and rest. Go to bed at a reasonable and consistent hour.

    • Balance activity and sedentary time. Be active enough each day to break a sweat. Exercise well within physical limits, again focusing on momentary enjoyment rather than pushing.

    • Increase physical involvement; decrease mental involvement.

    • Visit natural settings.

    • Be around grounded people.

    • Avoid gossip and trivia.

    • Clean and organize your living and working spaces.

    • Keep the body and clothing clean.

    • Dress comfortably, and in a dignified way.

    • Massage the body or at least the feet with grounding oil (e.g., hemp or sesame).

    • Do gentle, pleasurable movement throughout the day.

    • Limit time spent in contact with 'thinking machines' -- phone, computer, TV, etc. -- or avoid it altogether.

    • Do generous, compassionate actions. Humility and good acts naturally settle the mind.

    • Avoid negative behavior. Even if you do good action some of the time, if you participate in unethical or negative behavior your mind cannot find balance, regardless of the meditations you try.

    • Intentionally and consistently listen to the messages of the body, from taking the time to feel emotions, to peeing when it's time to pee.

    • Intentionally receive and steep in the positive. Pick out the positive aspects of experience and deliberately infuse yourself with appreciation and feelings of safety, contentment, and other grounded qualities.

    • Move and engage with tasks gently, not harshly. Exert yourself only up to about 70% of your capacity, then rest well.

    Physical meditation and meditative relaxation practices are very grounding. Prostrations, walking meditation, Restorative Yoga, and Tai Ji are examples of grounding practices. Gentle repetitive drumming or rattle-shaking, as in some shamanic traditions, is also grounding.
