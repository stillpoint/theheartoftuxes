    Good realization! You're very fortunate.

    There's a story about a monk who was very sincere and generous and wise. This monk was very careful to follow correct rules of conduct, and he always carried himself with dignity. Soon he started to become noticed by the people in his congregation, and he became more and more popular, giving brilliant speeches that were attended by many people.

    Around this time, the monk started to feel an itch on his thigh, and eventually a bump appeared, and then a sort of boil, which grew and grew. This became not only uncomfortable but inconvenient. Eventually, the monk had doctors look at the boil and lance it or treat it with plasters, but it kept returning. In fact, it started to look like a nose.

    The monk, who had previously been mostly concerned with the well-being of his congregation started to be concerned about this strange growth. Although it was very painful to do so, he would try to cut or burn off the nose-looking bump. But each time, it would only grow more. Soon brow, lips and cheekbones appeared, and eventually an entire face â€” there on his leg.

    From the moment the face was fully formed, its eyes opened and it began to swear in the most vile language, cursing the monk with vicious anger. Well, of course this meant no more public talks. The best the monk could do would be to try covering up the face enough to muffle its rants. More doctors were brought in, and other experts, but no one could help.

    Finally, exasperated, the monk himself yelled at the face, "What do you want from me!!?" And then the face started to talk to him. The monk learned of his own past life as a warrior, and the face's past life as the warrior's enemy. The warrior had stalked and killed his enemy, and then repented (which is why his mind was directed toward becoming a monk in the next life) but so much hatred had grown between them that the enemy swore to find him in the next life and make him pay.

    The monk had always been sincere and humble, only trying to benefit others; so the spirit of his enemy could never find him. But when he started giving talks and getting praise, a little seed appeared in his mind: "Oh, maybe I'm a great monk." In that moment, he became visible to his enemy, and the face appeared on his leg.

    So a couple of take-aways from this story: First, if we try to hide our faults and problems they only grow more nasty. We should talk to our demons and ask them what they need.

    Second, slight pride can defeat even sublime practice. We can undo our sincere effort if we start to seek our own glory or good situation. All merit and blessings should be given over to the benefit of all beings.

    Utter openhandedness is necessary, every time, moment to moment.
