    After one year of meditation, I think I'm starting to lose the grip.

    I'm very happy to meet you, to converse, and to help each other to wake up.

    Is it okay to continue? Yes, it's okay. Everything's okay.

    Eyes open or eyes closed, both are okay. We just look at the cause and effect relationship: a particular form has a particular influence.

    You should take care with language. To say "it was impossible" is your own story, not the truth. For example, you can keep your eyes open when talking with me, no problem. And you can be present and aware while doing it, no problem. But you make the thinking "I can't", and the story that keeping eyes open is too hard, and that is why you can't.

    What it means is that when you open your eyes a new kind of letting go is required, which you're not used to.

    Also, phrases like "I'm not smart enough" or thoughts that you are somehow small, insignificant, or flawed are untrue and unhelpful. You are a great being with a boundless capacity to love and a marvelous clear mind that can respond to any situation. And your sincerity has brought you here to this discussion. Believe in your great self.

    You have gotten used to keeping eyes closed, so you have developed an attachment to the dark, private, comfortable feeling you get. There's nothing wrong with that state; it's neither good nor bad. But if you are strongly attached to it, you are not free.

    If you can't be present with your eyes open, then how will you save yourself in life -- how will you bring comfort, ease, peace, clarity, stability into your day to day activities if you can only achieve them with closed eyes? So the commitment is not to seeking out your own comfortable situation; the commitment is to being able to function clearly, freely, and compassionately in your daily life, whether the situation is your favorite or not.

    That is the big difference that appears with respect to what we call aim, intent, or direction. If you only try to get your favorite situation, you can't be free. You will shut yourself away, try to avoid whatever could upset the carefully arranged situation, fear movement, resist the unknown. That is not life.

    For sure, you can and should continue using what helps you. If doing closed eyes meditation helps you to relax and enhances your life, why not continue? If it helps you come into contact with a peace that you can then use and offer to others in your interactions, wonderful. But if it is part of an attachment that only promises to get deeper, then you have to face some difficulty in order to be free.

    Fortunately or unfortunately, when one's mind starts to turn to awakening it is no longer possible to hide. I mean, we can try to hide for a while, but soon we find that we can't ignore the things we used to ignore. This can be difficult, even painful, I'm sorry. But I'm also happy that you are on the path. I hope you find much peace and take care of yourself well as you work things out. I, and the Sangha of seekers, am here for you.

    ~

    By the way, I'm also a dancer. Movement and meditation are fantastic companions that help shine on each other's shadows and balance each other's missing parts.
