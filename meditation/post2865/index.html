    Is it still effective to meditate sitting on a chair versus sitting on the floor?

    I think people are making a mistake in how they read my comment. Using a chair can be absolutely fine for meditation, and sometimes the very best option. I myself use a chair for at least half of the sittings during long retreats, and I often use a chair and desk for certain practices. I use it because it allows me to focus on the task, and without harming my body.

    Ultimately, practice means the practice of the mind. So liberation can be achieved in any posture. But that doesn't mean we aren't strongly influenced by the ways we use our bodies, the ways we relate to our environment and each other, the attitudes that we hold about method and schedule and other aspects of practice form. Why are posture and points of form taught at all? If the beginner doesn't understand why, he should find out rather than assuming it's a non-issue.

    What I was responding to with my original comment was not whether using a chair is okay, it was the notion that 'there's no right or wrong', or that 'whatever you feel like is right'. If we look with wisdom, we can find some truth within those sentiments, but they are very poorly worded, misleading statements. To say that there's no right or wrong way is to say that there's no purpose to the methods offered to us by enlightened beings.

    This is simple arrogance. It's delusion. Unfortunately, many beginners have this kind of arrogant delusion that they know better than the great masters do when it comes to the cause and effect of form.

    The view that posture makes no difference can only be held by someone very insensitive to the relationship between body and mind. The view that method makes no difference can only be held by someone ignorant about the causal relationship between method and liberation.

    Immature practitioners may cheer on glib, cavalier statements about practice. For instance, adolescent boys tend to love the iconoclast, the one who spits in the face of authority or puts on an air of disinterest. "Yeah, whatever." "Yeah, we'll that's just, like, your opinion, man." They cheer on indulgent, permissive expressions because it makes them feel better about their own doubts. It has nothing to do with awareness of cause and effect.

    Ultimately, yes, how you keep your mind is the significant point. But how well do you keep your mind without clear form, without the support of environment and lifestyle and correct relationship, without physical health and mindfulness of the body?

    Maybe a great achieved master can do without form, but the folks visiting these forums: no, not at all; correct form is very, very necessary in order to gain control of the spiritual process.

    The proof is in the pudding: the ones who fudge and compromise and go according to preference aren't the ones who actually progress and transform. This is because their ego habit is being constantly reinforced; they are proceeding based on their old narrow views and preferences.
