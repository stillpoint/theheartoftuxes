    Tips on overcoming this?

    You are talking about feeling or knowing that you're 'in the moment'. That's something altogether different from actually being in the moment.

    This is called 'checking', and it's the number one stumbling block for meditators: they don't only do the meditation, but they try to step outside of it to see how they're doing. This is not the practice, and must be abandoned. Even though we all hope for something to hold onto, some badge to wear or some mark in red pencil to confirm our achievement, this is not meditation. When you're checking, you're not in the moment.

    Checking includes looking for signs progress, labeling your session a success or failure, thinking about your physical or mental condition, trying to control what's happening with your breath, trying to analyze or label your state of mind or feeling, and all other examples of stepping out of your actual activity so you can look at it or try to control it. Drop all that. Only do it. Only do what you're doing.

    "But how can I know that I'm doing it?" That's the question that so many meditators ask. Well, you don't. That's not part of the deal. You only do what you're doing. When you are meditating, just meditate. When you are working, just work. When you are eating lunch, only eat lunch. When you are on a conference call, only take care of the conference call. Only do it. The practice comes from your center, from your activity; that is all.

    It's like when you learn to ski, and at some point after taking in all the instruction and getting your body all arranged you start to get self-fascinated and try to picture yourself and check how you look and feel. At that moment, you are no longer paying attention to the skiing, and that typically is an occasion when people wipe out. Let go of checking, and just ski. Just enjoy going down the hill and playing with the contours of the terrain.

    When you only do it, your mind and the situation are one. Resist the pressing urge to figure out what 'being one' feels like. Feeling has nothing to do with it. Feeling comes and goes; sometimes up, sometimes down, but always changing, changing. Whatever the feeling is doesn't matter.

    Also, don't check whether you are 'in the moment' all the time or not. Only: when you notice you've departed, return. No mess, no drama, no checking. Just return to doing what you're doing. This is how robins make their nests, how rain knows to fall downward, and how spring unfolds through you and everything around you. Only do it.
