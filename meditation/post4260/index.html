    Can someone confirm if this is meditation?

    Wonderful! That's great that you have that motivation.

    But again, don't get the order of things mixed up. Don't imagine another state or situation to be achieved, and then try to achieve it. You will only be following your own idea, your own illusion. So don't get fixated on a certain state of mind, or a particular feeling, or a particular experience. Only return to the present real moment -- this is where your true life is, the only place where your true life is. Anything that departs from this moment is a flight of fancy.

    So can you see that thinking of some other state or future experience takes you away from the present reality?

    It is only your idea that is illusory. What you really are, already, is complete and valid and pure and free and true. No need to look anywhere else. The looking elsewhere actually denies what you already have.

    So this moment is already complete. This moment is already it. Only your belief that there must be something else keeps you from your own completion.

    Therefore, it's not dependent on feeling. You don't need to feel any particular way; so you can just forget about trying to feel something. Where I live, today it's sunny. Yesterday it was cloudy. The day before that, it rained. Each situation is truth. If I think only sunny is correct, then I will have a problem in life.

    Likewise, sometimes I like my situation and sometimes I don't; and sometimes I don't care. Some experiences are pleasant; some are unpleasant; and some are neutral. That is life. Each situation is truth. But if I think that only pleasant situations are correct, or if I assume that truth must be delirious and mind-blowing, I have a big problem. No, each moment as-it-is is already truth. Therefore meditation and the whole path of practice is open-handed: it doesn't make something based on a limited idea, but rather allows truth to be as-is.

    This is a much more mature and much more human path than many doctrinal or religious paths. It includes everything. Your good mood and your grouchy mood are both truth. The person you like and the person you don't like are both worthy of compassion. Clarity is just clarity and not-knowing is just not-knowing, and both have their right time and situation.

    >Because right now I know that's not who I am but I can't seem to feel that way, I still feel like I'm a separate fixed ego in a universe that is not me.

    So first, never mind about feeling something in particular, and don't chase something other than what you are experiencing in the moment. Practice doesn't depend on feeling -- at all! Okay?

    Next, take care about substituting the idea of feeling for thinking. It's not that you feel separate, it's that you think of yourself as separate. If you were to actually feel what you're feeling, then immediately separateness would disappear. In fact, this is why people habitually avoid deep feeling: because it very soon becomes an experience of boundlessness, ungraspability, interconnection, and endless change. In a word, it becomes reality, which contradicts the false sense of self we try to prop up.

    And thirdly, of course you still think of yourself as a separate self. You have participated in that kind of thinking all your life (and according to many, through countless previous lives), and that pattern of thinking is ingrained and supported by almost everyone around you and by the cultural and physical structures made by humans. So yes, you will have that kind of thinking for some time yet. But you are very rare and fortunate in becoming aware of it, and being willing to let it go.

    I need to tell you again and again, and you need to seek out the teaching that tells you again and again:

    • Don't check your situation.
    • Don't think it needs to be something other than it is.
    • Don't entertain the belief that you're missing anything in this moment.
    • Don't compare your current state to anything you read, imagine, or have experienced in the past.

    Be open-handed about the present moment, and then truth will present itself automatically. If you then avoid checking, comparing, judging, and labeling it, truth can function on its own.

    In short, your job is as-is. Moment to moment, just like this.
