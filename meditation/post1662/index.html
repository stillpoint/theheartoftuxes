    Question for people who practice

    Each path has instruction that makes sense within its own system. You could get different instructions for different reasons, and they could all be correct within their own tradition. You could also get the same instructions with completely different purpose and meaning.

    So mixing methods and traditions is not a great idea. Taking some from column A and some from column B is not the way to study and learn correct practice. Picking and choosing is the habit of the mind of ignorance; to get beyond that mind you need to surrender to a method and course of training that is proven and trustworthy. So you also need to know enough about it to try it out and reasonably trust it; don't just go on blind faith.

    If you eventually want to taste a few different paths it would be better to try one at a time, and give the teaching and practice enough time to sink in or steep. You steep in it like tea steeping in the pot: you have to be with it long enough for the flavor to develop.

    Breathing-based style is one way (or rather a group of ways). There are many other styles possible. So focusing on breathing is not the number one rule. You are right, though, that an object of concentration (like the breath) can be something that you return to when thoughts take you away. You let thoughts go and let thoughts be, and keep returning to the object.

    Your question about breathing is not really asking for a bunch of various reports from various people within various practices. Many people here don't even understand correct meditation. Your question is really about how you should meditate. This doesn't come by consensus! Even if ten people agreed on an instruction, it could still be wrong; or it could be wrong for you.

    There are various ways to practice with the breath. Yes, it is possible to use the inner voice with a sort of mantra or repetition. In some cases it would involve counting; in other cases a repeated phrase or the syllables of an actual mantra; and in yet other cases the breath may harmonize with a process of inquiry. There are many ways.

    Before looking into ways and styles, the key point is why. What are you meditating for? Everything unfolds from that point.

    If you seek clarity, the question remains: why seek clarity? What will you use it for? Some people want relief from stress; some want deeper peace. Others may be inspired to awaken from the dream of life. Then there is the possibility of embodying the awakened path from moment to moment, which is not so much a seeking as an unfolding. The highest motivation is to offer yourself to the awakening and well-being of all other beings.

    You need more teaching about clarity. You also need to digest the teaching in correct practice. This requires both good instruction and your own effort and time. It's not something that you will attain as soon as it is explained (not unless you're an exceptional being on the verge of enlightenment). It's a process. It takes time.

    Clarity doesn't depend on feeling. Any feeling can appear, remain for a while, and disappear. It all happens within clarity. So clarity is beyond all thought and feeling, beyond all experience. When you are completely openhanded with the moment, neither adding anything to it nor subtracting your awareness, that can be called 'clarity'. Using words is possible; and no words is also possible.

    So watching the breath sometimes means feel the bodily sensation at a certain point; it sometimes means experience and follow the flow of the breath itself, from outside the body down into the belly and back out; it sometimes means be aware of processes set in motion by the breath or be aware of the effect of breath on the mind; it sometimes means count the breathing to yourself, or aloud; it sometimes means use a phrase together with the breath; it sometimes means find the mind that is aware of the breath; and it sometimes means direct the breath internally (which is a yogic method requiring close instruction). If you get clear on your motivation, you will be better prepared to know what kind of method to use.
