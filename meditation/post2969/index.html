    Visualization while meditating?

    > Does anyone else...

    No phrase is as often repeated as this one in the Meditation subreddit. But this phrase grasps at straws. What good would it do to hear a thousand various opinions or mistakes? How will you know the right way?

    What the question really means is that you are uncertain and looking for confirmation. Confirmation will not come from people agreeing arbitrarily on a certain idea or method; it will come from correct teaching, from seeing the actual cause-and-effect relationship of proper meditation.

    You want to know whether what you do is meditation. If it is officially recognized as meditation, then what?

    The correct aim and motivation have to be clarified.

    If you've stumbled upon something that relaxes you, that's probably fine. If it doesn't hurt you or cause you to withdraw from life, and if it doesn't hurt someone else, it should be fine to continue if you want to continue. It is a personal relaxation.

    It's not the only thing that would relax you; it's just what you happened to come up with.

    But this is not meditation. Meditation doesn't try to push away certain feelings. Meditation doesn't judge certain states as unworthy of attention. You let go of stress in meditation, but you let it go so that you can be calmly present with all real things â€” present with thoughts, present with feelings, present in relationship with other people, present with all kinds of situations and experiences.

    So meditation is not about trying to find an ideal situation for yourself. It's about developing the courage and fortitude to be with life as it is.

    There is a proper motivation for meditation, and there is a proper form to follow. The form lights up the places where where you cling, avoid, or ignore, so you can bring yourself back to awareness and equanimity. Therefore, correct meditation has an overall compassionate direction: you show up for life openhandedly and openheartedly.

    If you like visualization, there are indeed methods that involve visualization; but correct meditation isn't something you invent based on your whim. If you do, you only make your whim stronger, and your whim then controls you. To proceed this way is to build your own ignorance into the structure being constructed: every part of it will have your ignorance built into it. Meditation shines a light on your ignorance so that you can overcome it. That is why you offer yourself to the method rather than trying to figure out your own comfortable way.
