    How do you meditate away the fear of not being able to love?

    If you want to be able to offer love, you have to have it in yourself. That means loving yourself as you are, including your abilities and your shortcomings, the qualities hidden or not yet fully expressed, and the qualities that actually are there which you may have a hard time seeing in yourself. If you want to love others, you have to cultivate this love in yourself -- as-you-are, with aspects seen or unseen.

    You are following up on this forum, asking advice, looking into the issue. This is already an expression of love, is it not?

    But meditation is not for suppressing any part of your mind. It is not that you "meditate away fear" but that through meditation you begin to see more clearly the reality of things, and so the causes of fear are removed and fear dissipates.

    Being with yourself, as-is, is love. It is like a blank page that is ready to accept anything written on it. The page doesn't pick and choose; it doesn't say that certain feelings must be there and others must not. So a blank page is like true love. Likewise, when you are present with someone, offering undivided attention without requiring them to be anything in particular, that is love for the other. When this love responds freely, it becomes compassion.

    So in meditation, your mind should be like a blank page: "feel free to write anything on me -- everything is okay". If you are doing breath counting, then just keep returning to the counting, neither grasping nor rejecting any states that arise. Don't try and make love happen, don't try to avoid fear; just keep returning to the method.

    Returning to open presence is the practice of love.

    Love is not the same thing as kind feelings. Love is very pure and open. And the representations of love in popular media -- the yearning, clinging, suffering drama in movies and songs -- has nothing to do with real love. That is more properly called "attachment". Take care that you don't practice attachment; let go of wanting and manipulating.

    If you are in need of emotional healing, there are many specific meditations and therapies that can help. The first thing, though, is always starting with the blank sheet: put it all down and be present with what is, as it is.

    As to emotional healing, Metta and Tonglen meditations can be helpful. Also, chanting is very healing to the emotions, as you express yourself through the voice. If you feel that love is hidden because of some emotional wound you have suffered, it may be helpful to have a guide who can lead you back to yourself: a good therapist or counsellor, or in some cases a meditation teacher.
