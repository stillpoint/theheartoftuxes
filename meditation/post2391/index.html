    Does mindfulness destroy spontaneous behavior?

    Focus more on action. The problem of attaching to thinking is itself a phantom problem made by thinking. Most of the time no big decision has to be made, and much of the rest of the time it doesn't matter so much what you decide.

    Keep your mind simple. Only do what you're doing in the moment. Other future things will be taken care of as they appear. Don't buy into the illusion that you have to arrange everything. That's still a kind of arrogance at work, another manifestation of the mind's habitual busy churning and manipulating. One of the greatest skills you can develop is the skill of letting things okay out by themselves, letting the 'greater power' do it.

    So moment by moment, only do it. Participate, rather than trying to hold yourself or the situation in a certain configuration. Do action-style practice rather than thinking-style practice. Then gradually you will learn to trust your activity and the activity of the world. You have to let go before you can see that it's okay to let go.

    So keep your relationship with the moment very, very simple. Only do what you're doing. No need to check whether your awareness is at a particular level or whether the right thoughts are appearing.

    Training with a teacher and in a traditional practice setting can give you a lot of good practice at just doing it. Many an enlightened master built the foundation of his/her practice upon plain work like cleaning toilets, cooking meals, tending the garden, or going to daily employment.
