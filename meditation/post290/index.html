    Meditation affecting conversations? Is there a thing as too much meditation?

    Was there a need to say something?

    When you become more clear, maybe you see that idle chatter is a waste. Your silence can be a profound, even life-changing, help to others.

    But if you then think that you should remain silent, that's already missing the mark. Only follow the situation. Talk when necessary; don't talk when not necessary; protect silence when protecting silence is necessary. Correct meditation means not holding any opinion, not holding "my way".

    One time, having just finished a week-long retreat I sat down next to someone at lunch. There was a palpable feeling of silence and clarity when I sat down by him. I started up a conversation, and he responded. That's when I found out that he had just come out of a three month retreat. We then continued to eat silently together, and it was wonderful.

    I had finished a week of retreat, so I was already very silent and clear; but I soon learned that much more was possible. For his part, my new friend did not begrudge some words and friendly contact â€” he was willing to engage in conversation, even if it was obviously not his preference. He was showing up for my sake. I felt the kindness of his willingness; it touched me deeply.

    You can similarly touch people with your silence, but also with your willingness to show up according to the situation.

    As to whether there is such a thing as too much meditation: when meditation becomes an escape or part of an agenda to control the mind, already it is too much. Even a minute of this is already "too much".

    Furthermore, meditation is only one part of life. The aim is to prepare you for awakening, for correct relationship, for appropriate action in the world. Sometimes it will be time to devote yourself completely to meditation. At meditation time, only do meditation. This may be for half an hour, or for many hours, or for three years. After meditation time, come back and use your clear mind to help all beings. Only follow the situation that arises around you and through you.
