    Do I need to follow a pattern of meditation?

    Being able to relax and open your mind is already a form. Wanting a result and not wanting to read or follow are also forms. It's very difficult to escape form.

    You can be just as effective meditating freely on your own, if it is correct meditation; that is, if it is truly open, present, non-seeking, and non-avoiding. Some people naturally have this aspiration or motivation — they can hardly stand the prospect of not being clear about life, and so without even thinking about it they let go of opinions and allow their mind to rest.

    But our mind habits are deep and thick and tricky. Without a clear form to show us when we're veering off the road we can easily get sidetracked. The biggest problem is trying to arrange your mind according to "I want". If your meditation is free of "I want this/I don't want that", then I'd say no problem — go ahead and do your thing. But already in your comment you have both "I want" and "I don't want"; so for sure your meditation will not actually be relaxed and open, but moving toward some things and away from others.

    Meditation means your mind doesn't move toward or away from anything.

    Consider the impetus of the self-image to preserve itself and the status quo of its situation. Of course the self-image will hate to let go of that control. Of course the self-image will fear its own death through relaxation and openness. This is where the discomfort with following a particular form arises: the self is uncomfortable with openness, and the self is uncomfortable discipline.

    But what the self-image fears and hates, your true being loves. Your true nature does have the impeccable discipline of complete openness, and it loves your efforts to that end.

    Zen Master Dogen said that deep practice will always feel like something is being imposed on you. Why? Because deep practice is always shining light on your illusions, your laziness, your stuck patterns, your indulgence.

    You can avoid it if you treasure your closed mind too much, or you can go through hell and come out the other side.
