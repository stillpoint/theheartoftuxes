    In the separate issue of meditation:

    • Are you clear on your purpose?

    • Have you sought out a proven traditional method that can achieve that purpose?

    • Have you learned it from a single, capable, affirmed source?

    • Have you learned it well, deeply? (Do you understand both the method and reason for the method?)

    • Do you apply it perfectly and consistently, without deviating?

    These conditions have to be met. The method is there to relieve you of your figuring, dithering, grasping, avoiding, holding, checking, making, wanting, anxious mind. In terms of meditation alone (besides the organic or psychological issues you may be facing), you have to put down your own manipulation, your own habit of trying to figure and hedge and get what you want, and just return to the method. Only do the method. Only do the method. Only do the method.

    > "The Great Way is not difficult for those who do not pick and choose."

    The method is there to relieve you of your picking and choosing mind, which is the mechanism of anxiety (though other physical, mental, and environmental causes and conditions may be in play).
