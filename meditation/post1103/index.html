    TheHeartofTuxes

    It is not supposed to feel like anything in particular. It is being with reality; and reality has no particular flavor.

    Events that come and go have all different kinds of feelings; but the true mind that perceives them is clear and open like space -- no flavor, no color.

    So if you seek a particular feeling, that means you are attaching and expecting and making, not meditating. Meditating means: "don't touch."

    Don't touch your experience; don't try to manipulate your situation. Pay attention, and let it be.

    >A student once asked his teacher, a brilliant Zen nun, how she dealt with difficulties and conflicts.

    >She replied, "I watch."

    This is correct, pure practice. But people have very deep, automatic attaching habit. You always want something to do, some big remote control with which to keep busy adjusting things.

    You know how when you were a kid you wanted to press all the elevator buttons? Well, this habit only becomes deeper and more complex as you age. You try to adjust your feeling; you try to manipulate what other people think of you; you try to grasp what you like and avoid or destroy what you don't like.

    A world of billions of people with this same manipulating habit is chaos. We do all kinds of bad action to each other and to our world with this habit. It all comes from misunderstanding who we really are, and trying to serve this false, misunderstood 'self'.

    Meditation goes in the other direction: don't touch.

    ~

    Don't try to invent your own practice. Learn properly.

    Don't make assumptions about what meditation is. The assumptions will be based on your current ignorance, so it will be difficult or impossible to move beyond your current limitations. Learn a correct, proven method from a living teacher in a traditional course of training.

    Even at the best of times, most people are not capable of going straight to pure attentiveness. These days, ungroundedness, distraction, and anxiety are epidemic; so it is even less likely that people will be able to just practice letting-be. Therefore, methods, teachings, supportive environments and group settings, and other aids are provided. But if you don't access them, don't value them, or don't use them consistently these supports can't help you.
