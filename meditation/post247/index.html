    Sitting meditation is not recommended for you. It can bring you too close to your agitated, chaotic mind; and you use it to improperly suppress emotion (which of course will cause you to boil over, like a pot that has the lid pushed down on it).

    Physical practice is recommended, and is often very beneficial. It doesn't bring you too close to your thinking, but it involves you fully so that your thinking can settle. Furthermore, physical practice is very, very helpful for bringing agitated energy down out of the head and back to its centered home, low in the body.

    Don't chase trance. Trying to manipulate your mind and make it do tricks puts pressure on it, which then prompts a competing rebellion, because the mind wants to be free. Let the mind be. Don't touch.

    Emphasize settling, relaxing downward into the earth. When you get caught up in how your mind is working, and you judge it, and you try to control it, all your activity is up high. You become disconnected, drawn into an illusory world. Trying to battle illusion with illusion is a losing prospect. Let it go and settle. The body is extremely helpful for this. It is our number one aid in grounding; but we often 'leave that club in the golf bag' -- we often fail to use our best tool.

    I've written a lot on physical practice; you can check my posting history or do a search. Prostrations are excellent practice. Sun salutations are excellent practice. You can try Taiji or any of a number of forms of Yoga. There are many styles of movement practice. The main thing is using the body to get untangled from the head, and then seeking the energy down. When energy settles downward, already that is calmness and clarity.

    Some forms of movement practice can actually embrace anger or any other emotion. You can do prostrations with fiery intent, or even with rage. Free movement practices ('conscious dance', such as Open Floor, Authentic Movement, 5 Rhythms, etc.) can also embrace any energy or emotion you can come up with. It helps you avoid fighting with yourself. And then the energy gets discharged rather than built up and engaged in a battle.

    ~

    Besides physical practice, mantra and chanting are usually better than plain sitting meditations in cases like yours. The thing is, you need to do many many repetitions. You can choose a practice and try it. Often enough, one will be too agitated to even do mantra or chanting. In that case, turn to physical practice. But if either of these work for you, you can mix them into your schedule: do some prostrations and then some chanting, and then some mantra. You could even do prostrations and mantra together, which helps involve you even more.

    During the day, chanting is helpful for smoothing the emotions. As above, you can express any feeling in your chanting; and the sing-song quality will help you to have a simple and joyful mind. Just singing a few bars of a chant throughout the day helps you lighten up about things.

    ~

    With regard to falling of from practice, this is why we use all kinds of external structures to help us. Almost no one can do it by willpower alone. Being with a wise, centered teacher is an incredible benefit. Being with a group of sincere practitioners is an incredible benefit. There are many supportive structures, like:

    • spending consistent time with a teacher,

    • practicing with a group,

    • using rituals and routines,

    • setting schedules,

    • making commitments and vows,

    • cleaning and beautifying one's living space,

    • regulating sleep schedule,

    • balanced eating,

    • balanced physical activity and rest,

    • balanced work and rest,

    • clear and supportive relationships,

    • cutting down and cutting out agitating talk, reading, images, and media,

    • using touchstones like altars, sacred images, and other reminders of your highest intentions,

    • and most of all, performing positive ethical actions, being kind and generous, and avoiding negative ethical actions -- all other supports are useless if you agitate your mind by harming others

    All of these supports help the mind be settled, centered, clear, present. Even without sitting meditation or any other practice, these have the power to center your mind very strongly.
