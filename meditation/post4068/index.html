    How to meditate through a dark place?

    Yes, that's exactly it. This is both how your mind finds balance and how you build strength in practice. By letting go of thoughts that come up and returning to the method of meditation your mind finds its natural resting point. The thoughts may or may not go away, and may or may not come back; that doesn't matter. What matters is that you express your freedom from them by letting them be whatever they are, and returning your attention to the meditation. Quickly, effortlessly return. A thousand times, a million times.

    All thoughts and experiences come to you by natural process, according to your previous activities. You can't prevent them from coming up because the seeds have already been planted by your past decisions and actions. What you can control is what you do with them in the present moment: do you hold on and create personal drama, making new seeds that will ripen into related thoughts and experiences in the future; or do you choose momentary peace?

    In short form: what arises is none of your business; but returning is wonderful. Your will and ability to return to the present moment, without forcing your mind one way or the other, is your all-important power. It's as if you loosen your grip on the steering wheel so that the car can find its straight path, or as if you take the weight off of a scale so that it can return to zero and be ready to measure something accurately.

    It is not necessary to know how to fix everything. We put our trust in something deeper and greater. The world and your true being have innate intelligence. By letting go and returning you allow that intelligence to function by itself. You get out of the way of truth and let it do whatever it needs to do.

    So during meditation, when thoughts take you away, as soon as you notice (which may be in an instant or after ten minutes) then immediately and gently return to the breath. When you're taken away again, immediately and gently return again. The returning is your peace, your freedom, your power. It is always available to you; it's up to you to make use of it.

    Outside of meditation, in daily life, continue letting go of the thoughts that take you away. Immediately and gently return. What you return to is the situation that faces you. Meditation time: return to breath. Waking time: wash your face. Working time: do your work. Someone needs help: just help.

    Also, at times when you're not sure how to proceed, just follow that situation: just don't know. Decision time will arise by itself, so then you just decide. At any time, just follow the situation. If you don't know what your situation is, then see where your feet are standing or see what is in front of your face. Sometimes your body will tell you the situation, whether it's time to eat or time to cry. Only follow.

    By returning to the moment and following the situation, you allow the world's intelligence to move very smoothly through you. "Spring comes, and the grass grows by itself." Your activity will not be based on the agitation of opinion and reaction, but on the unfolding moment, the place where your true life is.

    In terms of doubts and upset in relationships, this letting go and returning not only helps you to be sane and healthy, it also embraces the other in openmindedness. You let go of bad thoughts that could get in the way of seeing the truth or helping the other person; and you let go of nice thoughts that could get in the way of dignity and proper boundaries. You are not tied to one way of reacting, but have all your options open. As the moment unfolds you are ready to follow it. And if you make a mistake you can soon correct it.

    ~

    I will also again encourage communication. Meditation can help you find your center; but you need to bring that center into life, into relationship. When someone senses that you are coming from a centered place rather than a reactive one, they will more easily confide in you. Communicating your doubts and wishes in an openhanded way may help you get to the truth together.
