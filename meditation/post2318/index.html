    Will mindfulness help with this?

    I'm very happy to help if I can. If there's something I don't have experience with or something I don't think I can speak to, I'll let you know.

    The kinds of things you're trying are fine. Feeling the breeze or other sensations, for instance, help to bring you back to reality. Your habit of thinking is very strong, so the thinking will return. That's okay.

    Also, it is in the nature of the mind to think, and this is true for everyone; so because of that too, the thinking will return. That's still okay.

    But when you take the attitude that the thinking is a problem and you go to mindfulness in order to escape the thinking, then you are making a bad pattern, a bad relationship with yourself.

    You try many things to take your mind away. Sometimes mindfulness of sensations, sometimes weightlifting, sometimes work, sometimes TV.... All of these are okay, and your thinking is also okay; but if you run to these things to escape or push away your thinking, then you make an enemy of your own mind. Then the cold breeze is not just cold breeze. Cold breeze becomes the thing you have to cling to in order to escape your mind.

    Correct mindfulness includes everything. It doesn't push away part of reality so that you can be with another part. When you pay attention to the act of washing the dishes, that doesn't mean that you should force everything else away. It is much more settled than that. So take care that you don't try to use mindfulness as a drug to avoid some part of yourself.

    That means that you will require the courage to be with your thinking mind and be with your changing feelings. This is a power that can be developed. If you keep trying, then your power to be with yourself — as-is — will grow.

    Thoughts themselves are no problem. It is your particular attachment to your thoughts that makes a problem. You give them too much importance and you let them influence how you feel; and you also make more thinking about your thoughts! If, however, you practice being open-handed with your thinking, you will develop a calm and centered nature.

    That means you need to train your ability to let go of the moment and let it be what it is. Whatever is outside of you and whatever is inside of you, let it be as it is. Later, maybe, you can try to steer how things are going. But in the moment, the first thing is always to let it go and let it be.

    One useful tool for learning how to let things be is to have an object to work with. Many, many people use breath as an object of meditation, for example. Again and again, they return to the sensations or movements of breathing. When a thought comes up, they say "fine, you can be there; but right now I'm returning to awareness of my breath."

    Others might work with a mantra, a phrase of syllables that they repeat continuously through the day. This gives them something to return to when thoughts take them away.

    There are many different possible objects to work with. Breathing meditation is certainly broadly popular and effective. But for you I think mindfulness of your foundation would be useful. It is perhaps more simple and easier to access when you are taken away by the thinking. In this practice, you repeatedly return to awareness of the feet (if standing or walking), or of the foundation of the body (in other positions). If you are sitting, you return to awareness of the seat and feet. If you are lying down, you return to awareness of the places where your body is resting on the bed or sofa or floor. Having a clear object to return to can help you let go of the thoughts. But remember that letting go doesn't mean the thing you let go of will necessarily disappear. It just means that for a moment you are no longer letting it control you.

    Since this is a moment-to-moment practice, you will need to re-apply it often and endlessly. Keep trying and you will succeed.

    I will be happy to talk about bowing practice when I have some more time. In the meantime, good luck finding other support in getting beyond troubling patterns.
