    Let's say my faith in that approach has been confirmed many times over. I've had a pretty open communication with the source of answers since I was a child, so maybe I'm not so dependent on a process. But in the more authentic schools of Zen, this is the approach for deriving — not exactly 'answers' — but complete resolutions to koans, life questions, and teaching situations.

    The basis is that there is a source of wisdom, which is your original source, my original source, and every being's original source. You already have all answers; the issue is that concept and mind-habit cover or otherwise block your connection to your original wisdom. The solution is not to drum something up or seek something from the outside, but rather to silence — if only for a few moments — the din of opinion, conditioning, knee-jerk reaction, and assumption. To be concept-free; to allow space.

    In that space, your own wisdom arises.

    But that doesn't necessarily mean you get a thinking-style answer or a visitation from a spirit or some other magical power. Sometimes, maybe; but not as a rule. It means that when the mirror of mind is clear it will reflect clearly what it is facing. Rather than seeing your own conditioned thoughts you will see what is really there: water is wet; sugar is sweet; trees are green, and sway in the wind; the river flows between its banks. The more often you allow space/clarity to open up, the more access you have to your clear mirror-mind.

    And the more you will perceive what is moving in you — the things your living being already knows, but which you don't listen to.

    Many people look for answers outside, in thoughts, possessions, special experiences, and relationships. Outside things can only help you if your mind-mirror is clear. Trying to find external powers and knowledges, and trying to manipulate situations into your favorite outcomes, is like adding more noise, more dirt on the mirror.

    So the great Zen patriarch Ta Hui said:

    >If you want to study this path, you must understand right where you are.
    As soon as you rely on the slightest knowledge, you miss the scene right where you're standing.

    >When you've completely comprehended the scene right where you are,
    then all kinds of knowledge -- all without exception --
    are things right where you are.

    ~

    If you want a thoroughly enjoyable and free-flowing way to get clear and connect with your intuition, I highly recommend movement practice and free dance. When you move your body, your mind also begins to flow; and as you trust your movement, the body spontaneously expresses your deep knowing.
