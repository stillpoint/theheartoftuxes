    Can somebody please please do me a favour and just give me a few sentences on how I should meditate?

    Okay, no problem. You don't have to do breathing style meditation.

    For you I recommend a mantra. It is a phrase that you repeat, which provides something to return to. You repeat it without trying to get or avoid anything, and the phrase helps you to be present and to clear your mind.

    • Before each sitting, it's important to make a good, noble intention, to remember what you want to achieve and what is a worthy direction for your life. Voice to yourself or out loud: “Whatever benefit I get from this practice (whatever clarity, wisdom, goodness, and so on), may it pass to all beings and help them to be happy.

    If you are ready for it, you can make the intention bigger, to help all beings wake up from life's dream and be completely free of suffering.

    • Sit down with a solid foundation. If your knees can rest on the floor or mat and your butt firmly on a firm cushion, that's good. If you can kneel with a firm base, that's good to. If you can sit on a chair with your feet firmly on the floor and your butt firmly on the seat, that's good. And if you can stand with a firm stance, that's good. Any of these postures works well.

    Lying down is not an appropriate posture for meditation unless you have no other option (i.e., you are sick or injured).

    • Let your posture be alert (not tense) and relaxed (not lazy). Take the shape of your noble, peaceful, aware mind.

    • Keep the eyes slightly open, and let them softly receive. Nothing much has to be done with the eyes. They can gaze softly down or softly forward. Just don't close them.

    • Repeat the phrase Om Mani Padme Hum. This is one of the most popular mantras, practices by millions of people around the world. It won't do anything to you through magic power, and the meaning is not important; just the sound of the syllables is there to help you keep coming back to the present moment.

    • Repeat the mantra. You can use your voice to help you. When you are taken away by thinking, just return to saying the mantra and hearing it, either internally or through your voice and hearing. Very simple.

    • If there is a particular mental or emotional difficulty, you can repeat the mantra to include it. Use that energy. Sad or angry, you can do sad or angry mantra for a while. You can also do it slower or faster. Faster mantra helps you deal with lots of fast thinking, and helps you pay attention when you are sleepy; slower mantra helps you express calmness.

    In one simple translation, Om means everything, Mani means the pure, bright, indestructible jewel-like mind, Padme means moment to moment life unfolding like a lotus flower opening, and Hum means as-is. So you keep your clear mind moment to moment, and your mind harmonizes with the universe. It's not necessary to think of the meaning.

    “Mantra" means “mind protection". It protects your mind if you do it constantly throughout the day. It is not magic; the protection happens because you have something to return to: you let go of trains of thought that harm you, trains of thought that agitate you and get you involved in unreal imagining, and you return to the simple moment. When you are present in the moment you can respond to it, rather than being led around by imagination and habit. That is protection.

    • You can do mantra as a sitting meditation, or throughout the day in the background of your mind. Both ways are very good for you. Also very easy, very simple. When your mind can be simple you can clear and happy.
