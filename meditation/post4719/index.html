    Anyone else have trouble keeping their eyes closed?

    There have been many, many, many posts on eyes open versus eyes closed.

    Whether your eyes should be open or closed depends on the method and on the instruction passed down in the lineage you're practicing in. If you're not clear on the method and teaching tradition, that's should come first before speculating about what you should do; meditation isn't 'mix and match', there are important reasons for points of guidance and differences between various schools.

    In most cases, just continuing to practice sincerely will resolve these kinds of issues. The trouble will become not so big a deal, the concentration will develop, the attention will not be diverted.

    But depending on the type of practice, it very well may be much better to keep the eyes open. What is the method you're practicing, and what is the instruction given for the method? While many vipassana methods will be practiced with closed eyes, with concentration meditations that follow the breath or count the breath it's actually quite important to keep the eyes open. This has the triply positive effect of opening the practice to the world, staving off insidious dream states, and encouraging alert wakefulness.

    As to clearing the mind more easily with closed eyes, this too tends to be resolved by simply continuing the practice. One shouldn't assume that easy means good. You can already do what's easy for you; that's why it's easy. But in order to break into new territory you need to achieve what you don't already do and what doesn't necessarily come easily to you. This is why it is necessary to be clear on the method and to follow it closely: the alternative is to rely on an understanding that is yet immature and inexperienced, which only keeps you lingering in that state rather than breaking through to new awareness.
