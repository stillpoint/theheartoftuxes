    Previous post on mudras (hand positions) in general.

    In the mudra you're talking about, forming the circle with index or other fingers, the different contact points close circuits in the energetic body, which emphasize the circulation of energy in particular pathways, promoting specific abilities and attainments.

    While it can be one of the things that helps you to perform the meditation, it is perceived at a subtle level that is usually only gained after considerable experience. In the beginning stages it tends to be less important than what we consider the 'basics', like generally upright posture (relaxed and awake), learning a proper method, understanding the method, and maintaining the right openhanded attitude.

    Whether the hand position is necessary depends on your aim, method, and the level at which you're operating. All of this should be checked with your teacher. If your teacher says it's important, do it; if your teacher says you can change according to your preference, then you are free to adjust as comfortable.

    If you're only starting out and don't have a teacher, you should at least learn a good, proven method from a reliable source, and keep to that method. Don't mix methods, and don't mess around with the method since that invites your preference and ego to run the show.

    If you don't care to cultivate meditation beyond sitting quietly for temporary benefit, you can just sit in any comfortable but wakeful posture.

    As to the duration of your sittings, this too depends on your motivation and your capacity. Why do you do meditation? How long have you been practicing? How is it going? You don't have to respond; but these are significant points. For most people, it is better to do less but do it very consistently than to force the issue and then experience disappointment, resistance, and giving up.
