    TheHeartofTuxes

    Very basically: without mental discipline, your experience depends on outer circumstances: in order to be content and compassionate you require a certain environment, a nice situation, the right kind of people, and so on.

    But life is not always nice like that. Things change, settings are never perfect, situations go up and down, you accumulate some things you don't want and you lose some things you do want, and you encounter many people who aren't your favorite. The outer situation can range from not so pleasant to downright threatening. Without control over your mental process, your mind can only follow the situation, going up and down, around and around without clear direction.

    But with mental discipline you can become stable, less reactive, less controlled by circumstances. You can have contentment and happiness even if you don't get your favorite thing. You can be clear and kind even if the person facing you is upset or confused. You don't have to be stressed about gain and loss.

    Perhaps most importantly, your life can have a clear direction: you can choose to live with wisdom rather than reactivity. Unless the mind is trained, even if you sincerely want to do it, you won't be able to: you will always be dragged around by circumstances.

    This tendency only becomes compounded. It's why many people become more and more crusty and sour the older they become. Since their reactivity doesn't allow their wisdom to be applied, they thus get themselves into bad relationships, or they behave without integrity and suffer the consequences, or they are impatient and act rashly and are led about by unhealthy habits, or they can't apply constant awareness and thus miss many warning signs and opportunities. (Or all of the above.) Therefore they receive a great deal of suffering in life — suffering that they themselves have invited — and they have more and more reason to develop resentment and defensiveness; so they lose their youthful sense of possibility and willingness to trust, and in some cases are pretty much dead years before their life actually ends.

    When you consider people who have achieved what they want to achieve, it very rarely comes by accident, out of the blue. Some ability to envision and create consistently across time is required. This is also a form of mental discipline. Meditation and mindfulness can help develop this kind of ability; and with good teaching and high-level approach it can also help you become aware of the right things to create, the right things to apply your effort to. This again points to clarity of direction in life: not only getting temporary benefit that will eventually go away, but getting something that will last and be valuable in the big picture, throughout life and beyond death.
