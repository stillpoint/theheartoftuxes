    Meditation bench?

    If you can borrow one to try, or if the store has a good return policy, that might be a good option for you. I used a bench for a while when injured, but found that while it does relieve pressure from within the knee joint, it also seemed to place more pressure on the front of the knee in contact with the mat. One bodypart that is particularly relieved by a meditation bench is the ankle. If you have any injury or pain in your ankles, a bench can be helpful.

    A couple of other points to consider:

    • Check whether the front edge of the seat digs into the back of the thigh.

    • The higher you sit, the less inherent stability of the posture; it may be just perfect for you, or you may find it a bit teetering.

    • Check whether the combination of seat angle and surface allow you to be stable in one posture, or cause you to slide down.

    • Some find a wood seat too hard, some find a cushioned seat too soft. Soft cushioning can in some cases be more painful than a firm seat over time, because while the tissue over the sit bones can adapt to hardness, a soft cushion wraps around the sit bones and puts constant pressure on the surrounding tissues, producing an ache that tends to worsen over the duration of a sitting. A soft seat may be comfortable for the first 20 minutes of a sitting and agony for the remaining duration.

    I hang on to my bench, in case I incur another knee injury, or in case someone I sit with needs it; but my experience with the bench tends to evoke the axiom "everything is Dukkha" (nothing in itself is completely satisfactory).

    If you find that a bench is not the answer for you, you might want to consider extra cushions for support in kneeling or cross-legged postures on the mat. Experiment with a set of cushions that allow you to lift the hips off the mat while completely supporting the knees from below. You may even want to stack cushions in or on your lap to support the hands. Never mind what it looks like; key on what works.

    In many practice centers it's also allowed to switch between chair and mat from sitting to sitting (or even within a single sitting).
