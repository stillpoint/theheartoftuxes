    Experience about meditation and initial bias

    Our instinctive functions make their own appraisals within the first split-second of meeting someone. Our mind-habits flesh these out with further reactions, speculations, projections, and so on. Our discriminating faculty draws a line between what is reality-based and what is just thought or fantasy. Three different movements of the mind.

    In order for the faculties to work properly, you have to repeatedly (and ideally, continuously) re-set. Drop it, clear it, return to zero. This is what correct meditation does; and it's a skill that you can develop and make use of in life, more and more.

    Just being aware that your thoughts are — at best — mere stagnant approximations of reality may help move you toward what you have to do; but then you have to do it: you have to let it all go in a moment. This is how things find their own correct place.

    You may be interested in the book Social Intelligence, by Daniel Goleman. In it, he talks about the different pathways through which we perceive each other. There's the "low road", which makes instant, instinctive summations; and there's the "high road", which takes a more reasoned approach. Each of these has its correct place and correct function; but the way that it all works together is through your intentional ungrasping.

    Perceive the moment without trying to get or avoid anything. That is meditation. The various forms of meditation just find different ways to get you to practice that repeatedly.

    As you say, these habits of judgment are "almost human". They are a natural process that works itself out based on previous conditioning. Your previous actions and intentions, your education, your cultural conditioning, your upbringing, your family situation, your emotional training — all of these contribute to the way life moves through you. The unfolding of it is something you can't stop. What you can do is let it go and return to neutral awareness. Let it go again and again and again, ten million times returning to the moment with open hands. Then your ability to remain there, in open clarity, will grow; and you will clean out your conditioning. The old habits will move through you for a time and then dissipate, and you will be free to follow your correct intention rather than your habit.
