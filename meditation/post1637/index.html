    This points to the limitation of apps: among other drawbacks, they don't respond to needs and questions that arise organically, and they don't address all facets of broad training. Traditional training with a living teacher can be complete and responsive; it can teach you about mindfulness throughout the day, not only isolated sitting method.

    Often you can use the same object during daily activity as you use in sitting meditation, just with broader, more open awareness. That is, don't focus on the object to do away with all other objects as in concentration-style practice, but use the object as a background anchor for presence -- keep contact with it, but without the need for it to be exclusive or even foremost in your mind.

    So if you are taught to focus on breathing during meditation, you can keep a background awareness of breathing to maintain functional presence throughout the day.

    That is one way. Not all teachers and systems will do it this way, and you should consult with your teacher to see what is appropriate, but this is one possibility.

    You can also make a point of just doing what you are doing. As in sitting, don't wrestle with thoughts, trying to drive them away. Just put the emphasis on being non-distracted, and engage with whatever you are doing. Let the thoughts come and go without reacting to them. This already demonstrates that you are aware of the thought arising. If you don't let your mind be moved by the thought, neither judging it good nor bad, then the thought can't control you; it is just seen as the flicker of mental activity.

    Some people use supports to help them maintain mindfulness and resist being pushed and pulled by thoughts. Tactile support in the form of a mala (meditation beads) is used by some devout people in some traditionally religious cultures (as well as some pretenders).

    With or without breads, a great many people use the support of a mantra. Some people never stop doing their mantra, every moment of the day. Since mantra gives you something to return to with the attention rather than getting swept along in thoughts, it lives up to the meaning of the word: mantra means "mind protection". For those proficient in its use, even otherwise very traumatic events, and even special instances of curse or possession, can be dispelled by consistently returning to the mantra.

    Again, as above, it is important not to use the mantra object in concentration-style when you ate moving about your day, as it could lead to you withdrawing attention from important situations, as when you're driving or doing dangerous work, our when you ought to be showing up for and responding to someone else or some crucial task. When you're sitting safely in meditation, you might choose to withdraw the mind (or you may not -- there are different ways of meditating as well); but when you are up and about, phrase show up for whatever situation is facing you.

    Traditional mantras have unique qualities, unique energies that merge with you when you repeat them many thousands of times. Some mantras are associated with particular beings, and bring forth their vow and presence. Some practitioners repeat their mantra several million times throughout their life; and they can become saint-like in their compassion and presence through this practice.

    Yet another option for background anchor is feeling your physical basis, your contact with ground or other physical support (chair, bed, sofa). For the most part, this entails keeping some gentle awareness of your feet on the ground or your butt on the seat as you go about your activities. It is simple, non-intrusive, and it has the added benefit of drawing you into the most stabilizing influence in your life: your body.

    As mentioned, there are also non-object-oriented ways of meditating presence. If you want to try those, best learn from a teacher and try it out in guided practice settings. Zen or Dzogchen, for instance, may recommend plain non-distraction without a particular object. That tends to rely on having connected and become familiar with the basic awareness, so you know what you're surrendering to.
