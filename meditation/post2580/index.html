    Need help with meditation.

    The beginnings of things are often uncertain, so it's completely appropriate not to know exactly what's going on, not to know what you should be experiencing or If you're doing it “right". In fact, when you do start to know these things, it's an unfortunate loss of openness.

    It is important, however, to be clear on your method. There shouldn't be doubt about what you should be doing with your mind when you meditate. If you have found a good method, just follow it. Let nothing separate you from the method. Many unusual experiences may arise, monotony and boredom may arise, thrills and insights may arise, liking something and not liking something else may arise... but none of that should take you away from the method. Following this piece of advice will help you tremendously in your meditation career. It will help you stay on track and avoid wasting your time.

    I'll go further to say that all of the magnificent, or bizarre, or mundane things that arise in meditation come from your own mind. They are projections that bubble up from within. There is no need to engage with them and get distracted. Be like a cat watching a mouse-hole, don't let anything divert your attention. This will help you avoid going down pointless side roads, possibly saving you years or even lifetimes of wrong practice.

    My question to you is: why meditate? This is a question that can be looked into repeatedly, even as you progress in your practice. Your relationship with practice can, and should be, refined as you let go of grasping and as your mind becomes more open and compassionate. But this point is clear: if you meditate only for “benefits", you can't go very far with it. Instead, as flightm0de wrote, I urge you to keep an open, curious, exploring attitude. Let things be as they are and don't be greedy; this practice will transform your world into a lively and clear -- if still ungraspable -- one.

    Edit: I think you're on the right track in seeking a teacher and a group to practice with. You may stick with a teacher for a long time or you may eventually move on and look for another once you understand your own mind better, but it's helpful to keep putting yourself out there, receiving feedback on your practice. You are still the primary guide for yourself, and you should trust what you know, but a good teacher can help you in ways you may not be able to imagine.

    If you need to talk about method, let me know. Good luck, and thank you for your practice!
