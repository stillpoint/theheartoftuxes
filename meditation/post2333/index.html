    meditation and insanity

    This kind of difficulty is so extremely common among pot smokers who meditate that I'm surprised there isn't more awareness about it. The correlation (if not causal connection) is very clear.

    First: If you feel less sane, stop doing the thing. Until you have gathered the circumstances — internal and external — that will allow you to safely let go of the grip on your mind, it's your duty to protect that precious and vulnerable faculty.

    Second: Get your life in order. Many of the benefits you seek through meditation can be achieved, and in a more grounded way, through change in behavior and lifestyle. Start with the basics: sleep schedule, food, exercise, and work. Then move on to a spring cleaning of your living space and habits. Pay attention to what you ingest: solid and liquid and gaseous things that go in your mouth, images and words that go in your eyes, sounds and words that go in your ears, and the things you pursue with your body. When your body is balanced and your life is in order, meditation will happen naturally and there will be no fear about letting go.

    Third: If you don't want to give up practice completely, apply yourself to a less intense mindfulness practice (maintaining simple awareness of what you're doing throughout the day) and a physical meditation (bowing/manual labor/taiji/yoga/running).

    You are too attached to the dream material that bubbles up from the depths, so it's not good for you to be in close contact with it until you have built the container for it. Take the pressure cooker off the heat. Build a solid and safe container for practice by rectifying your bodymind, schedule, and environment. Get out of the head and in the body so your mind can be simple and comfortable.

    That's more than enough to go on for months, if not years. It will lay the foundation for true meditation.
