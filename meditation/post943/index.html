    Looking for reassurance that this is OK

    The aim is to identify with reality and disidentify with unreality.

    All that commentary, including naming parts of yourself ("The Coxswain," etc.) is mental fabrication. Judging how you're feeling, how it's going, your condition, your situationâ€¦ it's all mental fabrication. The sooner you stop thinking this is real, the better it will go for you. It is because you think of it as real and substantial that it is a problem for you. If you realize it is an insubstantial dream there will be no reason for agitation.

    So there is really no strategy necessary, only clarity about your practice: when unreality arises, drop it immediately. Don't linger, don't play, don't figure, don't check. Just drop it immediately.

    Practice should not be conceived of as getting rid of something or grasping something else. The more you try to arrange things, the more you build up a relationship with the unreal. You think of your mind as something substantial and you think of yourself as something substantial. Furthermore, you think that one of these dream constructs is in opposition to the other. This is identifying with unreality.

    When you drop it, that is identifying with reality.

    It's not that the thoughts no longer arise; it's that you don't identify them as "you". You are the space in which everything happens.

    It's not that you never get distracted; it's that you return to the moment without adding more fabrication. You are already awareness, without the need to make anything.

    Spacious and aware: this is your true nature, which doesn't need any external thing to support it.

    One key quality of the mental fabrication is that it is dualistic: there is a "you" and a "something" of note. The "something" is deemed good, bad, or neutral; but those are empty labels that lead away from the actual experience, which is non-dual.

    Another key quality of the mental fabrication is that it depends on time: there is a sense of a situation that has been developing or remaining over, what?, the past few seconds or minutes, or the past several sittings. Time, too, is empty; it leads away from true life, which is completely momentary. So your meditation doesn't happen over the course of several minutes, or half an hour, or across weeks or months.

    There is no time in which your meditation happens; there is only the moment. In the moment, all feelings and all thoughts are just truth. The thought "nice carpet" may arise. The feeling you name claustrophobic may arise. In the moment, it is just as real as the sound of cars on the road, the feel of clothing on the skin, the movement of breath, and so on. It is only when you give it extra meaning that it becomes a problem.

    Furthermore, it is not true that you have to return many times, not within practice. Within practice, you only have to return this time. And if you don't identify with unreality, you don't even have to return at all.

    Identify with reality by being openhanded in the moment. Be the space in which everything happens; don't attach to the objects that arise, linger, or pass away. Then everything is already truth. You don't have to add or subtract anything yourself.

    If you say that's what you've been trying to do, fine. Keep practicing.
