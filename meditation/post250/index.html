    Why can't you do a retreat?

    When some people say "I can't", others say "I can... I will." That's often the difference between going deeper or staying on a plateau.

    Definitely, external form clarifies your practice. If not 10 days retreat, then a week. If not a week, then a weekend. If not a weekend, then a day sitting. If not a day, then a half day; or extra practice. If not that, a workshop or class series, or membership and weekly practice with a teacher and group.

    The teacher and group supply many supportive functions for going deeper, not the least of which is that you show up for the sake of others, and support them in their effort to go deeper. Add to that the encouragement and inspiration and example of others, and the regular contact with correct teaching and correct conduct, and the opportunity for live and lively Dharma interaction.

    If those possibilities are still truly unavailable to you (and you're not just resistant), then establishing more clear form and commitment is of benefit. Try, not only sitting meditation, but physical practice, voice practice, and compassion practice/dedicating merit to others. Make your sitting periods clear in duration and form. Be meticulous about the form.

    And be meticulous about not checking. Rather than judging your progress more frequently, go in the other direction and be more momentary and pure in your effort: only take care of the moment, with your whole life-and-death gathered there.

    Good teaching is so important. It is arrogant to think one knows all there is to know, or that one is the best judge of one's own progress. If a person is so confident in their practice they should have no qualms whatsoever in presenting themselves to a teacher and surrendering to a practice. It is the tradition of aspiring monks to visit other schools and styles and give up their own preferences and opinions for a time, giving themselves over to instruction with great humility and zeal.
