    It's difficult and somewhat inappropriate to give you advice outside of a traditional course of training. We don't know what kind of meditation you are doing, what method, for what purpose, and in the context of what other practices and teachings.

    There would be different instructions for different styles. Concentration style focuses on the object, to the exclusion of all other objects. It tends to be more susceptible to the kind of mistake you describe, an agenda of pushing away aspects of the mind or aspects of reality. Therefore it is very, very important in concentration practice to start with the proper motivation and attitude. The proper motivation is compassion for others — practicing to save not only yourself but all other from suffering life. The proper attitude is non-grasping — complete determination to let everything be; complete surrender.

    Other meditation is maybe not so object-oriented and strident as concentration-style. The whole approach is more broad and relaxed (though the application takes a particular kind of effort). Some people find true meditation more difficult for this reason, as they don't have that very vivid distinction between the object of concentration and all else. But this distinction is also what gets people into trouble when doing concentration style.

    So the advice here depends on the style you are practicing. That's the first point: are you clear on what style you are practicing; are you clear on the method? It is surprisingly easy to be unclear, as we glean bits of information from different sources, from different traditions, from people of different levels of attainment. So choosing one definite method is necessary. Choose a proven traditional method, and learn it well, and then apply it without hesitation.

    If you are clear on the method and have faith in it, then you can just go back to the breath, armed with what you now know. Don't avoid or push away anything. In your attitude, let it all be.

    But that doesn't mean you have to check. Don't hold anxiety or fear. Don't check whether you're doing it, or how well you're doing it. Only be sincere in your effort. It comes from your center; so if your center is sincere, never mind about anything else. It is not your job to check and analyze. If you have a teacher, that is your teacher's job. If you don't have a teacher, you place your faith in the method and in your innate awakened nature.

    So only return to your method. If you feel at a gut level that concentration style itself is contrived (and I would tend to agree with you, or at least say that it's easier to depart from pure openhandedness with it), then you can change your practice.

    What is your aim? What is the best, highest, most complete fulfillment of meditation?
