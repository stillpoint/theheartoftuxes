    This is a very good topic.

    >...all I observe is a moment with fewer thoughts.

    This is where you need to be meticulous, and refrain from skipping over something or labeling something 'insignificant':

    Fewer thoughts, or no thought?

    A moment, or a generalization across time?

    What actually is there in the moment?

    Self-inquiry mustn't be conceived of as searching for a particular thing ('self', 'consciousness', 'mind', etc.). Although the searching is necessary, and should be carried out assiduously, don't expect to find an object to grasp. The ungrasping of all objects is really the mechanism for returning to reality.

    What may happen is that you may come to a point where you find nothing to grasp, nothing of significance, nothing to identify with or know. There may be a sense of groundlessness. Some meditators have a feeling of falling when they waver between this point and grasping at objects.

    It is a question without the arrival of an answer. And that is the answer. The openness itself, beyond form, beyond limitation, with no defined object created, is it. There is a voidness there, but it is not just voidness. It is not only vacant or dull or dead. It is completely aware.

    Meditators come upon a stumbling block when they think that the ungraspability or 'nothing in particular' quality of this point means it is insignificant, or that it can't be what is being sought. In fact, when anything appears, it is already that point. It is already mind, made of mind, appearing withing mind.

    Don't think that the questioning or seeking-without-finding is separate from the mind that is being sought.

    You ask "Did this have an effect on you?" If you are concerned about effects, you are concerned about objects â€” the content that arises in the mind. This is not the practice. The content is illusion. It's dream-stuff. You are to seek the mind itself, within which (or as which) the content appears. So don't get hooked by your checking/judging/thinking/analyzing about what arises.

    Become friends with Unknowing. That's the basic direction of self-inquiry.
