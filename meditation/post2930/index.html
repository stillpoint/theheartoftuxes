    An unlikely start

    A great many people have had great many of the same and similar experiences. It means you went around in your imagination. It's great that you sat down to meditate, but this is not meditation; this is more like daydreaming or letting your thoughts drift. You can name it a 'revelation', and certainly it can feel nice, just like having a dream can feel nice; but it is all engaged with appearances in the mind. Meditation lets go of attachment to appearances.

    You do not seem to be clear on a method. The first steps in meditation practice are to understand your correct motivation, and then to find and learn a good, proven method that aligns with the motivation. If the motivation is to wake up from the dream of life, then you need a method that does not indulge in dreaming. In fact, if your motivation respects reality at all, the method should involve letting go of attachment to thought, not following it and being preoccupied with it.

    There are some meditation methods in which one is instructed to sit as if one is a mountain: the body-and-mind is grounded, noble in posture, and imperturbably still. But a mountain doesn't push or pull. It doesn't rush toward anything or avoid anything. It is completely centered in itself, and its peak is open to the sky.

    So likewise in your meditation, you must not try to push any thoughts away, and neither may you follow them as you were doing. You must sit centered in yourself and let pass any thoughts and images that might appear. You must not be moved by thought.

    If you try to get rid of thoughts, already you are moved by them: they are moving you into an aggressive, pushing relationship with them. If you follow thoughts such as images of yourself, dream stories, speculations, concepts, or any wise-sounding or nonsensical inner dialog, then also you are being moved around — controlled and possessed — by your thoughts. This is completely opposite to meditation. In meditation you must take on the symbolic qualities of a mountain; you must be unmoved by anything that appears.

    Your dream images don't have any significance other than what you give them. If you give them any significance, that is adding more thought after the fact. You could follow layer upon layer of stories and intrigue, thinking of your experience as something wonderful, and only getting more and more lost in the illusion of your mind. Some people do this and call it meditation or spiritual practice; they only become more and more deluded. You can meet many people who are very much living in their own fantasy world. Sometimes these people meet each other and make a stronger fantasy world. Much of our suffering global situation is caused by people living in their world of thought rather than the world of reality.

    If you want to do proper meditation you should attend caringly to the first two steps: understand your correct motivation, and find and learn a clear and proven method of meditation (which usually means a traditional method and one passed down by an awakened teacher).
