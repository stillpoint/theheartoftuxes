    Meditation (possibly faulty) reservations

    Your personality will always change. It keeps evolving, based partly on your choices and new-found knowledge, and based partly on your unconsciousness and even your ignorance. You will always keep becoming someone new.

    If you want more say over that process — if you want to be able to steer it toward positive change — then awareness is essential. There is no other way.

    Wishful thinking won't work. Ignoring parts of life out of fear won't help. Trying not to change will prove impossible. Trying to force behavior upon yourself will generate unforeseen reactions.

    Only when you can witness your own inner actions and reactions from a place of stability will you be able to have some degree of choice, of freedom. Otherwise you're not much different from a worm that follows its habit.

    In Buddhism there is a teaching about the Eight Worldly Concerns. These are often called the Eight Worldly Winds, because they are the things that tend to blow us here and there without us being able to exert any degree of control. It is only when we gain awareness of these Winds and train our mental stability that we start to be free of them.

    The Winds are as follows (in rhyming form for easy memorizing):

    > Loss and Gain

    > Fame and Shame

    > Pleasure and Pain

    > Praise and Blame

    It doesn't take much to understand that, "yes, I am constantly blown around by these Winds".

    Meditation is training the mind not to be blown about by these Winds. You keep a mind that doesn't move. Then when various situations unfold you can begin to see your habitual worm-like reactions to them. For instance, like many (most?) people you may always grasp for pleasure and always run away from pain; you may always seek praise and always react defensively to blame; and so on.

    A free person doesn't necessarily react that way. A hero, like a fireman or a loving mother, may go toward pain and endure it for the sake of others. A person with noble self-control may make healthy and compassionate choices rather than be led down a road of ruin by sensual pleasure. A self-possessed leader may be able to prevail in wise courses of action over the long term rather than get worn down or swayed by praise and blame.

    When your concentration grows in meditation, sometimes pain will arise and you won't automatically recoil. Pain will, in that moment, not have the power to move your mind. Sometimes the thought of a pizza or sex may appear and you won't immediately run to get it. Sometimes someone may blame you for your action and instead of reacting mindlessly you will learn from the truth in it and let any falsehood waft by like a dandelion seed.

    Awareness and stability mean you can control your life. You don't get to choose everything that happens or stop things from unfolding, but you can gain harmony with the unfolding.

    With meditation, your thinking will not necessarily shut off, but you have a chance to be free of it. You have a chance to use your thinking as one of your many resources, such as arms that move, or the sense of hearing, or the ability to feel someone's sorrow. You can use your thinking rather than be controlled by it.

    At times, your thinking may indeed become quiet and you may indeed feel your small, wind-shaken self dissolve. Who you really are is always there, bigger than you can conceive. It's not foreign. It's not a danger. It's the most 'you' part of you. There's no need to make or avoid anything. Sincerity will keep you safe. But yes, you have to decide whether you have the courage not to pre-plan what you are going to see or feel.

    At the bottom of it all, are you willing to trust yourself?
