    How do I create more distance from my thoughts..?

    If you conceive of yourself in one place, struggling against your thoughts in another place, you are creating a division that doesn't originally exist. Thus you are creating the schism, the anxiety, yourself by opposing one part of the mind with another part.

    The key in the meditative approach is in what you identify with. Identify yourself as the Witness of all thoughts, visions, senses, emotions, events, and experiences. You are not your body. You are not your thoughts. Merely witness.

    The more you do the practice of witnessing, the more easily you will move into the role of the Witness. This will calm anxiety, as you will experience directly that the Witness is constant, stable, open, aware, and never disturbed by any appearances.

    Your original Witness mind is like the sky: open and clear. Anything can appear in it freely, but the space of the sky is not marked or harmed in any way. Likewise, anything can appear in your awareness, but the awareness itself is continuously open and unmarked, unmoved by what appears.

    If you identify with thoughts, you will label some as wanted and some as unwanted, some as good and some as bad. You will try to grasp the wanted and avoid the unwanted. So you will be moved around by appearances. This is agitation; this is anxiety.

    The mind that attaches to appearances is very anxious. It is especially anxious about letting go and resting. Because it thinks of itself as just another appearance, just another form, it sees its death in resting. "If I become the sky, I will die!" This anxiety doesn't come from your original sky-like nature, but from your attachment to appearance.

    Therefore, if you stop identifying with appearances and instead identify as the sky-like Witness, you can rest in your original nature and no appearances — no thoughts or events of any kind — will disturb or harm you.

    So what is the approach? 'Sky-like' means completely open and ungrasping. Only watch; only perceive. No need to hold any sense of "I am doing this practice". Even that is another appearance. Merely perceive all appearances.

    The sky-like Witness is also very still and stable. Find a posture that will allow you to remain comfortably still and awake, your body taking on the quality of the still and awake sky-like mind.

    If you need further guidance on the approach, go gaze at the sky. Understand its constant and open quality; take it in. Then keep that Witness mind. That is what you really are.

    Soon, appearance and Witness will merge. From the beginning they were not separate, but thinking made them seem separate. When subject and object come together, that is awakening. No need to strive for it. Only do the sky-like Witness practice.
