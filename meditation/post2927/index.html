    How often/long to practice?

    30 minutes every day is better than an hour every other day.

    Consistency is important, for a couple of reasons:

    First, the consistency can merge with your life. Rather than thinking that the effects of meditation only have to do with your sitting periods, you can realize that meditation affects your whole day and how you unfold yourself as a human being. And then it becomes very interesting! Your unfolding is no longer compartmentalized into an hour here, 30 minutes there; it is everything you do with your body, speech, and mind, whether awake or asleep. That is the gateway to great transformation.

    Second, it is important to create a sound foundation for what you are going to build. If you are going to build a practice, it should be on firm footing rather than on shaky ground. So it is much better to do shorter sittings (even perhaps much shorter than 30 minutes at first) very consistently than to force the issue and end up feeling discouraged and hopeless.

    Having the repeated experience of meditation as something refreshing, positive, and even at first completely pleasurable will reinforce your positive connection with it. This will sink into you, into your mind and life. When you think "meditation" you will also think "ah, wonderful!" When it's time to meditate, you won't resist. That is a good basis for future progress. Once that foundation has been laid, and you can see for yourself the valid direction and good results of a consistent practice, then you will be ready to forge ahead into more challenging practice, overcoming all attachments and habits.

    Maybe let your meditation session be a bit more integrated into every day. Instead of something scheduled like a contingent appointment, let it be something you just do every morning and/or evening, like brushing your teeth or eating a meal or putting on and taking off clothing. You clean your teeth every day; so clean your mind every day. You take body nourishment every day; so take the mind nourishment of open presence every day. You don clothing every day; so clothe yourself in spaciousness. You take off clothing every day; so let go of all contrivance and dependance.

    ~

    Since you like physical activity, I'll just point out that physical activity is a very good companion to sitting. Having a run or doing a bit of movement before sitting helps to calm and smooth the energy. The traditional companion practice to sitting is prostration practice, which is very vigorous, whole-body-and-breath, rhythmic movement. It is both strength and cardio training, though the mind doesn't keep a gaining idea when performing it. Prostrations are almost identical to the very highly respected 'Hindu Squats', and condition not only the muscles but also the breath and energy and internal organs. After a set of prostrations, you are really ready to sit!

    ~

    If you don't have firm meditation cushions, you can use a folded blanket or two, like a yoga blanket or flannel or wool blanket (not a puffy quilted one). The foundation under the seat should be firm, and elevated enough to allow the knees to rest comfortably and firmly on the meditation mat, rug, or spread-out blanket. This creates a stable triangle. When the bottom triangle is stable and in correct alignment, the upper body can relax. When the bottom triangle is out of whack, muscular effort is required to keep you in position, which engenders tension and pain. If the cushioning has to be so high for the knees to touch that it becomes precarious, it is much better to sit in a chair.
