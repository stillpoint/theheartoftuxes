    > What do I need to do to overcome this?

    First, drop 'overcoming'.

    > It's like an impassable wall.

    Why is it like an impassable wall? It is like this because you make the idea that it is. Without your idea, it is only like what it is. Only the momentary pattern of mind and energy. Only like this.

    Return to openhandedness in your practice. Don't make ideas about your situation. Don't check your situation to judge what's happening. Only return to the method.

    For this returning to the method to be clear, you need to have the proper method for your aim; you need to have learned it well from an capable, authorized source; and you need to apply it precisely without adding or subtracting based on your own opinion and whim.

    > I allowed everything in my body and mind to surface without resistance.

    This too is only your idea. We do the practice of allowing; but we don't know if we are really allowing everything deeply. This is because there are many parts of our own minds, many patterns and movements, that we don't see. Since we don't see them, we can't say that we have completely given over in surrender at that level.

    Only do the practice, don't label it. Don't make yourself into a 'something'.

    > I decided to keep being the neutral observer.

    Good. This is the practice.

    It is not expected that you will be perfect at it. It is not expected that you will achieve pure witnessing at every moment, in every situation. You still have karma; old patterns of manipulation and self-deception will keep arising for quite a while, despite your sincere aspiration. Thus the need for patience and perseverance.

    What is expected is that you try. Keep applying the mind in being a neutral observer. Keep returning to the method. If the method is not clear, clarify it. Learn a proven, traditional method that suits your aim; learn it from a capable, achieved living teacher; and then just do it without wavering.

    > It came to a point where I could feel the front of my brain, and this aura of wisdom and clarity.

    This is more thinking. It is a story you make. You experience certain sensory phenomena or mental phenomena (thoughts, images), and you interpret them and make them into the story about your brain, and aura, and wisdom and clarity.

    That is not clarity! Clarity is putting all of that down.

    If there is a sign, a phenomenon, an object that you can point to, it's not clarity. Clarity is the clear open space within which signs, phenomena, and objects appear. If you are not spacious and unknowing, in that moment you are not clear; you are only making a story about clarity.

    > Then out of nowhere, anxiety physical symptoms struck my whole body making me feel almost paralyzed....

    The first thing that comes to mind here is to get checked out neurologically and psychologically. Just because it appears in the context of meditation doesn't mean meditation causes it. There may be an organic or environmental or psychological cause. You should consult the related health professionals, if only to rule out other causes.

    It's not within the scope of this forum, but how is your sleep? You may want to check it out with a sleep specialist. This field addresses not only what happens during sleep itself, but in general the way the brain and body communicate in daily hormonal cycles.

    Hypnagogia/hpynapompia are known patterns. They're being studied and treated; so you should address this issue separately from meditation.

    But one thing common among sufferers of this kind of paralysis is that they may often attribute the chemical or psychological disconnection to some other external force. Superstition takes root, and a cycle of anxiety and intensified symptoms begins. Some may imagine demonic possession. You imagine an impassable wall (among other things). It's very important not to make these stories. Be aware of how your mind departs from simple witnessing and starts attaching to phenomena. You will help yourself in both meditation and healing.

    > ...then back to my plain old anxious self.

    Your plain old anxious self is already correct meditation, if you don't attach to it, resist it, try to control it, or make stories about it. Just return to your proper method of meditation. If you don't have faith in your method, as I said above that is the first issue to take care of by learning a proven method from a capable source. if you have faith in your method, only do it. Don't check your situation. Don't make stories about what you're gaining or losing. Don't make an idea about what you are or what you are not. Only do the meditation. Then you can get clear.

    ~

    Drop 'overcoming'. When you try to overcome something that arises in your mind, you put your (imagined) self in opposition to the (imagined) object. Originally, the object and you are not separate; but you make separation and opposition. Furthermore, by trying to eradicate part of yourself you only drive that part into unconsciousness, where it will control you from the shadows. View the old film Nosferatu by F. W. Murnau: it is only when the woman finally embraces the monster that it finally dissolves. Likewise, when inner monsters are embraced in compassion and equanimity, they dissolve. Only do the meditation, don't make a monster.

    ~

    And once again, let's all note that meditation is not a blanket cure for psychological issues. Get the proper diagnosis and treatment for anxiety disorder or other issues that may be in play.
