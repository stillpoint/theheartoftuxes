    Your original, basic mind always reflects clearly. Whatever appears in the moment, original mind reflects or records it like a clear mirror. When comparing comes in, that's already the addition of thinking; so you can know that you are adding thinking atop your clear mind in that moment.

    Original Mind is "just like this". This is called 'Moment World' or 'Truth'.

    Checking, judging, comparing, and speculating are "this versus that". This is called 'Opposites World'.

    In Moment World, everything is as it is. In Opposites World, you have already made and perpetuated the idea of separate beings and objects. So Opposites World is suffering; it is a world of disconnected, incapable beings adrift in vastness.

    Therefore, if you compare like this, you already have suffering -- the suffering of disconnection and egotism. You may be different, of course; but if you don't recognize also your innate unity you are still stuck in the same ignorance of those others you are comparing yourself with.

    So progress in wisdom is not meant to separate you, but to allow you to unify with others and help them also progress in wisdom. But for that to happen, you need to find your true center and become stable, so that you don't get pushed around our swallowed up by the influence of others who don't have wisdom. You have to become stable in wisdom.

    That requires ongoing practice: to keep returning to Original Mind.

    This is another case of all complaints returning to one point. Whatever problems appear in life, the one problem behind them all is that we don't understand our true self -- we don't reside firmly in Original Mind. So, although certainly you can deal with issues in particular practical ways, each according to the specific situation, the basic, most practical solution behind all of them is to attain your true self, your true mind.

    It is right to have some spiritual pride. That means having pride in the right thing, having faith in the true way instead of joining the rat race of delusion. But if that gets inflated into a sense of superiority, you can realize it's the same egocentric ignorance that functions in almost all of us: until you have thrown off delusion forever, you're still in the same boat with all us ignorant, suffering folks.

    You have a precious Way-Seeking Mind. I can perceive it in your writing. This is called Tao Shin ("Way Mind") in Chinese; and it is revered as the seed of enlightenment and the vehicle of true practice. If you want to nurture and grow your Way Mind, keep seeking out its good growing conditions: authentic teacher, good spiritual friends, environment conducive of honesty and introspection, practices of generosity and compassion and investigation, and so on. Living examples of clarity and compassion are especially valuable in nurturing the Way Mind; so the more you can hang out with sincere practitioners, teachers, monks and nuns, and masters, the better.

    When you progress and become stable in wisdom, you can return to your friends and family with open-heartedness, only helping them without requiring their validation or change of viewpoint. Then your light can shine to them and kindle their own inquiry, their own Way-Seeking Mind.

    ~

    Yes, you can label all thinking-based realizations as not grounded in reality. But then you have to act on that realization. Just that idea is not enough. You have to then return to reality: put it all down, don't make anything with your mind, and allow Original Mind to function by itself, like a mirror that reflects when something appears before it and is still and open when nothing appears before it.
