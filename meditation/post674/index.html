    Fear of realization (awakening)

    "I" dare not. It's a problem for the "I", the small one, the ignorant one.

    When the ignorant one is dispelled, the great wide one — the wise one — is revealed.

    Not only does the great wide one have no problem with realization, it already includes realization, and always has.

    There are many ways to deal with the fear. Many different paths and methods. But very basically, you either keep going and get better, or you stop and suffer. You suffer, and the suffering continues, and the suffering builds up until you see that your stopping is useless, at which point you again proceed.

    Most serious fear and feelings of depersonalization that we encounter in meditation are associated with drug use or other addictive mind-altering behaviors. The self-image grows out of proportion, and the self-control of the mind is hampered beyond the usual. If this is the case with you, changing those conditions will help reduce the fear.

    This problem of feeling like you could go deeper, but being afraid to, is part of a broader issue of checking. Checking is the biggest problem in meditation. It means leaving the meditation itself, and thinking about your situation and condition. You step outside and look back. Then you make a judgment, and you label the situation "good" or "bad" or whatever other label you may have.

    This is a big mistake in practice. It's none of your business to check. Your business is to be sincere. Only do it. But when you give yourself permission to check, then all these judgments and reactive thoughts arise, and they are what bind you. You project where you think things are headed, and you try to control the outcome. Again, that's none of your business. It only means you are no longer doing correct meditation.

    So your job is to follow the method of meditation. Is it a good method? Is it proven? Is it taught correctly? Have you learned it well? Do you apply it well? If those conditions are met, then don't check your situation. Don't check how you're doing or where you think things are headed. Only take care of the moment.

    Rest assured: if something unbearable comes up, then at that point you can ditch. You will be able to respond, because you have been present. If the mind is wandering, you can't respond; but since you are practicing momentary presence, you can respond to anything that arises.

    But if you keep your attention in the moment there is nothing you can't handle.

    You just have to know whether you've suffered enough to let go. If you haven't suffered enough, then keep holding on to your current "I" situation. If you've had enough, give yourself over to the practice.

    What the ego hates, the soul loves. What little I fears, big I embraces.
