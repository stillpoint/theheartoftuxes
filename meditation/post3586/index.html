    Meditation may help you. It may be helpful within a broader context of other helpful conditions, like counseling, therapy, self-help, lifestyle changes, improvement in environment, relationships, fitness, and many other possibilities. Or it may be the wrong thing for you, especially if you don't understand the appropriate motivation and method.

    An overwhelmingly large proportion of visitors to this forum seem to think or hope that meditation will turn out to be a magic pill or cure-all for their woes. But it's often the case that some other action is necessary, or at least much more likely to bring about positive change. It depends on your particular situation and your particular constitution and capacity.

    Learning how to handle emotions should ideally come from good parenting. But how many of us can claim that we've received that? Therefore it often happens that we need to re-parent ourselves somehow. One way is through therapy, where the therapist provides the compassionate container, the compassionate context, within which we can re-experience the difficulties and wounds that brought us to our anguished state. Re-living the traumas in the context of attention and compassion, we gradually learn how to do that for ourselves: the compassion becomes internalized, becomes our own. Then we can meet all kinds of emotions with ease and equanimity and kind understanding.

    The emotions then become aspects of wisdom rather than intruders.

    Regarding depression, there are many possible causes (biochemical imbalance, family dysfunction, trauma, lifestyle issues, modern societal malaise, and so on). Sometimes there are multiple originating causes; and if the depression goes on for considerable time, other causes enter the picture; for example, a trauma that with good parenting might have been easily digested becomes a sticking point, one withdraws and becomes reactive, social health suffers, one becomes stagnant, fitness and brain chemistry change, and finally normal and healthy lifestyle habits become impacted.

    This is one common pathway among many possibilities. But it just goes to show that one can't necessarily pin one's hopes on meditation alone. Sometimes one needs to get moving physically. That could be much more influential than sitting alone and still at a time when one has already been alone and still for so long. Or emotional expression could be very helpful: writing or singing or dancing or acting... or talking sincerely with a friend or compassionate neutral party.

    If you're still looking for a meditation method, seek out a proven traditional method and just try.
