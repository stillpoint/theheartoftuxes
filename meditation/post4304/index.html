    Few thought about Sri Ramana Maharishis Self Inquiry [ /w TL;DR ]

    No, I don't think so. Of course I could be wrong; I'm just speaking from my own experience, having met with thousands of meditators. I wouldn't assume that the circles I move in encompass the whole global reality.

    If there's interest, one might look into the kind of "involvement" that's present. So many different challenges may crop up among meditators: self-doubt, difficulty concentrating, depression, anger, pain, self-aggrandizement, confusion about certain mental or sensory events, and so on. When you look into the lifestyle and daily activity of the meditator, there's usually a connection to be seen between what's coming up in the meditation and how they use their minds and bodies in everyday life. Most of us have some forms of addiction, some mind habits, and some blind spots. Why do they take the form they do? Its the particular issue of feelings of depersonalization and panic at the loss of self that in my experience, and the experience of some teachers I've conversed with, has a high correlation to drug use.

    We're not talking about someone who smoked pot once or twice 20 years ago, but about some significant usage that has an influence in one's life â€” perhaps an inclination to use drugs to bolster the sense of self or avoid the sense of self (which is a different way of bolstering). We should just know when we're speculating, either way. To state outright that there is a causal connection is a leap. It might make sense to think so, but just because it makes sense doesn't mean it is so. All I'm saying is that I and others see a correlation, and that perhaps one can help oneself with that information.
