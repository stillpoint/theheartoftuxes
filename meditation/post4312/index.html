    Finding space/time to meditate when never alone

    I understand the difficulty, but essentially this is saying that you can't show up for others unless the situation is perfectly calm and quiet. How will you offer compassion to others in trying circumstances; how will you bring peace to people who are not at rest; how will you show up for others if you don't practice your peaceful, stable, aware practice among them?

    The only limit to the situation is the one your own mind places on it. If you say 'can't', then you can't; and if you say 'can', then you can. Just as with the method itself, don't let thoughts or outer events get the best of you. Return to the method, and put your faith in it. Then your center will become stronger and you will be able to have peace even in a war zone.

    From your partner's side, it is important for him to give you space to do your practice. This is very important for both of you; it goes beyond your one-on-one relationship. It is very bad karma to get in the way of someone's effort to become awakened and generate peace and compassion; it would create harmful seeds in your partner's mind, the seeds of disdain for the most important things in life. You may want to talk about this.

    I suggest doing your practice without a limiting idea. Only do it. Then you might take a walk alone to experience some space and get on with the day.

    Another alternative might be to join a sitting group or temple/church/community center where you can use the meditation room. But if you keep looking outside for an ideal situation the search will never end, because such a thing doesn't exist. Either way, make your center stronger.

    Good luck!

    Edit: Oh, another good solution: get up early, before your partner is out of bed. Early morning is a beautiful and very effective time to practice. In most meditation centers I've lived at, 5am wake-up for practice is the rule; mountain temples have 3am wake-up.
