    Good herbal teas?

    What effect are you looking for? From what I hear and have learned (don't take my word for it â€” research it yourself):

    For a tea-like drink that doesn't have the unbalancing effect of caffeine (jitters, sweat, anxiety, highs and lows) you can try Rooibos, which is available in many stores now.

    For calmness of body and mind, and mild tonification of energy, find Long-An (Dragon Eyes) berries at a Chinese herb store. 2 or 3 in a mug, or a small handful boiled in a pot, make a mild and sweet drink.

    For more centering effect you can mix ground Lotus Seed (also available at Chinese herb stores) with a grain drink. They may try to substitute Fox Nut, which has a darker brown skin; it's okay, but Lotus is better for centering. It calms the heart and invites your energy to settle there. Occasionally drinking this can encourage meditation.

    For both support in meditation and support of deep resources (e.g., in cases of adrenal exhaustion), the Ayurvedic remedy of Ashwagandha Powder is recommended. You can mix it into hot drinks like coffee substitutes or smoothies.

    Regular herbal teas found in most stores do well too: Chamomile calms the digestive system and can relax you (good for cramps & tension). Mint tends to relax you by helping pent-up energies flow; it is also good for calming the digestive system, and has a fresh feel. For those who already have thin, flighty energy Mint can sometimes increase it because of its spreading quality, so then something more substantial (like Long-An) is better.

    Check these out for yourself.
