    Well, I mean, it is alright. Everything is alright.

    Sitting or standing, it's alright. Thinking or not thinking, it's alright. Living or dying, it's alright. Being massaged in a spa or being raped or tortured, it's alright.

    How wide is your 'alright'? How much does it include?

    If 'alright' is just an idea you're trying to impose, to convince yourself of something, then that is not correct practice. Trying to convince yourself something is alright will generate the accompanying mind that it's not alright. If you make an opposite, its opposite will appear. If you make "all mine", then "not yours" will come up strongly in your life. If you make "everything has to be nice", then the dark, ugly mind will want to live. Meditation is complete, not opposites thinking.

    Alright has to be boundlessly wide, wide enough to take your mind away.

    But these kinds of tortuous permutations just go to show the foolishness of trying to invent your own practice. You tried to invent your own practice, and now you're coming to strangers to solicit some opinions, and... what next? Will you sift through the various views and choose your favorite? Based on what â€” because you like it? You can understand it? You can't understand it? It has a bunch of upvotes?

    If you try to invent your own practice, your current blind spots will be built into it. You won't be able to see what you already can't see; and your not-seeing will be rehearsed and deepened and taken for 'meditation'.

    Get proper teaching from a proper source. Then you can attain 'it's alright' rather than just thinking about it.

    In practice, 'alright' means only do it. Don't believe your opinion; don't get yanked around by your like and dislike. Only do it. Any kind of internal or external situations, only do it. That is true 'alright', which is completely independent. It doesn't require anything to be a particular way.

    But in order to do that, you need to have correct motivation and correct method. So go to a proper source and learn meditation properly.
