    non-human guru

    The problem arises when the person's imagination and opinion, and thus ignorance, color the interaction. With a human guru you can be checked and guided back to clarity.

    It is possible to work with deities, plant guides, and the like; but a human teacher should initiate you into that process and oversee your relationship until ignorance no longer threatens to take you down the wrong path. Our capacity for self-delusion is immense and insidious. The very thought "I know better" has taken so many to hell.

    The important basis is knowing beyond doubt where to go for refuge. People habitually seek power, which is the control-seeking dynamic of the ego. It's not that power is wrong, or even unnecessary; it's that seeking it places one in the wrong relationship to it. It puts the cart before the horse. First the motivation must be clarified, then the container — the person's character and capacity — must be built. Already that is power, and power then arises naturally.

    Even when a practitioner works with a human teacher, it usually takes many years of dedicated practice together before their view is clarified and they know what to rely upon; and many people fall off the path before this is achieved. How much more likely will the falling-off be when the "teacher" can't bring their voice to bear on one's ignorance?

    >…still useless and discouraging to the many people here who have no access where they live to any personal instruction.

    What constitutes "no access"? Most people have no access because they decide they have no access. They are not willing to do what it takes to get access. You can join online communities, have skype meetings, travel farther afield, do more research, ask around, look harder, ask for introductions. You can attend a more distant community less frequently; you can save the money to travel half-way across the world for a meeting or retreat every couple of years. You can give up your idea of your favorite and embrace what's within reach.

    Some people are more at a disadvantage financially, geographically, socially. In that case, the greater dedication and devotion required also become a gift.

    Let's remember that our situations, our entire constellation of conditions, come from our own karma, our own actions. If we don't have the right conditions, then we have to turn around our activity to start getting them: eliminate negative behaviors, be more generous, keep the aim of awakening always close to heart. When the karmic output changes, the conditions will change.

    Some masters have had non-human teachers. Famously, Nagarjuna and some in his lineage were taught by Nagas. But they already were far advanced on the path, had extreme stability of practice, extreme clarity of mind, and already had the ability to communicate directly with these other beings. In short, they were already masters when they received further guidance. That's not the kind of situation we're talking about here.
