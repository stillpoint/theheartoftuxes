    >You read the stories of those who find their true teacher being overcome with a 'knowing' that they have found the right one. Is this typical?

    It is all dependent on your karma: what links have you made in the past, and how have you maintained them? Have you been a disciple in the past, have you honored, respected, served, appreciated your teacher? Have you practiced their teaching diligently, establishing it as a living stream in your life? Have you made prayers for your teacher's well-being; have you made aspirations and vows to return as a human, return to Buddhadharma, return to this lineage and teacher in future lives? These are the kinds of conditions that might lead you to 'rediscover' your teacher.

    Or if you have performed a great many sincere and generous acts, developed a great deal of positive karmic merit, reached out with intent toward awakening through association with enlightening beings, taken steps and efforts and pains to seek and find a teacher, then even if you don't have strong ties through previous practice together, a teacher may appear who matches the calling of your being. This is not always someone you like! It is someone who can wake you up!

    So, no, I wouldn't say that it's typical, inasmuch as special devotion to Dharma is not typical. But if the karma is there, then the result of karma ('karma-phala', or the 'fruit of karma') is there.

    Don't worry about having a special experience. Don't make stories about yourself. Don't self-aggrandize. Don't envision yourself as the hero or star of a quest, deserving some special scene to play out. Don't check your teachers, as if you're more qualified than they are to determine their usefulness in your development. Don't see your teachers as merely tools or resources there for your own benefit.

    Participate in awakening together with all beings. It is a very long-term project, over countless millions of lifetimes, over endless time: we all grow up together, we are all interdependent, so we all bring something to the table.

    It's not important that your teacher be better than all other teachers. It's not like buying a computer gaming rig or chainsaw where we 'demand the best'.

    It's merely important that your teacher be wiser than you, and capable of contributing to your awakening, and qualified and verified within their lineage to teach (so they do minimal harm to their students and to the Dharma and to the lineage).

    There are countless character types, within countless conditions, with countless mind sicknesses. Therefore the kinds of benefit are also countless; and the numbers and kinds of beings who bestow benefit are also countless. Someone else's great 'true' teacher may not be yours. Or they may be, even if you don't yet recognize it. There may be a very good, very blessèd reason you might not recognize your teacher -- perhaps they can influence you better, more subtly, more intensely, more deeply, more musically or mystically or magically or plainly or quickly or harmlessly that way.

    When you are sincere, Dharma will respond. But you have to take the steps. Don't expect it to come to you. Don't expect it to be what you expect. Show up generously without requiring something back. Then you can wake up and be a great being, supporting the well-being, integrity, and awakening of others.

    Originally, you are not a needy, grasping, entitled person. So allow teachers and teaching situations to quickly strip those negative qualities and obscurations away.
