    Anger. Loneliness. Jealousy. Desperation. Lust. I want to rise above it. they overwhelm me. meditation is the cure?

    Mindfulness, more than meditation, will help you have stability within the emotions.

    Meditation can help you see that the 'you' that you think is beset by emotions is unreal and empty, so that's indeed a cure; but it takes time, and it requires a way to keep that realization functioning throughout daily life.

    Mindfulness — moment to moment awareness — will help you see when you're taking the bait, and let go. The mind drops baited hooks in the water for you. If you buy into the illusion of self or the supposed reality of the emotions, you swallow the hook. Awareness will help you to see "Oh, there's a dangling hook. No need to swallow it."

    That means that emotions can be there without you getting taken away by them. 'Taken away' means your train of thought gets involved and you see the whole situation as inherently real and inescapable. So when you practice awareness you are more able to let go of the train of thought that makes a drama out of basic emotions.

    Some practices, like Tantra, actually turn negatives like petty emotions into the material of awakening. You may want to research Tantra and find a teacher.

    What I outline above, and what most people on these forums write about in relation to emotions is a bit high-level practice, and already requires some stability of mind, concentrative power, and consistency of practice to be successful. But there are other strategies that can support your quest for harmony with the emotions.

    • Make your environment supportive. Tidy, clean, spacious, beautiful living space will settle the mind and help you remember your more divine intentions.

    • Balanced eating (regular schedule, not too much or too little, qood quality and variety), balanced sleeping (regular schedule, not too much or too little), balanced exercise, body care (cleanliness and dressing well), balanced work and rest, and balanced intake of words and images will go a long way to balancing and calming your emotions.

    • Change the channel. When you find yourself disturbed or upset, just immediately do something different.

    • Change your body posture — sit or stand upright, with noble carriage; get up and walk; change from tense and defensive posture to relaxed and self-possessed.

    • Change your view — let your eyes see something different. Turn around. Open the drapes; look out the window. Go for a walk. See a tree; see the sky; see the earth. See many possibilities, many potential paths.

    • Change your activity — quickly do something different. Ride a bike. Call a friend. Do a handstand. Make crazy faces. Anything to shift from what seems like a one-track chain of events.

    • Physical practice is very useful to discharge and ground emotional energy. Get out of the head and into the body. Bowing, running, swimming, cycling, free dance, Taiji, Yoga, and other forms of physical practice are helpful.

    • Express it — create something fueled by the emotion. This is called turning suffering into art; you express the thing and then you move on. (Don't let expression descend into indulgence and self-obsession.) Writing, visual arts, music, and acting are all possible ways to channel difficulty into something meaningful and healing; but the king of all is free dance. There is hardly any other activity where you can immediately and eloquently integrate feeling, thinking, and physically doing. Free dance allows you to express both huge and subtle energies, explore your relationship with them, see them from different perspectives, have them reflected by others, and drop them in a moment. The very act of dancing is the practice of moving on to the next thing; it is quite healing, quite optimistic. Five Rhythms, Movement Medicine, Soul Motion, Open Floor Movement Practice, and Authentic Movement are especially helpful modalities for accepting, integrating, and gaining mastery over emotions.
