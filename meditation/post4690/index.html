    A question for those who practice concentration meditation

    I don't want a particular answer. I am here to help you find the way. There's a question from within you that isn't spoken in the words you write, but is stronger than any of them.

    You are already a millionaire. You have super intelligence already. But you don't know how to use your millions. Like many lottery winners, even if you get more and more maybe you will squander it and be worse off than ever. You may die next week, or tomorrow; what can you do?

    It is still not clear whether you have actually learned a method or not.

    If you use the sound of breath, why? The method and the reason for it should be clear. That means the whole purpose of pursuing anything should be clear. If it is not clear, the correct response isn't to sweep the whole issue under the carpet and disregard it, hoping that good times will come. One has to have the courage to face the uncertainty and not-knowing, and to look into the issue. Why do anything in life?

    Working with sound, if you keep yourself separate from the sound you prolong the illusion of duality. You and sound are not two separate things. The idea of separation hovers over your true experience and clouds it; but if you actually listen, you can find the truth of what I say. The sound is your mind; your mind is the sound. That place where sound appears is you.

    But you — your mind — does not come and go. Sound comes and goes, but the place where it appears does not come and go. So sound is not the primary point. It is just an object, just a form of appearance. All forms come and go, but your mind does not come and go. This is primary point: the place where all things appear. And this is where your correct meditation resides, not in the object of attention.

    In other words, you can pay attention to the object; but the meditation doesn't key on the object, it keys on the attention. When the object fades and disappears, the attention is still there. So rest in that.

    There is restlessness and anxiety because you are used to identifying as a separate self. So having a self on one side doing the meditation, and an object on the other side being focused upon, helps maintain that comfortable illusion of separation. But because it is separate and because it is illusion, it is not at all comfortable. This is where suffering appears in your life.

    You can help yourself become very concentrated and clear by letting go of the object when it disappears. Rest in the open attention. You are the space where it happens, so rest as that space, and let it happen. Breath sound comes: let it come. Breath sound goes: let it go. No breath sound: let it be.

    It is open, ungraspable. That is correct meditation. Grasping and holding is not correct meditation. Grasping and holding are what make you unclear and unconcentrated. Distraction appears because the mind grasps at an object... then another... then another. Ungrasping is clarity. All things can appear as they are, within the meditative space of mind.

    So become friends with emptiness. Train yourself in becoming comfortable with that which is so far uncomfortable or squirmy: the openness of having no particular object.

    Where you put your focus is Unknowing.
