    How do I meditate when sick?

    "G'uuuhhhhh.... sniff one.... sniff, cough... two... snort three... wheeze four...."

    If it were a major flu with fever I'd say lie down and rest, and make sure that you're getting proper treatment if you have a fever. With a minor flu, you can continue to sit.

    >At this rate it will be weeks before I can meditate successfully.

    What is success?

    It is true, you can't concentrate for more than a minute or two. You can't concentrate for more than a second or two. Concentration doesn't happen in any other time. You can only concentrate for a moment. If it is not in the moment, it is not concentration.

    So the whole process is that conditions — karmic results — take you away, and then you return. The only difference between the last couple of days and any other time is just a matter of scale. You still get taken away by conditions in other sittings, and it's still up to you to let it go and return; it just has been happening at a different pace recently. So, no problem. You still have to return.

    If you give up and drop the practice, the terrorists win! That is, if you give up, that means that conditions have controlled your will to return. You have allowed conditions to temporarily stop you from returning. That is your choice, and your karma: you make this giving-up karma, so later you will have the giving-up result.

    But if you don't give up and only try, conditions start to lose power over your mind. Good situation and bad situation no longer sway you; you only keep returning. That is already very, very good practice.

    Eventually your returning becomes so strong that you don't leave. Sickness, pain, difficulty — nothing will take you away from your center. Then your mind's light can shine everywhere, because it won't be controlled by circumstances.

    But first, you just return. Never mind about 'lately'. Never mind about the duration of the sitting. Only return just now, just one time. Practice only ever happens in the present moment, never over a period of time. We only talk about time so we can conveniently communicate about practice and so on; but real life is only ever momentary, and that is what you return to.

    Your flu can be a great teacher. It can show you how much ease you can bring to your practice. If you hold and resist and push you will be so very tired; but behind the symptoms there is still an awareness that is effortless. The symptoms appear within that awareness. So it's possible to let it all be; and when you do drop the judgment and resistance you can find a very comfortable place, not only within the flu symptoms, but within life and death. Just the plain awareness....

    In practice centers the level of motivation and commitment tends to be much higher than in general society, so most people sit through all kinds of illnesses. Not only flu, but cancer, heart disease, AIDS, and so on. The strictness varies according to the center. Some require pretty strict adherence to schedule; others allow a certain amount of time off each week, sort of like vacation days that you can use as you see fit.

    But that's a practice situation where there's a lot more support: the teacher, the teaching, the setting, the schedule, and the other participants all function as supports for you when your own motivation or wisdom wander. When you're on your own, you have to rely on your own best judgment and inner truth.

    Best judgment means finding the middle way between extremes. Do more than your lazy patterns like, but don't push yourself out of pride or guilt. Know when taking it easy helps your practice and when it hurts your practice.

    Inner truth means you have a sincere heart that will tell you in complete honesty what you can and can't abide. The inner voice may say now is the time to give yourself completely over to practice, and any thought to the contrary can go to hell. Or the inner voice may say that there is no benefit to harming yourself, and that you can also be generous to yourself as you would to anyone else. Basically, you check in to see what you can live with — what course of action will bring your mind peace.

    Just don't attach to some image of what meditation is supposed to look or feel like. That's all crap. Only do it; that's what it's supposed to be like. Just do it to the best of your ability, following the situation. When it's hot, do sweating meditation; when it's cold, do shivering meditation; and when there's a flu, do sore, delirious, sniffling meditation.

    You actually aren't sick. 'Sick' is an idea, a label attached to a group of experiences. The experiences themselves don't say "I am sick". So there is just sniffling, aching, nodding off, dullness, and so on; and all of this is just truth.
