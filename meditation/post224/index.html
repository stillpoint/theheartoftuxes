    Wrong meditation can be problematic, or even in rare cases dangerous. And even good meditation, when applied with the wrong attitude or in the wrong situation, can also be a risk.

    For instance, I've seen people receiving the same instruction from the same master and practicing it in the same course of training; but (while others deepened their stability) because of the manipulative relationship they had with their own mind, their own result was eruption of internal pressure, and psychotic break. Wanting and making don't belong in proper meditation.

    Just from the questions in your post and the information that you do and don't provide, it seems clear that you are not equipped to lead your own practice. Rather than think about more, more, more, I'd encourage you to make your foundation very clear and strong. That means:

    • learning properly, from a verified teacher

    • adopting only one, proven, traditional method

    • learning the method completely

    • following the method meticulously

    Do this first; get the basis correct first. Later, you can think about 'more, more, more'.

    The base of the basis, the root of the root, so to speak, is your correct direction in life. Why do meditation? This should be clear. What does meditation have to do with your life's purpose? Correct motivation comes first. This is the stable foundation for everything that is built later, up to the highest level meditation. If you only do 'more' without understanding your correct relationship to practice, in essence you will be practicing and increasing incorrect relationship.
