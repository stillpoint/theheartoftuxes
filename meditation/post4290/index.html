    Can't go deep into inner body

    >But if you're encountering this emotional pain right now, you're headed in the right direction. Things will start to free up as you are ready to let them go.

    There is some truth to this in certain circumstances, but it is too broad and optimistic a statement. If your practice is correct — if you have the proper relationship to your mind and the things that arise in it — then it would be fine to encourage you to keep going.

    But it is not clear that this is the case. Pursuing an ideal state, trying to make depth appear, and seeking invulnerability all involve a similar mistake, and this mistake may be creeping into your practice. Your emotional pain may be a sign that you need to back off or stop or get more clarity on your approach. It's not that sadness or heaviness are at all bad, but we readers in this thread have no insight into the origins of your emotions, and we should not be cavalier in our recommendations about them.

    Dreamrabbit's offhand assertion that you're headed in the right direction may be understandably encouraging, but it is nonetheless careless and irresponsible.

    ~

    Be aware that energy follows attention. (We say that "mind makes everything".) If you bring your attention to your chest, your emotional center, then it will naturally prompt emotions to arise. More force in fixing the attention there will tend to bring up more forceful experiences. Similarly, bringing attention up into the head will tend to generate more thinking; and bringing attention down to the lower belly or feet will tend to settle the emotions and thinking.

    Correct meditation isn't making something happen, it's opening the hands (figuratively) to the moment. Just as lightning finds its path of least resistance, connecting sky to ground, your proper groundedness doesn't come from making your thinking or feeling or energy do something in particular; your proper groundedness comes from letting everything be what it is. Then your energy can find its own path to balance. Emotion can be just what it is, without resistance, just like the weather can be what it is. In some climates it rains a lot. Sometimes you will cry deeply and for a long time.
