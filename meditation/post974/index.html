    Shpongle for meditation?

    The way your post comes off is a bit reactionary, though I don't believe that's your intent.

    You ask what the problem is, but I already wrote that there is no problem. It's fine. >Relaxing or drifting along with a song is okay, but it is not meditation.

    You can use words however you want, but there is a particular, common, and meaningful definition of meditation that doesn't jibe with using a piece of music to occupy the mind and lead it somewhere.

    >I believe concentration is the exact opposite of meditation

    What is the opposite of concentration? This is vague and conceptual. In casual terms, if concentration is focus then its opposite is utter distraction and lack of focus. Any presence and connection with reality gleaned with concentration would by definition be completely absent.

    I don't think that's what you mean to say. I think perhaps you don't like concentration.

    >music puts me into a trance

    Trance is a concentrative state. In the experience you're talking about, music or sound or movies or light shows or trains of thought occupy the monkey-mind and thus make it more subdued and comfortable. This moves in the direction of concentration, and away from broad distraction, but it is still relying on a certain source of distraction rather than one's own ability to disengage and focus. This is not training one's ability, but using an external source to take away one's mind.

    That's fine, no problem; you can do it if you want; and it's less harmful than a thousand other things you could get into. But it is essentially the same as using drugs or watching TV in order to take the mind away. So one should be honest about it and simply say "I choose to relax this way".

    Folks use the word meditation to describe these actions because they want to justify their laziness. They hope that by using spiritual terminology some of it will rub off on their experience, when really they're doing not much more than having a nice nap. I would not be a good friend if I didn't point that out, as much as these folks might resist and become defensive when their pursuit of comfort is questioned.

    In any case, neither concentration nor trance is the same as clarity. Clear mind doesn't attach to any object, whether a song or an object of concentration practice. Clear mind is bright and awake, and functions perfectly in any situation, whereas in trance (whether we're talking about true trance or about simply falling into a sleepy, dreamy state) a part of one's experience disappears, and one can't function well even in plain everyday tasks.
