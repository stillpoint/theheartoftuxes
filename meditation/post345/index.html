    It has nothing to do with the girl, but stems from attachment and rejection issues in your childhood, especially having to do with your mother, and the overall sense of incompletion and insufficiency that almost all of us share. You have not yet developed a full and stable sense of self-worth — the knowledge that you are already worthwhile and complete, not having to rely on any person or situation or event to fulfill you.

    Your relationship with the girl was mostly self-created, imagined. This is evident because she was not into the relationship and yet you made much of the pursuit; so your 'connection' was in your mind. Again, this is almost certainly because something about her appearance or quality reverberated with your past attachment to your mother and the qualities you yearn for in yourself.

    This kind of thing becomes all the more charged, all the more urgent with men who have been taught by their fathers and male peers to take on narrow, tight, emotionless versions of masculinity. Willingness to feel is absolutely essential to masculine expression; a true man's heart is full and clear. But often the role models we are offered are one-dimensional and closed; and the more we distance ourselves from our own femininity, the more urgently we project its gloriousness and magic and inscrutability on the women in our lives. Because we don't nurture these qualities in ourselves, they appear frustratingly alluring and out of reach in others. We chase outside of ourselves for something that is rightly part of our complete being.

    So in the long term, relief is found when you come to personal fulfillment and develop a strong self-worth based on your own worthy values and qualities (not on acquiring possessions, recognition, and relationships). In Taoist terms, this is referred to as 'nurturing both the Yin and Yang in oneself', and is even couched in terms of 'having a spiritual baby' — fulfilling masculine and feminine, active and passive, doing and feeling; and letting those qualities and energies circulate and integrate to produce a wonderful, shining fulfillment.

    In the short term, you can make a practice of letting go and returning to your own integrity. Whether you see it as thinking that clings to another, generating uncomfortable feelings, or whether you see it as actual 'sticky' cords of energy that trap you (and the other person) in relationship hindrance and reactivity, you can practice letting go of the harmful clinging and agitation each time it arises.

    Every time your mind goes toward the object of frustration, picture yourself releasing lines of energy or light. You may even feel the agitation in your belly, and the relief that comes with intentionally releasing those lines that clamp into your insides. Know that this is a compassionate act: you are also freeing the other being, the girl, to experience her own integrity. You are letting her be free of your clinging and agitation. If you actually care about her as a person rather than a possession, you will allow her to return to her own integrity, her own chosen movement in life.

    Every time you let go of these lines of energy you heal yourself and your discomfort. You also contribute to your longer-term integrity; because each time you let go you acknowledge that you are already essentially okay. You are already essentially complete. This moment is enough; you are enough; you don't need anything or anyone. So the more you practice this, the more you activate your self-worth.

    Then you can focus on things that actually develop a healthy self-worth — generosity rather than grasping; creative acts rather than hoping and wanting; care for others rather than trying to fill perceived 'holes' in yourself. Go out into the world and do noble, worthy acts, and become happy in your own right, not depending on any outside person or situation.

    Only trying to get rid of negative states is difficult. Something has to be there to fill the role that the negativity has been playing. But if you focus on doing beneficial acts, helping others, pursuing your healthy interests, expressing your sincere creative impulses, then you may be so full of vibrancy and occupied with positivity that the negative has no more room in your life.

    By the way, your post illustrates the most basic truth taught by the Buddha: all clinging creates suffering.
