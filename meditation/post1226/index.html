    There's no one way. People will find different information or encounter different opportunities, and according to their different needs they will stumble along in different ways. Even if there were a good plan for it, who follows plans?

    The first thing is start from the beginning, start at the center of the issue. Why meditate? There are a thousand of methods of meditation, and infinite other things you could be doing. Why do one thing instead of another?

    That means clarify the aim of your life -- what you are doing with your time and effort before you die. When this point is clear, choices come clear.

    If your aim is to understand your true self, wake up from the dream of life, and help all other beings wake up and gain unending contentment, then the plan is simple: find a capable, awakened teacher within a tradition, and learn from them and practice what they teach. They have gained what you seek; they know how to get there; and they know how to convey that to others.

    If you are looking to start on your own, the main thing is to be present in reality. There are many methods and many styles; but the main thing is that they help you be present in the real world and be okay with it. When you are okay with things-as-they-are on an ongoing basis, you no longer have to be selfish, manipulative, or self-destructive. It's good for you, and it's good for the world.

    So choose a traditional method -- one that has been passed down by very clear and compassionate beings, and that has been proven over time and through the attainment of many millions of other practitioners, and that has a curated system of teaching in sort of it. Traditional courses of teaching tend to be more complete, and their teachers more accountable.

    Choose one, learn it well, and then find out for yourself, by gaining results, whether you can put more trust in it. Even if someone tells you "This is the right way" you won't know until you verify it through your own activity. So activity is pivotal. After you know a little, more knowledge isn't necessary -- acting on the knowledge is necessary.

    What do you like? Choose one clearly, and use it to help you be present with reality.

    As to the various different forms: many firms appear because there ate many types of mind and many types of mind sickness. So different medicines appear.

    But also, sometimes people get ideas and invent medicines without knowing how to use them. That also happens.

    Body posture at this stage is just to help you be present. Be upright and settled: body and mind alert and relaxed. Don't give up either alertness or mental relaxation with the posture you take. So sitting on a chair is fine. Sit near the edge, with feet firmly planted. Let the body stack up on itself naturally so there's not a lot of effort needed to keep it upright.

    Keep the eyes slightly open. Take a few deeper, conscious breaths to invite body and mind into the present moment. Then follow your chosen method.

    Consistency is more important than heroics. Do a few minutes at a time to start, and once the consistency and enjoyment have been established you can start exercising your ability to extend the presence for longer periods. If you push to do longer durations too soon your body and mind will fight you, you will get discouraged, and you may give up altogether and never get the benefit of ongoing meditation.

    People want to do it themselves because they are afraid of newness, afraid of being seen, afraid of having to change. They don't yet realize that newness is where the fun is. They haven't yet seen examples of experienced meditator and teachers living in a very fresh, happy, engaged way. But the isolation of people who try to do it all themselves limits them severely: they never get a view other than their own. So the ignorance and habits they start with tend to continue. Faults never get removed; misconceptions grow; preference rather than awakening leads the show.

    So again, finding a teacher and practice situation is the best thing you can do in terms of learning 'how to do practice'.
