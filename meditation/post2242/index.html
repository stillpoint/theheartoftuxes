    The crux of the matter is the motivation behind the practice. You say that you do it to put yourself to sleep. That is a different direction than doing it to continue the practice of clarity even when you are going to sleep.

    Usually sleep and dream practices are assigned only after the practitioner has stability in practice during waking hours. It is important to know how to use your mind correctly, and to have the right openhanded, compassionate, devoted motivation, and for these aspects to get deeply seated in your being so they become a natural expression of your life. If you are still wavering in method or motivation, bringing it into sleep will likely confuse the issue, as your unconscious tendencies will take over. But if you are clear and fairly stable in your practice, continuing it even throughout your sleep (or working in that direction by practicing clarity and surrender when you go to sleep and wake up) can be very, very beneficial.

    Basically, it's important to do things in the right order. This includes getting proper training from a living guide, if you are serious about progress and are looking to face the parts of your mind where more pitfalls appear. Get the proper training... then apply it devotedly... then you will naturally progress to practices that are suited to you (including possibly sleep meditation).

    If it's just a matter of finding a technique to help you sleep (rather than looking to maintain a mind practice continuously), I agree with minimalisto that you could use a separate method so you don't develop the habit of letting yourself sleep when you should be waking up (in meditation).
