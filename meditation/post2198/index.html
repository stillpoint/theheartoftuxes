    I'm not sure a 'practice' is the best thing. Often it is best addressed either by getting out into the world and actively expressing yourself and relating to others, or through psychotherapeutic process (or both).

    We often hope that we can take care of things in isolation, when in fact the isolation contributes to our stuck patterns. When you are active, your self is being expressed; and when you are active in something that you find worthwhile, your expressed self becomes associated with esteem, with value. So if the self-denegrating habit is not too deep-set, just trying new things, putting yourself into new situations, following your interests, and interacting with other humans in edifying ways will develop the self-esteem. Basically, you get to see yourself taking on challenges and being okay, or even enriched; and you get to see yourself performing valuable actions, from helping others feel better by offering compliments and encouraging speech, to working on projects that establish safe drinking water or education, to building a business or spiritual community together.

    But we may be carrying wounds from our upbringing or other life events that derail many of our life efforts. In that case it is usually necessary, or at least helpful, to revisit those wounds in a completely new context — a context of awareness and compassion. A good therapist provides this context, whereby you can experience the same trauma in a completely different way, and thus write a new life story.

    Often, self-esteem is not best addressed by fixating on the self. It seems ironic, but it makes sense; because your true, lively, confident expression comes from your center and moves outward. It doesn't circle back and look at you, which is what you do when you brood and mull over "my problems". So expression tends to be key. It starts at your center and radiates out into the world.

    I suppose that one 'practice' you could try is free writing (as taught by Natalie Goldberg in Writing Down The Bones or Julie Cameron in The Artist's Way). For that matter, you could try free dance or other forms where you invite yourself to express yourself spontaneously, trusting whatever arises with bold, all-embracing heart. The dark, the light, the glorious, the petty — even (or especially) the energies and expressions you don't think of as 'you'. Own it all!

    But venture out; don't make the mistake of thinking it will happen in a cloistered environment controlled by you, controlled by the same mind that is making and experiencing the problematic pattern.

    Edit: By the way, I wanted to say that you already display insight and awareness. You know that you have a pattern of avoidance. So I wanted to congratulate you on this awareness. You already have a sense of your next step; so take it. You can decide not to doubt yourself. Then, if the next step turns out to lead to discomfort, wonderful! That's real life! And if the next step turns out to be a mistake, wonderful! Then you know something new. And you can alter your course, based on the new knowledge. You can take the next step.

    Soon comfort and discomfort, success and mistake won't be the big deal. Soon your next step will keep moving into next step and next step, and that never-ending movement will already be self-confidence, regardless of the story that goes on in the head.
