    My eyes force open when meditating

    >I really want to try to know myself better, become great at quieting my mind, and attempt things like OBEs but I feel this is preventing me from something.

    Those are three completely different aims.

    To know yourself is wonderful — for you and for others. It means you can overcome the habits of mind and patterns of behavior that hurt you and those around you.

    Quieting the mind is necessary to a point, because attachment to the noise in your mind is what prevents you from seeing what is really there. Attachment to your thinking prevents you from seeing your true self. But "becoming great" at quieting your mind is an ego trip. The goal makes more noise in your mind, more thinking, more pattern to attach to.

    Quiet is already the nature of your true self, so if you find your true self you will find true quiet. No need to make the self-image of an expert or magician or someone more special than others.

    Out of Body Experiences are not necessary. They are possible once you have attained your true self, but if you don't understand who you really are, Out of Body Experiences are bullshit — they're a waste of time, confusing you rather than helping you.

    What you need is an In This Body Experience. Have you had one? We live all our lives supposedly in our bodies, but we don't really inhabit them. Instead of experiencing our walking, eating, crying, washing, working, laughing, we live up in our heads divorced from real life. Our emotions say one thing, our thoughts fly off in other directions, and our action becomes something else altogether. Then our relationships, families, and world become all disconnected. No integrity. The life of fantasy, vegging out, and unconscious reaction is what we habitually invest in. This distance from reality causes us to value things that are not valuable (like, say OBEs) — we give them imaginary value through our imagining.

    So you need In the Body Experiences: grounding in the simple moment, coming to terms with it. Learning to live with yourself without seeking anything outside. This is correct meditation: put it all down, just be with yourself without trying to make anything or escape anything.

    If you put it all down well, soon your true self will appear. You stop making something with your mind, then the whole scene settles enough for you to see clearly what was already there.

    When dust and dirt blow around, you can't see well; maybe you even have to close your eyes. So it doesn't help to run around trying to make something happen; you only kick up more dust and dirt. You have to stop and wait. Soon the scene behind the dust will appear, right in front of you.

    Likewise, you can't make realization appear. You have to stop, wait, let go of making. Then your mind will calm enough for you to see what is really there.

    Never mind about lights, images, visions, other dimensions, traveling without your body, and so on. You already travel without your body when you are distracted — you are doing one thing in your mind and another thing in your body. So please come back to your real life, do correct meditation, and soon understand your true self.
