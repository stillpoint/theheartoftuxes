    Where did you hear that? Those are your own words, not mine. You made it a judgment about what has already passed; I'm writing about what one can yet do.

    You can twist it however you want in your self-obsessed enterprise; I'm just noting the invitation that each season of life offers, our ideal harmony with our time of life. There's the springtime of life, when gentle nurturing naturally supports growth; the summer, when going forth is called for; the autumn, when bearing fruit and consolidating befit the situation; and the winter, when one ideally lets go and opens one's hands and heart to stillness and death (having harmonized well with the previous seasons).

    This is the DNA of life, the pattern of its cycles of aging; but of course it often doesn't go that way. As someone who missed out on ideal nurturing as a child, and someone who failed to rally themselves well and learn all the lessons of young-adulthood, I can commiserate about feeling lost; and I can vouch for the fact that nevertheless the nurturing still has to happen, and the lessons still have to be learned, whether in their proper time or not.

    Lacking a sane society — and it's not sane — and absent the community and elders that should be calling us to ourselves, we are left to wend our way, fairly alone but not completely abandoned. But in the final analysis, and after all complaints have been lodged, the task still faces you. How do you choose to meet this living moment?

    If you only want to use this forum to whine and gripe and proclaim powerlessness, I guess that's your prerogative; but it would seem that good advice and warm support could be plied to a better purpose: to set to the task of living well.
