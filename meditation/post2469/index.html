    Deliberately introducing noise to the environment during meditation

    My personal take on it: don't make a special idea about anything.

    Every situation is already unsuitable, unsatisfactory. We know this as an aspect of life, of Dukkha. So you will always have some challenge to deal with.

    Yes, it's good to enter new practice situations that challenge you. Not only in terms of sound, but in more human terms as well: sitting with those you fear or dislike, finding common ground with those who annoy you, helping those you'd rather avoid or who you think are beyond help, and so on.

    Those challenges really bring focus to how insular and controlling our self-centered attitude is.

    On the other hand, ideal practice situations -- as ideal as you can manage -- help you to go deeper into more subtle levels of meditation, and provide the 'safe space' in which the intrepid meditator approaches and moves through fearsome or otherwise challenging inner territory.

    In a way, if you don't feel challenged it may mean you could be putting more effort into your meditation. It's not just a feel-good process; there are profoundly deep layers of neurosis and self-cherishing to be penetrated, and mountains of karma to be eroded.

    But that's not to say that outer challenge is not also necessary. Traditionally, Mahayana and Zen monks would travel to other temples and teachers, in part for the challenge of adapting to new circumstances.

    There is a risk, though, of becoming too manipulative of your practice. Keep it simple. It's not about tweaking the outer form to speed your progress. If you find yourself thinking about outer form, it will very often mean that you are avoiding simple-mindedness; that is, you are attaching to objects in avoidance of the simplicity and emptiness of meditation.

    The best way of getting the right challenge in practice is, most often, to practice with others. Find a teacher, find a group, and commit to practice with them. This removes or at least lessens the self-centered tweaking that many meditators fall prey to.
