    A new experience

    First of all, why does it matter what label we'd give to your experience? It arose, it passed, and now it's over. It followed the life cycle of all appearance. Nothing special.

    Don't be so self-fascinated. Ego likes to take the spiritual process and make it into its own playground. Instead of moving on to the next thing with open awareness, something grabs your mind, and your mind sticks to it. That's ego. You hope either to have concern dispelled or pride confirmed. Both come from thinking about the self. This is not correct practice.

    All meditators will have some kind of new experience at some point. If there is more pressure in the psyche, from attachment or suppression, or if the method is forced or otherwise performed wrong, the experiences tend to be more dramatic and unusual. But in any case, all kinds of things will bubble up from the unconscious or otherwise gather in the mind because certain boundaries are opening up. It is not at all significant except to the ego, which seeks to label and control the agenda.

    'Deep focus' means what? If it feels like something in particular, that's not focus, that's accompanying feeling. Focus means you are with the object of concentration. If you merge with the object, there's no sense of 'you' or of the quality of the object or quality of the experience. Sometimes concentration engenders comfortable feelings. Sometimes comfortable feelings appear from other sources, not necessarily because of the meditation.

    Tingling, vibrating, bubbling, expansion and contraction, emptiness, flow, wafting, shifting, pulsing, and many other types of sensations may appear. In some cases it's because they are there already, and when the mind quiets down, one starts to perceive them. They are no longer covered up by the busy thinking mind. In other cases, they appear as projections of mind and its inner movements, not because they apply to physical or energetic phenomena, per se.

    In either case, there's nothing special to do about it other than do the meditation rather than get sidetracked and self-fascinated.

    Energetically, some of the things you mention happen to be signs related to the 'Liver' process according to Chinese Medicine, which governs the free flow of emotion, blood, and energy. When Liver energy is pent up or deficient it can manifest as tics and spasms. Likewise, when Liver energy is released, similar signs can appear. Twitching, tingling, and joyful bursts of laughter are all possible signs of a release of Liver energy. It can be due to relaxation (as in meditation), or because of something you ate or some activity you engaged in.

    If you attach to these kinds of manifestations as signs of attainment you'll be feeding your ego and making your practice result-based. That is not correct practice. Correct practice means you face the moment openhandedly. That is all. No loss or gain. No past or future. No stories about yourself. Only do it, moment to moment.
