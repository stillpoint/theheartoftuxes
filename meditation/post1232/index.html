    Pranayama should be learned from a pranayama teacher, not from random tips given by random anonymous sources. "Does anyone ever...?" is the scourge of the contemporary meditation movement. As if these profound technologies could be learned through chance anecdotes or popular opinion!

    Why don't you ask your teacher for further guidance, or for a recommended source for pranayama practice?

    The breath tends to move more fully through one side of the nose at a time, and it shifts between nostrils throughout the day. This reflects a shift between the active internal channels. As with all things in nature, there is a cycle to the internal energy (prana) of the body.

    In addition to this side-to-side cycle, many (if not most) of us are somewhat out of balance in the way our breath and prana circulate. Certain pranayama exercises like alternate nostril breathing help balance the flow, and these are among the first and most frequently practiced.

    Changing the side you sleep on (and thus which nostril is the upper one) can also affect which side is more active. Although balance between channels is encouraged, flow through the left side is said to support the more sublime qualities of calmness and insight, so lying on the right side is usually the recommended posture for sleep and dream yogas.

    Pranayama is a support within the context of a traditional wholistic training regimen. It is an obstacle for those who attach to it or use it inappropriately.

    If you are learning a proper traditional meditation method, breathing will be included. It is not necessary to look elsewhere for secret techniques. Ultimately, everything follows mind, and if you use your mind in a balanced, non-grasping, non-manipulative way your breathing and energy flow will become correct and balanced.
