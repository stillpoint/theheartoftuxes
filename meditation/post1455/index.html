    Meditating why lying down,impossible?

    Body posture and mind posture are intimately connected; one deeply influences the other. While it's not impossible, it is extremely unlikely for any but the most motivated or advanced meditators to be able to stay alert while lying down.

    Some "meditations" use the lying-down posture for greater access to relaxation and sleep or dream states. This is not clear meditation, but can still be beneficial. If your aim is to wake up and be complete, upright posture is much, much better for meditation.

    It is extremely fortunate that you are aware that you space out while lying down. Many people think that they are meditating when in fact they are just drifting in semi-conscious states, and so not only do they waste the opportunity for true meditation, their opinions develop on that basis and they become confused about the correct way. An insidious attachment to sleep or escape often makes this problem one of the more intractable among meditators.

    My question for you is why you want to meditate lying down. Finding your self-support and upright nature is an important part of clear practice. You have a mind that is noble and true and not dependent on anything. Uncovering that mind requires letting go of petty, indulgent, and attached patterns of body use and thought. There is a profundity to sitting or standing upright and finding your relaxation within that form.

    If you have a particular aspiration connected to lying down (rather than mere laziness), you can look into dedicated sleep and dream practices. These are not substitutes for waking, sitting, and active practices, but they can highlight the correct use of the lying-down posture. One of the best books on this that I've come across is The Tibetan Yogas Of Dream And Sleep by Tenzin Wangyal Rinpoche. Any sleep or dream practice should be guided by a teacher: beyond the fact that most people are not equipped to guide their own practice past the most basic levels, dabbling in unconscious material without very strict form and very clear guidance is a recipe for mental illness and harm.
