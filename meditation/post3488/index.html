    TheHeartofTuxes

    It's good to consciously recall a shared aim, rather than just going through the motions. It brings meaning to how you go through the day, not only whether you win or lose in your efforts: "Let's bring forth the intent to be present, compassionate, and effective, for the benefit and happiness (or fulfillment) of those we care for, and their families, and for each other, and for ourselves."

    You can tweak it to be something that everyone can get behind: "May we appreciate the opportunity to help with our presence, our skill, and our effort." There are many possibilities; but the main point is that some guiding principle, some direction be offered. Why be present? If direction is not offered, some people will not make the connection, and morning meditation will just be an awkward few moments of self-consciousness.

    Intentionally practicing re-setting at the beginning of the day will eventually make re-setting possible at times when stress and reactivity are coming up. The emphasis can be on simple presence. You can just offer the invitation to be present and aware for a couple of minutes; and people can figure out on their own how to do that. You can offer awareness of breath as an option "if you need something to bring you back to presence". It doesn't have to be a big deal.

    We all share an inner aspiration to be alive and happy, and to help others be alive and happy. I am convinced that if we find a way to voice that shared aspiration it will be well accepted. Maybe it can be couched as a way of establishing mental health: "Let's take a few moments of balanced, stress-free attention, so we can hopefully share it with others throughout the day."

    Although structure is very helpful for beginning meditators, if they haven't bought in to the idea of meditation the structure will become an imposition. That is why I suspect it will go over better if you offer an intent with clear direction and then leave the minute or two of presence more open, unless and until others ask for more structure.
