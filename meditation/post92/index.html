    So really the discussion is not so much about meditation; it's about how to turn your mind and behavior toward positive activity.

    Proper, wholistic traditional training is important. It addresses life direction, lifestyle, and all levels of self. Most people who start meditating from online sources, books, or apps mistakenly believe that meditation is purely a mental practice, and that it is sufficient for developing the character and attaining happiness; but that's a narrow, mistaken view. Physical practice, lifestyle balance, correct relationship, ethical activity, and ongoing learning are all important as well — not only meditation in isolation.

    If you connect with an attained teacher in a traditional path you can have living example, living encouragement, living teaching, living correction, and living meeting of minds. You won't have to do it alone; you won't have to fill the emptiness on your own.

    It's extremely difficult to simply quit negative activity. You understand this in your experience: what fills the space formerly filled by the negative activity? So it's very useful to have positive view, positive intent, and positive activity. And not only that, but to access them repeatedly. This means establishing supportive structures in your life: routines, scheduled activities, repeated conscious intentions, commitments, supportive relationships, supportive lifestyle habits, and so on. These stuctures — these rituals and relationships — are there to help you find the way when you lose the way. They are there to help you return to positivity when you would otherwise forget or be unable to do it on your own. Studying with a teacher and joining a practice community or group fulfills many of these helpful structures.

    You don't have to try Buddhism, but I'll just share a teaching from Buddhism that might offer some helpful perspective for you. This is the concept of Three Poisons: craving, aversion, and ignorance. These are the three poisonous activities of our minds. When we encounter something we experience as pleasant, we tend to crave it. When we encounter something we experience as unpleasant, we tend to try avoiding or destroying it. And when we encounter neutral things, we tend to ignore them. All of these reactions take us away from harmonious, conscious relationship with reality. They pull our minds and hearts here and there, and result in suffering.

    Any action based on craving, aversion, or ignorance results in suffering.

    So you noted your strong craving activity — activity based on attaching to pleasure. It is this activity that results in your current stuckness and suffering. You can't do anything about your past action; but you can be aware of the connection and refrain from making more reactive and poisonous action.

    If you keep your mind from rushing toward or away from anything, that is already positive activity. It is 'good karma', the cause for happiness now and in the future. So regardless of whether your experience is pleasant, unpleasant, or neutral, keep your mind simple and unattached. Don't try to grasp anything; don't try to avoid anything; and don't ignore what's happening. Don't make, want, check, or hold. Only follow the moment clearly. Then your good action will get more and more good results, and your way will unfold happily.

    It's not about pushing through in the usual sense. Pushing through usually means you have an idea of some ideal future state or destination. But that's about something else 'over there', at some other unreal time. Rather than pushing through, you just arrive in clarity in this moment. This moment is your job.

    All this and much more you can learn and get support for with a good teacher and group.
