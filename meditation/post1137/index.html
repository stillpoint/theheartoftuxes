    TheHeartofTuxes

    Life has inherent difficulty at times; but problem comes from thinking. If you don't make and attach to negative thinking, this and every situation is workable, and will soon change to your benefit.

    In tumultuous circumstances, limit your attention: take care of what's right in front of your face, attend to the small detail of life just now. You can't control the great movement of the world; but you can align with it better. So in a sense, dying is correct; just not the dying of the body: you have to let the holding, grasping, controlling motives die.

    To improve your situation, first remove the most serious negative mind state. You are an intelligent and sensitive person; you are capable of knowing when you're in a negative mind state. Removing it is the first order of business. This is like a medical triage that attends to acute fever before getting at other issues, because the fever is most urgently threatening.

    So change the foremost negative mind state that's active just now. Apply cures that you've become familiar with, whether they're practice methods, or self-care, or something that you know through your experience will help bring you to positivity.

    If you can't apply a remedy, change the channel: do something else. Get out of the house or apartment and walk around the block. Or do unstructured movement for a few minutes. Or dive into work. Or go see a movie. When the negative mental state is no longer controlling you, in that window of opportunity you can apply mindfulness or other chosen practice.

    Physical practice and physical activity are very, very good for helping you get out of your head (where problems are generated) and into your body. Dancing, walking, running, swimming, rowing, vigorous Yoga or guided restorative Yoga....

    In times of crisis, get clear and focused. Cut away the unnecessary. Drop what doesn't help you. Get back to the matter of the moment. Take care of the small detail of living: eat when it's time to eat; sleep when it's time to sleep; clean up when it's time to clean up; and so on.

    It can also be helpful to use external supports, like being around people who have clear and positive direction, participating in positive structured activities, using your schedule to help you, arranging your living space in a more simple, organized, and nurturing way.

    In terms of personal relationship attachments, I can pass on a shamanic practice that is very helpful. In the other reality, shamans see lines of light connecting all beings. You don't have to see or even believe in these lines of light, but the practice is to imagine and let go of lines that have wrongly stuck you together with someone else. Essentially, you practice letting each other return to yourselves, return to your own integrity. When you fixate on the other person and that empty or twisted feeling appears in your belly, intentionally relax the belly and picture releasing the stuck lines binding you (and them). Do this repeatedly whenever the stress mounts, and you will soon return to yourself.
