    So it sounds like your talk of trust is all a lie, and that you don't actually trust life, you want to force your own way upon it.

    The hot summer of your life has its own heat. It doesn't require more and more. The hot summer of your life requires water.

    The cycle in summer is not only grow, grow, grow. That is only the apparent manifestation. But if you understand cause and effect you know that the cycle in summer is nourish - grow - nourish - grow. It is time for activity, yes, but it is time for activity that contributes to growth. The farmer doesn't just run around with the jitters; nor does the farmer tug on his crops, saying "it is my one and only aim to get corn!" The farmer brings nourishment and water to the soil, occasionally weeds the patch, watches for invasive species, and so on.

    In Taoist language, a situation or person only full of active Yang soon burns out and becomes completely passive; which is not what you want. But active Yang nourished in proper balance by calm and receptive Yin will grow powerful.

    This doesn't mean you give up your goal. It means you see the temporary as temporary, and the illusory as illusory. Then you work with that.

    It also doesn't mean you give up being active and striving. But your activity is also informed by letting-go and letting-be, and getting proper rest, and having the courage to be openhanded with your agitated, confused self.

    Yes, at a certain age there is a lot of energy for doing. The summer of life is the right time to try many things, experience many styles and perspectives, and take great leaps. But most people (and I dare say, most young people) are not so much in tune with the summer of life as they are triggered by it into their own narrow views. Rather than harmonizing with the real nature of the time, one uses the idea of "my summer" as justification for hyperactivity, obsession, and forcing one's way.

    It is why young men and women are most easily manipulated into becoming soldiers or terrorists. "My goal" is ego, which is easily manipulated.

    We are controlled by our doing habit. Doing is our way of avoiding the uncomfortable truth of self. We can't be with ourselves, so we do, do, do. We make big ideas and try to push them through; and all the while, billions of others are making their own ideas and trying to push them through. It is the scourge of the world.

    So it's not that you shouldn't have a goal, or be active, or make effort; but that they shouldn't be an avoidance of what you really are at rest. The cool, dark water of your being is necessary for growth. That means the basis of trust or faith is ungrasping, undoing, unknowing, not some contrived busywork.
