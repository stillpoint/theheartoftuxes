    Where did I say that?

    Please be careful and meticulous about language. "Let go" does not equal "blank state".

    Once, I was sitting outside in the sun and a butterfly landed on my shoulder. I didn't try to catch it as some people do; I let it go. But it stayed on my shoulder. A while later it circled around me and landed again and rested with me. Finally, after some time, it flitted off into the distance.

    So letting go doesn't necessarily mean that things disappear. It means you don't grasp them. When you let go of thinking, it means that you just don't follow it. You keep your center; and when thinking appears, you let it be, like a butterfly flitting nearby or even resting with you. You just don't follow and follow and follow.

    Is that clear?

    Occasionally you may be 'butterfly-free' â€” you may go through a few moments or seconds without thought. That's not insignificant, but it's part of the scene that keeps changing. If you attach to no-thoughts by trying to hold onto it or force it to happen, you lose your center. If you attach to thoughts by trying to avoid or destroy them, or by following them along lines of thinking, you lose your center. Just keep the mind that doesn't move, regardless of the scene that appears.

    So yes, you reset after every situation; but you don't try to manipulate your mind into any particular state. It is more simple than that, more openhanded. Relax and participate with what's happening.

    I also don't like parts of American culture. But some things I like very much. We are disconnected and arrogant and lack many freedoms; but we also have tremendous benefits, access to many teachings and many cultures, and the freedom and leisure to decide what to believe and how to pursue our beliefs. There are many very, very precious blessings to living in America.

    And the parts that we don't like are also necessary. They come about by cause and effect; we create the good and bad parts together. And so it is necessary for us to experience those parts and wake up to them, so we can end that karma.

    When our center is strong, we can be in America and live American-style, but we can help people rather than be tossed about by what we want and what we don't have. When our center is not strong, we may need to find a controlled practice environment in order to keep being turned back to practice, back to our center.
