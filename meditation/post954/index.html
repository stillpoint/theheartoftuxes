    What is a good way to earn a living that promotes mindfulness and can coexist side by side with a serious meditation practice?

    People have tremendously wrong, fantastical notions about what a monk is, what a monk's life is like, and what a monk's life is for.

    Monk doesn't mean "I get my ideal meditation situation". Monk means "I surrender my self-cherishing, I surrender my gaining idea, I surrender my possessions and opinions, all for the sake of others." A monk's life is only for others.

    Furthermore, a correct monk is very assiduous, very hard-working. Personal practice is often the least of a monk's schedule; but even so, it is on schedule and not depending on whim or condition. Inspired or bored, want or not want, healthy or ill, strong or weak, able or not able — a monk just shows up for practice, shows up for the teacher and fellow monks, shows up for the people he serves.

    Monks don't get their favorite situation. If they have their own room, they're lucky; sometimes they sleep side by side on mats. And sometimes they don't sleep at all, because they have nighttime practice or work assigned. Sleeping together, working together, practicing together, sleeping on schedule, working on schedule, practicing on schedule, eating on schedule, following what the teacher says, performing the same daily ceremonies over and over, performing ceremonies for others whenever they need them — it is not a job where you get your favorite situation. Even so, a correct monk takes on all of that, and more; a correct monk wants to do more.

    When a trainee (monastic or layperson) goes away to retreat, this is not a vacuum, not an escape! They leave behind all the things that had served as distractions, all the diversions and relationships and work that keep a person from facing themselves; and they spend their days, weeks, months, or years looking into their mind and all the shit within it. This is really the farthest thing from escaping trouble; it is going headlong into the difficulties that one carries around within, in order to uproot them and be free of them... ...for the sake of all other beings.

    But the person who thinks by joining a monastery he will be able to avoid some of life's problems is indeed escaping, just as a person who goes out for entertainment night after night with friends, or sits at home on the computer, or gets blotto with drugs or alcohol is escaping. The common denominator is the self-cherishing attitude and the habit of putting it at the center of one's life.
