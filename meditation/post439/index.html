    As I write so often in this forum, meditation is not a panacea. Often other practice, therapy, or practical action in life are required to regain integrity.

    Next, in terms of meditation itself having an effect, if we consider how much of the day we engage in habitual or unconscious activity, we can see that our idea of 'moderately regular' practice represents a very small proportion of life, and a very small balancing force upon which to pin our hopes. Even a moment of meditation is beneficial; but having said that, half an hour or an hour at a time, 'fairly regularly' can't balance the effect of extremely frequent, ongoing, life-long, and intense patterns of indulgence, avoidance, aggression, unconsciousness, etc.

    Correct practice is necessary; so, proper learning from a proper teacher. And endless, moment-to-moment application is eventually expected. It's not so much something you get as something you actualize or express, moment by moment.

    ~

    Patterns that keep arising despite our best intentions, and that seem to take control beyond our reach, are almost always the effect of our human shadow -- the parts of us that we disown, deny, hide, avoid, ignore, or try to destroy. These are often common human flaws that we try to rise above, and thus unwittingly force into unconsciousness. But also the shadow often contains positive material that because of upbringing or other causes we don't feel privy to or we believe are not appropriate (for instance, the vast majority of western society shares a shadow of joy: we don't experience or express joy fully and spontaneously; it gets shunted into the dark unaware parts of our being.)

    When our psychic and emotional material is pushed into unconsciousness, it controls us without our awareness. Only after the fact, after the deed is done and the light is shone on our character do we realize what has happened. Most commonly, our unconscious shadow material gets projected externally, and then we believe that we are constantly beset by bad circumstances and flawed people, or that what we seek is always 'out there'; but actually all that shadow material is our own.

    Control begins when we start to recognize and admit our disowned parts. This is also the gateway of broad compassion, recognizing that we all share a common humanity, and that the highest and lowest consciousness are both possible for us. "There, but for the Grace of God, go I."

    ~
