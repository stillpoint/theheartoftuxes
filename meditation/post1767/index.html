    >Ok, truly, what I would want to get out of mirror meditation is to transfer my sense of centeredness and stillness that I get from meditating to social situations.

    Bridging sitting meditation and activity in the world comes from correct, wholistic life training as can be found in many traditional spiritual paths. Self-invented and charismatic-leader paths (like Won, according to some) are usually skewed and limited.

    ~

    Correct meditation beautifully relieves us of our attachment to unreal thoughts and mental habits. It is truly a breath of fresh air and the spring of new life when you achieve that benefit.

    But attachment to thought and habit is very deep and at times very subtle, insidious. It takes over when we don't know it's taking over. Some experienced voices would call it the insidious agenda of ego to recapture control of the process. The great master Chogyam Trungpa called it 'Spiritual Materialism'. (See his classic book of the same name.)

    It happens so easily, outside of our consciousness: we get something real, something sincere, but then slip into mistaken concepts like "I got something real" and "I should get more of that". Very soon, without noticing it, we have objectified practice. Instead of just continuing the true practice — faithfully, with openhanded equanimity — we shift into trying to manipulate the situation, control outcomes, amass attainments.

    Unbeknownst to us, the 'gaining mind', the mind we were so relieved to let go of, is back in the driver's seat.

    So don't just brush it off as "one man's gimmick...." Be attentive to the guidance of experienced brothers and sisters, be sharply attentive to teachings. Look into them, consider them. Don't just dismiss things that fail to pump you up. If it seems to shine a harsh light, honor it as a great benefit.

    The point here is to look into the fact that you are (or let's say, may be) looking for a gimmick or shortcut at all.

    Have you learned a proper meditation method?

    Have you learned it well and completely?

    Do you have faith in it?

    If you don't have faith in it, get the proper training to remove doubt. If you do have faith in it, why don't you continue? Why flit like a butterfly to something else?

    You want to change your mind. Good! But you are not the one to change your mind. The controlling mind does not get rid of the controlling mind. Don't touch! Don't manipulate! Let it be! Have faith in correct meditation (if you are in fact doing correct meditation), and see it through. Your effort, your sincere devotion, and your consistency will get the results, not your figuring and arranging.

    Mirror meditation has some big drawbacks. It's kind of like when you're traversing a high rope bridge and your guide instructs you not to look down. You can lose your spirit; your spirit can rush out of you and your integrity can disintegrate. When you're climbing, this means that you are no longer together with your body, but fear and imagination have taken hold. When you're meditating, this means that you are no longer together with your bodymind in practice, and some object or concept has grabbed you and stolen your spirit. (Don't get attached to my use of the word 'spirit'; understand the meaning, understand where it's pointing.)

    A mirror can very easily 'steal your spirit' by getting you fixated on the image rather than centered in your actual self. You look at the image and you think "there's me". But that's not you! You've fallen for the mirage. That's not you at all. That's what's meant by losing your spirit: you lose your center, your settled, integrated presence. Instead, you become fascinated with objects.

    And not only does the mirror already have this effect, you are also heading there yourself already. You are thinking of using a technique to change yourself, so already two minds — the one doing the changing, and the one being changed. It is a disease.

    It's not that some mirror meditations aren't beneficial for those with very strong center and clear direction in practice (though if you have strong center and clear direction, why mess around with mirrors?); but it has an innate risk. This self-deluding effect has on many occasions prompted deluded views and psychotic breaks. Ask psychologists who have worked with unstable people trying all kinds of fancy practices in order to get 'special' visions or energies. It's not impossible to do harm.

    Next, "I feel centered" is already a mistake, already not centered. Can you see why? Look into this.

    Don't tinker. Don't mess about. Don't put your own limited, as yet unilluminated mind in control of the process. Get proper guidance. Find a safe and supportive container for practice.
