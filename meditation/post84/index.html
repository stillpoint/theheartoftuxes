    Are you asking me to convince you? I'm just reporting, not convincing.

    It would be better if you just took it in and considered it for a while. If we're very quick to reply, we often miss seeing our own assumptions.

    What part of mind do you think is unique to you? Where does your language come from? Where do the images you deal with come from? How do you come to the conclusion that there's a world 'out there'? All sights, sounds, smells, tastes, sensations, and languages are given to you from something or someone else. So what part is unique to you in particular?

    Next, scientifically, what are thought and action made of? If you want to say that your thoughts are yours alone, let's see what they are based upon. Thought (or action) requires the organ, the brain (or muscle), and the nourishment of that organ with hormones and sugar and other chemicals derived from food, liquid, and breath... which are derived from plant and animal, river and lake, and atmosphere... which are derived from planetary and cosmic matter and energy. In terms of the scientific cause-and-effect basis of thought and action, they belong to the universe. Without sun there is no thought. Without ocean and sky there is no thought. Without the complementary activity of countless objects and beings there is no thought. And this is true for all human and animal thinkers. So what part supposedly belongs to you?

    If you want to test it out, I suppose you could stop eating, drinking, and breathing, and then try to see what belongs to 'you' alone. Even then, after death all that supposedly belongs to you will return to the earth and sky.

    Another way of saying this is that you have thoughts, you have feelings, you have a body, you have experiences, you have relationships, you have a living space, you have clothing, you have possessions, you have hopes and wishes and history.... All of this you have, so it is not you. You are the one who has it.

    So who has all of this? For whom (or what) does the entire universe of experience appear, like a movie played on a screen?

    Don't answer too fast from assumptions, but look yourself to find where this thing (if it's anything) is. Where is it to be found, not in theory, not in name, but in reality?

    When I was six years old, I already knew that Descartes was not complete with his famous argument. He said "I think, therefore I am." But this assumes "I".

    Next, if no thinking, then what? "I" disappears.

    So it is very interesting if you don't cling to what some call the "I-thought", the sense of being something in particular. It is a very ingrained habit; but if you don't hold that thought, the result is very interesting.

    When you watch a movie, everything seems real. Soon you forget it is a movie. There is drama — gain, loss, romance, life, death — there are chases and explosions, different characters come and go, different places arise and pass away. All of that change, all of that drama is not real. The screen, the unmoving thing, is real. Likewise, if you find the one unmoving thing for which all appearing and disappearing happens, you find what is real.

    Then, everything can be real. When you know you are in a movie, then you can know the sense in which the movie is real and the sense in which it is unreal. It is the same way with mental projections as it is with cinematic projections: if you know the one unmoving thing (which is not really a 'thing'), then you can know the sense in which objects, beings, phenomena, and all of life's dramas are real and the sense in which they are unreal.

    When you dream, there are all sorts of characters and situations unfolding. Images, conversations, deep emotions. Even you appear as a character in your dream. It can be so enthralling or so terrifying! But when you wake up, soon you are no longer wrapped up in the dream. You can realize "Oh, that was all my own mind." All those different characters, including the 'you' character, were made by your own mind.

    Waking life is essentially no different from dreaming life. The question is whether you will wake up and realize where all the various characters and situations come from.
