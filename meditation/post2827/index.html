    TheHeartofTuxes

    All things in nature find their own natural flow, their own proper place and relationship-in-movement. But humans upset their own flow and the flow of nature by imposing opinions and views and habits, and generally trying to manipulate situations.

    For example, the energy of lightning finds its path of least resistance between earth and sky. The movement of water flows freely, finding its lowest point.

    Similarly, if you consciously refrain from manipulating your mind and situation, your emotional and mental and physical energies will all find their appropriate relationship. So meditation means keeping a mind that doesn't move to hold or avoid or manipulate anything. Meditation means keeping a not-moving mind.

    If you can do that, no further guidance is needed. But because we've built up a lot of agitated, grasping, manipulating habits, we find it difficult to keep a not-moving mind without support. So we use method to help us. Therefore, you should find a good, proven, traditional method and apply it meticulously (without manipulating the method!). Many methods offered online or in books take one object and use it as a focus to help practice unmoving mind. You don't have to shut down thought, you only have to practice returning to the method. That means that thought begins to grab you less and less, and your inner and outer energies can all find their proper place on their own.

    But don't expect everything to come to you on a platter. It's not a matter of just asking and then receiving the perfect cure. You have to seek, and research, and try. And then try and try. Sometimes you may need to gather more supports — a teacher, an environment, a schedule, an intention, a more authentic method — and then try again.

    But even without these supports, your own mind determines the outcome. If you are sincere and keep trying, you will get results. "Even a dull axe will fell the tree if you keep chopping."

    Meditation has two important aspects: relaxed and awake. Both are important; and they should be in balance. Not too relaxed and dull, not so sharply focused that tension becomes a problem. When you are relaxed you can focus very sharply, but the acquisitive, manipulative mind is let go of, so its tension doesn't disrupt the process.

    Let your mind settle into its primordial, natural state a moment at a time.
