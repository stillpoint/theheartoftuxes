    i want to start the mindfulness of the breath meditation but i experience difficulty in breathing

    I echo mrbundle's encouragement to get medical and psychological assessment before proceeding to next steps, if only to assure you that there is no underlying issue that needs to be resolved.

    Although I believe a very structured course of training -- pranayama breathing in yoga, Qigong breathing exercises or the like, or even singing lessons -- will help you learn to breathe deeply and naturally, there are many, many meditation methods not based on breathing.

    You don't have to beg off meditation altogether just because of this issue; walking meditation, movement meditation, self-inquiry, mantra, hwadu, body scan, metta and other 'divine abidings' practices, concentration based on objects of sight or hearing, devotional practice, and visualization are just some of the categories of possible meditation work.

    But even if you try one or more of these other options, it will help you to develop a deep and natural pattern of breathing, and to move beyond self-limiting thought habits. Dedicated breath training could come sooner or later in your meditation career, but it will be helpful at some point.
