    If you do want to use lying-down posture for its intended purpose, in addition to clear-minded sitting meditation, here are a couple of possibilities. Take them or leave them as suits you:

    • Savasana or Shavasana ("corpse pose") is a Yoga posture and practice of progressive relaxation. In it, you consciously release tension and withdraw from engagement with the bodymind; so like a corpse you settle, settle, settle, and withdraw from the outside world. This can have a calming and healing effect, and can help inform your clear-minded sitting practice. Since it is about withdrawing inward, it is not a substitute for the upright sitting that openly includes everything (includes both inner and outer worlds and allows them to merge).

    • Piecing together the body is another good, simple practice that can be done when going to sleep and when waking up. Lying down, you bring each bodypart into awareness, like piecing together a puzzle until it is complete. Start with hands, then add feet, include all four limbs, include the torso and head... until the body is whole within your awareness. It doesn't matter what you feel or don't feel; you simply allow your listening to include the whole body. This is also healing, and helps to keep you clear within your dreaming if performed when going to sleep, or arrive clearly in the day if performed when waking. This is also only a preparation, not a substitute, for clear-minded sitting meditation.

    Either of the lying-down meditations can be done in a couple of minutes, or can be drawn out for longer.
