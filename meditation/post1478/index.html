    Question- I want to move my meditation to the next level with a retreat, but don't know where to get started. Thoughts?

    You write that you feel trapped by your conditioning and self-concept, and that you want to develop a more stable, mature, and open mind. That's great! I'm happy to hear that.

    You also write that you want more fulfilling relationships, and you ask if I think that's too selfish. So my question to you is whether that is sincere. I believe you are being sincere in saying what you're hoping to achieve. So if that is what is truly moving through you, then I respect it. You don't need to check how it fits in with anyone's judgment; you only need to be sincere and act on that sincerity.

    Becoming more stable, mature, and open-minded will, I believe, not only lead to more fulfilling relationships with all people, it will help everyone who passes through your life. Benefit for you, benefit for others. Win-win. I see nothing wrong with that unless it becomes a self-glorification or develops a grasping quality.

    In fact, we can run into problems by becoming too lofty in our ideas. Aspiration should be free to soar to its full extent; but if we push our aspiration along to match high-falutin' ideas, the aspiration no longer has its own organic life. Inspiration is lost, and replaced by egocentric agenda. So simple sincerity will help you take care of your precious aspiration.

    Meditations where you watch the breath or count the breaths tend to be safe, beneficial, and broadly applicable to many types of people. This is the heart of character development, as you let go and establish your open presence breath by breath. Any thought, distraction, or sense of grasping appears: let it go and return to the breath.

    Some teachers will key on these practices; others will use them in concert with other meditations; and others yet will suggest alternative meditations.

    Two main points to consider are:

    • Meditation should help you practice presence — returning to this simple moment rather than trying to "go elsewhere", to some other state or dimension

    • Whatever you seek is already available to you in (or as) your own mind. No teacher or being or outer force can bestow your own true self on you.

    So correct meditation is not grasping or forcing; and correct meditation points you back to your own mind. There are many possible methods, but those two points are important.
