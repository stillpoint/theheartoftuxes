    title: I have a beginner question: I have began meditating using some good instructions. But I am having trouble with mindfulness -it seems harder and a bit separate from meditation. How do you do it? write a journal? summary: 10.0 authors: TheHeartofTuxes date: 2015-02-18 23:16:29 some_url:

    Some types of meditation will have a narrow focus, to let go of mental excursions and stay calmly with one thing. Mindfulness tends to have a broad focus, so that you can function well in life, but with awareness. So you can see that the two practices complement each other; meditation may deepen, mindfulness may open.

    Some methods meld the two practices into one.

    The term mindfulness, like the term meditation is used in many different ways, and is taken up in many different methods with many different objects of focus (or sometimes with no object). But in general, you can think of mindfulness as just not losing awareness. Just don't drift out of awareness regarding what you're doing in the moment and what's going on around you.

    This, of course, is easy to say; but it is the work of a lifetime. Many things will take you out of awareness, and if you keep practicing mindfulness you will gradually be able to identify them and let them go, so they won't control your mind any more. The practice of returning simply to what you're doing and what's happening is key. Keep returning, and your returning will get stronger. Keep returning, and the things that take your mind away will have less of a grasp on you. Keep returning, and your response will become more and more simple, more and more clear... so the wisdom growing in the silence of your meditation will be able to be expressed in your everyday activity.

    Many of us are easily taken out of awareness by so many things — sex, entertainment, pain, hunger, gossip, pride, and so on. The things that take us away are so common and so numerous that we may have a hard time identifying what awareness/mindfulness is. We only barely see that we are constantly unaware; we don't know what it is to be aware.

    The solution is not to try and hold on to a way of acting or feeling that we might associate with awareness. (Maybe we saw a monk in a movie or tv show, for example.) One of the suggestions for the average person practicing mindfulness is to use one consistent object as an anchor, something you can come back to every time.

    One such anchor is your physical base: feeling the feet when standing or walking; feeling the butt and feet when sitting, and feeling your body's contact with the bed, sofa, or floor when you're lying down. You can keep coming back to an easy awareness of your physical base as you perform your daily activities. It doesn't have to be a mega-intense concentration — this isn't concentration meditation, it's just something in the background to keep you somewhat present. You can have a small part of your attention in your feet when you listen to someone, for instance; and you can feel your seat and feet under you when you're at the computer.

    Other traditional objects and methods exist (breath, sound, the activity of the hands, whole-body awareness, mood awareness, ethical activity, mantra repetition, and many more), and each method will have its strong points and weak points, but the important point is that they all bring you back to the present moment. It's just that simple. You go about your life normally, but you just keep coming back to awareness in the moment.

    Does that help you understand?
