    Weed motivation

    That is weed motivation, not seeker's motivation. Your seeker's sincerity and motivation is yours it doesn't belong to or depend on external support.

    >Yesterday, I had a bad meditation session; it was not so productive as it use[d] to be.

    Motivation is killed by depending on results.

    What constitutes a 'bad' session, in your opinion? What constitutes 'productive', or 'not productive'? You are expecting a certain experience or feeling, so when you don't have that experience or feeling you judge the practice. That is wrong perspective. That is clinging to experience and feeling, which is the same neurotic pattern encouraged by weed smoking: you smoke weed in order to get a certain experience and feeling and to avoid other experience and feeling. So the mind can't be complete.

    Correct practice completely overturns that neurotic pattern of grasping at experience. Correct practice is completely free, not depending on any particular situation. Everything is okay; you just stay aware. What you like is okay, what you don't like is okay, and what is neutral is okay. Just stay aware.

    If you only chase what you like, what you like becomes your prison. If you only avoid what you don't like, what you don't like becomes your prison. And if you ignore what is neutral, what is neutral becomes your prison.

    So it is up to you — not a drug — to supply the interest and sincerity and motivation and awareness.

    If you go to the gym to do bicep curls, but always have a friend lifting most of the weight for you, you can't claim to be lifting the weight. You have no right to walk around proud of lifting 150 pounds if you're only doing 10 pounds worth of effort. Likewise, if you use drugs to generate the interest you can't claim it as your interest, your practice.

    To go further in the analogy, if you go to the gym and your helper is not there, when you try to lift the weight it will suddenly be very difficult if not impossible. It's not the fault of the practice or of the weight, it is because you have not done the actual training. So when you approach meditation and find it difficult or impossible, it's just because you need more practice — correct practice.

    So in meditation, only follow the method. Don't check result.

    When the mind wanders from the method and starts to check how things are going, and looks around at the scenery, you must drop that and return to the method. Be faithful to the method, because that is your teacher, your mind-mirror. The mirror shows you when your mind has wandered away, and over the course of many practice sessions it also shows you how the mind wanders away, and what triggers and attachments cause the mind to wander; so in the mirror of practice you get to see your own situation — your karma, the cause-and-effect of how you keep yourself dull and limited.

    But if you don't follow the method faithfully, that mind-mirror can't work. If you compromise on the method, giving yourself 'outs', you will not be able to see when and why the mind goes out of control; and thus you will never have self-control and never be free.

    So only follow the method, and drop all checking of your feeling, situation, and condition.

    Don't confuse 'spectacular' with correct. As-is is correct. Don't make any special ideas or require other than what is. If it's boring, then you must apply interest, and you must redouble your effort in returning to the method.

    Do you have a method? Is it a proven method? Is the method very clear to you — do you know how to carry it out at every step? If you try inventing your own way it is like hiring an idiot to be your guru.
