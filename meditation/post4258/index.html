    >Why should it be this way? Why can't it be another way?

    Rather than 'should', which can be taken as a sort of moralistic proscription, we might talk about cause and effect. 'Should' in this case would be a short-hand way of saying that if you do things a certain way, you get certain results; and if you do things another way, you get other results; and therefore, if you want certain results you 'should' accumulate their effective causes.

    So we can certainly look into why things should be done a certain way. And it's not that they can't be done another way. There are many ways. If your aim is broad, may methods and styles will suffice. If your aim is specific and extraordinary, then very specific and strict causes are required. Not because being strict is better in some moralistic sense, but because those are the causes leading to the particular result.
