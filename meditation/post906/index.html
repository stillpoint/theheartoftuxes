    I love meditating, but I've noticed the sits in which I'm truly present only come about in a certain state and it makes me worried.

    You will be okay. You just need to take the correct steps to return to balance. When your bodymind is more balanced you will naturally be happier and more self-possessed.

    You are not the best one to judge the quality of your presence. You don't yet know what constitutes presence, you don't yet know what is correct meditation, and certainly you are not clear enough to make those judgements about presence when you are high. All you know is that certain signs you deem significant appear in connection with smoking weed.

    You have willingly given your mind over to repeatedly getting high, so your mind is now conditioned by your past actions; it is now more difficult for you to feel okay on your own. That is called karma. You make a cause, you get a result. If you change the cause, you can change the result. Because there is now a physical and mental pattern established, it will take some time and effort for you to find both chemical balance and freedom from dependency.

    The true meditative mind is free. It is not thrown around by circumstances, and it doesn't rely on something else for its support. No outside thing can make you free. Even if that were possible, your dependency on that thing would be bondage.

    The shared human predicament is a great anxiety with openness. The world is open, boundless, ever-changing. The moment is empty, clear. But humans always want to grasp and hold and make and control; so the open, empty moment is very uncomfortable for their grasping mind. Humans, in general, seek ways to escape open, empty moments. We look for something to occupy us, to take away our agitated mind. Even without being aware of it, we desperately seek those crutches.

    Many things can take our uncomfortable thinking mind away temporarily — TV, movies, sex, drugs, partying — but when those things go away, not only do we then experience the openness and emptiness of the new moment, we are less capable of being in it without anxiety. If we have not practiced presence — if we have given our minds over to external objects — the ability to be calm and clear escapes us.

    But we have a mind behind our agitated, grasping, thinking mind. This is the true mind, the original clarity that you always possess. When agitating habits are overturned, your original clarity shines forth. No situation is a problem; your original mind is already perfectly stable, perfectly content, and perfectly able to respond to any situation.

    So meditation means returning to your original clear and stable mind. You practice the mind that doesn't depend on anything. You need to practice not depending on getting high, not depending on certain thoughts or experiences, not depending on any particular state of mind or feeling in the body.

    For a time, because of the pattern that you created, this may be uncomfortable. But it moves in the direction of real peace, self-sufficiency, and freedom.

    Meditation goes against the stream. Your habit is to avoid certain situations or states of mind by getting high; but meditation goes in the other direction. It sits facing everything.

    In meditation, you apply your awareness to the method, and truth appears by itself. If you have created an unhealthy pattern in your mind, then that unhealthy pattern appears for you to see. That is truth. Don't judge that as inferior meditation; don't think that that is not presence. Only continue.

    Don't let your thinking control you. Don't let it determine whether you will do meditation or not. If you want to be free, you have to sit through reality, not just your favorite situation, okay?

    It is very interesting that although you know THC alters brain chemistry, you suspect that the sober mind is the unhealthy one. This shows the depth of your attachment — how much you want your intoxication to be okay. But that has the situation backwards. You need to let go of the grip yourself. When you do the letting go, you strengthen your free and independent mind.

    Everything is okay. Smoking weed is okay, drinking is okay... even killing is okay. But everything you do is a cause that has a specific effect. Bad causes have bad effects. Grasping causes have suffering effects. There is no escaping the effect. But you can change your causes and get a more healthy result.

    Everything is okay. High is okay, and clear is okay. Also, being still within the moment no matter what arises in your mind is okay. Do you believe that? Can you be okay with the moment as it is, without trying to change it to something else?

    P S: I echo the sentiments of others, that you should seek help if anxiety or depression become overwhelming. Sometimes you need to help yourself in other ways before you can start proper meditation.
