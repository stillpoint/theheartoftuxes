    Is guided meditation 'OK' for beginners?

    If it's a good meditation, and taught well, and appropriate for you, then yes guided meditation can be a good way to learn and to develop the ability to coach yourself.

    Sometimes we practice with an external instructor and external witness until we become clear on the process and our inner witness becomes strong and stable.

    You can try both. Try guided, and try on your own. Both have advantages and difficulties. With guided meditation there may be less flow and less personal impetus. With solo meditation one may forget the process or become too distracted to proceed. Either way, once practice is developed your skill will increase and you will be able to make good use of any situation.

    But just because guided meditation is not a problem in principle doesn't mean that every guided meditation is fine. There are many posers out there, many people who maybe got a little something but have missed the big picture, many people who have misconstrued a true experience within the framework of their own beliefs, many people who care more about their own persona as a teacher or wise one than they do about your progress and well-being.

    In addition, there are many techniques that don't really go anywhere — they're more like temporary feel-good experiences than anything transformative. And finally, there are a lot of practices that go contrary to the wise path; they may for example require you to use your mind in an unnatural way or attach to things or suppress things that are not necessary to attach to or suppress.

    So the greater question is what is correct meditation, and why meditate at all. What is the correct purpose of meditation? It should be the same as the correct purpose of your life. If it doesn't support your true job in life, kick it to the curb.

    Therefore you need to clarify your motivation, your direction in life, if you are to be confident in giving yourself over to someone else guiding your mind. You need to know that this is a good path, a good practice, and that the guide truly has your best interests at heart.

    If visualization is used, it should be very clear to you why. What is the purpose? What is the expected result? What are the signs of progress? This all must be clarified; and it is your responsibility to clarify it for yourself. You can get help, but until you meet a master that you (intelligently) trust 100%, no one else is in charge of protecting your mind and your path.

    Hypnosis is, of course, risky business. It can be useful, for planting beneficial seeds in your mind, letting go of neurotic attachments, and so on; but you need to take care about who or what has access to your deep mind. In any case, hypnosis is a possible side practice, but not true meditation.

    You should look into the purpose of your life, and thus the purpose of meditation. If you need someone to tell you, I can tell you; but it's best for you to look into it sincerely, deeply, repeatedly. It is not something that is checked off the list once in life. It may be a lifelong process of clarification. But it is worth it.

    There is an inconceivably huge difference between someone who has clarified his/her life purpose and someone who has not, even if they are practicing the same meditation. Someone may have been meditating for many years, may have developed a lot of skill and special power; but if he has not clarified his direction — has not found his proper motivation — then that skill and power can't be wielded properly. It's like having access to powerful medicine or weapons and not knowing what to do with them. But on the other hand, if you know what to do, you can use even an herb or stick you find by the road to help yourself and others. It is the same with meditation.
