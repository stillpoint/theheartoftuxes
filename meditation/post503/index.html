    Starting zen mind, beginners mind - differences from mindfulness?

    That's pretty much how I understand it. In the final analysis, the two paths can come to the same experience, but in the beginning my sense is that mindfulness does seem to retain this sense of "I am being mindful". (And later, that is let go of.)

    When you say that Zen meditative practice aims for a collapse in the subject - object dichotomy, and that experience and experiencer become one, that is partially correct; but the first and most important point is that we begin from non-separation. To say that we "become one" is misleading, because we have never been separate. It's only our false thinking that makes us believe we are ever separate from life.

    I think that the practitioner's experience may be quite similar among the various practices. At first the effort to get it right is both a separation and an aid (in that it occupies much of the thinking mind). Later, both an unrestrained flow of awareness and a more drifting distraction may appear. And with mastery a greater consistency and ease appear.

    Besides method, intent and foundational view are very important — perhaps more important — and very influential on the resulting experience. It's like they say about drug trips: the setting and intention definitely shape the trip, along with the drug itself.

    After some amount of discussing, I like to bring it back to real experience; so I'd like to invite you to talk about times when you're experienced non-separation. Caught by surprise by the full moon... connecting with a baby's eyes... gliding down a long hill on a bike... eating an exotic fruit for the first time... stubbing the little toe....?
