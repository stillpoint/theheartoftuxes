    TheHeartofTuxes

    The generation stage is to establish the warmhearted feeling in yourself — to make real the feeling of softness, gratitude, tenderness, and well-wishing, and to feel it.

    Once you connect to the feeling, you deepen it and let it grow in you. It is like steeping tea: you let it become stronger, flavoring your experience, flavoring the moment. Or it is like kindling a fire: you offer it some good fuel, and soon it radiates and grows, filling your whole being with warmth and light.

    Then, having established the warm-heartedness, you can than radiate it outward — and there are various patterns for doing this. Depending on the instructions you might send the Metta out to various categories of beings, starting with those you feel closest and most affectionate toward and continuing on to other more difficult categories. Or you might radiate outward in space to the four quarter, the four sub-quarters, and above and below, spreading Metta to the reaches of the universe and permeating every realm deeply and intensely with kindness.

    So the suggestion of who or what to use as a trigger for lovingkindness is contingent; it's whatever works for you, whatever generates natural and heartfelt warmth in your heart.

    Usually it's not recommended to use people who are very close to you, either because you've seen too much of their bad side, or because your relationship may be too enmeshed and complicated, or because it would incite too much attachment (i.e., instead of generating pure lovingkindness it also generates romantic or lustful or needy feelings).

    It's often suggested to think of someone not so close to you who has at any time given you something out of plain generosity, without asking for anything in return. This could be a stranger who helped you in some way, or a teacher who benefited you selflessly, or some more distant family member who has displayed kindness toward you. These types of examples tend to generate a pure gratitude and natural wish for their well-being.

    But as has been written elsewhere in this thread, our animal friends are often the ones who have the most tender and unguarded access to our hearts. Bringing to mind a beloved pet or suffering animal or any innocent person or being might easily generate those warm feelings.

    At times when you're so hurt and beset that you can't think of anyone else, it may also happen that you think of yourself with great tenderness, as if you were protecting a younger version of yourself. This can also generate Metta.

    It doesn't matter what triggers your experience of lovingkindness; what matters is that the feeling is pure and that it becomes established in your body and heart. Then you can share it. (And eventually share it with your friends, perhaps with the understanding that they are victims of ignorance and conditioning, and if they could do better they would.)
