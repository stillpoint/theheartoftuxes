    This is not the place to learn meditation. Too many different opinions; too many different styles; too many different motivations; too many different levels of understanding; too many different abilities/inabilities to convey the principles clearly.

    It's not something that will be transmitted in a single online post; not in depth, anyway. Go to a proper tradition that teaches meditation, and learn from someone who him/herself has learned very deeply, practiced and experienced very consistently, fully attained the good results of the practice, and has been trained and confirmed to teach.

    Find a traditional Yoga studio, Buddhist practice center, traditional Taiji or Taoist practice center, Christian meditation center, or authorized and lineage-recognized shamanic teacher.

    Barring that, a great many people here and elsewhere recommend Culadasa's The Mind Illuminated as a very well presented, logical, step-by-step guide. You might start there and in the meantime look for face-to-face teaching.

    At the bottom of the issue is your own clarity about direction. Why meditate? Why do anything in life? When your own purpose is unclear, of course you will encounter confusion about what to try and who to believe. But when you understand your clear direction — the right purpose of your life and effort — then your choices about meditation practice can be clear.

    What draws you to the idea of meditation? What do you think or hope you might get or change?

    A lot of people start out looking for some kind of success in life by becoming more focused or calmer; but the question still stands what is the purpose of that success. A more meaningful life direction is to wake up — to become clear and free of the snares of habit, conditioning, opinion, and habit... to become truly fulfilled and help all others become fulfilled... to help all people without discrimination, and to help all animals and other beings, and to help the world itself.

    This is the greater purpose; but since many people are not yet open to this purpose, in the meantime they may seek out the positive benefits of focus, calmness, stress relief, and so on. These are still positive reasons.

    But you need to take the time to study well. People these days are lazy and greedy, and used to getting things with the push of a button. Have a question? Google it! Want a product? Instant-buy it online! But deep knowledge will take time and effort to gain. If you choose quickly without good reason, you become limited by your own choices; you paint yourself into a corner. And if you make quick assumptions about what an instruction means, you often miss the real teaching.

    "Shutting your mind down" is right in some respects and wrong in some respects. Who can teach you the difference? Not just someone with a personal philosophy about it. A lot of people talk with confidence but without actually knowing. Find a proper teacher.

    In general, meditation means not moving the mind. You don't run toward things and clutch at them; and you don't run away from things and avoid them. Also, you don't just become sleepy and ignore things. Meditation lets reality be what it is, without interfering.

    That's pure meditation. But most people are too agitated to be able to just let the mind be still; and they have too much of a habit of grasping and avoiding to just let the mind be without manipulating it. Therefore, methods are offered to help us let go and let be. When you have a clear, correct method you have something very definite to come back to when your mind goes wandering off and attaching to things.

    So find a correct traditional method, preferably taught by a qualified, attained teacher. Then just do it without fidgeting and manipulating. Your mind will become clear and you can help yourself and all others.
