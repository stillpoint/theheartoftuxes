    Another meditation session...another wasted 20 minutes...

    1. You are a beginner. You are experiencing beginner issues. That is appropriate.

    2. Why do you think instead of paying attention to the breath? I'm not posing this as a provocative rhetorical question, I'm asking you why you do more thinking than paying attention.

    Likely you have faced your mind enough to have an idea of what's going on. At some level you still value your thinking more than you value the practice; you trust your thinking more than you trust your basic, naked mind.

    1. Reading and watching articles and talks doesn't constitute proper support for practice. Reading and watching from many various sources leads to confusion and even conflict, as paths, perspectives, and methods differ. And the notion that more ideas will help has it all ass-backwards; rather, the confused and rampant thinking is behind your troubles, and you need to get out of the head and into the real moment.

    2. You, or any beginner, are not qualified to guide your own practice. The things you don't know and habitually fail to see will always influence your choices. You need someone who can faithfully hold the perspective from above â€” from above self-concern and its related patterns. In short, for progress in authentic practice you need a teacher. Find and submit to a capable teacher and practice group. Studying together with others will form you in ways that are impossible in isolation.

    If you can't control your mind yourself, you have to find the conditions that will do it for you. Cleaning and organizing your environment and schedule, establishing relationships supportive of awakening, leading a lifestyle conducive of peace and stability and awareness, submitting to a practice form and schedule and setting, and most of all spending lots of time with living vehicles of Truth can provide you with the conditions necessary to control your mind.

    1. 'Stuck' is not stuck. Your thinking is stuck. You think you are stuck, so you are stuck. But if you have a moment without that thinking, you are just here where you are.

    So where are your feet standing right now? What is that scene? In a moment of open questioning you see through the veil of thought and real life appears: the computer screen, the sights and sounds and smells around you, etc..

    Look out at the night sky. At that moment, what are you? Don't know: only... night sky. Already that is unstuck.

    But the mind-habit, in the next moment, thinks that the openness and emptiness of just this is insufficient. It rejects the possibility that this scene, this moment is already okay, is already complete, is already unstuck and free and finished. Therefore proper training is necessary, to overcome the mind-habit and return to your original freedom. So please keep it simple, find where your feet are standing, don't get misled by the various teachings (don't think there is something special to know), find a teacher, and do your correct practice whether it is advanced practice or beginner practice.
