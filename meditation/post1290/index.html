    TheHeartofTuxes

    Mind makes everything. Anything that has been created by humans has first had its seed in the mind. Some people understand quite well how seeds grow into results; but they don't necessarily understand the right kinds of results to seek, or the results of seeking results.

    Asking for things in order to get your favorite situation is a very low-class intention. Maybe someone can get what they wish for, but perhaps they don't understand that it is all based on cause and effect, and that there is a cost for what we receive.

    In any case, gaining things for self-centered purposes only deepens self-centeredness. One becomes possessed by that which they seek to possess. This goes for powers and traits as well as material things and relationships.

Hahahahaha! ...OK, now your turn." > > The Zen Master replied with "Oh, you look like a Buddha! Hahahahaha!" (His very hearty and prolonged laugh was unsettling to the emperor.) > > The emperor shook his head: "No, you didn't understand the game. We are having a friendly game of insults here. You must try to insult me. Don't worry, we're just friends here." > > But the Zen Master replied: "I understood the game fine. A pig sees everything as 'pig'. A Buddha sees everything as 'Buddha'."

    So this is the point of intention. If you have 'pig' intention, you get 'pig' result. You see the world from selfish eyes, and the world appears as a collection of objects always separate from you. It is a world of dissatisfaction. You can get something, but it can never fulfill you.

    But if you have 'Buddha' intention, you can begin to see the world in its true form, full of purity and beauty and blessing, and you can get 'Buddha' result. This doesn't have to mean Buddhist religion, but it must go beyond the small, impoverished, grasping self.

    So the important point isn't that a certain practice works to get you what you wish for. The important point is to understand the difference between the million things you could get that won't help you, and the one thing you could get that will.
