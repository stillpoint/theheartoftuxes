    After one year of meditation, I think I'm starting to lose the grip.

    The yoga practices usually found in the west are very good for dealing with stress and anxiety. They give you tools for not only relaxing, but applying that relaxation in specific ways in the bodymind.

    To achieve deep peace, concentration-style meditation is indicated. This is a practice that uses a definite object, to which you return repeatedly. Every time your thoughts take you away, you return to the object of meditation. Gradually your ability to return is developed, and you are distracted less and less. When you no longer depart, the mind is perfectly clear and still; events can arise in it, but it doesn't move in reaction to them. This is profoundly peaceful and rejuvenating.

    To extend that centeredness through the day, mindfulness practice is indicated. This means that you keep returning to the present moment throughout the day. It can be an open mindfulness practice where you simply attend to whatever you are doing moment to moment, or it can include a structure or object to help you to return, such as feeling your contact with the floor at all times or doing a mantra while you make your way through the day.

    There are pros and cons to every practice, and many practices have quite different aims and effects. But in general there are many practices that are "good enough", the main thing that helps you is your own consistency in letting go of trains of thought and returning to the present moment.

    Now, taking the question further: Your interim purpose is to reduce anxiety and improve focus. What are your calmness and focus for? What do you intend to do with them? Because only trying to get your own good situation can't help you in the end: the grasping attitude "for me" always keeps you separate from that which you seek, keeps you separate from your original home. "For everyone" is already complete; it acknowledges that you already have everything, and doesn't put a false separation between you and the source of your happiness.

    "For everyone" includes not only people, but animals and all beings, and the sky and earth.
