    I've been watching my breath for months. What about chakras?

    Namasté.

    Experiencing or meditating with the chakras was significant enough to you to ask about it specifically. So I asked why the particular interest.

    Curiosity is wonderful, and I hope you (we!) preserve it. But, yes, the purpose is important. It's not that you should pretend to have a purpose if you don't — you should be true to whatever your actual situation is — but this issue should be clear. Wandering around in meditation without a clear direction is at best running the risk of wasting time and potential benefit, and at worst can lead you down harmful paths and bring much suffering to yourself and others.

    When we go out in search of power without knowing what to do with power, that is stepping into danger. What is power for? What kind of power do we need, and what kind do we already have? If we gain some kind of knowledge or special energy, what will we do with it?

    All of these questions come back to your direction in life: why do anything? What are you hoping to attain, and what aim is the most beneficial for you and for the world you live in? There are many, many different kinds of meditation, contemplation, and concentration practices; and they have many different aims and results. Often people go toward a practice because it sounds exotic, or because others have talked it up, or because they envision getting some advantage over others. These are unclear beginnings, unclear initiating causes. Unclear cause produces unclear result.

    To get a clear result, a clear cause is necessary. Given the dominance of our habits over our minds, and the tendencies toward laxity and unclarity that humans share, meditative pursuit will come to naught if a clear direction and clear view is not present. We fall back on the old strategy of following our preference, and so whatever method we choose can't possibly liberate us: when we get close to shedding the old habit, the old habit asserts itself more strongly. Clear direction is necessary to cut through the old habit, to give you the will to continue beyond merely clambering for what you like and running away from what you don't like.

    Chakras are not important in themselves. They are an idea, a way of talking about patterns. The usefulness of practicing with chakras is in looking into and experiencing one's blockages and strengths, and clarifying them in appropriate ways, building the right foundation and then the right structures upon it in proper order. Chakra work may help to organize that kind of practice.

    But it is only one way among many. Chakras and all physical, energetic, and mental formations come from mind. If your mind is clear, then your chakras are clear. If you have no mind, then... what?

    Your questions can be answered, but they depend on the question behind them: What do you want? What are you seeking, in meditation, in life? If you tell me that, I can address the question of various styles and methods. If that is not clear, then all styles and methods will be unclear.
