    This Is The Weirdest Yet The Most Peaceful Feeling You Will Ever Experience While Meditating

    The linked article is lame. It is both vague and misleading, and confuses concepts. Furthermore, to state that the experience of kundalini is "the most peaceful feeling you will ever experience" is simply untrue, and arrogantly so. It's a statement from someone who just can't conceive that there's anything beyond their own (real or imagined) experience.

    Kundalini effect is not at all enlightenment, but rather the mark of an opening or flow that may be experienced on the way to enlightenment. In some cases, intense energetic manifestations may appear in connection with heart and head openings; but the intensity has more to do with blockage than with flow: like an electrical circuit, the heat appears because there is resistance, not because of openness. That is, the system has been loaded before it is ready to receive the current. True spiritual current is cool and clear, not all worked up into a hot bother.

    Furthermore, there are increasingly subtle states of bliss and peace that each abandon the clinging and agitation of the previous. The feeling of a strong energetic or sensory flow would be considered quite gross and agitating from the perspective of a more refined state. Again, real flow is clear, frictionless. Kundalini becomes most noticeable because of attachments and blockages, not because of its free flow. One moment of plain openhanded presence is already kundalini.

    And finally, the emphasis on feeling itself is to be abandoned, whether sooner or later along the path. Some traditions key on feeling until the subtle awareness behind the feeling is well cultivated. Other traditions recognize from the start that feeling is just another empty phenomenon to be released.

    These dramatic experiences sometimes arise, but to focus on the drama as if that has anything to do with enlightenment is totally misguided. It is makyo, one's own mental projection. And to exalt the drama as if it's spiritually significant is the venture of the ego: spiritual materialism.
