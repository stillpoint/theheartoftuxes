    How does one navigate the intense pleasure of meditative stillness?

    >Is this energy to be corralled?

    If you let it be, it will move correctly. If you attach to it, it will become your latest demon.

    >It frightens me because it feels limitless.

    Anything, felt beyond the first surface shallowness, is limitless. That is why everything, but everything, can be a gateway to the boundless and eternal.

    And that is also why this boundlessness and eternity are not special. It is just what has always been here, working the way it has always worked. The only new thing is a new perception, a new view.

    It doesn't frighten you because it feels limitless; it frightens you because you think of it as limitless, and (understandably) your mind habit is attached to the illusion of limited, known, controllable things.

    >It's so overwhelming that it genuinely turns me on to an unbearable degree.

    That is fine, but it doesn't mean anything in itself. Many things appear, change, and disappear. Get used to having strange new experiences, because that is part of the career of any meditator. We just need not to be distracted by them. Know your direction and follow it. Stay open-handed, no matter what arises.

    If you allow yourself to return to object-fascination, the manipulative mind-habit will then know how to get you to turn away from your purpose every time: it will keep bringing up powerful or strange experiences. If you start to think much of yourself for having these experiences, the manipulative mind-habit will know how to get you to create a self-image, and will keep producing things that make you think of yourself as special. Just drop it.

    In fact, if you think about it you will add energy to it; and if you wrestle with it you will increase the pressure. Alexander Technique and newly-found meditation both open doors and release energies. Let them go their own way, don't think that you are the one to determine what should be done with them. If you start trying to steer energy you become a dictator of your mind — you make a Syrian civil war in your mind.

    ~

    There are schools of meditation where energies are in fact cultivated and guided. If that approach suits you — if you have a naturally balanced, compassionate, ungrasping character — and you want to do energy work, you need to hook up with a very experienced and capable master. It requires a guide who can, himself or herself, field the energy without being thrown off. Someone who is repeatedly and reliably able to go beyond ego, see the right path for you, and lead you along it. Close work together, and a level of trust, are required. Be aware that some teachers drawn to this type of work are themselves power-hungry, and their influence can vary from blind spots in relating to out-and-out vampiric-style possession. Also be aware that once you set the ball rolling, so to speak, a certain chain of events may arise that requires your further engagement whether you like it or not.

    If you have a very accomplished teacher and a regulated lifestyle, energy practice can have good results. But it always has the inherent risk of self-fascination and attachment to energetic manifestations. We see many, many people who have correctly cultivated their energy and have attained concentrative or charismatic powers, but who have not similarly clarified their life direction or character. Their beneficial influence on the world is haphazard, and they often suffer and cause suffering proportionally to their power.

    That is why, though I recognize the sort of masculine, power-seeking path as a valid one, I trust the feminine, open-handed path more. Water knows where to flow. Lightning finds its path of least resistance. Day and night follow each other perfectly. If you stay open-handed, everything will go where it needs to go.

    ~

    Mental, physical, and energetic manifestations that arise in meditation are referred to as makyo — phantoms or projections of the mind that is gradually being purified. Check meditation guides for the term makyo. You can also read my previous note to another meditator.

    Final note: in any case, it is best to find a good teacher sooner rather than later. And in cases where "overwhelming" becomes a frequent occurrence, the live, face-to-face guidance of someone who has been there and who has led many others through the territory is ten thousand times better than the scattered opinions of folks online, who have only the most fleeting and surface relationship with you and your situation.
