    True, pure meditation means no attachment to anything. The mind doesn't move. Nothing is given the power to push or pull you.

    In pure meditation, there is no need to try to be in the moment, because you already are the moment. It is as if your mind is perfectly still and open space, within which anything can happen. Appearing, remaining, and disappearing are all possible; you just don't attach to any of it. If a sensation appears, no problem. If a sensation disappears, no problem. You don't try to perceive or not perceive. Only don't touch.

    So in pure meditation there is no acknowledging. You don't go out after particular things, and you don't avoid other things. Actually, 'things' become none of your business. That is a very good way to describe meditation! 'Things' become none of your business.

    This is the mind that is clear like space, or clear like a perfect mirror. Anything can appear or disappear, and the space only spaciously allows it; the mirror only reflects it.

    But most people have a very strong attaching habit. Even if they receive this instruction it is difficult for them to stop automatically attaching to 'things'. A certain thought will appear, about anger or love or spirituality, or whatever they think is important, and they will have a hard time not following it. A certain sensation will appear, and they will have a hard time not making something special of it.

    So for these (most) people, extra help is added to pure meditation: they are given one clear object to return to. This one thing helps them practice letting go of all other things. And once their letting-go is very well practiced and strong, they naturally experience pure meditation.

    In object-meditation, the object must be clear and consistent. When you're trying to overcome the unstable, wandering mind, it won't do to have an object of meditation that promotes instability and wandering.

    So you can choose either pure non-grasping meditation, or you can choose an object-related method; but if you choose an object-related method it should be very clear what you are supposed to bring your mind back to at any moment. No vagueness.
