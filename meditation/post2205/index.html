    TheHeartofTuxes

    The instruction varies from school to school, teacher to teacher; so your experience reflects the situation out there. The important thing is to follow your teacher or the teaching source you're working with.

    Classically, the circle formed by the fingers should frame the dantien ('elixir field') between one and three inches below the navel. This can have some repercussions on energy cultivation, but it tends not to be as significant as many other issues, such as upright posture, mounting tension, adherence to method, etc., especially for beginners.

    First of all, the posture should be both awake and relaxed. Both aspects should be respected. Holding the hands higher can reflect a more upright posture and generally sharper quality of sitting. That's not to say that a more settled energy is necessarily wrong; it depends on the course of training, and that's why you should adhere to one source and method. Instructions make sense within the broader context of training within the school and tradition. You don't always know the deeper reasons until much later in your practice career.

    Hands in the lap may feel more relaxed, and if so that is fine. But you might also find that it can draw the shoulders down and/or forward, which can develop into tension and pain. On the other hand, some find that holding the hands unsupported puts a burden on the shoulders (trapezius muscles).

    One may wonder how one can be expected to keep the hands 'up' just below the navel. First of all, there should be an open circle to the arms, not a slouch. This circle, embracing the smaller circle of the hands, is an inherently strong structure, and it also promotes an open attitude -- both open and contained. When the mind is concentrated, the energy of the practice keeps the arms and hands in place without much muscular effort. There just seems to be a circulation of energy that is self-supporting.

    If it becomes burdensome to hold the hands like this, you can use a support cushion, placing one edge on the floor and angling the other edge under the hands. Some Japanese cushion sets include a support cushion for this purpose.

    Both ways are okay. More important is how are your mind and body postures. Follow the teaching you've received.
