    Awareness vs Thoughts?

    Meditation practice means you make a good trade: you trade in your thinking world for truth world. That is, you exchange labels and ideas for actual experience.

    So the effort in meditation is directed to letting the mind be very simple, letting the mind have a very direct relationship with the real moment. You taste an orange: only orange taste. You dive into a lake: only the cool, wet freshness. You meet someone you love: only present together, nothing extra. You sit with the breath: only breathing, no thoughts about it.

    Our thinking minds are very active. That is the correct function of the thinking -- to be active and go many places, making connections and new possibilities. But our habit is to let that function run uncontrolled, so when we need to pay attention to one thing we can't; our minds are running off.

    This is only a problem because of our attachment to thinking. We follow the thoughts and believe that they are our reality. If we could refrain from following thoughts, thoughts could come and go without disturbing us.

    When birds fly in the sky, they don't make a mark or harm the sky. Your original clear mind is clear like the sky: anything can appear in it without disturbing it. The clarity of the sky-mind is the primary state. But our habit is to follow the flitting birds and to see only them and to believe they are the essential reality.

    Meditation trains the ability to let go of the flitting thoughts and return to the clear sky nature of the mind. Disengaging from myriad formations that come and go -- thoughts, feelings, perceptions -- and returning again and again to the single object of meditation (or to plain openness in objectless meditation), one allows one's original clear sky mind to come forth into its correct place as the basis of reality.

    Simple, simple. Only return to the method of meditation, and let the mind be simple enough to experience the moment without any labeling or commenting about it. If it's difficult to believe that that is possible, It just means that attachment to thinking is strong -- so far it's the only reality you can imagine -- so more practice is necessary, and soon spaces will appear in the clouds where you can glimpse the clear sky mind.

    In simple terms, at first you will just not make a big deal of thoughts that appear. Later, as your power of concentration grows you will not attach to the thoughts that appear at all, and later still as the reality of the clear sky mind comes forward the thoughts themselves will settle and stop appearing unless you intend them to appear: only still clarity of mind, like an empty sky.
