    Loving kindness meditation is the ultimate piss off

    If you don't want to extend compassion to all others, you are not ready to be boundless; you are not ready to be your complete self. So that's just where you are. For a while, you want to hold onto your small and incomplete self and the pain that goes with it.

    You may think it's ridiculous to say that you want to hold onto your pain. You may think that you desperately want to get rid of your pain. That may be true too; but holding grudges and justifying hatred is already pain that you hold.

    We don't start off perfectly. That is why it is called 'practice'. We try, and fail. We try again, succeed a little, then fail. Try, try, try, try, try..... How else would you develop any sort of ability, whether it's developing compassion or perfecting a wrist shot?

    If you're not ready to see other beings as essentially equal to you, struggling with their own ignorance and suffering, then just do limited compassion practice. Don't think of people who trigger you. Find some being you can extend compassion to. Understand their vulnerability, their innocence, their pain, and wish them well from your heart to theirs. Sometimes a generous teacher could be the object of your meditation; sometimes a family pet or other innocent animal.

    When you start to connect to actual warm-heartedness it is because you recognize that being's situation, which is essentially the same as your own. We all face pain, sickness, old age, and death. We all fail to get everything we yearn for. We all fail to escape what we don't want. We all fail to hold on to any good situation forever. We all lose our possessions, our positions, our loved ones. We are all vulnerable in the face of the vast universe. We are all subject to our own ignorance. These are universally shared situations.

    The initial softness in lovingkindness practice is important. It is not a conceptual enterprise. Neither can you force yourself into it. You have to find a soft spot, the place where your own wounding or weakness connects with that of others.

    If you think of a wounded fawn, innocent and pure... bewildered by its injury and pain... does your heart not want to rush to comfort it, to do whatever it can do to ease the fawn's pain? We are all in this situation, bewildered by our suffering patterns. It is not the person that is the enemy, it is their ignorance. Their boorishness or aggression comes from past causes; it is the shape of their personal ignorance, just as we all have our own shape of ignorance. If they knew better, they would do better; but instead they are bearing the burden of ignorance and the results it brings into their lives.

    For example, just as your own anger and hostility are blanketing your feelings of woundedness and vulnerability, the anger and hostility of your enemies is really rooted in their own deep wounding. If you were wounded in exactly the way they were, perhaps you would have behaved in the same way.

    You don't need to open too far. Actually, if you push too far you could hurt yourself, and it may take longer to open. What you need is to find that first soft spot, and practice with that.

    Where is your soft spot right now?
