    Are there any kind of meditation that makes you feel tired afterwards? I feel like I want to meditate AND exercise at the same time today.

    A very excellent meditation performed daily in many temples, and over longer durations for special practice, is prostration practice. This is a rhythmic, whole-body-and-breath practice that trains one-pointed concentration within activity, provides opportunity for devotional practice (although it is not required), greatly enhances groundedness, and vigorously exercises the whole body.

    As a physical exercise, prostrations call on the major muscle groups of the legs and core, as well as the arms and chest, the breathing, and cardiovascular system. The practice is somewhat similar to Sun Salutations in Yoga, another great practice. The movements of prostrations massage the internal organs, enhancing digestion and elimination, and strengthen the floor of the pelvis, helping to retain vitality. The unity of posture and breath enhance physical, mental, and emotional integrity, and train the balance.

    As an adjunct to sitting, prostrations very quickly bring stuck energy out of the head (and thinking) and down into the center of the body. After a round of bows one feels more clear, invigorated, and ready to sit.

    As a meditation in itself, prostration practice allows for full expression: you can pour yourself out, emptying to the earth what needs to be emptied, and then stand up out of suffering in your upright and noble body-and-mind. In some traditions prostrations can be combined with other practices like repentance (either daily repentance or in special circumstances), going for refuge (in Buddhism, to the Buddha, Dharma, and Sangha), doing deity practice or other purification visualization, and/or repeating mantras. In other traditions, just doing the bowing with complete presence is the entire practice.

    Prostration practice is nearly the same movement as Hindu Squats, an exercise lauded for its development of endurance and lower body strength. One of the greatest wrestlers in history, The 'Great' Gama, used to do 5,000 squats daily as one of the two major exercises in his training (along with Cat Stretches).

    Prostration practice is recommended especially at times when the mind is too agitated to even think of sitting still, and during times of crisis. Its power to quickly ground and center the mind is widely recognized. Serious practitioners may commit to 500, or 1,000 or more prostrations daily. Special practice includes a day of 10,000 prostrations (a practice I've taken part in — about 8 hours of bowing), or a month of midnight prostration practice — getting up at 12 midnight or 1am and doing a set number of repetitions — both of which clear the mind and build spiritual energy very markedly. If there is a serious obstacle in your life or spiritual path, this kind of practice can dissolve it.
