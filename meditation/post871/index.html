    If you have not been instructed to close the eyes, keep them open. Half-open, with soft gaze, is the traditional form in most 'waking up' styles. This goes along with the cultivation of both 'inner' and 'outer' realms of experience, harmonizing and unifying them rather than just withdrawing from the world.

    Unless there is a clear purpose and clear function (like for example, a definite visualization or internally focused body scanning), closed eyes leads to drifting and dreaming. The agitated, object-clinging mind still seeks objects to cling to, so without a clear engagement the eyes may move around looking for something to glom onto. When the eyes are opened you can gaze slightly downward and take in the floor in front of you, or straight ahead and take in the wall or expansive scene. In general, we don't focus on anything with a drilling vision but rather soften all the senses and let them receive. That is, we don't 'go out through the sense doors'.

    In either case, eyes open or eyes closed, the method must be clear. You should know why you are doing the method and how to do the method. Thus, at any moment you know what to return to. This prevents both mind and eyes from wandering.

    Do you have a clear, proven method? Has it been taught in a complete way by a valid, able, confirmed source? Have you learned it completely, and do you apply it meticulously? These steps contribute in great measure to the clarity of your practice; and if any of the steps is not fulfilled, doubt and wavering will tend to be present in your practice.

    Eye strain often goes along with a grasping attitude in meditation. It's as if one reaches forward through the eyes, with patterns of muscle tension that also thrust the head forward and clench the jaw and belly, etc. Occasionally it works the other way, with physical tension arising first and visual tension following. Both are remedied by finding proper neutral posture in both body and mind: relax the body and find its self-supporting posture; breathe openly and naturally; let go of grasping attitude; and return faithfully and openhandedly to the method, relying only on the method to digest all mental or emotional issues during meditation. That is why you need a proper method, proper instruction, and full trust in the method.
