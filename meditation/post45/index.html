    This was a beautiful story to read. What a lovely, human experience: the peaceful practice, the unknown frustration, the breakthrough of grief, the full sobbing, the letting-go, and the resolution. Very beautiful.

    We don't necessarily know what is moving through. But it is necessary to let it move.

    > "If you're going through hell, keep going!"

    In a sense, enlightenment simply means that you never get stuck. Everything moves on, and you don't linger or get stuck through attachment.

    So meditation is one practice of letting things move. You bring your mind to its original still and open nature, and the inner and outer worlds are allowed to flow and arrange themselves. Like sitting still in a river. It is the embodiment of faith in yourself and in life. You don't need to know the reason for everything, and you don't need to control everything. Thus meditation, when performed openhandedly, is a healing activity: all energies move in their own correct pathways.

    So what was previously pent up and held in may find its way out. This can apply to emotion, and to thought, and to physical tension and patterning. (Body, heart, mind -- the whole being.) You may find emotions moving. Sometimes sadness, sometimes anger, joy, kindheartedness, or some unnamed feeling, etc.

    Or you may experience many thoughts arising -- sometimes lots of similar material, sometimes unusual or unexpected dialog or images. And then also, just the release of tension or energy may happen, with jerks or shakes or sudden relaxation.

    The main thing is to let it move through, and not get caught up in what's moving. Only let it be what it is. Then when finished, finished. Move on. So it sounds like your experience was right. When it's time to meditate, just meditate. When it's time to cry, just cry. When it's time to eat, just eat.

    > During this time, I felt the frustration build within me that I didn't know how to release because I very rarely cry or express my emotions freely.

    Holding things in, of course, leads to them coming out more forcefully and unexpectedly. It sounds as though you were remarkably fortunate and skillful in your interaction, just following the movement of the process. It shows that you have an intuitive resource, a wisdom that you can tap into. Even if your learned habit is to suppress emotion, you have the capacity to be natural with your emotions and energies, and to move on.

    Interestingly, your experiences seems to confirm something I referred to in a recent post, that some sources claim emotions last for a maximum of 90 seconds; and if an episode lasts longer it means that at some level we are regenerating the emotion (through our thinking or holding-on).

    Sitting meditation is one way of letting-be. You might be interested in other ways of being natural with and accepting your inner life. Journaling can be a way not only of letting things flow, but also making sense of things. Physical practice (like prostrations, Yoga, Taiji, or just rhythmic whole-body movement) is very good at smoothing out energy and encouraging flow. Free dance and conscious movement are excellent for feeling, expressing, integrating, and transforming the inner life. Art and art therapy can be enjoyable, creative ways to get to know and trust your feelings.
