    Is it possible to bring repressed desires to the fore; using meditation?

    I'm very glad if it helps.

    Remember that what is real is all completely momentary. In this moment you have the chance to put it all down and allow what is natural to speak for itself. In this moment you have the freedom to move in any direction.

    There is the broad view and the momentary view, the view of time and what happens over the course of time, and the view from within the living moment. Of course it can be helpful to see our patterns over time, to be aware of the changes we go through from phase to phase, to be conscious of our personal story and how it has been influenced by past relationships, environment, and events. But the practice -- the place where you actually have influence over your life -- is utterly momentary. It is only ever just now.

    Old patterns, established through deeply influential relationships and sometimes through profound wounds, and participated in over many years, do not disappear without similarly deep and consistent effort on our part. Or rather, they can disappear quite easily, but they come back for a time, depending on our karma (i.e., depending on how we have used our intentions and actions in the past).

    It is not our focus to get rid of everything negative in our minds. That is indeed our aim over the broad view, our aim over time; but our effort is very momentary. We just return to neutral in this moment.

    It is also very sincere. Just keep your right relationship to your mind, from moment to moment, and your sincerity will do its work on your being. Eventually everything will be purified; but that is not your concern. Your concern is to let go of the grip just this moment. Just keep your natural mind, without thinking about results.

    And keeping your natural mind means letting it all be. Easy times are easy, difficult times are difficult. Both are correct. Some people like you, some people don't. That is bound to happen. Sometimes you are clear and can see your way very well, sometimes you are not so clear and the way is dim. No problem, only follow the momentary situation.

    When it's time to get up, get up. When it's time to wash the body or clean the room, just do it. When it's time to work, you work, and when it's time to rest, you rest. Joyful time brings a sense of lightness, crying time brings tears, angry time requires clear communication of needs and boundaries.

    Continue to allow the moment to be what it is, and your innate natural mind will emerge.

    Good luck!
