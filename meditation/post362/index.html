    Spirit Guide?

    You may have met a spirit guide, but it's important to understand that no spirit guide exists separate from your own mind. It's not necessary to think of this as something special; just follow the wisdom that unfolds in you -- however it appears -- then let it go.

    One should also realize that there is no guarantee that all visions and voices that arise in you will be clear, wise, and benign. You need to distinguish the appropriate from the inappropriate.

    I say these things without prejudice against your experience. It is indeed possible to meet guides in meditational visions or in dreams; one just needs to refrain from making stories about their origins, and instead continue to turn attention to the task of finding the source of all beings and phenomena.

    It may play out that when you come into a more conscious command of your innate wisdom, the spirit guide will no longer need to appear in the form of a vision.

    Why did the image appear in the form of a woman? The formations that appear in our minds are culminations of past causes meeting current conditions. They are shaped by the structures built deep in your unconscious that move toward some things and away from others. The short-form title for this is â€œkarma". You make the world that appears for you.

    In dream language, the message that came to you represented a feminine aspect of the issue -- soft, indistinct, flowing, mysterious. It is perhaps interesting that a neutral, non-human formation didn't appear, nor a masculine form indicating a more defined, formal, or effortful aspect. So it may be of personal significance to your situation to proceed by embracing your intuition instead of trying to nail everything down in thinking-understanding, and sitting open-handedly with the great mystery instead of trying to make things happen through forms and efforts.

    In short, continue by opening your hands and letting it all be.

    I will refrain from talking about my personal experience, because I don't think it leads to a good relationship with practice to focus on these things, as comforting as they may be when they arise. They're best kept private, and secondary to the central aim of meditation.
