    Does it really matter how you sit?

    There are very clear and influential relationships between body and mind. Posture of body and posture of mind are closely tied to each other. Many of these relationships are only evident when you have developed greater awareness and sensitivity; but that doesn't reduce their effect or importance, it only means that you don't always see what is influencing you and you don't always understand until later why certain things are suggested.

    But even given the significance of physical form, it is secondary to the mind. If you have a clear direction and strong aspiration, then you can meditate in any posture, even hanging from chains.

    Sitting on the ground has wonderful benefits. Coming close to the earth is not to be underestimated. Our evolution has been intimately connected to the earth for many millions of years; it is only very recently that we have disconnected ourselves from the earth. Both our physical and our spiritual foods circulate through us from earth and sky. This is not a poetic, airy-fairy statement; it is a very simple view of what moves through us â€” a view that is easily seen by anyone willing to look.

    So sitting on the earth does have a meaning for us. Symbolic, yes, but that is not nearly all. Our shared illusion is that we are small separate beings in a vast and overwhelming universe of separate beings. When that illusion is dispelled, we come to realize that we are everything, we are all of life. Sitting on the earth is an embodiment and establishment of that realization. It's not necessary to sit on the ground, but it does have an influence.

    For example, have you ever gone camping and slept on the forest floor? Have you ever lain down on the beach? There is a certain soulful comfort, like coming home, is there not?

    In more mechanistic terms, the stability that comes from sitting on the floor or on a solid supporting platform allows the physical structure to find its balance, which allows the mind to ungrasp and relax. Sitting under your own power, without external support, trains your ability to sustain yourself. (For, when you no longer have the support, what will you do?)
