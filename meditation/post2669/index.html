    >...what I mean is feeling that I am quarreling with my ego as an 'other'.

    This goes under the category of thinking. It is just more thinking, along with all of the other thinking that is really besides the point. The only problem is that you attach to this particular thinking as more significant or real than the rest.

    Your thinking makes “I", and your thinking makes “ego", and all other characters in mind. When the mind is clear, the sun rises the east, water is wet, and sparrows chirp. Everything is just as-is.

    Here you can apply your rationality. Understanding that this whole ego-self dialog is just more thinking, you can strongly let it go or cut it off when it arises. Your letting-go doesn't necessarily mean it will go away or stay away all of a sudden; but you let it go nonetheless. You determine not to add more fuel to the fire. Gradually, the illusion of some important issue being played out will subside.

    Never mind the dialog, never mind the material within the mind. It is all equally illusory. Your engagement is with the open attention itself, the screen upon which the whole movie plays out. Don't get hooked by what's projected onto the screen.

    You say “easier said than done". Yes! Of course! That's why meditation is a practice, and not a pill you can swallow or a ticket you can buy. You need to do it. And you need to do it with the depth and consistency that can at least match all the negative patterns that you have created in your life (and, according to some, in all your past lives as well). Practice correctly, and these issues will dissolve.

    From what you say, my sense is that physical practice may help you a lot. You still need to clarify the steps and questions I posted in my other comment.
