    A little hesitant to start meditating

    Maybe it's a bit like people (women, especially) who fear they're going to become massive, hulking mounds of muscle if they start lifting weights. They don't realize that it takes a lot of hard effort over a long period of time, with slow and gradual gains, to get to that point... and that you can stop at any time if what's happening doesn't suit you.

    >I get mental images of becoming some crazy "enlightened" being...

    This is interesting, and a bit funny. Why should "enlightened" mean crazy rather than sane? One of the most significant results of correct meditation is that you can become far more comfortable with yourself and with the everyday situations you navigate. The reason some meditators may go into seemingly extreme forms of service is that those forms reflect what they really want — it moves through them naturally. Maybe before any practice we tend to ignore it when a friend is struggling, or we won't bother to help our mother carry the groceries. Later, when compassion develops and we're more aware and less selfish, we may help the friend or help the mother; but it won't be because we're forcing ourselves, it will be because we want to — it makes us happy. And even further on, maybe we will start helping people who aren't only friends or relatives, or even start helping people who are angry or weird or who have something against us; but again, it will be because the action comes from us naturally, and makes us happy.

    On the other hand, maybe the action that comes from you naturally and makes you happy will turn out to be the study of microbiology, or getting into big business, or working in a construction crew. The outside form isn't important. When your awareness and compassion develop, you will be able to move through all different kinds of situations freely. You just won't be hurting yourself and others as much.

    The first part of your statement, "I get mental images", is very significant. You are having difficulty and trepidation now because of the mental images you're making. That is the very point of meditation, to get free of the illusions that cause you distress, and to relate directly with reality. So in this case, the reality is that you don't know what it will be like in the future; you only know that you are enjoying practicing mindfulness lately. Creating mental images that cover up reality and cause you distress — this is the craziness that we can avoid through meditation.

    >I'm almost afraid of becoming more aware of who I am and my consciousness

    This is indeed fear-inducing for the part of you that wants to hold on to ignorance and unconsciousness. The part of you that wants to think of itself as something in particular, and always tries to get its perfect comfort, is very threatened by a practice that shines the light of awareness on it. But the part that is holding on has the qualities of immaturity and death. Life is always moving, always growing, always unfolding. Anything that resists change and growth resists life itself. It is a childish attitude, hoping that difficult things will never come, hoping that one's opinions will never have to be surrendered, hoping that laziness will equal happiness. It amounts to wanting to stay protected by one's mother throughout life.

    But you know the satisfaction that comes with achieving something on your own. You know the greater sense of inner stability that comes from taking on challenges rather than avoiding them. The mature person realizes that life will always be changing, that there will be many losses and many new gains; and the mature person becomes better at changing with change, better at moving on and unfolding into new forms.

    Meditation is the practice of this maturity: Moment to moment, you let go of the old and face the new. Moment to moment you let go of illusory images and ideas, and you return to plain reality.

    You're getting ahead of yourself with your imagining. Meditation provides the strength to tackle life. That is why meditators often venture farther afield: they have the strength to do it where others are too shy or incapable. So if you continue to practice meditation, you will develop the strength and centeredness that will allow you to face more of life naturally and happily. What you do with that strength will be completely up to you.
