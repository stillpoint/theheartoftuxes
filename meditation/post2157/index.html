    TheHeartofTuxes

    It's harder. That's all.

    ~

    Your body hurts. This is 'body-hurts' truth. You feel weak and the posture slumps. This is 'weak and slumping' truth. The mind wanders and has to be brought back more frequently. Never mind; practice is momentary, not a story.

    If you have to breathe through your mouth, no problem. This is extremely common. In retreat, in residential practice, and in monastic settings, someone is always getting sick. And the illness usually spreads through the community for some time. We're all in the same boat. Life means pain, sickness, aging, and death are shared realities. If we wish to go beyond our usual relationship with life, where we're completely controlled by our like and dislike, then we need to face these basic truths. Sometimes you just slog through it. Actually, often you just slog through it. Abandon the notion that your practice is always supposed to feel nice or be easy. You will gain as much, if not more, by continuing to show up even through difficulty.

    Remember: you are not 'sick'. That is just a story your mind makes. Everything is just truth.

    Having said all that, if you are at home on your own, it can be difficult to do it all yourself. In community you have all this supporting structure to help you: schedule, form, mutual support, good examples, pride (yes! the one of the rare situations where your pride actually helps you, by causing you to show up), teaching, supportive environment, and so on. On your own, showing up may often devolve into forcing yourself to sit rather than seeing clearly the reason for practice and feeling deeply your motivation. So sometimes you might manage your practice by somehow keeping touch with practice without pushing so hard that you give up completely.

    I find that using a mala helps a lot when I'm sick. Even through the haze, sleepiness, and pain, working the beads of the mala helps maintain some background awareness. It's up to you how strict you want to be, but you might decide for example to do much shorter formal sittings but to spend a lot more time with mindfulness and mantra practices in bed.

    In the future, if you come across a Tibetan prayer wheel or buy one (they're available for purchase online), this is another very easy way to do excellent practice even when very sick or weak.

    Walking meditation is also helpful during illness, for several reasons: The physical contact with the ground is a support to the dull mind (similar to the effect of fingers touching a mala, but with even more contact, and with the help of gravity). The gentle movement is easy enough to perform, changes the scenery and perspective, and helps the mind and energy from getting stagnant. The movement also helps move and drain the fluids in the head and body. The automatic arrival of the next step provides a structure that helps you keep coming back to presence.

    It's also okay to relax and rest. Some meditators don't realize this. They understand it in their thinking, but they don't really embrace it in their bodymind. Enjoying a hot bath and nap, and a good long time away from electronics, can be refreshing on many levels, beyond its helpful effect on the immune system. When the body aches, you can intentionally relax and rest instead of building up inner antagonism against your situation.

    This carries over into sitting. One very clever strategy in sitting meditation is to make yourself ever more internally comfortable. That is, instead of seeking to always change your environment to suit you, you relax your mind and body more and more. You use the power of your intention to let go and settle. You let gravity take away tension. You let breath soothe discomfort. You find and connect to the places in your body and mind that are comfortable, and you grow that comfort.

    This is a very interesting talent; and it has a very clever way of using pleasure-seeking as a way to keep you in the present moment. So take care not to assume that your situation is only uncomfortable or difficult. You can use your almost magical power to find comfort and ease at any moment.
