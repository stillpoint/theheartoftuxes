    There are many good ways, and some not so good ways. A few programs have sinister motives behind them.

    So you need to do your research. Just getting a consensus in reddit is not sufficient. YOU need to do the work, step by step.

    But if you choose a traditional path, taught by someone capable and verified by their lineage, then the best path is the one you carry out, the one you put into practice.

    I've said many times that there is a broad misconception about finding "the best path", as if one is out shopping. This misconception is a form of the ignorant, object-attached, picking-and-choosing mind. This is the very mind we are trying to uproot!

    A good path will put the ego into discomfort. It will (eventually) shine light on your faults, and will call you to go to situations that previously disturbed you and let go of situations that you previously clung to. But it will do this step by step, in proper order, in the context of the particular path.

    You need to take responsibility by not being lazy, letting go of the picking-and-choosing mentality, doing proper research, and offering your own time, effort, and resources to the path.

    There's no perfect path out there for you. Each way has its good and bad points, its light and shadow. A way is perfect because you make your own effort perfect; you show up perfectly.

    A person who proceeds in a selfish and lazy way can't make use of any 'best' program. A person who proceeds with wise and enterprising engagement can make the best of many ways.

    If you look at traditional paths in Buddhism, in Yoga, in shamanism, in Christianity... you can find great awakened beings in all of these. But some paths have more clear teaching and teachers; so you need to do your research.

    The final point is really the first point: "best" for what?

    You are asking people to assume they know what's best for you; but we don't even know what your aim is. Do you?

    The first point, before seeking out a program, is to know what you are looking for. Why do meditation? Why do anything in life? Why be alive and make an effort at something? When your life direction is clear, then you can distinguish the correct program that will further that direction. When your life direction is unclear, all kinds of advice will fail to stick.
