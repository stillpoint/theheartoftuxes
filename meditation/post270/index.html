    These things come up at the beginning because a new and unusual situation is presented to the mind. The noise covering the less conscious layers is dimmed, and new things rush in to fill the void.

    These things also arise when the mind is used incorrectly in meditation, pushing to achieve or avoid particular states. The manipulative mindset puts pressure on the mind, which forces up active and vivid material from the depths of the dreaming mind.

    Visions, sounds, physical manifestations, energetic manifestations, and non-ordinary states may also emerge due to correct use of the mind in meditation, when the gentle opening allows greater communication between layers of mind, and previously suppressed material bubbles forth. If you don't touch it, it's purification — it makes your being clear as the material moves out. If you touch it and engage it with your thinking or manipulating, you just create more mental material and attaching habit, the very stuff you're working to clear out.

    The ego (the sense of selfhood) always seeks control. That pattern in the mind doesn't want you to relax, let go, and get clear. It doesn't want you to be broad life; it wants you to always stay a small, unmoving, known entity. So it will cast baited hooks into the waters to try and get you to bite. If you are engaging with the material in your mind, you stay a small, unchanging, separate self.

    At first, the ego's bait may be random distraction. As you improve your concentration and let go of this kind of attachment the ego will cast juicier bait: interesting 'insights' or special visions. If you let go of those consistently, the ego will keep searching for the thing that grabs you. Maybe frightening experiences, or — after you've overcome all of these — thoughts of pride and greatness. It's always looking for the thing that will catch your mind like velcro and get you to 'do', to exist as ego.

    The very best thing you can do for yourself, the thing that will save you years and lifetimes of diversion and trouble, is to treat all of that material as mere illusions, wisps of smoke in the air and bubbles on a stream, and to very simply and quickly return to your method of meditation. Entanglement can be endless. Learn to let it go, don't take the bait, and return to practice. This will take you along the path, far beyond those who attach.
