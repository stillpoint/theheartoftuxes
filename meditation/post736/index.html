    What is a good meditation technique for my third eye?

    Sometimes spectacularly transformative things can happen, energy-wise; but so much of what people attribute to openings is actually blockage or local stagnation. Spiritual energy is cool and subtle; it doesn't show up in these overt ways. True openings are accompanied by transformation of character and insight, not just pressure or heat or visions. But I guess people want to think of themselves as special. (Not that that's what you're doing, OP.)

    Let it all be, let go of holding, making, and manipulating all energy, and then energy will go where it needs to go, like lightning finding its ground or water flowing to the ocean. The bottom line is: do you trust yourself enough not to manipulate anything artificially?

    Next, you shouldn't assume that your forehead energy existence is a third eye manifestation. It may be, it may not be. It may be somewhat associated with the third eye, or it may be completely tied up in it, or it may be something else altogether. But what is clear is that if you hold an assumption about it you will have a harder time seeing what it really is. You will be dealing with your idea rather than a living, shifting, interconnected reality.

    If the manifestation is a problem and you need a specific practice to deal with it, I recommend the Water meditation, known in several varied forms in Taoism, Qigong, and Zen. Bruce Frantzis describes it in detail in some of his teaching materials. Zen Master Hakuin recommended it as a healing practice for these (and worse) kinds of blockages and stagnation. In general, it is a conscious dissolving from the crown of the head downward. One can use the sense of dissolving, or can picture water or thick, fragrant cream pouring down and softening and dissolving all tensions and blockages.

    This is a more specific application of my first point: in order to use energy correctly -- to have your right relationship with it -- you have to let it go and let it be. Grasping can indeed make something happen, but the wrong relationship of grasping will be built into what you get.

    Anyway, consider how much you can get by grasping and how much you can get by ungrasping: A hand can only grasp a handful of ocean; an ungrasped ocean is far more powerful. Likewise, what your limited effort can hold -- even the effort of an expert -- is tiny in comparison to what un-holding can hold.

    One of the biggest mistakes in practice is to think "I am doing practice." Making the idea "my energy" is already a mistake, already illusion. Why toy with local energies when you can get everything? I hope you turn to the important point behind it all.

    Good luck!

    Edit: Also check my recent post on movement meditation and the movement component of practice. Practices like Taiji, Bagua, free dance, and especially prostrations will, I'm sure, help you bring the energy down out of your head and into your more stable lower tantien, and will help it all flow much better.
