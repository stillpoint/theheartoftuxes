    Making a habit of practice

    > I guess my goal with meditation is results based as the ultimate goal from it is enlightenment.

    That's not bad, and also not complete. In a sense we may aspire to enlightenment, and that is a correct fruition of our human life. But we must also realize that before we attain it, enlightenment is nothing more than an idea for us, and attaching to any idea — even a sublime one — limits us and causes us to lose our way.

    The complete way is to have complete faith in Original Enlightenment, meaning that from the very beginningless beginning you are already enlightened. There is not a single thing lacking in you. Try sitting down with complete faith in that: "I am already complete. This world, this life, this moment is already complete." That inevitably brings you back to the moment, because there is no longer anything whatsoever to be sought outside of you in some imagined future or some imagined other realm.

    In real life, the traditions that have focused the most on the notion and pursuit of enlightenment really may not talk about it much at all. I've practiced at many different Zen centers, in many different schools, and there's actually not a lot of talk about enlightenment. Some high-falutin' concept isn't the point; the point is: what is in front of your face right now?

    So instead of holding up enlightenment as something you need to chase, elders and guides in Zen will rather have you clean the toilets or do some gardening. If they talk about anything, they are more likely to talk about being clear. Moment to moment, let go of opinion and return to uncontrived engagement with what faces you. This is already of the same nature as your Original Enlightenment, and if you maintain your practice it merges with your Original Enlightenment more and more. Then one day you may find that the bottom drops out of your previous world-view and everything comes clear and bright.

    But these breakthrough events are not called "enlightenment experiences" because you get enlightenment. You already have it! They are called enlightenment experiences because it dawns on you that you already have it. You cease to think that you are in any way deficient or separate from vast life.

    So you should have this faith from the very start. Don't doubt it! If you doubt it, you turn away from reality and you turn away from your own wellspring of wisdom. This is not a matter of pride, it is a matter of being what you really are. So when you sit in meditation, don't sit as little Dulcette doing meditation, sit as the entire universe unfolding in its simple way right here. All mysteries and powers are included.

    You sit the same way the stars sit in the sky, the same way rocks and water sit on the earth. Or you might say that you keep a mind like space: anything and everything can happen in space, and space is not disturbed or marked by anything that appears, and space is not harmed by anything that disappears.

    Sitting this way means that your heart can be as it is, moment to moment. No need to hold it in a particular form. "As-is" is already Original Enlightenment, okay?

    Good luck!
