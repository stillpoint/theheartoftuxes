    This is very, very common. But before addressing your concern, one thing we need to note is that feeling, alone, doesn't determine success. The fact that you intend kindness and practice kindness is very significant, very beneficial, regardless of the feelings that by their very nature ebb and flow.

    Three things you can do:

    • Keep trying. When the ground is hard, you have to keep digging; eventually you will get to the treasure. If you stop trying because it's hard, you defeat yourself.

    • Get appropriate support. Live teaching, with a living role-model of metta; practicing with others who share your sincere motivation, and who are also facing and overcoming these kinds of challenges; receiving instructions and encouragements that ease the way; taking on practice commitments that keep you moving consistently in a positive direction — these are the kinds of supports that are very helpful. And by participating in these relationships you also help others in their practice.

    • In metta practice, find the point where your heart softens; that is the entry. It's commonly recommended to evoke the memory of someone who has helped you selflessly, like a teacher or family elder who showed up for you more than might be expected. It might only be an isolated experience during which you experienced selfless care; maybe it was even a stranger who offered a kind word or gift. This can, if you remember the kindness and feel gratitude for it, bring you to your more sensitive heart. These remembered scenarios can be turned around too, putting yourself in the position of the caregiver, and imagining their warm, protective feelings to the vulnerable you. You might even consider what you would say or offer to your younger self, the one who was waiting for love to appear.

    Many metta practitioners work with images of vulnerable animals or current or past pets to get at the soft heart. For me, if I think of my (now deceased) cat lying down on my chest and purring, or remember the feelings I experienced when she was sick, I very quickly return to my soft, warm, caring heart. That feeling is then what is nurtured and radiated out to self and others.

    >This may have something to do with the fact that I have received little love during my life....

    Yes, of course this is a major cause — perhaps the major cause — of vacant feeling, low affect. If it wasn't put into you it's hard to call it up. So you need to take more care of yourself. Use your intention to face the issue and apply the cure of loving care; use your power of creative imagination do develop skillful gateways to the soft, vulnerable heart; use your power of concentration to keep coming back to the task.

    Outside of metta practice you may do well to express yourself. It is very important, very healthy. Maybe write a journal: the pages are like the Bodhisattva of Compassion — open, ready to accept whatever you express. Maybe do free writing practice (see Writing Down The Bones by Natalie Goldberg). Or art, or music, or free dance, or some other form of expression. Physical practice like bowing is also very, very, very helpful in promoting flow.

    There are many good forms of therapy that help you access your feelings and self-care. Art therapy, music therapy, dance therapy, and traditional talk therapy and counseling (among others) are possible options to aid your quest for emotional flow and integrity.
