    Have Never Meditated

    That is wonderful that you see the risk of getting swept away by the busy events of life, and that you are looking to be self-possessed and engaged. You may or may not realize it, but this awareness is extremely valuable, extremely fortunate. It gives you the chance to help yourself and those around you.

    Yes, absolutely, you can practice returning to real life; you can become more okay with real life; and you can get the good short-term and long-term results of that practice. Momentary presence is the practice: being here just now.

    Whether you practice for a long time or a short time, any practice of presence is a benefit. How could returning to reality not be a benefit? One moment of clarity helps you and everyone.

    You already know the place to start. You start where you are. Just now, let go of trying to get or make anything else, and bring awareness to what's happening. This doesn't have to feel like anything in particular. The important point is the application of awareness. What's happening — the content of that awareness — will always change, change, change. Sometimes nice, sometimes not nice, sometimes so-so. But what is wonderful is that your awareness can embrace it, can be stable, can be nobly undisturbed by any loss or gain. As life comes and goes, as your fortunes rise and fall, your awareness can become brighter.... more stable, establishing peace and happiness that don't depend on the ups and downs.

    So always start where you are. Just here, just now, put it all down and just perceive the momentary scene.

    Your second "place to start" can be finding a good, proven method of meditation and following it. If this can be from a live teacher, that's much, much better; but if it's from a book you can also benefit. There are many good methods, and there are many false methods that won't help you, or won't help you much. The good methods will tend to have one point in common: non-grasping application of attention. You are not trying to make anything happen, but rather bringing your open presence to whatever moment arises, as-is.
