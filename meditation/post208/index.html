    Zen Master Joan Halifax spoke of a time when she was visiting friends in the countryside and went cross-country skiing alone. She lost her way, darkness fell, and she began to get cold and exhausted. It was then — at the time when clear thought was most critical to her survival — that all kinds of junk started to pour out of her mind, in particular all kinds of violent or just trivial and stupid images from TV and movies.

    This is what happens. The Storehouse Consciousness stores everything. Fortunate for her, she was very experienced in meditation, in coming back to reality; and she was very aware of how the mind works. So she strongly rejected those images and found her way back to her real situation (and also found her way back to her friends' cabin). She says the experience showed her how we are responsible for what we take in, and how the cause (in this case consumption of images, stories, trains of thought) has inevitable effect.

    There is a method of dispelling demons and monsters. The way to dispel all demons is compassion. Running away and avoiding one one hand, or aggression and conflict on the other hand, only make the demons bigger in your mind. But all demons and monsters share the same essential heart that we all have; it has only been twisted by great wanting or anger or hurt.

    So you can come from a position of strength by offering compassion and turning to face and even welcome your inner demons. You can ask them what they need. Feed it to them. You can use your power of imagination to face your demon, tune in to its need, and feed it what it craves. See it taking the food and becoming satisfied. When a demon becomes satisfied it becomes an aspect of wisdom or power. What part of you needs to be fed in order to turn to wisdom?
