    Inexperienced meditator here; looking for advice on some things.

    Difficulty and achievement in meditation are matters of cause and effect. To get a certain result you need to have the contributing causes, and to eliminate a certain result you need to eliminate the contributing causes.

    I'm asking about the root causes operating in your situation. As most meditators can attest, the problem you name at first blush is often only part of the story, if even relevant at all. In order to help you in your situation, I need to see if you are clear on the reason for choosing your method and for making the choices that you make in your practice.

    You write that you are inexperienced, and that you've been interested in meditation off an on. You write that your motivations are to "increase focus, improve my 'mind's eye' and imaginative skills, and to have my tulpa gain full independence. …to explore everything I possibly can do with my mind and unlock every hidden secret within human reach." You say that you've tried tulpaforcing, audio-based hypnosis, and focusing on an apple. And finally, you say that you feel panic and conclude that your mind fears losing control.

    On their own, these are very common dynamics among newcomers to meditation. Together, they form a picture of big ideas based on wavering foundation; but we need to check whether this is really the case, or whether you have more to say about it. What you wrote in your post all suggests quite a bit of unclarity about the correct direction and purpose of meditation. And in the interest of both setting aside assumptions and also pointing you to the central issue I asked you to respond on this point. Is it on your radar? Are you aware of the reasons why you chose the things you've been trying — not the things you hope to get, but the ideas and forces that drove you to these decisions?

    Tulpaforcing is a derivative practice, quite divorced in its contemporary application from its proper context and tradition. It is easily perverted into a misguided, egoic pursuit that plays with illusion. Those fascinated with strange states of mind and internal dramas are easily drawn to the practice. It is also widely associated with drug use. Its modern online form is more "dungeons and dragons" than "Great Bodhisattva Way". If you want to use tulpaforcing correctly, the correct underlying motivation is necessary. The greater the use of imagination and mental excursion, the more essential a clear and unshakeable motive is.

    Is this a momentous matter, or just dabbling? If you are serious about it, then let's start at the foundation.

    So my original response to you was very much to the point. The fact that you (and some others) don't see why it is salient shows that this is a weak connection for you (and them). There's no reason why you need to believe me, and I may not be the best person to make recommendations; but if you want to look deeply and sincerely into what meditation can do for your life, this is the starting place: Why do you eat every day?

    If you want to proceed, we can see how to adjust your relationship to this practice or find a practice that is a better fit for you in your particular situation.
