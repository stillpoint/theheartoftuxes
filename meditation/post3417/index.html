    The problem isn't that you can't switch off the thinking mind; the problem is that you are attached to the thinking mind. You attach to the things in it, assuming them to be important. Thus they move you.

    The practice of meditation is to be unmoved. You practice not moving, not attaching to mental objects like thoughts and images. The thoughts and images can appear, remain, or leave; but you don't follow them.

    In most popular meditations, this is made easier by using an object. You return to one object so that you can practice letting go of all others. It is like using one thorn to remove others from your foot.

    Really, the object of meditation is not special. It just needs to be clear and neutral, not something that will agitate you further. When you have a proper object, stay with it. If you get taken away, return. Let this one object save you from all others; let it save you from getting caught up in analysis.

    When meditating, you can leave behind hopes of getting rid of certain thoughts. You can leave behind hopes of falling asleep, or getting other benefits. Don't think of anything you may or may not get in the future. Only let the one object be clear. Keep returning to it.

    When meditating, you don't need to blame yourself for getting distracted. You are distracted because of many other factors. It is your mind habit, created by certain misunderstandings and wounds in your upbringing, and deepened through years of habitual behavior. So it is natural process. Never mind; only return to the one object of meditation. That is how you express your freedom, and how you train your stability.

    As much as possible, don't move from the object. Then, gradually, you will learn how to detach from the analytical mind. You don't kill the mind; but it no longer controls you because you no longer hold and follow and believe in it.

    At higher levels of concentration, the thinking mind actually does settle and become silent at times; but this is not the true attainment. The true attainment is not to depend even upon silence. Keep your center still, and it doesn't matter whether something is moving in your thinking or not.
