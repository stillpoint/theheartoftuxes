    TheHeartofTuxes

    Deep meditation means you do it. Don't attach to any particular state. Don't try to check, label, compare, and judge your state or level or attainment. Only do it. That is deep meditation.

    When distracted, return.

    When you fall away from meditation, return.

    Cause and effect will of course be apparent in your practice. If you have not been consistent, you will see the effects. If a disruption in your body or heart or mind or living situation has created disturbance in your schedule or your sitting, you will see the effects. But what will you do, stop returning to reality? No, the practice is to just come back. Return to the method.

    Never mind the story. Never mind "my mind does such-and-such'. All that is just natural process, natural coming and going. For aeons you have been swallowed up in the coming and going of appearances. But now you have applied yourself to the task of freedom from the coming and going of appearances.

    Therefore, whether appearances are coming or staying for a time or going, your job is only to return to the method. That is the 'tip': return. Don't check. You can assume that you will be taken away a trillion billion times. That's all appearance. Don't get caught up in it. Only return. Your returning is wonderful.

    If your mind is particularly agitated, you may need to adjust the circumstances of your practice, to get more support for your stability of mind:

    • consistent practice

    • correct posture

    • correct relaxation

    • natural breath

    • physical practice (very helpful before or instead of sitting meditation)

    • clean, organized living and practice space

    • regulated diet, sleep schedule, and physical activity

    • clear and supportive relationships

    • proper work, and proper balance of work and rest

    • ethical behavior (you can't have a balanced mind if you do bad action)

    • proper teaching

    • consistent relationship with a teacher

    • practice community
