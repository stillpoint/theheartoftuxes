    Experience about meditation and initial bias

    Yes. Snap judgments are part of an almost bottomless outpouring of unreasonable, petty, or negative activities from humans. The root cause of all of them is ignorance. Only attend to ignorance and waking up from it; the rest will be taken care of in its time.

    It is similar to the way we may avoid getting dragged into gossip or a petty or trivial conversation. "No thank you. You continue if you want; I'll be on my way."

    Be careful about how you use your words. Saying "I should focus myself towards bringing myself towards a more neutral state" is correct in a way, but if you envision it as a state that you have to bring about and hold in some way, that just encourages you to judge your own situation and condition. Instead of just being neutral you may then become fixated on checking whether you are neutral, figuring out what that is supposed to look like or feel like, trying to be that, and struggling against things that supposedly don't look or feel like it.

    No phrasing will be absolutely accurate, but I will often recommend "ungrasping", "unknowing", and "letting it be". You settle and relax the grip of the mind rather than seeking out any state or getting bound up in any object. This is expressed completely in the moment. That is important. Open awareness is utterly momentary. There is no such thing as "I was neutral for an hour" â€” that's just a story one makes up about one's experience. If you are truly neutral, then only the present reality appears.

    In meditation, this momentary expression follows the method. Thought comes: drop it without making another thought, and return to the method. "Don't cry over spilt milk." Milk spills: immediately turn to the cloth to wipe it up, then continue on your way. Snap judgment comes: immediately drop it, then return to following the momentary situation. In meditation, follow the method. In daily life, follow what's happening.

    Dropping it doesn't have to mean it will go away. You should not care whether it stays or goes; that's none of your business. Your business is keeping your own mind in the right relationship, which means aware and ungrasping.
