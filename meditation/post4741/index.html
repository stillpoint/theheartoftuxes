    Stick with 1 style or experiment with a few

    One likes to think that there is a situation that will just 'click' with one's personality and make it easy to achieve great heights; but that's a fallacy and a fantasy. Achievement is gained in exact proportion to correct effort in correct method.

    Going step by step up the mountain is how you get to the top. If you shift around to start another route up, that's fine, and you may have gained some kind of experience from the previous effort, but you are still essentially starting from the bottom and you are still essentially climbing step by step. These things can't be skipped.

    And there are many correct methods. Of course, there are many more shallow or bad methods as well. So it is best to find a traditional, proven method, taught by a well regarded and achieved master — someone who has attained the fruits of the practice; if not the 'mountaintop', then at least 'high up and experienced in climbing'.

    More important than style is the teacher. If you have the opportunity to study with a very stable and accomplished teacher, that is very precious, and you should thank your lucky stars for the opportunity. If you have the opportunity to study with a true master, that is exceedingly rare and inconceivably valuable, and you should not hesitate to do everything in your power to deepen your connection and follow their guidance.

    Once you learn the practice and integrate it in your life, it will become an organic part of you. Then it will 'suit' you. Before you yourself integrate the practice by learning and doing enough, there is no style that will suit you. This is because the ego will always resist good practice. The self-image and the habit of preference don't want to go away, so they will dislike and resist. The integral being, however, loves the rigor and truth of practice — it comes alive! But only through participating, not by arranging and doubting and hedging bets.

    That is not to say there is only one way to go about the search. You may indeed try out several different styles. It doesn't mean you will necessarily know any more (or less) than if you stick with one. You may try this and that, you may get confused, it may prompt a crisis, you may give up... and then you may be ready to start from a very good foundation.

    Or you may stick with one style and teacher for a long while, until you start to feel the practice in your bones, and you may at that point feel the need to explore or seek more depth.

    Most spiritual journeys are not all one way or the other. You may try one practice with different teachers, or you may read some books before trying, or you may have some fortuitous meetings with other students or teachers. Your travels may bring up new connections; your gains and losses in life may shift your priorities.

    So in view of all this one may ask a very good question: what is the central point? What's the point of it all; what's the goal? That is a very good guiding question for you. Why meditate or do any spiritual practice, or anything in life? What is the central point?

    • All suffering comes from mind. As your mind goes, so goes your life. As our minds go collectively, so goes our world.

    • We will certainly die.

    • We do not know when we will die, but when death comes we will surely feel it comes too soon.

    • At the time of death, none of our possessions, relationships, ideas, habits, awards, roles, or achievements can help us. The only thing that can help is the actual spiritual practice we have done in life: if we have acted with clarity and compassion throughout our life, we may have some ability to guide our mind at a deep level; but if we have acted with unclarity or selfish attachment for much of our life, the deep mind will continue in that vein, flailing about in unclarity and being enslaved to attachment (and thus creating the causes of ongoing suffering).

    • Therefore, meditation is for the purpose of developing clarity and compassion, to awaken from the dream of life,and to save yourself and save all beings from going around and around in suffering.

    If you keep that central purpose foremost in your heart, you will be able to distinguish between true spirituality and fake packaging.
