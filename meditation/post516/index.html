    Newbie meditation experience

    Yes, this and many other feelings, thoughts, visions, energetic manifestations, physical manifestations, doubts and realizations, and sensations are all normal. They are also all beside the point, and only serve as impediments if you attach to them.

    No need to figure out what it means. Things will clarify best when you just return openly to the present moment, which is the thrust of meditation practice. Don't stay in the past with the already-gone crying experience.

    But I'm interested in your statement that you "tried not to get attached to them". What does that mean?

    When it's time to cry, just cry. Then move on. The crying and the moving on are both non-attachment. Trying to push something away, or hide it or avoid it â€” that is attachment.

    So only do your meditation, and when you are not meditating just follow the situation. When it's time to eat, just eat; when it's time to cry, just cry; when it's time to help, just help.

    So one of the slogans in the Tibetan Seven Points Of Mind Training is:

    >Post-meditation, be a child of illusion.

    Even though all of these events are like phantoms that appear and disappear, in your day-to-day life just "be a child of" the situation. Only follow what's arising.

    During meditation, although it is certainly permissible to cry or feel anger or fear or love or joy, your job is to follow the method of meditation. You will work out a lot of these things on your own just by doing the practice; and what you gain will be a thousand times more helpful than whatever someone here can tell you.

    But you will hopefully find that meditation can embrace everything. There is no event or phenomenon that needs to be left out; meditation shines a light on it all, just like the sun shining on the earth: the sun doesn't decide that something is bad and doesn't deserve its light, or that something is good and should receive more, the sun only shines.

    If you are counting breaths when sadness arises, then sadness is counting breaths. (Not to say that sadness is the only reason why someone would cry.) If you are chanting when sadness arises, then sadness is chanting. What arises doesn't belong to you, it belongs to the world, so let it be. Things come up from the depths; let them. Things disperse and return to nothing; let them.
