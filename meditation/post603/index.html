    That's a lie. You are lying to yourself when you say "I am unable to dis-spell the illusion that they... deserve my rumination...."

    You should say instead this way:
    "I have tried a little bit in one way, but I have been judging my effort and results."

    Patience is a very important key to practice. Not the only key, but a very important one. These days, who has patience? And who has taught us patience? Certainly not many of our leaders, icons, media... and also certainly not many of our fellow Redditors, who clamor to have their idea of attainment achieved immediately and without much effort.

    We have a deep, deep, deep, deep, deep, deep habit of attaching to the illusion of thought. If you expect to overturn that habit completely, it will take commensurate effort:

    • correct method

    • correct motivation and approach

    • correct application of the method (i.e., not hedging, tinkering, doubting, mixing it)

    • consistent application of the method

    • application with right intensity

    In meditation, we don't actually concern ourselves with dispelling the illusion. We dispel our attachment to it. We see the lila — the play of manifestation, the mind's movie-like projections — but we don't let it move us. That is meditation: not moving, regardless of the projections that arise and pass.

    In order to achieve this, use structure properly. If you rely only on your own guesses, that deep, deep, deep, deep, deep, deep habit will take over. Even if you try doing 'spiritual' activities, they will be habit-driven spiritual activities. Even if you try understanding by reading and analyzing, it will be habit-driven reading and analyzing.

    So we use structure (correct structure) as something unconditional, something beyond habit. A teacher who can impart a proven method surely and in a way you can digest (which takes time); a teacher who has attained the fruit of the practice, who has gone beyond deep habit or who can at least reliably access that view when necessary — this is the kind of guide who can see you through past your own illusion. Someone who can hold that unmoving space for you, demonstrate it, teach it, lead you to it, lead you back to it when you wander off unawares, reflect it to you when you have it (because you don't necessarily know)... and train you in carrying it and expressing it throughout countless life situations: this is a guide who can help you.

    And you use the structure of one very clear method of meditation as an unconditional influence, returning to the method no matter what.

    And you use the structure of practice commitment: a set duration, a vow to do your best and not give up within that duration, an understanding of what to do with your body and mind at any juncture.

    And you use the structure of schedule, group practice, and lifestyle adjustments to help keep you coming back to practice in the right way.
