    Help me hack the reality

    >Cogito ergo sum.

    So if no cogito, then "ergo quid?" (If I don't think, then 'therefore what?') This is the point. Thinking makes "I". If you put down thinking, "I" disappears. No personal "I", no shared "I", no universal "I". One must put down all thinking to attain reality.

    Likewise, "I will probably never know" seems humble, but it actually perpetuates the dualism. One can't say, whether not knowing is probable, or likely, or possible, or otherwise. But there is an assumption here: that knowing belongs to a self.

    The mind stuck in conceptualization can only imagine a conceptualized world. So the alternative to assuming becomes speculating: If I can't assume that I know, then the only thing left must be to guess. But to the mind free of conceptualization, there is a verifiable reality. It is found directly, not through speculating, guessing, figuring. It is found and experienced; and from that arises a confidence that is absolute. There is no 'maybe' to it. Not because one has adopted a belief with religious fervor; because that kind of belief is on shaky ground, and the fervor is a compensation for the shakiness. No, the ground of your mind is a reality to be found and confirmed through direct experience. If you put down all concepts you can go straight there in an instant.

    Putting down all concepts is Un-knowing; but it is also a kind of knowing.
