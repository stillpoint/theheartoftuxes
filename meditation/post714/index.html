    >That is not special, only another phase. A step along the path. The question is whether you now want to focus on and obsess about the experiences, or whether you will continue sincere practice.

    >When the minds starts to calm down, the backlog of suppressed unconscious material bursts forth. Depending on the method of meditation and one's relationship to one's own mind, extra pressure exerted can make this bursting forth even more intense and eventful. What bursts forth is not significant. It is just mind stuff. "I feel this, I think this, I sense this, I... I... I..." it is all mind-stuff couched in a story about "I". Let it go.

    >Why practice meditation? To be self-fascinated? To get abilities that one thinks of as superior? Why do you practice meditation?

    >Meditation is for the purpose of liberating you from your "I" story. If you then engage in your "I" story because of meditation, it is self-defeating, a wasted effort. Let it go, and return to a clear method.

    >So the next important issue is: find and follow a clear method. Do you have one? Do you need some guidance in that regard?
