    Hello. I have an extremely lazy mind.

    I have an extremely laxy mind too. That's okay. What do you want?

    Meditation is easy; so meditation is a great option if you are lazy. But if your mind goes around and around thinking this and that, consuming reading and tv and conversation and internal dialog, this is not lazy — it is very effortful! Your thinking is a big expenditure of energy. 80% of your energy goes to thinking.

    It's not that meditation is difficult or that your everyday life is easier. It's that your everyday life is common. You're used to it. When we get used to suffering, we often prefer to have it rather than have a comfort we don't yet know. We prefer to keep what we already know, even if it is not helping us.

    The essential solution isn't to force yourself to do more meditation. That may work for rare people who are very headstrong and always finish what they decide to do; then, the meditation itself will help clarify their minds, and they will see the connection between discipline and result. But the essential solution is to understand why meditation is necessary for you. You have to look at your life and see what is truly working and what is not; what you need to let go of and what you need to nurture.

    Seeing what causes suffering in your life, and what you can do to get out of it, is the first step of the Noble Eightfold Path in Buddhism: Right View. But this is not just a Buddhist truth. In order to progress well, you have to at least begin to see where the problem lies and what progress entails. So first, right view; then right intent — what are you going to do about it?

    Seeing will give you the correct motivation, because then the movement will come from within you: it will be your motivation, based on what you have realized, not something forced on you according to what someone else said.

    So, are you content, happy, at peace? If so, maybe you should continue doing what you're doing. Only come to meditation if that's what you really want — if you see that it will help you.

    ~

    If, upon assessing your life, you find that you can't do without meditation, let me know if you want further help getting an effective practice. You should have a clear method, and you should know what to do with your body and mind when you practice. You should also have an idea of why the chosen practice has the form it does. Then you can set aside doubt and just do it.
