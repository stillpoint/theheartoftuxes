    Unattained (unenlightened) people don't necessarily know.

    You can know some things through ongoing contact. For instance, you can see whether someone's actions match their words consistently, whether they are clear and compassionate in their activity, and so on. You will be better able to perceive these things when you yourself are clear on your correct direction in life. People who have a selfish approach, greedy for knowledge and power, will be more easily swayed by flashy talk and displays of authority. Those clear about their correct direction — to wake up and help all beings — will more easily perceive when someone else is aligned with that direction as well.

    Masters in a traditional lineage have been trained and confirmed by other masters. So this is a way of checking that someone actually got what they or others say they got. You can inquire about their lineage and the confirmation they've received. In the Zen tradition, when someone passes the entire course of koans and also wakes up completely and attains the 'dharma' of their master, they go around to other masters to be tested and confirmed.

    In a few cases the confirmation may be vague, as when a master has changed lineages or when their teacher has died. It doesn't necessarily mean the teacher is any less capable; but in such cases you may want to take a measured approach.

    The gradual approach is recommended anyway, for students who have this concern. Let your commitment deepen along with your deepening experience, knowledge, and trust. The more you know, the more evidence you get that your teacher has integrity, and the more experience you have with them in actual teaching situations, the more you can let go of doubt and settle in.

    So at the beginning it's not necessary to dive in head-first. You can attend some talks or public services. You can take an introductory course (which is usually required anyway). You can later commit to weekly classes and do some retreats. Gradually, gradually you can align with the lineage; and then it will be like a river flowing through you — a river from the ancient Buddhas, through your master, and now through you.

    Although it is not necessary (and not recommended for most) to dive in without questioning, for some it turns out to be their path. Some people are very 'hot' types, who do everything all-out. When they succeed, it's big success; and when they fail, it's big failure; and both experiences unfold as their path. So some people may court disaster in order to completely surrender themselves.

    But for most of us it's a good idea to research and go gradually. You can do a Google search; you can check cult-watch websites to see if a teacher or school is listed; you can ask other students and generally note how the students of the school conduct themselves. Beginners can't necessarily be said to represent the school; but you might get some information on how the practice unfolds by interacting with advanced students and teachers. Again, are they clear; do they function well in life; are they compassionate; are they forthcoming...?
