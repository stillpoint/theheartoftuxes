    keep getting tired during/after meditation...

    The issue of back support stood out for me as well (as xtpattx pointed out). I have a bit more to say about it.

    The spine is not to be kept 'straight', as is often taught. That's just a way of saying your posture should be upright and self-supporting. When the weight of the body is aligned properly over the skeleton the muscles don't have to be tense to maintain the posture. That not only relieves you of pain, it facilitates the flow of breath and energy and disengages the mind from constant doing. A deep and openhanded state of contentedness is possible.

    The human spine is not meant to be straight. It has natural curves that form supportive arches, like the arches that support the mass of a cathedral or bridge. Find the posture that is most self-supporting, where the head rests upon the neck without falling in any direction; and where the upper body rests above the pelvis and sit-bones without slouching or being held rigidly. This is an ongoing process of becoming sensitive to the setup of the body. It's seldom possible to get it perfect; but just try your best each time and then let it be.

    Sinking into the mat or chair seat, into the earth, provides groundedness. The bodymind is willing to be as real as the earth. Relaxing tension provides lightness. The bodymind is free to take its noble, natural upright posture and rise up and out into the universe. Both movements are there together, down and up.

    There are many possible reasons for tiredness in meditation. You can search my posting profile for the term 'sleep' to find other discussions.

    Possible reasons for tiredness:

    • You could be exerting physical effort to keep yourself upright.

    • You could be forcing or blocking your breath. This can be done from within, in an attempt to control your state or grasp or avoid a certain experience; or it can be done from the outside, by being in a posture that gets in the way of free breath. Breathing should be free and natural; if it is out of balance or shut down it can obviously lead to tiredness. Breath is life, after all; so your relationship to breath and life should be open.

    • You could be struggling in your mind to maintain a particular state. This takes a lot of energy. Let go of all states. Engage your method with complete openhandedness, not a gaining idea.

    • You could be tired in your life, and meditation may just reveal your true condition. You need proper and regular sleep, proper rest besides sleep, and proper activity. There are required in good balance.

    • You could be eating too much, or eating food that contributes to dullness, or eating right before sitting. All of these can make your meditation tired and listless.

    • You could be avoiding something in meditation. Emotional issues or other personal patterns may be revealed in meditation, bubbling up from the subconscious. There are a lot of things that most of us avoid seeing or facing; but since meditation is completely openhanded, eventually we will experience every part of our being. Although therapy can help one through some material, the traditional cure is to keep sitting with a very openhanded intent. If this is the cause, eventually the tiredness will lift as your will to face your mind becomes strong and all-encompassing.
