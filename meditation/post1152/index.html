    I think you've posted before on this issue, yes?

    Mere belief will always have underlying doubt. The mind is aware that it is being forced into a certain posture or perspective without the basis of something that you actually see for yourself through direct experience. Philosophy can provide the beginning framework, but philosophy and thinking-understanding are not transformative.

    Correct practice is transformative.

    What does "practice according to canonical gospels" mean? You might have to get a bit non-canonical in your approach, depending on who is interpreting the canon, though you certainly can base practice on the gospels. There is one source and one love, regardless of what it's named. If you offer selfless kindness and open non-judging presence to the people and situations you encounter, that is both the teaching of the gospels and correct meditation.

    In order to apply any of the teachings, awareness is absolutely essential. If you are not present and clear in the moment -- if your mind is off somewhere else fantasizing or worrying or checking -- it doesn't matter what philosophy or practice you espouse; you won't be there in the moment to carry it out.

    So philosophy must be actualized; it must be made real through grounding in real life, which is the real moment as it is, without the overlay of preconception, opinion, expectation, preference, and habit. If you want to apply the teachings of Jesus you have to return to the real moment, without holding an idea. You put your trust in Jesus, not in your idea.

    Jesus said "I am the Way, the Truth, and the Life." This is a three part teaching. What is the correct Way of life? What is Truth? Where do you find true Life?

    Life is not an idea; it is the real moment unfolding despite our ideas. (But our ideas also shape that unfolding.)

    Truth is not an idea. Can you see that the sky is blue? Can you taste the tartness of a lemon? Do you attain the wetness of water? Truth is very simple.

    The Way is not an idea. When all ideas are let go of, in that moment the view of Truth appears: "Ah, water is wet!" When you realize this, your mind is not separate from Truth, not separate from Jesus, not separate from all beings. The continued unfolding of this is the Way. Clear mind and compassionate, uncontrived heart unfold moment to moment to moment.

    If you are practicing open, non-grasping, non-making awareness in the moment, that is correct meditation and correct Christian spirituality.

    Christ Consciousness is in no other place than here and now.

    Water is wet. This is very clear. Everyone can understand it, but not everyone mind is simple and clear enough to attain it. There usually some chatter or idea covering up the pain truth. If you attain the Truth, though, it is true regardless of religion or philosophy. Water is wet whether you are Christian or Buddhist or atheist.

    When your mind is simple and open, the wetness of water is Jesus; the tartness of lemon is Jesus.

    The question is whether you trust the immanence and omnipresence and guidance of Jesus, or whether you only want to hold ideas about it. If you trust, you can open your hands and return to reality without adding anything. This is faith, not belief. Faith means you can let go of believing any particular idea, because there is trust in the essential truth and goodness of reality. You let things speak for themselves, and you let Jesus function without the interference of your views, opinions, and distractions.

    In the moment, be simple and aware. Respond to the needs of the moment. Take care of those you're with.

    Is that enough?

    A good teacher will help you. I can't help but think that India, the spiritual mother of the world, is lacking in attained masters. You don't need to change religions; but meeting and spending time with an awakened being will, I'm sure, help you.
