    There's a lot of subjective story to that, which amounts to the same thing: vagueness and distraction.

    For example, you write that your practice is "being fully connected to life and reality"; but you still wonder about "depth". So actually, you don't know if you are fully connected, and you don't know if it's reality. You say that you let the separation between seer and seen disappear; but still you speculate about depth and frequency. So this matter of non-separation is not clear; it's still somewhat in the realm of ideas.

    Trying to find out what you're going to get in the future is checking. You're not just doing the practice, because you don't believe in it. And you don't believe in it because you aren't yet clear on what constitutes practice. So getting the basis in order is necessary; learning well is necessary, to remove this kind of doubt and unclarity.

    You certainly can try that style, but without correct form and good understanding you will waver between compromising the practice and checking it. Compromising means you follow your whim and abandon ship whenever you please; checking means that you label and judge what happened and try to figure out your path as you go.

    Many people, especially newcomers to meditation, think that freedom means shedding form and doing whatever they want. That's not freedom; it's ego. It's the wandering, attached mind seeking its opportunity to continue wandering. It seeks just a bit of laxity, a bit of vagueness â€” enough to fool you about what you're doing.

    Freedom means being able to show up continuously, for every situation, not just picking and choosing when to drop in. It means that your least favorite situation is equal to your most favorite, and that nice feeling doesn't determine whether you will be present.

    If you understand and believe in your non-separation practice, then only do it. No need to check levels or probabilities for the future. When you are not separate from the moment, you are complete. Nothing else is necessary, no extra experience, no filigree. But if you don't understand, then proper teaching is necessary; and commitment to a form is necessary so you can see when and how you wander.
