    This is a balanced view, with good questions.

    Yes, if it's not important to you, no need to learn well. (But human nature being what it is, that doesn't prevent you from spreading misinformation to others.)

    As to possible difficulties with teachers, that's why you would do proper research, clarify your aim and direction, and avoid attaching to empty promises (instead cleaving to positive and integral intentions).

    My question back to you is what scenario you imagine might move you to learn meditation without actually learning it? Like, in what situation might you want to sort of mess around with it non-productively; and do you think this subreddit should serve that kind of intent?

    >...it seems nonsensical to me that anyone starting out with meditation, or just meddling in it for 6 years, would be motivated enough to go and find a teacher.

    There seems to be quite a fear here, as if a teacher is some strange alien being and approaching one is some momentous event fraught with danger. Maybe this attitude arises when teachers are avoided. Not unlike imagining that the immigrant family living down the street is a looming threat.

    I'm not saying someone has to go study with a teacher. I'm not saying that someone will go or should feel bad if they don't go. I'm saying that a good teacher is the foremost cause for proper learning. When (if) you're gathering conditions that make your meditation fruitful, a teacher is a very good condition to gather.

    >But I am highly suspicious of your implied claim that you can only learn proper meditation from someone who has gotten the proper know-how passed down from his teacher.

    Yes, that suspicion. How does that suspicion get addressed? How does it get confirmed, or refuted and set aside?

    I'm not implying; so please don't make a straw man argument.

    Maybe someone can do well enough on their own; but someone who has dabbled for six years, and then some, and is still unclear, and is reaching out for clarity -- for that person, you might bring forth some awareness of cause and effect: "if you want to know more clearly and relieve doubt, gather the causes that lead to clarity". An online forum of anonymous amateurs is not a good cause for the sought-after result.

    On the other hand, someone who has trained extensively in a proven method (proven by millions of people over centuries, perhaps), who has gone through all the territory one is now going through or will soon face, who has attained the fruit of the practice -- the result one is seeking in the first place, who has been trained to convey the practice, whose attainment and readiness have been tested and confirmed, and who has experience leading others to that attachment... that person is a good ally to have of you want to actually get some significant result. It doesn't mean that no-one can get a result without a teacher; it just means that cause and effect are operating.
