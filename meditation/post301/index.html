    I feel like I'm loosing my identity.

    This is not necessarily bad. From time to time we become more intimate with the emptiness of everything. The question is whether you can be intimate with emptiness without being swallowed by it.

    These kinds of feelings are not uncommon. In my experience the vast majority of people who experience depersonalization in meditation are influenced by drugs in their lifestyle or some other addictive mind-altering influence (sex addiction, gaming, gambling, food addiction, etc.). I'm not saying that necessarily applies to you, but just bringing it to your attention. More often than not, this correlation exists.

    Meditation shouldn't be the thing that makes you disconnected. It may be that meditation is bringing certain things to awareness that have other causes. It may also be that in incorrect striving, grasping, manipulating attitude in meditation puts pressure on your mind, which, of course, will be resented and rejected.

    If you feel that meditation is making you less sane, then stop. Do what makes you feel more sane. Very simple.

    First things first: get your feet on the ground and achieve balance. Proper meditation should usually help to achieve balance, but if you are already out of balance because of other things happening in your life, you won't be able to force balance through meditation.

    • How is your sleep schedule and quality of sleep?

    • How is your eating: healthy, balanced, and regular? Drug-free?

    • Are you enjoying regular physical activity/exercise?

    • How do you use your mind through the day: what kinds of ideas, interactions, and images are you taking in? What comes in must come out.

    • Are you working or studying regularly?

    The balance of your life will show up in your mind; therefore it will show up in meditation.

    As to the meditation itself, you should be practicing with a proven method, you should understand the method well, and you should apply it meticulously. That is why people here are asking you what kind of meditation you're doing; they want to see the cause-and-effect of how you're using your mind.

    To tell the truth, a lot of what you say sounds like depression; so you may want to look into that. It seems like it's sort of permeating your life, so you may benefit tremendously by getting some guidance or some treatment from someone qualified to deal with depression.

    If it's only a matter of imbalance, the points I mentioned above usually have a big effect. When you sleep and eat well, and get moving regularly, your bodymind becomes happier and more ready to engage.

    Another thing that helps a lot, and in fact is specific medicine for depression, is expression. Singing, creating art, doing free writing, or writing in a journal are all great healing activities because they get you engaged in your heart and get your heart flowing outward. Expressing yourself in free dance is particularly beneficial, as it engages all levels of self (body, mind, heart, spirit).
