    I love meditating, but I've noticed the sits in which I'm truly present only come about in a certain state and it makes me worried.

    > â€¦my qualia while sitting was much more profound and seemed to be an experience in which I was very present when I was high.

    Take care not to make assumptions here. You don't know whether you were "more" or "very" present. High or sober, you don't know.

    What is "present"? What is "profound"? Is the perception of air currents on the skin more profound than thoughts moving here and there? These are both just objects. The presence is the witness of those objects.

    Some drugs, in some situations, can show us what it is like when the thinking mind calms or stops. But the witness has always been functioning. The drug does not make the witness function. Once we have a taste of clear mind or the benefit of stopping it is up to us to develop our own ability to return to clear mind, or to stop clinging to thinking.

    If you hold the idea that sitting is more profound when high, that is dependency. Your idea is dependent.

    But original mind is not dependent; and correct practice is not dependent.

    Actually, until we develop an unusually strong (one-in-a-million) practice we depend on a lot of supports. We depend on proper balance in our eating and sleeping; we depend on proper relationships and nurturing environment. For people who are very stuck in their thinking, certain drug experiences can be beneficial. But please consider whether you may be getting stuck because of ongoing drug experiences.

    It is a complete illusion to associate discomfort and unease with wrong practice. Indulgence runs rampant in our society's mindset. We tend very strongly to think that if we don't like it, it must be wrong. That's a big obstacle to a practice that seeks reality. Reality is sometimes nice, sometimes nasty, and sometimes neutral. Our task is to show up for all of it.

    So don't be thrown around by "I like" and "I don't like". Don't check and judge your practice: "it was better at that time, worse at this time". Checking and judging are also indulgence, the activity of the ego.
