    Crosspost from /r/survivalist - 11 in 1 Survival Tool

    That tool has almost no value in most survival situations. It's a credit card wrench with one sharp edge, and as such the only part of the item that will function as well an independent, dedicated version of the tool is the ruler. A separate, true wrench, saw, knife, or can opener will work better than the 11-in-1 for the same functions.

    The saw portion is not really a saw but a plain serrated edge. It has neither the tooth shape, sharpness, nor length to be useful for anything but cutting a small notch in a branch.

    The knife portion is not really a knife but one sharpened edge. It has neither the grip (for control), bevel angle, blade length and depth, point, or curve to be useful for much more than cutting a piece of cordage.

    "Sextant/direction wrench"? Does that refer to the hole with lines around it? Yeah, useful... &#3232;_&#3232;

    When will you need small wrenches or rulers in the wilderness, or during urban disasters? This tool will likely not help you at all to acquire shelter or drinking water, and will be extremely limited in its ability to support firemaking. For example, even if you could manage an extremely firm grip on the card, the bevel angle of the cutting edge is too wide for shaving curls on a feather stick for use as tinder.

    The card may find some use as a glove box tool if you have gear that uses nuts and bolts that may need tightening (and what gear would that be?) and if those nuts and bolts are accommodated by the the wrench holes. But then you have the problem of achieving torque on the wrench without slicing your hand open with the cutting edge, saw, and can opener. As to "survival", it's a total gimmick marketed to people who have no experience in the actual activities involved in survival scenarios.
