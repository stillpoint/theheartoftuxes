    Natural first aid questions.

    There are often a few broad-use materials available in whatever wilderness area you're traveling in. The following apply to northern US and Canada, but might provide you with some springboards for further research:

    • The balsam (sap) from Balsam Fir and Porsild's White Spruce.

    Found in blisters visible on the bark of these trees, the sappy balsam liquid is very effective as an antiseptic and treatment for cuts and burns.

    • The rhizome of Sweet Flag (Acorus Calamus — a plant found in many regions across the world) is thought of as a panacea for all kinds of internal problems like digestive upset, fever, and headaches. It is considered a vermifuge (anti-worm), antiseptic, and antiprotozoal, so is used to treat all kinds of problems caused by micro-organisms. It is also said to have strong antispasmodic properties.

    The rhizome has a very intense taste — you know it's medicinal when you chew it. The oil of the plant has been used as insect repellant and insecticide.

    • Sphagnum Moss can be used as a compress for wounds, or as a pad or diaper. It has antiseptic properties, unlike many other mosses.

    • True Tinder Fungus, or Chaga, is also considered a panacea and is used by some as medicine for diseases as wide-ranging as kidney disease, epilepsy, cardiovascular disease, and cancer. It is thought to be a substance full of vitality, and is used to boost immunity and general vitality of the body, to reduce inflammation, and as an anti-oxidant. The mushroom has long been used as a folk remedy as well as a medical treatment for cancer in Russia/Siberia. Some studies have found the fungus effective against HIV and Influenza.

    The True Tinder Fungus is a sacred herb or sacrament for many North American indigenous peoples. When burned it has a very pleasant aroma that is purifying to the atmosphere, drives off insects, is thought to cure headaches, and have effect on the mind anywhere from engendering calm focus to inducing visions, depending on the density and amount of smoke inhaled. It can be used as incense to promote feelings of congeniality and ease among companions. A burning piece of the fungus can also be placed on or near an acupressure point, similar to moxibustion.

    Finally, the surface of the fungus was sometimes placed over wounds, as a styptic and healing agent.

    True Tinder Fungus is also, of course, perhaps the best tinder for flint-and-steel firestarting, and can be used as a way to carry fire (a coal extender) or as a slow-burning fuel.
