    Enlightenment

    >My point was neither exist.

    Yes, that's right. I completely agree. But that doesn't mean there's no feeling. Far from losing that capacity, awakened beings perfect it, as well as other powers.

    Not existing doesn't mean non-existent.

    >The Bodhisattva has Dharma (Nature's Duty) in our realm, the non bodhisattva's do not have duties. The enlightened one, who is not a bodhisattva, has no duty.

    Now you're the one proposing something existing (a duty).

    Do you really mean to say that Bodhisattva behave according to a duty; and that any being who is not a Bodhisattva doesn't have a duty?

    >Only bodhisattvas maintain a presence outside of Nirvana because of their Duty.

    That's simply and evidently incorrect. The Buddha didn't disappear upon his complete enlightenment; he taught and tended to many others for decades.

    >All awakened beings eventually disregard the world and depart (Laozi, Bodhidharma, Huike, Milarepa).

    Really? These beings didn't offer many teachings and blessings, didn't transmit their Dharma to others?

    Milarepa was instructed to go into retreat and meditate; but this was in order to serve others. He emerged to eventually teach countless people. He trained and brought to awakening 25 disciples. He wrote five large volumes of teachings, including his famous 'songs'. He saw through an assassination plot but drank the poison for the sake of the assassins, and converted them through his actions. Even taking the rainbow body and dissolving into emptiness was out of his compassion for others.

    Even Bodhidharma and Laozi, as relative hermits, emerged to teach others, both leaving behind lineages and written teachings.

    Huike continued to teach for 40 years after transmission from Bodhisattva. He had a huge following, and worked devotedly despite government suppression and antagonism from other teachers. He brought Sengcan to awakening and gave him transmission. Far from "disregarding and departing", Huike continued to offer himself to others until his death, at 107 years of age.

    These beings are the farthest thing from "departing and disregarding the world"! They are in and for the world far more than most of us.

    >He who renouncing all activities, who is free of all the limitations of time, space and direction, worships his own Atman which is present everywhere...

    That's kind of an awkward way of putting it, making a duality where none exists. The statement requires a very liberal sense of 'worships'.
