    How to wake up with depression and transcend it?

    Make it not about "I, my, me".

    All the problems — the self-torment, the reaction to it, the judgment of that, the suppression of this, the despondency about the whole thing — they all come from your thinking; and this thinking is all based on "I". More "I": more suffering. More thinking about how "I" is suffering: more suffering yet.

    Less "I" thinking: less suffering. No "I" thinking: no suffering.

    So one good way, for you in particular but for all of us in general, is to cultivate compassion and generosity. Grow your care for others, and express it in your interactions and in your daily activity and speech and thought. Let your first thoughts in the morning be about how you will benefit others today rather than what's wrong with your (extremely, outrageously fortunate) life.

    This is called generating and actualizing Bodhicitta, the mind of enlightenment. Bodhicitta is the mind that is for others, not for me. That's because it is your original complete mind, the mind that already has enough, already has everything. But you don't know that yet, so you have to (A) generate it and (B) put it into action.

    You may find may good ways if you try, but the classic way to generate Bodhicitta is to consider that all beings have at some point in your countless rebirths been very dear and loving to you, and in fact have been your mother, offering you the profoundly sincere love of a mother... and to consider further that all of these beings who at some point have been so dear and so caring to you are all in the same suffering situation, rising and falling in endless cycles of loss and struggle, subject to all the physical, mental, and emotional pain that we know (and more).

    When you take to heart that we all love life, all crave happiness, and are all so stuck in cycles of suffering, a natural and deep desire to help all others arises. This is what we are to generate first thing in the morning, and before and after every formal practice, at every significant turn of life, and throughout our mundane activity. This warm, sincere, heartfelt desire for the well-being of others.

    Sometimes you can trigger it by something close to your heart. Remembering a kind teacher can bring you to that warm place, or thinking of a beloved pet or even of a suffering animal you've encountered. Realize that we are all in the same boat. We all desire freedom from pain and we all desire love and contentment.

    The second part of the process is actually doing something with the compassion that arises. In the morning you can make a vow to give your effort, your life, to benefit all beings. You can make a wish to uplift yourself and not to taint your effort, your life, with selfish thoughts and deeds. And you can actually start carrying out your good vows and wishes immediately with a morning practice, whether it is devotional or meditative (or both).

    Throughout the day, let your decisions and actions be guided by genuine compassion. It's one thing to talk about 'transcending', but it takes your effort in getting over selfishness to actually let others go ahead of you. Don't worry about winning. Be content. Don't worry about others having more than you. Be happy for them, share their joy. This purifies your petty mind.

    You, like all of us, have led a life of self-centeredness and self-aggrandizement. Many years, if not lifetimes, of selfish habit have permeated your being. Therefore you have to make the effort, situation by situation, to extricate yourself. It doesn't happen like popping a pill and then shazam! all problems disappear. You have to do it; you have to save yourself moment by moment, situation by situation. Let it not be about "I, my, me" but about the person or scene that you are facing.

    >If you seek to be acknowledged by the ten thousand things, the ten thousand things can't help you. If you acknowledge the ten thousand things, the ten thousand things themselves acknowledge and uplift you. [Paraphrase from a famous saying.]
