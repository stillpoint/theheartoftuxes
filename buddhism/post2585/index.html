    I feel like I'm doing this all for selfish intentions

    Whether you're bad, or trying to be good, or trying to get the benefit of being good, or not wanting to be trying to be good, there's one thing common to all these situations: self-centeredness. All of these have too much "I".

    Correct practice is just momentary. Correct practice is also radiant: it moves from your center, outward. So in correct practice you don't step outside of the situation and look back at yourself to see how you're doing, judge it, and label it worthy or not worthy. That is called 'checking'. Don't check your practice.

    One aspect of checking is that it deals with the unreal. It holds an illusion of self; it holds an illusion that the self and the situation are separate; and it holds the illusion of a story-line moving from past to future, that has to be manipulated. Don't check your practice; drop all of these illusions. Your real life is right here and now.

    So don't check good or bad, gain or loss, spiritual or mundane. Don't check your situation or condition. Don't check results. Only return to the plain, simple moment.

    When you return to the plain moment you can be sincere. If you know, you know; if you don't know, you don't know. That is sincerity. Sometimes you gain, sometimes you lose â€” either way no problem; just stay with the moment.

    So only take care of your momentary sincerity, and your practice will progress much better and faster than if you try to arrange everything yourself. In fact, if you are sincere in the moment, even though you don't realize it that is already enlightened activity. Then you have only to keep returning, or just don't give it up in exchange for illusion.
