    Electronics are our talismans that ward off the spiritual vacuum of modernity

    Have you ever had the experience of sitting around a fire with close friends, your faces all illuminated by it, the food you share cooked by it...? The sincerity you share is like the fire's light, and the communal feeling you share is like the fire's warmth. And on the tails of that experience have you then had the chance to see the cold, empty glow of a television?

    For millions of years we gathered around a warm (but sharp, dangerous) light to establish our survival together. Now we gather, or probably at least as often, sit alone by a cold, manipulated light in attempts to disperse the anxiety we feel in our separation.

    The things we yearn for all represent a return to our original self, and it is difficult to separate that return to original self from our sense of original place or home -- from actual, physical contexts that are simple and alive and direct: earth, wind, fire, stars, ocean... plain talk, direct gestures, silence....

    The proliferation of electronics is, if we admit it, a proliferation of our busy mind. We are making more and more thinking and talking machines, and they take us farther away from place. Or they tend to. In essence, they are not good or bad; but the effect that electronics are having on us is that we have many more thoughts and conversations, but no longer know what our feet our touching and where we are standing.

    In the past, we would regularly be informed by the movements of place, such as a mild breeze at night; informed that our inner and outer worlds are in intimate movement together. Often we might experience no separation between inner and outer -- the wind would move through us and our bodymind would be the expanse of the world. We would, many times a day, be brought back to our big body -- our sky-wide mind, our ocean heart, our earthy stability.

    These days, we are disembodied voices and images, merely sharing concepts of mind and heart, and transmitting pictures of earth and sky far removed from what earth and sky actually are. We, as a society, may actually believe more and more that Nature is something 'out there'.

    But the interesting thing about electronics is their speed and connectivity. As we are losing our connection to place, we are gaining a social mind -- a rapidly self-communicating entity that is largely mental. An idea flashes out over social media and very quickly it becomes part of the communal consciousness.

    In the old days, people might travel months to meet a teacher, and then receive one snippet of knowledge. Nowadays everyone knows everything; we have access to countless spiritual technologies, information about political movements around the world, recipes for any kind of food, permutations of all kinds of music, and so on and so on. It sometimes seems like we're a vast flock of starlings, all fluttering and flashing and shimmering in waves, expanding, contracting, but all somehow interconnected. Where is the flock headed? Ungrounded, the flock just follows the flock; and something unfolds through it.
