    The 'spirit', in the Traditional Chinese Medicine meaning of the word, as life force and presence, shows through the eyes. Some masters can perceive if a person is close to death, whether they've had an awakening experience, and whether they are truthful, just from the gleam that comes through the eyes.

    But for all the overwhelming (for some) onrush of energy and information coming through the eyes, they are also the well of vital contact that we seek. One may be moved to avoid connecting, based on patterns that arise from emotional wounds, but more deeply one is moved to connect. At birth, we seek out the eyes and face of our mother; and in life we seek out the eyes and faces of other humans. Our frequent reconnection is an important part of our well-being. Re-affirming our place in the tribe is deeply consoling to us. Getting the human energy of eye contact seems to be as vital as awareness of lineage, connection to place, and the exchange of work for food.

    It's not the eye contact itself but the thinking that causes you distress. You make stories and project your inner material onto the other person.

    You are very right that your own confidence is a central issue. Along with that, settling the mind and learning to be free of the thoughts that come through will also contribute to ease.

    And -- very important -- moving from a self-centered approach to a compassionate one will not only make a huge difference in this situation, but will be a sea-change in your life.

    Lack of confidence has many possible causes and mechanisms. As you have discovered, the eyes have a great deal of power to them, both psychologically and perhaps also energetically. They are like an instant expression of being, like an instant computer graphic display showing mood and thought, level of presence, level of sincerity, reactivity, compassion or aggression, and so on.

    We also check the eyes as an instinctual survival mechanism, to see what's up, whether the situation is safe or dangerous. These things are written deeply into our genetic makeup and affect us on a gut level. It's why law enforcement personal and interrogators are trained in the dynamics of eye contact.

    But although there is a power there, the reason the power disturbs you lies in your own thinking. There may be a fear behind avoiding eye contact, but there's also a pride behind it. It's an unwillingness to be seen; and that part of the motivation has to do with always demanding that engagement be on your terms.

    Compassion uproots that because the focus is no longer centered on you and your agenda. There's no fear of losing control of the message you're sending, because you're just sincere in your wish for the well-being of others.

    When you are fully wrapped up in being the host for others, there's little room for self-centered worries. It's empowering.
