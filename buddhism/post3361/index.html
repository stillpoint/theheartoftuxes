    TheHeartofTuxes

    There are many, many options.

    First of all, you can enjoy your walk. That is wonderful, and healthy; and it vitalizes you and instills you with joy that you can share with others. When you are enjoying you are present.

    If you want more of a structure, there are many possible practices. Mantra (in various forms of method), mindfulness of breath, mindfulness of posture, mindfulness of sensation, mindfulness of enlightening factors, the Four Immeasurables, and other practices are possible.

    I recommend against very focused concentration-style practices while you're out for a walk, since it can be unsafe. If your attention withdraws you might walk into danger without knowing it.

    Mindfulness of feet on the ground is a very good practice for many reasons, and it is very adaptable. Practicing while walking is a natural match. You can also practice it as you go about your day, as an anchor for constant mindfulness. And if you want, you can practice it in a more focused way as a samatha/concentration-style method.

    But in its basic form, you feel your feet on the ground. Gravity helps you in this practice, emphasizing your relationship with the Earth, your embodied presence, your place in the here and now. Wherever you step, wherever you stand, you have your place in the midst of things.

    Step by step you can refresh your presence. Feel each footfall as new. The aim is to actually be present with the sensation, not just thinking of walking in general. When you are present with your steps, each step is new and unique, each step has a life of its own.

    When you are in a sheltered place you can bring more attention to the feet if you want to practice concentration. But the more common function of this practice is to provide a background anchor for your regular activity. Enjoy your walk, enjoy your day. Having some background awareness of feet on the ground will keep you present and aware.

    Another very beneficial aspect of this practice is that it tends to bring you into your body, where you can have a more real and settled engagement with life. Our society, our world, is living up in the dreamworld of the head and flying off into virtual 'realities'. We are losing contact with our ground. Awareness of the feet has a strong grounding influence that pulls you down into your body and into relationship with place. You find your context, no longer a small, solitary being, no longer a disembodied train of thought floating aimlessly.

    If you like this practice you can continue it in all postures. Sitting, be aware of your butt and legs on the seat and your feet on the floor. Sitting cross-legged on the floor, feel your areas of contact. Lying down, feel your body's footprint on its support. In any posture, feel your foundation, the place where you interface with the (physical) ground.

    This practice can also be performed when the mind is busy or scattered. Some meditations become quite difficult when you are agitated, but this method is easy enough, and helps settle the mind without wrestling. One only returns gently to the feeling of feet on the ground.

    Since this practice is very easy to maintain and very adaptable, and because it brings one out of the head and into the body, and into relationship with place, it is a very good method for many practitioners.
