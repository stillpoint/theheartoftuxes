    Our karma does not always permit us the ideal situation for study and practice. It is because of our past negative relationship with Dharma. That means we need to maintain our devotion and keep doing positive Dharmic activity, even if we don't have our best situation. This creates positive seeds that in the future will unfold into better conditions.

    If, on the other hand, we allow our less-than-favorite situation to bring forth defeatism and bitterness, we only create negative seeds and don't necessarily get any closer to the Dharma.

    So patience is necessary. And in the time between now and when you first meet your teacher, you can prepare the conditions for the teaching you will receive. You can 'till the field', so to speak, to make it ready to be planted. This includes promoting harmony in your family, being generous, and maintaining strong devotion to the practice of Dharma.

    Even before you meet your teacher(s), you can call out to them. The true teacher is not a body, not a mere person, so you can always call out with your heart, with your prayerful intent, beseeching them to remember you and help you and guide you. All true teachers are happy to respond to sincere need and sincere desire to awaken, so even before you meet the person you can establish connection and welcome guidance.

    You can also establish a strong practical base in your life, maintaining physical health, developing strong and caring relationships, maturing in responsibilities, taking on work and making money, and generally becoming capable in many things. This will provide the foundation for all you wish to try in the future, and will also show your family that you are level-headed and able to take care of yourself. Once you are ready, you will be able to quickly take advantage of opportunities that come your way.

    All students of Dharma should be very capable in life. The Buddha required this of his monk disciples. From being able to mend clothing, to being able to cook, to being able to tend to your health, capability brings a great measure of freedom and independence with it.
