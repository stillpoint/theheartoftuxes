    How to cope with death of a very close person?..

    While it is natural to experience great sorrow, you should let it arrive and flow through without holding on to the feeling. This is for the sake of the departed, your grandfather: departed beings are much more sensitive than those with bodies, so extremes of emotion can be disturbing and especially can cause the being to linger in attachment (e.g., to family members) rather than moving on clearly toward good rebirth or liberation. Everything you can do to be clear — not suppress, but be clear — will be helpful to your grandfather and to your grieving relatives.

    There are many practices that support the consciousness of the departed in their journey through intermediate states. After one loses one's body there may be drifting and confusion, just as you have vague and confused consciousness when you are very sick or when you lose some connection with the body in sleep or very sleepy waking states. So offering clear presence to your grandfather's consciousness as it passes on can be very helpful; and at the same time it can be very healing to you, as, in a sense, you also go through a passage.

    Chanting in particular is healing for the heart. You can pour the emotion of the moment into the chanting, and you can eventually come to very clear, awake equanimity through it.

    I see that you favor Tibetan style. In that tradition, the most famous mantra chanted at death is

    >Om Mani Padme Hum

    This is the short mantra of Chenrezig / Avalokitesvara. If you wish to perform the long mantra, you can recite as follows, but the most important thing is your sincerity and clarity.

    Long version:

    >Namo Ratna Trayaya

    >Nama Arya Jnyana Sagara /

    >Vairochana Byu Rajaya Tathagata

    >Namasarva Tathagate Bhya

    >Arhate Bhya

    >Samyak Sambuddhe Bhya

    >Nama Arya Avalokiteshvaraya Bodhisattvaya Mahasattvaya

    >Maha Karunikaya Tadyatha Om

    >Dhara Dhara

    >Dhiri Dhiri

    >Dhuru Dhuru

    >Itte Vatte

    >Chale Chale

    >Prachale Prachale

    >Kusume Kusume

    >Vare Ili Mili

    >Chiti Jala Apanaye Svaha

    Medicine Buddha practice is also very helpful for benefitting the deceased and for healing one's own grief or anxiety. Many temples offer regular Medicine Buddha services, so you can look in your area; or you can do the practice from one of the resources linked on this page under Medicine Buddha Sadhana.

    It is said to be especially helpful to practice for the departed during the 7th-day, windows at which time their consciousness has a greater opportunity to turn from confusion to clarity. So you might do special practice alone or with others on Wednesdays until 49 days have passed.

    Next, for your own healing expression is very, very helpful. You can transform your suffering into very meaningful art. Write poetry, do some visual artwork (whether literal or abstract), dance your inner experience, create altars or art installations, or do free writing or journalling. All of these are very healing activities.

    Finally, if you want something very simple, you can just play a rhythmic instrument. In native North American spirituality and in many other tribal cultures a rattle or shaker is used, just shaken rhythmically over and over. Of course, a constant drum beat is also possible. If you have Tibetan instruments, using the Damaru is also an option. Just some constant pulse of sound.
