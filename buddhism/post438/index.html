    Only do it.

    (No sarcasm.)

    It's either that simple, or the theme of practice far too involved to be handled in an online post. Duality appears where? It is not in the nature of things, but in the nature of the thinking mind that creates 'things'. So your practice is to participate completely, and not to hold yourself separate.

    There are many, many different approaches to sex in spiritual practice. Some are paths of renunciation; some are paths of integration; some are paths of transcendence; some are paths or primordial perfection. A flavor for everyone, it seems.

    You may be interested in Mark Epstein's writings, which bridge Buddhism and Western Psychology; especially Open To Desire: Embracing A Lust For Life (also published as ...The Truth About What The Buddha Taught).

    Here is an interview with Epstein in TriCycle Magazine on the topic of relationships and spirituality (available with subscription).

    Excerpt:

    >It’s very important to me that these images [of sexual union] exist on the walls of the temples because it shows that profound realization can be attained right in the midst of sex and that it’s not possible only in some other realm—that this deepest of emotional states has within it the most profound level of consciousness. That’s just wonderful.

    Also of interest: Epstein's book Going To Pieces Without Falling Apart is organized on the traditional Tibetan Buddhist stages of realization, Looking, Smiling, Embracing, and Orgasm, which describe the stages using the metaphor of human sexual relationship and the movement from duality to unity.

    Any of the vehicles of Buddhism will train you in resolving the duality of sex (and other duality). Some are more overt and pointed than others, but my experience by and large is that traditional schools and teachers are very open and grounded when talking about sex. The Dharma is basically practical, not moralistic.

    Perhaps Tantric Tibetan Buddhism tends to deal with sex most directly, in case that is of interest to you.

    See, for instance Jeffrey Hopkins' reworking of a source text in Sex, Orgasm, And The Mind Of Clear Light into a commentary on gay spiritual sex.

    Here's an excerpt of Hopkins' commentary in Death, Sleep, and Orgasm: Gateways To The Mind Of Clear Light, offering different angles on practices that deal directly with sex and orgasm:

    >Because the more subtle levels of consciousness are considered to be more powerful and thus more effective in realizing the truth, the systems of Highest Yoga Tantra seek to manifest the mind of clear light by way of various techniques.

    >One of these techniques is blissful orgasm because, according to the psychology of Highest Yoga Tantra, orgasm involves the ceasing of the grosser levels of consciousness and manifestation of the more subtle levels, as do going to sleep, ending a dream, sneezing, fainting, and dying. In intense orgasm, the mind withdraws from the diverse objects of the other senses and is focused, eventually exclusively, on sexual bliss. Ordinary, distracted mind paying attention to a multitude of objects vanishes. The intention in using a blissful, orgasmic mind in the spiritual path is to manifest the most subtle level of consciousness, the mind of clear light, and use its greater power and hence effectiveness to realize the true nature of mind, stripped of its distractions and peripheral manifestations.

    >The pleasure of orgasm is so intense that the mind becomes totally fascinated and entranced with pleasure such that both the usual conceptual mind and the appearances that accompany it melt away, leaving the innermost mind in its pristine state. In orgasm, the phenomena of ordinary life that are so concrete and solid that they seem to have their own independent existence melt into the expanse of the reality behind appearances.

    >Through consciously experiencing this process, one can realize that ordinary appearances and afflictive emotions are over-concretized. In this way sex can become a spiritual practice through which the exaggerated status of afflictive emotions is subsumed in the source state. Nevertheless, due to the fact that all of the usual conceptual minds cease temporarily during intense sex, many experience these states as a dimming of the mind into an emotional state that is withdrawn to the point of uselessness. Some even manifest a dread of orgasm and, if they still seek sexuality, advocate sexual pleasure devoid of orgasm. The state of orgasm is viewed by such persons as hopelessly stupid...

    ~

    Only doing it means dropping all concepts, dropping the mind that makes duality. Participating completely in the movement of the moment allows the original clear mind to function. As described in the 'view' of Dzogchen (here by Tsoknyi Rinpoche):

    > The secret mind is right there, the view of no concept, you might try to understand it with concepts. The real difficulty lies here, to understand that this is the wisdom of our awareness. It is said that the wisdom of the view cannot be conceived or understood, because it is beyond concept. But if you have great merit, uncompelled devotion, compassion or intelligence it is possible to recognize, or if the master gives you a shock of the five sufferings. Also at the very end of an orgasm, you completely let go.

    >So, how to be able to let go completely of how we see things?

    The crux is to let go of seeing things in any particular way, and relax as our original mind (our 'secret' mind) which by its very nature is nondual. (Actually, beyond dual and non-dual.)
