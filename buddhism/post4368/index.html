    Yes! There is death meditation practice!

    You can visualize death with a calm mind; it doesn't have to be violent.

    Contemplations like the Nine-Point contemplation and the Four Thoughts That Turn The Mind are very effective. And exceptionally perceptive masters (especially from the Indo-Tibetan traditions) have outlined the process of dying in great detail. We can learn the stages of this process and rehearse them, training both our stability of awareness and our equanimity for the time when we really approach our death.

    u/druid3ka has posted a video I would recommend. The meditation on dissolution of the elements at death is available from many sources online. It can also be found in concise form in the Tibetan Book Of Living And Dying, nominally by Sogyal Rinpoche, but likely actually written by Andrew Harvey.

    There is also the Theravadin contemplation of the compound nature of the body, in which you meticulously note all the tissues, fluids, and other substances that make up the body. Traditionally, a monk might perform this practice in a charnel ground, while viewing corpses in various states of decay or being cremated.

    Various traditions have their own way of 'leaning into' awareness of impermanence rather than avoiding it. Ask your teachers and community members.

    And spending time with the aging and dying, while practicing and offering compassion and equanimity, is also very good for bringing the issue home to your heart.
