    Feeling like I can't move. How do I get out of this phase?

    Not necessarily a Buddhist teaching, but just some advice from experience:

    First, ease off 'getting away' from things that arise in practice and in life. We have very strong tendency to think certain parts of the path are worthy and certain parts are not. This is our judgmental thinking; it doesn't reflect the path. But we pick out things we'd rather not go through, things that we think we should be beyond, or better than. We pick out negative states and try to eradicate them. It seems reasonable, but it is not good practice, for two reasons:

    First, it doesn't reflect reality. Negative states will continue to arise throughout your life; you'd best realize that you are human.

    Second, in trying to get rid of them quickly you fail to own them and digest them. Then the negative states don't disappear, because you haven't actually worked through them. They just slip into the shadows where you don't notice them any more; and they control you from the unconscious places in your mind.

    So the first thing is to rearrange your attitude toward the states you go through. Take an example from the Taoists, and how easy-going is their attitude to up and down, gain and loss. For the practice mind, everything is 'no problem'.

    Next, you've identified the issue very expertly: lack of movement. You can help yourself by moving. What part of you is the key? What needs to move?

    Often just moving physically can produce profound changes. You can start by flopping around and groaning, like a tired cat on the floor or on your bed. You become one with your situation, in movement. Tired and grumpy? Do tired and grumpy movement for a minute. Dull and inert? Take five different dull and inert postures. The point is that you can be completely in harmony with your situation and still move. You don't have to push to be something different, and you don't have to cave in to any particular state, giving up your living flow.

    If even that is too much, you can wiggle fingers and toes... take a deeper breath... flop over to the other side.... Just move one thing in one way. That often opens the possibility for you.

    But maybe there is another level of self that is calling for movement. Do you need to express something emotionally? Do you need to express something to someone else, to be clear together? Do you need to pour yourself out without knowing what will appear... into a journal, or in song, or on the dancefloor?

    Or does a life decision, big or small, have to be made... for better or worse, just to get back your, flow, your living connection with yourself?

    As I say, this isn't necessarily Buddhist; but it's not necessarily not Buddhist. But you yourself already know that some kind of movement is required for your return to integrity.
