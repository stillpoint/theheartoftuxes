    How do you deal with all of the terrible things happening in the world?

    Where do all these terrible things come from? This is the central point; and this is what you — we — have to awaken to. We have to understand what is behind the illusion of self, that drives suffering.

    Know how to protect your mind. If awareness of terrible things motivates you to clarify your mind, then allow it to do that, and simply return to that true work. If awareness of terrible things overwhelms you or causes you to get stuck, then you have to retreat. You don't retreat from the world or from reality; but you withdraw your attention from distraction and worry and rumination about things you are as yet powerless to change. This self-protection can take the form of arranging your surroundings and relationships (knowing when to avoid TV, newspapers, Facebook, gossiping friends, depressing pursuits), or it can take the form of mantra — mind-protection. With mantra, the mind is protected because it has something to return to, moment to moment to moment throughout the day; so it doesn't wander down useless and agitating trails of thought and emotion.

    Your faith is not wearing thin. The evidence for this is that you come to this forum and connect with others, seeking the way. If your faith was wearing thin you wouldn't be here.

    It only seems to you like faith is wearing thin because your thoughts are going around and around. But your thoughts are not where your faith resides. Look at an image of Buddha with His hands open: open hands is where faith resides. Look at a natural landscape, or an ocean, or the night sky. This is also open-handedness. Relax, and 'open the hands' of your heart and mind, letting it all go. Then you will return to the central point and faith will unfold.

    Next, you say that you feel helpless unless there's someone to help. This isn't bad; helping can be very healing for both parties, and for others around them. But is this becoming an ego character as well? Who are you if you are not helping? If you are not in relation to something or someone? There is still something precious there, something real and powerful and valid, even if you don't lift a finger.

    I'm not saying stop helping; I'm saying that even without helping, you are okay, and everything is okay. But this is not something you can experience unless you put it all down, open your hands, and embrace the whole miraculous disaster that's right in front of your nose.

    Finally, 'terrible' is an idea, a label applied to thoughts and memories. If you don't make the ideas 'wonderful' or 'terrible', 'good' or 'bad', then what is facing you this moment? What is actually in front of your nose, actually at your feet? This is where you connect to reality, not through speculating, labelling, or figuring out. When your relationship to life becomes more simple (coming down out of the thinking realm), the terrible will find its correct place.

    Our agitation with the suffering in the world is connected with our agitation about ourself — the two are linked both within and below our usual consciousness. We have a foundational agitation about who we are if we are not related to something — if we are not doing or making or pursuing or helping someone or something. Thinking ourselves to be small beings within something vast and boundless, we fear the vastness and boundlessness. We don't realize that we are the vastness, the boundlessness, and all the wonderful and terrible things that unfold there. Clarify your mind, and the whole world will be clarified. If you know who you are and where the world comes from, you will know this to be true.
