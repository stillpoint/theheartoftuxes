    TheHeartofTuxes

    There are two sides of one's personal story — useful and useless. It is helpful to be aware of how you are engaging with 'story'.

    Much of what you write is story. It's not what is actually happening, but your interpretation of it, couching it in a narrative, with symbols and similes and plot lines.

    A great number of our problems appear because we make stories in our own minds rather than dealing directly with things-as-they-are in the moment. We can't do anything about the past; it has already happened. Our only point of effective activity, our only effective influence, is in the present moment. We can only deal with things now.

    But what can happen in the present moment is that our story gets so built up to outrageous proportions that it overwhelms us. We create this idea that some issue has been building up over time, and has overtaken us. In the face of that story, what chance do we have to change it? What chance does our small, tired effort have over this epic drama?

    So we need to be aware of how we make stories, and infuse them with emotion, and give them power over us. We need to be aware of when we go off into the unreal expanse of time rather than being real and present, where we can actually make a difference. That is, we need to limit our attention to just here and just now. Where are your feet standing? What is in front of your face? Start there. That is called letting go of 'story'; that is called 'practice'.

    The other side — the useful side of 'story' — is the way it can teach us about who we are and what has been steering our behavior. So we may take a realistic look at the causes and effects of our life. What happened and why did it happen — what were the causes and conditions that brought it about?

    Going back into causes and conditions can provide a context in which self-kindness arises. Rather than just seeing the mistake, you also see the reason for the mistake. You can offer yourself compassion and encouragement. We all have psychic wounds from imperfect parenting, the pains of childhood, the traumas of maturing, the disconnections from family, ancestry, land, nature, and so on. We all have these wounds. Recognizing them can help relieve you of hopelessness, because then you can set about to healing the wounds, changing the causes and conditions, and deriving different results.

    So this is the difference between being swamped by your story, when it appears in a sort of unconscious brooding, and using your story to develop awareness of your karma so that you can change it.

    Ways of practicing differ widely. Some ways are 'put it all down', let go of all story, and everything will begin to arrange itself according to its own innate intelligence, the intelligence behind everything. Other ways are more specific: they may use creative visualization, intentional engagement with negative qualities to transform them into positive ones (which is Tantra), or other skillful means such as art or dialog to bring awareness to the story's chain of cause-and-effect and express and purge stuck emotions.

    Movement practice, playback theater, art therapy or art practice, journaling, vision quest, and other modalities are available (some within Buddhist context, some outside of it) to help resolve and harness the life story toward a liberative purpose.
