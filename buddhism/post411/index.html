    What is the purpose of prayer beads in the context of Buddhism?

    108 is widely considered a sacred or auspicious number.

    In part this is because it represents the interpenetration of masculine and feminine energies. Arising from ancient Hindu and pre-Hindu religions where the godhead is understood as a union of masculine and feminine, the numeric representation is understandably considered auspicious.

    The even prime number 2 is balanced, symmetrical and therefore unmoving, receptive, and expressive of the feminine. The odd prime number 3 is unbalanced, asymmetrical, and therefore in movement, active, and expressive of the masculine. (Sometimes the origin or source number 1 is included in the calculation.)

    Extending the dimensionality, 2^2 = 4, and 3^3 = 27. When 'married' (multiplied) they yield 108, the number of feminine and masculine unity in its full dimensionality. (The source number 1^1 could also be included in the calculation.)

    Whether in deference to the sacred number or out of some other causality or coincidence, the Buddha is said to have identified 108 kleshas or defilements of mind. So doing one round of a mala with 108 mantras, dharanis, prostrations, or other practice may be seen as 'breaking even' for that session in terms of purifying the defilements. The practice of doing 108 prostrations upon waking is said in some traditions to purify the defilements that you would otherwise generate simply by starting another day in a state of non-enlightenment. (This doesn't account for the vast mountains of defiled karma incurred from previous days and lifetimes.)

    108 is also yielded by multiplying the six senses or sense consciousnesses through the Three Times by the Three Poisons and their inner or outer origins: 6 x 3 x 3 x 2= 108. (I'm not sure if this is how the number of kleshas is derived, or if that's something completely different.)

    In Tibetan traditions the 108 is sometimes spoken of as an even 100 plus a safety margin for the inevitable distractions or other glitches that arise in practice; so finishing the entire string is counted as only 100 toward whatever accumulations are being performed.

    In older yogic traditions 108 is said to reflect certain energetic patterns as well as astronomic relationships. For example, the number of meridians connecting to the heart chakra is said to be 108, as is the number of 'sacred locations' (marma, where the subtle body and the physical body intersect) on the body. The distance between moon and earth is said to be roughly 108 times the moon's diameter (and likewise for the sun). It is not clear whether these are regarded as independent manifestations of a universal synchronistic pattern, or as an attempt to shoehorn teachings into a seeming organized structure.

    In the popular Sri Yantra meditative symbol of Hindu yoga, there are 54 triple-intersections (also called marma, like the intersections of the body), and with masculine and feminine aspects they yield 108. Sanskrit is considered both phonically and numerologically perfect, with 54 letters in their masculine and feminine aspects.

    Traditional Chinese energy arts also recognize the significance of 108. Again, it is not clear whether this has been adopted from Indian sources or revealed independently.
