    title: [X-Post from /r/Meditation] Metta meditation as a standalone practice summary: 3.0 authors: TheHeartofTuxes date: 2015-03-22 08:55:08 some_url:

    Metta is a very wonderful practice, and it would be very beneficial to continue it. It develops the concentration, heals emotional wounds, clarifies your direction in life, and benefits all other beings. Yes, please do the practice!

    You can indeed linger at the level where you offer compassion to yourself -- it's most important to heal this relationship, so that you can become a vessel of compassion for others. Just please don't get stuck there, only thinking of yourself. Linger until you're ready; but there should be the intent to move on and share your compassionate heart with others.

    To begin offering to others, you can consider someone who is very easy to feel compassion or gratitude for. (Romantic partners are not ideal objects at first because of the attachments that easily get increased.) The classic suggestion is to bring to mind a teacher or other person who at any time offered you generous support without asking anything in return. But many meditators will start with, for example, a family pet as the object of their meditation. The dynamic is the same: "This being has offered me love and solace without asking anything in return." It is instantly heartwarming. It is usually not difficult to sincerely extend your best wishes once you think of another being for whom you are thankful.

    As I said, you can certainly linger in the stage of offering yourself compassion for the sake of healing, but the intent to move outward should be there nonetheless. Extending your compassion is important, and for reasons you may not yet imagine. It is important not only for the sake of the others, but to facilitate your own deep healing.

    You already have a boundless, pure heart of compassion that is never exhausted and free of harm. This is the nature of your true mind. You already have (you already are) a bottomless and perfect source of wisdom and compassion. The issue is how to realize and actualize that. When you offer compassion to others fearlessly, generously, you have a chance to start realizing the boundless and unconditional nature of your own mind. It is not just something that other great beings have. It is not something that you get by accident or through special processes or possessions -- the mind of enlightenment is already who you are, waiting to be revealed.

    So while my encouragement shouldn't be taken as a moralistic â€œyou should do such-and-such", if you begin to extend your compassion to others you can more quickly realize your own deep resources and your own true nature... and that is true healing.

    Moving outward with your compassion calls on your resources, so it creates a flow. If you generate this flow regularly it begins to flow by itself, and your offering becomes easier and more natural.

    At some point it may also be useful to you to realize that thinking only of yourself promotes suffering. This doesn't mean you shouldn't take long, generous periods of self-care! But if you happen to notice that you're only thinking of yourself and your own situation and condition, then guaranteed: that will be a suffering situation. You can move out of that suffering by reducing the amount of I, my, me thinking and offering yourself to others.

    Thank you for your wonderful Metta practice!
