    What is a concrete, brief definition of "compassion?"

    In Buddhism, compassion is different from "no ill-will". It is not merely refraining from a negative state of mind or action, but a movement toward a positive, unifying, completing state of mind or action.

    Next, I think we can't say that compassion is merely a feeling or wish (to help others). That is part of it, but since compassion seeks to complete or to heal the suffering within its circle of awareness it can't stop at just feeling without acting (or else there would be no actual healing). Feeling without acting would maybe be called "pity".

    So compassion is a response, an immediate, unreserved, reflective response to suffering. Love radiates; compassion responds. Thus in the Brahmaviharas (the Divine Abodes, or Four Immeasurables) love is expressed in wishing happiness and the causes of happiness for all, and compassion is expressed in wishing freedom from suffering and its causes, for all. Wishing, but also intending and in fact vowing and undertaking to accomplish. (This is why many images of Avalokitesvara, the Bodhisattva of Compassion, show him/her seated with a leg extended, ready to stand and move toward any situation requiring compassionate aid.)

    This feeling, wish, intention, and vow to eliminate suffering has two aspects, one that is trained and developed, and another that is innate and perfect and everlasting. The true nature of compassion is uncontrived, appearing as an aspect of primordial mind. So your original mind already is perfect compassion. This mind's reflective quality, embracing everything in the mirror of awareness, is also exactly its compassion. There is no separation between awareness and compassion, no middle-man, no intervening process.

    The uncontrived quality of true compassion is also very important. It is not an idea. It is not a pre-visualized plan for the future. Putting down all ideas, the mirror of primordial mind functions. If nothing appears, the mirror is clear and silent; if suffering appears, the mirror responds with perfectly appropriate compassion.

    So we might say that compassion is the immediate, uncontrived response to relieve suffering; and we may say that it is an aspect of original mind, in which all things (seen as universal interdependence) are unified.

    Perhaps a simpler definition is "wherever there is suffering, I'll be there to help overcome it".

    We should understand that only enlightenment (i.e., attaining and fully expressing that primordial mind) can totally overcome all suffering. Any other soothing or easing is only temporary and conditional and limited in scope. Therefore the correct actualization of compassion is based on the vow to quickly gain our own enlightenment and the enlightenment of all others.
