    I need opinions... Anything.

    You do not need opinions! Throw them out like stinky old garbage. Dogma, belief, and opinion can't help you resolve this issue. You have to find out for yourself: what is a thing of value?

    To find out, and to resolve issues of life and death, you have to know what it is that lives and dies. This is something that can be seen directly.

    There is a method that has been used by many traditions, and that has brought many, many people to awakening regarding the issue of what is born, lives, dies, and experiences the repercussions of life: this method is self-inquiry. One tries to find the 'self' at the center of one's experience. It is quite a simple practice, but may require ongoing effort over weeks, months, or years. Still, if everything is meaningless unless you understand the central point, might as well devote all your remaining resources to resolving it.

    The problem is that one's habit persists, of behaving as if meaningless things do have meaning. One falls back into pursuing trivial things and getting caught up in old dramas rather than following through on clarifying the central point. In this context, being 'destroyed a little' is very beneficial, as it keeps you on track; it keeps you close to the central issue and reminds you of the utter necessity of resolving it.

    I hope you pursue the central issue of life and death, resolve the question of who is born, lives, dies, and experiences, and awaken to the one thing of value.

    Outline of self-inquiry practice:

    • Try to find yourself â€” the one who does or experiences anything, the audience for whom the entire 'movie' of life appears.

    • Consider that you are not your body, nor your possessions, actions, feelings, nor thoughts; those all belong to you, but are not you. Consider also that you are not just the word or concept of mind, but the actual mind at the center of everything you experience. Try then to find that mind.

    • Soon you come to an impasse, a state where there is nothing that can be grasped. It is open doubt. Like an eyeball that can't see itself, the experiencer can't go outside of itself and look back on itself. So you reach a point where you can't go any further; there's only ungraspability and unknowing.

    • Rest in that Unknowing. It is not something to be chased after or drummed up; but when you come upon it you don't have to fill it in with anything, nor do you have to resist or escape it. Don't think that not-knowing is valueless; that judgment is just ego talking. Not-knowing awareness is the thing of value. Rest in it, and your life will be clarified.

    • If you can completely attain Unknowing in a moment, you will see the nature of the self and understand the basis for the issues of life, death, karma, and meaning. Even if you don't attain it completely, maintaining the practice for a moment makes the moment clear, and maintaining it for an extended time makes your life clear.
