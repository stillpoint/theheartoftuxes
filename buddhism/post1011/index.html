    Soul is as good a name as any for the integrity of parts of self. It's not a thing in itself, but a coming-together of other moving and compound associations.

    Like, when there's music and dancing and lights and stage and audience, we call that a 'show'; but if you point to an audience member or a light or a dance step and ask "is the show there?", that's not really correct. The show is just a combination of moving parts (which themselves are combinations of moving parts); and we give that relationship the name 'show'.

    Likewise, when physical, mental, emotional, and situational aspects of self are aligned, we might call that 'soul'. Buddhism doesn't use that language, but it points to the truth that everything is mutually made. There is no separate self-existing thing or being; it's all co-created.

    The problem comes when people jump to the conclusion that names always point to existing things. We confuse names with reality, and live in a world of names (words and thoughts) rather than the real world of ever-changing compound relationships.

    This is Buddhist teaching, so you've just been listening and following a Buddhist teacher, sorry.

    Actually, no one expects you to completely trust a teaching or teacher. That's a bad misconception of the relationship. 'Teaching' means you move from not knowing to knowing. To have complete trust, that would presume that you already know everything; or else that you were acting on baseless abandon. In reality, you just take step after step, reasonably.

    When you find something of worth -- be it a bit of knowledge or a meaningful question -- then you can take the next step in following it up. When you have rubbed shoulders with a teacher for a while and see that they're kind and truthful, and their speech and action match, then you can take another step in terms of openness or commitment.

    It's no skin off your nose to listen without judgment. People scare themselves so much that they think they have to avoid or be resistant to teachers. Is this a persistent myth, that some kind of mesmerism or cult brainwashing is going to happen? Is it the result of extreme social disconnection, because these people live life online and up in the head? Buddhist teachers comprise an extremely high proportion of exceptionally kind and wise humans. Don't spook yourself.
