    The 'consensus' is available to those who have awakened, like the Buddha and other enlightened beings. It doesn't depend on opinion or agreement.

    Given your questions, I'd recommend that you consider your learning an ongoing long-term activity rather than looking for a one-shot deal to tie it all up and drop it in your lap.

    It's okay not to understand some things for a while. That happens at every stage up to complete enlightenment. You learn a bit, then you find out how much more you don't know. But the risk of trying to get it all at once is that you may think you actually have something, and interrupt the learning process. Not to mention that anything that can be explained in an online blurb is liable to be extremely simplistic and limited (if it even points in the right direction â€” how many people posting actually understand the points they're talking about?)

    Next, it's not necessary to form personal beliefs on these things. They'll form anyway, regardless of your intentions, since that's how our human situation works. They're all illusion, all mental formations, not truth. If you do find yourself holding or developing an opinion, let it go. If you hold something with your hand, you can't pick something else up. Likewise, if you hold an opinion, you can't pick up something new, something beyond the opinion. And actually, Dharma lies beyond opinion.

    Realize that the urge to gain knowledge is the ego's clamoring for control. This clamoring is directly opposed to boundlessness; so in spiritual pursuit you have to decide one direction or the other: hold on and try to control life (which is impossible), or allow your boundless nature to begin speaking for itself. If you proceed gradually and do the work of step-by-step study, and if you ground that study in openhanded, letting-go meditation practice, the conceptual structures will be built much more securely and correctly than if you grasp at these things.

    "I think that..." and "I don't think that..." are just distractions. You can spend years or lifetimes following them, building up your I think this-and-that, all the while missing the central point, which is to wake up and free yourself and all others from suffering.

    Whether there is a true you or not, whether there is transcending or not, whether you take different forms of life or not, you still have the problem of your unclarity and dissatisfaction right now, and your impending death soon enough. What can you do?
