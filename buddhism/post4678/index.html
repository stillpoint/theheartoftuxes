    Reading the Bodhisattva vows, found one to be confusing. Please explain.

    It means that it's a mistake to accept the teachings half-heartedly, denying the transcendent and perfect quality of the more basic teachings simply because they are more basic.

    While a certain evolution and deepening may be seen from the teachings of the elders to the later Mahayana, Vajrayana, and Zen teachings, one shouldn't think of any of the Vehicles as deficient. All of Buddha's teachings are to be considered correct and complete. Any of the Vehicles can bring you to enlightenment. The evidence for this is that there are great enlightened beings within each tradition.

    In short, the fact that another perspective is shown in, say, the Tantra tradition does not in any way mean that the Shravaka/Sutra tradition is lacking or unable to purify one of clouded states, or is insufficient to lead one to enlightenment.

    This precept or vow is closely related to the self-doubt that may arise in practice. One may believe in the Buddha, Dharma, and Sangha, and may be engaged in committed practice, but a doubt may become seated in one's mind and heart â€” an inner belief that the Dharma might work for others but not for oneself. Many experienced students come down with this affliction, wherein they practice halfheartedly, or only for temporary goals (or simply keep at it because they see no lifestyle alternatives), because at heart they don't strongly believe that they themselves are innately enlightened and can achieve enlightenment.

    In both of these situations, faith in the teaching is at issue. 'Sort of' having faith is not enough. All doubt must eventually be eradicated. In part this happens through reason and investigation, in part through example and relationship with superior practitioners, and in part through gaining the fruit of practice with one's own effort. In any case, it is right that the vows shine light on these areas of possible doubt, so they can be met head-on. Complacency on these issues prevents breakthrough and keeps the practitioner falling back into Samsara endlessly.
