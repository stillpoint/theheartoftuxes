    I think I went off the path, but not sure

    There are several possible reasons for the manifestations that appeared for you. But the main thing is not to be attached to manifestation at all.

    Don't get stuck in any particular state. Don't attach to any feeling or experience. Don't make trains of thought about your situation or condition. Just return to the method of meditation. That is why the method is there: to liberate you from attachment to manifestation. In other words, if you want to be free, then you have to practice letting go of thoughts and concerns about "I, my, me" and all the usual inflated dramas that revolve around the self.

    Sometimes people have openings of what they call the 'third eye'. It has become a sort of spectacular legend. But many things need to open, and many things will open. The 'third eye' is not special.

    Most of us have a lot of energy stagnating or blocked in our head, in part because we think way, way too much. So, far more commonly, pressure in the head has to do either with energy piling up behind a blockage or piled-up energy being momentarily released. This is called 'qi effect' (or 'chi effect'), and is what most people, in their self-centered, inflated dramas, mistake for a spiritual experience. It really doesn't mean anything other than you've been distracted from your meditation.

    Properly returning to meditation will help the energy settle down out of the head. So the pressure may or may not go away, but your overthinking will definitely settle.

    Your curiosity and your impulse to figure out your own practice point to a larger issue: beginners, almost by definition, aren't capable of guiding their own practice. The wisdom has to be gained. The experience has to be gained. If it could be bought like an online diploma, everyone would already be perfectly accomplished, in utter self-control, full of insight and compassion, and without a care in the world. But that is not the case; one has to do correct practice, and do it consistently. So in nearly all cases, a teacher is necessary. We need an outside party who can hold the view beyond the ego — someone who can teach and re-teach the right motive and method, someone who can take over the checking so we don't have to do it, someone who can see through our indulgence, doubt, vagueness, and habit, and who can keep guiding us back to the path.

    You didn't go off the path because of your momentary experience; but you went off the path when you attached to it. Now that you know, you can let go and re-set.

    The Buddha said "Nothing whatsoever is worthy of attachment."
