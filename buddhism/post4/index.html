    How to deal with a major fuck up?

    >I'm trying to take some good out of the bad, as I decided to stop drinking, and will use the tattoo as a reminder why. But other than that, I just have to deal with it.

    So you already know. Use your mistake to banish future mistakes. Consider it deeply, feel the remorse and repentance deeply, and use that energy to make positive changes.

    There are two aspects to making positive changes. First, there's the aspiration not only to improve, but to go beyond up-and-down life, to go beyond sometimes getting better and sometimes getting worse. This is the aspiration to awaken, to understand your true self, and thus understand the basis of all of these kinds of mistakes.

    We all have certain scars, whether they are visible or whether they exist within our minds and hearts. We have scars of our run-ins with life. Sometimes it's because of a mistake. Sometimes it's just because things happened that way, and we were at the wrong place at the wrong time. But Buddhism teaches that all of our situations come from our own minds â€” from our intentions and actions.

    Some scars are marks of gateway experiences, experiences that open us to a sea-change in life, bringing new perspective. In traditional native societies those marks would happen in a ritual way, signifying and prompting passages from one chapter of life to the next. In our modern world we tend to stumble onto these passages, these gateway experiences.

    You can take the opportunity to look deeply into the reason for recklessness, the reason for showing off, the reason for stepping beyond boundaries you already have. It's not about deciding never to make a mistake again. If you avoid all mistakes, then you avoid life. But use the energy of this situation to penetrate with deeper understanding of your own mind and habits. In that way, a mistake becomes a stepping stone toward awakening.

    Drunkenness has something to do with the event, but it was not the cause. Don't fool yourself. Even when drunk you still have a sense of right and wrong; it's just that you more easily abandon that sense.

    What is not useful is self-blame. See the mistake clearly, even if it takes a lot of time and introspection; but once you see the mistake clearly, just correct it and move on. Self-blame or shame does not help you to wake up. Humility, yes â€” very useful. Shame, not so much. You shouldn't think poorly of yourself, because that will just lead you to align with that poor vision. Rather, think highly of yourself, so that these temptations will be very trivial in comparison.

    You must see yourself as an emerging Buddha: noble, radiant, and with tremendous gifts to offer the world. This mistake, if you make good use of it, can be the root of some of those gifts.
