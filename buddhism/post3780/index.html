    How can one be an informed and engaged citizen while still remaining detached?

    Good goal... hopefully, liberation for everyone.

    Liberation includes everything; there is no avoiding in it. If there's somewhere we can't be, something we refuse to see or feel, that's not liberation.

    A young sprout needs to be protected. Although it may be stronger than we think, it is not yet strong enough to weather the world. Likewise, our practice may need to be protected until it grows mature, at which point we are able to remain clear in all kinds of circumstances. In fact, if we hide away it may weaken us.

    There are many stories of very sincere practitioners who went away to long retreats or isolated study with a master, and who upon completion of their stint returned to society thinking that they had cleared away their karma... only to find that was not the case. Away from certain difficult situations they could hone their meditation but they could not purify their karma.

    So we can speak in terms of cool practice and hot practice. Cool practice has a very nice situation: the conditions are protected and meditation goes easily; relationship is not much of an issue. In cool practice one may feel like one is making good progress, but the character is not developed well. Cool practice can't 'cook' the student.

    Hot practice faces difficulty and requires the student to be in relationship with others, so not only is ego constantly being challenged but the function of meditation's still point is constantly being trained. One may feel like one isn't making good progress, but the character develops well. Hot practice can 'cook' the student, helping them grow up.
