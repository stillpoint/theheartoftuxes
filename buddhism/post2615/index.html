    I am unclear about how to respond to these negative feelings a regular aquaintance of mine evokes

    Do you trust yourself?

    People often ask how they should respond. My question is how do you respond?

    We don't need to shove the truth into a closet. Of course, there are useful ways to communicate and there are ways that create difficulty; but do you see a reason why you shouldn't be sincere?

    Bragging and flaunting could be unconscious requests for meaning. No one who is balanced and happy needs to trumpet the fact. So the right bit of sincerity may help this person tremendously, may free him from mistaken notions and burdensome ego characters. You risk the possibility that he will hate you or put you down; but are you willing to take on that burden for the sake of his and your eventual awakening? — to get down to brass tacks, so to speak?

    If one truly takes refuge in the Dharma, that means relying on truth, not on strategy. Then it becomes an easy choice, because there is no choice; you only express and enact sincerity. But if that is your route, two things apply:

    First; it has to be done out of love, sincerely for his sake and not in order to eliminate your own discomfort. In fact, you could be willing to take on more discomfort for his sake.

    Second; that light of truth has to shine on you equally. You can express your discomfort, but you must admit that it is your discomfort, not his problem. This added layer of sincerity and vulnerability is vital to correct relationship; and it can be transformative — there is an undeniable Dharmic courage that goes along with it.

    ~

    With any issue of annoyance, there is in all likelihood some shadow material; that is, the thing that annoys us in others is the thing we push into the shadows in ourselves. Aspects of self that we reject or ignore or remain ignorant about move out of the light of consciousness and control us from the unconscious realm. We then project that material out onto the world and onto others. If our mind is sly and deceptive, we find the world an untrustworthy place; if we don't admit our own stupidity and clumsiness, we somehow keep running into dolts and idiots at every turn. The reality is not out there in the world; it is projected from our own minds. Likewise, if our mind is completely in the light we see the world as a Buddha Realm, and every being as an Enlightened One.

    As an exercise in compassion for the wealthy man, for you, and for all others you may encounter, check how you yourself brag and flaunt and lord it over others. It can happen just as easily from a position of more limited wealth, or perhaps based on certain talents or qualities, relationships, physical attributes, social position, and so on. Use this as an opportunity to clean your shadow.
