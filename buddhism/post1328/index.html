    I was aware that some students of Zen might make that leap.

    Words are empty. The main thing here is: do you understand the aim and function of my speech? If you see the reason for saying it and where it points, then you can know how it aligns with Zen. If you don't see the aim and function, you get trapped by words.

    Let's remember that OP expressed his/her confusion and disdain, and asked for plain speech. Hui Neng's attainment was superior — in a sense — but Shen Hsiu's teaching is not wrong; and for those who can't grasp Hui Neng's attainment, Shen Hsiu's teaching brings about understanding and returns the mind to acquiescence, and is also superior in a sense. (The community of monks who originally celebrated Shen Hsiu's poem were not just clueless amateurs. But there was another step for them....)

    Really, in Zen there is no level, no superior or inferior. There is only momentary function.

    This point is revealed by the "waking the woman" koan:

    >In the time of Buddha Shakyamuni, Manjusri went to the assemblage of the Buddhas. When he arrived there, the conference was over and each Buddha had returned to his own Buddha-land. Only one woman was yet unmoved in deep samadhi.

    >Manjusri asked Buddha Shakyamuni how it was possible for this woman to reach this state, one which even he could not attain. "Bring her out from Samadhi and ask her yourself," said the Buddha.

    >Manjusri walked around the women three times and snapped his fingers. She still remained in samadhi. So by his miracle power he transported her to a high heaven and tried his best to call her, but in vain.

    >Buddha Shakyamuni said: "Even a hundred thousand Manjusris could not disturb her, but below this place, past twelve hundred million countries, is a Bodhisattva, Mo-myo, seed of delusion. If he comes here, she will awaken."

    >No sooner had the Buddha spoken than that Bodhisattva sprang up from the earth and bowed and paid homage to the Buddha. Buddha directed him to arouse the girl. The Bodhisattva went in front of the woman and snapped his fingers, and in that instant the girl came out from her deep samadhi.

    Manjusri is a much higher-level Bodhisattva than Mo-myo! Why can't he wake the woman? This is the same point.
