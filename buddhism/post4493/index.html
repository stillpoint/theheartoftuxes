    I've been finding it very difficult to meditate properly lately. Can someone here help me?

    Physical practice is a great compassion to sitting, and helps address most of your stated issues.

    When thinking starts to take over, energy gets stuck in the head and upper body body, and tension appears. Likewise, when physical or emotional tension appear, energy gets stuck in the head and upper body, and thinking starts to churn. It can become a feedback loop of discomfort and resistance.

    A common approach is the 'just sit through it' approach, which can work if one is dedicated enough, and especially if one keeps returning to the openhanded, ungrasping attitude essential in meditation. But it is also difficult for many and frustratingly impossible for some, besides being a bit unbalanced. It is a forceful, masculine approach; but not the only approach.

    Traditional training always includes the body and physical training. The body is your primary grounding influence, helping to keep you from flying off into the world of the thinking mind. When body energy flows, the mind is much more settled; and vice versa. Physical practice is not done merely as another form of meditation; it has a particular effect, of bringing the energy down out of the head and shoulders, and out of stuck emotional holding patterns in the heart.

    I'm sure you've experienced the satisfaction of being 'body tired' after good physical work like chopping wood, or after a swim or run. The whole-body-and-breath activity settles you. It is an ideal time to sit in meditation: the body is loose and relaxed, the mind is grounded, the energy is flowing, the breath is natural... and you are too tired to think much!

    There are other good strategies for addressing your complaints, and others are touching on them, but I also recommend physical practice as a very beneficial and important compassion to sitting. Walking, movement meditation, conscious dance, Taiji or Yoga, and especially prostration practice help to regulate the bodymind and energy.

    Prostration practice has the added benefit of being a vehicle for putting down all that you are carrying. Each time you bow down to the ground, you can dump your worries, attachments, and karmic accumulations; and each time you stand, you can stand up into fresh new life. Bowing, releasing suffering; standing, rising as your true clear nature. It is a whole-body, whole-breath, and whole-being movement that is very healthy in many ways; and it is a respected practice in many temples and centers around the world.

    Also, there are a lot of points of guidance on posture available in the teachings. I've written extensively on the subject. You can do a search of my posting profile. Learning correct, natural alignment of the body, as well as correct posture of mind, can be an enormous relief! And there are many ways to gain support for the body and still maintain an upright, aware posture.
