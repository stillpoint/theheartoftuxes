    We distinguish between kamma, which is intent-and-action, and kamma phala, which is the fruit or result of kamma.

    Our kamma, our intentions and actions, can bring us into situations that that may severely limit our opportunities to determine our own outcomes. This is how a single negative act could trigger a series of many, many lifetimes of suffering, in which we have neither capacity nor opportunity to do beneficial action and develop merit. Once we lose human intellect and consciousness we can be swept up in dull suffering that continues for thousands or millions of lifetimes until the suffering itself expends the kammic causes and opens the window for a positive rebirth.

    And as you note, even just having a human birth is no guarantee that we will have sufficient conditions to practice Dhamma. So if we're here in this subreddit with enough intelligence and leisure to be discussing Dhamma, it is an extremely rare and valuable situation; and we can't take for granted that we'll be able to maintain the situation. Even later in this life we may lose mental capacity or encounter a calamity (illness, war, crime) that destroys our chance to practice Dhamma and ensure a positive rebirth.

    A fetus or even a worm still has kamma, although it is far less conscious. The level of control a fetus has on its life is very low, very dull and weak. Choice has more to do with reactively moving toward nourishment and away from pain; altruism and mindfulness are usually out of the question (though the fetus of an awakened being may still have a strong stream of Dhammic intent moving through; some babies are born seemingly more aware, and almost immediately yearn for association with goodness, truth, and the forms and teachings of their lineage). Much depends on the subtle impressions carried over in the mind from either ongoing compassion in the past or ongoing attachment distraction, and indulgence. When conscious control is not available, one is very susceptible to outside conditions, and one's fate depends on the store of subtle impressions still unfolding -- one's store of merit.

    Short answer: yes, what happens in the womb is still tied to a being's kamma.
