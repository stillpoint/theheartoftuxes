    Why bother about labels. Only do it.

    The true Buddhist is one who embodies the Dharma in thought, speech, and activity; that embodiment just happens to be unfolding in the context of the Buddhist path. But the doing it is the main point. Never mind about "am I this or am I that?"

    Nominally, people will self-identify as Buddhist for a host of different reasons. That's okay. Of all world religions, Buddhism seems most open to this; and perhaps that's precisely because it keeps putting the emphasis on walking the talk. As long as we are doing our best to embody compassion, mental stability, and awakening, it's no skin off our nose that someone else claims to be a Buddhist because they happened to read a certain book.

    Formally, many schools agree that one 'officially' becomes a Buddhist by taking refuge in the Three Jewels (not only in ceremony but in their heart, understanding, and intent). Taking refuge sincerely depends on at least a fundamental understanding of the Buddha's basic teachings (as u/HappyPizzas said, understanding the Four Noble Truths and Three Marks Of Existence, at least on a basic level).

    Faith and understanding are interdependent. Faith opens you to understanding; but understanding is also a basis of faith. That's why Right View is the first spoke of the Noble Eightfold Path. Blind faith without any basis in understanding is foolishness.

    The classic scripture The Awakening Of Faith In The Mahayana teaches that there are two aspects of faith, which can be called conditioned and unconditioned. Conditioned faith grows with practice: when you understand a teaching and put it into practice, then you find it verified in your experience and naturally faith grows. Unconditioned faith is your own innate, essential nature that is originally non-separate from Buddha's awakened mind: this aspect doesn't depend on anything; it is already in complete unity with the Dharma, since the beginningless beginning.
