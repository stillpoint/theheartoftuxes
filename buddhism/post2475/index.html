    You have access if you try.

    If you are willing to work, you can change your conditions. If you are willing to travel, you can access teachers farther from you. If you are willing to be patient, you don't have to rule out less regular meetings, and you can wait until opportunities appear.

    Meeting your teacher is one of the most significant turning points not only in this lifetime, but across many lifetimes. It turns around the entire arc of endless rebirth in suffering, turning it toward liberation. Incredibly significant. And even if it takes a lifetime or more, it is well worth the effort.

    If despite all sincere effort you absolutely can't meet a teacher, that means that you haven't developed the karmic merit to allow such a meeting: you have gained the incredibly fortunate circumstance of a human form, but you have fallen short by not being able to contact Dharma deeply. In that case, you need to purify your karma: eliminate negative thoughts and behaviors, and generate great compassionate intent, and perform many deeds of kindness and generosity toward others. Making offerings to enlightening beings like Buddhas and Bodhisattvas, with very sincere and compassionate intent to liberate all suffering beings, is another way of accumulating the karmic merit that will change your situation. You can also heartfully pray and call to your yet-unknown teacher, establishing connections deep in your consciousness that will guide you through future births.

    >How to minimize the ability for our blind spots and preferences to take advantage of us, if such a thing is inescapable?

    When people ask this kind of question after learning of the importance of a teacher, it often indicates that they are giving short shrift to the teaching. They have heard the guidance only on a surface level, and have set it aside with the intent to try to figure things out on their own. Often it is the agenda of the ego that is being served, as one hopes to remain isolated and in control of how things go â€” avoiding exposure to situations that demand change of habits, abandonment of cherished opinions, venture into unknown territory, and dissolution of self-image.

    You can certainly ask this question, but it shouldn't be at the expense of tremendous effort to find a guide a long the path.

    On Spiritual Bypassing: Link

    You can follow through with further online searches and extracurricular reading. As I said earlier, the main points are the willingness to look into and be with parts of the self that you tend to distance yourself from, and the compassion to do so regularly (that is, to do it without further self-condemnation). Personal journaling that keeps revisiting shadow themes, art practices, and certain movement practices like Open Floor and 5Rhythms that keep bringing you back to both the light and shadow aspects of your character are some of the methods people use for working with shadow. Psychotherapy is sometimes helpful. And certainly being in relationship with clear intention to be truthful and wake up is very helpful. (This includes all intentional relationships, whether with family or romantic connections or with spiritual friends and teachers â€” they all eventually highlight the places where we hide from ourselves. This is another reason why practicing with a teacher and within a community is so beneficial.)

    Take care not to be so eager to minimize blind spots and preferences, as this can become another bypassing strategy. It is important to see and acknowledge and rest with the parts of ourselves that we tend to see as ugly or impossible. A sense of humor is extremely helpful along the path; it removes the psychological charge and allows us to keep encountering our human greatness and pettiness with a light touch. This brings to mind other viable paths for shadow work: clowning therapy, mask work, and ritual theater.

    If we take life too seriously, we create shadow. If we take life too lightly, we abandon our precious chance to wake up. (Taking things lightly also has a 'shadow side', which is giving in to mere triviality â€” indulging in gaming, gossip, entertainment, and sarcasm without ever getting at truth that can transform.)

    Next, any correct practice with a very clear form and commitment will provide a mirror for your mind. If you commit to a practice regularly, and avoid fidgeting and tweaking it, you will soon meet all the various ways you tend to resist or avoid plain everyday reality. Your 'mind will come up', and you will get a chance to see your karma. But as soon as you abandon regular commitment or start to manipulate the practice, you destroy the mirror: it no longer reflects your patterns clearly. Many people know this on an intuitive level, and tinker with their practice, thinking that they are being very clever when in fact they are just avoiding being with their own mind in the space of clarity.

    Even if you commit to a regular practice, though, if you do it alone it is almost impossible to avoid fooling yourself or catering to your current preferences and misunderstandings. That is why a very stable and compassionate external guide is more precious than the greatest mundane treasure you could find: they can bring you back to your true mind, which no title, possession, or relationship can do by itself.
