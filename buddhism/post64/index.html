    I just saw this on /r/nofap, what does it mean exactly?

    Without the craving, it not only is neutral in terms of cause and effect, but can generate very powerful energy for love, concentration, insight, etc. The question is whether you can engage in sex without craving -- and without fooling yourself.

    I should note that you are not the only one involved, and that karmic issues arise any time a partner is in the picture. Even if you don't have sexual craving, if you act without awareness and without care for your partner you can also create karmic causes for suffering (yours and theirs). Inciting your partner's craving could, for instance, send you both to a place of suffering.

    Some spiritual paths embrace sexuality as a generator of power and a potential gateway of profound openness. The moment of orgasm is a fairly unique, albeit brief, window into non-duality. Some paths use this aspect of sexuality and expand the window, training the ability to be non-dual without necessitating craving or the loss of spiritual energy that comes with orgasm.

    Other paths may not necessarily work directly with sex, but instead realize the essential purity of all life. Abstinence becomes not as big an issue, since the perception of purity is developed. You still can't do harmful action, but the emphasis is on developing pure perception. This is one of the differences in Buddhism between paths that rely on strict codes of behavior and paths based on insight into the essential nature of things. There are different paths for different levels of insight and capacity. (The original quote you posted is an example of a path based on regulating/purifying behavior.)

    Theravada tends to emphasise purity of conduct. Tantra tends to work skillfully in transforming energy. Ati Yoga/Dzogchen, and to some extent Zen tend to work on pure perception. This is within Buddhism; many other paths exist, and not only religious in nature. One may find one of the many psychotherapeutic paths to be very liberating, for example.

    Within Buddhism you should also take into account that cultural views and tendencies will almost inevitably shape any course of training. You have to learn to separate the wisdom of the path from the cultural filters and blind spots.
