    How do you distinguish between "judging" and "wise discernment"?

    In analytical terms, wise discernment brings one closer to the awareness of the impermanence and interdependence of all phenomena. It does not create or encourage the creation of ideas and views that make things into separate or permanent entities. So while one can distinguish between good and bad behavior, beneficial or harmful relationships, one doesn't hold an idea that the 'good' or 'bad' has any independent reality. Good and bad exist momentarily, and in relationship to a situation and world that are always in movement.

    To hold an idea that something is good or bad is to make a world of opposites with one's thinking; and that's the domain of the ego -- making a world of strict, unmoving divisions between things.

    Another example: Wise discernment is to see the streams of interdependent movement within any being -- what we call a 'tree' is a flowing constellation of water, earth, sun, genetics, environment, and infinite constituents and relationships. We can use the term and idea 'tree' without being stuck in it. But if we think that the word or idea 'tree' refers to an independent, knowable entity, that is false discernment.

    If someone cheats me and I think "that's a bad person" or even "that's bad action", then I'm being judgmental. If someone cheats me and I see that they and the cheating are vehicles for an ancient stream of causes and results and relationships, then I can correctly think "that's bad action" but it will be informed by discernment -- my thought will not be attached to any fixed entity. I will not be creating a world of ghosts with my thinking.

    Beyond analysis, and in more direct terms, wise discernment is utterly spontaneous. It is one's pure expression free of any intent. The morning sunrise is wise discernment; maple leaves responding to wind or rain is wise discernment. When that pure responsiveness appears in our activity, then that is our wise discernment: you're cold, and you simply pull your collar up around your neck. So it's said that Bodhisattva action is like adjusting your pillow in your sleep.
