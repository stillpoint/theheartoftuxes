    Depressing philosophical thoughts

    The word love is problematic. One word is used in many different ways, with sometimes quite opposite meanings; and yet people still use the word as if it has a consistent meaning that everyone understands.

    Yes, there are meanings of the word love that are all about biological imperative, attachment, fantasy, personal aggrandizement, and base desire. There are also meanings of the word that are all about surrender, open-handedness, reality,unity, compassion, offering, equanimity, and uplifting actions toward others.

    There's no need to think that just because the same word is used these various meanings are necessarily connected. It's just a mistake in usage -- a shared mistake about the meaning of the word.

    Before jumping to disappointment and depression, why not consider the best meaning of the word? Or better yet, why not aim beyond the word or concept, to the living truth?

    A good example of love was in the James Bond film Casino Royale from a few years ago. In the scene, James Bond has already lost a whole load of his government's money at poker, has had a brutal fight down flights of stairs with machete-wielding assassins, and has just been poisoned with a drug that stops his heart. Bond actually dies, but after a few seconds is revived by the girl (via defibrillator). As he regains consciousness he sees her, and his first words are â€œAre you okay?"

    You'd forgive a guy for having a bit of self-concern after an ordeal like that, but his immediate, sincere, uncontrived response was to care for the person he was facing. That is love. Love doesn't come about by strategy or plan; it's not created through effort. It is the natural response of our original mind.

    Now, if Bond would have the same care for everyone, that would be a true fulfillment of love. Shining on all equally, without discrimination; like the sun's light and warmth radiating without picking and choosing.

    Don't pick a gross, flawed concept and then feel disappointment over it. Find the more sublime way, the more noble way; and find how it lives through your actual activity.
