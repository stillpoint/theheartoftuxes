    Why is taking rebirth as an animal considered to be worse than taking rebirth as a human if we are equal beings?

    Equal in our original nature, but not equal in our ability to wake up to our original nature. Also not equal in our opportunity and capacity to understand and practice methods that lead to awakening.

    The human birth has a good balance of leisure and rigor. Higher births may have an easier time of it, living in very cushy situations for eons; but because it is so comfortable and it seems like it will last forever there is no impetus to practice, and when death comes all the merit that led to such a comfortable life will have been used up — the beings may fall directly to hell realms. On the other hand, lower births in animal and hell realms in general have too much pain, fear, hunger, and other forms of suffering to even begin to contemplate original nature, much less practice to reveal it. Furthermore, limitations of language and other faculties prevent animals from making much conscious progress. The aggression or desire they express as animals may karmically determine their rebirth as animals for many, many lifetimes, as they lack the insight and will to change their intentions and behaviors.

    Once you fall into dullness, don't think that there's any guarantee you'll be able to pull yourself out! It may all go on automatic for many eons of suffering, until the karma of aggression, desire, and dullness happens to be purified (by living through so much suffering).

    It sometimes happens that the poor karma of getting an animal body combines with the fortunate karma of having contact with the Dharma (as with animals that hang out around temples or with practitioners or masters). In rare cases it may be possible that a sort of practice-mind continues even in an animal birth. The story of the devoted cow Lakshmi, who would visit with Sri Ramana Maharshi daily, is one such example.

    It also happens very often, of course, that even the great fortune of having a human body does not provide opportunity for awakening practice, as with those who are too handicapped to understand, too beset by violence or impoverished to have time for practice, or who never meet with good teaching. Even those who have quite ideal conditions — good body and mind, sufficient leisure, and contact with the Dharma — often waste their rare and precious opportunity.

    All of the realms are projections of mind. In a dream, you may encounter higher and lower beings; you may have wonderful powers, or you may have frustrating limitations or be beset by horrifying menaces. When you wake up from your dream, you understand that the nature of all of those beings was the same: all mind. Whether higher or lower, all beings have the same essential substance; but relatively few are able to actually guide their transit out of the suffering dream.

    This is all explanation-style; but you can't understand the reality of it through explanation. You have to see it for yourself. In order to understand what happens to various beings you have to understand what it is to be a being: who are you, really? What is it that is in the center of your experience, seeing, hearing, feeling, thinking everything? Find who you really are, and you can understand what goes through rebirth and what, on the other hand, is beyond birth and death.
