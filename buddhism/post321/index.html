    What is the purpose/meaning of different hand positions while meditating?

    When a friend across the street waves an open hand at you, you understand that. When you look at a guy's girlfriend and he walks toward you with clenched fists, a very particular energy is conveyed. When you've come to the breaking point in a frustrating argument and you just give up, there's a particular movement that is expressed naturally and without even thinking about it.

    These are all types of mudra: formations of posture and energy. They reflect certain aspects of mind. Mind is primary, formations follow the mind. But they also work in the other direction, formations influencing the mind.

    So suppose you have been walking around a busy city all day and are tired to the point of distraction; but just then, a luminous person crosses your path — maybe a monk who is the very image of nobility and presence; or maybe a graceful dancer, self-possessed and radiant. Just then, your own mind brightens. You forget your tiredness and even forget yourself. Your posture lifts and straightens, your eyes open. This is mudra: mind, body, and energy are one posture.

    In sitting meditation, there are some basic and overt reasons for keeping the hands in certain positions, and there may also be some subtle or esoteric reasons. Also, some reasons act on a symbolic/mental level, some act on physiology, and some reasons may be more simple (like promoting balance and preventing tension) or arbitrary (when it's a cultural trapping, or just because the hands have to be kept still in some form).

    Meditation embraces the complete moment. It allows everything to be here. So some symmetrical and hand-touching-hand positions, for instance, encourage both sides of the brain, and both active and passive aspects of energy, to be involved equally.

    The level of the hands may invite certain aspects of one's being more strongly. For example, when you are falling in love with someone you may instinctively clasp each other's hands (or your own) in front of your hearts. It happens naturally. Or when you are struggling to think of something, you may bring your hands to your head. Many meditations act as cures for energy patterns stuck up high in the bodymind — hyperactive thinking or easily unbalanced feeling — so the hands rest lower, activating involvement with the more stable center (and the "gut" experience, etc.).

    Some variations of mudra will have perceptible meaning only to advanced or specialized meditators working with very subtle differences in the mind's movement. But there are layers of meaning. Some signs represent teaching, others represent bestowing gifts or quelling fears, others yet convey intense diamond-like clarity or infinite openness. One uses them as a reminder as well as an expression of the mind in you that is already that (already diamond clear and infinitely open).

    Perhaps the most wonderful thing about hand positions is that their ultimate meaning is just what they are. Ultimately, there is no "other" meaning: a hand extended toward you is a hand extended toward you. You already understand exactly what it is, without adding a single idea.
