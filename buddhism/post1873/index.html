    Becoming a mitra

    Although it's a completely speculative thing, based on your imagination, if you are not willing to face those who might confront or attack you for your way of life, then you don't really have a basis of faith yet. So perhaps you're not ready to take this step.

    The phrase 'becoming a Mitra' is an awkward way of saying it. It's just using a label to talk about a way of life and a commitment; in Buddhism you actually don't try to become any 'thing'. So this is just words. The main thing is to be right with yourself, to understand your own level of commitment and be at peace with it.

    You say that you already believe and carry out the requirements for Mitra. (In many communities 'Mitra' is called 'taking the lay precepts'.) That is wonderful. If this is sincere and complete, maybe it doesn't matter so much what you call yourself or what others see you as. But as I wrote above, there is a level of resistance -- whether it's fear or shame or mistrust -- that indicates you might not really believe in this path yet. So more study and practice are necessary: doing the things that build your faith.

    These ceremonies are not meaningless. They serve as seals; they give a temporal and physical form to one's commitment or attainment. If performed with sincere devotion, this can act deeply on your mind, bringing you back to the path of Dharma in future lives our in situations where you might wander off in this life. It is a support not only for you but for your Sangha -- so they can experience new life, like the coming of blossoms in springtime -- and for those who know you -- so the question can assist in their own hearts: "what is worth committing to?"

    But you absolutely should be sincere in taking a ceremony like this. If you have doubt or bad feeling, it could also seal that in you, making an obstacle where there needn't be one.

    To say that you need to be at peace with your own level of commitment doesn't mean that you don't challenge yourself. Life always moves into the unknown, into newness. If you're not moving into new territory, you're stagnant and in some ways already dead. To be vibrantly alive and happy, some challenge is necessary. But it is up to you to take those steps. Don't be pressured into it by opinion.

    So I ask you possibly: does this represent what you know in your wisdom and in your heart, or is your thinking just going around and around about it? Your being already has a direction in which it's moving. See if you can perceive that and show up for it. That is integrity.
