    TheHeartofTuxes

    I want to pass on one of the most practical, beneficial teachings I have ever received, and that you will ever receive. I hope you take it to heart:

    You can't understand it through thinking.

    Thinking-understanding isn't attainment. If you get that point, it will save you tremendous trouble and distraction from the path. It will save you from losing years, and perhaps many lifetimes, of wandering in pursuit of the wrong thing.

    The primal point is to attain emptiness. But that is not the final point. Some religious/spiritual paths end there, but in Buddhism the next step is very important: how does that emptiness or Wisdom function correctly in life?

    Many masters warn of the delusion implicit in only staying in emptiness. As I said, some paths stop at the Absolute. They say, "To be one with God is enough; it is the ultimate." But when your child runs across a busy highway, or when your friend is hurtling down a path of self-destruction, do you say "That's okay; I'm one with God", and then merge with emptiness?

    No, the emptiness is the clear point from which Compassion and Wisdom emerge. So you flash emptiness, but you go run after the child and save them from harm, or you meet with your friend and help them.

    Please, please, be very careful with what you assume. You want to understand, which is lovely, and fine; but you are attaching to words and it's making you go too fast. This is one of the many reasons why a teacher is indispensable: when you go too fast or to slow, it can be reflected to you -- you can see it and adjust; and when assumptions and misconceptions arise, they can be quickly corrected, instead of becoming the basis for every-growing structures that form obstacles to attainment (and that will inevitably and often painfully have to be dismantled).

    >"Nothing" does not exist. ...?

    This is next place where you maybe go too fast. It is not bad that you have a drive to understand, but don't think that understanding comes primarily from explanation. When you attain emptiness, then you will start to get insight into the sense in which things 'exist' or not. Things don't 'exist', but water is still wet, and a slap in the face still stings. Even when you have the primal point, the final point -- the right function of water, slapping, eating, thinking, living -- still needs to be fulfilled.

    Be aware of the illogic behind treating 'nothingness' as a thing that either exists or doesn't.

    But most important is to let it all go, let things return to their own recognizance. Things will speak for themselves. Don't touch anything with your mind; don't make, don't want, don't hold, don't judge, don't avoid. Return to the empty point without attaching to it. A capable and certified teacher is indispensable in guiding you through this and further territory.
