    What I would suggest is not so much reading, but more practicing, and also allowing a big question to work on you.

    We have this bad habit, this bad perception, that our thinking will accomplish the path for us. "If only I get the right understanding." But you already have everything. If you want to practice Zen, you have to have that faith: "I already have everything."

    Then, let it be. Practice doesn't try to pick something up and hold it; practice puts everything down. Even if you do a practice that keeps a focus on a particular object, the purpose of that is to help you put everything else down. And then at some point, you put down the object as well, and you, your wisdom mind, and life merge.

    In some Zen traditions, a big question is used to effect this putting-down. A big question is bigger than the thinking can conceive, so it frees us from the small mind and small self. "Who am I?" ........?

    Once, Emperor Wu summoned the great holy man Bodhidharma to reflect on Buddhism together, and to get some recognition fo the Emperor's good deeds in supporting Buddhism. But the meeting resulted in frustration for the Emperor when Bodhidharma undercut all of the Emperor's supposed achievements. "No, you didn't get any merit. It's all empty."

    The Emperor asked, "Well, what about this sacred teaching of Dharma?"

    And Bodhidharma replied, "Nothing sacred. Only empty."

    And then maybe the Emperor thought that he had caught Bodhidharma, because he asked, "Well, then, who are you?" (Meaning, if nothing is sacred, then who is this supposedly holy person in front of me?)

    And Bodhidharma replied, "Don't know."

    This unknowing essence is most important. The treasure isn't gaining knowledge or advancement, but allowing the unknowing to become clear. It is your true mind, your true substance, before thinking appears. And this true substance is also Bodhidharma's true substance and Buddha's true substance.

    So in Zen, letting go and returning to the true substance before thinking is most important. If you are not thinking, then already the practice you are learning from your previous or current teaching is enough. Just keep returning to your mind before thinking.

    That is why I say that correct practice and allowing a big question to work on you will help you really study Zen.
