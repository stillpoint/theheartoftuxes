    What do you mean by "acting this way"? What way?

    I never said anything about dropping your current life. But, you see, that's the kind of story you carry; that's the kind of preconception or misconception that already resides in you. "Having a teacher means I need to give up my life and go live on a mountain."

    And without someone to perceive your mind and reflect it back to you, this kind of misconception -- and many more that you don't know you have -- will continue. Even if you read some good teaching, it will be filtered through wrong view; and therefore the wrong view will persist, and confirm itself, and feed off of itself.

    The vast majority of what is considered 'reasonable skepticism' here is really fear. People are afraid to look into something new. They are afraid to shed light on their opinions and habits, lest they -- gasp! -- have to give them up.

    But giving up the ignorance and fruitless habit is precisely what we want and need! It's why you came here to ask a question.

    We want it, but we're afraid of what it will take to get it. We want our complete self, but we don't want to let go of our incomplete opinions. They're old and comfortable and familiar, so we hold them like a teddy bear, even as we wish to grow up.

    We want contentment, but we don't want to practice being contented with things-as-they-are. We instead choose to arrange our favorite situation, time and again. Arrange, arrange, arrange.

    We sincerely want to be compassionate, but we don't want to bother doing compassion in our daily activity, with the real person facing us.

    And we yearn to rest, to just be ourselves, to not have to fake it; but we still look for the angle, the way to get 'on top of things'.

    This is Samsara. Samsara is already your suffering mind. It's not some philosophy that goes up against some other philosophy; it's the name for your current struggle. Saying you have no confidence in what's on the other side of Samsara is saying that you don't actually want relief, contentment, completion, freedom -- you don't actually want to give up clinging.

    So this is why it's 'one way or the other'. It's not a philosophy you have to buy into, is just the clear distinction between whether you are engaging in deluded, incomplete, clinging life activity... or not.
