    I understand the practical issues (I've encountered many). But if it were a great rock concert or a hiking trip, or something else that really inspires you, you might set about gathering your resources and finding a way to make it happen instead of just saying it's too hard.

    Zen and other Dharma teachings are far more precious than most things we put the bulk of our energy into.

    A 5 hour drive is not that long. I've driven 12 hours in a borrowed car numerous times, more than I can remember, to attend the center where I could study with my master and teachers, and take part in workshops, retreats, ceremonies, and residential practice. On a few occasions I went with a friend who was interested in trying the practice, or with other members the center hooked me up with. One time, I drove an abbess (nun) in her donated Cadillac, along with another monk. These were people I didn't know, but because I was eager and looking for ways to get to the center the connections appeared.

    I've also been broke and scraping together a living, but on several occasions managed to save up enough over the course of a year to visit other teachers or communities across the country. Many centers offer work-study deals that reduce or eliminate the fee for study; and some offer scholarships donated by other members.

    If you contact a main temple or center, in many cases they can link you to satellite groups or members in your community, and you can practice together or pool resources to access teachings.

    Some teachers and schools offer correspondence by letter or email, through which you can ask questions to clarify your practice.

    Other teachers and schools offer online webinars and other long-range access. Online sittings and other events have been offered on r/Buddhism; do a search. My own current master connects via Skype to those in our lineage who would like to attend weekly classes but live far away.

    Less ideal, but still better than wandering and speculating, are recorded teaching series on video or podcast, as well as invitations to practice commitments taken up by practitioners nationally or internationally.

    If you can't go weekly, try monthly. If you can't go monthly, try yearly or whenever you can. Keep finding ways to connect. Hold a fundraiser or meetup and invite teachers to your community for talks and retreats. Try, try, try.

    "I can't" eliminates all possibility. "Let's try" opens possibility.

    If there's something I can do to help, let me know; and I'm sure many people here would be willing to give recommendations if you want to share your locale.
