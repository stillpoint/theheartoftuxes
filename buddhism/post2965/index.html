    What happens to the souls of those that commit suicide?

    The body is our foremost grounding influence. As we experience during dreams, disconnection from the body leaves us very vulnerable to distraction from rigorous practice and enduring positive mental states. Furthermore, the transitions at birth and death are extremely influential on the mind stream: a clear death, during which the mind is filled with compassion can propel it toward awakening; an unclear death, during which the mind is filled with hesitation, doubt, fear, or self-centered thought can propel it toward nightmare-like drifting states.

    Most influential on after-death movement is how the mind has been used at a deep level during life. What has been the most frequent, ongoing state? Has it been clinging; has it been lazy; has it been dull? Has it been clear and bright; has it been consitently compassionate? These are the states that will continue when all the surface elements dissipate at death.

    Think of how you've reacted at times of utter crisis, when the whole world has been pulled from under you. (If you've even had the chance to experience your mind this way.) At times when you've lost your mind, have clarity and compassion taken over automatically, or have grasping habit or confusion taken over? This gives you some small indication of how the mind will move after death. The whole world, including your body and thoughts, will be removed. What deep habit will continue?

    There are very serious karmic repercussions to taking a human life, regardless of whether it is 'your own' or someone else's. One shouldn't be so selfish as to assume that this body and life stream belong only to oneself. We all belong to each other, we all create and sustain and shape each other; so killing is never an isolated act.

    The crux of karmic results is based on the level of self-involvement of the acting agent, as opposed to their compassion. Is the aim 'for oneself', or is the aim 'for all beings'? How sincere and embodied is the aim? Is it based on fantasy, or based on clear seeing? The shapes of these intention-causes determine the shapes of the results.

    One thing that should be made clear is that there is no 'escape' through suicide. People might imagine that it makes things easier; but really it temporarily avoids things that one will eventually have to face. Whatever karmic results you have coming, whatever effects you have caused, you will have to experience and deal with. But when you no longer have a body and no longer have control over the movement of your mind, the results you were avoiding will easily overwhelm and confuse you. So falling into hell realms and severe states of confusion, delusion, and suffering is very likely after a suicide based on avoidance. Add to the causes the possible tumultuous emotions of the suicide itself, and it becomes very likely (read: almost certain) that the mind-stream will fall into severe suffering states.

    Within the suffering states there is little recourse. Even if Dharma is present, one can't recognize it, much less follow it. Lifetime after lifetime of directionless suffering ensue, since positive causes can't be built up and negative causes can't be eliminated. The only thing that eventually brings the mind stream to relative clarity again is enduring enough suffering to expunge the karmic causes. There is no guarantee that this will happen within even dozens or hundreds or thousands of rebirths, much less within the next lifetime.

    There may be some cases where profound compassion is the intent behind a suicide. For example, the self-immolating monks who brought world attention to persecution during the the Vietnam war. Their motivation was not for themselves, but (perhaps completely) for others. This would certainly bring different results; and their minds were prepared for the transition at death through entry into samadhi (radical concentration). I can't speculate on the kinds of results obtained by their actions.
