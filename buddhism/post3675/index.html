    It is very, very good to look into this issue carefully. It is really more important than anything else in this dream, because it takes you in the direction of waking up. Let's look into it together:

    Who are you? It is not a definition or an opinion. Vagueness betrays your uncertainty about it.

    In relative, contingent matters, there are many possible ways to think and act. Opinions about how to go about things may differ; and that's no problem. But in matters of absolute truth, opinion has nothing to do with it. So to say that your view may be right or mine may be right is utterly beside the point. The point here is not what you choose to believe about yourself, the point is what you are — what is your essential being, your essential nature?

    If you base your life on mere opinion, that is the arrogance of opinion. Saying that one should not challenge another's opinion can be a way of avoiding truth, avoiding the fact that you don't actually know.

    If you base your life on vagueness — "well, maybe it's this, and maybe it's that" — this allows unconsciousness to control your life.

    You actually do not know what you are; you only have ideas that you believe in. You can agree or disagree all you want, but that is all concept, opinion, dream. It is not getting at the central point of life.

    What are you? You already understand that if you answer with any role, you paint yourself into a corner. If you say you are a husband, well that's not all, right? When you no longer have a wife, you are not a husband. Before you were married, you were not a husband. But before and after marriage, you still have the idea "me". So these are only names that you give to certain roles you play. They are not the one who plays the roles and experiences them. Who is the one for whom all this happens?

    You say "I" am husband, brother, friend, etc. What is the "I" that is at the center of all of these? The role comes and goes, depending on the situation. Likewise, 'person' and 'individual' are just labels that you apply. You have not identified what they refer to. They are as empty and unreal as the other roles that you admit don't define you.

    Again, I am not talking about applying some label or concept as a philosophical definition of what you are. I'm looking into the reality. Who is the one reading these words right now? Who experiences all the events of life — all that love and pain that "you" want to hold?

    You say you are many things, but that is just vague talk about vague opinion. If you name any of those things, can you find yourself there?

    "You" have a wife. What is it that has a wife? The body? Do you think that one body 'has' another body?

    You say you are human, but you can't express what that is. So actually, you don't know what you are, yes?

    You say "I am me." Yes, so what is that "me"? The word 'me' is just a word. Where does it point? Where is the thing that is not just a word, not just an opinion?
