    How do "lower" beings accumulate good kamma?

    How do lower beings accumulate good karma? Very difficult. As DespreTine writes, the actual karma made is simpler, and with a smaller range of results; so it takes a long, long time for beings in lower realms to change their situation to one in which significant progress toward awakening is possible — possibly many, many, many lifetimes. Unless there is already a considerable accumulation of merit yet to ripen, it may just be a case of waiting for enough suffering — enough ripening of negative karma — to turn one's mind back to the Dharma. (Take for example someone who has a very strong connection to the Buddhist path, but made a terrible mistake that lands them in an animal realm rebirth. The connection and devotion may be enough to turn the being toward a human birth within relatively fewer lifetimes.)

    We can see a similar dynamic at play in our own human lives. Certain bad actions can land a person in a situation where they hang out with nasty people or because of their environment or relationships they continue doing negative actions daily — drug habit, joining a gang, getting involved in shady business deals, etc. Then not only is more negative karma created, it also becomes more and more difficult for the person to extricate him/herself from the situation. The outer environment becomes a hindrance, the habit and inertia of repeated behavior become hindrances, the relationships created become hindrances, and the mind becomes conditioned by all these and starts to see the world differently — even the ability to have a clear view and hold on to positive aims becomes diminished.

    Then, few possibilities for escape exist:

    • Spiral downward, counting on some positive karma from the past to ripen and offer a way out; in the meantime building mountains of negative karma, the fruits of which will have to be endured in the future. Sometimes, in a fortunate/unfortunate stroke, a calamity such as a great loss or terminal illness can turn a person's mind around toward goodness — the calamity arising as a blend of both negative and positive karma.

    • Recognize the truth about how karma works, and continue to do positive actions even in the face of difficulty and failure, realizing that the negativity you're going through is a necessary result of your past behavior, and that you will need to accumulate merit in order to progress in the future. We know that our environments tend to keep us in a certain perspective, and people who have become used to us acting out negatively, say, may have a vested interest in keeping us that way, may want to keep us down in their hole; so there's a definite risk involved that negative karma will come to a head when we turn to the positive. It takes courage and insight to keep doing what's right, and accumulating positive karma nonetheless.

    • Speed up the ripening of the negative karma so that a workable situation can appear sooner. Sometimes one can see great pain and tragedy coming as a result of one's actions. If one turns around the negative, selfish mindset and takes an attitude of integrity, one may then choose to fully experience that painful result (rather than try once again to avoid it), and therefore be finished with it sooner. In addition, the pain of spiritual practice can speed the burning of negative karma, if it is done with a compassionate mindset (not simply adopting asceticism with the thought that asceticism alone is enough).

    • Maintain high spiritual aspiration, and maintain Unknowing. Taking refuge in the Three Jewels (Buddha, Dharma, Sangha) and dedicating one's whole life to them is far more significant and weighty than any worldly karma, so the way those bad seeds ripen and unfold can be changed, from something that keeps you in worldly ignorance to something that purifies the ignorance. You still may experience painful results, but the pain is no longer useless. Furthermore (more erudite buddhitors please feel free to fill in here) when no self-idea appears in the mind, positive and negative karma is not made; old seeds can expend themselves within that space.

    The precarious nature of our temporarily workable human life — in which we have the opportunity to actually progress spiritually — and the very real possibility of losing it without recourse for countless suffering lifetimes, just shows us how very valuable our good situation is: we have awareness, we have intellect, we have a certain amount of leisure to do what we choose. This is a very, very precious situation; and to take it lightly would be a grave mistake.
