    Compassion is not the same as self-sacrifice. One doesn't harm the self in order to help others.

    The practice is aimed at softening the grip of self-obsession. Your well-being does not depend on you being self-centered and greedy. In fact, you will be happier in a life of compassion. You just need to learn the living meaning of compassion â€” its practice, not its concept.

    That is why we say that true practice trades ideas for real experience. Just giving you words will not help. You need to learn the right way to practice, and you need to get that from a living source, a living example of how compassion ennobles and energizes one's life.

    The trouble you describe comes from basing action on ideas. The fluid, organic, sublime-and-messy human psyche is forced into boxes based on dogma and empty tradition. It is unnatural and unhealthy, and it causes the mind to rebel.

    The correct aim is not to force yourself into some idea of goodness, but to learn to trust your actual, innate goodness. The former assumes we are sinful; the latter comes from insight into the actual nature of self.

    The bottom line is: can you trust yourself?

    Originally, your true nature is already wise and compassionate, already good. Chogyam Trungpa called this your 'basic human goodness'. But only reading or thinking or believing this can't help you. You have to perceive it. You have to discover this point for yourself. Therefore, it is important to study with an awakened being, someone who has revealed this basic human goodness, and who can help you reveal it for yourself.

    In the meantime, you don't need a manifesto. There is no guidebook, no ruler, no set of standards. You don't need an idea to hold onto. If you are present in your senses and in your heart, then you will see what is needed from moment to moment. In any situation, your basic human goodness already knows how to behave. It is not an idea; you see it if you let go of ideas.

    So that depends on the practice of letting go of ideas, moment to moment. Show up clear seeing, clear hearing, clear smelling, clear tasting, clear touching, and clearly perceiving thought. Show up with simple mind, and you will know how to act from situation to situation.

    And if you don't know, practice trusting yourself. When you make a mistake, correct it quickly and with confidence. That is the practice. It doesn't rely on having an idea in mind.
