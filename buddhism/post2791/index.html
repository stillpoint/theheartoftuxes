    There's something I find hard to come to terms about Buddhism.

    That's funny! "Buddhist utopia"!

    I'm sorry, but that's your own non-scientific imagination at work. You make an idea of a supposed Buddhist utopia. You decide what that utopia would be. You imagine how everyone would act. You imagine everyone to be a monk. It's a completely imaginary, fabricated scene.

    You are afraid that science and Buddhism don't go together. Nothing could be farther from the truth. Buddhism relies on truth. Insofar as science relies on truth, it's a perfect match. (And let's admit that the institutions and behaviors that sometimes arise in both Buddhism and the science world aren't always truth-based, but rather are sometimes ego-based and reactionary.)

    Why wouldn't science contribute to inner peace and happiness? Do you believe that it can? We need many kinds of medicine, many kinds of knowledge — for the body, the mind, the heart, the spirit, relationships, environment….

    You can consider Buddhism to be the science of the cause-and-effect of suffering. If you haven't studied it much, you'll be blown away by the vast and profound knowledge amassed on the subject, by beings curious to understand. If you don't know Buddhism well, please don't make shallow assumptions about it, but apply your curiosity and find out. For your own sake as well as others'.
