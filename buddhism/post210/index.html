    Aware of heartbeat while meditating

    It may depend on which method of meditation you are using. Are you clear on your method?

    As the surface layers of your mind calm down, you will become aware of many things. Phenomena that usually go unnoticed will come to the forefront of your awareness, and many events both physiological and mental may seem strange to you. It's all par for the course, all very normal, and nothing that millions of other meditators haven't gone through as well. The guidance is to return to the method.

    In some cases a teacher might alter your method a bit if something is a big problem; but usually there is much more benefit to just continuing. That's how you begin to identify all the various things to let go of, and how you begin to strengthen your power of letting go. If you try to adjust the method to every new glitch, what you are training is not letting go but rather manipulating your situation; you are not getting free of the things that sway your mind, but rather going along with them swaying your mind.

    The heartbeat dominates your awareness because at some level you make a big deal of it. It has been going on all this time, all your life, no problem. Nothing has changed except your awareness and your thinking about it. If your awareness is just open, there is no 'good' and 'bad': everything that arises is just true. So the feeling of the heartbeat is just another part of the ever-changing landscape. Every event should be met with this open awareness, like it's no big deal either way, and you should keep returning faithfully to the method.

    Therefore it is very necessary to have a good method you can be faithful with â€” i.e., one that is proven, and well conveyed, and that you can reasonably understand the purpose of. And it is very necessary to follow the method closely. Let the method teach you; don't try to tweak or otherwise manipulate your situation, but rather practice stability.

    What method are you following? How do you try to feel the breath? Has this been assigned by a teacher? (If so, please go talk to your teacher about it rather than asking random anonymous folks on reddit!) It's possible that clarifying your method of following the breath, or your engagement with it, could render this issue moot.
