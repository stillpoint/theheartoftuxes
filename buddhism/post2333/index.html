    The topics you're interested in are mostly dealt with in the Gandavyuha, the final volume. You might want to start with that.

    Hua Yen/Kegon and Pure Land schools of Buddhism in China, Korea, and Japan study the Avatamsaka. For proper study and unimaginable elucidation these would be your best bets: step-by-step learning from someone who has attained the complete view of the Avatamsaka (i.e., someone fully enlightened). And if you want to remove some of the intermediate steps of conceptualization and go straight to getting that view yourself, practice Zen.

    Sangharakshita offers a few pages of context in his Survey Of Buddhism. D. T. Suzuki touches on the Avatamsaka in his Essays On Zen Buddhism. Thich Nhat Hanh gave a series of talks on the sutra, ~~which you might find on podcast or video.~~ [Edit: Thich Nhat Hanh's talks were compiled in an audio book, The Ultimate Dimension.] Master Hsuan Hua is perhaps the most famous contemporary HuaYen master, and many of his teachings refer to the sutra.

    In terms of modern English-language books on Hua Yen, check Francis Cook's Hua-Yen Buddhism: The Jewel Net Of Indra and Garma Chang's The Buddhist Teaching Of Totality.

    Historical sources include Fa Tsang and Hsuan Tsang. The rabbit hole!
