    TheHeartofTuxes

    Learn and practice the Dharma for ten thousand lifetimes, non-stop.

    Soon find a proper teacher to guide you as to how. A proper teacher is very important; because if you try to do it yourself, your own blind spots, habits, preferences, and ignorance will steer how things go. You need proper guidance that remains stable at all those times when you and your life waver.

    And don't think in terms of 'getting rid of' negative parts of your character. It doesn't happen that way. Since we ingrain these bad mind habits and actions all our life, and through countless previous lives, negative states will continue for a long, long time. It is natural process.

    Young people, especially, want things to move very quickly; they seek instant results. This has both good and bad aspects to it. But if you try to destroy your negative side, you only push it into unconsciousness, where it will control you. Know the difference between disowning and purifying. Disowning parts of yourself creates an unconscious shadow: the disowned aspects are still there; you just ignore and deny them. Therefore you are no longer able to work with them consciously, and you are no longer able to be fully sincere.

    Using spirituality to avoid parts of life and self is called Spiritual Bypassing. Avoid Spiritual Bypassing by going slow and focusing on the moment. Put the emphasis on acting with integrity just now. Don't become the mad scientist or grand wizard who tries to control everything. Learn gradually, apply balanced effort that gradually increases in commitment as your awareness and capacity increase.

    Don't try to do it all at once. This is not an area where a single heroic (or obsessive) activity can save the day.

    Self-kindness will go a long way. It is very important to think of yourself as essentially a great and pure being. You are not the flawed one; the flawed one arises by natural process, because of causes and conditions. But originally, you are pure and clear and compassionate -- that is what you really are. The other stuff is like a layer of dirt on a radiant golden sphere.

    When a person plants a farm field or garden, they don't then tear the whole thing up, looking for trouble. They let it be, and give it regular care. When you provide water and light, things will grow by themselves. Don't tear up your garden looking for weeds. Just care for it. When you come upon a weed, you can pluck it out. No big deal.

    Likewise, when you notice a negative thought or action appearing, you can quickly pluck it out. Drop it, in the moment; quickly move on to the next thing. It helps to have a couple of go-to activities in your pocket for times when you didn't know what to do. Maybe relaxing the whole body with gravity and taking ten conscious breaths could be a go-to activity to interrupt and let go of negative states. Maybe something more vigorous, like going for a bike ride or run. Many spiritual practitioners do prostrations -- many, many prostrations -- to help stabilize their minds.

    Just take it slow and easy, be kind to yourself, soon connect with a teacher, and take care of your moments rather than trying to control your whole life.
