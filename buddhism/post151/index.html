    TheHeartofTuxes

    Why does corn come up at the end of summer, when you plant it in the spring? Why should stalks and ears of corn not spring up as soon as we have the seed? It takes time for the seed to come to fruition.

    The process is clear if you consider the conditions necessary for seeds to ripen. Certain seeds will produce certain crops, but only when conditions are met. Different seeds grow at different rates. The seed may sit in a bag on a shelf for some years before it finds ground. Once it finds ground and starts to germinate, its unfolding depends on environmental conditions â€” water, light, heat, time, and the influence of other growing things around it.

    As a note of interest, archaeologists have grown a sapling from a 2,000-year-old seed. So we don't necessarily know when our karma will express its result; it may be immediate, or it may be in future lifetimes or any time in between.

    If you consider your own life, you can see that you are constantly planting both positive and negative seeds. You do some good things and you do some petty things. Maybe you help grandma with her groceries, but then you indulge in lustful thoughts about the neighbor. Or maybe you are charitable with your money, but you frequently lie. Or sometimes it's vice versa. You might treat a partner lovingly for years, but suddenly and intensely generate thoughts of resentment or hate. These causes unfold together, bringing results in their own times.

    It is like an entire landscape, where some trees have been growing for centuries while others are only sprouting. Causes and results are constantly being processed at different times and rates. Also, you may see the forest and say "that's a thick pine forest"; but perhaps the underground aquifers have already changed due to unseen influences, and in a few years the forest may change or go away altogether. It all comes from cause and effect; but what we see right now, in this limited slice of time and in our limited perception, doesn't necessarily indicate all the causes.

    The unfolding of karma is similar to that of seeds into vegetation, but is not limited in the way plants are. Plants are formed of earthbound matter; karma is formed of mind. All the possibilities apparent in dreams, and more, are within the range of karma.

    Karma will certainly come to fruition in its time, depending on the type and intensity of intention and behavior that birthed it (created the 'seed'), and the kind of ground it finds. For example, when the seed of addictive karma finds the object of addiction (drugs, sex, electronic media, food...) it will flourish; but if one does spiritual practice and regulates external influences, the way the unfolding happens can change. It still unfolds, but the result is not set in stone.

    Then there is the karma we share collectively, based on the things we think and do together: partner karma, enemy karma, community karma, cultural karma, national karma, religious karma, global karma, and so on. All of these, interpenetrating. Individual karma makes your life; collective karma makes this world.

    This is expressed from my understanding, based on study and teachings; I can't claim to see all karmic pathways.
