    Modern metta masters? Is there metta in Zen?

    In many East Asian meditative traditions there is an awareness of the lower belly as the energetic center. It is not "centers" (plural), but this particular center (as the root of one's activity) that is sometimes emphasized.

    Rather than physical/muscular strength, the teacher is recommending rootedness, to be able to relate to all kinds of people and situations without becoming unbalanced. So when you encounter someone very attractive your grasping mind doesn't arise, when you encounter someone very capable your jealousy or self-judgment doesn't arise, and when you encounter someone very angry or disgusting your aversion or escaping mind doesn't arise. In this way you can act clearly and compassionately regardless of what faces you. And even if you are thrown off balance, if you "have a strong center" you can regain your balance quickly.

    Nowhere is this more telling than in facing your own mind. Your habits and blind spots, by definition, are the things that throw you off-kilter; but if you develop a strong center those patterns have less and less control over your activity.

    Some paths will intentionally cultivate energy in the lower abdomen; other paths may reference the "center" but allow it to develop naturally by itself in the course of training; other paths yet may adapt the training to the individual and his or her set of proclivities.

    Sometimes the "center" is referred to as the dantien or "elixir field", although various systems recognize upper dantiens as well. In general, if you tend to engage life through the thinking your energy becomes centered in the head, and your responses become unbalanced toward the mental. This is most prevalent — one could say epidemic — in modern society: we habitually try to encounter and solve everything through thinking. Our addiction to thinking machines (TVs, computers, smartphones, etc.) only exacerbates this imbalance.

    If you mostly engage the world through the emotional feelings, your energy becomes centered in the heart and easily becomes unbalanced toward the feeling experiences. Everything becomes an emotional upheaval — one moment so sad, the next moment spilling over with raucous laughter. Empathy with the dramas of others moves the mind around and leaves you less capable of clear, unadorned action. If you see people who seem to respond too much to emotional stimuli, it is likely that they are unbalanced in the heart.

    The lower-belly center is said to relate to stability, similar to a structure that has its greatest strength in its foundation, or a ship that has a large and stable keel and is thus able to use the biggest sails and ride the most turbulent seas.

    This way of organizing understanding around the energetics of the body is not universal. Various systems teach of different relationships and encourage different cultivation of so-called energy centers. So talking about "your center" is just one way of using language in order to convey the notion of stability. It is a short-hand way of saying that we need to develop the ability to be stable and unperturbed by the ups and downs of life, while improving our ability to respond sensitively and appropriately.

    I tend to mistrust systems that tell me how and where to direct my attention or energy; so I found myself in a very broad, open-handed path. Rather than trying to make something in particular happen with the mind, we were encouraged to let it be. Let it be, put it all down, release the grip — then all energies find their own appropriate pathways.

    Lightning finds its own path through the sky and earth; water finds its own course to the sea. Similarly, if we ungrasp and unknow, the movements essential to our lives find their own correct pathways very naturally. Then everything works with its own perfect intelligence. And this includes what several of my teachers called "the center": when you let it all be, energy settles in the center and the center becomes gradually stronger and more stable. No need for any extra cultivation.

    Moment to moment, let it be, and your center will not only become stronger but it will function appropriately, moving you to appropriate responses in all situations.
