    How do "lower" beings accumulate good kamma?

    The salient point is how this all affects you practically, in terms of going beyond endless cycles of suffering. So the important thing for you is to understand that karma is always an issue until you awaken completely and perfectly, leaving those cycles behind.

    There is much explanation for what and why, but the important thing is the functional knowledge â€” the knowledge that will make the crucial difference in your life. Insofar as you cling to a (false) self, your karma is made by and affects that self. Absolutely; no escaping it.

    In Vajrayana they teach about the mindstream. The gross part of the mindstream is connected to the body, and dissipates when the body dissipates at death. The subtle part of the mindstream continues with imprints that have gone deeper than mere bodily issues. (That is why karma is not only about action, but about action-intent. Strong intents, positive or negative, make strong and deep impressions on the mind-stream.)

    Now, the term "mind-stream" should not be taken as a thing that is eternally existing. In fact, no word should be taken as a thing. Words are only conveniences, pointing toward something we hope to understand; but the word is not the understanding. If you ask how a being could go from one form of life to another due to their intentions, I ask you: How do you go from one role to another in your life? How do your body, living circumstances, and relationships change according to your intentions?

    Moment to moment you are going from one form of life to another. Who you are now differs from who you were a moment ago. And yet you sense or make "something" that seems to continue. The substance of all mind-streams is the same: one mind. Your mind and my mind, and all beings and all things are one mind. Because of coincidental circumstances, beings seem to arise, make the mistake of thinking of themselves as separate beings, and hence create positive and negative karma and all the complications that follow.

    The non-thing that seems to continue, the mind-stream, has the same empty nature as everything else. It is not that some object lasts as a self. But the emptiness can be said to be timeless.

    The process of transmitting karma from one moment to another or from one life to another has been likened to a candle lighting another, and that candle lighting the next: no wax or wick from the first candle gets passed to the second, but the light can nonetheless be passed. Another example is of a seal making an imprint in wax: no substance from the seal passes to the wax, but nevertheless there is an exact imprint. You can consider this the next time you walk barefoot on the beach. "Ah, this is like karma transmitted moment to moment, life by life."
