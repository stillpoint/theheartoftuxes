    Have any here been to a Buddhist monastery?

    I've been to many residential centers, temples, and monasteries in North America and East Asia. The question "how was it?" too broad for me to address in detail, but one thing comes to mind: in each of these practice places, regardless of the school and tradition it's connected with I have felt more at home than almost anywhere else in the world (even my own "home"). I have felt supported at a very deep level, as if part of an open secret — a subtle but very powerful background of intent and follow-through.

    It is quite wonderful and remarkable to live in a place where every aspect of life, every structure, every form is a participation in waking up. The man-made forms and the natural forms unfold together, and there's a sense of each being, each person and animal and plant, and even earth and air and water, doing its own part in the great awakening. It's a feeling of greater belonging in the world.

    It can also, understandably, be tough at times for someone not used to the structure, or not used to that particular structure. The way to deal with the toughness is to be momentary. Take care of the moment, and all will go well.

    Also in common among monasteries I've visited is the palpable feel of taut practice and firm devotion. At times this may feel heavy. There's definitely an agitation that the ego feels about losing the option to follow whims and get its own way (and this may mushroom into panic for some); but mostly I have felt the quiet joy shared by everyone, to be working together in mutual faithfulness, knowing and seeing evidence that we are all striving to clean our minds and hearts for everyone's benefit.

    It is also deeply moving to be in the presence of great beings — a master, or many masters, and what may amount to a pantheon of very clear, very devoted, very sharp and capable people. The only thing I can think of that's similar to feeling the presence of these great beings is something vast and powerful in nature: like feeling a mountain range, or towering thundercloud, or even the power of a great wild animal. And there you will be, sitting and walking and working among them in the pantheon yourself… emerging as a great being yourself.
