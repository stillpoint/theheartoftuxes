    Sadness, grieving, and depression are different things.

    How are you with sadness and grief? What's your relationship to those emotions?

    It's lovely that you care about your mother. If you want to help things flow you have to be okay with those emotions flowing through you. Sometimes we shut other people down not for their sake but because we can't stand the energy they're bringing up.

    As long as things are moving, I don't think you have to worry. If things aren't moving, consider how you can welcome movement. Offer your own heart by connecting in intimate talk, or joining your mother in sadness, or offering joyful and grateful ways of remembering your uncle.

    But on the other hand, you don't have to put up with egoistic crap. Many of us make a show of our grief, either to gain attention or to think of ourselves in a certain way. What starts out as maybe a way of revisiting tender feelings can become a ritual of repeating our sad story to ourselves. We do this because we prefer the certainty of playing a role (even if it's the role of a hard-done-by victim) to the utter groundlessness of death and letting go.

    If you see that this kind of role playing is going on, you don't have to enable it or participate in it. You can make a different invitation, maybe remembering some of your uncle's good qualities and the benefit he brought you, and considering how you can help them stay alive in your own lives. Or maybe you can say "If you want to grieve, let's really grieve" and turn up the intensity, get into the feelings, and express them in talk or journaling or art. Or you may offer that you are here for your mother, but only if she can join you in reality. This is the kind of invitation you can give if someone is getting lost in their own story; but otherwise let others process things in their own way, and offer a lot of space. There's nothing more precious than showing up in presence and openhandedness, without trying to steer the way things go.
