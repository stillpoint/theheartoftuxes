    Is it true that, by default, whenever I am meditating, I am actually doing something the ego does not want? I feel like whenever I meditate I am fighting the ego, because one of the objectives of meditation is to dissolve the ego, or at least lower its "volume". Can someone explain?

    >My question to you would be: if you are aware of the ego´s dominance... how do you then look at the ego? Do you not hate it?

    You look at it with intelligence. You study and learn and consider that this whole drama is not real. While the illusion of selfhood is the root cause of every problem in our individual and collective lives, you recognize that it is illusion.

    With intelligence, you refrain from participating in that illusion. You give it no more power, no more attention.

    So what, then, is real? This is what you aim at, not an illusory enemy.

    “I hate it" is already ego; so that's a problem. It makes a subject and object, which is illusion. And not only that, it establishes the illusion strongly with intense emotion. The inner fight plays into the hands of ego.

    Don't participate. Don't touch. Don't take the bait. Don't engage in the drama.

    When ego says “wow, ego is terrible; you should really get rid of it", don't take the bait. Say instead “you have no power to distract me from reality." Then return, without agenda, to the simple present moment.

    >I hate it and I know there is a part of me that also is able to ignore the ego, or just let it be. But for some reason I cannot access this state very often, or at least not as often as I´d like.

    That's why it's called 'practice ', my friend. You keep trying.

    “I hate it", “I know", “I cannot", and “I'd like" are all self-centered thoughts. To follow them would be to perpetuate ego. Never mind all that. Just return to the simple moment; that is your practice.

    1. Know your correct life direction. Clarify this point repeatedly.

    2. Access good and consistent teaching. Don't flit about between paths, but learn one path well. Find a teacher and treasure her/him as the most precious gift in your life.

    3. Learn the meditation method well, including the how, the why, and the signs of attainment. Follow the method meticulously. Use it as the place where you disengage from the illusion of ego and offer yourself to reality.

    >Are you able to have compassion with ego (or whatever you want to call that entitiy which always wants things to be different)?

    Compassion is compassion. It doesn't pick and choose objects. Return to open-handed presence, and that is already compassion. Then just follow the situation plainly.

    No need to imagine yourself as the knight in shining armor, riding in to slay the dragon and make everything right. Everything is already okay.

    The struggle is generated by thinking. Because you have an overthinking habit, it seems like there are more problems than there really are. When the thinking calms down life will be more simple and the phantoms will dissipate.

    So open the hands. Don't touch. When the thought arises that ego or one of its patterns has to be eradicated, don't touch. Don't participate in that egoic thinking. It may continue to appear, but you don't have to participate in it. Keep returning to simple, grounded reality: in meditation, follow the method; outside of meditation, follow the situation. Actual sound, actual sights, actual bodily sensations, actual smells and flavors are your allies, returning you to reality.

    Never mind what you like or don't like; never mind what you can or can't do. Just be openly aware right now. That's all; that's it. Awakening is here; people just don't believe it can be that simple.
