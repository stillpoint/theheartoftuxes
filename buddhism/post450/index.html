    First things first. Of course, you have to recognize the harm, and then take steps to move away from it. This kind of positive desire is categorized as 'aspiration'. Much later, when the path has been established, aspiration is not held as an external object but rather digested in one's entire being.

    First you train in non-attachment to thought. The desire is still there, but it doesn't control you. This is a long process.

    Next, doing complete and integral practice — keeping correct relationship moment to moment to moment — gradually purifies the desire itself. This is a very long process. For example, there are many people who have awakened to their true nature, and understand clearly that they are not the 'self' that desires; but they still have many patterns of clinging, aggression, and ignorance built up over countless lifetimes; and those are yet to be purified.

    When full awakening is completed with full purification of tendencies, that is called Buddhahood.

    The how means following the Noble Eightfold Path — submitting to a teacher and practice situation, taking up meditative practice, training the character and lifestyle situation by situation, practicing the perfections of Generosity, Ethical Conduct, Vigor, Patience, Meditation, and Wisdom.

    It is a complete life path, not a trick or pill or shopping item you can pick up. As much effort as you have put into creating the desires over decades and lifetimes, that is the extent of the material to be cleared away.

    But there is good news: the desire and other negative tendencies are illusion. They exist on the illusory surface of life. Your true, liberated nature is much deeper and, well, real. When you contact your true nature, although there is still a mountain of karmic material to be dealt with, there is a lightness to the process because you can know that it is not real. There can be joy in living with integrity, rather than desperation to eliminate part of your character.

    Be careful with trying to destroy desire too quickly. This can create denial that pushes your human flaws into the shadows. On the outside you may try to appear perfect, and on the inside you will be controlled by the desiring parts of you that are no longer conscious. This is how, for instance, priests who start with the most pure intentions can commit the most horrific abuse of children — it's because in their effort to be pure they have driven human flaws from the conscious realm where they are admitted and worked with, to the unconscious realm where they control behavior from the shadows.
