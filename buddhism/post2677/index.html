    I feel as if I intellectually understand all I need to understand; but I do not realize them.

    Join the club! You've described the majority of spiritual seekers. We don't need more books, more workshops, more blogs and forums, more journals, more discussion. We need to do it.

    Michael_dorfman gave great advice in his post. Your activity, conception, and peace of mind all need to be attended to in proper measure. Living a good and healthy and ethical lifestyle, learning the Dharma and overturning wrong thinking, and practicing meditation and self-faith all support each other; and all are necessary.

    A teacher, practice group, and devotion to a form are further supports for actually doing the practice.

    But know that when you ask how you can BECOME the truth, you are talking about something that is already here, everywhere and every moment. You can't become the truth because you and truth are never separate. So all the understanding that you have can't help you arrive any closer. What is necessary is a radically different activity, that of letting go, surrendering, embodying as opposed to reaching and making.

    You always already see the truth directly; but you just don't trust what you see or what you hear. This computer screen is truth; the seat you're on and the room you're in is truth; the sounds you are hearing are truth. The whole living moment is truth, and it is not separate from you; it is alive as the scene you're in right now. Only your thinking keeps you separate; it says "no, that can't possibly be IT; it has to be more lofty or special", and so you make lofty and special ideas to chase. If you are able to be without concepts for a moment, the living reality will permeate you. That will be a moment of being a Buddha.

    If you can get a moment, you can get all of truth. So either let go and let the moment happen by itself; or if you can't do that, then find a method that will gradually settle your mind to the point where it can let go and let the moment happen by itself. That will be the expression of your true being. And it will be no different from plain day-to-day activity.
