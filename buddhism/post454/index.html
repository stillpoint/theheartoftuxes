    TheHeartofTuxes

    Have you been hit by a surprise snowball? In that moment you lose the sense of "I am being hit". There is only POW!

    Practice means allowing life to hit you in that way, without making anything extra, without making an idea of a separate self or center.

    It is a fairly high-level understanding to actually know it through your experience, but originally there is no center. Your center is everywhere.

    The idea that there is a center of experience is conditionally true. It is true as long as you have the sense of self. That is how self-inquiry (also known as immoko) works: you seek the 'witness' or center of experience; and, unable to find it, the mind rests in original clarity.

    But once the illusion of selfhood is overturned, all experience is known as mind. That means that everything we consider internal and everything we consider external is all the same substance, just mind. Even different perspectives appear as simply manifestations of one mind. There is no 'center' to that.

    It's not that you see through the eyes of someone else, but that their seeing and your not-seeing also are not ascribed a center, but are embraced in (or rather, as) one mind.

    In the sky, we can see clouds, and clouds upon clouds, and clouds within clouds. Variously obscuring and revealing, and revealing obscuring, and obscuring revealing... revealing light and revealing shadow, obscuring light and obscuring shadow, and both revealing and obscuring both light and shadow. All of these various aspects are embraced in the space of sky, which has no center. Original mind is something like this. But both the cloud-substance and the sky-substance are the same mind.

    You might be interested in the famous teachings of Master Fazang (Fa Ts'ang), who lived in China from 643 to 712 CE. Fazang created a Room Of Mirrors for the then empress, to demonstrate interpenetration and individuality. Another famous teaching of his is that of the Golden Lion.

    By extension, the view of the Avatamsaka/Huayan (Hua Yen) school (which was greatly popularized if not founded by Fazang) describes interpenetration and individuality. No doubt the most famous analogy from the Avatamsaka Sutra is that of Indra's Net, an infinite net stretching in all directions, with a jewel at each intersection of net-thread, that both infinitely transmits and infinitely reflects every other jewel, including the infinite transmissions and reflections within every other jewel.

    Great attained masters see reality in this way; but remember that the direct experience of this is utterly simple. Moment to moment, just POW!, just the plain moment as-it-is. The simple mind, like the sky, embraces all relationships.
