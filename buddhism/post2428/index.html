    There's an important difference between 'open cup' and vagueness. I'm sorry, but your attack of me is indeed defensive reaction. Instead of coming to the point, following the line of investigation, you stop and place the focus elsewhere: "well, what about you?" It's not that it's not a valid question; but if you look objectively at what happened in our conversation, you can see that you turned away from the investigation and got your back up and threw the question back at me. This is that hubris arising.

    What exactly do you think is my claim to the "only correct view"? What do you think I'm saying? I'm inviting you to discover what is at the center of your life, beyond "your view" versus "my view". It is not about view, or opinion, or concept. That is why I am not answering you with a concept. If I gave you a word or phrase ("I am this body... I am my thoughts... I am spirit... ") we could argue about what the concept means and whether the word is correct or not correct. But beyond concept and beyond opinion and beyond labeling, what is the actual reality? This is your sticking point. You want to hold a view, and you assume that I am holding a view. It's not about holding a view; it's about seeing what is actually there. It's not about learning more, gaining more concepts or organizing them in certain ways; it's about letting go of all concepts so you can have direct experience. If you hold your concept, you block your direct experience. I'm sorry it is frustrating for you, but it is an indication that your cup is actually not empty. You are intimidated by the investigation rather than interested in it.

    I could answer you, but so far you are only working on a conceptual level; so how can you receive my answer by seeing where it points, rather than thinking of it just as a 'view', a concept or opinion? There's no evidence that you are prepared to receive my response; so for your sake I am not complicating the issue with it. If you insist that you can receive it with your 'open cup', I will respond; but you have to demonstrate that you can let go of opinion.

    Your original reply had a question, based on a misconception about self. This is what I'm responding to. If you don't want to follow the investigation, no problem. It doesn't change the issue one bit. The issue will still be there.

    "I know that I am." What is that 'I'? What is 'am'?

    Of course you have a deeply seated sense that "you are". That is an ancient habit, established repeatedly by you and your family and society. The whole world is full of people who have this sense, this vague certainty. It is the dream of selfhood. Finding the actuality is a matter of investigating beyond that assumption. The will to investigate and the capacity to carry it through are both necessary; and perhaps these are missing for the time being.

    Good luck.
