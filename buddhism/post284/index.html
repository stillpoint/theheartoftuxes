    It's not the martial art, it's the martial artist.

    It's not the mantra, its that you apply it well and frequently.

    All traditional Buddhist mantras are good mantras that can uproot anger and frustration.

    There are three very famous mantras associated with Avalokitesvara, the Bodhisattva of Compassion, which quell anger like water calming a fire. There is a short mantra, a slightly longer mantra, and a much longer mantra (a dharani, which can be recited or chanted).

    • The first is repeating the name of Avalokitesvara, and calling on his (in some cases her) great compassion. As you unify with the mantra, your mind becomes clear, and your own pure compassionate Avalokitesvara nature appears:

    >Kwanseum Bosal

    (This is the Korean form. In Chinese, Guan Shi Yin Bu Sa.)

    • The second is the mantra evoking the mandala (unfoldment) of Avalokitesvara himself (in transliterated Sanskrit):

    >Om Mani Padme Hum

    • The third is the Great Compassion Dharani, evoking, expressing, and honoring the many facets and embodiments of Avalokitesvara:

    Mantra has an essence, but not magic. The magic is your empowerment of the mantra, by practicing it many, many, many, many times. You have to practice the mantra many thousands and millions of times, sincerely and one-pointedly. Then your Great Compassion Mind appears. If you practice mantra a lot, and often, then you can overcome anger. But we have been practicing — rehearsing — anger with great energy daily for many years and countless lifetimes, so we can't expect a smattering of mantra to erase that energy.

    As you practice continuously, your mind will become clear and bright, and even if the ancient energy of anger flares up it will find no foothold, nothing to take hold of. Then gradually you can change your karma.
