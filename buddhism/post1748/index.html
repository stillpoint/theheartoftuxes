    Share your thoughts on the The Avatamsaka Sutra

    Suppose you travel to Venice and meet someone there and fall deeply in love. Then suppose someone is showing around a photograph of Venice, saying "this is what Venice is like". There is certainly a connection; and the photograph may even recall something meaningful for you; but the actual living truth is the thing. Venice, the place, and the Venice in your heart are inseparable; they are real together. Your activity, your participation is Venice, and it is worth more than a million photographs.

    Whether we're talking about the Avatamsaka Sutra or actually reading and studying the Avatamsaka Sutra, this is not nearly as valuable as a moment of the actual living truth. As magnificent and lofty as it may appear, what the Avatamsaka Sutra points to is not something far away. It is right in front of our noses.

    ~

    Parts of the Avatamsaka are like the movie The Avengers. Much of it is superhero stories. It describes these congregations of great beings, and it goes on exhaustively, listing great being after great being. The length and detail and rhythm of it seem beginingless and endless, making it rise above story; without the usual landmarks, it moves beyond a sense of time and space. So even without considering the words, just the way they're presented is a teaching that points to a truth within or beyond the detail, within or beyond the time-and-space bounds of our common perception.

    Each great being presented has a unique name, like a superhero name; and that name reflects the particular quality of the being's greatness; and that greatness comes from the being's previous suffering. This is a wonderful teaching: our own particular suffering is the practice of our own particular greatness; in finding our way through and out of our suffering situation we gain the ability to liberate ourselves and others. The seed of the superhero is in every real situation.

    You may imagine what name you will rightly claim once you find your way through. Once you turn around what binds you it becomes your power; you can almost see the future and understand what your particular gift to the world will be, by understanding what is binding you now.

    And you might then see this real world you stand in right now as a world of (inevitable) great beings, a realm where everything is the unfolding or blossoming of Original Awareness, a universe of luminance upon luminance, always opening. It is like an endless string of opening flowers, perhaps one of the reasons for the name Avatamsaka ("Flower Garland").
