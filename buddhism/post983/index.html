    Why is it said that one enlightened being brings benefit to many?

    People don't even come close to understanding the benefit of an enlightened being, because they only imagine 'being' and 'benefit' from their own deluded perspective. Because you don't understand who you really are, you can't conceive of what an enlightened being is. You think you and other beings (including enlightened ones) are separate entities in a world of separate entities. You don't understand the interpenetration of things: that everything is co-created by everything else, in infinite and ongoing mutual unfolding.

    But if you become clear on who or what you really are, then you can understand the nature of life, you can know what the world is really like, you can perceive the boundlessness and interpenetration of all things, and you can conceive of the range of an enlightened being's activity.

    Because an enlightened being is no longer limited by delusion about a separate personal self, her/his activity is no longer limited to separate personal interaction. The awake and awakening quality of their being permeates everywhere.

    For example, when you have a dream, you are one character among many in the dream, all following the dramas of contention, desire, passion, fear, relief, romance, intrigue, and a lot of mere drifting around and around. The whole world of dream beings creates this story, this theater. In the dream, there may even be amazingly vivid encounters with holy people and miraculous entities.

    But the awake being is not a character in the dream. The awake being is the one who wakes up and realizes that the whole drama — all of that churning, urgent, involved story — is projection of mind. The dream's true nature is perceived, and the whole world of dream characters is liberated.

    This is similar to the influence an enlightened being can have. They attain universal mind, and are thus a universal awakening force. But it depends on the kind and degree of enlightenment. It doesn't just end at the first glimpse.
