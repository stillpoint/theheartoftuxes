    Quick Question

    First understand what constitutes a "being", then you can understand issues related to birth, death, reincarnation, and the like.

    So what are you, truly? This can't be found through thinking or thought-based dialog. It can't be explained through metaphors and examples. It has to be seen directly.

    In Buddhist cosmology, there are all kinds of beings in multiple realms and in multiple dimensions, all mutually creating. It is not as if there is a limited amount of "being" material that a limited number of beings are made from, like toys on an assembly line. In fact, to think in terms of a being existing is a big mistake. In Buddhism, it is taught that one of the inescapable truths of life is anatta, the truth that there is no separate, eternally existing self or soul. When extended as an understanding of the nature of all reality, this is called asvabhava â€” literally "no own-being". Nothing exists in and of itself, but rather everything is compound and mutually made. So for example, a car doesn't exist separately; it is made of many materials and designed forms that come together in what we call a "car", and these materials and forms are derived from many other sources (appearing through geologic processes, mined from the earth, refined in processing plants, shaped by the efforts of factory workers, based on the visions and knowledge of designers and the requirements of the marketplace, the legal system, the desires of the consumers, and the laws of physics), and the car then also shapes other things (rusts and degrades and returns to the earth, provides the conditions for many activities like work and travel, shapes lives through traffic jams and accidents and pollution, and so on). Likewise, what you think of as a "person" is an ever-changing collection of substances and processes, mutually created by and creating the rest of the world.

    Furthermore, mind or consciousness is not "possessed" by a person; we merely identify with particular streams of mind and think of them as "me". Streams can sometimes flow into each other or divide.

    If you look closely, you can see that many people have animal-like consciousness. They have a particular dullness or pattern of fear or desire that is very animal-like, not really the noble awareness that is the birthright of a human life. You may even find people who are very much like ghosts; almost not here, so very consumed by obsession or divorced from reality. Also, sometimes you encounter an animal that is decidedly more aware or compassionate or human-like than others. This all may demonstrate the ways we carry and express patterns of karma that we have created over many lifetimes. Some of us are just barely in this human realm; others, like masters who can choose their own reincarnation, are very firmly in it, and able to go beyond as well.

    But any talk of what happens after death and how it happens will be speculative until you yourself realize the nature of your mind: what is it, if anything, that is uniquely and essentially you? When you attain your own mind, these issues will come clear.
