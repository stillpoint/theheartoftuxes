    Help me, how can I accept the value of nirvana?

    You have a very fortunate perspective and motivation. Very fortunate. I believe it indicates a lot of practice in previous lives, and recurring will to benefit others. But although your view has a good direction, it is not clear. You are attached to concepts. And that shows that there are both advantages and disadvantages of doing a lot of scriptural study, and especially doing it on your own without a guide who can quickly correct misconceptions or attachments.

    Your concern hinges on two possible mistakes: first, that there is a “somebody" or “something" that experiences Paranirvana; second, that Paranirvana means everything goes away. For the former self, everything disappears; but to think that there is no result and no continuing benefit is to fall into annihilationism, one of the two mistaken extreme views.

    So you have a habit of encountering these teachings through the thinking-understanding, and your habit is taking over now. You are trying to use concepts and thinking-understanding to grasp something that is not only beyond them, but is the complete overturning of them. Therefore as I recommended previously, if you want to go further in actual real-life attainment you will need to digest these things through practice, by letting go of all concepts moment to moment.

    You can get a glimpse or taste of the nature of Paranirvana through practice. Even before you attain full liberation, you can perceive its nature; because the nature of your mind, and of everything, is already that.

    Don't let your progress hinge on whether you can grasp something in your thinking-understanding. That is very limited. Small mind. “Throw yourself into the abode of Buddha."

    You have already had many insights into the central, foundational importance of clarifying these issues. Now is the time to seek out a teacher. Don't say “I can't" or “I don't think it's possible". Rather, say “I will try even if it takes a hundred lifetimes." The churning of suffering continues unabated, so what is the option?

    There are many great masters in Europe. Maybe you will have to do some research; maybe it will take patience; maybe it will take effort. What is the alternative -- give up on the whole issue? If you are concerned with the possibility of wasting effort through Paranirvana before it is even a remote possibility for you, what right do you have to waste effort by giving up on finding a teacher when this is a very real possibility for you?

    Maybe you will travel to a master who can cut through your confusion in one blow. Maybe, as in the old days, you have infrequent meetings while carrying on your practice in the meantime. Maybe you will discover a practice group in your area. Maybe a teacher can visit. Maybe you can make an online connection and study via Skype. It's time to put your insights into action. Be willing to make every effort and give up everything in order to resolve your doubts.

    In the coming days I'll check around to see if I can find any practice opportunities in your area; but you should make the effort yourself -- it will go better for you karmically if you take responsibility for your connection with your teacher and begin inviting him/her right now, before you have met in person.
