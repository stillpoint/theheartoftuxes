    The first, most important point is to know and attain and express wholeheartedly the correct purpose and function of lucid dreaming.

    Your question about choosing skillful goals shows that this issue has not yet become clear and mature for you. So first things first: correct motivation — motivation overall, for your practice and your whole life — has to be clear. This is taught meticulously in proper traditional training with a master. Finding a good teacher, starting a course of training, and building a real foundation step by step is much more important than dallying with lucid dreaming.

    I don't know how long you've been practicing or how much training you've undertaken, but one of the most common situations for beginners is that their aspiration outstrips the reality of their capacity. It is both a beautiful thing and a negative block. The energy of pure aspiration can take a person far. But without the proper motivation and understanding to accompany it, the aspiration itself soon becomes the tool of ego: the practitioner seeks spiritual gain for the sake of gain or self-aggrandizement or 'spiritual athleticism'. It is quite fascinating how quickly and unconsciously the ego takes control of goals.

    So it's not that you adopt a list of skillful goals and fit yourself into that form (though it commonly happens that way from time to time along the path). But really, your center should first be correct; and then the choices unfold from there. When the center — the basic underlying motivation — is correct, then you can recognize skillful goals as skillful, and unskillful ones as unskillful.

    It is taught that there are three levels of positive motivation (and many kinds of negative motivation).

    The lowest positive motivation is to gain powers and benefits for oneself temporarily, to progress and get better at life and reduce difficulty. Although this is a positive motivation, it is still subject to the ups and downs of mood, whim, and habit; so it is only temporary. Samasara still has a hold.

    The middling positive motivation is to gain complete unexcelled enlightenment: to wake up from the dream of life, perceive the true nature of self and existence, and do away with suffering.

    Many people don't even imagine what the highest level of motivation is until they are taught. It doesn't occur to them what could be higher than getting complete unexcelled enlightenment. This shows that our shared mindset, the ignorant, selfish view, is so ingrained that we can't even imagine pure motivation, much less embody it.

    The highest motivation is the aspiration to gain complete unexcelled enlightenment for all beings, to ferry all beings out of suffering, and to secure liberation from Samsara for all beings.

    This pure motivation is the important foundation for lucid dreaming. Without that basis strongly and firmly formed in the heart and mind, the relative unconsciousness and groundlessness of sleep allows one to fall prey to subtle and not-so-subtle agendas of spiritual materialism — egoic grasping in the guise of spirituality.

    That is why a stable, awakened external guide is followed; and why the enlightening and protective power of transmission is gained; and why the solid foundation of pure motivation is previously established through teachings and preliminary practices.
