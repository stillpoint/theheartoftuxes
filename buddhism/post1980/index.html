    No Compassion from Parents

    >Anyway, how can I teach them compassion while simultaneously not losing my mind?

    You teach compassion by being compassionate, yourself. You teach unconditional love by being unconditionally loving, yourself. You teach being free of expectations by being free of expectations, yourself.

    As tempting as it is to place the blame outside oneself, in these cases it is never one-sided. Both you and your parents co-create your shared situation.

    If you want more understanding there are things you can do. It won't guarantee understanding, but it will provide the right conditions for it to arise. You can practice clear communication, stating your needs and wants and the reasons for them, and making clear requests. You can also listen to your parents' needs and wants, hear and take to heart the reasons they have them, and do your utmost to sincerely respond to their requests. It doesn't mean you have to do whatever they say; it means you make the effort to listen, and let the listening move your heart sincerely toward the action it is want to take, rather than the action the fear, spite, and ego habit want to take.

    You can check out Nonviolent Communication (NVC) for a simple structure to follow that helps you get your point across in ways that encourage acceptance and appropriate response.

    You can make efforts to be self-supportive financially. Just seeing that you are taking responsibility for yourself may change some parts of your parents' responses to you. But they will always be flawed. Until we attain perfect enlightenment, we will always be flawed. So you can learn to accept the flaws — yours and your parents' — compassionately; or you can take steps to leave the situation.

    Some of these troubling behaviors, like persistent and unreasonable expectations, function as forces that form your independence. They frustrate you to the point where you take action to get out from under them. Sometimes frustration with parents becomes the main motivation to leave and start living independently, especially when fear or certain wounds or habits have gotten in the way of ideal growth.

    Panic and rage are extreme emotions. If you feel these are getting out of control you need to find support. Your parents may hope, in their own hearts, to be there for you; but they may be unconscious or otherwise incapable. Perhaps they had similar issues with their parents and never learned how to parent well. So you may need to find other supports, like school or community counselors, free support groups, free meditation groups, mental health phone lines, social workers,etc.

    Finding a volunteer position at a charity may turn out to be helpful to you, giving you a purposeful feeling, showing your parents your sense of initiative, and providing a situation where you can be away from them and relate in your natural way.
