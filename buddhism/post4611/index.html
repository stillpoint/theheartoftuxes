    How to have empathy and compassion for people who don't have empathy and compassion for others?

    I mean that the disowned and unconscious parts of our minds are the places where our own 'demons' reside, and the sources of our perceiving and labeling others as monsters. We call someone a monster rather than a human just like us because we try to push away that aspect of ourselves, rather than admitting to it and offering compassion.

    Likewise, you may label people who are quick to label, quick to judge, because you are trying to think of yourself as openminded and openhearted. If this is the case, your approach may be more about an ideology and strategy than real compassion.

    To learn more about the shadow and how it works, Google 'shadow' and 'Debbie Ford'. She has several articles online, and a few books available. Her Dark Side Of The Light Chasers should be required reading for spiritual seekers.

    > ...when what he did is a result of suffering that he didn't ask to be born with.

    In part, yes. He is subject to conditions. But his karma — his past intentions and actions — co-created those conditions. He would not have encountered negative conditions if his past acts had not been negative. That is the teaching of Buddhism. It doesn't remove the need for our understanding and compassion; but by the same token, everyone has responsibility for their own situation. One is not born into an accident.

    But if you give a schizophrenic the benefit of the doubt because he is subject to conditions, you also have to give judgmental people the benefit of the doubt because they are equally subject to conditions. If their situation didn't cause them to be ignorant and shallow and uncaring, their view and behavior would be different. You can't hold one side without holding the other. But the fact that you want to champion one side and condemn the other shows that you have an axe to grind, that you want to think of yourself in a certain light or avoid certain hard truths. What you are condemning is actually part of yourself; it is not the people or situation outside, it is your inside situation.
