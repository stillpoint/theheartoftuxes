    It's just so sad to see so much speculation in this thread. The first mistake is in inviting others to speculate. The second mistake is in taking the bait. The ego is so attached to its self-view as 'someone who knows' that we are willing to trade truth for this sham, this disguise.

    "I don't know" is a perfectly valid, honorable, sincere, true answer to this question. Why don't we see two dozen responses saying "I don't know"?

    So disappointing.

    What would an enlightened being do? Ultimately it's not based on rules but on the enlightened mind. If you really want to know -- if this is not just a discursive whimsy -- then get a glimpse of enlightenment yourself, and see the source from which the action arises.

    People imagine that enlightened beings are the same as them, the same as regular people -- that they go about life with similar perspectives and strategies, and that enlightenment is some kind of thing that is attained, like a power-up in the game of life. But enlightened beings are radically changed; the illusion of a self that supposedly performs an activity or is subject to an experience no longer controls them. Since it is such a radically different way of being, the common person has difficulty conceiving of it.

    Asking "what would they do" is already ignorant, already based on a false presumption.

    So trying to conceive of it by guessing at how an enlightened being would behave is doomed to failure. The attempt is based on ignorant assumptions of the nature of self, treating a being who has gone beyond self as if they would act somehow predictably, as if they were still bound by our values and motives.

    An enlightened being would not be separate from the unfolding situation. That's all we can say. Whatever action arose would not come from a presumed self, but from the one mind embracing all beings.

    So if you actually want to know what that's like, you have to get at least a glimpse of that one mind.
