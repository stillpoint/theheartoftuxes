    Regarding "A Garland of Advice" by Zurmang Gharwang Rinpoche, I have a few general questions on malas.

    You are right, of course, that in its most common expressions, Soto Zen relies on shikantaza and meticulous attention to everyday activity as the forms of practice. I'm not attached to a thesis that every path is flexible. Within Soto Zen there is also counseling according to the student's character, a body of koans, and the most eminently adaptable relationship of direct transmission of mind. But you're right that for the most part everyone sits and breathes.

    As to accessing the ferocity that can clear away obstacles: I don't know about practices associated with Fudo Myo-o. Maybe ask your Soto teacher.

    Are you in a school that practices bowing? At some Soto centers, prostrations are done first thing in the morning, though this may be a more modern or syncretic addition to training. This is a very good clearing practice, and a very good accompaniment to sitting practice -- physically, energetically, and mentally.

    In preparation practice you can express any ferocity that appears for you, express it as the vigor of sinking and standing, and maintaining the continuous line of presence and even zeal throughout the up-and-down. When the body is surrendered to gravity and the head touches the mat, you can let go of everything. When the breath comes in and the body rises straight up, you can stand as the fresh new universe.

    Committing to a set number of prostrations daily, or carrying out a vow to complete a certain number within a certain time, can help you find the drive to cut through obstacles. The practice itself also deeply settles the energy, so the mind becomes very centered and less affected by doubt and wavering. Prostrations can be an emergency method in times of crisis or when one is to agitated to even sit.

    Most deities have associated mantras, but I don't know what that would be in the case of Fudo Myo-o. As you are aware, in the Soto path great trust is placed in sitting as your original being. How does that trust okay out for you?
