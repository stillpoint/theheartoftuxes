    Does anyone here have an occult background or practice, or practice Buddhism from an occult viewpoint?

    I read it, but really didn't find it at all interesting. (Sorry! I hope you find someone more interesting to converse with.)

    I suppose I've been extremely fortunate in meeting exceptional masters. Having witnessed daily their spontaneous, grounded, sincere, compassionate activity I feel utterly satisfied about their teaching and the path. I know the equation is a simple one, of my own effort producing my own results; so talk of auxiliary routes or techniques to 'speed' or 'broaden' the process don't grab me. I feel no need or desire to wander away from the precious thing I've found.

    There are many good paths. But teachings and methods have an integrity to them within a system and lineage. The context imbues teachings with meaning that is lost out of context. The lineage empowers the relationships that interconnect within it. The methods and forms inform and support each other, and comprise an intelligent progression. Taking parts of a path out of context without having attained the full fruition of the path means putting one's own limited view ahead of the profound attainments that lie ahead. I mean, it may work, and there may be karmic reasons for going that route; but there may also be some arrogance involved, and one runs the risk of trading down: you may get the result of your own limited view, not of an integral path.

    The routes you're looking into are somewhat arcane, especially for Buddhists, so you may have to be patient in seeking a friend with both interest and a reasonable bit of wisdom. If you have a specific issue at the forefront, I can listen; but I don't want to devote time and mind-space looking for new praxis when my teachers have already given me the world.
