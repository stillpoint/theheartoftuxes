    Do you know what Zen is?

    There are too many comments from others here for me to reply right away; but I wanted to reply to yours because of the frustration I read in it.

    The question "What is Zen?" was asked, if you understand it that way, in the original post title. It was also asked in the Cypress Tree koan referred to in several places in this thread; it was asked by the monk addressing Zen Master Joshu. But most of all, it is being asked continuously through our lives. It is the central question of your life and mine, and behind everything we try. All questions, all movements have this original question behind them.

    In the koan it was posed as "Why did Bodhidharma come to China?" The Dharma is everywhere, so why did Bodhidharma bother to travel across the mountains? Why do you bother to get up every day; why feed yourself and stay alive and do whatever you do? Why post a single word in this forum? These are all, at the bottom of it, the same question.

    The response Joshu gave is very clear. Joshu and the monk were walking through the temple grounds, passing the garden. When asked about the central point of practice, the central point of life, Joshu's clear mind reflected the whole situation: just this here â€” this tree, this garden.

    Opinion, discourse, analysis, and thinking-it-through can't grasp the perfection of this response, its appropriateness and precision.

    But this is not an answer ensconced in the past. It is alive and real and relevant. It is as if you yourself are walking in that temple, passing that tree in the garden. The significant difference is that you are very attached to words, and so you think that a sensible answer must have words and explanation. It is not that the answer is confusing, but that it is more simple than you can accept. In fact, you confuse yourself by seeking something else. (I am not saying this with an argumentative intent, but with an aim to help you understand the nature of Zen teaching.)

    That is why I again pointed to the tree. And here I mean an actual tree that you actually experience. The whole meaning is there; it is there to be seen, and no amount of explaining can do the seeing for you. When you are facing that tree, that is your whole life. You need to grasp that moment for yourself. You need to get simple enough to receive that.

    But actually, I made a mistake and should have been more specific. At this moment, in this situation, never mind the tree in the garden.

    "What is Zen?"

    "Reading this comment on this reddit.com/r/Buddhism page."

    There is nothing extra and nothing hidden. Hopefully my explanation will help you to understand the point; but hopefully you will also understand that my explanation is a million miles away from the point. The simple truth is not confusing; it is plain and simple, right in front of your nose; but if you are thinking, you won't grasp it.

    I hope you get that there is no riddle here, and no attempt to obfuscate or lead anyone astray. It is just that the mind's thinking habit does not accept â€” refuses to follow through on â€” simple reality. That is why practice is necessary, and talking about it is pretty much the worst approach.

    I hope you, with all of us, get a true moment.
