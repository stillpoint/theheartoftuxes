    How do I get started?

    Maybe learn about it first, before deciding that it's the path for you.

    Deciding to honor peace is very wonderful. If you start basing your actions on what supports peace, and eliminating actions that destroy peace, that is a very good start to the Buddhist path or any spiritual path.

    Even the fact that you don't yet know everything can be embraced with peace. So you don't know yet! -- that's completely appropriate for a newcomer. If you grasp at knowledge or push to know too fast, that disturbs your peace. But if you relax and loosen the grip, loosen the need to get something, then your real life in the moment begins to appear for you: you can start to see, smell, taste, touch, and hear what's actually going on this moment.

    If you grasp at knowledge or results, you put yourself in an imagined future. It's as if you're insisting that this moment is not good enough. But Buddhism teaches that your peace of mind allows the moment to be good enough. If your mind chases things, no moment can be enough for you. If you open your hands, just now -- your true life -- is enough.

    So this is a very good start: to practice “just now is enough". Regardless of the kind of situation that appears, pleasant or unpleasant, you can say “Okay... this is okay. I can show up for this."

    If you practice this a lot, moment to moment, then your 'enough' mind will get stronger and stronger. You will develop the ability to bring forth peace in any situation, not only for yourself, but for others.

    So this is my final suggestion for where to start: please include others. If you only want things for yourself your heart will stay small and uncomfortable, and you will have a problem getting 'enough' mind. But if you offer others your good wishes and intentions your heart will quickly become spacious, peaceful, and happy.

    Your happiness comes not from getting knowledge nor from seeking only your own happiness (because by grasping at it you lose the mind that says This moment is enough); your happiness comes from assuring that others are getting what they need (your mind says I have enough; how can I help you?).
