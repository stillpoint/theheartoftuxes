    Do you carry a weapon for self defense?

    Everything unfolds through karma, through the cause and effect of our intentions and actions.

    So the first point is that there's no loophole to intention -- you can't pretend not to have disdain for others' well-being when in fact you do. If you carry a weapon for the purpose of -- maybe, somehow, some day -- harming someone with it, then you will reap the karmic results of having that intention. Those karmic results may in fact bring you into harm's way.

    You can't escape warranted karmic effects. If you put something into motion, you will have to deal with the results. So any violent encounters you may have are already based on your past action and intention. There's nothing you can do to avoid the karmic effect, but what you can do is avoid creating further suffering, further violent karmic effects.

    Karmic results can't be avoided, but they can be steered. All but the very grave negative deeds (killing, spilling the blood of a Buddha, causing dissension in the Sangha, and a couple of others) can be purified. That is to say, with an appropriate practice the karmic effect can be expended through the practice rather than through life situations. This is called 'practice suffering', the only kind of suffering that can actually help clarify your life.

    So the upshot is that the best self-defense is ultimately keeping compassion in your heart and doing lots of good purifying practice. I travelled with a teacher who would go into the darkest alleys of the city without a second thought; no doubt because he simply had no fear. Another teacher said â€œI don't intend harm to anyone, so what is there for me to fear? I haven't made harmful karma."

    For those of us carrying around dubious karma, we may have to augment our compassionate attitude and purification practice with common sense, and stay alert and avoid situations where we could run into trouble. This is a case of possibly having the seed of violent karma within us, but avoiding the ground -- the ripening conditions -- that could bring the seed to fruition. Then little by little as we practice, the seed itself gets purified, expended through the practice.

    Our minds habitually turn to externals. This is a kind of mind sickness. So when we wander to imaginary future scenarios that engender fear, we habitually look to external solutions. This is how Samsara, the cycle of suffering, gets perpetuated. The solution is not external!

    We should instead look into the basis of our fear itself; we should look into the illusion of selfhood, the illusion of something to be defended, the illusion that there is an â€œI" that is more valuable than other â€œI's". It is ignorance about self that is the true violence, the lasting enemy.

    Compassion is inherently courageous. When you bring forth the heart that cares deeply about others there is naturally less illusion about the self, less self-cherishing, less fear. This in itself is protective. You may yet have to go through pain at the hands of others; but don't let your fear about it cause you to create new suffering karma. This is your only option if you wish to escape the cycle. Know what really is the thing to be defended.

    Compassion is the basis of enlightenment. There is no liberation without giving up your self-cherishing. The heart and mind have to become as wide and all-embracing as we say they are. We can talk all we want about infinity and unconditional love and emptiness and oneness, but only putting them into action in our daily lives will actually mean anything.
