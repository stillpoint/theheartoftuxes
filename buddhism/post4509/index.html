    Slightly new to Buddhism, was wondering if I could be steered in the correct way.

    You might do better with self-review practices, counseling, life coaching, or psychotherapy.

    Meditation and Dharma teachings are wonderful, and can help you; but your ingrained habits will not necessarily be overturned in self-guided practice.

    There is a strong urge, once you become aware of your faults in character, to immediately try to avoid or destroy them. It seems to be a very reasonable approach, but it is not helpful. In fact, it can lead to disaster, as it pushes those flaws out of your awareness and causes them to work behind the scenes, from the unconscious, where you have no control over them.

    This is called Spiritual Bypassing. In seeking to improve ourselves we cling to ideals rather than dealing directly with the mess. It's the roomful of junk when guests are over: "No, don't go in there!" It's skeletons in the closet. It's the elephant in the room, that no one's talking about.

    If you want to get beyond these strong negative traits you need an external (and compassionate) structure that can hold the course for you and hold the view for you at times when you'd otherwise wander off out of distraction, habit, fear, and ignorance.

    In the therapeutic setting, the counselor or therapist is that compassionate and stable influence, allowing you to work through the mess without avoiding it. You experience the wounds that caused the negative behavior, you feel them deeply, and then you can move on in freedom. We are seldom motivated enough to face these things on our own, and even if we were, we still shift in and out of clarity about them. Sometimes, as you know, our habitual reactions catch us unaware.

    For someone who is already reasonably centered, practice with a meditation teacher and group may provide that external structure that holds the higher view. But for someone whose negative reactions come up strongly and habitually, they need to be addressed directly before much progress can be made in spiritual practice. Otherwise it will become a drama in which one part of the mind tries to force another part of the mind into submission. That's not good practice, and it's not healthy for your life.
