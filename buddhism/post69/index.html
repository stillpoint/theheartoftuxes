    TheHeartofTuxes

    The first point is that everything comes from mind. This is not the same as saying "it's all in your head", because the same is true of you, me, and all people, animals, and other beings we can meet: we all equally come from mind. But it is important not to ascribe ultimate, external reality to any being, much less ghost-like entities.

    The rule for dealing with aggressive or intrusive spirits is compassion. The first reaction of many is to try to 'get rid of' the entities; but these are for the most part confused and anguished beings who are strongly grasping at being. They are like us when we are very confused and grasping. Compassion helps everyone involved, and can free the strong grasping that defines the spirit's relationship with you.

    The next point to absorb is that these relationships unfold according to karma. It happens because of what you believe, what you hope for and cling to, and what your actions open you to. People who go looking for power in the wrong places open themselves to this kind of intrusion, for instance. So clarifying your own karma through ethical activity and meditative practice is critical in protecting you from intrusions.

    If you make stories in your own mind, if you remain distracted and object-attached, and if you chase after special powers or mess around with occult energies, it is like sending out invitations to other entities to get involved in your life. These entities may be manipulative or needy humans, or they may be manipulative or needy spirits.

    Some entities are very powerful and aggressive. Perhaps they've spent countless lifetimes being annoyed, jealous, scandalized. Some beings take the role of protectors, and may take great offense to how humans treat the natural environment or act with unconscious disdain of other realms, for instance. Often these powerful beings can't be appeased by one's solitary attempts (compassion notwithstanding). In these cases, communal compassionate practices or the protection of powerful masters -- those who have conscious access to other realms -- may be required.

    And yes, many 'beings' are only dreamlike projections of one's own wandering mind. But some people become fascinated with these dreams rather than keeping the Dharma foremost; and so they live in a dream world and unfold dream stories. We all do this to some extent, living in unreality; but some more than others. This is mental illness. The cure is return to reality: Where are the feet standing? What do the eyes see? What does the tongue taste? Grounding in plain, simple reality is essential.

    Ultimately, beings come from mind. If you keep no mind, you will have no problem with beings.
