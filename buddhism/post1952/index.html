    Any advice on thinking too much?

    > Isn't the whole point of meditation to gain insight and wisdom about the world around you?

    No, not at all. This is a shallow interpretation of meditation, and is based on the notion of a separate self that needs to get something extra from outside. This mistake actually comes from thinking. The wisdom aimed at in meditation is not thinking-understanding.

    Examination is good, and necessary; but thinking can never find the final answer, because thinking itself is limited. It is only a tool. And agitating yourself with thinking makes you more crazy; so you have to know where balance lies, and how to regain that balance.

    The aim of meditation is to actually be the moment. At first, many people may interpret "be here now" to mean that they have to wrestle themselves into the present moment. That means that their attachment to thinking has caused the illusion of separateness from the present moment. But "be here now" means that this moment is already it -- you are already here and now. Meditation means actualizing the universe-moment, participating in it completely. So although your thinking goes around and around looking for an insight, looking for the right handle to grab among everything that's going on, none of that is necessary. What is necessary is to develop skill in disengaging with thought, letting go. Then all things can be themselves: that is wisdom.

    > these thoughts are driving me crazy and making me distant from the world around me

    That's so wonderful that you know it's your thoughts that are making the problem! Many people don't have that level of insight. So now, what can you do about it?

    Thought is not the problem; attachment to thought is the problem. So don't seek ideas. Don't even seek the right idea. Don't make a 'should' about how to deal with the mind. Only let it be, each time. Trying to make something is crazy; letting it be is sane. This approach takes courage.

    At first, this may seem difficult. If it seems difficult then you have to limit your focus to just this moment, not on concepts, not on what has been going on for the past year or week or minute. Just right now, what happens if you just let go and let everything be? If you do that, then truth appears by itself: sound is just sound, sight is just sight, feeling is just feeling, thinking is just thinking. The whir of the computer; the light coming through the window, the feeling of sitting in the chair....

    Now apply that letting go a million times in all your different life situations.

    Sometimes we'll find that we're so up in our heads, so engaged with thinking that it's very difficult to let go. So (some) meditation techniques are offered that give something to focus on so you can let go of everything else. It's very important that meditation doesn't at any time become trying to get something or trying to make something (like peace) happen. Only allow the moment to be as it is, without naming it good or bad, and everything will gradually settle into its right place, including your understanding.

    If the mind is even too agitated to try meditation, then strenuous, whole-body movement is excellent. Full prostrations (bowing), running, swimming, rowing, and free dance are wonderful emergency measures for helping you get out of your head and into the moment.

    Just don't make an idea of what the moment is. If you don't make an idea, what do you hear, what do you see? Real life is there.
