    Becoming a mitra

    Yes, there are very definite commitments involved. It recognizes the advancement of your insight and your application of it. Once you make the commitment, any transgression will have more serious karmic repercussions.

    In your training career you may go through several gateways and ceremonies, choosing to take on greater and greater commitment. This is an expression of your enlightened nature. It is not the only way, but it is one way. You move strongly, determinedly forward, taking on greater and greater commitment, until you are willing and able to commit yourself 100% to awakening all beings through Dharma.

    There should be a balance to it -- you need to be right about it in yourself, because from one perspective it is a very momentous and auspicious occasion. So you must jump in blindly. But it sounds as though you know enough about the requirements and about your own intention. So what is left is to remove doubt.

    You don't need to be certain. You need to be willing to step into the unknown as a testament of your faith, your confidence that the teaching and your own essential goodness will be up to the task. I think it was in the film Three Kings where the experienced veteran tells the newbie that you don't get to be certain before you act -- that's not the way it works. The way it works is you do the thing and then you overcome the fear.

    So this is another part of training. Do You commit yourself to this path? By stepping forward with confidence you gain faith, you gain strength.

    All other thinking about it may be simply a waste of mind space. Remove this doubt. Whether you take the ceremony or not isn't the issue. Removing your doubt is the issue: removing the obstacles to your complete unity with Dharma. If you don't start now, when will you start?

    In fact, the thought that you are not worthy enough is clearly identified in the scriptures as one type of laziness. You avoid taking a stand, you avoid doing what can help you, with the thought that you are not deserving or capable. From a movement standpoint, it is simply a refusal to move. The thinking is illusion.

    You already see the value. It is up to you to pick it up or refuse to pick it up. Your life results are completely up to you.

    Likewise, the worry that you will get caught up in labels is allowing illusory thought to run the show. If that's a concern, then don't get caught up in labels. Let go of your pride, let go of you mulling and brooding, and just do it, moment to moment. If you err, then quickly correct yourself and get back on the path. Fussing with these kinds of doubtful thoughts is just a stalling tactic.

    You are worthy. By virtue of being born, you are worthy of Dharma. By virtue of being in ignorance and suffering, you are worthy of whatever will lead you out of them. By virtue of your current wisdom and practice, you are worthy of taking on commitments. By virtue of your effort, your trying, you are worthy of the precepts.

    You will not make it sincere by setting up the external form perfectly. That is not possible. You will make it sincere by applying your sincerity again and again and again.
