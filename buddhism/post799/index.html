    TheHeartofTuxes

    Any non-monastic Buddhist practice center will train you in applying the formal practice to everyday life. I mean, monastic practice centers will also do that, but they focus on monastic lifestyle, which will have some overlap but won't necessarily address all of your specific life issues.

    If you are very sincere about devoting your life to Dharma, and finding your expression of it within regular life, then I highly recommend residential practice: frequent or long retreats, and especially living in a practice center. Then you will have many chances to merge practice and daily life. Most people who live in non-monastic practice centers have jobs and relationships, but hone their ability to function in the world through the supportive direction, training, and environment of the practice center.

    It is very good that you recognize the need to look into the core issue of your relationship with Samsara. It is, of course, not thrown off so easily, just on a whim. But the fact that you recognize the need and are willing to look into it is very, very significant. The vast mass of population on Earth never gets to that point; so this is a very auspicious and significant milepost for you.

    Since the karmic baggage we have accumulated, as well as the Samsaric situations in which we find ourselves, will both influence us and at times obscure our direction in life, we need to keep revisiting this question of life direction. We need to do it regularly, not just based on occasions of inspiration. If it is based only on our mood, there will still be benefit but it won't take hold at a level that counteracts the habits and delusions we've already accumulated. But if we revisit the question of life direction regularly — as a matter of ritual, say — then it will have a chance to work into our life and mind deeply. The regularity will begin to seat clear direction in us, and will start to overthrow long-held negative patterns.

    So studying with a teacher who can consistently provide both the clear teaching and the clear example is very beneficial. Practicing with a group is also stronger in this respect than practicing alone. Making and keeping commitments to practice schedule that includes reflection on life direction also establish it in you more deeply.

    Whether you study with a teacher and group immediately or later, in any case I recommend daily encounter with the Four Thoughts That Turn The Mind (from Samsara to Dharma), or similar reflections on impermanence and life direction that are offered by various Buddhist traditions.

    Even if for some unfortunate karmic reason you don't immediately seek out and practice with an awakened teacher, you will still benefit tremendously by being with sincere practitioners and awakened beings, at every opportunity. The more you can receive live Dharma teaching from live people, the more it will speak to your deep knowing mind. You may need to hear a teaching twenty times or ten thousand times before you connect to it in your gut and realize the truth of it, and leap to action on the basis of it. So every opportunity to learn and practice should be taken. Even more so, as you recognize, since external circumstances can move your mind around so easily.

    The first thing is to clarify your direction; then become stable in your practice. The primary skill is letting go of objects — not 'taking the bait' in life, not getting caught by things — and resting in clear mind. You then train in applying this ungrasping, clear point to all different kinds of life situations — work relationship, study relationship, romantic relationship, family relationship, physical and emotional self-care, etc.

    When you practice regularly, your center will become stronger, and external situations will control you less and less; and eventually not at all. Then you will be able to act out of complete clarity and compassion, not depending on outer circumstances. Whether you are in heaven or hell will not matter; you will unfold the Great Bodhisattva Way. Heaven will be an opportunity to emanate tremendous benefit; hell will be an opportunity to relieve others of their suffering, and deepen your own dedication and compassion.
