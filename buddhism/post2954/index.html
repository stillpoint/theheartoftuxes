    Readings on selflessness...

    Reading only helps so far as you put it into action. Reading in itself doesn't generate selflessness; and if you start to think that you know something, it can even take you further away!

    There are very good, simple practices offered. Generating Bodhicitta (as in Tibetan Buddhist Ngondro/Foundational practice) is the essential point of altruism and compassion, practiced in a clear and simple way. (Podcast here.)

    Metta practice (guided here) as well as other Brahmavihara practices are universally revered among the various Buddhist traditions.

    Tonglen ('Exchanging Suffering with Compassion') is another popular and powerful practice of unconditional offering. (Guided here.) (Podcast here.)

    In Zen, the repetition of the Four Great Vows while doing prostrations has a similar direction.

    ~

    For an example of boundless, unbridled, overflowing compassion, read Śantideva's (alt.: Shantideva) The Way Of The Bodhisattva. This can be read as inspiration, as a bracing and rigorous standard, and as accompaniment to practice as you make efforts to be mindful of and embody each point in your daily life. Śantideva's writings have been linked previously on r/Buddhism.

    Please also read my post on the proper use of regret. It includes links to the story of King Ashoka, who is a luminous example of someone who transformed from doing very evil deeds to doing exceptionally good ones, based on the power of his willingness to see the results of his own actions, and to feel regret to his core. But it's important that the regret has a direction, and that you move on! Regret should point you toward your next actions, not keep you wallowing in the past.

    Coincidentally, the great teacher, translator, and artist Kazuaki Tanahashi gives teachings on the life of King Ashoka, and also offered this translation of the Four Ways of Giving ("Four Methods Of Guidance", or four selfless offerings).
