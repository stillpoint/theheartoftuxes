    This might be a stupid request, but I want to meet some Arahants. Where might they be found?

    You might consider that meeting a good teacher, even if not an Arahant, is also an extremely fortunate and beneficial opportunity. Likewise, having the opportunity to practice together with other sincere seekers is a very precious chance.

    Zen Master Thich Nhat Hanh says that the time of the individual Buddha is passing; the next Buddha will be a community. Perhaps he sees that the increasing complexity and interconnection of the human world means that associations based on Dharma are becoming even more significant than before. (When Ananda, with joy, realized and asserted that spiritual friendship is at least half of the spiritual life, the Buddha told him strongly "Say it not so, Ananda! Say it not so! Spiritual friendship is the whole of the spiritual life.)

    It is a well-established tradition in India to absorb, learn from, and merge with the silence of a self-realized being. And clearly pathways of influence can be formed on deep levels that we may not even notice at first. But the Buddhist way puts responsibility on the individual to do the work of self-liberation. "Be a light unto yourself." If you rely on someone else's depth of silence, then when that person is not near you, then what? Moments like this one, for the rest of your life, what can you do â€” how can you find your true life right now, not in some ideal future?

    Furthermore, if you do not have a relationship with silence yourself, how will you digest the profound silence of a master? If you want to receive it and make use of it, you must also attain it yourself. Your post is not a "stupid request"; it shows aspiration for something deeper in life. But it is extremely important not to expect others to do the work for you. You must have faith that you are already complete, and that you already have everything you need. The Truth is not outside of you, and can't be bestowed by anyone else.

    Whether you find a magnificent situation, or whether you find a flawed situation, or whether you continue regular life, the big question of life-and-death still faces you; and that is something that you have to resolve yourself. So while I hope that you find an Arahant (or many!) to learn from, I encourage you to make use of every situation and offer your sincere seeking heart to every relationship.

    Good luck in your practice and life.

    Edit: I also want to share a perspective that was kindly offered to me by one of my teachers. When I was younger I traveled to many temples and meditation centers, in many various religions and traditions, seeking a good teacher and a good living situation. When I finally found my primary teacher, even then I was checking and comparing: "Will this be the best place for me? Does this meditation style suit my temperament? Will the connection to this center sustain me even though I have to travel to be here?" and so on. One teacher kindly answered my questions and affirmed that it's okay to look for an appropriate situation, but also, he said, that I have my own Dharma to share, and sometimes I can travel and be here to share what I have and help others without always needing to get something for myself. How wonderful!

    So even if you don't find your perfect situation, sometimes you can go to a place where people are doing practice and offer yourself and your effort and your wisdom to others, not requiring something in return.
