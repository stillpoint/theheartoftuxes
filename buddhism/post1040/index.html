    What kind of beings? There are many kinds of beings in many realms. For example, I have a friend who lives on the west coast, not where I live these days. It is taught that there are beings who we can't see because we don't have the subtle perception to see them; and there are beings who just live in other realms (and our perception is not broad enough to see them).

    Apparently there are many great (human) masters, now and throughout history, who have purified their perception enough to see these beings directly. Not only masters, but some 'spiritual athletes' who have not yet gained total control of themselves but whose perception is subtle can perceive these other beings.

    There are many who trust the accounts of their masters and believe strongly that these beings exist, and they live their lives as if these beings exist. This kind of thing should have a very good foundation for it: there should be a preponderance of evidence that you can reasonably trust a master's teachings. Even so, you will never know for sure unless you experience it yourself.

    The upshot of these accounts is that here and now is a Buddha realm if we have pure perception. In some traditions this is very significant, as the practitioner consciously and systematically relies on the blessings and protections of many supportive and enlightening beings. In other traditions it's more of a moot point, and one proceeds on the basis of one's own good intentions and efforts (and whatever blessings unseen beings may bestow because of that).

    The important point is this: all beings and all realms, even the Buddhas, all come from your mind. It is all projection of mind.

    So if you don't make or hold any concept whatsoever, then what...?

    In the first stage of spiritual progress, one knows only the material world and thinks that is all there is. One is controlled by attachment to name and form.

    In the middle stage, one begins to see the interconnection of all things and develops a psychological understanding of how outer beings are connected to one's inner experience. "There but for the grace of God go I." For instance, one begins to perceive the animal aspects of one's own mind, the god-like aspects, and the aspects that are represented by the many people one meets. It's an entry into archetypal or psychological awareness. One experiences the development of subtle awareness: as one practices, new insights and perceptions appear. Thus one opens to the possibility (and eventually the reality) of other realms and yet-unseen meanings.

    In the further (one might say 'upper') stage, one perceives that all beings and manifestations are one's own mind, and that one's own mind is all. It is no longer merely a good teaching or nice idea, but a direct experience, a direct insight. The illusion of separate self, other beings, and 'things' is seen through. This is great Wisdom, Unity, and great Love.
