    Could somebody explain some things I read in "The diamond cutter". (emptyness and karma)

    Fine, no problem. That opinion is your karma. You experience the results of your view and your clinging.

    The Dalai Lama told a story about one of his teachers, I think Dilgo Khyentse Rinpoche, who was captured and imprisoned in horrible conditions by the Chinese. He suffered terrible abuse.

    Some time after his release, the Dalai Llama asked him about the experience. Intense Rinpoche replied that “A couple of times I was in danger."

    “In danger" -- what does that mean? The Dalai Lama thought maybe a couple of times he was in danger of dying. But the conditions were so bad that he was actually often in danger of dying. So what kind of danger?

    The Rinpoche explained that a couple of times he found it difficult to have compassion for his guards -- a couple of times he was in danger of losing his kind-heartedness.

    So even those who do terrible bad action can be encountered with warmth and kindness. The wisdom eye sees that it is not the person, but rather their ignorance, that is performing the harm. Nothing inherent in the person makes them only one way and not the other.

    If it were the case that such character traits were inherent in the person, then they wouldn't be caused, and would never develop or change; they would just be there from birth and at every moment through life. But if we look more deeply, with the wisdom eye, with the compassion eye, we see that these things are caused.

    Deficient upbringing, pain and suffering, bad teaching, dysfunctional relationship, absence of love... many factors pay into the development of a nasty character. But when the boss goes home, maybe he has a moment of tenderness for his child, or maybe his heart breaks under the pressure of work, or maybe he does something generous for a neighbor. So the nastiness is something arising from causes and conditions, not inherent.

    You can see pictures of Hitler being gentle with a child, or smiling with a companion. The evil comes from a whole lineage of causes. It is not inexorable.

    We want to think of character traits as inherent and unchanging because we crave having things certain. We want our labels, our view of things, to be the truth. But that is the work of our ego, our ignorance, and our fear of emptiness.

    Emptiness means that there is nothing to grasp; everything is interrelated and in flux. This terrifies us. So our cowardice prompts us to label a person or situation and believe that's the last word. If we had courage, we would look at the movement of cause and effect, and know that this person we have labeled is just us in another situation. If you were in the boss's life, with his ancestry, childhood, upbringing, and life experiences, that would be you standing there in the boss's role and posture and activity.

    Furthermore, you cause your boss's nastiness by seeing him as only a nasty person. If you fix someone as a monster in your mind, you encourage that aspect and only that aspect to appear. Your projection and reaction paint your relationship into a corner. But very often, if you come around to their way of seeing things tension can release on both sides, and new relationship is possible. Sometimes it takes more than a single gesture, but more often than you think, a single gesture can do it.
