    How do you find motivation?

    â€œNothing matters" is a perversion of thought. It is an opinion that has been made and held, not based on reality and not based on Buddha's teaching.

    One can see the inherent emptiness of all things, but that's not the same thing. Emptiness means interconnection, interdependence. Everything is empty of any resident nature, because a thing is created by everything else. But this emptiness doesn't mean nothing matters. In fact, you could say it means that everything matters -- you influence and are influenced by everything.

    All sentient beings share one common situation: they have illusion about selfhood, and so they suffer. If you don't actively turn your eyes away from this truth your heart will be deeply affected by our common plight. The anguish you experience in life is the same anguish shared by all beings. You experience it as your own, but it is one suffering situation that we all share. So the motivation is to do away with the suffering, to liberate oneself and all others from it.

    The traditional and extremely effective practice for clarifying and building motivation is awareness of death. The practice goes far beyond just visiting the idea as a concept once in a while. You take to heart the inevitability and unpredictability of death, in a very intentional, organized, and consistent way, seeing how karma (your intentions and actions) steers your path after death, creating the causes either for suffering or for liberation. And even if you don't believe in successive lives, deep acknowledgement of death will profoundly reorganize your motivation and priorities.

    In death meditation, you consciously visualize the entire process of dying, so that it can become deeply seated in you as a knowledge, a reality. This is a tremendous motivator, acknowledged by masters of all traditions.

    I can also vouch for the effectiveness of the practice, and honor it as a profound and essential gift you can give yourself. When I started doing death meditation, it very quickly arranged my priorities and cut off huge portions of trivial crap from my life. It is something (I intend and pray) I will always value and practice.
