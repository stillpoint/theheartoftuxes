    You are trying to cogitate your way through a koan, a process which is meant to go beyond cogitation. This is not the way to approach a koan.

    If you want to study koans, go to a teacher who works with them and begin a proper course of training. An online forum is a terrible place to learn, and if you try to be your own koan master it will be like the doctor who tries to be his own patient: the patient has a fool for a doctor.

    Our human predicament is that we are fooled by our thinking, and we don't understand who or what we really are. That means we don't understand what anything really is. We attach to words and concepts, thinking of them as real, and we have great difficulty even perceiving reality, much less relating to it harmoniously. All troubles brought to the world by humans have this base cause.

    Koan practice means training in reliance on the mind of Wisdom rather than the making, grasping, thinking mind that is usually running the show. That is, one trains in being guided by truth rather than ego.

    The first step is to be able to reliably access the basis of truth, which is Unknowing. One has to be able to put it all down, to let go of concept and return to 'zero'. Both sitting meditation practice and the guidance of the master in koan interview train the student in relying on this primary point, this basic clear mind, rather than attaching to names and forms.

    The koans themselves then have many different roles: initially, they are hung like bait to see if the student really trusts her/his own basic mind, or whether she/he still attaches to names and forms. The koan then points out where more letting-go is required.

    But koans originally encapsulate an instance of awakening: a case in which someone was brought to clarity, brought to their own basic mind. So actually attaining a koan means getting this basic mind. It is a precious moment, a precious gift of essentially the same awakening as possessed by the person in the koan, the Zen Master presenting the koan, and Buddha himself. (Though the moment your basic mind comes to the forefront has the same essence as the Buddha's awakening, it can only be called completely the same when it has transformed you through-and-through, eliminating every last trace of obscuration and affliction.)

    Next, beyond just reliance on the basic Unknowing mind, and beyond just attaining it in a flash of insight, koans then train the student in how to apply that mind in all kinds of situations, from relating to enlightenment itself to everyday life situations like work and household management, to big questions like the nature of reality, to encountering mystical experiences, to issues like "what is love?" The koan trains the student in how their original, basic clear mind functions properly from situation to situation, moment to moment. It teaches one to trust oneself, to trust one's spontaneous Wisdom (neither dogma nor just impetuous or unconscious action) and one's spontaneous compassion (not just sappy, nicey-nice philosophies).

    So in encountering koans it is very necessary to let go of trying to figure and understand through conceptual thinking, and instead to trust Unknowing. This requires a teacher to point out that Unknowing mind, to guide you in practicing it, to highlight when — unbeknownst to you — conceptualization has snuck in, to weed out misperceptions and obscuring habits, to provide the example of perfect moment-to-moment expression of Unknowing, and to catalyze your awakenings.
