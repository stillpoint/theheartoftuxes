    Too lazy to perfect my karma(s).

    You don't correct aeons of karma, you correct your relationship to the moment. Your idea of your karma is just an idea, just a story. Getting caught up in that story and battling your own mind creates more of the same karma. The only place where you can actually make a difference is the present moment. So you have to take care of your relationship to the moment.

    Yes, we have immense karmic burdens -- mountains on mountains of it. So our intent, our commitment, needs to be infinite. But the way that commitment functions is completely momentary.

    In terms of cutting through your patterns and gaining a better motivation, two things have been recommended by countless masters:

    1. Engage in daily mindfulness of death and impermanence. Practicing with The Four Thoughts That Turn The Mind and/or doing daily death meditation will transform your motivation. Guaranteed.

    2. Study with a capable teacher and practice with a group of sincere seekers. We have ignorance and habit built into us, and our ignorant habit mind is not capable of keeping a view beyond itself; therefore we go to someone who has attained the fruition of training, and who can hold that view (beyond ego) for us consistently, and who can guide us at every turn as to how to overcome our inner and outer obstacles.

    If your own motivation and stability are not strong enough to keep you from falling off the path time after time you need to use an external support structure until you have enough strength to do it on your own. You need to thrust yourself into a situation that is good for you, that will train you despite your likes and dislikes, that will keep you going despite your lazy habits.

    Your karma opens and closes. There will be many times when you are bound to the path, when you don't care, or when you are too dull or distracted to even see the life-and-death issue. But then the window will open.

    Sometimes this happens because you suffer a great loss or are otherwise shaken to the core in life... and then you see that you can't waste your time any more. Sometimes it will happen seemingly by Grace or through a fortunate encounter.

    When the window opens and you see that you have to get on with clarifying your life, jump on that opportunity and take action. Commit yourself to a structure that will keep you engaged in practice even when the window closes for you. That means committing to a teacher and group, and may even mean changing your living situation and relationships.
