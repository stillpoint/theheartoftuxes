    Help me on my intricate path.

    Love for all involved. Love, love, love.

    Your judgment won't change her mind, nor heal the wounds incurred. You must love her no matter what she chooses to do.

    As far as I know, in the USA at least, a man (or other partner) doesn't have a legal say in what a woman chooses to do with her pregnancy. But from the perspective of the Path, it is your duty to put down your opinion. This is how your mind's light shines to others — through openminded and openhearted presence. Your mind's light doesn't shine through opinion.

    I notice that you call it your girlfriend's pregnancy, without stating your involvement. Are you the one who got her pregnant? What I see is a desperate clinging for control, over the situation and even over another person, rather than an acknowledgement of your part in creating the situation through your own actions and unclarity. So please take a breath, open your hands, and just do nothing for some moments.

    If you are concerned about insulting life, then you must show up with openness and love, to support others rather than have them do your bidding. You do your part, and be patient with others who are not there yet.

    In terms of special practice, you can do prayer, mantra practice, and chanting for everyone, including the unborn being. This should not be conceived as a way to try to get someone to change, but as an unconditional offering of your best qualities — peace, goodwill, kindness, vulnerability, openhandedness, presence, patience, and the aspiration to awaken beyond birth and death. You can chant the name or mantra of the Bodhisattva of Compassion, or the name or mantra of the Earth Womb Bodhisattva, who protects unborn children and ferries beings between birth and death.

    You can also repeat the mantra of your choice moment to moment throughout the day. That will help everyone. It will protect your mind from the negative and chaotic churning thoughts, and it will project compassion to all others involved.

    Bodhisattva of Compassion, "One Who Hears The World Sound" / Sansk: Avalokitesvara / Ch: Kwan Yin / Kor: Kwan Se Um Bosal / Tib: Chenresig

    Name chanting: kwanseum bosal

    Mantra: om mani padme hum

    Dharani (long mantra) chanting: any of the translations of the Great Compassion Dharani

    ~

    Earth Womb Bodhisattva / Sansk: Ksitigarbha / Ch: Dizang / Korean: Jijang Bosal / Tib: Sa-E Nyingpo

    name chanting: jijang bosal

    mantra chanting: namaḥ samantabuddhānāṃ ha ha ha sutanu svāhā

    So, you can chant or repeat to yourself "jijang bosal jijang bosal jijang bosal jijang bosal…" or "om mani padme hum om mani padme hum om mani padme hum…", or any of the others listed. The main thing is just to do it sincerely. It will center your mind, and is a healing practice for everyone.

    If you have much difficulty letting go of the strong opinion and feeling you are swirling in, I recommend a practice that comes to us from outside of Buddhism: ho'oponopono. This is a Hawaiian mantra that embodies four meanings. I recommend either repeating the four meanings and ending with this mantra, or using one of the mantras listed above, but with the feeling of all four meanings. Those meanings are:

    I love you

    I'm sorry

    Please forgive me

    Thank you

    Repeat these meanings sincerely, out loud, to heal the situation.
