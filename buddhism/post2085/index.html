    TheHeartofTuxes

    It sounds like you're eager to face everything, face everything, face everything. There's a dedicated, zealous energy behind it. But is there also clinging to opinion? In facing everything, are you able to hear what others are saying here?

    'Facing' is only one part; another part is the openhandedness and awareness you bring to that which you face. If you look with equanimity you may see that we go in and out, sometimes private and sometimes public, sometimes engaging with worldly issues and sometimes engaging with spiritual ones.

    The physical heart opens and closes as it moves blood in and out. The lungs expand and contract as they move breath in and out. And the emotional heart also has its rhythms of expansion and contraction.

    It is very often the case that we need to protect ourselves, at least temporarily. Maybe we step out of an argument so we can return to a non-reactive, compassionate mind state, at which time we can return and communicate better with the other party.

    Maybe we find our minds attaching to trivial or disturbing things in the media, and we step away from the inundation so we can perceive more deeply, at which time we can make more meaningful decisions about how to engage -- with purpose, and with effectiveness.

    Maybe we burn out and need to recharge in an environment of silence, away from nattering influences for a while. Then 'when our batteries are recharged', we can be more stable and steadfast in the face of very difficult realities.

    If you only force yourself to face difficulty, how do you avoid making a shadow? The Buddha realized the futility -- the exhaustion -- implicit in ascetic practices. The aim is not to use only difficulty to (one hopes) purify oneself, but to find the middle way where proper balance of nourishment and difficulty helps you to face reality.

    Do you have a teacher, and do you do regular practice and go on retreats with your teacher? In isolation we often develop and attach to lopsided opinions. The hard-headed opinion becomes the refuge of the ego. So if we're serious about facing reality, submitting to the clear eye of a teacher and the clear teaching and structure of group practice is important -- to avoid giving the ego those havens.
