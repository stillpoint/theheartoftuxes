    Betrayed by Buddhism

    Not believing can be compatible with Buddhism. Some (like Stephen Batchelor) suggest that it is a very good foundation indeed.

    I think that to follow the Buddhist way, perhaps the one thing you do need to have some faith in is causality. You have to be aware that cause and effect hold true â€” whatever you do has a certain result; and if causes and conditions remain the same they lead to the same results.

    But having faith in that should come from actually seeing it to be true, not from an opinion that you borrow from what someone else says. In fact, you can be thankful that your friend is helping you to strip away opinions that are not based in your personal experience. You don't want to build a house of cards, right?

    So the Buddhist symbol of a diamond is your guiding principle: rather than shaky concepts you want something that is pure and unbreakable, something that you can experience for yourself and have 100% confidence in.

    Since this is not a matter of believing but of seeing, your laboratory is still open for business. It doesn't matter whether you call it Buddhism or Christianity or atheism or whatever; what matters is that you find the truth for yourself. That means that you have to continue the process you started with your friend, tearing down the conceptual structures that prevent you from seeing clearly. The name for that is meditation.

    I don't know what you mean by "achieve through meditating, without practice". If you become aware of how your mind works, you will see that everything in your life affects and is affected by your mind. You will see how lifestyle, relationship, patterns of speech, activities, and consumption of all kinds can change your mind for better or for worse. Hang out with the wrong people or eat the wrong food, and it is harder to find peace and satisfaction. Do generous deeds and use your speech well, and peace of mind comes more naturally.

    This is Buddhism; it lays out a path based on natural laws, on cause and effect, so that you can help yourself and others. Very simple. That simplicity just gets lost in all the talk sometimes.
