    I want to dedicate more of my time and thoughts to buddhism, and I need some help

    Hi, Sangha sister. Thank you for reaching out.

    It's interesting how things move in life. Sometimes the reasons you connect with people are not the reasons you think, and sometimes the ways experiences move through you are not the ways you can conceive. So true life is beyond thinking.

    You already have a practice mind. Maybe you have heard this term in Japanese: Do Shin, or Way Mind. You have a way-seeking mind. The important thing is to keep nurturing this mind. Outside form is not as important; there are many possible forms of lifestyle and livelihood. Holding to the Great Way in your heart and mind is most important.

    If you hold to the Great Way moment to moment, whatever you do will benefit the world. So there is no need to set up one thing opposite another. Spiritual practice doesn't have to exclude family; carrying for what's in front of you doesn't have to exclude elephants or any suffering being. So your mind should not be narrow; it should not be object-oriented. No one thing is enough.

    Just your own family is not enough. Only carrying for elephants is not enough. And only bikkhuni style is not enough. No form is enough. But if your mind is enough, then any style is enough, any form will do. There are even many stories of awakened women who would bring people to realization by sleeping with them. If your mind and heart are clear, whatever situation you are in can be a vehicle of Dharma.

    You are right that you need to be confident in your vows if you choose to be a bikkhuni. You don't need certainty, but you need strong commitment. The energy should only go forward; it should not look back in doubt and second-guessing. Even if a mistake is made, if you keep moving forward it becomes correct, and the mistake transforms to awakening.

    But bikkhuni mind is not about a good situation, and bikkhuni mind is not about whether you can do it or not. Bikkhuni mind means you offer yourself completely, whether you can do it or not. The sincerity of your offering is the great, seemingly magical power. There is nothing that monks and nuns have that other people lack. The fact that they repeatedly give up self-concern and repeatedly return to effort only for the benefit of others is what saves them. It is not something you can know you will be able to fulfill; so it's not about knowing, it's just about doing.

    This offering is a full and pure embodiment of the way-seeking mind. â€œMy life is for Buddha. My life is for all others." So please understand that this is not limited to bikkhuni lifestyle. Keeping your full and pure way-seeking mind is your human job, whether you become a nun or a mother or an animal worker or a soldier or a movie star.

    Rather than trying to decide the future through your thinking, let your life unfold moment to moment. Let your way-seeking mind find its way. It is expressed through walking and cooking, through hello and goodbye, through succeeding and through failing. Your Dharma includes everything, okay? If you trust that, it will unfold perfectly, as it has been.

    So how much do you trust the Way? You don't do it through your thinking and your trying-to-make-it-happen. The Way, and in particular your way, is already unfolding moment to moment.

    Together in Sangha.
