    There is nothing permanent, not even your true self.

    What teachings are you citing; where did the Buddha mention True Self?

    The Buddha didn't ask us to believe in something; He encouraged us to find out for ourselves.

    The important point for you is going to be moving beyond concept to actual experience. You have to make that trade, and actually experience what, if anything, is your 'self'. Ideas can only help in a shallow way -- they may help us communicate; but they can't help us attain what we're communicating about.

    So before speculating on whether there is or is not a true self, and whether it is or is not eternal, actual attainment is necessary. Before calling ourselves Buddhists or atheists or whatever, let's see what's really there. Who is the one reading this post? What is at the center of all your experience? It's not as easy as picking a flower or choosing an opinion; it takes some effort.

    Once you get what is at the center of your experience, you will be in a position to know what it means to say that it either exists or doesn't exist, is either momentary or timeless.

    The main quality of thought is division. Thought divides reality into supposed entities. This division tends to proliferate; as soon as you divide the world with thought, more and more divisions, more and more thoughts appear. New thoughts are sought out to support previous ones, because in the final analysis no thought -- no thing -- has substance of its own. So our illusion is supported by its own proliferation.

    For example, we hear about a 'True Self' and start to imagine what that may be like. Along the way, many assumptions are made about the nature of existence, substance, self, and our relationships to those ideas. We may imagine that 'True Self' is like 'I, my, me self', that there is some personal identification there. But all our personal identification comes in relationship to objects -- to those thoughts and the world of things that those thoughts create. True Self has no object; there is no personal identification there.

    Any idea whatsoever will not be it; because the nature of ideas is to form divisions, to create subject and object. When subject and object merge, that is what we call True Self.

    So, if this is not just intellectual indulgence and you actually want to know, better get to merging subject and object. Find a good method of putting down thinking, putting down ideas, and going to the center of your experience.
