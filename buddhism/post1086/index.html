    A mantra of compassion could be helpful. Three come to mind, originating from Asia but now of course practiced worldwide:

    1. Avalokitesvara/Chenresig is the Bodhisattva of Compassion. His mantra is very famous, and one of the most widely practiced (if not the most): Om Mani Padme Hum

    If you like, you can use Tibetan style pronunciation (Ohm Manee Pehmay Hoong). It is the same mantra. This generates compassion for all beings, of course including you. It brings blessings in response to all kinds of painful or suffering situations.

    1. Repeating the name of Avalokitesvara. This is a very popular practice in East Asia: Gwan Shi Yin Pusa (Chinese) or Kwan Se Um Bosal (Korean)

    2. The Great Compassion Dharani (Maha Karuna Dharani) is practiced in traditions originating throughout South and East Asia.

    • You can practice any of these continuously throughout the day.

    • You can also silently do from one to thirty strings (1 to 30 malas of 108 beads/repetitions each) of either of the shorter mantras daily as sitting meditation.

    • You can chant the Great Compassion Dharani once or several times daily to engage the voice and breath in training. Chanting soothes the emotions, expresses devotion, and the voice can aid concentration.

    • You can chant the Great Compassion Dharani (along with a wooden musical block, or drum, or rattle if you like) for an hour or more at a time, as special practice to change your karma quickly.

    You may also want to help yourself by looking into your mental and emotional patterns with a capable guide like a counselor or therapist. Meditation should not be considered a cure-all; there are many situations where other action is required. Trying to do it all yourself often leads to failure and further self-blame; the external perspective can be very helpful, and will often turn a situation around where mere dogged effort can't. The reason is that the blind, habitual mind one seeks to overturn is the same mind leading the process.
