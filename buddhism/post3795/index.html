    Could somebody explain some things I read in "The diamond cutter". (emptyness and karma)

    Even 'boss' and 'person' are concepts projected into reality, stories and labels that may be solely our own or may be shared by others, but stories and labels nonetheless.

    The idea 'unpleasant' comes from where? Shouting appears, certain sounds come from the mouth, certain postures are taken by the body. But 'unpleasant ' comes from within your own mind.

    When you look up into the night sky, maybe you see the Big Dipper, or Orion's belt. This is because someone showed you how to find those shapes, so now you find them automatically. Originally, the Big Dipper and Orion didn't exist. If you had looked up into the sky before being shown those shapes, they would not have appeared and said “I am Big Dipper" and “I am Orion".

    But since you have those shapes in your thinking, it's very difficult not to see them now. You point to the sky and say “there is the Big Dipper; and it is real, and you can't tell me it doesn't exist, because I'm looking right at it." But what you are seeing is your idea. You identify and name the shadow, and so you believe strongly that it has independent existence, apart from what you have made.

    What is up in the sky is just stars. You pick out a pattern. You name it. You insist on its existence. Therefore for you, the Big Dipper is real. But for someone who sees more clearly, the Big Dipper is known to be just an arbitrary pattern or collection. It's seen as a useful concept, but nothing with its own reality.

    The same stars in that region could be said to be a 'bear' (Ursa Major). So which is it, Ursa, or Dipper? Any number of patterns can be picked out and named. It's the picking out and naming that the existence is based on; originally, those things don't exist.

    Likewise there are constellations of events that we identify and label as 'nasty' or unpleasant' or 'forthcoming' or 'gracious'; but those are just patterns that we identify and label. The patterns don't originally exist -- they don't appear independent of our own concepts and judgments. But we (well, you more than I) believe in their existence, thus we (you more than I) live a life of illusion, unreality.
