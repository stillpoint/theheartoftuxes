    Can Magic Mushrooms make you more altruistic?

    Using magic mushrooms is using an outer method, an item outside of you own self and your own resources. Some experiences that you get by outer methods can be very beneficial; but outer methods can't change your destiny, only you can do that through your own intention and action. (Here I'm using the word "destiny" in a very colloquial way. What it really means is that you can't escape the results of what you do with your own mind moment to moment, day by day.)

    If you use magic mushrooms or any other object to shape your situation — getting a better feeling, achieving a different viewpoint, even sometimes getting more money or being admired by others — that change of situation will withdraw something from your karmic bank account. If you have a lot of good intent and action from the past stored in your account, you can get a very good situation. But your account is still being used up. Usually we are not aware of how much or how little we have in our karmic accounts, we only enjoy spending and getting a good situation.

    But if you don't contribute to your account with good thoughts and deeds, it will inevitably be used up and your situation will suddenly turn sour and painful. We see this all the time: "how the mighty have fallen!" Many times we see someone who seems to have it all — health, wealth, good looks, fame — and yet if we look we can also see the seeds of their self-destruction: relying on their good health, they may become weak-willed and reckless; reveling in their wealth, their greed may grow; wielding their good looks, they may neglect beauty of character; coasting on their fame, they may be lost when attention turns elsewhere. Without the will and ability to control one's own mind, the negative influences grow. And the more external support is sought, the less practiced one becomes at taking care of ones own mind; the ability atrophies. The 'good karma' account dwindles and the 'bad karma' account overtakes it.

    Cause and effect are clear. When the negative overtakes the positive, then you get a great deal of suffering as a result.

    So although magic mushrooms or other drugs or experiences may be an aid for some people, they are not the primary issue; and depending on your mind habit — how addicted you are to external support vs how self-reliant you are — powerful experiences can take you very quickly down the wrong road. Without developing a strong foundation of mind and the capacity to stay grounded in all situations, which are gained through hard work over time, experiences that are very powerful or opening can unbalance your life. It is often the case that quite sincere people who have powerful openings without a firmly established practice get torn apart by the difference between the possibility they have seen and the reality of their current petty habits and weak will. Having said that, no one can discount the value of a glimpse of boundlessness. Seeing through the illusion of a world of separate objects and beings is extremely precious, whether the experience is gained through meditation, through falling in love, or by taking drugs; and that experience can be like a guiding light that is never totally lost. It's just that attainment through external means most often (I'd say almost always) leaves one unprepared to deal with the enormity of the issue. And then you're left with either the hard road of doing the work anyway, or the suffering road of giving up and living with the loss and shame of that.

    Although we can't say if psilocybin is good or bad for you in your situation, we can say that whether you use it or not the best course of action, karmically, is to rely on your own resources — your own mind and effort — as soon as possible.

    And there is another very important, central point: our ultimate aim is not to build the good karmic bank account and clear the bad account. Good karma is still karma; it is still enmeshment in the endless up and down cycle of suffering the Buddhists call Samsara. Our ultimate aim is no karma. Liberation from good-and-bad. So don't seek your best situation; only seek your true mind, whether the situation is one that you like or one that you don't like.
