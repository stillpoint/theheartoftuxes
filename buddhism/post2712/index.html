    Questions from an open minded friend..

    If your actual perception is oceanic and infinite, without distinction, then perhaps karma would not be an issue. But your actual experience and way of seeing life is as an individual. As much as you may logically understand or emotionally aspire to a person-free view, your current (illusory) situation is that you are a separate person relating in specific ways and doing particular activities. This is what generates the personal cause-and-effect that is know as personal karma.

    A person's intention and action makes personal karma. A group's intention and action makes group karma.

    >If we are all the same body of energy, how could karma effect our next life?

    If this is only theoretical, it may become more difficult to understand. But if you look at the way things really unfold in your everyday life, it can come clear.

    If you realize that you can't escape your own mind in dreams, even though awareness of the body isn't there, it may be easier to understand that the mind records all of your intentions and actions without fail. All good and bad, regular and strange activity you go through, and especially the things your intent and actions make you believe about yourself, is recorded, and it bubbles up in your dreams in various ways â€” sometimes that very night and sometimes years later. Just as in dreaming sleep you still have a sense of self even though you don't have awareness of a body, the sense of self may continue even when connection to the body gets fully severed after death.

    This "same body of energy" that you talk about is what is posting in this subreddit as Chadgunz, and what reads these words, and what eats and drinks and has memories and feels happy or sad. All of those things happen by cause and effect (the process of karma).
