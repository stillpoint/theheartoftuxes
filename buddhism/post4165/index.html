    Release muscle tension meditation!

    There is no "best" way, just various ways that may bring forth various results for you in your situation.

    Release/relaxation is an important facet of almost all Yoga training, and some streams of Yoga focus on it. You might want to check out Yin Yoga or Yoga Therapeutic Ball Massage.

    There is a form of meditation that very skillfully uses the interface of tension to train presence. I believe I may have learned it from Zen and Yoga teacher Michael Stone; hopefully I can do it justice here:

    It is essentially a mindfulness practice, something you do not only on the meditation mat but throughout the day. The main activity is to soften and bring comfort to your relationship to pain and tension. You can intentionally soften and release the tension itself, going in the opposite way from tensing up; but the practice doesn't depend on things being a particular way. The focus is on bringing comfort to yourself from your own inner resources rather than manipulating anything in the body or outside.

    As you bring awareness to the body, you can feel the places that are comfortable. You may feel ease, or calmness, a pleasurable sensation, a pleasurable emotional sensation, or whatever pleasure or comfort appears as part of the transient landscape of the body. Bring attention to that comfort and enjoy it, settling in, allowing it to take center stage.

    There are a few benefits here. First, you engage with comfort, which helps you to relax and helps your energy to flow. Second, you disengage from trying to manipulate the situation, which contributes to the buildup of tension. Third, you are present with the body, so presence gets trained.

    When you get the hang of softening and sinking into comfort without having to change your outer situation, you can apply that to pain and tension. You can bring your attention to the painful spot and find your own increasing comfort with it, intentionally easing your relationship to the pain. It's as if you're camping in a thunderstorm: there's nothing to do but hunker down and relax. All kinds of noise and activity can be happening outside, but you intentionally let go and sink into the comfort of your sleeping bag. Thus the entire situation takes on a comfortable quality for you.

    It's also like a parent soothing an injured child. The care and ease of the parent actually goes into the child and helps them experience more ease, which supports the healing process.

    You have a say as to how you respond to pain and tension. Like the US Marines, you can always improve your situation. You may not be able to make things outside the way you want them, but you can increase your internal comfort with them. In terms of tension, this automatically helps the tension to self-release.

    Many people don't know what it means to relax or let go physically; therefore guidance and physical practice are helpful. One method is to tense the muscles and then let go, feeling the difference in sensation and the difference in internal relationship with the bodypart. One then goes farther in the direction of letting go. The letting-go intent is applied over the duration of a practice as opposed to being thought of as a one-shot deal.

    Breath is also very effective. The major part of Yoga posture practice is breath; the smaller part is the actual shape the body takes. Learning how to breathe deeply and smoothly will enhance energy flow as well as blood nourishment to tense and injured areas. This in itself can be therapeutic. In addition, energy follows mind, so bringing attention to an area brings energy there as well. Thus you can "breathe into" the jaw or other place of tension, visualizing it as a nourishing, soothing influence. The power of imagination also shouldn't be underestimated, although simple attention already has great potential effect.
