    What meditation can I do to be mindful while using the computer? And while using Reddit? And while chatting?

    Here is my detailed post on the topic of meditation and study, from another thread a few weeks ago. Much of it will speak to your question.

    I recommend a body-based awareness when working on computer. Either being aware of body posture (which is one of the two "mindfulnesses" deemed essential in the Foundations Of Mindfulness in Buddhism) or being aware of the physical foundation â€” the feet when standing or walking, the feet and seat when sitting, or the surface of the body resting on the bed when lying down.

    Other physical anchors are possible, but these two are simple, cover large enough areas to allow the mind to take in things more broadly, and help to ground your emotional and mental reactivity as well, which addresses the second part of your question.

    As to compassion and equanimity: it doesn't come from thinking. If you envision a way of acting that you then try to fit into, you won't be able to respond to the changing situation. Compassion and equanimity are tied together; they appear through your open-handed presence. That is to say, you already have a compassionate mind and heart as long as you don't cover them up with habit, conditioning, opinion, and thinking.

    Being present is the first point. If you are present, you have a chance to be free of reactivity. Your habitual reaction may come up, but if you are aware in the moment you don't have to follow it.

    Feeling and trusting your inner truth is the second point. Sometimes it is correct to say "you're right; I'll step aside". Even if the other person is only 1% right and you are 99% right, sometimes that's correct.

    But sometimes it's correct to set a boundary and say "I'm sorry, you can't treat me that way". Sometimes it hurts everyone if you let yourself be taken advantage of.

    Trolling, teasing, and unfocused anger usually don't need to be defended against. One can take the high road, so to speak, and not get dragged down into petty exchanges. But true injustice requires a response.

    Your own wisdom responds to each situation. There is a difference between the ego-based anger you feel when someone teases or insults you and the righteous anger you feel when an injustice is being done. The former can actually elicit feelings of pity for the person whose sense of worth depends on putting others down. The latter may require some kind of boundary to be setâ€¦ and held.

    Being present is the first key. If you are present, you have a chance of responding. Trusting your own wisdom and inner movement is the second key. At some level, you always know in the moment what the correct action is; it's a matter of learning how to trust that knowing and follow it up with action.

    Conscious dance practices like Authentic Movement and 5Rhythms can help you learn to trust your inner movement. Zen practice, and in particular the spontaneous exchange between master and student in koan interview, trains the same ability. Many expressive arts can develop the ability as well, but it depends on one's approach to the art.
