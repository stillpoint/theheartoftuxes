    What do you do when you feel lonely?

    I'm an extreme introvert by nature, and growing up I was exceptionally shy, even past my teenage years. I identify three things that helped me tremendously with both shyness and loneliness:

    First, good training in real-life situations. I have had the great fortune to study with true masters in day-to-day situations, first in practice groups, then in retreats, then in residential training. I couldn't begin to voice the different kinds of life lessons I've learned by applying practice in daily life. When the motivation behind your days' activities is clear, many, many problems drop away. Confidence grows; self-defeat diminishes. When you have frequent contact with living examples of sincere effort and enlightened activity, you can know where to aim. These lessons go into you, deeper than thought.

    Physical practice, and especially free dance have been tremendous for helping me understand, accept, and digest my emotional life. In free dance/movement practices you have the space to feel exactly what you feel, and to put that into movement. No need to hide. No need to manipulate. The opportunity to express the full range of human emotion — reasonable or unreasonable — is extremely precious. Free movement practice is a modality that would help many people to mature.

    We tend to enhance our thinking without growing in our relationship to ourselves and others. As a result we become twisted. An open floor and the permission to be whatever you sincerely are is tremendously precious. It is the same permission as you should have on a meditation cushion; but putting it into movement can unlock a whole level of liberation that would otherwise only be accessed in advanced levels of meditation. I've written on dance and movement practice in previous posts; you can search my posting history.

    Last, but certainly not least, practicing compassion has been one of the biggest healing factors. The mind turns from tortured self-concern to expansive, loving concern for others. The difference is like breaking through the surface of an ocean and being able to breathe again. Daily reconnection with the right compassionate motivation behind all one's activities is very important. It is important to start the day off knowing what the day is for! Frequent dedication of one's efforts and attainments, giving them over to the benefit of others and not merely hoarding things for oneself — this too is very important. And doing practices that help one get in touch with and express one's boundless heart — also very important.

    These three trainings in compassion — daily clear motivation; dedicating efforts and attainments to others; and doing compassion practice (for example, Metta, Karuna, and Tonglen practices) — help me not to suffer in loneliness. I still experience it sometimes, but it doesn't swallow me. And I can use my own suffering to connect to the greater suffering in the world. Then it's no longer 'all about me', and life has a greater flow.

    ~

    One more point; I might call it 'point 3-B': The more you throw yourself into situations, the more you can learn to trust yourself. The more you participate, the less anxiety there is. You can love what you love, and participate. Put the focus on doing rather than figuring out how or what to do. This can turn life around and bring flow.

    ~
