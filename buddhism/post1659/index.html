    A question about anagarika?

    First, you have mentioned a couple of times that people are inclined to follow your guidance or leadership even though you are not clear or strong enough to fulfill those roles. You must not pose as a leader or accept that kind of attachment from others! You must not! It is cruel and harmful to mislead sincere seekers, and it is very damaging to your own practice. Very bad karma that leads to further suffering for you down the road. Even in the present life and situation your own clarity can be further diminished, your self-respect eroded, and your natural spontaneous sincerity destroyed by trying to lead or teach when you are not qualified to.

    Don't do it, okay? Diffuse the whole situation by being up front with people, disclosing your weak points. Then you don't have to hold a secret, and the weak points can have the room they need for healing and growth. Later, when the time is right, you can help others in this way.

    I think you know this and were asking for some confirmation. If you need to hear it again I will say it again.

    I agree that mental illness is not well accepted or supported in broad society. Some people are trying to change that, incorporating a more holistic, integrated, and 'patient-centered' approach (though even the term 'patient' is problematic); but it can take a lot of digging and some luck to find these resources.

    I also agree that modern society has aspects that contribute to mental disease. That is because of what we make together with our minds. Thinking a lot about the problems of society is in itself an unbalanced and illness-promoting activity. Even if you are right in some respects, being right doesn't necessarily help you.

    By the same token, turning toward virtue and positivity is not necessarily the same thing as rejecting society or getting your ideal situation. Integral health includes offering acceptance and compassion to the dark or broken parts as well as the parts we naturally esteem.

    We should be establishing 'an ecology of love' rather than patterns of spite, resentment, or blame; because those patterns shape our life.

    Our world originates in our mind. If the mind is agitated, the world is agitated. If the mind is blissful peace, the world is a paradise.

    I always marvel at the different ways people experience the same day, the same moment. For a happy mind, sun or rain are both wonderful; birds singing and children playing are blessings. For a grumpy mind, sun is too bright or hot, or seems to be mocking one's mood, rain is a terrible burden, and birds and children are just troublesome noise. If your mind is clear and grounded, society is fine; everything is just truth.

    You seem to be a very intelligent, sensitive, and introspective person. That is so wonderful. It's extremely fortunate that you understand your need for structure and discipline, beyond just understanding teachings. If you are able to work now, regular work may also provide grounding and structure, helping you get out of your head, and helping you grow the resources through which more options can become available to you.

    If you make a bit of money, more of the training opportunities will become available to you. If you are 'done' with consumerism and have no need to acquire wealth, that's fantastic. Then you can get a job anyway, and be free even of your distaste for that milieu. Put down your tight opinions and grumpiness about society, and just work. That is already discipline. Any practice center or temple would require essentially the same practice.

    If mental health issues still keep you from working (and you should consult with a trusted third party to help determine this, not only relying on your own preference and self-diagnosis), then do follow up on Bucon's recommendations, contact some centers and teachers, and see what they have to say about practice opportunities.

    I can add that the Kwan Um School Of Zen has built a temple in the area in recent years -- maybe in the desert. Check with their head office.

    Good luck.
