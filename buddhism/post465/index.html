    >If I knew for a fact that this was the last hour of my life....

    There's a great story about this:

    >A high-level practitioner (a hermit yogi or monk) was doing intensive practice retreat in a cave by the ocean. He was armed with extensive knowledge about overcoming all kinds of obstacles that arise in practice, including how to deal with demons. For each type of demon that appeared, he knew the correct mantra to dispel it.

    >So as happens with intensive practice, many demons would arise during his retreat. Week by week, encounter by encounter, the hermit practitioner would use the right mantras and make the demons disappear.

    >One day, two very large and strong demons appeared. The hermit identified them and used the appropriate mantra to make them go away (or so he thought). But they didn't go away. "No problem," he thought "it must be this other mantra that dispels them." But each time he tried, the demons only approached closer and closer.

    >Soon the hermit could feel their demon breath blowing on him and their demon hands grasping him. He started to get desperate, searching his mind for every technique he knew, every teaching. As he was performing all his frantic recitations and visualizations, the demons put him into a bag, tied it up, and carried him out of the cave.

    >Finally the hermit came to the end of his knowledge. There was nothing else he could try. He heard the sound of the ocean getting closer and closer, and knew that he would soon be thrown off the cliff into the heaving water below.

    >"Oh, my life is over! And what was it all for? What did I accomplish? There's nothing I can do now to ward off death! And who am I, after all — what is this thing that will soon die?"

    >And only this open, answerless question remained: "What is it?"

    >After some time, the hermit realized that the sound of the ocean was no longer getting closer, and that he was no longer moving. He managed to loosen the rope closing the bag restricting him, and scrambled out of it. There were no demons to be seen. Only the cliff and the ocean below... its waves... its sound....

    >If we could see from a higher vantage, we could know this: Once the hermit came to the end of his knowledge and merged with the boundless question of life and death, he became too heavy for the demons to carry. They gave up and left him. The great question of life and death is beyond knowledge, before thinking, and too big for any demon. Thus the hermit found his true meditation.
