    Practices to address instant judgment

    One part is the 'instant' part. The knee-jerk reaction can be defused at the site of its attack through the practice of mindfulness. When your attention becomes honed you will be able to perceive the moment when the reaction appears, and you will then be able to let go of it in the moment. This requires ongoing practice, to bring the mind to momentary awareness â€” to the acuity with which you can see the thinking operate from moment to moment.

    The other part is why the reaction appears in the first place. This is your shadow: the disowned, ignored, shunned, denied part of your own being. At some point, you were taught or otherwise conditioned to look down upon a certain part of yourself, and to try to make it not exist. Your (usually unconscious) effort to do away with this part of your own mind and character causes you to be devoid of compassion when it appear in others. You project the idea of 'bad' onto others, but it is really your own mind that you are reacting to.

    So if you want to be free of the unconscious reaction, you need to own your shadow. You need to bring those parts of yourself now shoved into darkness back into the light of awareness and acceptance. This means that you have to graduate from immature spirituality that is based on an ideal image to a mature spirituality that sees with equanimity all the faults and flaws of your human nature, along with all the inconceivable glories. (Positive traits can also be thrust into the shadows. For example, some people have been taught that overflowing joy is inappropriate, or that love is weak.)

    I highly recommend Shadow Work and work on Spiritual Bypassing to all people serious about practice. It keeps the spiritual path from turning into a somewhat fundamentalist and illusory ideal, and opens the way for a very broad compassion that can embrace all kinds of people and all kinds of behaviors.
