    New,have some questions about karma, ect

    > 1. If reincarnation occurs, is there any way that we can have access to these prior lives.

    If we set aside the question of how you get from one life to the next, please let me know how you get from one day to the next, or from one moment to the next. What continues, if anything? What is lost, if anything?

    From this perspective, how do you access your prior life earlier today, or yesterday, or three years ago? It is the same. Some people are clear enough, have cleared away so much of the mental clutter and disturbance that we gather during our lives, that they can see back to before this birth; just as some people are clear enough to see back to their lives a decade ago while others may forget much more.

    But memory is not the only way to access your prior lives, and not even the best way. If you can let go of all grasping and the confusion that comes from it, you will have a moment of clarity. In this clear moment there is no time. Time comes from thinking, so if you have a clear moment all times become one, become clear. Within that clear moment you can function clearly, and everything that has come before will be in support of your action. Pouring a glass of water will not be at all separate from the evolution of the countless galaxies; they will both draw from the same source.

    > 2. If/can karma follow you through these lives how can you know how to learn from the past.

    If you stay aware, you can learn. If you lose awareness, you can't learn; then you will make mistakes that make problems, and these problems will eventually wake you up again.

    In spiritual practice, we use form to help us learn. The form is like a mirror: if you are meticulous about the form, very soon you will see your mind at work. "I like this... I don't like that... I want this... I don't want that...." And you will see the habitual, essentially boring ways that you always try to get what you like and avoid what you don't like. So the form helps you to see your karma unfolding. When you see it, then you can do something about it. If you don't see your karma, your karma controls you.

    But if you don't stay true to a form, and instead make compromises and excuses, then it will be hard for you to understand all the subtle tricks that your mind plays on you. You will believe everything your habit says to you. Therefore, the form of practice is sometimes uncomfortable on the outside, but very nourishing on the inside.

    > 3. I have heard that there are different spiritual planes can that be expanded.

    Sometimes you talk with someone in person; sometimes you talk with them on the phone, but you can still have a sense of them, a feeling of them. In the old days when people wrote letters to each other, you could have a true experience of a person's presence through a few words written on paper. And even further, sometimes you sit without even making words or movements, and something magnificent and mysterious is transmitted to you, through you. These can all be examples of different 'planes', different arenas of contact. And many more exist.

    We only see a very small part of life, just the surface. But so much happens below the surface. Some of our seeds of action are germinating, growing, unfolding every day without our knowledge. We only partly know it when they burst through the surface and something observable happens in our life; but the rest of the time there's this great, vast dance happening within and below.

    But as I said in response to your first question, if you have a clear moment, all of these planes become one — all planes are here. There is no good purpose to looking elsewhere. Your true life is right here. If it were somewhere else, it would not be your true life. So your spiritual effort should be in always returning here by opening your hands and putting everything down. Ungrasp everything, and you can find the clear moment within which everything is included.

    > 4. What is a quote that can be used in everyday life.

    What is the great purpose of your life? Do you try to get something; do you try to avoid something? Do you live only for yourself, or do you live for others? If you find your correct direction in life, you can make use of quotes. If you don't find your correct direction, quotes will cause confusion and lead to into murky suffering hell. Look around Reddit, and you can see many, many people heading to murky suffering hell, or already in it.

    You don't need a quote; you need to look into your life deeply. Then you can find your own wisdom; you won't have to rely on someone else's words, which is always a sketchy proposition.
