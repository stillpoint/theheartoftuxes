    What is this?

    Do you want to comprehend the feelings, or do you want to help the feelings move naturally, with grounding and context? They are different agendas.

    We habitually try to control things through our thinking. That is the ego's main tool for maintaining its status quo: the thinking. So take care when you start to engage emotions with a controlling, thought-based approach.

    The suffering of the world is immense, inconceivable, overwhelming. It is necessary, as someone who intends to actually ease and eventually overcome the world's suffering, to avoid getting stuck... to avoid getting overwhelmed to the point where you lose your breath, your motivation, your flow, your sanity.

    One side of this is about maintaining flow. A balance is necessary as you grow your capacity to face suffering. Don't take on too much darkness; protect your innocent nature and budding spirituality. Also, make use of supports like wise friends, teachers, practice groups, teachings, healing activities, nurturing environments, and appropriate self-care. Your ability to face the nasty truth must be cultivated in balance with your capacity. As your capacity grows, you can go further into it.

    But keep the emotions moving. Write, sing, share, create art, dance, keep a journal.... And get the body moving, don't get stuck anywhere. Then the movement of your life will digest this issue and turn it into compassion â€” your compassion. It is like your digestive system: it has to keep moving to work properly.

    The other side of this issue is purifying your perception. Because you still have the illusion of selfhood and a world of separately existing beings, you are rocked by suffering. It is illusory suffering, but the illusory being experiences it as reality.

    But when you learn about the nature of existence, and when you do good practice to purify your perception, you will begin to see the essential purity and goodness of everything. Even extreme darkness will be seen as essentially bright and awakened, and you will be grounded deeply in this essential goodness rather than the illusion of suffering. Then you will truly be able to help suffering beings: you will be able and willing to work with the illusion, but you will not be as disturbed by it (and eventually, not disturbed at all).

    So two sides: (1) let the emotions be what they are, but let them flow; and (2) transform your depression into motivation to practice, to purify your perception and realize the essential goodness of all existence.

    This is a very formulaic way of stating it; but if you meet a true teacher you can experience the profound liveliness and love within the practice. One Dharma friend expressed something very similar to a teacher: the immense and unending suffering in the world. The teacher didn't preach; he cried with her. Just cried.

    Yes, sometimes it's time to cry; and sometimes it's time to just do what needs to be done.
