    Without more investigation, no one here can say whether that sensation is a sign of a certain attainment or not. There is only speculation.

Almost all meditators can, after some regular practice, relate to tingling sensations or many other emotional or energetic phenomena that come by virtue of relaxing parts of the bodymind that have been previously bound up. It can happen in a moment... you can make it happen now, simply by relaxing and loving. Although it feels good, it does not necessarily have much to do with the goal of awakening, other than being a pleasant sign that you are letting go.

    There are also many energetic events that come when newly-released or newly-built energy meets the next bodymind blockages along the way. Some teachers call this "Qi effect", and warn that this is one of the most common stumbling blocks for students: what they think of as signs of bliss or attainment are in fact just buildups of energy. This can be understood better when you take to heart that correctly flowing energy has an empty feeling â€” it is not strongly stimulating or buzzing or emotionally strong, but rather neutral.

    Even when energetic events signify actual attainment of new meditative levels, those signs are for the teacher to interpret, not the practitioner. One of the best, most fortunate teachings I ever received (and more than one master has conveyed it to me) is not to concern myself with special energy or unusual events. Please embrace this deeply; it will save you much trouble, as it has done for me. A single entanglement with special energy can take an otherwise sincere practitioner down a side trail that lasts for years, if not lifetimes. Spare yourself that trouble.

    When the mind becomes concentrated, detaching from distraction, the energy previously tied up in preoccupation becomes available, and the doors of the unconscious mind begin to open. All sorts of old stories and new projections can be released. But this is just a different kind of thinking; and it is our job as meditators not to linger or toy with it. Keep your eye on the prize; keep your heart sincerely fixed only on awakening; keep your bodymind engaged only with the method of practice.

    Some teachers couch these phenomena in terms of the ego trying to regain control.
