    How do you fix the issue that tax money is going to fund war?

    1.Put it all down.

    Repeatedly let go of everything. Ten thousand times, a hundred billion times, drop all holding, making, wanting, and checking. This is practice. You have to do it.

    2.Stop talking.

    Put the stopper in the bottle. Talking only convinces you that you know something you don't. Make your putting-it-all-down practice the priority above the ego's talking.

    3.Cut down reading and other engagement with words. If you're serious about it, cut off reading completely for a period of practice.

    Reading also stimulates the ego's concept-driven agenda. It also convinces you that you know something you don't. It's like eating huge meals and not digesting them: the food doesn't nourish you, it just makes you bloated.

    Your practice should be ten times your talking and reading. For every hour of reading and talking you do, you should be doing ten hours of meditation, bowing, chanting, walking, or other spiritual practice. Get rid of the idea that reading spiritual materials makes you spiritual!

    4.Digest your knowledge through practice.

    You already know a lot! But your ideas far outstrip your embodiment. You need to create a proper home for those ideas by letting them go and focusing on active practice. With practice, your conception will become your own. Words will actually illuminate rather than agitate and confuse. You will begin to become a living example rather than a dead word-spout.

    5.Find a teacher and practice group, commit to periods of practice with them, and commit to helping your teacher and Sangha.

    No one is really qualified to guide their own spiritual development alone. Even the masters all credit their masters with helping them find the way. Those who achieve attainment seemingly alone may be famous, but they are extremely rare -- one in many billions of people -- and they are able to do it only because of their previous training over many lifetimes.

    Submitting to the guidance of a teacher is a step toward overcoming self-centeredness. Committing to practice periods with a structure and a group keeps you going when your own habit would cause you to fall off. The practice periods will have the same boundaries as above: no talking, no reading, put it all down, practice. And for good reason!

    Good luck! Will you do it?
