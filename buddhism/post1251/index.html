    What does it mean, if anything, to be a 'good person' in Buddhism?

    There is no set 'person', no one thing that you are.

    Your limbs and organs and bodily tissues and fluids are always moving. Your thoughts and emotions and relationships are always moving. There is nothing you can point to as the 'person' who would get the good label attached to it. To live is to be in movement. But because you attach to thinking you feel stuck. Put the emphasis on action and you can be free of your stuck thinking.

    Because people use the word good they get confused, and think that it refers to some kind of substance, like either you have the 'goodness' mojo or you don't. It's not like that. Don't attach to the word, then you can see that everything is in motion. It is all activity.

    The word 'good' is just short-form. It's a way of saying that certain activities have certain results, like relieving suffering, sharing joy, respecting life, offering support, and waking up from the dream of life. The activity is the important thing â€” actually doing what you already know very well is good to do.

    But your essence, your original non-moving mind behind all the things that move, is already pure goodness. It is already perfectly aware and compassionate. Your connection to this basic mind appears at first as vague intuition and conscience; but if you do proper spiritual practice it becomes more and more clear, more and more functional in your life.

    You already know what is good. Like Bob Dylan wrote, "people don't do what they believe in, they just do what fits in, then they repent". 'Good' means you actually do it; you do the hard thing that you already know is the right thing to do.

    So with the emphasis on doing, that means that your relief will be found in practice. Thinking about it without doing is depressing. You get sick of yourself, sick of your lazy, ineffective mind. But taking action is relief.

    Find a teacher and a practice center, and do hard training. Learn how to sit in meditation, how to work mindfully, and how to carry out compassion in everday life. Or if you can't even do that much, just get moving in your life. One moment of complete activity is freedom from the prison of expectation.
