    What suttas or talks could help me face a painful surgery I'll be having on Monday?

    Are you going to be able to use the words to make the experience better? The crux of the issue is what you put into practice.

    Practice is non-avoidance. One thing that can help is to have a curiosity about your fear and self-cherishing. Look closely at how the fear arises, and what feeds it. Aware that self-cherishing is the source of suffering, distinguish between the plain fact of momentary pain, on one hand, and the patterns of thought and reaction that form suffering, on the other.

    Initiation ceremonies around the world involve pain. From the scars cut into the young men of aboriginal tribes in Africa and Pacific islands during rites of passage, to the incense burns made on the head or arms of novitiate monks in Buddhist ordination ceremonies, the pain and its primal, visceral connection to the wound of being human is honored. In the moment of sharp pain, the mind is clear; thinking is cut or burned away, and you are intensely present. There is only 'Oww!' It is a moment of reality, and it connects you to the pain of the world. Thus it serves as a marker, a gate that you pass as you proceed to greater awareness and responsibility.

    We can use painful situations to purify our karma. It is not that pain is good or purifying in itself, but we can use it to wake up, and to ignite compassion. You wake up by being present with as pain â€” just the clear experience in the moment, with nothing added and nothing subtracted. You ignite compassion with the awareness that: >*whatever pain I am experiencing, there are countless, myriad others experiencing much more. And whatever pain I am experiencing, it is one expression of the pain all beings live with and the suffering of their unclear relationship to it. To live is to experience pain, imperfection, sickness, loss, aging, and death. So may this pain remind me to my core of the great need of all beings, to awaken and be free of the ocean of suffering. May I ease the pain and suffering of all beings, just as I wish to have my pain and suffering eased."

    Connecting your pain to the pain of all beings helps you and it helps all beings. It helps you by getting you out of your self-centered circle of woe, and it helps others by sparking your radiant compassion. Even the first little warm wish for the well-being of others spreads infinitely into the world.

    When facing the threat of pain, realize that your reality is only the present moment. The threat is not here yet, and you create more stress and tension for yourself if you let your mind wander to the future and to your unreal dreams of what might happen. This stress and tension actually do increase your perceived pain! But if you stay present, you never have to bear anything unbearable. The moment is always bearable, because it has no duration. So if you have any kind of problem, limit your involvement to a smaller and smaller duration, until you are only involved in a moment that has no length.

    There is also a style of practice one master called 'Diamond Pain Samadhi', which means becoming one with pain. You let go of all avoidance, and the pain actually contributes energy to your concentration. With complete release, the concentration becomes effortless, and an unspeakably profound silence-bliss appears. It is as if you find the eye of the hurricane, within which you can float effortlessly.

    But who knows if any of this will help when the shit hits the fan. Whether it's the upcoming operation or one of the many other physically or emotionally painful things you will face in your life, what will determine how you face it? Only what you put into practice deeply and repeatedly. Just thinking of a mental formula can't help you much.

    In the final analysis, there is really no strategy. There's only life that keeps moving. So keep moving.
