    TheHeartofTuxes

    Try a lot of stuff. Get active. Risk disappointment and embarrassment for the sake of clarity, engagement, self-expression, movement, and fun.

    Self-expression is important. It's especially important for young people to learn how to express themselves in many ways. The education system in most countries glosses over this important area. For example, art, writing, and music are considered separate fields, closed to others; and business, science, and sports are engaged for their results rather than as paths of self-expression. Really, anything you do can express your true heart, your true nature. But it's important to do that rather than shrink from life or make it into a dry, heady sort of planned enterprise.

    When you get moving, what is important to you will start to emerge. Your purpose is already moving in you. How could it be otherwise? You are not separate from who you are. Who you are is the most real aspect of you. But it gets covered with analysis, second-guessing, guilt and shame, emotional wounding, familial or cultural programming, trying to please others, and also by simple stagnation and atrophy through not being expressed.

    Moving the body will get you more in the flow, more in tune with what is moving at other levels. Some non-structured movement should be part of every day. Just doing repetitive exercise does get parts of you moving, but it's not complete; it doesn't offer the chance for new things to appear. Dance, play, roving and wandering, and intuitive or free-form rolling and stretching allow the bodymind to contact, investigate, and express what's moving in you from day to day.

    Other forms of expression that help get you in touch with purpose include visual art, music, creative writing, poetry, journalling, dialog therapy, acting modalities like playback theater, or creative living space arrangement (like perhaps creating altars). Let's disabuse ourselves of the notion that art is just for certain 'special' people. It is the birthright of everyone to be able to express themselves without inhibition. And when you open that channel, what is really there within you will come pouring out, clear as day. It may (it will) sometimes change; but then you can change with it and still be true to yourself rather than trying to be something you're not.

    Try a lot of stuff. Get active. Adventure. If you try something and it sucks, you'll have a lot more information about yourself and the world than if you had stayed at home in self-protection mode.

How is sometimes a helpful thing to consider.
