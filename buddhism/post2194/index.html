    Sometimes people feel threatened by teaching forms — by commitments and guidelines of conduct and forms of practice. It means they don't fully see the benefit and need for Dharma; the ego is still holding out for its piece of the pie (actually, it wants the whole pie). The part that feels threatened by teachings that point to right conduct is ego. There are many, many guidelines in Buddhism that teach us correct function, correct relationship. From one perspective, all the teachings show us how to behave, how to come to our own embodiment of compassion. It's not about me forcing you or you forcing me; but it is very much about us learning and undertaking correct activity.

    Since you ask, here are a few examples from the vast treasury of Dharma teaching correct action and relationship:

    >The Noble Eightfold Path
    >Right Understanding
    Right Intent
    Right Speech
    Right Action
    Right Livelihood
    Right Effort
    Right Mindfulness
    Right Concentration

    >If one harms living beings
    He cannot be considered noble.
    Only by exercising harmlessness toward living beings
    Can one be called noble. > >Neither learning nor embracing solemn vows,
    Nor achieving a great state of concentration,
    Nor living alone will assure nirvana.
    Nirvana is only achieved by right action.
    – Dhammapada

    ~

    >We put the Dharma of Liberation in the center of the mandala; surrounding it are the other key elements of the mandala: retreats, with a year-round schedule; study… and right relationship, cultivating wise relationship with each other, with the earth, and with other beings, following all the steps of the eightfold path.
    – Jack Kornfield, Bringing Home The Dharma: Awakening Right Where You Are

    ~

    >Once again, the purpose of Buddhism is to attain truth, attain prajna (wisdom); that means attain correct way, correct life. Then you can save all beings. This is human beings’ correct job. Attain your true self means attain universal substance; attain universal substance means attain whole-world situation; attain whole-world situation means attain your correct job. How? It means, moment to moment, keep your correct situation, correct function, correct relationship. That means, moment to moment, how do you help others? Not only human beings, but this whole world. When enlightenment and correct life come together, that means your life becomes truth, the suffering world becomes paradise. Then you can change this suffering world into paradise for others. This is human beings’ correct job; this is the purpose of Buddhism.
    – Zen Master Seung Sahn, The Purposes Of Buddhism

    ~

    >We can address every being we meet — not only human beings but also animals — as essentially Buddha. We talk with Buddha, eat with Buddha, drink with Buddha, go to sleep with Buddha, and wake up with Buddha. In Western terms, we speak of being in the presence of God or meeting God all the time. > >In the phenomenal world, we make a shift from self-centeredness, where we are constantly defending ourselves, to realizing there is nothing but Buddha or mind.
    – Prabhasa Dharma, About Relationship: Being The Buddha, Being Love

    ~

    >We can’t exist by ourselves alone. We can only inter-be. I am made only of non-me elements, such as the Earth, the sun, parents, and ancestors. In a relationship, if you can see the nature of interbeing between you and the other person, you can see that his suffering is your own suffering, and your happiness is his own happiness.
    – Thich Nhat Hanh, How To Love
