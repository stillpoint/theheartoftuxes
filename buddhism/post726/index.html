    5 Ways to Help victims of the Nepal Earthquake (opportunity for generosity!)

    >I just can't see prayer/meditation as actually helping....

    Many, many enlightened masters disagree. These masters of course also encourage all other kinds of help, including monetary donations; but they are very clear on the point that prayer can be of benefit.

    If we're very attached to our old notion of who or what we are, then of course we may mistrust prayer. Believing that a person is a separate clod of flesh in a world of separate objects, there is little reason to believe in the connectivity of beings. Meditation can awaken you to a direct experience of your own mind as the substance of the world — not in a theoretical or deductive way, but directly. Seeing your own nature and the nature of the world, you can know that what you make with your mind influences the whole field, and that you can direct this influence.

    It's like seeing your dream. Before you know that you're dreaming, your dream character is subject to all the tumultuous forces within the dream, and doesn't understand its true connection to the other dream characters and events. But once awareness appears, you see that all the events and beings in the dream come from your mind; you realize that you have influence on the dream: the whole dream universe is mind-matter. Likewise, before you understand the nature of your life you are tossed about by the forces of the world, thinking of yourself as separate; but upon waking up you can not only influence your own unfolding but you can benefit others in theirs.

    ~

    As Tenzin Wangyal Rinpoche says in The Tibetan Yogas Of Dream And Sleep:

    >Normally the dream is thought to be "unreal," as opposed to "real" waking life. But there is nothing more real than dream. This statement only makes sense once it is understood that normal waking life is as unreal as dream, and in exactly the same way.

    ~

    This explanation on the influence of prayer is from a talk by Her Eminence Khandro Rinpoche:

    >During my years of trying to explain the power of sound, prayers, and aspirations, there were always people who did not understand—no matter how I explained. So, although it is neither nice nor polite to say, it seems not to work. When everything else fails, I say it in the following way: I ask if they recognize the power of gossip, the power of rumors. This gives you the idea of what words, prayers, or sound can actually do. Energy or subtle energy—energy in the sense of something that is not form–oriented, but is rather an expressive quality that we can call the expressive energy or the power of subtle energy—is something that needs to be recognized and understood. Of course, there is positive and negative energy. Since we see and understand negative energy such as gossip or rumor easily, we can also understand what they can create. Negative energy creates a reverberation in the space that is felt by everyone, although there might be no substantial evidence of its existence. If you reverse that into the positive aspect, such as collective praying or collective aspirations, a tremendously powerful positive energy develops. From the perspective of Buddhist teachings, planting a seed of karma that has the powerful ability to create good consequences is easy for Buddhists to understand. So, reversing the course of karma, especially the collective karma, through individual effort together with the collective effort of the community, has the power of reversing collective conditions.

    ~
