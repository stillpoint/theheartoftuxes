    Yes, this is all your overactive thinking mind intervening. Meditation means no intervening. Put it all down. When an issue arises, don't touch. When an intriguing question comes up, don't follow it. When doubt appears, don't give it any attention. These are all just different forms of thought. Put it all down. Then the world can speak for itself. The sky is blue. Water is wet. When you are cold, you put on a sweater. When your friend is in trouble, help them. Very simple. It didn't come from thinking, but from letting go of thinking, and just showing up moment to moment.Truth doesn't require your thinking and figuring; just let it speak for itself, without trying to interpret.

    So you need to choose one proven, traditional practice, and just do it. Try it for some weeks, then settle into it for some months, then apply yourself to it for some years. No need to understand everything with your thinking. No need to figure out an easier way. No need to feel any particular way or get any particular experience. Only do it. Do the 'ashes work' of just performing the meditation practice every time, whether your energy is up or down, whether you feel inspired or draggy. Only do it, and sitting by sitting, moment by moment, let go of your grasping mind.

    The mind that clutches at understanding creates confusion. This is a very important point for newcomers to be aware of. Usually we think it's the other way around; and usually our education says we should pursue every concept and question. But in meditation you find that the grasping mind creates confusion. Letting go of thoughts and concepts allows your original wisdom to function.

    >Put it all down, and truth appears by itself.

    If you have a particularly overactive thinking mind, there are remedies for that, methods of practice that work well with it. If you have a particularly active questioning mind, there are practices that use questioning too. But instead of letting each new question proliferate and less to mute and more thinking, you use one big question to cut through all thinking.

    (If your mind is chronically overactive, it may indicate some mental or emotional condition that requires treatment before or in addition to meditation.)

    As soon as possible, find a capable teacher. Your running mind is not capable of inventing or leading you through practice. It only creates more illusion. It is ego to think that you can figure it all out. You need the assistance of a strong, clear leader to guide you.

    Until you find a teacher, you can try a traditional form of meditation. In this case, the clear form (the step-by-step instructions and method) will help you, will be your guide and mirror. As soon as you deviate from the form you will know that your thinking mind is trying to intervene.

    They important thing is to attain a moment. A true moment, a simple moment. Put it all down, and attain a moment. All the thinking in the world can't touch that one point.

    Proliferation of thinking takes you further away from natural life. You supplant natural and harmonious activity with fabricated ideas. It becomes more awkward, requires more distance from people and situations, and leaves you more tired and at odds with yourself. Put down the thinking, and just follow each situation naturally.

    No need to figure out the universe. The universe has already figured itself out, and is moving, changing moment to moment on its own. Let go of 'my idea', 'my understanding', and 'my way'. Just follow each situation with a simple, sincere mind. If you do this completely, before you even realize it you will have come into harmony with the universe, and the universe's understanding will be your own. It just doesn't happen through grasping-thinking.

    I hope you apply yourself to a clear, traditional method of meditation, learn to let go of your thinking, and attain a true moment.
