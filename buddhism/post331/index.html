    How can I better prevent negative thoughts?

    You're talking about psychological dynamics. Sometimes these kinds of self-harming behaviors are done as a sort of deal with oneself, a trade made so that you can continue indulging in bad action. Subconsciously, the idea is held that it is okay to continue the bad action because you're paying for it with bad feeling and self-blame. The cure, clearly, is to become aware of the inner deals and to stop doing the bad action (stop being insincere about it).

    In the short term, you don't prevent negative thoughts, but you can free yourself of attachment to them. Even in a single moment, you can let go of clinging. That is the practice: just dealing with the present moment. You limit your attention and activity to just what's real, which is the present moment, and you let go of engagement with imagined futures.

    With detachment you can understand all thoughts as having the same illusory nature: whether they are positive or negative or neutral, they are all dream material. Then the negativity no longer has the power to control your life.

    Settling the mind through various practices like physical practice or concentration/Å›amatha style meditations will calm the thoughts in general, including negative ones.

    Doing positive practice like mantra (positive speech), visualization (positive mind or vision), the Brahmaviharas (lovingkindness, compassion, equanimity, and mutual joy), recitation (positive speech and intent), or devotional practice (positive emotion and intent) will leave little opportunity for negative thoughts to enter, and will replace lingering negativity with the positive.

    In fact, any practice done constantly throughout the day will be a protection against negative thoughts, as long as you wield the skill of letting go of whatever arises.

    Anger, pride, jealousy, attachment, and ignorance -- known as the five poisons -- are the major categories of negative thoughts and actions. Here is a talk that outlines the Theravada, Mahayana, and Vajrayana approaches to dealing with negativity.

    But an important aspect of spiritual practice that is so often overlooked on these forums is your everyday activity. All the meditation in the world can't compensate for bad action done in the rest of your life. The results of karma will certainly arise. So if we're talking about actually eliminating negative thoughts, it is necessary to purify your actions. If you act out of compassion toward all others, the seeds of negativity will not be planted, and so you will not reap negativity; and the seeds of positivity will be planted, so you will reap positivity.

    Therefore the six perfect qualities of generosity, patience, discipline, vigor in practice, meditation, and wisdom must be cultivated in everyday life if you seek to eliminate negativity. Cause and effect are very clear. For some time you will have to deal with the results of old seeds of negativity that you've planted as they gradually ripen and manifest; and you can deal with them by practicing non-attachment, non-reactivity. But if you don't create new negative seeds, gradually the old seeds will be expended and you will enjoy greater and greater clarity and freedom.
