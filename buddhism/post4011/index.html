    What do you tell yourself during points in life where you find yourself on the verge of being stressed/out of sorts?

    >I have moments where I do very well managing the stress, but I have other times where my mind seems to be running a mile a minute with various financial situations.

    It may be helpful to reflect on this: If we fail to look deeply, our stress tends to feel as if we are doing something about the situation. To be neutral and open-handed can seem uncomfortably, almost unbearably, disengaged. We habitually move to fill in the openness, so we add our own drama to the situation.

    Our worry and stress then seem like we're at least doing something about it. But this is misplaced. If we look more deeply, we can see that the worry and stress don't add anything useful; they just fill in the spaciousness, the uncertainty.

    We would often rather be sick with worry than experience openness. At a primal level, this has to do with maintaining our illusion of selfhood. Instead of letting go and letting be we choose anxiety and pain, so that we can experience something certain. (So that we can experience a self that feels and does something certain.)

    But a balanced, openhanded mind is not only possible in the face of difficulty, it is more effective than worry and stress.

    So far, the world has kept you alive. It has provided you with countless talents and resources. Focus on using those talents and resources to advance your position. Take practical steps to improve your situation. But realize that your worry will not bring you a millimeter closer to security.

    Meditation by itself may not do anything to change your financial situation. Your situation arose from particular causes; and those causes need to be adjusted in order for your situation to change. You have to do something.

    What meditation can help with is in developing the ability to let go of unreality. In meditation, you practice returning to the present moment, repeatedly letting go of any distraction. The more you practice this, the better equipped you are to free yourself of those trains of thought that take you toward frightful imagined scenarios and churning worry. You develop the ability to let go and return to the present moment, like developing a letting-go muscle.

    Returning to the present moment, you can see the situation in front of you, and you can take steps to improve it.

    Furthermore, you can't fix it all in one fell swoop. This situation developed over a long period of time, as a series of causes brought a series of effects. You can't overturn that evolutionary process, you can only refrain from bad causes and establish good causes in the moment, thus beginning and gradually cultivating a new evolutionary process. You can't do the whole thing at once; but step by step you can change the way things unfold.

    It all begins with letting go and letting be. You must return to the moment first before you can do effective action. Worry is in the unreal, imagined future; self-blame is in the unreal, dead past. Your actual life, and the place where you can exert your effort effectively, is right here right now, undistracted by worry or blame.

    You have to apply letting-go and returning to the moment repeatedly, countless times. Again, again, again, let go of the unreal imagined scenario and return to this situation in front of your face. Ten thousand times; ten thousand years; ten thousand lifetimes: let go, and return to the moment. Then, just do the next necessary thing.

    Good luck! (To all of us.)
