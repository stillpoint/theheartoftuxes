    TheHeartofTuxes

    "Call it whatever the hell you want" is not so wrong, at least partly. The label is true because you say it is true, no other reason. Originally, nothing had a label.

    So we make our own worlds out of our own labels. But when we put down all labels, the true world speaks for itself. Robin sings robin song. Human speaks human words. The sky is blue. Water is wet. The true world speaks for itself.

    We get confused because we attach to labels and believe in them rather than hearing the true world speak. If you are confused, it's because you attached to the words.

    That is the function of questions like this: to highlight attachment. Then you can know "Ah, I'm all caught up in words. Words can't make this correct." And you can let go and return to clarity.

    But if a person doesn't know that they should let go and return to clarity, they may try to solve the question by using words. So they get more confused and maybe hate Zen-style speech. The speech has a very wonderful clarifying function, but not everyone takes it up.

    It's like failing to use a sponge and soap when you need to clean something: you look at the sponge and soap, and yell "Well, what good were you when I was over there trying to clean the house?"

    There are also those who only like the undefined part of the speech, not the clarifying part. They are very attached to words, and communicate in vague and haughty ways to mimic teachers who can actually use words this way. It's sad that these people get stuck pretending like children rather than getting to true world.
