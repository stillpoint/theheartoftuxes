    Mantra(s) for compassion?

    The kind of discursive curiosity that always seeks another object to handle goes contrary to compassion. Compassion "has enough".

    There is a good function of curiosity and a bad function of curiosity. The good function is engagement with life. Rather than writing things off as "known", we look into them deeply. It's easy to think that we know the world simply because we have collected a lot of names and descriptions of things; but curiosity brings us to investigate, to look into the mystery.

    The bad function of curiosity is distraction. This is not really curiosity but monkey-mind. Even when it has a fruit in its hand, monkey mind rushes out for another. It seeks distraction; it always tries to "trade up" and get more power and control; it has difficulty trusting things-as-they-are.

    Know the difference between engaging curiosity or fascination or awe on the one hand, and distracted, dissatisfied monkey-mind on the other hand.

    The important part of doing mantra work for compassion is the heart behind the mantra. You don't "get" compassion. You let your own innate, original, limitless heart of compassion shine.

    Trying to "get" would be a denial of your original faculties. Instead, we start with the faith that we are already complete. Calling on your heart of compassion, you bring it forward, make it more of a living reality.

    So in mantra practice for compassion, the practitioner offers all merit, all benefit, to others. "May any merit that comes through this practice pass to others for their awakening and the easing of their suffering." Intend it and feel it and be sincere with it, and compassion flows.

    If you fulfill that part, any mantra will work. Even "pizza pizza" will work if you have a sincere heart behind it. The different formulations of mantras may have some influence in connection with specific practices that would be offered by a master in conjunction with an initiation and assigned method, but the ones noted by others in this thread all do well for the practice of compassion:

    • Om mani padme hum
    • Kwan Se Um Bosal
    • Om Ah Hum Guru Vajra Padma Siddhi Hum (same as mantra recommended by Vistril in this thread)
    • May you be well, may you be happy, may you be free of suffering

    In repeating the mantra, the mind becomes more intimate with it and eventually merges with the mantra. There is no longer a sense of a mantra and someone doing it, there is only om mani padme hum om mani padme hum om mani padme humâ€¦ (or whatever mantra is being practiced). The mantra becomes the moment, the moment becomes the mantra. The heart's mood becomes the mantra, events and surroundings become the mantra. This is the "prayer that becomes one with God", as Christians may say.

    Compassion means your heart and the world are not separate. There's nothing to get or make. Only merge with practice and reveal the mind-and-heart that is not separate.

    So the Commentary On The Vajra Guru Mantra says >If one recites the mantra three or seven million times, one is never separate from the Buddhas of the three times and one becomes inseparable from me [the master or guru].

    This is already true: one has never been separate from the Buddhas of all times and places; one just reveals that by merging with practice.
