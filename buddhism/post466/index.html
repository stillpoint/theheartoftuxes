    Siddhis: good

    Attachment to siddhis: bad

    Attaining siddhi through pure practice: good

    Seeking siddhi as the central aim: not pure practice: bad

    But perhaps the initial question is what siddhi actually refers to. Thinking of siddhi as an object, a 'something' that can be attained is a bit of a low-class view. Practice and attainment are one. For example, when a flower opens, its openness is the siddhi of openness, but there is no object or power separate from the flower, and there is no flower separate from the process of opening.

    So siddhi is very grounded in the actuality or actualization of practice. But since people habitually make illusory objects and attach to them, the notion of siddhi gets dumbed-down to mean some kind of separate item or faculty.

    Within the context of boundless compassion, the pursuit of siddhi is actually called for; it becomes a vow of benefit. But again, due to the habitual and narrow-minded activity of common people, the cart is often put before the horse, and the pursuit of siddhis becomes a cynical secret agenda. I would think that if you are in a lineage wherein siddhis are a significant aim, the preliminary training of that lineage would do everything possible to assure that your compassionate view and motivation are in place, sincere and vital and growing, well before 'powers' become an issue.

    Ultimately, all manifestations are empty projections of mind; and in practice, all manifestations are directed toward complete unexcelled enlightenment for all beings. So the real meaning of siddhi is practice-and-attainment of complete enlightenment, nothing more or less. "The ten thousand things return to One."
