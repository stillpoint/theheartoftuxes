    Can someone help me understand this aspect of Buddhism?

    What is it that you name "love"? Please define that.

    What is it that you name "relationship?"

    You are always in relationship. The question is what kind of relationship. Is it based on greed, desire, lust, loneliness, emptiness, generosity, joy...? Is it mutual or one-sided? Are both parties working toward the same goal, or does each one hold private agendas that are selfish or unconscious? Is your effort based on getting something for yourself or offering something to another; and if it's a combination, what is the balance?

    Are you aware in relationship, or are you asleep?

    Is the relationship appropriate in the time, place, situation, intent, and people involved? What is being lost, what is being gained; what is being held onto, what is being given up?

    Buddhism is clarity of relationship. Not a single thing exists alone; everything is formed by everything else. The great master Maha Ghosananda says that everything is "eating", around and around in a cycle. This is the same point: we all consume each other; we are all made of each other.

    There is no life or love or Buddhism without relationship. Buddhism aims at the flowering of relationship, where one's intimacy with everything is unblocked. Clear and luminous.

    From an absolute point of view, one may say that it is necessary to be without relationship, in the sense that one must uproot the illusion of separation: there are no objects, no beings outside of your mind, and no "you" that relates to them. This realization is necessary. But you don't stay there. You return to relationship — even if it is illusory. You return to it out of compassion for those who are stuck in the illusory world, lonely and suffering. You shine your light to others.

    Never mind whether it is right or wrong for a monk to have had romantic or sexual relationships. What do you need for your completion? Where do you have to go and what do you need to try in order to move freely? Ultimately, you need to be able to accept all of life; there must be no grasping and no rejecting any part of life.

    It often turns out that people who try to be something too early have to go back and finish parts of their life education later, at which time the natural order of things may be somewhat mixed up. For example, people who were not allowed to play as children grow up with wounds that persist; and those wounds are not healed until they — as adults — learn and allow themselves to play. In essence, they have to complete their childhood decades later.

    I would encourage you to look into the profound issue of self-acceptance on all fronts, as opposed to trying to fit into a certain form (trying to be a monk or a "good person", for instance). Do you have faith in your original goodness — as-is? Can you be comfortable with the complete range of your human thoughts and emotions? You don't have to act out every last darkness of the human soul, but you do need to look at it, approach it, and embrace it as a reality. Without that, there is no compassion; you will not have love to offer others if you can't love your own incomplete, flawed, unpredictable, changeable, overwhelming, mysterious, ungraspable life.
