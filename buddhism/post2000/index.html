    TheHeartofTuxes

    We don't know how things will turn out. Only be sincere; don't attach to results. That's the best I can offer. Your desired result may be unselfish; but requiring it and imposing it on others or abandoning others because of it would still be selfish. Ego attaches to ideas; showing up with compassion is open-handed.

    So it is like one Zen Master said when asked whether it is worthwhile to hang on to lovely and useful possessions, or to get rid of them as well: "It would be better to have it as if you didn't have it."

    It would be better to have your goal as if you didn't have it. It is not bad to have a plan; but realize that it's a work in progress, subject to change as you gather new information, and possible to let go of â€” lest you lose yourself in chasing it. Don't let your goal possess you. That means, moment to moment don't make the idea "my goal". Then you can devote yourself to what you find worthy.

    When Jesus was in the Garden of Gethsemane, and saw his fate laid out before him, he prayed "Father, if it be Your will, take this cup from my lips [please don't make me go through this]. But nevertheless, let Your will, not mine, be done." Let the the Buddha-mind's 'idea' unfold, not your own. Sometimes it unfolds as your idea, or the idea that appears for you gets involved in the great unfolding of things; but not out of attachment, not out of forcing an opinion or keeping a narrow and exclusive vision. We can have our intent and our plan; but the way we go about it ought to be open-handed, open-hearted, open-minded, and soft-elbowed (like when you're in a busy marketplace, instead of holding your elbows out to jostle people away, you soften them and find mutual flow). Whatever we do is not just about us. Whatever you choose is not just about you and your vision; because if it is, you become like Saddam Hussein versus George Bush, or Palestinian state versus Israeli state, each side trying to impose its own idea.
