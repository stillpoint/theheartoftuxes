    Do you see yourself as a Buddhist?

    What is the point of it all?

    When Jesus was challenged about his acts of healing on the Sabbath, he replied that the Sabbath was made for man, not man for the Sabbath. Religion is there to help us wake up and act with compassion and wisdom; if it gets in the way of that, brush it aside.

    The aim of Buddha's Dharma — the teaching about the truth — is to help you wake up to that Dharma, that truth, and save yourself and others from suffering. All the teachings and techniques and institutions and other trappings aim at that point. It is very simple.

    Although Buddhism has a distinct quality of practical investigation (ehipassiko, or "come see for yourself"), there is a point that people often forget. "Whatever works for you" is only one side of it. The other side is that we're starting from a position of ignorance, and if we have access to great wisdom that has been tested and proven, and that we have found so far to be reliable, we should offer up our own preconceptions and doubts in order to embrace that wisdom.

    Change is not easy. The ego hates change; the ego wants to think of itself as the all-powerful expert on how things should be. So part of the challenge in accepting wisdom is the challenge of admitting and feeling our need for it, our lack and our suffering. It's that old story played out in Bruce Lee's Enter The Dragon where he keeps pouring tea into his student's cup as it overflows: we have to empty the old if we are to receive the new. Egos don't like to empty; so it takes some rationality and some courage to start embracing Buddhist teaching or whatever wisdom path we encounter.

    It won't go deep if nothing in you gets cut down.

    So while it's right to check, at first, to see if a way is going in the right direction, take care not to check too much. Take care not to put your own hesitation and short-sightedness in front of a greater wisdom coming your way.

    In most cases, you don't have to accept much at all. There may be a few matters of form to follow if you join a group; but that's just because some kind of form has to be followed. You don't have to believe much, you don't have to sign up to name yourself something or play some role. Usually, you're just expected to look sincerely into the matter of your life.

    If you join a group or sit with a teacher, your listening should just be open. Not holding belief, and not rejecting anything. Like a boulder in a stream, you listen and the teachings just flow past. You don't have to analyze, you don't have to decide what to cling to and what to push away. As you practice, the teachings take root where they need to. As your experience deepens, the teachings will reflect it. And then they may invite you to take the next step… and the next one….

    In your post you are worrying about the future; so at that time your mind is out of balance and can't find its way. Never mind. You'll know where to turn if you take care of your current situation.

    Also never mind what other people think or do. My life is not your life; my conditions and reasons are not your conditions and reasons. What we share is our deep direction — our inside job — to wake up. The waking-up path can take a million different forms, but it appears in the moment. Just this moment, where are you?
