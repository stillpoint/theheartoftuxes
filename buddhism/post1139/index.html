    Modern metta masters? Is there metta in Zen?

    Kindness and compassion are of utmost importance in Zen, but the method is different. Zen doesn't start from an image or feeling that you generate. â€œDon't make anything!" The practice of Zen is based on trust in one's original mind, from which kindness and compassion flow naturally and inevitably.

    For the most part, Zen relies on the deep source of spontaneous action -- the practice moves from the inside, out. But following forms and practicing with others also trains the practitioner in surrender and kindness. Admittedly, many misunderstand the forms and use them to drive emotions away -- to sweep them under the rug, so to speak.

    There's a famous koan about lovingkindness versus suppression of feeling: An old woman who has been providing food and shelter a monk for a considerable time decides to check his progress. She sends her daughter to embrace him and invite him to respond. When the old woman hears of his response (he merely answers with "a withered branch on a cold rock", or some such phase), she drives him away and burns his heritage, lamenting her wasted generosity.

    If I recall correctly, the questions for a student studying this koan are (1) Why did the woman drive the monk away? and (2) If you the monk, how could have responded?

    There are various expressions of metta practice in Zen; and if you're looking for more overtly obvious renditions of lovingkindness practice in Zen, check out Thich Nhat Hanh, who blends traditional Zen with Theravada, and adds his own delightful flavor.
