    TheHeartofTuxes

    I've experienced not so much meditation while lucid dreaming as lucid dreaming while meditating -- a practice maintained non-stop even through sleep. I've also spontaneously become lucid in dreams by virtue of my dream character turning to practice, then realizing that it/I was dreaming. (Disclosure: I also study with a Vajrayana master.)

    I don't ascribe to the view that lucid dreaming is harmless. The very reason people seek it out -- its influence at subconscious levels -- is the reason it is volatile and dangerous business. Of course, everyone likes to think "never mind, I got this"; but the fundamental question looms large: can you trust the one who's overseeing the process?

    We have to remember that we start from ignorance. Even if we exclude the insincere, thrill-seeking, New-Agey, self-intoxicated psychonauts from the discussion, even those of us with sincere intent still have deeply habitual self-clinging and reactivity. It is our shared human situation. Those habits and reactions then steer the boat through dreamworld. Spiritual materialism can arise at the snap of a finger; and without the grounding influence of body and waking senses, one's mind easily follows it's own subtle self-fooling habit.

    The reason why masterful guidance, psychic protection, establishment of prerequisite skills, and stable, proven compassionate direction are required in Vajrayana's dream practice isn't for the sake of hoarding the practice only for a select elite; it's because the ones with the clearest perception know what we're on about -- they know how the mind works and what twists it can come up with.

    We may be quick to condemn the way we humans stick our noses into everything natural and try to manipulate it to our ends, polluting the world in the process; but sometimes we're not so rigorous with our own self-manipulation. We're similarly hell-bent on placing our stamp on every last inch of wilderness, outer or inner.

    In my experience, many people interested in lucid dreaming have a hidden agenda, unbeknownst to them: the agenda of control. They want the BIG remote control, the one with all the buttons, which will eliminate all uncertainty, render known all unpredictability, and hem in the boundless void. "If I can get down into the wiring of my mind," they think, "I can fix everything."

    It's not like that. How tragic it would be, within a stilted, frustrated life of control-seeking, to venture into the territory that is free, at least in one's dreams -- the dark, flowing, expansive territory in which one finds one's respite and regeneration -- and set up camp like the Spanish invasion of the Americas, determined to bring it all under one's rule.

    So who or what is at the helm? What is the real agenda?

    I urge seekers not to be power-hungry, because the very hunger is already the mistake, already the poison. I urge seekers not to look for shortcuts, because the bypassing itself is the hidden sabotage.

    Investigate, for instance, why you want to sidestep being accountable to a teacher and a rigorous form. The tricky little agendas are what you'll implant deep in your mind. It is very easy to make your situation much, much worse.

    Those who have already awakened offer steps and protocols for your protection and speedy advancement. If you want to go fast, follow the steps without looking down side roads. I've never encountered any among those who fearfully or arrogantly avoid teachers, who progressed very far at all, much less quickly. They become mostly collectors of methods and views and experiences and ego characters, but year after year their character and habit doesn't significantly change.

    Having said all that, maybe one in ten million is on a lone path and can keep their pole star in sight. It likely means that they've already studied with great teachers in the past.
