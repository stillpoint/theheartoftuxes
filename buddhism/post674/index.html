    Being in awe can expand time and enhance well-being

    I would say that it's not quite the same, but not different. Rejoicing and awe share the effect of unity or 'disappearing', where you as a personality disappear and no residue remains outside of the experience. You (the subject) and the experience (the object) merge. That is an aspect of the enlightened mind.

    In Buddhism, the four Brahmaviharas or 'divine abidings' are taught as four facets or aspects of enlightenment: lovingkindness (metta), compassion (karuna), shared joy (mudita), and equanimity (upekkha).

    The third of these, shared joy (perhaps what you will be looking into as 'rejoicing') is said by the great Namgyal Rinpoche to excellent and important medicine for our contemporary society — a fast path for us as a community to attain enlightenment. The classic example of this joy is the frolicking of a young horse. Have you seen horses frisking in the fields, not in response to anything but the energy coursing through them? Or in springtime, if you happen to see a group of baby squirrels tumbling over each other and popping up into the air like popcorn.... Or puppies knocking down a toddler in their exuberance. The joy takes over; it is overflowing; no thought of "I am experiencing joy" remains, there is only the joy, and the whole universe is joyful at that moment.

    I would say that awe is like the background to that, the base layer. Otherwise named Unknowing or Open Awareness or Stopping, awe has less of the emotional content (although it may facilitate it). It is like when you are struck by a view of the full moon in the instant when racing clouds part; or when you experience love at first sight, disappearing in another's eyes: before the thrill or overflow of the heart, there is just... [speechlessness]. Your mind has stopped, and only its reflection — the moon, or the eyes — exists.

    From a personal standpoint, what both rejoicing and awe share is your willingness and ability to give yourself over, to be filled with the moment.

    Edit: Awe and rejoicing are two of our most precious, most valuable human skills, and yet they are almost completely overlooked in our education. Instead our society values a mind filled with concepts and opinions.

    Soen Nakagawa Roshi, in one of my favorite quotes, said:

    > Nowadays, there is no one capable of being dumbfounded.
