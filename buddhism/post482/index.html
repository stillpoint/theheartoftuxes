    >Is there something I am missing, or overlooking?

    That is all thinking-style. Practice is doing-style, not thinking-style.

    Thinking can't reach it. It can only help you to the point where you know what action to take. After that point, more thinking only takes you away. It's like missing your stop while riding the bus: the more you stay on the bus, the farther away you go.

    So you have to get off the thinking bus ride, and actually enter the world and practice. You have to do it.

    Next, Alan Watts had his own way of thinking and speaking. Krishnamurti had his own way. And other teachers and sources each have their way. Trying to find your own enlightened nature by comparing these different sources is like missing your stop and then getting on other buses. You go around and around.

    You have to get off the thinking bus, and do a practice. You have to apply it. Regardless of what someone says, and regardless of what ideas you come up with, you won't discover the truth without getting off the bus.

    In a sense, returning to normal life is the goal; but we are very, very confused between our common, usual life and normal life. Normal life means everything is okay: what you like can come and go, and what you don't like can come and go. Everything is no problem; you remain openhanded and aware and compassionate, not depending on any circumstance.

    That is normal life, but who lives like that? One person in a million, maybe. Our common life is a life of attachment. We chase after the things we want to feel and we avoid the things we don't want to feel. Oh, and we completely numb out to the rest of life. It's all about "I, my, me": my opinion matters most; it's most important that I get what I want. It even gets carried over into supposedly spiritual pursuits: my enlightenment is the one that matters; and it has to happen in a style that's comfortable for me. So we are always chasing and avoiding and numbing out, chasing and avoiding and numbing out, around, around, around.

    On the one hand, there is common life going around and around; on the other hand, there is normal life that embodies care for all things and freedom from all attachments.

    Getting off the thinking bus is necessary. Find a teacher in a traditional practice, learn the practice, and do it. The doing is itself enlightenment. Even before you realize it, when your action is complete you are already expressing your complete nature. It doesn't depend on what you believe. It doesn't depend on what you feel.
