    This ego is very strong, what should be done?

    That's great that you recognize your situation, and that you further are willing to seek out advice and face it.

    Extremely few people are capable of guiding their own practice. When an extra-strong ego is part of the constellation of conditions, the possibility of finding your way alone pretty much disappears. Ego, after all, has no motivation to eliminate ego.

    You acknowledge that your patterns get the best of you much of the time, even overshadowing what you understand theoretically. Therefore you need to find external conditions that will support (read: control) your way-seeking mind.

    I suggest submitting to a teacher and a course of training. Studying in formal situations with others will be extremely beneficial for you: the form of the training and the need to harmonize with the group will work on the mindset that only wants it's own way, bringing it to the forefront of your awareness and eroding it situation by situation.

    If you are serious about progressing, you should throw yourself into a course of study, frequent retreats, and even residential training.

    Another very useful step for you is to meet practice on a heart level rather than just through thinking and talking. It is important for your correct life direction to take seat deeply in your being, and not just on the surface; because there will be many times when you come to that crossroads between what is right and what you like; and your direction has to be firm enough to see you through.

    Awareness of death and death meditation is the best way I know of for clarifying your life direction on a heart level. You understand and embrace your impermanence as a real and imminent truth, not merely as a theoretical possibility; and that puts your priorities in order quite naturally.

    But if you can't even bring yourself to try death meditation because your patterns get in the way (as they do for most of us), then you should spend even more time around strong practitioners and awakened beings, so you can be carried along in their wake, so to speak.

    Physical practice will also be effective for you; and at this point it may mean almost any kind of physical activity -- anything that helps you get out of your head and into your body -- into real, simple, everyday life. Physical practice is also a traditional method of controlling the wandering will.

    I've seen many of your posts now, and have had a number of encounters with you on various threads, and its clear that you have both a sincere aspiration and an attachment to thinking-understanding. This is an extremely common combination among seekers: the thinking and its habits tend to dominate the spirit of sincere effort. Getting into the physical reality and out of the thinking realm, where all these patterns and obstacles take hold, is very helpful. (But the ego mistrusts it, because there's no thinking-knowledge to grab onto.)

    Finally, if the patterns are so dominant that you can't bring yourself to apply any of the remedies, you have to suffer more. That is, you have to go through enough agony or be faced with such great loss or fear of loss that you wake up and start to make an appropriate effort.

    Cause and effect are clear. What you get from practice is directly related to what you put into it. There is no other liberative mechanism; you do it. Shallow effort: shallow result. Ordinary effort: ordinary result. Extraordinary effort: extraordinary result.

    Good luck, and let me know if I can help further.
