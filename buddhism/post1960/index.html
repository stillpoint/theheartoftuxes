    I want to learn more about Buddhism. Help!

    There is no single, definitive book in Buddhism like the Bible or Quran. There are many different schools of thought and training within Buddhism, and while there is some overlap of basic principles there is also much disagreement on which teachings are authentic and which texts are accepted.

    The Buddha's teachings are comprised in older, basic teachings of the Pali Canon (in three categories known as the Tripitaka) as well as in the later, evolved teachings of the Mahayana Canon. The actual texts in these collections number in the tens of thousands.

    • A very good, accessible original text that presents well the attitude of Buddhism is the Dhammapada. I recommend the translation by Ananda Maitreya.

    • The Dhammacakkappavattana Sutta is the first sermon delivered by the Buddha; and as such, it serves as a good introduction to the principles of his teachings. This online version uses the word "stress" in place of what is usually translated as "suffering", though neither word is perfect. It talks about the nature of all things as unsatisfactory, insubstantial, and ever-changing; and it lays out the path of how best to deal with that.

    • A sutra on classic methods of breath-based meditation is the Anapanasati Sutta ("Sutta" is Sutra in Pali).

    • For a better understanding of basic principles, I'd recommend a book that organizes those principles rather than reading through sutra after sutra. What The Buddha Taught by Walpola Rahula is a very good exposition of basic principles in the old school of Theravada Buddhism, but which most schools of Buddhism will likely agree upon. (Some editions of this book also include selections from the Dhammapada and some Pali Sutras.)

    There are also some wonderful and well-known books written by modern masters which transmit the Buddha's teaching in the different approaches and flavors of the various schools. Some that come to mind are:

    Food for the Heart: The Collected Teachings of Ajahn Chah (Theravada)

    Wisdom of No Escape and the Path of Loving-Kindness By Pema Chodron (Tibetan)

    The Buddhism of Tibet By H. H. The Dalai Lama (Tibetan)

    Zen Mind, Beginner's Mind By Shunryu Suzuki (Zen)

    Three Pillars of Zen By Philip Kapleau (Zen)

    The Heart of Buddha's Teaching By Thich Nhat Hanh (Zen & Theravada)
