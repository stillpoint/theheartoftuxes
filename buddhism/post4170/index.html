    Anything that ever happens in the world is only happening (experienced) in the mind.

    No Zen master is attached to language. No Zen master hides behind others: “Well, so-and-so said...." So, no, this is not a satisfactory response.

    Zen Master Ma Tzu taught “mind is Buddha," but when people attached to his words he then taught “no mind, no Buddha." Buddha himself said that all beings have Buddha-nature. But people attached to that phrase, so Zen master Chao Chou taught “mu!" -- “Does a dog have Buddha-nature? No!"

    I'm not saying this to cut you down, but to help cut off your attachment. You are interested in Zen, so you should be introduced to the direct way, not settling for “chattering Zen".

    There is, of course truth to the statement that everything is mind; but if you say it, you are attached to words. This is not Zen, and this is not direct pointing to reality. You are depending on the scriptures rather than expressing the living truth beyond words.

    How, then, can you find and express the mind-is-everything that is beyond words and before thinking? This question is very wide, so it has many possible responses. Zen Master Chao Chou, having just come from his temple courtyard responded with “The cypress tree in the courtyard." This is a vibrant, immediate, no-thinking response.

    So without trying to prove anything or hearken back to a concept, and before making any other ideas, what is the real scene facing you just now? What is your “cypress tree in the courtyard"?

    When you access it, you don't need to argue for the idea that mind is everything; you don't need to demean yourself by coming down on one side of an argument. If mind is everything, the living moment speaks for itself. This is why Zen Masters never depended on speech or no-speech. “Go drink tea" is a very high-level teaching, beyond any explanation. So whenever you or someone else are stuck in the conceptual realm, you can invite them to go drink tea -- to go have a real experience, truth beyond words.

    Very simple. It's so simple that people can't deal with it. Many can't believe that's all there is to it: “Just tea? There must be some secret meaning." But truth speaks for itself. If you now go have a cup of tea, you will get some Zen.

    So before making concepts, what is mind? Do you have a good answer? If you don't want to continue posting here, we can continue the conversation in private.
