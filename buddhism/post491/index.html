    How to deal with attachment to views and opinions?

    You already know that your opinions are illusion and that your view is relative and fallible. You wouldn't be asking this question if you weren't already aware of the hypocrisy involved in attaching strongly to opinion.

    The pivotal matter is what you will do about it. Will you defend an identity built upon illusion, or will you open your hands to the truth, in which you are not always "right" and "better"?

    What will you place at the center of your life: ego, or truth?

    This is the first decisive act in Buddhism: going for refuge. Where do you take refuge? Do you choose to put your trust in your enlightened nature, or do you choose to put your trust in an identity cobbled together by opinion? Cause and effect are clear; as your choice goes, so will your life unfold.

    >…I can't help but do it from the starting point that I am obviously the one who is right….

    This in itself is one of the thoughts you use to fool yourself, and which form the structure of your self-image. "I can't help it" is not sincere, is it? It's more like "I don't want to" or "I'm afraid to" or "I lose awareness and follow my habit". With sincerity you can work with the situation; without sincerity you are trapped in your own self-deception.

    It takes courage to open your hands; but when you do, you regain your great being. Because of your habitual opinionation, you have built a self-image that is tight and narrow. It's like wearing clothing that's too small. Very uncomfortable. The smaller the self-identity, the more vulnerable it feels, and the more you are driven to defend it. You put yourself in a small self, and then that small self feels vulnerable, exposed within a vast and unpredictable universe; so then you build up more self-image, more opinion, more habit to bolster the sense of solidity. This is Samsara, the cycle of suffering.

    But originally, you are complete. You are not a small self within a vast and threatening universe; you are the vastness that contains the universe. Your true self doesn't need to be defended. Embodying non-defense is going to the Buddha for refuge — relying on your great nature rather than a small, illusory self. It's just that relying on your great nature feels unknown and ungraspable to the small self. So to save yourself you have to die, in a way: you have to go through the territory of unknowing and ungrasping so that you can identify yourself as your vast, open, enlightened original nature.

    Meditation, then, is the practice of ungrasping and returning to your original nature. It is the practice of taking refuge in Buddha — your own uncontrived mind. Whether the meditation works with an object or without an object, the meditator repeatedly ungrasps and settles. All of these political, philosophical, personal, or spiritual opinions are like fishing hooks that you swallow; thus they control you. Meditation means: don't take the hook — any hook. No holding opinion, no making imaginary scenarios, no chasing trains of thought or other distractions. When you don't take the hook, your Buddha mind appears.

    Much of Buddhist ethical training also has to do with not taking the hook. Adopting precepts of right conduct can help you identify the ways that you get hooked and pulled away from your original enlightened nature. You might want to consider both (proper) meditation practice and taking Buddhist precepts of conduct.

    >The Great Way is not difficult for those who do not pick and choose.

    (Hsin Hsin Ming - The Mind Of Faith, by Zen Master Seng Ts'an)

    ~

    This is the first line of the great classic Hsin Hsin Ming. You already have the Great Way, as long as you don't make "right" and "wrong", "my way" and "your way". When you come to a situation where your opinionation is inflamed, if at that moment you open your hands and let it all be, you will already be traversing the Great Way and expressing your enlightened nature. You will be able to see, hear, taste, smell, feel, and think clearly; and everything will already be truth. Even your own deluded mind will be truth, as you will be able to see "Oh, I want to defend myself; I want to say that I am right."

    I hope you take refuge in the thing that can offer refuge, and abandon the thing that leads to suffering.
